        Thanks to those who responded to my question on main jet size for
Solex PII-40's on a 356 with NPR 1720 p/c's and jsut under 9:1 compression.
A summary of the advice follows:

        The vote is evenly split on whether I need to go leaner than stock,
or richer than stock (no one recommended using the stock jets.)  As to
verifying correct mixture, most felt that plug color was the way to do it.
The major disenting opinion was that you can't get it right w/o an exhaust
gas analyzer, because of the sensitivity of the mixture to jetting, and
subjectiveness of plug color.  I think the key here is access to a gas
analyzer such that you can play with it w/o paying through the nose.  No
opinions were expressed regarding potential engine damage due to running too
rich or lean.
