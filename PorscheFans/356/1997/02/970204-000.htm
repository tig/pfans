Porsche put the holes in there for breakin at a time when it was felt that
this area needed the lubrication early in the engine's life, and a time when
oil didn't work nearly as well as modern oil does. They specifically told
dealers to plug the holes because it was also felt that this area should NOT
get extra oil during its normal life. Furthermore, having these extra holes
draining from the oil pressure system means that less oil is available for
the bearings. Not much, but some. Perhaps that's why they wanted these to be
open during breakin only, when revs and loads were (hopefully) lower. In
those days engines were more sensitive to breakin than modern designs, which
really don't need much at all, and drivers were more likely to adhere to the
breakin instructions. Once the car came in for its first service, the shop
plugged the holes and said it was OK to go for it, they drove the cars
differently.

Bottom line, Steve, leave 'em plugged.
