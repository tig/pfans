I've been lurking on this list for several months now and have never seen the
issue of unleaded gas going into older engines mentioned.  I would like to
know what solutions 912 and 356 owners implement.  Hardened valve seats ?
Lead substitute ?  Not-to-worry ?  I do use a spurt of lead substitute in 
each full tank of gas in my '68 912.  After two years no obvious side-effects
 - webers not gummed, plugs normal - but no idea about valve condition.

Years ago I restored a '50 Stude Pickup and had new seats installed in the 
block during the flat-6 rebuild.  Is this at all a practice when rebuilding
Porsche engines? (Of course in the head, not in the block).  911 or 912/356.

I presume we're all fueled by gas and leaded pumps are scarce country-wide
so I'm surmising we've all probably given this thought at some time.

Thanks for comments.
