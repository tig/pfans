A few notes while we are on the topic...

1)	The P-901 (the 4 spoke early engine yoke) is not ideal for later
engines.  Re: There's no cut out for one of the DME sensors and the
factory claims that  the early yoke is not strong enough.  Also the
early yoke  appears to not be able to accommodate a dual mass flywheel.
On the upside I've had no trouble mounting my '96 993 motor w/o the
flywheel to the stand (I'm preparing .  For those of you putting
3.6liter engine in earlier cars (not using the dual mass flywheel) you
can get away with the cheaper earlier yoke.  You can buy the early and
later yokes from Braun Tools, I can give you contact information if you
are interesting in buying a yoke).

2)	The fit into a standard cheap-o engine stand (the type you buy
at CostCo) isn't completely optimal.  As you tilt the engine over, it
pivots on the stand as you would expect.  However there's a gap between
the flange on the yoke-shaft and the inboard edge of engine stand's
holder tube within which the yoke shaft is inserted, also note that the
engine is slightly angled upwards away from the stand...  As you rotate
the engine on the stand this gap reduces due to gravity.   Thus the
retaining pin holes on the yoke and the engine stand no longer align up.
Lifting the engine with a jack allows you to readjust the alignment of
the pin holes.  The long term fix is to use a spacer collar to fix this
problem (I plan to make one up on my lathe).

3)	For transmission jacks, I really like the Wudel creeper style
jack.  It's a little pricey but has a terrific range and is very easy to
use.  "Jacks" in Seattle sells them locally.  I do not suggest buying
the Porsche or VW adapter plate. re: I've never gotten either of them to
work correctly, just use two chunks of ¾ inch plywood mounted to the
standard plate works great (I can give you the dimensions).  Some people
add carpet to the plywood plate to give a better grip and trap oil
drips, I found this reduces the life span of the plate due to moisture.
On the Wudel jack I recommend you buy the lever style control/adjustment
handle.  The knob sticks and in hard to control (the one on my jack
actually stripped out).

		I am borrowing an engine stand from a friend (Thanks,
Scott!!!). It is a combination of an inexpensive standard engine stand
and the P-901 (is that right?) Porsche engine yoke. The cylindrical
mount of the yoke fits perfectly into the engine stand. So, don't buy
the whole German engine stand, just buy the yoke and use a less
expensive stand.

		I also borrowed a transmission jack (Thanks, Leeds!!!)
which really eased the engine/transmission removal process. I attached
the yoke to the engine while it was still on the jack after removing the
tranny. The jack was able to lift the engine high enough to reach the
yoke mounting hole on the stand upright.

		I must say that having the engine on the stand make it a
real pleasure to work on. This is the first time I have adjusted 911
valves and I can't imagine what a hassle it must be to do it in the car.
And, no, I didn't pull the engine to adjust the valves. The tranny is
getting rebuilt and I am installing a new clutch and SSI headers. I
can't wait to get this car back together!
