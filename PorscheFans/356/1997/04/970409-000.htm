For those who responded to my query.My rotors need turning because they are
pitted.This is because here in Ontario we probably get more salt on the road
than California gets snow (just kidding).Even with regular driving all of my
cars (Probe and Jag), the rotors still get pitted and after turning a couple
of times are replaced.
I have done one and its thickness now is 14.0 mm.
In a major restoration,unless one has unlimited funds,the first approach is
"can we restore,recycle or re-use". This tends to save big gobs of
cash.Remember I have to buy parts from the USA and pay a 40% exchange
rate(yesterdays rate) and 7% Fed tax and shipping and brokerage. To give you
an example.I purchased a used pedal assembly from Easy(California)Their
price was $35.00. (Could not get one elsewhere,new or used).The final landed
cost using UPS was $98 Canadian.
Maybe this will tell you how lucky you are when you suffer 356 disease,and
live in the USA.
