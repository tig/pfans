Hi guys, here's my experience BUT this is with a race engine:

69 912, 12:1, major cam, ported, 1720 shastas etc.

Solex PII-40 v35 good across the whole range even with the overlap of the 
cam,
Weber IDA48 v40 good just before the top but no good everywhere else.

These carbs were both setup on the dyno.  The Solex's transition, and high 
speed enrichment jets allow a full curve tune.  The Webers always went lean 
in transition unless you used huge mains which requires even huger airs but 
then you went lean at the very top.  We tried smaller webers but couldn't 
get near the rpm.  Weird.  Oh 6" velocity stacks where also used.  Really 
helps on a overlapped cam.

With a normal cam and a shorter RPM range I could see the webers working 
just fine.

Oh and Webers are not allowed for vintage racing!
