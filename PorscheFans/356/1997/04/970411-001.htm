In your recent discussion on the mechanics of braking, you made a statement
which I'd like you to amplify:viz."all the grooves do is add to the total
suface area of the pad,just as if it were longer or wider".
Now I believe that if there are grooves in the rotor, what they do is reduce
the area of the rotor that comes into contact with the pad.This is more so
with the modern very hard composition pad, as softer pads might tend to push
into the groove. The area of the pad in contact with the rotor is reduced,by
the area of the grooves(not very much).To be more exact if the grooves are
not centred i.e. concentric,but spiral(I've seen this),then the amount of
reduction of area of rotor is more complex.
So how do you come to your conclusion that the effective area of the pad
increases?The pad area is constant and the the transmitted pressure per
square unit stays constant.The receiving area of rotor declines ,so
effective brake pressure on the rotor declines.
