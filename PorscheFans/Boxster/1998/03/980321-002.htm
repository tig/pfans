One last note on quality/image,

In "The Machine that Changed the World" - they quoted a JD Powers initial
quality report (page 92) that gave average quality of the cars produced in
each area of the world as follows:

1. Japanese, built in Japan: 60 assembly defects per 100 vehicles
2. Japanese, built in USA: 65 /100 vehicles
3. American, built in USA: 82.3/100 vehicles
4. All Europe, built in Europe: 97.0/100 vehicles.

In the 1997 JD Powers report I just posted, the numbers for the Boxster were:
Boxster: 174/100 vehicles.

Assuming "defects" described in the book match the report by the same
company (JD Powers) in the web page, it seems that Porsche has a ways to
go.  I suspect with the new design that both the Boxster and the new 911
body style will quickly come down below at least the USA defect numbers.
