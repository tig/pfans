In the Ferrari, I think the shifting is done with paddles behind the
steering wheel, whereas Porsche's  tiptronic is done with buttons on the
steering wheel.  (The difference is that the buttons rotate with the wheel
and may be hard to find in a hurry.)

I'm not quite sure what you were asking w.r.t. the 993, but the 993 comes
with a 4-speed tiptronic while the Boxster comes with a 5-speed tiptronic.
It handles the extra power of the 993 just fine.  I believe the 996 uses the
newer 5-speed as well.

The main difference between the Ferrari F1 transmission and the Tiptronic is
that the F1 uses a hydraulically actuated clutch whereas the Tiptronic uses
a torque converter more like a conventional automatic.
