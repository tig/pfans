Indeed, there is an oil dipstick available in the rear trunk.  On p 99
of my owners manual, where it describes the oil dipstick it says "The
oil level should be regularly checked on the oil level gauge in the
instrument panel."  It doesn't say when you should instead use the
dipstick.  Just as well -- don't want oil dripping in the trunk!

Another oil related oddity:  the manual says "Engine oils should
preferably be used as synthetic or semisynthetic oils or ask your
authorized Porsche dealer for Porsche tested and approved all-season
oils."  This conflicts with a sticker I saw inside the engine
compartment of a new 911 twin turbo.  The sticker said (paraphrasing)
"all Porsches are shipped exclusively with Mobil-1 and Porsche
recommends only using Mobil 1 oil".  I wonder why the Boxster manual
doesn't encourage you to use Mobil-1.
