Berkeley Johnston also posted some good replies to the same questions. The
Grand Tour is a pretty good deal, and it doesn't get the publicity it
deserves. Dealers have no incentive to push it, since they sell the car
either way. Also it used to be that Euro delivery cars did not count toward a
dealer's allocation. Now they do, so they don't care where you pick it up. In
the old days, any car sold as Euro delivery was in addition to their
stateside allotment. The price was lower but then, even the smaller profit on
Euro delivery was pure gravy and didn't take away from anticipated stateside
deliveries. Now, they do, so dealers don't push Euro delivery.
