Sales and customer deliveries across the product line are one of the "points"
affecting dealer allocations.  A dealer holding deliveries would receive
reduced allocations and PCNA has also informed them against the practice of
artificial price escalation which could further affect future allocations.
 So, I'd subscribe the allegation noted to "myth" or just poor business
practice of the local dealer.

As to availability, Porsche has increased production capacity by outsourcing
to a supplemental production facility (Valmet Automotive, Inc) in Finland
with delivery capability of 5000 cars per year starting in the autumn of
1997.  Porsche further announced it's investigating estabilishing a
production facility in the United States, it's largest export market.

Any "limited availability" of Boxsters is likely to be as short-term as is
paying in excess of MSRP...
