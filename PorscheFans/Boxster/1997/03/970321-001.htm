Thank goodness the Boxster isn't the same as a 911!

It is my opinion the only persons qualified to comment on the differences or
comparisons between the 911 and Boxster (986) are those of us who have owned
both.  
Merely having a perception of the two doesn't cut it.  I'd rather hear warts
and all, not tops only.

A drive around the block, down the street, or for a whole day in a Boxster
does not impart what ownership is about.  Not by any stretch, okay?
Even mine, at 3,000 miles, is delivering new input to the quotient.  [Each
and every day it gets better to me, as a car, and better than my 911 ever
was.]

If someone wants to split hairs between the 993 & 911 they have every reason
to, the 993 is very different than the older 911.  A better topic for
discussion, the subtle and not so subtle differences between those two;  they
do share major design characteristics.  But it doesn't belong here.

Maybe it was missed somewhere along the way;  the Boxster engine is in the
middle, not the back and it's half as much money to buy.  It's a ROADSTER;
 an open automobile having a single seat in the front for two or three people
and a luggage compartment in the back.
It's not a GTP tube car with 700 h.p., nor airplane without wings.  But then,
neither is a 911 or 993 (is it?)

In considering my purchase I'll admit to NOT having thought about changing
the water pump.  
How could I have been so foolish?!
