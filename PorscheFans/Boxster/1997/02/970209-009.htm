Bill:

1. Never ever go to a mechanical car wash. Wash it yourself.
2. Use a 2 step process: glaze first for mirror finish - then wax to keep
it that way.
3. Use a high carnuba content liquid wax. Any of the 3 M's work: Mother's,
Malms, Meguiars.
4. Wax about  every three months. In between wash only with cold water, you
don't need detergent unless it's really dirty. Detergent removes the wax.
After a few waxings your paint will seem to be an inch deep.
5. Avoid mechanical buffers, do it by hand. If you can get ahold of some
old cloth diapers, there is nothing better for buffing and wax application.
Of course, wax immediately after washing. Figure 2-3 hours for the whole
job; it's a workout, but worth it.
