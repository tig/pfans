       The other note that mentioned the 70MPH vibration was very
  interesting. As a Miata owner and club member I have read about and
  experienced a dreaded high-speed (well, OK, only 65MPH) vibration. It has
  been traced to a wheel balance problem. The car is very sensitive to any
  imbalance. All wheels must be dynamically (spun) as opposed to statically
  balanced. Most places I've seen perform static balancing. This is fine
  for a family minivan but not for sports cars like the Boxster or Miata. I
  would check for the problem in this fashion: wheel balance first; tire
  "roundness" (flat spotted) second; wheel "roundness" (bent) third;
  4-wheel alignment fourth; check suspension components last. Good luck and
  please post what the problem turned out to be, if you find it. By the
  way, how bad is the vibration?
