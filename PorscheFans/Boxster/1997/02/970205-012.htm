Update on Boxsters from Finland. Feb. 5, -97.

German politicians & unions opposite Porsche's decision
to move part of Boxster production to Finland.
Wendelin Wiedeking from Prosche defend their decision 
by stating that production in Finland is the best solution.
Germans claim that their competitiveness will suffer
and also that the unemployment is in record numbers 
after war. There is also car manufacturing plants in Germany
witch arent utilizing their capacity fully.
Porsche had checked the German factories, but Finland won.
Lower salary cost had no significant meaning, but the    
flexibility of the Finnish factory had. Finnish factory will 
produce yearly 5 000 Boxsters, and production will start 
this year.
Wendeling Wiedeking state also, economy was not crucial. 
Quality is high and production can start quickly, within 
half year.

Wiedeking do not believe that the "Finnish Porsche" will
be resisted like 924. He continue that this model was 
produced by VW daughter company Audi and Porsche 
customers never accepted it.
