In terms of color popularity, I think red is the most "popular" for
Porsches and sports cars in general.  Some love it; some hate it.  Seems
most Porsches I see are either red, black, or white.  In my opinion, get
the color you really want, even if you have to wait a while for it.  You
will be reminded of it every time you're in the car.

>Another opinion, I drove the car and have a deposit down on it, but
>can't pick it up because it is in an auto show all of next weekend--it
>seems a bit crazy to have a buyer with cash, but an auto show comes
>first. Their only other car is the red demo that they of course want to
>keep on the lot for test drives. I am concerned with people at the auto
>show somehow damaging the car. Should I demand some concession on the
>car as a result?

They probably will not give you any kind of deal due to the show.
Hopefully the car will be locked at the show, so Bubba and his huge key
ring can't sit in your pristine leather seats.  Just go over the car with a
fine-toothed comb before you lay your money down, and whatever you do,
don't drive it home before any damages (scratches, dings, etc.) are
repaired to *your* satisfaction.  If you note any damages, I'd give them an
extra deposit to hold the car (to show your good faith) until repairs are
complete.  Then do the deal.

>They are in a strong position as of course there are a bunch of people
>that will take it immediately if I don't.

If the car isn't what you want and in the condition you want, let someone
else have it.  There will be others.  No doubt you will be very emotional
at this point, and may not be critical enough.  If you can, take a friend
with you who is more subjective.  Be prepared to leave without the car if
necessary.  If you settle due to dealer pressure, you will regret it later.

>BTW, the dealer has been very nice and has indicated that he will call
>right away if he gets in any other cars and can let me have mine. I
>believe they are being honorable, just trying to juggle priorities. BTW
>the car is in Riverside, CA and will be in the Orange County auto show
>this weekend.

Maybe I'm jaded, but don't trust them.  Good luck!
