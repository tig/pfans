Dear Autoweek:

Why are we comparing a car with a tail that looks like a kit car from a
bad B-Grade sci-fi armageddon movie, a suspension copied, probably
poorly and 20 years late, from the Porsche 944 or 928, lousy panel fit
and finish (my observation), and no convertible option, to a sports car
like the Porsche Boxster?  
Looking at the road tests over the past year between BWM, Porsche,
Viper, Ferrari, Corvette, it looks to me like Corvettes are always
coming in last to the old 911 design.  GM, with all its resources can't
beat a 50 year old design that stated with what was basically a Beetle,
and they aren't anywhere near the Boxster in beauty of design or
handling, only in getting 60 horsepower and 24 mph more more out of a
much larger engine.  
Considering the GM experiences with aluminum engines, service, paint
life, honesty, design flaws and the dealer network, if I want a sports
car I'll take the Porsche.  If I want a great stereo and a car to take
down to Indianapolis Raceway Park for the Tuesday drag races I'll buy a
Firebird Formula with the Corvette engine.
