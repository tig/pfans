The smoking on start up is due to an increase in the volume of oil sprayed up
under the piston. This is true of the 95 & up 911 engines as well as the
Boxter engine. This is also contributory to the oil consumption in later 911s
though the Boxter does not seem to consume nearly as much, assumably due to
lower cylender temps from liquid cooling. Smokin is at it's worst when a cold
engine is started and run a very short time then left to sit for a long
period. The next start will smoke like crazy.. 

As far as cleaning goes, a good sheepskin mit is the prefered tool, it will
float the dirt away from the surface during the wash, diapers, sponges and
other such items will not.

On the question of "chrome wheels", an absoult no-no on a Porsche. The wheel
is designed to dissapate heat from the brakes with the airflow thru the wheel
to cool it. If it is chromed, it does not dissapate the heat properly and can
result in overheating brakes. Polish them instead.
