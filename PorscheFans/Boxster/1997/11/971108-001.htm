After reading all of the comments re Barrier in Seattle IM surprise that =
anyone still buys there.  Yogi said it best "They'er so busy no one goes =
there anymore".  I had similar problems with Taub Porsche in Santa =
Monica.  Arrogance fails to aptly describe the crappie way they handled =
my order including a refusal to show me the actual order ever.  I ended =
up taking a car which obviously was an allocation car and not a custom =
order (wrong options and tire size) because the dealer made it clear =
that folks were lined up and "If I didn't like the car as is -- no =
problem-- they'd sell it to someone else."  So I took the car but have =
not gone back.  Porsche in Reno was nice enough to introduce me to the =
regional sales guy who in turn connected me up with the service manager =
at Beverly Hills Porsche.  The entire experience was terrible and as =
this was the third Porsche I had ordered from the factory the process =
came as a real surprise.  Remember it was Porsche as few years ago who =
tried unsuccessfully to eliminate their entire dealer network and =
replace them with fixed price factory warehouse sales facilities staffed =
by trained non commission sales reps.  The dealers took Porsche to court =
and stopped them.  As for selling cars over sticker.  Here's what =
dealers do.. Sell a new care to a "friend of the house"  with an =
understanding that the dealer will take the car back as a used car after =
600 to 800 miles are on the odo.  The friend gets to drive a new rig for =
a period of time and the dealer gets to resell the car at what ever =
price the market will bear. Porsche NA man not like it but they can't do =
a think about it.
