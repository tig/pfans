Dan J. McDuff  wrote:

> My problem is excessive tire wear during club time trialing.  The
> car is a completely stock '86 951 using R1's, with front alignment
> set at 0 toe and -1.5 degrees camber.  This setup worked well for
> autocrossing, but not so well on the track.  Apparently during
> hard braking, the front geometry changes towards more positive toe
> and more negative camber, which is causing some rather aggressive
> scrub wear on the inside of the front tires.  I intend to dial in
> some negative toe, but would welcome any recommendations on more
> suitable alignment settings.

Dan:

Static toe-in at the front wheels MAY help with your tire-wear 
problem, but it'll probably have negative consequences in terms of 
increased corner-entry understeer.

I assume that you have the bump-steer problem because your car's been
lowered.  I know nothing about the water-cooled Porsches, but
bump-steer can be dialed out of the air-cooled cars by moving the
steering rack... Does anyone make steering-rack spacers for the 951?
