    RENO, Nev., June 4 /PRNewswire/ -- The Porsche web site will provide live
reports on the upcoming 1997 24 Hours of Le Mans endurance race, it was
announced here today.
          The site, which may be found at www.porsche.com, will keep Porsche
race
fans up to date on the efforts of the Porsche factory GT1 teams and all of
the
customer prototype, GT1 and GT2 teams during the June 14-15 classic.
          With 14 overall wins, Porsche is the most successful manufacturer
in the
history of the event.  Last year, a Porsche prototype driven by Manuel
Reuter,
Davy Jones and Alexander Wurz took the checkered fag after 354 laps, just in
front of two Porsche factory 911 GT1 cars.
          The new Porsche factory GT1 entries, driven by Hans Stuck-Thierry
Boutsen-
Bob Wollek and Ralf Kelleners-Emmanuel Collard-Yannick Dalmas, successfully
pre-qualified last month for next week's race-week qualifying.  All six
private GT1 teams also successfully pre-qualified.
          Among the Porsche customer teams to watch include the entry of
Mario
Andretti-Michael Andretti in a Porsche-powered Courage C3 6 prototype; the
GT1
team of Franz Konrad and Mauro Baldi; and the Roock Racing entry of Andre
Ahrle-Andy Pilgrim-Pierre Yver in a GT2.
