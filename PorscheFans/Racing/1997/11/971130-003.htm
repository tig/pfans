Just a couple of quick responses to two posts. 

Chris posted:
>I just stopped at a Racetek cage dealer and looked at an installed
>cage in a wrecked 951 with no interior. I like the idea of a sill 
>mounting. It looks strong and makes a lot of sense. I mentioned to
>the dealer that looking at the design, it seems that the rear fold down 
>seat top has to come off (the cage has two rear braces that extend 
>into the hatch area). I don't have a problem with that, but he also
>mentioned that if I was going PCA club racing, the seat would have
>to remain, in a halfway state, neither fully up or fully down. He stated
>that interior items can be removed for safety reasons, but the PCA 
>rules folks have decreeded that the top half of the seat has to remain.

I have a RaceTec in my 951, as do several friends.  All of us have the top
half of the rear seat back removed, as it does interfere with the cage.  The
rules allow for that removal, if it interferes with the cage [as it does].
I don't think I have seen any 944 variation of  PCA club racer with a full
cage and the top half of the rear seat installed.  You should be fine [the
seat back ways about 35 pounds, so it helps offset the 90 pounds or so the
cage weighs].  I think the RaceTec is a good choice, above the Autopower,
although not as extensive [or expensive!] as some of the "Matter" [sp?]
styled cages that Porsche installed their Cup Cars.

On the Turbo S considering Big Reds up front -- Steve Meltzer posted:

>I am thinking about "big red"calipers for my 944TS. Will they take me
>out of my stock class? If I "run a chip", this will def'n change my
>classs, but would adding the calipers at that point make any difference?
>steve meltzer resurrected '88 944 turbo s, Zermatt silver. Mechanic
>wannabe, Mac Believer ..

The big calipers will move you up a class. If you do "chip" your car, you
can do both and only progress the one class [from E to D].  You can only
"Chip" the DME chip, not the KLR chip that effects boost however.  This is
probably NOT a competitive move from a car class perspective.  They won't
buy you much in terms of Class D.

Good luck whatever you decide.
