Q. Where do I get 500 lb. springs?  I have Koni Yellows on the front.
 Non-height adjustable.  All I've found is 200, 210 and 250 lb. 
springs.

A. You can get "slide over your existing shock" threaded sleeve 
adapters from Carrara.  I suggest Hyperco springs.  Or you can replace 
the existing shocks with a threaded body shock (Koni or Penske).

  << Make sure you have sufficient rebound damping for this heavy 
spring,
 and go relatively light on the anti-roll bars (23mm/19-21mm effective 
 rate).  This will give you a neutral car that corners flat but still 
 handles bumps well.  Incidentially, you will need to optimize the
 toe/camber for the tires you plan to use as well as precision weight 
 balance the car for this to work properly.  >>

Q. Can corner balancing be done without height adjustable struts?

A. You can use shims under the springs with the factory non-adjustable 
struts, and change the rear ride height with the spring plate 
adjusters, or the spline on the torsion bar in extreme cases.

Q. What do you recommend for camber and toe?

A. Depends on the tire and wheel combination.  If you have the 
aforementioned springs installed (500/400 or 500/30mm) and you are 
using Hoosiers (the best performance/price combination for "pure 
racing")  you will have good results with:
Front Camber -3
	Front Toe +1/8"
	Rear Camber -2.7
	Rear Toe 0"
