You will need some "Butler Built" ducts and the appropriate high temp 
hose.  Longacre is a good source for these.  If you want to see the 
"optimal" setup for a stock class 951 (or prepared) check out the 
picture at http://www.drextec.com/racing.htm.  This worked GREAT, but 
you have to cut the front spoiler somewhat (might be smart to buy a 
cheap fiberglass one and store the factory piece) and you have to 
relocate the oil cooler (I replaced it with a pair of Mocal Coolers 
from BAT).  You'll be able to run the hoses (get the 3" high temp 
versions) directly to a "cool brake" distribution duct on the front 
wheel hub.  You need a LOT of cooling with this car, and this will get 
you what you need,  Run slotted rotors with PFC 91 compound pads and 
AP 550 fluid and you will have brakes that actually last long enough 
for a PCA race.
