Thad, if you only change the engine to the specs of a 72 S, (and if a 
previous owner hasn't added a few things that could take it out of the 
production classes) it will be classified as a Production car.   

Consider staying with the engine as it now is and running in I for a 
season or so.  You'll likely have more than enough other things to do to 
get a competitive car (suspension, driving technique, exhaust, 
engine cooling, etc) to eat up your time and budget.  I  would 
suggest that you not change the engine until you have to rebuild it, or 
start winning in I class.  I ran my 73T in I class for awhile until the 
engine failed and I had to rebuild.  That's normally a large class, and 
I bet you'll be surprised at how competitive those pesky 914 2.0's and 
944's can be, especially on short twisty tracks.  

Another thing to consider is that F class is normally dominated by early 
944T's and 3.2 Carrera's.  I don't recall seeing any 2.4S club 
racers (I'm sure there are some), and wonder how competitive a 2.4S 
would be. You might want to run a season or two before committing the 
dollars to upgrading to 2.4S specs, during which you can solicit the 
expert opinions of others as to whether a 2.4S is likely to be a good F 
car.

If you've already decided, there is a forum member who bought a GT4S 
with a 2.4 engine, who is planning to upgrade to a 2.7 or 2.8.  Maybe 
he'd sell you his entire engine and rebuild yours, or perhaps there is 
some other area for mutual interest.  If you post a notice, he'll likely 
respond.  I don't recall his name right now.  

Just my 2 cents worth.
