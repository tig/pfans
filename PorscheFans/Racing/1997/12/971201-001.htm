> I would think the dual rate (stacked) spring would be an excellent
> combination for rough tracks, but you would have to control body 
roll
> more (anti-roll bars) than with an standard "single" rate spring.  I 
> found that the shock valving was rather critical with higher than
> stock rate springs, and careful adjustment of the bump with 500 lb. 
> springs should get you a pretty terrific handling car without going 
to
> a dual rate spring stack.  You already have a "progressive" setup in 
> the rear with your torsion bars in place with the 300 lb. springs as 
> helpers, so I wouldn't bother with a dual rate spring in the rear at 
> all.  The money might be better spent on hard (spherical) bushings 
ALL
> around in the suspension, and hard mounts for the rear subframe, as 
> these changes really provide an sharpness and consistency to the 944 
> that is sorely lacking with the stock bushings.  Unless the track is 
> really rough, or you need the weight transfer because you are 
running
> super high spring rates (oval track setup, 650 lbs. or higher) I
> wouldn't spend the money - you may not go any faster!
