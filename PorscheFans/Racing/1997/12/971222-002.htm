>There is a very significant reason for a thermostat besides just keeping
>it from over cooling. Cold weather starts. Now I don't know what
>temperature ranges your car sees, but here in Colorado, we can have
>track events below freezing and above 90 depending on time of year. I've
>already seen a few examples of burst oil coolers because they hadn't
>installed thermostats. Besides keeping a cooler from over cooling, a
>thermostat also reduces or cuts pressure going to the oil cooler when
>it's very cold and the oil is very heavy and thick. I run synthetic oil
>and I've still seen 80psi at startup once in a while in the winter.
>Keeping excessively high pressures out of an oil cooler is good idea.
>Once the oil gets warm, the pressure drops and does not pose a hazzard
>to the cooler. I have a 25row fan forced oil cooler with a pressure
>bypass thermostat. 

You are correct.  I have since installed a thermostat.   While I was 
running with no thermostat I was very mindful of the possibility of 
overpressurizing the cooler.  I too use Mobile 1 which helps.
