In the middle of last racing season i installed droop stops on my car, 

(they call it droop stops here in Sweden)it is a wire or something that 
prevents the front shock absorbers from extracting form the normal ride 
height. So, when hard cornering the inner front wheel rather leaves the 
ground than helps the car to roll. This was one of the biggest 
improvements on the car that year and i felt the car turn just like on 
rails. It also prevented the inner rear wheel from spinning so i do not 
longer suffer the lack of a LSD. Has anyone else tried this or have you 
already got it on your track cars? I run 700lbs springs front and 
600lbs rear on my 924 with TA R1s. Please respond to the list.
