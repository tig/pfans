Walter Fricke wrote:
> 
> Rob Channell wonders what the deal is with using nitrogen in tires.  He
> gives Boyle's law (or is it Charles'?) and reasons that the increase in
> pressure due to an increase in  heat in the same volume should be the
> same no matter what the gas might be.  I guess I had always understood
> the physics to be thus also.
> 
> Air in air tanks from compressors typically has moisture in it, sometimes
> quite a lot. Higher pressure = higher boiling point = humidity changes back
> to liquid?   What I have heard is that it is the moisture in the air which
> causes it not to follow Boyles law (that's not the way you hear it
> expressed at race tracks).  Analyzing the pressure behavior of such a
> mixed gas exceeds my knowledge, alas, though the theory sounds good.

Basically, this is correct. The moisture in compressed air is _not_
gasseous. It is in fact water suspended in air. When depressurised (from
100+ psi) and heated it becomes gas and expands significantly in volume. 

>  Nitrogen is "dry," no doubt because of the care used in its manufacture.
> As such it shouldn't suffer from this complication.  Its pressure still
> should increase with temperature.

Yup, but is more predictable (no humidity factor) and will expand less
as well.  

> Folks who haul one of those huge commercial tanks along to run air tools
> have nitrogen in them (is it the least expensive?), and so use it for tires.
> Does anyone think it really makes a difference for our 20 minute races?

Really? I would expect, if anything, a small nitrogen bottle for tires
and just a big 'ol compressor or tank of compressed air. Dry nitrogen is
a good bit more expensive than just plain old compressed air. As for
what kindof difference it makes? I dunno, it depends on how into the
toys of the trade you are. I suppose it might make things just a wee bit
better to predict, but can't see it make that much of a difference
unless you do it _all_ the time (read professional) and are battling
over tenths of a second. But what do I know? I'm just an amateur...
