Karen wrote:
> > I have had an aggravating problem occur to my 944 three times.  Each time
> it happened when I had driven my car hard -- twice after driving it for a
> day or two on the track, and once after a skiing weekend at high altitude
> at Lake Tahoe while driving it back.  I'm just really glad it hasn't
> happened on the track yet!
> 
> Anyway, it has happened on a long hill/mountain each time. I admit it, it
> is quite likely I was lugging it in 5th up the long steep hills after being
> so tired and not paying attention to downshifting.  The car skips a beat,
> hesitates,  loses power. As long as I don't accelerate, it acts fine. But
> as soon as I press down on the gas, it skips and continues to lurch until I
> back off.  It starts off just feeling as if my foot slipped on the
> accelerator, and gradually worsens until I have to pull off the road.  The
> first two times it happened I didn't try to turn it back on until the next
> day, when it was fine.  Last weekend I pulled over and went back after
> twenty minutes... it started up fine.
> 
> It _feels_ like a fuel problem to me--I had several Datsun Z's that would
> get clogged fuel filters and have the same symptoms, and replacing (or even
> wiggling) the fuel filter would cure it.  But I replaced my fuel filter,
> and it's happened twice since then.  My next guess is to replace the fuel
> pump.  But I hate to randomly replace parts, especially when they get
> successively more expensive.
> 
> Ihad a similiar problem with a 911 and it proved to be contaminated fuel. I used rubbing alcohol added to the gas - about a pint per 5 gal - don't fill the tank more than half way. The alcohol will mix with the water in the tank and burn off, fuel filter will, hopefully, catch the crud. Better yet, drain and flush the fuel tank. therren@aol.com
