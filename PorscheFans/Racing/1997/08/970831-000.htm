A very over-simplified answer is:

You didn't indicate whether you installed the Weltmeister Front or Rear 
bar but,.......the theory is basically the same. For Autocross, you 
would be quicker with a car that is neutral to slightly "loose". For 
that effect the front bar would be loosened and the rear bar tightened.
For Roadracing, a "loose" car may be difficult to control, unless one is 
an expert driver. Roadracing setups generally favor a "tighter" setup. 
This would be accomplished by tightening the front bar and loosening the 
rear bar. This should give you some understeer so that fast sweeping 
corners won't make the car easy to swap ends.

 There is a LOT more to this, What I mentioned here is a basic, 
thumbnail idea of what to change. Fred Pugn's book, "How to make your 
car handle" is an excellent guide to understanding this stuff written 
for the racer to understand and implement.
