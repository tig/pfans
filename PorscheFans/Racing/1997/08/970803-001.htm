My $.02-
I've been using a hand vacumm pump for bleeding for at least 10 years-works
great for me. I don't care about bubbles-I just get the pump resevoir about
half full of the old fluid from each corner. That's more than enough to purge
the lines. Of course, prior to that I have sucked the old fluid from the
master resevoir and added fresh fluid. I never have a problem with air or
moisture in the system, provided I change fluid often enough-at least before
each event. The pressure method works well also, but at trackside the vacumm
pump is easiest for me when I don't have a crew.
