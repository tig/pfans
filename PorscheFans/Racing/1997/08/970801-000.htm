Hi All,
   Rob made a good point.  We tend to hear alot of discussion 
about starting or cold tire pressures when the only thing that 
matters is hot or operating tire pressures.
   The bottom line is that there is no standard starting 
pressure.  You only care what the tire is doing when it is 
working at the level YOU need it to at any given moment.  
Ambient, track temperature, hangovers, etc. all affect how hot 
and thus hard your tires will be.  Obviously this situation 
changes day to day-morning to afternoon-run group to run group.
   I always try to have a dependable friend waiting in the pits 
with a tire gage.  Whenever I feel conditions changing, I come 
in as hot as I dare and let him/her bleed the tires down.  If 
they needed to be pumped up, I should probably park it anyway:)
   Forget cold temps.
