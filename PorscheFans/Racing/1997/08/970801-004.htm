Joshua Halder writes that he doesn't like the vacuum system (I don't
either - messy, uncertain if bubbles from system or just from around
bleeder), prefers the buddy system (convenient at track with lots of
buddies around and just a quick bleed needed, no set up or special
equipment needed), but has had bad as well as good experiences with
the air pressure bleeding system.

I'm all ears about the bad, Joshua.  I like this particularly for changing the
fluid in the whole system, or alone in garage.  Can't say as I've ever
noticed the obvious possible problems (water in the air gets into fluid, or
just the air used gets in) you'd think this method might have, but I'm
sometimes oblivious to problems, deny they have happened, etc.

For those who want to construct an air pressure bleeder system, see
Up-Fixin Volume V, page 46.  Even gives part numbers,  though any
method of closing the vent line will work, and you don't absolutely have
to have the 914 screw-on fittings - anything which screws onto a valve
stem and pokes the Schrader valve open so air can get into an attached
hose will do.  The author suggests using a tire with 37 psi in it as the
source of air pressure.  While using a tire is the genius of this system, I
think using less air pressure (the 25-30 which might normally reside in
the front tire nearest the reservoir is what I use, and if the tire were to
be low, it would work anyway.  No need to position the brake pedal.
