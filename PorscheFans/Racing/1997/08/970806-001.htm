My vote is for a set of track wheels and tires. The easiest tire
choice is BFG Comp T/A R1's; there are also DOT-legal Hoosiers.
DOT-legality is necessary if you are going to wear your tires
to the track, rather than put them on once you're there.
I can fit a set in the back of my car (I put them in
garbage bags, to keep things fairly clean), but I doubt
if you can. I feel more comfortable at higher (cornering,
hence also straight) speeds with the R1's than I ever did
with my street SP8000's.

Of course a little extra wheel width is nice too. 

The last thing to add is more power, assuming your engine is
running OK. More fun to get the most out of what you've got.
