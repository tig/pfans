The 1996 LeMans winner was built on a Jaguar XJR-14 suspension that had been
extensively run as a GTP car in the USA. The car was originally developed as
a World Sports Car (WSC) to compete at the Rolex 24 and the IMSA series, but
those idiots at IMSA banned turbocharged WSC's at the last minute, so it
never saw the light of day. The engine is a twin turbocharged 962 derivative.

The best article on the car that I have read, including detailed pictures of
the chasis and engine, appeared in Volume 6, Number 2 of Racecar Engineering
(1996), entitled "Dark Horse at LeMans: How a Jaguar Turned into a Porsche".

-Gerry Higgins
