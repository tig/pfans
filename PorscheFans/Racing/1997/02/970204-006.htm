While I agree that this seems stupid, consider that Porsche brought the
986 to market in a relatively short period of time with a new powerplant
that, to my knowledge, has not seen any service in a real racing 
environment.  The car has recently been launched and is receiving rave 
reviews with sales going strongly and the company is in the middle of a 
rather expensive commercial marketing push.  

Now envision said vehicle parked along the track with oil and 
*water* dripping from its underside in a cloud of smoke.  That's some
pretty expensive advertisement thrown right out the window not to
mention the potentially (likely) negative effect on sales of a product
that Porsche appears to be targeting to a broader demographic audience
than the 911 traditionally has.  My take is that Porsche is playing it
conservative since they would appear have much more to lose if the car 
fails than they stand to win if it does well.  

OTOH, I'm concerned about the potential that there may actually be 
a lack of confidence in the Boxter's ability to stand up to the 
abuse the 911 has always been known for...
