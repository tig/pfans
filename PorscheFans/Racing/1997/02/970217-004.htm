"Jay asked about setting yellow Konis"

If I remember, the rears come set about half way from the factory.  Unless you
really enjoy changing them, start at half way and see how they are.  All the way
soft is no good and hard will shake out your teeth.  You can start with the
fronts at soft and work your way up.  Crank them all the way hard to knock that
last tooth out.  Actually, even in the firm position, they still ride pretty
good.  When I switched it was like night and day, I will never run anything else
on the street.  Have fun, you will think you are driving a new car.
