>That's a good way to kill yourself. Running the straps that high take ALL
the support out of them, and in a front impact, you'll end up in the window.
>
>Try fabricating a bar that clamps to your roll bar at the correct level.
Also, do you have a seat back brace? how do you keep the shoulder belts on
your shoulders? For the $300-$500 investment in a good race seat, you gain a
LOT of safety.
>
>Are you planning on racing in PCA?  If so, you also need an anti-submarine
(or crotch) belt.
>
>For what it's worth, my advise is as follows:
>
>Get a racing seat, get a GOOD cage. Safety devices (solt through OG racing)
and Kelly Moss have excellent cages that bolt to the FRAME, not the floor.
Autopower is NOT a good cage. I have seen them tear through the floor and
colapse the roof in a rollover.
>
>Good 6 point harness, just like the PCA and SCCA rulebooks talk about.
>
>Window net that attaches to the cage, not the door (what happens to your
protection if the door opens?)
>
>Good helmet and suit.
>
>
>Everything else (go fast stuff) is secondary to survival. Speed will come
with experiance. 
>
>Sorry to preach, but I have seen a lot of people ignore this to their
detriment, and nothing is sadder than a driver (or a drivers family) in that
driver is killed or injured because he skimped on safety equipment.
>
