14 March, 1997
SEBRING, Fla. (March 14, 1997) -- Professional SportsCar Racing, Inc. today
announced a highly-successful and diverse board of directors to guide the
SportsCar racing sanctioning body as it advances world-class sports car
racing to the next level. The announcement was made by Jack Long, CEO of both
Professional SportsCar Racing, Inc. and its parent company the International
Motorsports Speedway Group (IMSG). The new board is comprised of Mr. Long,
Linda Clifford, general manager of Pikes Peak International Raceway; Daniel
M. Doyle, founder and president of Danka Industries, Inc.; Andrew Evans
non-executive chairman of the Professional SportsCar Racing board and founder
and president of Dominion Income Management Corp.; Ronnie Lott, four-time
Super Bowl champion and an analyst on FOX NFL Sunday; Charles Morgan,
founder, president and CEO of Acxiom Corporation; Craig T. Nelson,
producer/director and star of the ABC
television hit "Coach," and Steve Saleen, founder and president of Saleen
Performance. "Each member of this board has risen to the top of their
profession and are now committed to helping SportsCar racing realize its
potential," said Long.
"We have gathered some of the best business minds in the country," said
Evans. Each individual board member brings their own particular expertise
that will provide sound leadership as SportsCar develops into a successful,
world-class series of championships." Professional SportsCar Racing, Inc.,
the new name for the International Motor Sports Association, is the premier
sports car racing sanctioning body in North America. The sanctioning body
organizes five different championship series: the Exxon World SportsCar
Championship, Exxon Supreme GT Series presented by Virgin Interactive,
Endurance Championship for stock sports cars, Black Magic Pro Series for
World Sports Racers and the Ferrari Challenge.
