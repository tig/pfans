14 March, 1997
SEBRING, Fla. (March 14, 1997) -- After the entrants meeting this past
Tuesday and and
subsequent discussions, Professional SportsCar Racing, Inc. (SportsCar)
believes rules
stability is critical to future success. Therefore, there will be no
introduction of turbos in the
Exxon World SportsCar Championship in 1997. The potential of an invitational
will be
discussed with our World SportsCarSM entrants in the next three months. A
final decision on
such a program will be made by the First Union Six Hours of the Glen, June 1.
Furthermore, our participation in the new Sports Racer FIA Commission will be
important in
shaping the future of World SportsCar racing in North America.
"We have an excellent formula in our Exxon World SportsCar Championship. The
closest
racing ever, and I am certain the FIA Sports Racer Commission will take a
very close look at
our formula and our success." stated Andrew Evans, chairman of Professional
SportsCar
Racing.
Jack Long, CEO of SportsCar, added, "Our number one priority must be the core
contingent
in our Exxon World SportsCar Championship with rules stability for all
entrants and
manufacturers."
