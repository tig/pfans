>  1.  At present I have SSI heat exchanger exhausts that connect to a dual
>  inlet/single outlet 1974 muffler on the car.  When it was originally
>  installed, I could feel a substanial change in the cars perfromance.
>   Recently, I visited a PCA Club race and looked over other cars in my
future
>  class.  I noticed that each car was using headers and megafones as their
>  exhaust system.  My questions are:  Do I need to change to headers to be
>  competetive and will I really notice a difference from my currnet system?
>   Also, would the headers fit my 74 muffler?

You will lose some weight with headers right where you need it.
But the lap times will not suffer much - the SSI's flow very well
and are 5 pounds lighter than the stock early heat exchangers
on each side. Much lighter than stock SC setup.
Some headers are available with extensions to fit a '74 muffler.
I'd say keep the SSI's until you are driving to your best potential
and see how your car stacks up against others.
  
>  2.  At present I have 9x16 Fuchs on the rear of the car with 245/45/16

still
>  use the 245/45/16 tires or will I need to use some other size to meet the
3%
>  rolling radius rule?  Does anyone have a chart relating to wheel size,
tires
>  size and rolling radius?  Stock rear wheels and tires on my car are
225/50/
> 16 on 7x16 wheels.

As I read the rules you can use 9" on the rear, as factory were 8x15
optional and you are allowed 1" wider than stock.
And those tires should be OK for the rolling radius
but I don't have a chart.
