In defense of Jerry, (whose last name I don't even know but whom I have
traded numerous correspondences in private lately concerning the present
state of PCA club racing solely at my behest) I must state that I am
surprised that you find you must attack him after he freely contributed
his time to help in the running of the Pocono event. From what I
understand he was the T&S spotter on the roof of T&S while the race in
question occurred. You may disagree with his opinions, but you surely
must acknowledge his contributions to the event. As a former racer who
had great vantage I would think his observations should be held in great
esteem.

Regarding the state of club racing from my perspective (as a very
interested observer who is keenly interested in participating), I find
that I am somewhat discouraged by the difference between perception and
reality. On one hand you have vintage racing rules used ostentiably to
minimize contact and the lack of full safety equipment that is required
for racing by other organizations. On the other hand you have the
history of the last four races involving dozens of bent cars and 3 of
the last 4 events involving life threatening mishaps. 

I am coming to the conclusion that PCA club racing is just that. It has
lost whatever vintage roots with which it may have started and it is now
in the realm of wheel-to-wheel racing replete with big sponsors and
enclosed trailers. But there is nothing inherently wrong with this. As
an SCCA member I am fully aware of the current dilemna in club racing
that has allowed numerous groups to directly compete. However, it is
incumbent upon the organizers to properly assess the current situation
and respond by requiring the necessary level of safety equipment, e.g.
roll cages and 3-layer drivers suits (or 2 layer with Nomex underwear).

I would also suggest that the PCA closely examine the checks and
balances that the SCCA has evolved over decades to ensure that all
aspects of the racing program are functioning properly. As a start I
would recommend adopting the GCR.
