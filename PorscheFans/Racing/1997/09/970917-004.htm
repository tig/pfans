David Ferguson wrote:
> 
> Since Walt started this, here is the letter I sent to Harry Hall
> regarding the proposed rules changes:
> 
> Harry,
> 
> I would like to provide my feedback on the proposed PCA Club Racing rules
> changes for 1998.  I have only commented on rules in which I have a
> strong opinion.  Other proposals would be acceptable either way the rules
> committee decides.
> 
> GT Class Proposals
> 
> 1. Minimum weights -- I hope that any minimum weights we would impose for
> the GT classes include the driver weight.  I am, btw, a relative
> lightweight, but I think this is the fairest way to do any minimum
> weights.
> 
> Since I imagine there are a significant number of cars that will not
> currently meet these requirements, I would propose that there be a method
> that allows them to complete.  Specifically why don't you let cars that
> weight up to 200 lbs less than their class minimum race in the next
> higher class.   Absolute minimum weight of any car should be limited to
> 1400lbs.
> 
> I feel that the GT-3 and GT-4 weights are all too high (particularly if
> these are weights without the driver).  My reasoning is that a legal SCCA
> car (SCCA classes GT2 or GT3, prepared to much more restrictive
> tire/engine rules) would be far below these limits.  For example an SCCA
> legal GT2 (PCA GT3R) 914-6 or 911 can weigh 2030 with driver, the rules
> are already handicapping these cars by forcing them into the bottom of a
> class with larger displacement engines, unlimited tires, unlimited
> induction, significant aerodynamic advantages, but to also force them to
> add weight seems unfair.  I understand the cars can be changed to take
> advantage of PCA rules, but we should try to make it inviting (and
> competitive) for these cars to run with us.
> 
> I think a similar survey of PSCR rules should done for cars in GT1R and
> GT2R.  There is no sense in providing a barrier for people to bring cars
> out in the modified classes.
> 
> I would propose the following weight limits including driver (I've only
> listed classes I know something about):
> 
> GT-1   2400 lbs
> GT-2   2200 lbs
> GT-3   2050 lbs
> GT-4   1950 lbs
> 
> 2. Definition of "Porsche" and bodywork rule for GT classes.
> 
> Once again please consider a place for all the existing cars that might
> not meet these rules.  Make them run in GT-1, or create a new "unlimited"
> class, but don't add barriers that prevents people with cool cars (that
> existed long before there was club racing or these rules) from joining in
> the fun.  Club Racing should have a place for everyone.  There are a lot
> of cars with cut-up tubs that meet the rules of other sanctioning bodies
> but would not legally meet the "all structure from front to rear shock
> tower" must be present.
> 
> 4. Require metal valve stems (and caps).
> 
> I think this rule is unnecessary (and yes, I've had a non-reinforced
> rubber valve stem break at Heartland Park -- twice in one weekend).  The
> solution has already been provided by Porsche, either aluminum stem
> support (as on late 911 Fuchs) or wire supports around the top of the
> stem.  With these in place I have experienced no further problems at
> Heartland Park.  This is a good example of a rules that should be an
> advisory to people preparing cars, but not a rule to prevent someone from
> running after they've driven hundreds of miles and can't get metal valve
> stems in Lancaster, CA on a Saturday.
> 
> 
> Thanks for considering my opinions.
> 
> David Ferguson
> GT-3R 914-6 (SCCA legal GT-2).
