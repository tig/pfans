A

Here on the Leftcoast, the number of GT cars/race has been as great or
greater 
than the stock classes.  Many people in my region that time trial are 
intimidated by Club Racing, even though they may have dedicated track cars. =
 
They're intimidated by the perception that racing is much riskier than time 
trials.  They've been told that Club Racing is well run and as safe as
racing 
can be, vintage rules, 13/13, and you can run your street car etc.  Then
they 
go to an event as a spectator and see rubble and that perception is 
reinforced, so they decide to pass on joining the program and the program 
doesn't grow with additional participation..........this is the concern I 
have..... 
 
I think the volunteers who run our program are doing an excellent job of 
moving things along safely, given the tasks at hand.  Furthurmore, I think 
it's up to the participants to take more responsibility for the program and 
it's survival.   Yes, stuff happens on the race track.  But that's not an 
excuse for not policing, improving and enforcing the rules.  It's our
program 
and we should realize that when we make a bonehead move and crunch up a car,=
 
we're not just putting our own equipment at risk but putting the growth of
the 
program on the line.
