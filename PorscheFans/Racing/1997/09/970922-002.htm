Dear Bruce:
I am big on RSR front struts as they are already reinforced and don't crack
like the stock ones often eventually do.  However, the RSR inserts and rear
shock valving I don't like because they are based on having coil-overs. I'll
let you in on a secret -  the rebound is far too light! My secret will remain
how much rebound is right but I guarantee you that noone would believe me if
I told you anyway. But we've dynoed them and roadrace tested them and have it
right on the money!!  I don't know how anyone can get enough negative camber
(BFG R1's not considered in this discussion) without moving the front spindle
unless you totally modify the towers...  I personally moved up from 22mm to
23mm front and it really helped on the really high speed corners like we have
at Willow Springs (Brainerd too perhaps?). By the way does Paul Olson still
run with you in MN? If so please send him my best.
