I believe the race in question was the E Stock/F Stock race where a car
spun at the end of the back straight( which is the best passing corner)
and sat there without restarting. As time went on people started
ignoring the yellow at that corner. 

Since the race is limited to 10 laps they were in a bit of a "damned if
you do/ damned if you don't" situation. In a 'real' race, they would
have dispatched the pace car and towed the car away since you can't race
if you can't pass, but this sort of thing happens all the time in
vintage racing where they don't clear the track - they give the entrants
their lap time instead.

The turn was a long way from the viewing area where I was and I haven't
driven the track so it is hard for me to judge how much potential impact
the car actually had on safety (perhaps Barry Lenoble or someone else in
atendance can chime in...), but it was clearly near the track in what
seemed like a a possibly dangerous position. 

This is obviously still no excuse for passing under yellow. I am a bit
disappointed the administrators let this one slide, particularly
considering the recent spate of bent cars and hurt drivers.

I think the PCA has to decide if club racing is supposed to be more like
SCCA road racing or more like vintage racing. In either case the flags
cannot be ignored.
