I've noticed some comments on the finer edges of prep on 911's running in the
stock/prepared classes of club racing, i.e. spindle height, gusseting.  In
other words, replicating (and improving on) what the factory started doing
20-odd years ago on the race cars.

If I'm missing the boat here, and an RSR strut is considered a "stock" part,
then I guess I need to be clued in.  If not, I come up with only the
following for guidance in the (1996) club racing rulebook.  Or did something
dramatically change for '97?

General rules #4--"Any modification not specifically listed is not allowed."

Stock #2. Suspension-- "A. Suspension pick-up points must remain as stock in
location and type, and C. Any suspension setting not requiring machining or
modification of factory parts is allowed."

Prepared #2. Supension--"Slotting to achieve suspension settings. Pick-up
points cannot be welded or machined."

Notice I'm only talking about this in terms of PCA club racing, not local
rules which may specifically allow these modifications in "street" classes.

Comments?
