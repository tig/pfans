Monte,

I think I answered this or a virtually identical query before shutting
down for the holidays.  Just in case, and for your info as well, here's
the answer.

You can remove the cross piece and run stock, since you would no longer
have a wing (leading and trailing edge).  You can use any rear spoiler
(which will have a trailing edge only) stock or non-stock in configuration
and of any material at all, as long as you don't violate the size
restrictions which have been in the rule book since the beginning, or the
"aesthetically pleasing" provision that applies to anything you do to your
car.  Spoilers have always been free, it is only rear wings that are being
made an "improved" change for 97 and perhaps illegal in any stock class as
of 98 (and that latter is largely up to you racers and what we hear from
you at this point).

> Keeping in mind that I am writing this as a PCA Club racer and NOT with any
> authority from PCA national (stewards do not have anything to do with rule
> changes, that's up to the scrutineers), here are my intuitive responses:
> 
> (1) - As you know, your wing may be run (this year anyway) in stock class.
>  It just means that if you run it, you move up a class.  If it were me, I'd
> move up and run it as those wings, when properly adjusted, allow you to go
> much faster.  On an AIR RSR wing, one adjustment, in testing, made a three
> (3) second difference at Willow Springs.
> 
> (2) - If you remove the wing cross member, you still do not have the stock
> configuration.  Further, I doubt very much that you would have anything like
> the down force from your stock tail and would be at a distinct disadvantage.
>  When I was racing my '87 Carrera we had cameras mounted forward and backward
> at one time and you could see the rubber lip on the tail working . . .
> fascinating footage.  At the same time, as spoilers are free you might be
> able to remove the wing and run stock if the objective is to run stock.  If
> the objective is to run stock and go fast, I wouldn't take that option.
> 
> (3) - I suppose the reason for the question on fiber glass is because you can
> get a stock configuration in carbon fiber or fiber glass for a fraction of
> what it will cost to re-purchase a stock spoiler.  Do not quote me, but IF
> the configuration is the same AND you make weight for your class, I doubt if
> anyone is going to care.
