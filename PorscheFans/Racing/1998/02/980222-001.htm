You can't go wrong with Performance Friction which are, by far, the most
popular. They are all pretty good so this is not a particular endorsement for
PF's. Don't worry about things like "they are rough on rotors" with any of the
pads since a set of pads costs more than rotors anyway and something has to
go.
PF have a bunch of different compounds so get the advice of your local
supplier/guru or even call PF and tell them what you are planning to do and
they will steer you in the right direction. 
A good source for these pads and advice is OG Racing. I believe he (Bill Love)
also stocks other brands. Call the 800 operator for the number. Or look for
his ad in Pano or Excellence.
