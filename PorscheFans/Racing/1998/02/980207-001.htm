Hello P-Fans & Dana
	All of the "fixes" you mentioned will work. Like the old saying goes: You
wanna race? How fast do yaa wanna go? How much can yaa afford?  Speed=Money.

	I believe the '44 crankshaft is similar in design (one half and all) to the
928. On the 928 at high rpms the oil flow reverses above, say 5500 rpms. The
onlt way to solve this is redesign the oil flow inside of the crankshaft! I
think DEVEK has done this and others.
	Also the 944 cylinder head has a probem draining oil back to the oil
sump/pan. At sustained high rpms, oil get pumped and cannot return fast enough
to the oil pan/sump. I vagley remember a raceshop in Detroit durring the 951
Escort Series that would drill "a" hole in the cylinderhead "somewhere" so
that oil would return faster to the oil/pan. This shop charge something like
$3K to do this simply modifcation!!! 
	I think that a Accusump and changing your rod bearings ever winter is the
safest way to go.
