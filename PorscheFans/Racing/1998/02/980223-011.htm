Notice that they change the wedge in only one corner of the car. Also
note that NASCAR racers use springs as stiff as 2000 lb/inch. Finally,
consider that they normally turn counter-clockwise only (the cars,
that is, not the wedge-changers). What hapens when you change the 
wedge is that you change the 
proportion of weight carried by the two
diagonals, and since the cars always turn in the same direction,
the effect is to change the front-rear balance. I don't think
you'll see crews changing wedge on road courses, where they turn
both ways.
