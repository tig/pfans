   You see Mike, this is the common myth that most people believe; *tread design and depth is the most important factor in the rain*. The problem with this theory is that it does not take into account the durometer (softness/hardness/stickiness) of the given tire. What people don't realize is that a tire can still be *warmed up* even in wet conditions. By bringing it up to the warmest temp possible it will provide much more stick than a street tire like the Z50 or the S02. I have experimented in back to back tests with both Corvettes AND Porsches and the results are quite conclusive. The soft tire given enough tread (4 to 6/32) is always faster if given a chance to heat up a little. BTW, I am from Seattle and these tests were done at Portland International Raceway where it rains once in a while. Did you happen to catch the GI Joes 200 Cart PPG race last *JUNE*?? ;-)

   If your *tread is what matters* theory is true, then why would the *pro* racers *always* use rain grooved super soft rain compound slicks? It is the compound that matters the most, that's why ;-)
