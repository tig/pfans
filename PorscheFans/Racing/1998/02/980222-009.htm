Paul:

 Cryogenically treated rotors are less likely to wear out or crack. The
surfce becomes harder and more heat tolerant. All drilled rotors can
crack if heat stressed enough. There are many types of "grey cast iron"
and they all react differently to this treatment.

Generally speaking, the rotors last much longer, even when using some
aggressive brake pads and they don't seem to crack anywhere nearly as
quickly.
The caveat here is: "nearly". You can overstress an 11" or 12" rotor
much easier than a 13" or 14" one depending upon calipers, pads, and
cooling design.

FWIW, we have been happy with the results especially when you are forced
to use a certain rotor size.
