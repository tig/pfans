Josh:

  The cylinder head temp instrument is a capital idea :^) This is an
excellent tuning aid especially for air-cooled engines. Clinder head
temps change faster with load and mixture than exhaust gas temps do.
EGT's tend to lag slightly making one be quite careful about mixture
tuning by EGT, alone.

Cylinder head temps can range widely depending upon grade, throttle
position, mixture and whether or not you lug the engine any!!

Expect a range of 300deg to 450deg, except when coasting. That will
lower them even more. Anything over 450 can cause the steel valve seats
to drop away from the aluminum head. Valves can also gall and/or seize
in the guides as well. If the valve does momentarily stick in the guide
when open and the pistons are at Top Dead Center...................

Quite expensive, really.

Neat diagnostic tool for preventing disaster in engines that are
stressed. Turbo engines should all have them  :^)

Just our 2 cents worth.............
