VeryMobile wrote:

> 1)  Who has the best bushing hard plastic bushings?  I am probably going to
> use Weltmeister unless I get some better suggestions.
>

Weltmiester is pretty much the standard. There are a few companies that offer
machined delrin bushings. The machined bushings repotedly fit alot better than
the molded poly bushings. I don't remember the exact rpices, but I seem to recall
that they weren't too much more than the welt stuff.

> 2)  The lightweight flywheels I see advertised are $350.  Is that a fair price
> or should I be talking to a VW machine shop for a more affordable price.
>

Hmm, depends on the manufacturer. The racemaster stuff is pretty high quality
from what I hear, but they make very light flywheels and I've heard that you
don't want too light of a flywheel. I havn't experimented yet, but by research
has shown me that 10lbs is really the bottom limit.

> 3) Josh mentioned that 48mm throttle bodys are available.  Any contact names?
>

Try Rich Bontempi at High Performance House in Redwood City. HPH is not a cheap
place, but Rich and Larry are probably two of the most informed 914 guys that I
have ever talked to.

> 4) Any recommendations for body shops in the bay area that can "bulge" my rear
> stock fenders?  I can go to 7.5 inch rims (225/50 Hoosiers) for Club Racing
> (class I).  I talked to Body Style and they indicated about $500-$600 per side
> painted.  Ouch.
>

$500-$600 per side is pretty much the best pricing I've found. Unless you feel
like just doing the baseball bat technique, expect that to be the general going
price.
