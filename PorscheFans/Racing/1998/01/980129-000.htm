No. No. I don't think anyone said same "grip". Just same size patch in a
different shape. One could find an underlying message in the thread that the
shape of the patch _can_ make a difference in the grip, handling, lap times,
etc.. This is probably what most of us have experienced and why we use them.
With wider tires the patch shape reduces fore and aft and increases side to
side thus should handle lateral loads better and give better handling. BTW
taller tires vs shorter tires can affect lap times due to the gearing change. 

As for Keith's gas mileage question I would hazard a guess that drag created
by the greater frontal area of a wider tire and its additional weight, might
adversely affect mileage but only a small amount. I don't know about the
different shape patch affecting mileage.
