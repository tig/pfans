P924S wrote:

> No. No. I don't think anyone said same "grip". Just same size patch in a
> different shape. One could find an underlying message in the thread that the
> shape of the patch _can_ make a difference in the grip, handling, lap times,
> etc.. This is probably what most of us have experienced and why we use them.
> With wider tires the patch shape reduces fore and aft and increases side to
> side thus should handle lateral loads better and give better handling.

Sorry I havn't pitched back into this thread, work has been pretty busy this
week. I guess I was omitting the fact that in our "all things being equal"
format, the tire pressures were included in that. Yes, a narrow tire at x psi,
will deform more than a wider tire at the same pressure. Because the interior
volume increases with the wider tire, the overall air pressure holding up the
car will be greater. I guess I omitted that because for me I always considered
lower pressures when the tire width increases.

> BTW
> taller tires vs shorter tires can affect lap times due to the gearing change.

Yup. But depends on the track. Smaller tighter courses will probably reward
lower gearing more.

> As for Keith's gas mileage question I would hazard a guess that drag created
> by the greater frontal area of a wider tire and its additional weight, might
> adversely affect mileage but only a small amount. I don't know about the
> different shape patch affecting mileage.

I do know that alot of SSB racers have claimed faster lap times at open tracks
(like stapelton) with 185/60-14 tires than with the 205/55-14 tires of the same
manufacturer. Pretty much the same gearing, but less drag and, more to the
point, less rolling resistance.
