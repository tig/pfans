> Looking for opinions.  For 3 or 4 DE events a year, is it worth upgrading
> to high performance brake pads (e.g., Pagid, Hawke) over stock?
> Considerations balancing performance would be cost, wear (pad and rotors)
> and squeaking.  Car is daily driver with moderate suspension mods (22 adj.
> sways; 22/28 torsion), but otherwise stock.  TIA.

Scott, 

	In my opinion, the best way to go is to buy a set of race pads, and
change them at the track. Race pads will not be as friendly to your
rotors, and will dust up alot more than a street pad. But a street
compound pad can get boiled away in very short order at the track.
Changing pads is not a big deal, and not only will it help your rotors
last longer, but could very likely save you at the track.
