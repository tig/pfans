The earlier cars used DIN ratings. A '72 S rates at 190 DIN HP which =
would be
in the 205 - 210 SAE HP. I have seen that Porsche's ratings were =
generally
conservative and a lot of the engines actually would be well over the =
advertised value.
Unlike certain companies (GM, Ford) who would advertise the most =
optimistic value
for a blueprinted engine without any accessories then sell very =
out-of-spec but within tolerance engines with much less HP, more weight, =
AC, etc..., Porsche actually shipped engines that were at or close to =
blueprinted specs. In modern terms they were using
Taguchi's Parameter design and optimization to eliminate noise or =
variations in the=20
manufacturing process. I think at the time they were just being good =
craftsmen.

I'm not sure when (or if) Porsche changed the ratings.=20

I think the ratings are even more off on the

comparable and help the other inbalance in the rules which=20

more comparable.
