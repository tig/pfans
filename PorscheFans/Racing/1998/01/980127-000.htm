The tire patch debate is a good one, and I believe Forrest and "R.L."
are correct.  Although it's sometimes difficult to visualize, tire width
has no affect on contact patch size.  This sounds counter-intuitive; but
it's true.  It's not the tire that's holding the car up.  It's the air
pressure.  Sidewall stiffness can have an affect (run flat tires are the
extreme); however, the debate was centered around different sized tires
of similar construction.

The calculation  "psi / load = contact patch" holds true no matter what
size tire you run.  If you want to change tire patch size, you need to
change pressure.
