I think this thread has a few more emails left before it gets stale.

Remember that I recommended R tires for an autocrosser who appeared to
be interested in continuing in the sport, not for a newbie DE driver. 

But I really have to take exception to your comment that R tires
contribute to newbies getting in over their heads at DE events. I think
_any_ Porsche at a DE event in the hands of someone who has never
autocrossed or taken a car control clinic is an accident waiting to
happen. The instructors who get into 930s with newbies have to be
congratulated for their bravery (or stupitidy). But a friend of mine
will never do another DE because of the incident that happened to him
while instructing in a 356.

To me the answer to lessening the number of stupid incidents is simple
(and one that you hinted at in your message): Require everybody to
become proficient at autocrossing before you let them onto the track. By
proficient I mean being able to get within 4 seconds or so of an
accomplished autocrosser using similar equipment on street tires or
within 2 seconds using R tires. I can't stress how much it means to
learn car control in an open lot at slower speeds with nothing but cones
to hit _before_ you get to the track.

I have been autocrossing for 11 years and running DE for 2 years. All my
DEs have been on R tires and I have NEVER gone off the track. Having the
sense to switch from 11/10ths while autocrossing to 9/10ths at the track
is part of the reason, but the other is that I knew how to recover from
a spin before I needed to use that skill to save my car and my life. 

I would have been royally pissed if someone had told met that I couldn't
do DE on R tires when I was a newbie because R tires are much more
predictable and hence safer at the limits of adhesion.
