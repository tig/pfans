Chris Brown wrote:
> 
> Folks -
> Get real.  We have had seatback failures in Drivers Ed and PCA Racing.
> These failures have occurred with factory seats (86 911) and full race
> seats (Recaro SPG).  Both failures I personally witnessed involved heavy
> rear impacts, one into a guardrail at Watkins Glen and one into the wall
> of NASCAR 1 at Pocono.  Both impacts could have been better
> handled/survived with a rear seat brace.  In the case of the stock
> seats, the seatbacks for driver and passenger(!) both bent back to 45
> degree angles.  The Recaro seat was ripped from its mountings and
> allowed the driver to move about the cabin and strike body parts on the
> interior. This only makes common sense - a lever arm (the seat) fixed at
> only one point (the floor) and having a large moment applied to the
> other end of the arm (top of the seatback) will experience a very large
> bending force.  If you can provide another attachment point halfway (or
> higher) up the lever arm you dramatically reduce the bending force.
>  While we can fault PCA Racing rules makers for providing no spec for
> achieving this requirement, we cannot fault them for requiring it.
> IMHO, we have been extremely lucky that incidents like Mike Keller's
> have not happened to more people.  Lets step up and face the music -
> when you play in life threatening sports, you better take some real
> precautions.

Which makes it all the more puzzling why PCA wouldn't prescribe approved
and non-approved installations at the same time they legislated the
requirement!
