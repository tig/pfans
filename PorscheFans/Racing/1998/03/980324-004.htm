Jeff,

> How does one determine what needs to be padded?  I've heard that
> padding
> should be added to the roll cage anywhere your helmet could come in
> contact.
> Is this after allowing for seat belt stretch, cage deformation, etc?
> 
I padded the two top side bars from A pillar to B pillar and the bar
between the A pillars. I originally padded from that cross brace down
the A pillar bar for another foot and 1/2 but it cut off two much 
vision while looking at corners and I removed it.

> I'm finding that by padding the upper cross brace which spans
> A-pillars, I
> loose about 1/2 of my rear view through the windshield mounted mirror.
> I'm
> wondering if I compromise safety by cutting out that section of
> padding.
> 
I padded my 951 on that bar and I can see fine. But I also used the
dense celled foam that comes in half circles and its might be a lot
less in diameter. You might want to try that. One piece covers that
bar and its only about $5 from Racers Warehouse or Pegasus.

> Also, any suggestions on fastening the padding to the cage?
> 
I used a combination of 3M Trim Adhesive - also known as gorilla
snot :) - and tyraps.
