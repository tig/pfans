Howdy,

Well the event didn't go real well but at least I made it home OK...

Finally attended my first event in the new Carrera after being snowed
out twice. At the beginning of my 2nd session, at 110+ MPH the throttle
cut out just past the start/finish line.
I pulled off at station 3 and noticed there was no throttle resistance.
I reached in the engine to re-connect the throttle rod but it was 
nowhere to be found.

On the lunch break 6 people searched for nearly an hour all around
big bend where it must have fallen out. (Note to 911 owners :
this rod is a lousy design which allows the rod to fall out of the car
if the ball pops off! Adjust your pedal stop correctly and maybe modify
the rod with some sort of keeper!).

We never found the 2 foot long throttle rod, it blows my mind to think
how far it must have flown at those speeds. I just paid $2500
to have this engine professionally installed (and my old engine installed
in the donor car) to add insult to injury...

I rigged up a wire to attach the throttle to the engine which worked
well enough to get home. The car ran and handled great in the session that I
ran.
Hope to be ready for Lime Rock again next Saturday with the PCA.

Many thanks to Pat Salerno, Bruce Bellom, Ken Porteus, (sorry about
spellings!), the VW guy, and others who helped try to find the rod.
Now I have to buy one, I can use the unclaimed $100 reward money.
