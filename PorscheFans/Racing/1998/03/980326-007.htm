Again, I'm sorry to hear that, and as I wasn't there, I can't comment.
I do know that I've run a LOT of events with Metro over the years,
as well as events with a number of other regions (Conn Valley, Reisentoter,
Hudson Valley). They all seem pretty much the same to me. It's difficult
when you run with a new group, as they don't know you and you don't
know them. I remember the first event I ran with CVR. I had just received
my turbo S, and my instructor was a little nervous about riding with me.
The first session, he wanted me to brake BEFORE the first braking marker.
After a few laps, I realized I could brake much later than that, but I
didn't want to scare him. After the first session I explained that I 
could feel what the car was doing, and that I was firmly and comfortably
in control. I told him that I was going to start driving harder, and that
if I or he felt the car starting to reach the limits, we'd communicate
that to the other. It worked much better that way. I don't blame the
nervous instructor on CVR, it's just that he never met me before, and
didn't know anything about my ability.

Just be aware of what you're getting into. Your insurance is most likely
voided at any event featuring "Timed Runs." It doesn't necessarily make
the event any more dangerous, just that if something bad happens, like you
blow a hose, slip in your own coolant and smack the wall at a timed event,
it's your nickel. If the same thing happened at a straight driver ed 
event, your insurance would most likely cover you.

In any event, the purpose of driver education is not to run for speed,
but to be able to drive your car the way it was meant to be driven. If you're
"an aggressive driver on the edge" please do yourself and us a favor, and
stay away from future Metro events. That's not the kind of driver we want
at our events.
