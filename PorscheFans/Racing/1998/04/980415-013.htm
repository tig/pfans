If purchased in the fall, you must have the R3S02 tires which I run 42 psi
front and 44 psi rear (hot off the track). Depending on your suspension they
can also handle lots of negative camber. The new R3S03 tires have a very
different construction so that they can be run at much lower pressures (~10
psi lower) reportedly because too much of the "DOT tire racing community"
(i.e. BFG R-1 types) couldn't handle the idea of running such high pressures
so were continually running too low a pressure subsequently destroying the
tires prematurely and blaming it on Hoosier. Hoosier evidently got fed up and
changed.  I expect the stiffer belt construction will make the Hoosiers hold
up better in high acceleration and deceleration forces (as in the heavier and
hi-torque cars) which many of us believe was a weakness. We'll see how well
the new ones do and what pressures actually end up being the best for our
individual applications. I hope to get some feedback ASAP as I start using the
new ones next week at Phoenix Int'l Raceway.
