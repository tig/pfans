A recent thread mentions Split Fire plugs.  A few years ago I heard a couple
of horror stories about these plugs (from two separate sources).  Both stories
included the electrode (or piece thereof) breaking off and falling down into
the cylinder.  At least with one story (a 930) the scarring of the cylinder
required a tear-down and replacement of the liner.  I can't imagine a risk
like this is worth the questionable performance increase of these plugs.
