> I found the oversteer.  I went to an AX yesterday; new place.  It was
> a huge lot, about a 1min course (no double loops) and with several
> elevation changes.  On my second run I was coming up a hill into about
> a 60 degree box that led to a U-turn right before the finish.  There
> was about a 50-60 yard straight into the box and another 75-100 yard
> straight aproaching the U-turn at the finish.  Also, the first gate of
> the U-turn forced you to tighten your line (effectively a sweeper with
> a decreasing radius) after the box.  I came through the box probably
> at 50-55mph with the tail stepping out slightly, as I tightened up
> slightly for the U-turn (figuring I had enough grip left at the rear)
> the tail started to come around even more.  Uh-oh.  Time to correct.  
> 
> (Mind you, all my AX experience is an Integra GSR.  When the tail
> stepped out, a quick flick of the wheel brought it back in line no
> problem.  I soon learned that my experience in a FWD/front-engined car
> was not relevant.)
> 
> It is so much easier to follow your gut reaction than your brain's
> reaction.  At 60mph, I didn't even talk to my brain.  I immediately
> let off.  (OOOPPPPS).  Holy *&@$%!!!  I caught the spin with a quick
> reaction at the wheel.  However, rather than stepping back in line,
> the rear end just hung out like a cool surfer dude with his toes over
> the edge.  I proceeded to slide sideways for about 50 yards; smoke
> everywhere (tire fortunately). I ended up about 135 degrees off the
> direction I had intended to follow.  Well it was fun, nobody got hurt
