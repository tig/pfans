Rick- I recently faced the same decision on my '85 911. It had 6&7 x 15
standard Fuchs alloys (polished). I replaced them with 7.5 & 9 x 17 Mille
Miglia 993 cup replica alloys, Dunlop 205/50-17 and 255/40-17 SP8000 tires.
I haven't driven the car in the rain yet to comment on their wet performance.

Will this hurt resale? Depends on the buyer....if they are looking for
everything original, then of course it will. If they are looking for a
"newer" look, then you're ok. Gee, what insight I have. Of course you need
to factor in your improved satisfaction with the car while you still have it!

I would guess that your alloys are worth $800-1000 for the 4. Mine are a
bit smaller and I'm trying to sell them locally for $795, which includes
some D40M2 tires with 30% tread. If you sell them now and a later buyer
wants original Fuchs, they are readily available on the used market.

Prices? I paid $2125 total for the wheels/tires/center caps/silver lug
nuts/mount & balance/shipping. Got them from Robert Wood at Wheel
Enhancement. Super nice guy...knows his stuff. Good prices. Answered all
the questions I had and was genuinely interested in making sure I got the
right stuff. *Maybe* I could have saved a few dollars at one of the
discount houses and maybe not. In any event I'll use Wheel Enhancement again.
