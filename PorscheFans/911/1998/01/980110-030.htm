I wasn't sure I understood the gist of your question, but if you want to know
how the  interior light wiring is routed in the headliner/roof, sorry I can't
help. I had the headliner out a few years ago but I didn't notice or I forgot.

If you're wondering how the interior lights get power; they get power from the
glove box light switch assembly. If only one light goes ON, it might be just a
bad bulb (try switching bulbs). If neither interior light goes ON, first check
the fuse box. Also check the glove box light switch and make sure the wires
are connected. They must be connected correctly so the interior light circuit
receives power. If you have a factory manual, you can follow the wires in the
electrical schematic. This is really an important reason to have the factory
manual as other publications reproduce the schematic in B&W (Zoowee, try
following the circuits and seeing what wire is which!).

RE: 911 Brake Fluid Leak

Sounds like the rear seal in the master cylinder is leaking. If you let this
go too long, the pedal cluster bushings (plastic) will start to disintegrate,
then you'll have to replace them.

You can try rebuilding the master cylinder. A kit should run you about $20. A
new master cylinder is about $100.

Hi:  This is my first attempt at posting a response so here we go. 

In response to Scott Galaba's question about what heat codes to use for plugs
on his '87 carrera(I have one too)-Everyone I speak to recommends to get the
colder plug unless you're plugs look dirty from oil. I recently changed my
plugs with Bosch platinum plugs and got the colder set. IMPORTANT TIP! Before
you change them make sure the car is cold and spray some penetrating oil like
Liquid Wrench around the plugs and let the oil penetrate for at least 30
minutes. I didn't do that and sheared plug no 6(right rear most plug) and left
the threaded part of the plug in the engine which then had to be CAREFULLY
extracted. 
Regarding performance wires-I replaced by old cracked wires with Beru wires so
I don't have personal experience but I know some people who use them in their
Formula Vee race cars and they've reported noticable gains. 

In response to J.Uhalaine's question about brake lights not working.
I changed both brake switches on my car which has redundant switches located
on the master cylinder under the hood. I think your model is the same. You'll
have to move some hoses(easy job) to get to them and the whole job is pretty
simple. While you're backing out the old switch you might want to have someone
press the brake pedal to maintain positive pressure on the fluid and hopefully
avoid having to bleed the brakes. It worked great for me. The entire job to
replace both switches took less than one hour. Fortunately, the brake switches
were pretty cheap(around $15 each).
