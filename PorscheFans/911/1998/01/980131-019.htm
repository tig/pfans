'87-'89 models are essentially similar and are worth seeking out over the
earlier 3.2 Carreras because of their improved clutch and tranny.  An '86
is more desirable than an '84-'85 because it has the "improved" ventilation
system.  For lots of great info on buying a used 911, check the links at
http://www.haphazard.com/pcd/pcd_links.htm, and pick up copies of Pete
Zimmerman's "The Used 911 Story" and Bruce Anderson's "Porsche 911
Performance Handbook", both in their 2nd editions and available through
www.amazon.com and elsewhere.

And probably the two most useful pieces of basic advice are:  (1) find a
good independent Porsche mechanic to inspect the cars for you, and (2) be
patient!  Good luck!
