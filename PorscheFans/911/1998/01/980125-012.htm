   Although I`ve never had the fuel sender out of my 911, the thread
from yesterday describes it much like one I repaired in my `87 560SEL
Merc.  I had the same problems with intermittant fuel levels and no
warning light.  Go ahead and sand the wires.  I used 1000 grit with no
problems. 
I do recall having to make a special tool to remove the nut on the
bottom though.  I took a piece of 1/4" round steel, drilled a 1/8" hole
down the center and filed two flats into the end- in essence a little
spanner.  
       Re the power antennae from Walmart,  if they are from Harada they
are probably ok quality, but there are a lot of really poor quality ones
out there.  If the mast sections nest together poorly reception can
suffer, and  some of them use chrome plated hardware that rusts very
quickly.   Hope this helps.
