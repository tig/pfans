    Dunno, Larry... I'm a 914 guy, and I know very little about the
    various options for late-model 911s.

> this makes me very nervous since this implies that in situations
> where the spring/shock combination experiences extreme compression
> the "shorter" Euro spring would allow the "longer" U.S shaft of
> the shock to travel further within the barrel of the shock itself,
> potentially blowing the shock or driving the shaft through the
> bottom of the shock itself? (avoid potholes).

    Metal-to-metal contact is usually avoided through the use of
    "rubber" bump-stops on the shocks.  You still feel a shock when
    the car bottoms out on the bump-stops, but it's not as bad as if
    they wren't installed.

> Seems to me that one should swap the shocks also to avoid potential
> damage?

    You could, but -- as I've been discussing in private email with
    another list-member -- you probably don't want to do that if you
    can avoid it, since you'll be limiting suspension droop travel.
