	As my '72T Targa restoration progressed to the drivable stage, I found
that the shut-off solenoid on my MFI pump was not functioning. The
symptom was mainly backfire while deceleration.

	Deciphering the schematics in the Haynes manual showed that the
solenoid was connected to the RPM transducer. So, I checked the
transducer. 

	Sure enough, as I pulled it from the fuse panel, it rattled. I opened
it up and found a loose transistor and two dangling ones.

	A trip to Dow/Marvac and $5.40 later, I soldered in the replacements
and reinstalled the box. Now, I have a functioning shut-off solenoid.
