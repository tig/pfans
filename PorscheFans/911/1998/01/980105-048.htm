Hi all,

I have some data that I can offer you guys, but no definitive answers.

My '97 993 C4S came with 18" Turbo Look Design (TLD) wheels.  These are the 
wheels that look like the Technology wheels, but have solid spokes.  The 
offset of mine are 52mm front and 40mm rear.  The Carrera 4S has the same 
rear "widebody" of the Turbo.  

It was a while after I purschased the car that found that these are different
wheels.  They sure look the same.  See the TLD wheels on the Arena Red
Carrera 4S and the Technology wheels on the Silver TT at:

	http://205.230.206.225/newcar/bikfcpgd.jpg
	http://205.230.206.225/newcar/bamuxccg.jpg

I think it is now adding up to why the TLD's have a backspacing of 40mm and 
the Technology wheels have a greater backspacing of 60mm.  The smaller the
number, the wider the stance.  Porsche offers the Technology option (#408)
to the "skinnybody" Cab in '98 and the "skinnybody" Coupe in '97 at a 
cool $3229.  These wheels wouldn't fit with a offset of 40mm, so Porsche
has a 60mm offset and for the Turbo, uses a 25mm rear spacer.  No, facts
just guessing.  If anyone can confirm, I would appreciate that.

If my guess is correct, I am quite curious why Porsche would compromise
their flagship Turbo models' unsprung weight by using spacers.  Looking
at the '96 factory manuals, they say that the rear offset is 40mm on the
Turbo.  Maybe things have changed since '96.
