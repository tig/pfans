> Have the mechanic pull off the valve covers (on the side of low
> compression, start with the lower ones).  I think what you will find is
> a broken head stud.  The 3.0 Litre had dilivar studs in the bottom
> (exhaust side).  Though these studs did expand and contract with
> temperature once corroded they tended to snap.  Put a torque wrench and
> check the torque on all the stud while you're there.
> 
> The fix is to replace the stud.  This involves removal of the head and
> cylinder.  At that time you can check if there has been any head or
> cylinder damage.  The right fix is to replace all studs with raceware as
> they don't corrode like dilivar.
