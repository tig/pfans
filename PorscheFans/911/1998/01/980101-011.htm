Hi All! Just joined this list today. A great resource. 

I own a 1975 911S which has a 1974 2.7, CIS engine in it. This has been
my daily driver now for the past 11 years. Very reliable car.

Last week heard a realy NASTY ($$$$$) clicking sound come from the
engine on my way home. Performance did not seem to be affected.
Continued home keeping my fingers crossed. It sounded much like
excessive valve to rocker arm clearance on other cars. When I got home I
listened to the noise with the engine running. Seemed to be comming from
the left side closest to the rear. Shure sounded like bad valve
clearance or sticky valve. Pulled off the valve covers checked all the
clearances and watched while rotating the engine by hand....NADA,
everything checked out good. Closed it up and put some of the stuff to
unstick the valves in the crank case. Let it run for 5 minutes...still
no change in the sound.

 Pretty desperate now. Drained the oil and pulled the left timing chain
cover. Couldnt see anything wrong, nothing physically broken. Never felt
a bad tensioner, so didnt know if what I was feeling was good or bad.
Could, with one hand, easily depress the tensioner and it came right
back up. Disconnected the ignition and let my son crank it while I
watched. LO AND BEHOLD...there it was! The tensioner, apparently with no
oil but just the spring, was slamming up and down as it fought the
tensioner on the other side. THANKFULLY a previous owner had the
foresight to put guards on both of the tensioners. 

I am now waiting to get the 84 tensioner update kit that I ordered from
Vertex. BTW they seem to have the BEST prices around. The kit from them
costs $324 VS $449 from Performance Products and $529 from Tweeks. (Not
associated with Vertex in any way, just a VERY satisfied customer)

While installing this update kit I am contemplating replacing my leaky
oil return tubes with the colapsible type. Has anyone done this? Are
they difficult to install?

Regards, Rudolf Salomon
