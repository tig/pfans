> 1.The discussion about "dropping the clutch" has raised my interest in
> "dropping it into reverse".  Can anybody tell me at what speed I can safely
> "drop it into reverse"?
> 2. What I refer to by "dropping it into reverse" is shifting from a forward
> moving gear, with the car moving forward, directly into reverse, so as to
> lock up the rear wheels and make them spin. I think this would be a
> particulary nice effect going from stop light to light in front of shopping
> malls, or even coming off the freeway into a gas station or McDonalds.


The speed at which it is safe to do this is dependent on which gearbox you
have, and exactly what effect you're trying to get.

If you want to give a great effect by spreading little pieces of exploded
gearbox, dual-mass flywheel etc, over that offramp, then I'd think the 'drop
in reverse' speed would be anything over one or two MPH.

*IF* you have a G50 gearbox with reverse gear syncro, that is.

With an earlier gearbox with no reverse synchro, the effect would be more like:

GRIND... GRIND... (slow down) GRIND... GRIND... (slow down more) GRIND...
GRIND... (slow down to 0 MPH) Thunk! - i.e. it'd be more an audio show than
a visual effect. You'd gets lots of approving shouts from the crowd ("Hey,
Grind me a pound, too!") though.

Now... if you're talking about a Tiptronic - no experience, but it's equally
a BAD IDEA to drop it in reverse with the car moving forward!
