     Regarding technique and proper speed to shift to reverse while moving
forward.
There are two accepted methods. #1 At a fairly high rate of speed you heel
& toe till rear wheels lockup, then you can easily shift to reverse and
dump clutch @ 4000rpm (approx.) without causing any damage to gear teeth. 
(note: this has not been reliable on ABS equipped cars)
#2 Use the hand brake to lockup rear wheels before shifting. This requires
either a co-pilot, or not steering during the maneuver (preferred method)
     It must be noted that you should practice first on gravel, sand, ice,
or wet concrete till your technique is polished.
