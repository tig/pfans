Hi Ron;

There are a couple of areas you will want to focus some attention on.
These include the following areas:

- First, where your suspension ties into the belly pan near the fuel
tank.  This should be solid and without rust;

- Also, where the rear suspension (trailing arms) tie into the rear
supports - the areas near the support (rear longitudinal areas) need to
be solid as well;

- The floorpan *is* an integral part of the suspension, body - well,
entire car, so be sure that this was not simply brazed into place, but
rather, is properly welded.  I suggest you have the welds inspected by a
professional;

- As you probably know, the rear torsion bar tube is prone to rusting
out (from the inside out due to condensation) - check to be sure that
this is intact and not ready to break....

Finally, to add additional stiffening, you can have a cage welded in,
which will add both weight and chassis stiffness - but that would be
optional on your part.

I'm sure others will add to my list - but in the meantime, the areas
above should keep you busy for a while checking them out :-)
