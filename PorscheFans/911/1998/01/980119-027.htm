Jim,

Installing a larger swaybar in the front will INCREASE understeer (which
IS what you are experiencing); so that's not what you want to do.

Some practical ways to limit understeer include:

	1. Increase diameter of rear bar (use late G-50 turbo/carrera) 
	   or aftermarket, approx. 2 mm larger diameter from what you 	   have
now.
	2. Increase negative camber on front wheels 
	3. Decrease size of front torsion bars slightly.
	4. Decrease rate of front shocks, if adjustable.
	5. Install Turbo tie-rods.
	
Does the front end wander under straightline steering, or does it seem
firmly connected to the road?
