John- Yes, they can be a pain to remove. The knob is fairly fragile, and is
pushed on the stick shift, over a thin serrated/banded insert. The collar
serves to lock the knob on the shaft. I wish I could offer a magic trick
for removal but I can't. One tip- do not *twist* the knob to remove it as
that will surely break it. (Guess how I know that....new ones are around
$50 too.) Hope this helps.
