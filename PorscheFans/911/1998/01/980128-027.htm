	Does this problem also exist with the M030 (U.S) factory
	sport package and the factory (Euro) M030 lowering springs?

	This gap "seems" to make sense since I presume the the
	(U.S) shock is designed to be mated with a longer (U.S)
	spring, thus the shock will differ in that the lower
	perch will be offset and the shaft of the shock will be
	longer to accomodate the taller spring.

	If this assumption is correct then this makes me very 
	nervous since this implies that in situations where the
	spring/shock combination experiences extreme compression
	the "shorter" Euro spring would allow the "longer" U.S shaft
	of the shock to travel further within the barrel of the
	shock itself, potentially blowing the shock or driving the
	shaft through the bottom of the shock itself? (avoid
	potholes).

	Seems to me that one should swap the shocks also to avoid
	potential damage?

	Anyone better qualified to comment on this?
