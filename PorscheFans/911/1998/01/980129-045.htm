At 8:32 AM -0600 1/29/98, Cabrera, Albert (Al) wrote:
>I was wondering if anyone has replace the rear deck speakers on their
>Carrera?  I have an 86 coupe and am wanting to replace the rear deck
>speakers.  How do I get to them?  From inside the car or through the
>engine compartment (do I just peel back the sound deadening material)?

I found perfect-fit Blaupunkts at Best Buy--that was a while ago. I am
fairly sure that Cructhfields carries a few in the correct size. You pry
off the grill to access the mount screws.

>What are your thoughts on cutting the rear deck to make room for 6-1/2"
>speakers?  Will this decrease resale value?

This is a tough call, but I would say that, in general, it will if your car
is in good shape. If you attempt to sell someday and bill your car as a
"classic", then a buyer will note strange speakers and this could detract.
On the other hand, if the car is ragged and will go for a track car
someday, well, cut away. The likelihood of getting Lexus or Mercedes
quality sound out of a 911 is somewhat futile in my opinion.
