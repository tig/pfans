John,
I believe the "crinkle" paint you are speaking of is the same as you will
find available in electronic supply stores.  I used this many years ago to
make special electronic projects appear professional.  It can be applied
simply as a spray paint, and on drying will "crinkle"; although when placed
in an oven at low temperature, the "crinkle" was more pronounced and
provided a "baked on" finish.

A primer was never used on alluminum and it worked wonderfully.  Call around
to some electronic specialty "do-it-yourselfer" stores and you shall find it.

Hope this helps.
