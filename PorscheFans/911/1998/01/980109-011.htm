> What is an Ice Time Trial?
> 
> Basically, we take one frozen lake (10"+ of ice), arrange 
> a closed course (with optional snow banks), add some 
> timing equipment, provide a warm place to sit and observe 
> (the condo overlooking the lake), line-up the participants, 
> and then run some hot laps! It is unbeliveable fun!
> 
> Note to all scuba fans: we do not need your
> services - if there is any chance of seeing
> water, we do not run!

>                             Rules & Regulations
> 
> The Boston Chapter ice races are open to any and all licensed drivers.
> You do not need a BMW to run, nor must you be a member of the 
> BMW CCA.  Any car that can legally be driven on the street, may be 
> driven on the ice.
> 
> [you can usualy become a member at the race - NEDSM manager :)]
> 
> Vehicles that leak any fluids will NOT be allowed on the ice!
> 
> The cars are grouped by classes, which are determined by the drive
> train
> configuration and the tires.
> 
> The cars are let onto the course one at a time. You race against the
> clock
> for the fastest time in your class.  This is a very safe event. There
> is no 
> fender-to-fender racing.
> 
> There is a nominal entry fee for all drivers, generally about $15.00
> for
> We also have one Parker Spooner Memorial event per season when 
> the entry fee is $5.00 more, however the money is donated to charity.
> 
> Drivers may enter more than one car, and each car may have multiple
> drivers. However, all drivers are required to pay an additional entry
> fee 
> for each vehicle and class they enter.
> 
> To protect the club from liability, our event insurance policy
> mandates
> that ALL persons venturing out on the ice MUST sign the insurance 
> waiver.
> 
> Sometimes there's a "rent-a-wreck" available for anyone to drive. It
> could
> be anything from an Explorer to a Town Car, whatever Stan Jr. chooses.
> 
> Regular entry fees apply, plus an additional fee to help cover the
> rental 
> cost.
> 
> The classes are as follows......
> 
> A - D classes: Engine remote from drive wheels (Rear Wheel Drive)
> 
> A class: RWD + Summer tires. (no M+S rating)
> B class: RWD + all-season tires.
> C class: RWD + snow tires.
> D class: RWD + studded snow tires.
> 
> E - H classes: Engine over drive wheels (Front Wheel Drive)
> 
> E class: FWD + Summer tires (no M+S rating)
> F class: FWD + all-season tires.
> G class: FWD + snow tires.
> H class: FWD + studded snow tires.
> 
> R class: The "rental" car class. Whatever car it may be that day.
> 
> X class: All four-wheel drive vehicles, regardless of tire type, are
> grouped together in this class.
> 
> Exceptions: The Pontiac Fiero (Fiasco!) and Toyota MR2, being 
> rear wheel drive, but configured engine-over, are grouped in with 
> the front wheel drive classes.
>
