The cruise control in my '87 911 used to bleed off speed by about 0.5
mph/mile.
Unable to trace the problem to any obvious source, I eventually replaced
the CC
brain.  Works great now.
 
I've heard about this same thing from a few other Carrera owners.  The
CC
system may be the same in the 928.  In fact, the CC box has a 928.xxx.
prefix
for the porsche part number stamped on it.
 
The CC brain is VDO manufactured electronics package.  A new one lists
for
$600.  I called around bone yards and found a salvage from Parts Heaven
for
$125.  I wanted to repair the one I have, (I suspect a bad transistor or
something) but I cannot get an electrical schematic.  I also cannot find
anyone who will repair VDO electronics.

This evening I spoke with George Murphy, a contributor to The Star
magazine 
(MBCA) who has a sideline remanufacturing a variety of VDO products, and
he 
*believes* that the cruise control amplifiers used by M-B, Porsche, BMW
and 
a number of other auto manufacturers are the same.  You will want to
talk to 
him -- preferably with the VDO part number off of your unit in hand -- 
before sending it in.  His address(es) and phone number are:
 
     George Murphy
     Performance Analysis Co.
     1345 Oak Ridge Turnpike
     Suite 258
     Oak Ridge, TN  37830
 
     615/482-7260   ** 5 - 9 p.m. eastern **
On the phone he said that a system that will accellerate but not hold 
definitely has a failed amplifier.  His prices for remanufactured units
vary 
by type but are probably between $99 and $150 with core.
