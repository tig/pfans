>Would it be worth while machining my valve covers or buying the new late
>style valve covers

Try machining.  It won't cost you anything, and it might work.   I
"machined" the old covers on two early 911's, with some success.  I
"machined" them
by rubbing them on a piece of sandpaper placed on a flat plate, rubbing
until all high and low spots on the mating surfaces of the covers were
gone.  It only takes a few minutes.  I still found I needed to use a good
gasket sealer to keep everything dry.  (I like the gooey, sticky,
non-hardening Permatex-type stuff.)

It worked for quite a while, but in the end I replaced the covers with
the updated kind.  They, incidentally, aren't perfect either, but they're
better.  I'd give it a try, it might work for you.
