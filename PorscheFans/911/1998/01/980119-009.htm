I've got an '87 911 Targa now, and used to own a '68 Targa. Replacing the
front seal atop the windshield frame will help alot, but be sure there's no
unusual wear along the mating surface of the top itself. If folded often in
normal use, sometimes the vinyl at the fold points will crack or deform,
allowing a path for the water. 

Once the front seal is replaced, water will find its way in at the top
corner of the side windows where the vent window, the windshield frame, and
the side window meet. Replacing the side seals along the edges of the targa
top will alleviate this.

Do it yourself? Sure, if you're handy. Try and find someone to lend you a
workshop manual and read up on it first.
