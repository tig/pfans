To avoid damaging the outer visible part of the fender lip you can try 
to create the beginning of a crease halfway between the outer and inner 
portion with pliers.  Just put a shop cloth, or something in the jaws 
and work your way down along the fender.  Follow this with a wood bar, 
or the common baseball bat to do the "rolling".  BTW, rubbing usually 
takes place up around the 11 o'clock area on the d-side and 1 o'clock 
area on the p-side, if its going to happen at all.  Regular 16's for an 
'87 are 6" wide and they may not rub, even with lowering.
