Hi Chris,

Answers to your questions.

>1: Fold the wipers forward, "then cut away the rubber seal from outside
>the bezel."
>
>Is the bezel the metal trim ?  Should I just dig a knife into rubber
>around the outside of the metal trim ?  That's enough to get the
>windshield out ?

It appears they mean the  metal trim.  The idea is to cut through the rubber.
Don't spend a lot of time thinking about it.  Just cut.  If the rubber
hasn't hardened--it probably has--you can also do it by carefully, from
inside the car, pushing the rubber lip back under the sealing lip.  Cutting
the old seal is easier though.

>2: "Carefully prize away the bezel then cut through the rubber seal
>parallel to the windscreen ?"
>
>I thought the metal trim should be removed after the entire assembly was
>removed.  This sounds like it's saying to remove the metal trim first.

Right.  Remove the metal trim--carefully, so as not to bend it, **after**
you have the assembly out of the car.

>3: Remove windshield.
>
>4: Scrape away remaining seal or sealant.  Fix any rust spots.
>
>5: Get new moulding and smear a little soap and water solution into the
>bezel groove.
>
>Install the bezel so that there is approx. 0.4 in (1mm) gap at the top and
>bottom in the middle.
>
>??  Shouldn't I put the rubber on the window before inserting the metal
>trim ?

**YES**.
>
>6:  Place the moulding on the windscreen, and draw a piece of strong cord
>into the moulding at the top so that the cord overlaps by about 12in
>(30cm) in the top moulding.

>??  I think I see what this does.  It is used to pull the inside of the
>rubber seal around the sealing lip.  Mitchell Fieg posted a while back
>that width of the string is important.  Why ?  Where should the ends of
>the string end up before I start pulling ?

The diameter isn't critical.  About 1/8" is just about right.  You insert
the string into the groove of the seal before mounting the assembly on the
car.  One trick is to go around the seal twice with the string, so that if
when you
pull it out it there is a spot or two where it missed pulling the seal over
the lip, you get another chance without having to take off the entire
windshield assembly and start over again.  When you lay the string in, the
ends should overlap by about a foot.  Some guys like to start pulling from
the top, some from the bottom.  I don't think it makes any difference.

>7:  Put glycerine on sealing lip and insert windshield at bottom.

Soapy water works fine.

>
>8:  Slowly pull out cord so rubber lip is drawn over sealing lip of body.
>
>9:  Slide center clips over metal trim.
>
>10: Carefully prise the seal away from the metal, then from the glass,
>then inject a mastic-type sealing compound.  Ensure that the bezel is kept
>at an even distance from the windscreen aperture.
>
>?? What is this all about ?  I don't get this at all.

They're telling you to lift the edge of the seal a tiny bit, just enough to
to get the nozzle of a sealing compound under it, and to squeeze the sealer
in, evenly, as you go arounf the windshield.

Some folks use sealing compound, some don't.  I use it if the rubber seal
doesn't look like it's making a tight fit, which ususally occurs along the
top and in the upper corners.

>Thanks for any advice.

You're welcome.  Have fun!
