On my '88  3.2 carrera, with about 65,000 miles I find thin (5w40) mobile
one, fully synthetic oil, whilst probably best for running surfaces,
increases both oil consumption and minor leaks. Next oil change i'm back to
a high quality 20w50.

(My consumption varies between 0.25 and 1 litre per 1000km (compared to 1.5
litres per 1000 km max consumption listed in 911 handbook ie about 1 quart
per 500 miles). Oil Consumption seem to depend on vacuum, ie most when
idling in traffic or slow cruising, least on fast runs - which does seem to
make sense.)

Steve kelvin (porsche club UK tech supremo), confirmed this in a recent
article in porsche post winter '97 - pre 89 models over 75,000miles may be
better on Castrol GTX, Esso superlube X etc.

I know late carrera 3.2's are more prone to higher consumption due to
slightly doggy valve seal material (bad Dr. Porsche !!!). But no need to do
anything drastic.
