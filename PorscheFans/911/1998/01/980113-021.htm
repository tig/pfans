Robert_Garrett@notes.ymp.gov  wrote:

> I have seen several mentions of an "optima battery"; what is it?

    Bob:

    It's a battery whose electrical and mechanical specs are
    superior to most... It's smaller, lighter, and immune to
    vibration damage.

    It won't leak acid, it can't explode, and you can mount it in
    any position, even upside down.

    By the way... It's not a gel cell, no matter what everyone says.

> Where do you get one?

    Most performance-parts distributors sell them.

> How much do they cost?

    A lot... About three or four times as much as what you've been
    used to paying.

> Is this recommended over simply going to Wal Mart or Sears and
> getting the standard battery with as many cold cranking amps and
> (650 - 700? or so in a 60 month battery) and replacing the thing
> every few years for $50 or so?

    The Optimas are nice, but I personally wouldn't buy one for a
    street car; their advantages (to me) don't outweigh the high
    cost.
