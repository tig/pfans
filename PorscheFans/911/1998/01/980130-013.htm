When all refined before described methods have failed, there are still
two ways left, depending on whether you broke it or not.
Disconnect the brake line. This also can be a challenge if it remained
untouched for 15 years like it just was my case. Unscrew and remove
caliper's attachment bolts (extension lever advised + in some cases
hydraulic jack), retract brake pads carefully and there you go.
If not broken, then hold the caliber upside down  and block firmly the
faulty bleeder valve in a vice. It should stand up. Rotate carefully by
giving soft impulsions. You'll be surprised how easy it goes and if you
have been extra careful you might reuse the bleeder.
If it's broken, a good drill works fine if you don't shake. Check the
inside thread for future easy unlocking.
