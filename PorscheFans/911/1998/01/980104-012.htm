I receive so many answers all giving the right solution to my crazy
lights' question, that I decided to thank everybody in this collective
manner.

You can do the following as a temporary 30 mn repair, if you need to use
the car like I did and don't get yet a new part:
 
After getting to the light switch, clean the melted end of the black tip
using a thin electronics screwdriver, bend carefully contacts (thank you
Bruce Anderson) until it works approximately right, fine tune by locking
a small rectangle (size equivalent to a computer motherboard jumper) of
high temperature rubber (I cut it from an old spark plug connector)
between the origins of the two lower contacts and another one in the
groove above the third contact in order to slightly lower it. I drove it
the very same night and it worked fine. 

Thanks again to everybody.
