I've taken several different vehicles to Monterey from a converted van to a
35 ft motorhome (and have seen larger ones there). There is a permanent
campsite at the track with hookups but that is full long before the weekend
begins, although if you were going for the 356 Holiday the weekend before,
you might be able to get a spot. That is the best bet.

SCRAMP who manages the track has a habit of changing things from year to
year so you can't count on last year's experience being applicable this
year. There have been designated areas for camping/RVs, most recently for
me opposite the pits outside the track at the bottom of the hill. As I
recall, SCRAMP offers reserved sites which people take so they can be along
the fence, and the remaining spots are available on a drive up basis.
SCRAMP camping fees also make the normal RV park operators look like
pikers, but compared to $159 .......

Absolutely give them a call. If you don't have a brochure (I can't find
mine) get their number from the Monterey Tourist Bureau and you can get the
details.

Bottom line is that taking a motorhome is a good idea, as long as you can
last without the hookups (there are facilities for campers) or get into the
campground.
