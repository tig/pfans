993s have two jack points near each rear wheel, (that makes 6 total, I guess)
one for lifts and one for jacks, I think.  The owner's manual shows which
point is for which.  Previous 911s had a square hole in the side of each
rocker panel, covered by a black plastic plug you may have noticed about 1
foot in front of each rear wheel.  We used to stick the tongue of a special
OEM screw jack into that hole to jack it, or use swing arm points for lifting
by floor jacks, or the bottom of the engine.  993 engine mounts have liquid in
them so the car can't be jacked from the engine (or is not supposed to be).
The Weissach LSA axle cannot be used as a jack point, either.

Too many mechanics do not know about the engine mount and axle limitations and
can cause severe damage trying to do it their way (all the while swearing to
you that they know what they're doing).  I usually have the appropriate page
of my owner's manual handy and stand there with it while they maneuver the
lift arms beneath the car.  It is therefore unsafe to the worker because if
they bend a suspension arm after being so audacious, the 993 owner may hurt
them :-)
