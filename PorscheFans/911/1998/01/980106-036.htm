I think your '87 is the same as my '75 in this regard. There is a little
crush sleeve which is basically a fairly thin aluminum strip with
dimples creased into it which crush (hence the name) and provide a tight
friction fit.

Solution for removal is to pull like a SOB and then bonk yourself in the
nose when it finally comes loose.

Plan B would involve the use of a "U" shaped piece of wood and a hammer
which would fit around the shift lever and then use the hammer to tap
upward on the knob.

Plan C would involve removing the shifter housing (not the top of the
shifter mechanism...the springs will sproing and give you another bonk
on the nose) and taking it to a vise for subsequent removal via your
choice of BFH

Plan D would involve hacksaws on shift knob, general mayhem and
destruction of knob with subsequent need to buy a fancy Momo carbon
fibre knob as a replacement.

Plan E would be to just live with it as it is........
