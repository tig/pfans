Figured I'd take a shot at this, since I saw no other replies.  The
fore/aft play (0.125") in the coupler is normal and built into the
bushings, which have an oblong hole to accomodate this play.  The play
of concern is rotational, so you might check that.

You mentioned in your post that you want to improve shifting into
reverse and 5th, which are on opposite sides of the pattern.  Difficulty
shifting into these gears can be improved by adjusting the clamped
connection between the shift coupler and the shift rod.  You'll notice
that this connection can be rotated once the clamp is loosened.  If the
rotated position is not correct, you'll have difficulty with R or 5th,
but not with the "middle" gears of 2, 3 and 4.

Other things to check (and probably replace): 1. the ball/cup bushing at
the end of the shift lever. 2. the shift rod bushing-this is what the
rod between the shifter and the coupler in the rear rides in.  All these
parts are inexpensive and easy to replace when the shifter is out.  Good
Luck.
