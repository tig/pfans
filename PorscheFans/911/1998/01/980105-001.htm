Like cat people and dog people, the twain will never meet on some
issues. This is one of them.

Like the tailor who conveniently forgets that the customer ordered cuffs
on his tuxedo pants, I've always thought Porsche's omission of
cupholders was the Old Professor's way of telling us something--that
cars are meant for driving.  I've raised my sons with a rule of no
eating or drinking in the car and I'm proud of it--and when my little
one tells me not to leave the door of my '75 open for fear the "new
smell" will go it still smells new inside, I swell with pride on both
counts--the kid and the car.

Some of my dearest friends see otherwise, and that's fine too.

My best to all for '98, and may the smog ayatollahs smile down on us for
yet another year.
