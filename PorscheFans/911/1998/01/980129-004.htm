I wrote:

> > If all else is equal, more suspension travel is always preferable
> > to less.  Of course, it's usually hard to arrange things so you
> > have a lot of suspension travel AND don't have to compromise
> > other areas.

and Daniel van Niekerk  replied:

> Why?

    Mostly because having a lot of suspension travel means that you
    can use softer springs... Which means that:

    a.  Your tires will spend more time in firm contact with the
        ground,

    b.  Your car's balance won't be disturbed as much by bumps, etc., 
        and

    c.  You'll have a better feel for what your car's doing, so
        you'll be more comfortable (and faster) than if you're
        always wondering when your too-stiff car will suddenly snap
        away from you in a turn.
