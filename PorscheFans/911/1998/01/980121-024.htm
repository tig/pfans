The fan belt is adjusted by inserting/removing shims between the pulley
halves.  Inserting shims makes for less tension, removing them makes more
tension.  I guess it is just trial & error to get it right.  When you take
it apart, count the number of shims being used & judge off that for the new
belt.

BTW, when I replace my belt, I just put a box wrench on the big nut, then
squeeze the belt together with one hand (so the pulley won't slip), then
just give the free end of the wrench a whack with a rubber mallet.  The nut
loosens right up.  I have never been able to get enough torque by holding
the shaft with an allen wrench and turning the nut.
