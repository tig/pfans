Couple of things You might want to check while doing it:
- If brake fluid was added, it will overflow from reservoir when
  You push the pistons in. Will damage paint.
- Rubber boots, these get easily damaged when changing
  pads and pistons are not pushed enough in. =

  If damaged, change them, not difficult.
- There is (at least in my -85) a notch in the piston and
  this should be in a 20 deg. angle. Pistons rotate for some
  reason. =

  Hard to explain, it is well described in Haynes manual.
  I try, the notch should be on top of piston, about =

  0900 to 1300 clock. 0900 is at wheel center.

Like Bob Tindel say, channel lock pliers are good, I use
big screwdriver, but be easy, the pistons slide in nicely
but they have to go in straight.
