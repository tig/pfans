Joe:
The 3.0 liter engines as well as the 3.3 liter engines all carry a "930/xx"
type designation. The serial number indicates your engine (case) is a '78.
  Hopefully, the builder of the engine in question modified it internally
before installing all the turbo goodies.  However, the mods (or not) are all
internal to the engine which would allow you to determine compression ratio.
 You could probably get an estimate of the stroke of the crank by going
through the spark plug hole, but with the engine in the car you would end up
with a pretty crude measurement.  I think the difference in stroke is about 4
mm from the 3.0 liter to the 3.3 liter.  From having dissassembled quite a
few 911 engines, I can relate many surprises I have found inside, obviously
not the way they were assembled at the factory:  case mods, larger
pistons/cylinders, etc., so don't assume the case number designation will
determine what's inside.  

 A bore scope might allow you to see the flatness of the piston; looking
through the sparkplug hole, you could see the difference in the stock 3.0 CIS
piston dome and the 3.3 turbo piston dome.  Mods as to cylinder diameter or
to the head's combustion chamber would be almost impossible to determine
without a teardown.  The cylinder cooling fins are quite different on the
turbo; if you remove the cooling fan you could peer inside the cooling shroud
with a flashlight.  The absence of fins on the top of the cylinder would
indicate the turbo P/C.
