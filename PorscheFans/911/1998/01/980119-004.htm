We had a '71 911T with a Sporto.  Really different to drive.  You can start up
in 1st or 2nd as it has a torque convertor.  It has microswitches in the
gearshift lever that disengage a clutch to shift gears.  However, any time you
touch the lever it disengages the clutch!  But in town in traffic, you can
just leave it in 2nd and putt around.  Ours lasted about a year before I
converted it to a 5speed.  Everthing is there to do it, you just need the
trasnmission, clutch, flywheel, cable, pedal cluster etc.  In my opinion, the
only reason to buy a Sporto is to get a rustfree chassis, to put a 5 speed in!
If you don't have that, I'd stay away.  The Sporto set up is fairly complex,
with a fair number of adjustments.

If you are seeing rust arond the front suspension, it is probably because one
of the batteries leaked (it has two).  Look aroung the battery boxes for other
signs of corrosion.  If that is the only rust on the car, it can be fixed for
a few hundred dollars.  Aftermarket welded in  pans for that area are
available.  It should be done by someone familiar with 911 rust repair.  If
the rest of the chassis is rusted, stay away.
