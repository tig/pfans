Gary, for what it's worth, I had an '84 Carrera that continually passed a
standing idle emission test without a cat; this was in Western PA up until 
I got rid of it in ''96 with 135,000 miles.  Car barely recorded anything on
HC, CO, and CO2.  I don't know how this would translate into a dynamometer-
type test which I understand NJ now has, but generally the 3.0 and 3.2 engines
are pretty rugged from what I have heard.  Biggest problem would be valve
guides starting to go at about this mileage (mine were approaching
wear limits when I sold car, but bottom end was like new).

more, is my guess.  Then again, if state also does a visual check, you're
dead meat.
