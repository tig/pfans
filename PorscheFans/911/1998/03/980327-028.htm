Hi Eric,

I went from a '92 Carrera 2 (964330) to a '96 Carrera 4S (993140).  I loved
the '92 C2 but the '96 C4 has been a greater sweetheart.  I think the 993 is
the ultimate 911 as it gives the driver a bit more in terms of control for
that infamous tail happiness and has a slightly more forgiving ride.  Trust
me, it is not a night and day difference especially on the rear wheel drive
chassis.

The 964 engines had mechanical valve lifters.  These have to be adjusted every
15,000 miles but often go out of adjustment as early as 10,000 or 12,000 miles
and you must have them adjusted when in need.  This is an expensive part of
the service as at 15,000 miles the entire engine/transmission must come down.
All in all, the 993 with hydraulic lifters is less costly to maintain and
might be a better candidate as a daily driver.

Porsche also made some running changes to the chassis right when the '96 993
was introduced.  They beefed-up the wheel bearings and carriers to accommodate
the 18" wheels from the Turbo.  I have heard (unconfirmed) reports that rear
wheel bearings wear prematurely on the '95's.

In my opinion, you should consider the following:

1- If you are going to drive the car daily, think about maintenance costs.
Ask your local dealer or Porsche repair shop how much they charge for that
15,000 mile service.  Also, remember that the rear tires on any 911 last
anywhere between 7,000 to 15,000 miles at best.  The fronts can go at least
20,000 miles.  This is of course if you are not autocrossing or road racing.
Don't forget that oil change, each time you're going to need 9-10 quarts of
synthetic.

2- If you have decided on a 993, try to find a good '96 to take advantage of
the improvements in chassis and the added power (270 vs 282) via the Varioram
intake system.

3- If you are very concerned about the tail happiness of the 911, you can do
one of two things.  You can go to a PCA driver education course in your region
and learn how to control the rear wheel drive.  Or you can go for the Carrera
4 (993 better than 964) and pray.

Good luck!
