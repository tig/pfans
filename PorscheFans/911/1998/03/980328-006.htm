  Terry gave good advaice that I'd like to offer one more thing.
Assuming forthe moment that your car didn't foul plugs very easy before
you changed wires and plugs, that's where I would look first.

The plug connectors (those long jobs) are quite prone to failure. With
your VOM in hand, I would check for continuity from the inside of the
plug connector to the end of the wire that snaps into the distributor
cap. Sometimes the wires aren't making good contact where they join the
top of the connector or the internal resistor is bad (open).

With a good plug inserted into a connector and grounded, cranking the
engine should produce a fat, blue spark. If the spark color isn't blue,
then check all of the secondary wiring to each cylinder.

W5D's sound OK for this engine.
