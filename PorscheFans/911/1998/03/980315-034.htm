Robert Herbert wrote:

> Pfans,
>
> I am preparing to replace the shift knob on my '77 911S Coupe.  I know
> that the knob is press-fit (read PRESS!!-fit) onto the shift lever using
> a crush sleeve.  I recall (from Feb 20 archive) Walt Fricke advising
> that trying to remove/install the knob with the lever in the car
> "imposes stresses on the pivots in a 915 shift tower they aren't
> particularly designed or suited to take"

It is a tight press fit, but it is not all that tough. I'd make a "U" out of
3/4 plywood or similar which fits snugly around the shift lever and just
whack it from below a few times. NOt that it is all that hard to take out
the shift housing (about 5 bolts at the base of it), but is almost always
isn't necessary. I would not worry too much about a stock Porsche shift
lever, from what I recall of mine it is about 3/8" diameter and probably
could take whatever you could dish out short of a sledge hammer..

If you really do not care about the old knob, then why not take a saw and
just cut is from the side until you get to the sleeve and then take a
screwdriver and split it apart? Also recall that it is twistable with a
decent pipe wrench on it.

Hope this helps.
