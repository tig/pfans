Greg,
That will work fine.  I just repainted a set of polished center caps to 
match my new black Fuchs and they are a perfect match for the stock 
black paint.  I wet-scuffed them with 240 grit.  Then I painted with 
spray can primer, and next painted with Krylon semi-flat black paint.  
Use the normal stuff in the white can, not the high temp.  Don't use 
Rustoleum or any other type of paint, as the blacks have a strange 
reddish tint.  Next bake them in the oven (obviously you will have to 
remove the tires, but I feel this is the key to getting a good finish) 
at ~200 degrees for 30 minutes.  Don't get the oven too hot or you will 
oxidize the paint.  The baking process will harden the paint, and cut 
some of the gloss giving the perfect (stock) satin black texture/color.
