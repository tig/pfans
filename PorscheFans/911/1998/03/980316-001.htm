Donald L. Harvey  wrote:

> I have a car with 90,000 miles and have been using 20-50w Castrol
> since I purchased the car last year. I change the oil every
> 3000-3500 miles
> ....
> 1. Does high milage cars matter when changing from conventional to
> synthetics.

    Donald:

    LOW mileage engines may have problems with synthetics, since
    synthetic oil sometimes prevents rings from seating, but a 
    high-mileage engine won't exhibit those problems.

> 2. If it is not a problem (High Milage) what weight of oil should I
> use?

    20W50.  Don't use real racing oil, by the way... You need the
    detergents that are in street-car oils.

> 3. What about the tranny oil, is it a good idea to use synthetic
> oil?

    Sure, but make sure that you're using the right formulation.

    Most Porsche transmissions need a gear oil with "GL-5"
    protection... So if you decide to use Redline gear oil, for
    instance, you need to use 75W90 or 75W90NS rather than MTL or
    MT90.

> 4. Which is a good brand to use? (I have seen many brands at a
> variety of prices).

    Personally, I like Redline.

    Some people believe that Mobil 1 offers equivalent performance
    for half the price; I disagree, but not strongly enough to get
    into a big debate over it.
