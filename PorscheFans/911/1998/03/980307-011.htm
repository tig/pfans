Mat:

The "rule of thumb" in the automobile business is that grey-market cars should
be priced about 30 percent below US specification vehicles.  Many dealers
won't touch them with a ten foot pole.  However, this does not mean that you
could not find a good car at a very reasonable price that would satisfy your
needs (after all...this is an 18 year old vehicle).  As described, the price
would be about right (retail) for a US spec car...for grey-market
equivalent...$10-11K tops with a wholesale value of about $8500 max.  As
always, have a knowledgable techician check it out if you think you can
negotiate the price down to a more reasonable level.  None of these cars (thru
the 89 911) had power steering and the lack of AC is a "plus" in a car that
old (IMO of course).  The third "Z" in the serial number means it is a "Rest
of World" (non-USA/Canada) vehicle.
