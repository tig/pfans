Chris Ketchum  wrote:

> It seems I'm now in need of some new brake rotors for my '90 C2. 
> Initially I was looking at replacing the stock rotors with the cool
> looking "cross drilled" rotors, but having seen many cracked rotors
> at the track, I'm leaning towards gas slotted or stock rotors. 
> Would anyone care to comment on the pros/cons of each?

    Chris:

    Drilled rotors ALWAYS crack.  Slotted rotors work just as well
    and aren't nearly as susceptible to cracking, especially if
    there's a radius in the slots rather than sharp 90-degree
    angles.

> I like the cross drilled look....

    So does everyone else.

> .... but would I experience cracking in street application with
> several DE events annually?

    Yes.

> And who besides AJUSA sells quality rotors for the best price?  At
> $635 a set from AJUSA for either drilled or slotted, can the same
> quality be had at a better price anywhere else?

    Troutman will slot your rotors for something like $35 each... Or
    they can slot a set of new rotors and ship them to you.  I don't
    know what they charge for C2 rotors, so you should call them: 
    800 356-9307.
