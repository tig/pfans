On March 12, Chris Mellor writes:
>Can anyone point me towards an internet source for
>or tell me the torque, 0-60mph, 0-100mph and top
>speed numbers for the USA 911 Turbo of the 1986 model

Chris,

Take a look at the PCA Northeast website at
www.porschenet.com.
Go to their Tech Tips section
(www.porschenet.com/techtips.html)
and look at the entry entitled
'Everything you wanted to know about 911's in a FAQ format'.

In this section you will find a table that gives most of
the specs you asked about.  While the values are not shown
for the '86 930, they are for the '87, and are as follows:

0-60: 5.4 seconds
0-100: Not Given
Torque: 287
Top Speed: 162 mph

Note: You can also find this source on Michael Sherman's
website at Carnegie Mellon University, address:
For Website:
www.cs.cmu.edu/afs/andrew/usr18/mset/www/porsche.html
For 911 FAQ:
www.cs.cmu.edu/afs/andrew.cmu.edu/usr18/mset/www/text/911.faq

Note that Bruce Anderson also lists some additional values and lists
the same torque value:
Peak Torque: 287 lb/ft @ 4000 rpm (for the 3.3L engine)
(Rf: Porsche 911 Performance Handbook, pg. 111)

Hope this is of use.

Robert Herbert
