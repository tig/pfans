Wayne Michelsen - on  1 Mar 98, talking about "Re: Brake pad sensor wear 
lights", said:

> That is how they work.  The wear sensor is simple a loop of wire down
> into the brake pad and back.  When the pad wears down, the disk begins to
> wear into the sensor wire.  When the wire has been worn thru, the circuit
> is broken, and the little sensor light comes on.

Sorry to be a bit pedantic, but I understand the sensor to be a wire
encased in plastic. As the pad wears down, the plastic wears away until
eventually the wire is exposed, makes contact with the disk completing the
circuit and causing the light to come on. 

Some people just tie the sensors up out of the way. There are 2 caveats to
this : (a) the brake pads need to be manually examined frequently and (b)
if the car is sold on, the next owner *must* be informed.
