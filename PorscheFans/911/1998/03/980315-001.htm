The "scratch cream" advertised on TV has just become available at
Target.  It's $15 a tube and made in France as I recall.  I opened the
tube and looked at it--it looks to be just a very fine polish.  So long
as the color coat is not scratched through, you could massage the
scratch with it and it would be less noticeable.

There's a metal polish called Blue Magic (better than Simichrome and
half the price) that I've used on plastics (including watch crystals),
gold jewelry, and on a lacquered fountain pen.  It would probably have
the same result on automotive paint.
