Th problem sounds a lot like what I ran into with my 73 Targa.  I would =
shut off the car and go back a few hours later to find the batteries =
(have 2) dead.  I had the system tested and found that it was a bad =
diode in my alternator.  So when the car was running The alternator =
charged the battery.  Once the car was turned off the the bad diode =
would create a small voltage drain which in time (few hours) would drain =
the battery sufficiently to create a no start situation.  I had another =
alternator installed and my problem is now gone.  Havew you alternator =
checked before doing any replacement of ignition switches or wiring =
work.  Hope this was a help.
