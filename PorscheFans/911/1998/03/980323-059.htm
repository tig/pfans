My procedure for cleaning up an older set of wheels is to use 600, or
finer, grit wet sandpaper (3M strongly recommended).  Consider 1000 grit if
the wheels are not too bad).  The bright parts should look good after that.
 Tape off the silver parts, and paint using gloss black spray paint (I use
Rustoleum with great results).  Pull the tape while the paint is still a
little tackey - the paint will flow to prevent a sharp corner at the edge
of the paint.

You are ready to drive once the paint has hardened.  A clearcoat is extra
protection, but will not look original.  Wheels will hold up fine with
regular washing.  I paint the lug nuts at the same time.  If you can hold
off installing them for a couple of weeks, and use a good quality deep
socket, the nuts will look good as well.

I have used Wurth clearcoat, and have not been satisfied with its
durability.  It is a laquer paint, and will not hold up on a daily driver..
 MinWax gloss polyurethane spar varnish in a spray can, or the Chronar
clearcoat system in a spray gun last a long time and look great.
