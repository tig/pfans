For Hal Michael,
IMHO,  the WUR is frequently replaced when the wrench doesn't know what
else to try. At $280 a crack retail, this gets expensive. There is a
good article in Up-Fixin Volume VII, page 116 on making your WUR
adjustable. I just had that done (cost $20 at my favorite shop--could
have done it myself but I lack a drill press). Now I can dial in the
pressure I want to try--the graph in the manual gives a band of
pressures that are considered "in spec".

In the same Up-Fixin, there is another good article by The Bruce on
"knocking the plug" in the WUR. This is another way to adjust the
control pressure, albeit more laborious if you don't get it just right
the first time.

Contact me directly if you want to discuss this further, or if you want
copies of the articles.
