It may be wise to replace the bearings while you are in there.  It's
fairly inexpensive (maybe $25 a side, retail).

I use Valvolene Synthetic wheel bearing grease or all wheel bearings.
It's CV approved, which is some hard use, and excellent grease.  it's
also inexpensive at $5 per 14 oz. tube.

RE removal of the nut - it has an allen going through it (6mm) once
that is losedned the allen wrench can be used to turn the nut, or you
can use a screwdriver in the nut-slot. There is a slot in the hub
that the allen can be inserted through.  Both nuts are normal-rotation.
