The allen head on the shaft end of the strut is used to keep the shaft
from turning while you loosen the large nut holding the strut to the top
camber plate.  Having done this last year, at home on my 91 C4, I can
understand how it could happen, but it should not have! If the shop used
the correct tool, (I didn't, I made one sort of) the side stresses on
the allen head part of the strut should not be so great as to break the
side walls.  I'm not a professional mechanic so I can't say how "normal"
this is.  However, I usually don't expect to take my car somewhere and
get it returned less "fixed" than when I left it.
