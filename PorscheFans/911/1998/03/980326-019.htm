> I know the different methods of bleeding the brakes was discussed at
> length a few weeks ago, but I need a clarification on something before I
> start.  First of all, I will be employing the gravity method (no
> compressed air and no vacuum device).  My question is, when bleeding the
> brake fluid, is it okay or is it not okay to depress the pedal all the
> way to the floor?  One list member said it would not damage the master
> cylinder, another said it would.  Does anybody know for sure?  Also, is
> it absolutely necessary to depress the brake pedal in order to
> completely flush all the old fluid?  Or can I just let gravity do it?
> 

If you want to use the gravity method of bleeding then do not depress 
the brake pedal. If you do depress the brake pedal then the pistons 
in the M/C  travel past the holes in the M/C that allow the brake 
fluid in from the resevoir and hence you will get no flow.

This is actually a handy tip if you are removing a caliper for a 
short amount of time, press the brake pedal a bit and hold it there 
with a bit of wood or something to the drivers seat then you get 
little or no fluid loss whilst the pipe is disconnected.

I have not tried the gravity method but I can imagine it being ok for 
changing fluid but if you have a lot of air in the system I know how 
hard this is to remove using the other two methods and I wonder how 
good the gravity method would be for this.

Good luck and let us all know how you get on.
