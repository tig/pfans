Pfans,

I am preparing to replace the shift knob on my '77 911S Coupe.  I know
that the knob is press-fit (read PRESS!!-fit) onto the shift lever using
a crush sleeve.  I recall (from Feb 20 archive) Walt Fricke advising
that trying to remove/install the knob with the lever in the car
"imposes stresses on the pivots in a 915 shift tower they aren't
particularly designed or suited to take", so I'm preparing to take the
lever out.  There is a metal base, attached to the tunnel with 5
allen-head bolts, three in the rear and two at the front, along with the
shift tower that appears to have a top plate held on by two hexagonal
nuts. I also recall Dennis Kalma (from Feb 6 archive), writing about
installing a short-shift kit, warning about the high-tension springs
inside this tower.

The question is, how much of this hardware needs to come off to take out
the shift lever itself, solely for purposes of knob replacement?  I
assume it also makes sense to inspect/replace the ball at the base of
the lever and the bushings inside the tunnel.  Any tricks or additional
disassembly here?

Lastly, while I don't mind mangling the old knob, I feel a tad uneasy
about pounding on the new knob (either carbon-fiber capped or leather)
with a plastic pipe or rubber mallet.  There is no simpler way?

Thanks for your help.
