Hi Andy,

>The pistons do not move (turn) easily now that they're in the
>cylinder. Do I need to blow the pistons back out and insert them pre-set
>to the 20 degree angle?

Unfortunately, yes.  Sometimes you can turn them, but if you can't,
blow them out and insert them at the proper 20 degree angle.  You
needn't fret about being within a tenth of a degree.  It's
a pretty uncritical spec.  In fact,you can do just fine by eye-balling
it.  No need to make a template.  I think the template thing is a
typical Porsche fettish.

The good news is that blowing out a newly installed piston is easy.
It's a far cry from trying to blow out some 20 year-old pistons which
(at least in my experience) can take upwards of 150-200 psi.
