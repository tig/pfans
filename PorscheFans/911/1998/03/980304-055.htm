Ernie, I had an '84 Carrera which I sold prior to getting my 993.  Car had
135,000 hard miles on it (6-7 race track weekends a year).  New owner
wanted a compression/leakdown test done, 5 cylinders came out fine,
on 6th spark plug was frozen.  Snapped it off trying to get it out, 
requiring an engine removal and top-end teardown to repair.

Shop said that the valve guides were just about at the wear limits, 
recommended changing them.  They indicated ti was normal for this 
engine.   Bottom end of engine (pistons/cylinders, etc.) they said
was beautiful.   

I'd say maintain good maintenance and watch the spark plugs when
you change them; if they bind at all on removal, clean out the 
threads.  Also, don't let them go 50,000+ miles between changes
or you'll increase chances of them seizing.

Ray Calvo
