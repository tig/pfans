Bob,

Wear is not so slight at full power near the engine red line, even when at
normal temperature. Street cars of course spend very little time (on a
percentage basis) in this zone, so it is not usually a source of major
engine wear. Racers and some Porsche drivers spend significantly more time
in this zone, and it is a greater factor. Here a synthetic is probably a
good idea too. Also, if your car has heat problems, as many Porsches do in
warmer climes, a synthetic will probably prolong engine life and
reliability. Synthetics are supposed to have better heat transfer
characteristics, but I have never seen numbers for this. They will hold up
better at high temperatures, without a doubt. Of course, the best approach
is to address the heat problem more directly, like with a better oil cooler,
or cleaning out the rodent nest clogging your air channels.....
