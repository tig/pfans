     You bring up a good point.  I run Mobil 1 in my car because I get it at a
good price and so far I haven't heard anyone with bad experiences.  I change
it every 3,000 miles because I believe oil gets cantaminated by blow by
(exhuat gases) and is diluted with fuel over it's lifetime. I believe almost
nothing can be told by looking at oil and dirty looking oil may have as much
to do with the detergent aspect of the oil as it does with how long it's been
in or the condition of the engine.  At the margin it's clear Synthetics have
the edge, this may not be important but they're isn't much to argue against
it.  So it get's down to wheather you want to spend the extra money to feel
good about running synthetic oil in you car.  To me it's worth it because I
don't put many miles on my car and I do all the service myself.  Synthetics
tend to keep your engine a little cleaner (IMO) too.  Now,  if I was running a
fleet business I wouldn't be running synthetics in my vehicles.  I just don't
think it makes sense economically,  but emotionally?  :)
