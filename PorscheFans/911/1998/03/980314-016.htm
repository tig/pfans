Drew:
The numbers you got from the lab seem reasonable.  Since each oil brand
will have it's own additives comparing one car's numbers, on an absolute
scale, to anothers' isn't to useful.  You're correct it's the repeat
analysis that will tell you if your engine wear is increasing or
something is wrong.  The lab I use reprints my previous sample results
for each car so I can see trends and if there is a number out of limit
(had this on an Audi once) they flag it and advise calling them ASAP. 
They were right on a leaky injector diluting the oil.:) I'm assuming
they gave you the typical additional analysis of physical properties
showing viscosity breakdown, fuel/soot deposits.  I also get calcium,
magnesium, moly, phosporus, sodium, cadmium, titanium, and zinc. Some
are worthless tests but at $10.00USD it's great insurance.
