>To explain the elements you list and what they show, for engine oil:
>WEAR METALS:
>iron:  cylinders, rings, bearings, housings, rust
>lead:  bearing overlays
>copper: bushings, bearings, thrust-washers, oil cooler, sometimes used
>as additive
>aluminum:  pistons, bearings, pumps, rotors, thrust-washers
>CONTAMINANTS:
>silicon:  element used to determine the level of airborne dirt and
>abrasives in the oil (sometimes used as an anti-foam agent)
>boron:  present in most permanent anti-freeze systems and cooling system
>inhibitors (sometimes used as an additive)
>
>Obviously, the boron is an additive in your oil and you would be
>watching for this number to go down over time, showing depletion, as
>opposed to the other numbers going up as wear metals and dirt increase.
