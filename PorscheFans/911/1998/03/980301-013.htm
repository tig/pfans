Ernie, the sensors all have to work (i.e., no short across them) in order for
the lfgiht to go out.  On my  old'84, I could NEVER successfully transfer
sensors from one pad to another; always broke them.  Since I was changing
pads back & forth between track and street compounds prob. 4-7 times
a year, figured the wear sensors were superfluous.  Eliminated them by 
taking an old wear sensor unit and cutting the sensor off near the plug-in 
connector, stripping and wrapping the two ends together by the plug, and 
reinstalling just the plug back in place.  No stupid idiot light, and easier
pad replacement!
