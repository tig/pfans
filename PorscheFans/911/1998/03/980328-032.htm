I guess I was one of the ones indicating a problem with the standard 
pedal-push method.  I DID go through 2 master cylinders in about 3
years on my old '76 911 bleeding brakes this way, and I should note this
was a car that saw a lot of race track driver's education events and 
autocrosses, so brakes were probably bled 5- 15 tiimes per year.  When 
I switched to the pressure-bleed method on on that car (prob. about 40,000
miles on last MC when I sold car), used it on my '84 
Carrera (driven for 125,000 miles and 5-10 track events/year), and now
my 993 (with 27,000 miles and approx. 12 track events since Feb. '96
purchase), have not had a problem with a master cylinder wearing out.
I personally believe that over time they build up a wear ridge, and when 
you bleed them by pressing the pedal you push the seals past this ridge
 and wear them out prematurely.  Personal experience here, I am no
expert, so do what you want to.
