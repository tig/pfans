I depends.....but likely you will need to go at it from both locations.
The wire is just plain old piano wire, so I would take a look at the
flapper valves right over the rear axles and see if you can find where
the wire is broken (bets are it broke just at the point where it exits
the protective rubber boot under the car or at the set screw which
fastens to the flapper valve). If it is not broken there, then probably
a tug on the side which is broken will let you know immediately whether
it is just loose at the flapper or broken in the tube.

Given that it is broken, just get suitable sized piano wire, cut it to
length using the old one as a guide and then you just push it up the
guide tube and fasten it to the heater lever (it just wraps around
itself a few times) and then reset it back in position on the flapper.

NOt hard....just will take you an hour or so what with having to jack
the car up and so forth.
