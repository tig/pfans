Jeremy, don't consider myself an "expert", but have put close to 
300,000 miles on various 911's since 1976.

First I would ask is how much work do you intend to tackle yourself.
If all work is done by a shop; yes, they are high maintenance.  They
require valve adjustments, which is a several hundred $$$ job every
15,000 miles.  Then there are the 10 qt. oil changes.  Spark plug changes
are more difficlut (althogh probably not as bad as some US FWD 
trancerse V-6's).  Also parts run significantly more than for your neighbor's
 F150 pick-um-up, as you indicate (alleviated somewhat via mail-order).  
However, if you look at the maintenance as a hobby, it should not be 
expensive.  Work is not too hard, although might help to have knowledgeable
 friend  help you the first time.  Also, will need a shop manual; best are
from
Porsche but very expensive; Hayne's makes a good alternative.

Now, engines are very durable - should go 150,000 miles easily.  Suspensions
stay pretty solid, and don't require much maintenance besides occasional
front wheel grease pack.  Biggest problem in my experince is clutch/tranny;
I feel the 915 tranny used till '87 is weak,a nd cannot take a lot of abuse.

Have a good shop check over any potential purchase, and pick a shop
with a lot of 911 experience.  if you pick a car with a solid engine/tranny
and good body, you should get many pleasurable years out of it.

Good luck.
