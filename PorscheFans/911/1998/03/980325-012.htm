Walt, (and PFans Micheal and Lou)
  I gettrans parts from  Gary Fairbanks, 5 synchros for $100
for example. He advertises in Pano & Excellence. He's
not a real "people person" so know what you need before
you call. Every time I've ordered from him and I needed
a small used part (big pinion shaft nut, detent spring)
he's given it to me for free. I also bought a used cam tower
off him and it was in excellent condition at a fair price.
  I've limited my fun to the main & pinion shaft gears and
left the diff alone. I'd like an excuse to mess with the 
diff (put in an LSD?) but it looks like it would be a difficult 
job from a tools perspective. Heck, I don't even own any 
machinist's blue!
  As far a tools go, an old pro like yourself probably
has tricks I don't know about (so feel free to enlighten
me please!) but this is what I have used or had someone
else use for me (like the syncro tools). 
- Fork tool (this is so cheap there is no excuse not to have it)
- Torque wrench 
- 41mm deep socket - like 14 _inches_ deep! (can get buy 
         without this one but what a neat tool)
- 41mm torque wrench adapter - a plate with a
   1/2" square hole for the torque wrench and a 41mm
    hexagonal hole for the "big nut" on the mainshaft
- Impact wrench (+ air compressor)
- Big vise with aluminum jaws
- Factory manuals or equivelent
- Hydraulic press
- Neat synchro removal tools (2 required, one for 1&2nd and another for
3,4,5)
- Heat gun
- Drain plug hex drive

Automotion sells the neat synchro tool.
