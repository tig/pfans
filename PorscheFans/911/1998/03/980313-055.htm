Juergen did a good job explaining the vacuum switch, except that the valve is
closed initially at startup and *opens* 25-30 seconds after startup.  

The purpose of the vacuum line to the control pressure (warmup) regulator
through the vacuum switch is to richen the mixture during normal engine
operation when the throttle is opened, then the resulting reduced vacuum in
the intake manifold richens the fuel mixture via the control pressure
regulator.  The puposevacuum switch holds off vacuum during the first few
seconds of warmup, thereby richening the mixture.  So in warmed up operation,
an open (disconnected) vacuum line would prevent the vacuum from the manifold
from reaching the control pressure regulator, and richens the mixture,
increasing the RPMs.

I believe only the early SCs had this valve,  maybe the '78 and '79.
