Walter Fricke wrote:
> 
> Juha Vane ("and always wondering" - a kindred soul for sure) asks
> about which way the case through bolts install.  He has an 85 and the
> cap nut ends were on the 4-5-6 side (oil cooler) of the case.  He asks
> what is standard.  Well, on the 3.0 and earlier engines the cap nuts are
> on the 1-2-3 (distributor) side.  I remember this because the two studs
> under the oil cooler, which can only be one way, are opposite of the
> through bolts.  Don't ask me why or if it matters (hard to see how, but
> superstitious).
> 
> Juha notices another anomaly - the through bolt holes on the distributor
> side (where his bolt ends are) are not chamfered.  He wonders if this
> lack of chamfer is what caused the O rings to squeeze a little bit out
> despite his careful lubrication and despite the chamfer in the big washer.
>  I'll hazard a guess about the lack of chamfer - since you hold the bolt
> end in place while turning the cap nut, squeezing the O ring out on that
> side should be less likely.  Maybe the factory thought that by chamfering
> only one side they could save machining costs (however, since I am not
> at home I can't look at my fleet of broken older engine blocks to see if
> they have any chamfer at all.).
> 
> Those O rings are pesky critters, and despite my best efforts to hold
> things and tighten carefully I always see a little piece of O ring (they
> come in orange-red, blue, and clear that I've seen) sticking out here and
> there.  These are definitely a replacement item, as they are all mangled
> when you disassemble the case.  And it is something to worry about,
> because many of those bolts run through passages which see full
> engine oil pressure, which is just itching to leak out at the ends of the
> bolts.
> 
> However, my advice to Juha is to sleep well.  Mine have never leaked,
> so why shouldn't you be as lucky?  I think maybe it is only if you take no
> pains at all that you tear one in half or do something equally bad.
> Otherwise, the part which didn't squish out a bit is still inside forming a
> seal.
> 
> Just my opinion, though.  If anyone knows why I should be putting my
> through bolts on one way and not the other, or any old way if I didn't like
> some symmetry, I'd like to know.
