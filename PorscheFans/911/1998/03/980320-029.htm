The adjuster bolt (eccentric, as J says) is a 36mm, and the tightening bolt
(the one toward the front of the car) is a 24mm.  Both require a thin wrench to
access, much thinner than any I've found at usual hardware haunts.  I have
successfully used the Ride Height Wrench available for around $30 from Smart
Camber (http://www.smartcamber.com) which has both the 36mm and 24mm sizes with
handy offsets, although I've seen others advertised.  This wrench is a *must*
for DIY ride height adjustment on cars with the adjustable factory spring
plates.
