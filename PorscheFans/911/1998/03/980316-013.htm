911 shift knobs are a funny thing.  When you want them to stay they come
off, and when you want to remove them they won't budge.   Mine came off
in my hand in the middle of an autocross, and it turned out there was a
minute crack in it.  Figuring the stock knob was an overpriced cheapie,
I replaced it with a Momo and have never been sorry.  It doesn't have a
shift pattern on it, but feels much better--and besides, anyone who
doesn't know the shift pattern shouldn't be driving the car.

So my advice:  (1) live with the original till it cracks and wait for
the surprise when it comes off in your hand; (2) get a vise grip, induce
the crack yourself, and pull/twist it off.  It is, as the last writer
said, a press fit--with a corrugated strip of metal to hold it.
