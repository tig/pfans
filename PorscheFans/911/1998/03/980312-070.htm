Replacing the wire is not that big of a problem,  the wire is just plain
old piano wire.  I would take a look at the
flapper valves right over the rear axles and see if you can find out for
sure that the wire is broken (bets are it broke just at the point where
it exits the protective rubber boot under the car or at the set screw
which fastens to the flapper valve). If it is not broken there, then
probably a tug on the offending wire will let you know immediately
whether it is just loose at the flapper or broken in the tube.

Given that it is broken, just get suitable sized piano wire, cut it to
length using the old one as a guide and then you just push it up the
guide tube and fasten it to the heater lever (it just wraps around
itself a few times) and then reset it back in position on the flapper.

Not hard....just will take you an hour or so what with having to jack
the car up and so forth.
