To add to your confusion...

I am in the process of updating my '70 911 to a 3.0L and a 915 tranny. In
the mean time, my engine is not  done but I want to utilize the 915 by
adapting it to the 2.2L. Possible? Many say, "Yes." What I have been told
is to use a 915 (SC) pressure plate, disc and T/O bearing in combination
with the 2.2L Flywheel. Well, upon removing my 901 I find that I have an
aluminum pressure plate while the stock SC pressure plate is cast
iron...Yuck! Upon further inspection I realized that the aluminum p-plate
has 915 numbers and is 225 diameter...same as the SC p-plate. All I did
was install the 915 T/O bearing in my old p-plate and bolted it together.

So...I think they are interchangable!? I noticed that in the Automotion
catalog the 2.2 p-plate is $100 more than the 915 (SC) p-plate but I would
say that is cheap horsepower. I would go with the aluminum p-plate. Now
the clutch disc must stay as a 2.2 designed disc since the drive shaft on
the 901 transmission has a smaller diameter spline than a 915 tranny.

Hope some of this helps,
