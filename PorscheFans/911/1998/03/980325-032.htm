Fred,

> How difficult is it to pull a tach or clock? I have heard they are a
> friction fit.  How do you get a grip on them to pull it out?  Thanks for
> any help.

They are indeed a friction fit. They are a VERY TIGHT friction fit.

I removed mine a couple of weeks ago to straighten them and clean up the
rubber seals a bit.

I found it much easier to remove the smallest gauge (clock or gas/oil level)
and work my way in to the bigger gauges - the smaller ones come out more
easily, and with them out you can get your hands in and push the bigger
ones from behind.

I had to use a screwdriver to VERY VERY CAREFULLY apply pressure to pry the
gas/oil level gauge out. I was very concerned, naturally, that the 
screwdriver would slip and gouge the gauge bezel, so I applied only as much
pressure as necessary, and like I said, I was VERY VERY CAREFUL ;^)

Even with all the other gauges out, the tach was a bitch. I had both hands
in the dash pulling it from behind and was thrusting my whole body back
in the seat while making strange grunting noises. Eventually it came out...
