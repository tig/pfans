Hi Jamie,

If it's only beginning to show wear I would try to dye the worn spots and 
treat the leather before replacing. There's no assurance that new leather 
from another batch will match any better than spot treating what you have.

Help with sourcing and color codes for Porsche (aftermarket) leather dye 
can be obtained with the help of John Paterek's mail order Paterek 
Preferred in NJ at 201 635 0689. John is a technical advisor in the Porsche 
Club on restoration.

Leather treatment comes down to a few good products like Lexol or Tony 
Nancy's stuff. But a very good leather reconditioner for worn, cracked 
stuff is Soffener from Color-Plus (also in NJ).   Both Paterek and Car Care 
Specialties (also in NJ!! at 201.796.8300) can help with suggestions based 
on how old the leather is and I think they both carry all the products.
