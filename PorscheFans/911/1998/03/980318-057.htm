> Three quick questions about interior work:
> 
> 1)  Anyone have any experience with sound-deadening mats in doors
> (DynaMat or similar)?  Since I am replacing speakers, I like the idea of
> getting a clearer sound, but it seems that the mat would have to be
> quite thin to allow reassembly of the door panel (1/8 inch or so).
> 
I have used Dynamat. It is about 3/32nds inch thick. It is really dense and
does a great job of vibration damping. I am going to use that stuff from now
on whenever I do car audio work. I wanted to damp vibration in both the
inner panel and the outer door skin. I put several large pieces on the
inside of the outer skin and I covered the entire inner frame under the door
panel.

> 2) Does it make any sense to apply an undercoating to the interior metal
> floor pans?  The idea is to achieve a preservative effect, though I
> doubt it is seriously needed.  Just going on the assumption that all
> panels were coated on both sides originally.  Would this affect adhesion
> of glue for carpeting?
  
My '86 had thick sound deadening padding on the floor, over 1/8" thick. The
carpet was only glued on the inner rockers and the tunnel and I don't know
if this padding was used at those spots. 

> 3)  Does anyone use foam pad under carpet in a 911?  The idea here is
> the same as for home carpet: a little bit of cushioning and
> sound-deadening.  I'm thinking of a very thin home-type pad.
> 
I drove a 911 with 1/2" foam under the carpets. I didn't like it; it was too
soft, although the car's owner liked it. A carpet pad would be firmer than
foam...
