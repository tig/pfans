Jeff,
The part number you have appears correct. There are no instructions in the
kits I have seen. It helps to get an exploded drawing of the shifter assembly
from Haynes, factory SM, etc. to get a better idea of the parts relationship.
The parts in the kit look almost the same as the existing parts in your shift
housing. A bench vise will come in handy to D&A the unit as there are a couple
of strong coil springs (as you know) to remove and install. I had to do some
minor grinding and filing to achieve the desired amount of play (drag) of the
shift lever. You may or may not have to. Use some good grease to lube the
parts before you button it up, then check the shift tube bushings and coupler
for excessive wear and play. The 2-3, 4-5 shift has me reaching a bit too far
for comfort so I'm going to heat and bend the shift lever to get it a little
closer to me. On a scale of 1 (easy = change oil) to 10 (rebuild
transmission), I'd say this job is a 3-4.
