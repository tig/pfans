> 3) For the '74 2.7.  I have heard and read nightmare stories of the 2.7 motor on the '75 thru '77 models with the thermal reactors, but what's the story on the '74s?

74s are your best bet for a 2.7. They ran  cooler.  Still most people claim the 2.7 motor top end will require more frequent rebuilds than any other 911 power plant. See Bruce Anderson's "Porsche 911 Performance Handbook" for  details. Several PorscheFans report their 2.7 engines are robust and long-lived. If I knew a 2.7 engine was rebuilt properly , I would consider a 74.

> 4)  I remember my father owned a '72 T, and it seemed to be in the shop a lot for tune ups and valve adjustments, given that these early cars do not have any modern electronic controls, what are the maintenance schedules for the '73/'74 cars?

I believe they require a full tune-up and service (including valve adjustment) every 6,000 miles. Most people change the oil/filter every 3,000.  If you drive every day this certainly is high maintenance compared to a new Toyota, etc. If you use your car for recreation, it is not bad at all.
