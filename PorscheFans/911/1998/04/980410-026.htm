John and Bruce,

Bruce is right, backfiring that occurs when you lift off
the throttle is almost certainly due to a malfunction in
the system that shuts off fuel from the pump when you lift the
throttle but are at speed (greater than about 1600 rpm), but
doesn't shut off the pump when you lift the throttle at idle
(below about 1600 rpm).  It could be a switch adjustment, as
Bruce notes, but it can also be the rpm sensor.  In my case,
it was a bad rpm sensor on two cars.  I'm rebuilding a third now,
and have yet to check the rpm sensor, but I'd be willing to bet,
based on past data, that it's not working right.

The fine transistor leads tend to corrode away with time, and the
capacitors change value.

If you're backfiring persists, John, you might want to check
the operation of the sensor.
