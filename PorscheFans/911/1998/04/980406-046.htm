In my zealous fervor to explain recent findings about O2 sensors, I =
improperly specified that the sensor output as a higher voltage for a
leaner condition.  The exact opposite is true!  A rich condition will =
result in a higher voltages (~700mV) while a lean condition will result =
in the
lower voltages (~200mV).  It makes much more sense that way, since a =
failed sensor will produce little or no voltage.  The ECU interprets =
this
as a lean mixture and therefore attempts to richen it - ergo the =
"Default" rich condition I described.  Thanks to Walt Fricke for =
catching the error.
