Hi Shane

This is an oldie AND a goodie.  Disconnect your battery for about 10-20
seconds and then re-connect it and the light should go out (and stay
out).  If not then you DO have a brake problem or an electrical wiring
problem.

Also, do the sensible things like check the brake fluid; do
start-stop-start-stop driving (safely) until you think the brakes are
100% OK to the touch and make sure the car does not veer at all on each
start-stop - use "no hands" as well to check this; you could have them
checked properly by a P-Mechanic; research how long ago the brakes were
replaced and assess whether that's acceptable; any other safety check
you can think of (go through the Owner's Manual for hints on this part)
- after all, why waste a perfectly good Warning Light?

Give those brakes a good hard test.
