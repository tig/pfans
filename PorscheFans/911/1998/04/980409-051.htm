The O2 sensor is in the LHS heat exchanger. Its wire goes more or less up
from there through a grommet in the engine tin. It then plugs into a
connector on the LHS of the intake manifold. Pull the plug with the engine
hot and running. First, you should notice a change in the engine note. Next
take a sensitive voltmeter and measure the O2 sensor output. The plug that
comes from under the engine tin should have a couple of contacts in it. You
should get between 200 and 700 mV from one pin to ground if it is
functioning properly. Or at least this is how it worked on my '86.
