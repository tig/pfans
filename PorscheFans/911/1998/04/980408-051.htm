I sent a message earlier this week about my mishap with the locking 
wheel lugs breaking the key, and thus rendering my attempt to fix the 
brakes useless.  Since I bought them about a month ago from Vertex, I 
called and asked the salesmen about returning them.  He told me to 
speak to a manager, who was quick to insist that I had used an impact 
wrench and that I WAS to blame for the key breaking.  I stated that I 
had used them as directed, and the key had broken, so it sounds like a 
defect to me.  He then engaged in a childish argument, trying to wear 
me down and get me to leave him alone.  I would suggest that he should 
take some responsibility for the locks breaking, and he would interrupt 
my sentence and insist "WHAT DO YOU WANT ME TO DO????"  He had little 
regard for my input, but rather, anytime I would offer a suggestion or 
try to ask a question he would quickly reply "WHAT DO YOU WANT ME TO DO 
(ABOUT IT)???" (Sometimes he varied it a bit.)  Anyway, my $0.02 is 
that their customer service is less than I expect, so I'll find a new 
vendor for my parts.  So, on to the resolution.  I called the dealer 
with the hopes of getting a replacement key.  He told me to call the 
manufacturer (McGard) to avoid mark-up (if you can believe it!), so I 
called and they told me to mail it in, with the code #, and they'd ship 
me a new one for free!

My question:  I recently replaced the all the brake pads, and the

MC is bad, but the pedal is still pretty soft.  Also, the warning light 
is on.  What does this mean if the E-brake is off?  Low hydraulic 
pressure, sensors on the pads, poltergeist activity (to borrow from 
someone last month)???  I thought they didn't put the pad sensors on 
until 1987?  

Sorry this was so long, I just wanted to share my experience w/ Vertex
