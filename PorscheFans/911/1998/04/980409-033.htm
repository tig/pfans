Are your brake lines original? If so, I would replace them with new, OEM
lines. Older brake hoses can loose their strength so that when you press the
pedal the lines swell. This can give a spongy pedal feel. Stay away from
stainless brake lines unless you intend to replace them every year or two.
Stainless braided lines do wear out but you can't tell until they rupture.
Brake lines usually rupture at inopportune moments, so beware. At least with
all rubber brake lines you can tell when they are starting to wear out.

The '83 doesn't have pad thickness sensors (al least mine doesn't). The
brake warning light may be triggered by the pedal going to low, which would
certainly be the case if you pedal-bled the lines. I once read that there is
a reset switch on the MC, maybe others would know for sure.

BTW, I have had mixed experiences with Vertex. One has to remember that they
are the price low-baller and that customer service is low on their priority
list.
