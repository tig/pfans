The Haynes Manual is notorious for lulling you into a false sense of
security.  IMHO, removing the ball joints - either the nut on the bottom
or the pin through the strut - is the hardest part.  The a-arm itself will
come off pretty easily.  One of the ball joint pins came out relatively easily,
the other had to be drilled out after I pounded it into oblivion.

If you plan on replacing the a-arm bushings, I would remove the arm
completely, otherwise you could probably avoid disturbing that monster.
