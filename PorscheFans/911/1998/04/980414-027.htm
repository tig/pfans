>      1. Manual says it can be changed without removing the engine, is this
>      right. Any practical tips are also appreciated.

Yes, you can change the cooler w/engine in car.  A better way, IMO, is to do a "partial 
drop"; eg. remove axles at trans flanges, loosen or remove parts in engine bay which 
restrict movement, then drop it 6-8 inches.  You will gain amazing perspective and some 
extra elbow room.
> 
>      2. It is awfully expensive part. In case it it the cooler not the
>      seals is it possible to fix it?

Don't know...maybe someone else can answer.
> 
>      3. It is dropping oli on heat exchangher. I occasionally get smell of
>      burned oli inside when I have the heating on. Do I have a leak also in
>      the heat exchanger or is the air taken from such location that the
>      smoke would enter the cabin?

Heat exchangers rarely remain air-tight, what with vibration, corrosion, flying debris, 
etc.  Usully the first breaches are around the edges where the exchanger meets the 
pipes.  Wouldn't worry too much, unless problem continues after you've repaired cooler.
