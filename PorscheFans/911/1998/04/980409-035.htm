There is a trailing throttle anti-backfire device on the MFI setup. 
It is controlled by an electric switch on the driver's side rear 
injection stack. The switch location allows the throttle crescent to 
push on the switch (like a dome light switch) to shut off the fuel. 
When at idle it  turns the fuel back on. On my 73 the doesn't always 
turn the fuel back on, then it stalls. I have the switch adjusted so 
that the linkage doesn't quite touch the switch. I find with a good 
tune there is little backfire. Buy the Bosch MFI fuel injection book. 
It doesn't cost much. Stoddard has it.  Bruce
