Mat,

> I'm just wondering if anyone knows of an easy way to change the position
> (height) of the brake pedal on my '83 SC.  The way it is now the brake
> pedal is about 3-4 inches away from the gas pedal, making it impossible to
> heel/toe shift. I just need the brake to be a little closer to the gas. The
> car has new rotors, pads, and front caliper if this could have anything to
> do with it.  I think the previous owner did this himself, so I don't know
> if there is any adjustment that can be made in the pedal assembly. 

Not 100% positive about an SC, but my car ('90 C2) has an adjustable
brake pedal.

Remove the wooden floor board under the pedals and check for an adjustable
pushrod behind the pedal. If you do adjust it, be very careful to not set
it too low - remember that your pedal will go down under repeated heavy
stopping.

Alternately, if your accelerator pedal is adjustable, you can try adjusting
it UP a bit. Mine is, and I adjusted it rather than the brake. If you adjust
the accelerator up, make sure that you check and possibly readjust the
throttle valve stop in the engine compartment, so that full pedal deflection
won't result in a broken throttle stop.
