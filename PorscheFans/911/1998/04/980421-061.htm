I use a regular company for my '82 SC.  It costs about $800 for 7500
mi/year with a reasonable deductible etc. etc. (I live in a city that
isn't exactly well know for its low theft rate)  With this coverage I
can drive when and where I  want too.  I have not done any track events
yet, but will most likely get additional coverage when I do if my
company will not cover drivers ed.  From the several semi-negative posts
about Lealand-West they just do not seem to be something special.  My
point is, why the special company just for your Porsche?  Just use a
regular company and beat them up until you get a good rate.  I am sure
that the Porsche is not your only car if you drive less than 7k mi/yr so
threaten to take your $$ elsewhere.  Not only that, but most companies
do have programs for special cars, but they would rather you pay the
regular price };)  If you have a good record for many years you are an
insurance company's dream so use that to your advantage.  Power to the
Porsche fans!
