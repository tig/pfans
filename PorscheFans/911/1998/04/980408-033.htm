> > I am in the process of designing (and soon fabricating) an oil tank
> > to go in the smuglers box in the front of a 911 (this tank will be

> > be approximately 11 inches wide, 7.5 inches high, by about 9 inches

> > have the pickup (-16 or -20) either in the bottom center or on the
> > back (toward the fire wall) bottom, centered.  My concerns are in
> > designing baffling to prevent starvation.  I have heard mixed
> > reviews on the issue of aireation in the rectangular design (some
> > think the design needs to be circular to reduce aireation while
> > others think that the only way to reduce foaming is to install a
> > centrifuge).
>
>     Steven:
>
>     As you've heard, the ideal tank would be circular, with the inlet
>     coming in at an angle to persuade the oil to swirl around the
>     inner wall of the tank (the "centrifuge" effect that you
>     mentioned), and it'd be as tall and skinny as possible.
>
>     With your short, wide, rectangular tank, you're working against
>     some pretty major obstacles from the start... Are you SURE you
>     need a remote tank in that location?
>
> > Input concerning baffling or general design would be appreciated.
>
>     You need at least two baffles... For your rectangular tank,
>     you'll probably want to build them in the shape of four-sided
>     pyramids, with a big hole where the point of the pyramid would
>     be.
>
>     They get welded into the tank upside-down (i.e., with the
>     pyramids pointing down).
>
>     The upper baffle should be mounted below the inlet, but above
>     the surface of the oil in the tank.  You may want to punch a few
>     additional holes in it, in addition to the big one in the center.
>
>     The lower baffle is mounted down near the bottom of the tank,
>     just above the oil pickup.  If you choose the pyramid angle and
>     the size of the central hole correctly, it'll keep the pickup
>     from being uncovered.
>
>     If you have the time and materials, it might be instructive to
>     build a tank out of Lexan or plexiglass or something, so you can
>     see how your baffles perform... My guess, though, is that it'll
>     be hard to design a tank that'll fit in the smuggler's
>     compartment AND de-aerate the oil well AND prevent the pickup
>     from ever sucking air.
>
>     Why are you replacing the stock tank with this one?
