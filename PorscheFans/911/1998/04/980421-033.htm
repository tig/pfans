>Charles Becker  asked :
>
>>> Now my question -- I have a completely stock 1970 911E Targa that I 
>autocross, and I am looking for suggestions on how to spend money (i.e.,, 
>go faster). I have a limited racing budget, and I want the best bang for 
>the buck. Right now I have Michelin street tires, no front sway bar and 
>leaky front shocks. Needless to say, I need to do something. I want to get 
>new shocks and torsion bars, a front sway bar and stickier tires, but I 
>don't have the $$ to get everything at once.
>
>What do the experts suggest? What is the hot autocrossing/time trail setup 
>for these cars? <<
>
>The key is balance and control. I recommend close to stock specs
>as it will be easier to drive and nearly as fast as a highly
>modified car (my bone stock '72S was fastest in Production class
>and only beaten by one car in Improved classes at it's 2 parades).
>
>1. First thing to get are good shocks, Bilsteins or Konis work fine.
>Otherwise the car will be daangerous even on the street.
>
>2. Get a good alignment, lowered with 1-2 degrees camber
>and zero toe front, a bit in the rear. I like 1.5 front 2.5 rear camber
>but will wear the inside of the tires on the street.
>
>3. You will need sway bars front and rear. To start out you 
>could use stock takeoff 15mm bars but 19mm would be
>better with stock torsion bars. Stock torsion bars will be
>fine until you have figured out the rest of the car.
>My 23 year old bars worked fine.
>
>4. Now it would be time to get some tires - the best on
>stock 6" alloys are 225/50/15 BFG R1s.
>
>Other things to check/replace are rear torsion bar bushings
>on the end caps. They get REAL ugly. Don't worry about
>power you have enough to beat a 3.2 Carrera if you can
>outdrive him.
>

The '72 I recently bought was autoX'd by a PO.  This guy was pretty serious
about it and apparently was 1st in his region in 1983.  He wrote up a
little care and feeding note when he sold the car...with alignment specs, etc.

Front:  camber 1 degree 10 minutes, castor 6.5 degrees, toe-in 10 minutes
each side.  Rear:  camber 1 degree 30 minutes, toe-in 15 minutes each side.

This car already has Konis on it too.  Front are set 1 turn up from full
soft, rear just a hair down from full hard.

The sway bars he used have been removed.  The car has no front bar on it
any more, but it does have a rear bar.  And it's been lowered pretty
far...not sure how much, though.  There's little to no gap between the tire
and the fender.

I plan on taking it out for it's first AutoX this weekend.  I'm not sure
how close the alignment is to the spec above...but I'll let you know how it
goes.  This'll be tough considering this is my first 911 and I've only had
roughly 2 days behind the wheel since I bought it.  Time to look stupid!!
