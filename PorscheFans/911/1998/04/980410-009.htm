     Shane,
     
     I had the same problem with on my '77.  If I pulled the Aux. Reg. out 
     and checked it on the bench, it worked properly with the valve being 
     fully closed in about 2 minutes.  Then I checked for voltage at the 
     connector with the ignition key 'ON', had 12V.  So I put it back in 
     assuming it was not the problem and wrote it off.  I searched and 
     checked everything but the high idle still remained.  I then found 
     with the motor warm the valve was still wide open (found this after 
     many hours of searching for other culprits).  I re-checked the voltage 
     at the connector, had 12V.  I pulled the hoses and plugged them, 
     restarted the motor and the idle fell.  What I discovered was that the 
     contact strips inside the connector had slipped back and were not 
     making contact with the socket on the Aux. Reg. so the heating element 
     could not heat up.  I pushed the wires forward and made sure they did 
     not slip back when connected.  The motor idled like a champ.
     Hope this helps.
