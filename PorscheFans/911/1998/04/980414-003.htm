Subject:Hi-Energy Ignition Woes
Date:Tue, 14 Apr 1998 08:20:20 -0400
	I fitted my 1977 911S with a high-energy ignition system, and after a few
hundred satisfying miles, the motor began to skip a beat at about 3000 rpm
or so.  The problem grew more serious, until it began to die abruptly above
2000 rpm, then refire after a brief interval.  All components tested OK.
	The gremlin: The old coil.  Apparently, the new system generated enough
energy to punch through the insulation of the 20-year-old coil, causing the
engine to shut down.  Once the voltage dropped, the coil resumed its job,
causing the motor to restart.  Under static tests, the coil looked perfect.
 Under high stress, it failed.  The moral: Change the coil if you change
the ignition box on an older 911.
