Tory, I don't know where you bought the sway bar and U-tab kit, but
as i remember when I did this change on first my '76 then my '84, I welded
the U-tabs to the A-arms so that they were directly under the new sway
bar ends.

One warning - how are you mounting the sway bar to the body?  Just
bolting it to the sheet metal is a no-no; you'll tear up the body in no
time.  You need a reinforcing piece on the inside of the body.  I made
up a 3/16" steel plate the same outside dimension as the triangular
sway bar mount/bushing with a hole in the center for the bar to pass 
through.  I then drilled 3 holes at the corners and welded 3 bolts to the 
plate so they stick through the plate and through the 3 mounting holes
on the bushing mounting.  I placed this on the inside of the body and
bolted the new sway bar bushing/mount to the outside of this.  

I wrote up the procedure forthe Porsche Club of America magazine, and
they published it in Volume IX of their "Up-Fixin' der Porsche" technical
summary.

Good luck!
