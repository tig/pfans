I just read a couple postings on starting an early 911 with the hand throttle.
I have a '73 911T and I am interested in the proper way to start a "cold
engine".  I got the car last fall and the previous owner did not clue me into
anything (it is my first Porsche).  I read the manual and this is what I
do.........

1.Pull the hand throttle all the way up.
2.Start the car (it doesn't seem to want to start but it does on the first try
- after          
    about 5 seconds).
3.Let it run at high idle for 1 minute.
4.Depress the gas pedal briskly.
5.Lower the hand throttle as the gas pedal is coming back up.
6.Drive away!!!!!!!!

The oil level gauge is still near the red mark.  It takes a few minutes to get
the gauge into the upper white portion of the gauge.
 
Do any of you skilled veterans have any tips?
