Regarding Perry's mention of the '75 silver anniversary edition and
opening rear quarter windows, my observation is that these were the norm
on the 911/12 series from its inception.  Officially they may have been
an option, but most cars came with them.  The '76 912E was the first
model I recall that came only with fixed quarter windows, with the
mitered corners replaced by elbow moldings.  Soon thereafter all 911s
came this way--goodbye opening quarter windows, and hello to a minor bit
of cost-cutting.
