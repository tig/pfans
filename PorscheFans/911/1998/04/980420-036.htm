> Looking for recommendations and advice on lowering a 1998 911 C2S. I am
> considering having this done by the dealer prior to purchasing the car.
		
	Do you have the U.S M030 sport suspension option
	on your car or just the stock setup?

	I just had my 1998 C2S lowered, I obtained a set of the
	European M030 sport springs (and shocks) and had those
	installed. The car looks and handles great.

	(If you are interested I have my U.S spec M030 sport 
	 suspension for sale?)

	You should contact Jim McCarthy at Windward Performance
	(http://www.windward-perf.com) they have a lot of experience
	of doing this with a variety of 911's and they can also 
	sell you the parts well below what your dealer would sell
	them to you for.

	My uneducated guess is however that unless you have the 
	sport shocks, you will have to go with either H&R or
	Eibach's to lower the car. I dont think it is a good idea
	to mix sport springs with unmatched shocks.

	Hope this helps.

	Rgds

	- Larry.

	P.S

		I dont recommend lowering the car more than 30mm
	front and rear.
