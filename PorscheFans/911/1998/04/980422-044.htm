Welcome to the world of 911s and the 915 transmission (used in 911s from '72
through '86). I had an '86 with similar symptoms. I had the trans rebuilt.
It needed new 1st and 2nd gear syncros and 2nd gear slider and dog teeth.
These are very typical wear points for the 915. Such a rebuild is on the
order of $1K from a good p-car wrench. More if you don't pull the
engine/transmission yourself. I was quoted $1200 by one mechanic. I did the
R&R myself and saved about $200. This is also a real good time to do the
clutch.

How soon should you do this? Whenever your patience runs out. If you are
careful, you can drive it for quite some time in this condition. I would at
the very least change the fluid and put in Swepco 201. It may help somewhat.

The bottom line for 915 transmissions is shift slowly. You cannot power
shift them; they will self destruct if you do that. Just take your time and
finesse the shift. This is true even on 915s in like-new condition. It is
part of your car's character!

Another tip that a highly regarded local mechanic, Chris Powell, gave me was
always shift while the car is moving. When downshifting while coming to a
stop, pull it out of third or whatever before you stop moving. Now if your
first gear syncros are shot, you won't be able to put it into first, but at
least put it into neutral before stopping completely.

Good luck!
