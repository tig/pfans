Hi Sean,

A couple thoughts on your third question:
1) Make sure the velvet upper door seal at the top of the door frame is
in place and secure.

2) Look at the front of the door (nearest the windshield) just below
where the top trim piece is screwed in.  There should be 2 black plastic
caps (about 1" diameter) that fit into holes in the door.  If you see
the holes, you may want to get these caps, which can produce the noise
you describe.  There are other such holes in the door, but these are the
usual suspects.

Hope this helps, and good luck.
