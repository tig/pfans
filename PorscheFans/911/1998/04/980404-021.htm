Tim,

I have an '87 which is mechanically very similar.  I've had 2
starting problems with mine.  First, I had a bad O2 and/or
Cold start sensor which was effectively saying "Feed Me".
It resulted in severe flooding and therefore if I missed the
first spark, the engine just drowned itself.  Since you say
you don't smell fuel, I would say this is not your case.

Second, and a likely candidate for you, my DME relay failed.
The effect was an open circuit and zero voltage to the fuel
pump.  Therefore, although I cranked just fine, no fuel means
no fire and no start.  My temporary fix to get home was to
open the fuse panel in the luggage compartment, and re-route
the power for the electric windows to the fuel pump fuse.

So, if you want to check this, turn on the ignition, and
check the voltage at the fuel pump fuse.  If the DME relay
is bad, it's located under the driver's seat by the DME
control unit.

Keep Porsching,
Wayne Michelsen
'87 911 Targa  (w/good DME relay)
