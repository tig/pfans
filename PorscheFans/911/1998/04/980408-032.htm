Garland Phillips-FGP004  writes:

>I have a small oil leak around the chain cover on the driver's side (81
>SC). It looks easy to replace the gasket as I can access all retaining
>nuts. The one hang up is the horizontal piece of metal that is screwed
>to the chain cover. This metal seems to cover the gap between the engine
>and the rear body frame.
>
>What's the best way to change the chain cover? Lower the engine? Can it
>be changed without lowering? Tips?

I've done this two ways, but the easiest way is to remove the muffler and
the rear piece of engine "tin" (the horizontal piece of metal).  You don't have
to lower the engine.

I've done this without removing the muffler and just unbolting the tin and
moving it up out of the way, but it's a PITA that way.  Getting the muffler
off can also be painful, but makes the timing chain cover removal a snap.

Good luck,
