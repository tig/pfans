Going to 21/27 increases stiffness by 55.7% and 57% f/r which is a good
match. I have 21/27 and it works well for me but I also have adjustable
sway bars and had to stiffen the front sway bar a lot to dial out the
resulting oversteer (for a '74 with 23mm stock rear t-bars).   22/28
will also work well
but no personal experience here.

   >I have heard from someone running a 21/26 combo which is a 49%
   >increase
   >in the front and a 37% increase in the rear. Seems to me that this
   >combo
   >should produce more oversteer all else being equal(tire type and
   >pressure, sway bar size, etc).

21/26 results in 56%/36% rate increase which would result in 
more UNDERsteer (all else being equal).  I don't think you want this
combo
w/o adjustable sway bars to fine tune it.
