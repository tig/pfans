On Wed, 1 Apr 1998, John wrote:

> Imparts is advertising blue tint light bulbs for H4 headlights.  They of
> course claim they are brighter, whiter, etc.
> 
> I talked to a colleague who is an world-class expert in human vision.  He
> said that _theoretically_ a blue bulb (i.e., with a lot of shorter wave
> lengths photons) should result in better depth perception and better
> resolution in low light conditions.
> 
> Has anybody tried these bulbs and have any empirical results???  Are they
> practically any better??

Although they may "seem" brighter, physics says that a blue coated 
bulb will emit less overall light than a clear bulb of the same 
wattage and construction. In addition, the blue light has a greater 
tendancy to reflect off fog and rain (which is why amber lenses are 
popular for fog lights), reducing usable light in the situations 
where you need it most!

If you're going to upgrade to a real HID unit go for it. Otherwise, 
save the blue bulbs for the lowered Hondas with the three foot high 
spoilers.
