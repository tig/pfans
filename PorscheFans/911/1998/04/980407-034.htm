Buddy, 
This sounds like a pretty good setup, with the exception of the sub being =
placed in the trunk.  This is relatively inefficient as much of the bass =
will be lost in the firewall.  As a 911 owner, and father of three, I =
also understand the importance of space, however little there is of in in =
a 911.  I put a JL Audio microsub enclosure witha 8W6 subwoofer behind =
the driver's seat.  It is also removable if I need the space.  I would =
suggest you have the installer put everything but the sub in, and then =
try various enclosures before you buy.  I would try subs as small as 8" , =
10" and the 12" you suggest.  a 12"  sub is pretty big and takes up alot =
of space. (laws of physics also dictate size and power for low =
frequencies)  I would also suggest you try tubes, sealed and ported =
enclosures as each will give a different sound.  Don't buy until you hear =
what you like.  A good installer should let you experiement with a =
variety of setups.

IF you are so inclined, I happen to know someone that has a Z-Box Sub =
woofer  for a 911 that is brand new.  This is a custom fiberglass =
enclosure that replaces the entire rear seat, and houses two 10" =
subwoofers.  It takes up no more space than the stock seating =
arrangement.  email me if you are interested, I think he wants about $800 =
for it.  or call Audio Innovations at 800-892-6430 and ask for their =
catalog.  Rod makes an enclosure for the 911 that houses 2 8" subs.
Best of luck
