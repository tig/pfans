Bill:

I didn't get any responses before I started my project, so here's what I
did. By the way, there's a response to someone else's question on the 4/13
digest.

No removal of interior trim pieces is necessary. Here's what you'll need
for the job.

-- Door seals (I bought mine at Automation)
-- Velvet upper door seals (mine were in bad shape; also bought at
Automotion)
-- 3M Release agent
-- Adhesive remover (I used a 3M product)
-- Weatherstrip adhesive (I used the 3M black weatherstrip adhesive)

The job is simple and straightforward, but a little time consuming.  The
steps are:		

1) Unscrew the velvet upper door seal
2) Apply the release agent to the adhesive holding the rubber door seal and
pull the seal from the body. 
3) Clean the old adhesive from the body.  This is VERY time consuming, and
the most tedious part of the job.
4) Apply the weatherstrip adhesive (technique depends upon the type of
adhesive you use) and install the rubber door seal
5) Install the velvet upper door seal.

I would guess the whole project took about 2 hours or so.

Good luck!
