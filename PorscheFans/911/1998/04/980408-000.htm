Phil:
I have an "early" 911 in the form of an 83SC.  The car is very reliable
and has been relatively maintenance free.  I have never heard anyone
claim that the 915 gearbox is a problem at 80,000 miles, but I have
heard that SC engines last a very long time with examples hitting
200,000 miles without splitting the cases.
As far as the rust worm is concerned, fully galvanized bodies began with
the 76-77 cars, so one that has been properly maintained and not pranged
should not have this problem.
  
Find a clean well maintained example that suits your fancy and get it.
Your three year old will do fine in the back until she/he hits about 13
years old when her/his head will start to bang on the rear window  (like
my oldest son's does now).
