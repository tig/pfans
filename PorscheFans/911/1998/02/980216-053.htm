> Sorry to ask naive questions, but here goes:

> 1.	What did the factory do to the US spec 3.2 L engine in 1987 to
> increase the output to 217 HP versus 207 HP in 1986?

There is room for debate here. The factory certainly change the fuel
requirement from regular unleaded to premium unleaded. The programming on
the chip was changed to take advantage of this. Nothing in the engine
changed at all. This is the factory line on the change in HP. 

I believe Jerry Woods and Bruce Anderson have done some dyno tests that
suggest that there is not noticeable difference bewteen an '87 or later
brain box and an '86 or ealier one.

Will we ever know the truth? Does it really matter? What's 10 HP amongst
friends?
 
> 2.	What was the difference between the Euro spec 3.2L engine versus the
> US spec engine that accounts for the additional horsepower?  Is it
> entirely attributable to the absence of a catalytic converter on the
> euro engine, or is there more to it than that?

The compression ratio of the US engine is 9.3 to 1. The Europan engine is
10.3 to 1. That, along with a different chip and exhaust make up the HP
difference.
