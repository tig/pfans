But I had another problem in the winter that I never mentioned. When the 
cold weather would come my interior (dome) lights wouldn't light up when I 
opened the doors. I could fiddle with the switch on the door jamb and pull 
the plunger out and get the lights to come on. But sometimes they just 
wouldn't come on. I suspected a bit of corrosion on what is a very simple 
spring loaded switch. It was probably a combination of the rubber cover 
becoming stiff when cold and an aging spring that couldn't quite force the 
plunger to close the switch. I just lived with it during the cold weather. 
Eccentric hand-built cars and all that.

[snip]
The problem, you see, is that Porsche in their infinite wisdom provided a 
convenience feature on the 964. They decreed that with the key removed you 
should still be able to activate the windows and sunroof until you get out 
of the car. Allows you to close the windows if you forgot. Or to crank the 
roof a crack to let the heat escape while parked. And there is a circuit 
that is energized -- *until you leave*. And how, you ask, does your car 
know when you've left? Easy! When the dome lights come on. Then this 
convenience circuit is de-energized.

Since the door switches never tripped the dome lights this circuit was 
staying energized until my battery was drained. The fix was to replace the 
switches and rubber covers.
