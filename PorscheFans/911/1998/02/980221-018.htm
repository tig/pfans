Hi Andy,

It sounds like you speak from experience.  That's the kind of 
experience that nobody wants.  To answer your question, yes, Purple K 
and most other dry powders are corrosive.  They are very mild 
corrosives by themselves, but when you put them on a hot material the 
corrosive action is accelerated.  Dry powders do not cool the fire, 
they put it out chemically.  So the powder gets plenty of time to bake 
on.
