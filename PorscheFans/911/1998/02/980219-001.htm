There seems to be a big split between the grease / air crowd, just like water /
air cooled :-) I fall on the grease side, mainly because its  safer ( a piston
comes out VERY fast and hard with 100psi + behind it) and grease will push out
a piston that 160 psi won't ( experience here ). To be honest I never found the
cleanout that bad - IMHO they should be cleaned out this good anyway.

If you must use air however I made an adapter with a short piece of metal break
line. I cut off the one end and got a brass tubing fitting that had 1/8 npt
threads on the end ( in the plumbing section). Then put a 1/8 to 1/4 female
fitting on this. Now you can screw a air hose fitting on this. Now all you have
to do is clip on your air hose and you the air regulator to increase pressure
until the pistons start moving.
