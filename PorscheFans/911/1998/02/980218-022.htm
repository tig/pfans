> I realize the 911T was the detuned, carbureted entry level 911, but it is

Hmmm.  I find that my '71 911T always starts very easily (three 
pumps on the throttle and it starts!) and isn't too finiky.  However, 
for it to be this way everything needs to be in perfect tune.  

The Zenith carburetors are excellent carburetors.  I rebuilt both of 
mine a couple of years ago and it made a remarkable improvment.  
Make sure that all of the accellerator pumps and the over-run system 
is working .  It is very important to have them balanced properly.  
It is a lengthy process that is clearly detailed in the  factory 
workshop manual.

There is a bit of a lean surge at about 3000 rpm.  I believe this is 
due to the idle fuel mixture jets being a little undersized for smog 
purposes.

Make sure that the ignition system is working perfectly, and that the 
valves are in proper adjustment

My 911T isn't quite as smooth as my '72 BMW 2002, but it is pretty 
close.

> It also has the "sport" transmission with the dog leg first gear so I spend
> much too much time thinking about what gear I am in...

Even after I had my transmission rebuilt, it was still very stiff and 
particularly difficult to engage into first gear.  The Porsche 
synchronizing system of that era left much to be desired.

> 2) The Targa top is extremely noisy. Can the top be adjusted to eliminate
> wind noise?

If you replace the seals, it will quiet down considerably, but it 
will never be as quiet as a coupe.  I notice the wind leaks around 
the leading edges of the doors more than I do from the top.  There is 
a factory technical bulletin that explains all the intricacies of 
adjusting the top.  It is quiet helpful.

> 3) The steering is extremely light...It has wider than original Dunlop tires

Yes.  With the installation of turbo tie rod ends the car becomes "go 
cart" like.
