> >What am I looking for inside the cylinder walls and on the

> 
> The walls don't matter much.  The psiton rides on the seal, so
> pits in the walls don't harm anything.  Pits in the pistons do.
> If the pitting/corrosion on the pistons is only slight, it can usually
> be polished off with fine sandpaper (like 1000 grit) or steel wool
> (like #0000).

Just another point.  There will likely be some plaque buildup
in a few places.  It's hard and black/brown.  It looks like
corrosion, but it's not.  Before you take any sandpaper to
anything, just take a screwdriver or some kind of pick and
try to chip it off.  If that doesn't work, then sand away.
