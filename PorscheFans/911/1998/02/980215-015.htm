On my 73 T, the anti-backfire fuel shutoff is controlled by an 
electrical switch on the driver's side. The throttle linkage crescent 
moves against it to turn off fuel on trailing throttle conditions. 
Mine shut off the fuel well but didn't always turn it back on. I 
adjusted the switch (it is adjustable) so that the throttle crescent 
does not touch it. I found few backfires on a properly tuned engine. 
Stoddard also has the Bosch injection book in their catalog.  Bruce   
 PS The switch is on the left rear air horn.
