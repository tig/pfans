Hi Vic:

  We have fashioned small square aluminum plates and pop-riveted them in
place after painting. This does allow the option of using heat again at
a later date. The other method is to weld small circular plates of steel
underneath and paint everything.

Sealing all of the cracks and holes in the engine sheetmetal is very
important to keep dirt and heated exhaust gas from being pulled up from
below and behind the car into the cooling fan. This can contribute to
some hot running problems as well as spread dirt and oil film all over
the cylinders, heads, and the oil cooler.
