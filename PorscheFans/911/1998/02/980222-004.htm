Nill,

This will in no way harm your car. Sulphur is essentially an 
unwelcome contaminant in gasoline.  Reducing it will, however, 
probably raise the cost a few cents.  To make low sulphur gasoline, 
refineries must start with low sulphur base stocks, or else they 
have to do some additional refining to remove sulphur,  which is 
difficult.

I work as a Sr. Design Engineer in the fuel injection industry.  We 
are heavily involved in developing gasoline direct injection.  The 
new type of catalyst this requires is VERY sensitive to sulphur 
content.  Sulphur maximums must be 1/3 to 1/2 of current 
levels.  Expect the allowable sulphur content in all gasolines to 
drop over time.
