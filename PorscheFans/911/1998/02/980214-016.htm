Michael wrote about inoperative brakes and a sticking brake pedal at highway
speeds on a wet day.  One possible cause is swolen rubber brake lines that
connect to the caliper.  These lines have tiny inside diameter, which can
become
constricted as the rubber swells from water absorption.  A temporary fix can
sometimes be had by changing the brake fluid.  Since brake fluid (standard
types,
anyway) absorbs water, the new fluid can effectively suck the water out of the
rubber lines, opening them up.  I've used this procedure with some success on
my "other" car.  But another symptom of swolen rubber lines is that the brakes
tend not to release, and get hot.  Since Michael didn't mention this, my idea
may not be the problem.

Hope this helps,
