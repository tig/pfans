Stan Amanatidis  writes:

>He has a 1981 930 that has just developed this interesting condition of
>a clicking sound coming from the engine under heavy acceleration. You
>would almost think that the car was pinging. If you floor the
>accelarator the sound starts and just keeps going until you change
>gears. Under non heavy acceleration (normal driving conditions) the car
>does not make the noise.
>
>Even when the car is idling and you give the accelerator a quick
>"heavy"  press you can hear the click  The click is heard before the
>cars revs increase. If you gently accelerate, the click sound is not
>heard.

Does the frequency of the clicking proportional to engine speed?
If so, then:

I would check the CV joints.  I've seen them make noise under the
exact conditions you describe.  In my case, I was able to remove
them, clean 'em and repack 'em and the sound went away (this
was on a 944, but I think it should apply to a 930 also...)
