I replaced the rears and put new inserts in the original Bilsteins on my 79
SC.  To hold the shock from turning, I used a leather belt about 1.5" wide,
and passed it around the shock, through the belt buckle, and then a couple
more wraps around the shock. This provided enough friction to stop it
turning.

If you are lowering the car, you must remove a really big washer on the top
of the front shocks, between the top of the shock tube and the car body.
These were installed to change the range of the shock when meeting the
higher NA heigth requirements, and removing allows the shock to operate in
the intended Euro range.  I don't believe (but can't remember) that there
was such a washer on the rears.
