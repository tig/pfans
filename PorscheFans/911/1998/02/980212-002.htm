If you use grease to remove the caliper pistons, do it with the calipers off
the car.  To remove the grease a shop solvent bath will work just fine.  If
you dom't have one or access to one, first remove as much of the grease as you
can with rags or towels, then try brake clean and if that doesn't work, try
some carb cleaner.  The carb cleaner is a fairly strong solvent and will
attach certain paints as it is meant to remove carb deposits and varnish.  Get
every last bit of the grease out.  I don't know how much new Porsche pistons
cost but if reasonable and yours look scratched or pitted, replace them along
with the boots or seals.
