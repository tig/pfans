> I humbly submit one minor correction to your statement above, which in no way
> impacts the appropriateness of your response to Gregory.  It was not from "day
> one" that 911 and 912 shift knobs were a press fit from the factory, but from
> March 21, 1967 onward. My '66 has a threaded top on the shift lever, as did
> all 911 and 912's prior to that date. The knob has internal threads that screw
> onto the rod for these early cars, not the press fit with clamping sleeve that
> later cars have.
> 
> I only obtained first-hand knowledge of this piece of trivia while attempting
> installation of a Weltmeister short shift kit, which unfortunately ignores
> this variation and requires replacement of the shift knob on early cars, an
> added expense I had not anticipated. It is documented in the factory service
> manual for '65-'68 911s on pg.SR 29, which also has a picture and drawings of
> the homemade tool for removing the press fit knobs. On the next page, it has
> the picture you spoke of regarding driving the new knobs on, using special
> tool P 299!  I'll be looking for a piece of plastic pipe, as you suggested, or
> a hollowed out block of wood to use for this, myself.
