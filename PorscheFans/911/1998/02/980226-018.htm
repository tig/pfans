> Fred, just a question:  if you need these to do a brake bleed by the standard
> method of pumping the brake pedal, I would say avoid them.  This bleed
> procedure accelerates the master cylinder wear, since you force the piston
> past the normal travel points and prematurely damage the seals.  Replaced
> master cylinder twice on my old '76 and once on my later '84 before
> switching to a pressure bleed, where you connect a low pressure air source
> (15-20 psi or so) to brake reservoir cap and let air pressure force out
> fluid thru bleed ports - no pumping and no added wear on master cyl.  Have
> used this successfully for over 6 years without having to replace
> a master cylinder.  If you want more info, drop me a line.

P-Fans,

I have purchased and installed the Speedbleeders on my 79 SC. After reading the great 
despcription of the "self-leveling" method (hang hoses vertically from bleeder screws, 
fill fluid periodically, thought I'd try it with the speed bleeders.

It work fantastically!  I did both rears first, then both fronts.  On the rears, I 
actually filled the hoses twice, closing off the screw in between and emptying.  
Basically, I just let the system do its thing while changing tranny fluid and doing 
other maintenence; each end sat for about an hour.  The brake pedal feel is noticeably 
improved over my previous "pump-bleeding".

Don't know if the speed-bleeders or the methodology caused the improvement, but they 
certainlyy didn't hurt!
