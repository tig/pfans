The later cars did make a lot of use of KS (Kolbenschmidt?) Pistons and 
cylinders, which weren't Nickasil lined.  Two identical 911's for 
prospective purchase would have the buyer better off in the one with 
Nickasil (Mahle) because of its superior wear characteristics (I don't 
know anything which technically differentiates them other than the 
lining material and that Mahle pistons are forged, where KS are cast).

It is possible under the car to determine which you have.  Mahle liners 
have a slightly darker look to them (between the heads and the case).  
KS, I think, have the initials "KS" stamped somewhere on the outer 
surface.

Charlie Kindel, in your web pictures and your rebuild, did you have/use 
Mahle or KS?
