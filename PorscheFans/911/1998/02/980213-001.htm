I found it much easier (and no mess) to use a hand operated tyre pump
(bicycle pump). Undo one end of the hydraulic pipe that connects the two
pistons, attach the pump, close the bleed valve, put a rag tightly between
the pistons and pump. These pumps can generate more than 100psi which is
more than enough to remove the piston.
