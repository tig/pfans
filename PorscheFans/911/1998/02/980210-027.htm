Derek Jackson wrote:
> Hello PorscheFANS!!

> I'm a recent subscriber to the list, having just purchased a 1971 2.2 E Targa
> in very good original condition.  I love the car; however, I have some
> questions about the MFI and its intended operation:
> 
> 1.  When cold and using the fast idle lever, how long must you continue using
> the lever when ambient temps hover around 32 F??

While I don't think my car has ever seen a temperature as low as 32 F,
it's not uncommon for me to have the lever slightly raised after a couple
hours of operation.

> 2.  When cold and coasting to a stop with the clutch pushed in, the engine re
> vs drop to the bottom scale on the tach and the engine dies.  Car restarts
> instantly; however, I find myself  heel/toeing to keep the revs up wile
> braking.  What gives??

You don't have the lever up far enough.  All that lever does is engage a
stop on the throttle cable as it runs back to the motor, so it's
effectively able to do most of the heel/toe for you.

> 3.  Similarly, when warm and coasting to a stop with the clutch pushed in,
> the engine revs drop to the bottom scale of the tach, BUT, the engine
> catches and the revs pop back to a normal idle.  At night with the lights
> on this cause the alternator light to glow briefly before the engine
> catches again.

Yep.  I tend to use that alternator warning light as a "stall indicator",
and feed in a little gas at that point.

> 4.  What adjustments can be made to the system so that the revs drop to
> normal idle instead of bottoming out??

In my experience, simply leaving the lever up a bit should help with most
of these problems.  Then again, my FI pump is far from operating
perfectly so it's possible that a well operating MFI pump can be
convinced not to do such things.
