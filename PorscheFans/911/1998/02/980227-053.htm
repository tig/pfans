Hi James,

When your spoiler isn't coming up at speed have you tried using the manual 
switch on the center console? I'd be trying to get a sense of whether it's 
a power problem or a sensor problem? Do you ever see the spoiler warning 
light in your clock coming on or staying on?

If it's simply a loose connection, or for that matter on a '90 it might be 
a slightly corroded connection, then you might check the wiring harness 
that runs from the back of the firewall behind the airbox to the spoiler 
motor on the lid. I think there are at least two connectors in this harness 
that you can unplug, clean a bit with electrical contact cleaner and 
reseat. One is a multipin plug for the sub-loom, and the other is a smaller 
plug for the motor and the spoiler up/down limit microswitches.

This is from memory, but I'm sure you see it all if you dig a bit in the 
engine compartment.
