Re the fuel-line failures (from lovely US oxygenated gas and so on),
this might interest some owners considering the cost/$-invested in cars.

It's an *automatic* fire-extinguisher which is triggered by direct flame
contact with a hose which breaks down, releasing the extinguishant at
the point source of the fire. It's available in Halon, Du-Pont CFC-ok,
and powder versions. In two-line-out versions for snaking round rear & 
petrol tank areas. About 80ukp/120$US-125ukp/180$US for typical sizes.

No vested interest, just an interesting automatic system versus typical
Sparco/SPA AFFF manual/electric push-button systems. These work (I know)
but aren't automatic and actually cost 50-100% more. Think FIA/SFI etc
approval may be in the wings or available. Just 80$ vs 40-80k$ car with
fuel, oil-lines and that oil-line you rested your elbow on last week ;)
