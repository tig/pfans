Thanks to all who responded to my problem.  What happened was this:  The
first time I pressed the brake pedal after changing the pads, it went to
the floor while the pistons were mating to the new pads.  A couple of pumps
and the pedal was firm again.  Thanks to suggestions by several people, I
disconnected the battery for about 30 sec and the light reset itself.  The
best guess is that when the pedal went to the floor, the pressure in the
two circuits was different for a split second and that triggered the light.

Simple.  Only I did the battery thing in 42 degrees, pouring rain...ugh
