> Here's how Allan Caldwell describes it in the February '98 PNWR Spiel:
>
> After raising the front of the car and removing the front wheels, the
> grease caps on the wheel hubs are removed to gain access to the hub nuts.
> Getting the grease caps off some of the mid range 911s ('75-'85 time
> period) is probably the hardest part of the job since a press fit steel
> cap is used to fit inside the alloy hub edges. The only aid in extracting
> the cap is an indentation that runs around the periphery of the cap. The
> original idea was to place a 3/8 inch  or 1/2 inch pipe in the
> indentation and then strike the pipe with a hammer. An alternative is to
> use a large brass drift against the exterior edge of the indentation at
> several places and drive the cap out or try a large set of water pump
> pliers to lever out the cap. With the cap off, the clamping nut on the
> axle is easily accessed.
