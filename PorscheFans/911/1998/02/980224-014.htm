'92 was one of Porsche's low sales years in the States. Total sales were
only about 4-5k cars, so yours isn't all that rare in the context of the
year overall. $32k is within spitting distance of the general range for
these cars, which are overshadowed by the '95 993s introduced just 2 years
later. Those who can afford it are going for the 993s. Your car will hold
its value better than other marques; just don't expect an investment
windfall from ownership.

Is the dealer in MI also? What's their relationship to the wholesaler? The
inspection should include a compression and leakdown test (done on a warm
engine) to provide some perspective on the engine, especially if it's been
shipped. Excessive valve guide wear is the thing you want to look for. 

Try to get an idea of the clutch condition. You can bet that a "chipped"
car has been run enthusiastically, which is fine if all the needed
maintenance has been done. Have you seen pictures of the car?

3. The car also has an after market exhaust and the "chip".  This scares me
into thinking that the previous owner rode the car pretty hard.  I do not
really care about the extra 10hp that this chip gives me.  Would it be
better for the engine for me to put the original Porsche chip back in?
***************
The "chip replacement basically reprograms the engine control system to
provide greater spark advance and different fuel delivery, favoring
higher-RPM performance over economy, low-end torque, and emissions control.
In a normally aspirated car, you shouldn't see any great wear problems in
52k miles (other than possible valve guides). The aftermarket exhaust
should have been selected with the chip in mind in order to better "tune"
the system. If you're not planning to run real hard (frequent Porsche Club
of America driver's schools, for example) you won't have any problem.
**************

4. I read in Excellence magazine that you should take the engine under-tray
out.  Why would you do this?
************
Remember, these are air-cooled engines. Anything that can increase the flow
of cooling air to the engine will aid in prolonging its life. Removing the
undertray will provide more air to the engine compartment, and also make it
easier to access the underside of the engine for cleaning, oil changes, etc.
************

5. What is your rough guess on yearly maintenance, I plan about 10,000 miles
a year?  
************
My experience has been that a 10,000 mile car will get three oil changes
(11 quarts a clip!) annually (forget the factory manual and change it every
3,000 miles.) Also, plan on one "major" service visit (lube, align,
inspect, set CO, change belts, possible brake pads, etc.) that usually runs
me about $600. Depending on your driving habits, plan on a set of rear
tires every 12-15k miles, and fronts about every 15-20k. BFG Comp T/A ZR
street tires go for about $700 a set mail order, plus mounting.
