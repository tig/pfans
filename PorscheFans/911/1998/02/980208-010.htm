Joe, there's a simpler way to do it than removing major components:

Pop the circlip off the bellcrank pivot shaft, pull the bellcrank out from
the throttle body end of the shaft. Remove all pieces of the old bushing.
Take your new bushing and make a diagonal cut through one side. Open the
bushing and slide it over the shaft. Reseat the pivot, and replace the
outer bushing (intact) and the circlip.

Hope this is clear.
