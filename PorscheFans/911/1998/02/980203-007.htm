>I bought my first Porsche a little over a month ago. It's a red '89 Carrera
>Targa, all stock, very clean, 47K miles. I love it, except for one small
>problem. When idling at normal operating termperature (e.g. sitting at a stop
>light with my foot off the gas), the engine will surge up to 2K RPM for
>several seconds, then drop back down again. Sometimes it'll surge up, drop
>back to 1.2K RPM, surge up again, etc, before finally dropping back
>to normal.
>Anyone know what may be wrong? All leads appreciated!

Not sure whether '89 has the same engine, but I had a similar problem 
with '85 Carrera (3.2 engine). Initially I could solve it by cleaning 
the auxiliary air valve (just spray inside with lubricant such as WD40 
which takes away the carbon deposit) but problem re-appeared. I finally 
sorted it out by replacing the auxiliary air valve (i.e. valve in front 
of throttle body between two rubber hoses). Approx. pnd 90.- in the UK.
