	Good news your Chrysler Jeep dealership parts department sell HD springs
for $35 each. The higher the alphabetical symbol the higher the spring
rate. They have stickers on the coils. My Jeep Grand cherokee with factory
class 3 tow package sagged badly when I hooked it up to the open trailer
with the stock springs-  it looked like a drag car leaving the line. Very
unsafe. With Bilstein shocks ($200 for 4 MCNeil Auto parts in Car & Driver
and Autoweek) and OEM HD coil springs the car handles much better w/o the
trailer and sags less with it.  Also, the spring installation is easy and
fast as the shocks and springs are not coil over but are mounted
separately. A simple satisfying do it yourself project. I dont know about
the HR lowering springs and trailering.
