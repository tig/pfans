Peter.Demeester in Belgium queries the list re buying a late
Carrera:

> What is holding me back from paying for it and driving it home :
>
> 1) it does not have A/C. OK, I live in Belgium, this is not California or
> Florida, but even a little sun in spring will heat up a car's interior to an
> uncomfortable level. As my 911 will be my third car, and thus good weather
> only, I'd like to find out through this list how badly you need A/C in a 911.
> And what the disadvantages are of having one (does it work properly ? does it
> need lots of maintenance ? how well does it age ? (as I am considering cars
> that are 10 years old)).

Don't worry about this.  911 and "climate control" is almost an oxymoron
anyway.  The A/C does not work that well.  Most people who feel they can
survive without it are happy without it.  I took mine out, but probably
would have left it in if it worked better.

> 2) it does have a sun roof. Good for ventilation, but I remember reading
> about leakages. This sun roof is 10 years old, what about the chance of
> it leaking ? How noisy is a 911 sunroof anyway ? (I've driven several
> cars
> that had quiet sunroofs, others were real nuisances due to excessive
> noise levels or sounds) in if it worked better.

Jeeze.  This makes me wonder if a 911 is for you.  This is a level of
granularity re: creature comforts that is out of the domain of most
911 enthusiasts.  Worring if the sunroof might leak is like wondering
if your brakes may eventually need replacing.  A targa is another
story since there is a lot more "seal surface area" to worry about and
perpetual leaking seems to be standard equipment.  The issue of noise
is relative also since a 911 is anything but quiet and refined on
any front.

> 3) it has 9" wide turbo rims at the rear. Great looks, but they do get
> very
> close to the bodywork. And I suppose the 245 tire size does get a little
> more expensive than the 8" 225 sizes commonly used on this model. And
> what about the car's balance, handling ? How is it affected by the use
> of wider wheels and tires at the rear (front are "classic" 7x16 205's).
>
> 4) the previous owner lowered the car to the extreme. Again great looks,
> but am I not better off raising it back to it's normal ride height ?

For a 911 enthusiast this is a dream scenario.  The PO has graciously done
for you what you would most likely want in about 1 year anyway :)
Most people agonize over how to justify the cost of really cool
but essentially unneccessary mods.  Unless you are a purist or someone
who never plans to learn to use the car's performance capabilities
this is the setup you want.  And, you are getting it at essentially no
cost since the car appears to be priced reasonably.

Forget how cool it looks.  Just do this:  Buy the car and get used to
for a while.  Then find a stock one and drive it.  The warm smug feeling
you get will last a long time!  The fact that it looks cool is a happy
coincidence.

This car sounds like a great deal as long as it checks out mechanically.
Make sure you know it's oil consumption habits or if the valve guides
have been fixed.

Good luck and let us know if you buy it!
