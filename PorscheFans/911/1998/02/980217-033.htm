Some observations from my 73 T, 2.4 w/CIS
> 
> Hello everyone,
> I am fairly new on the list having recently bought a decently restored
> 1970 911T Targa about 6 weeks ago (and driven it everyday since). I
> would appreciate any opinions on the following observations I have had
> about the car.
> 
> 1) Now I don't consider myself a "wimp", but....this car is much less
> "driveable" on a daily basis than my '67 912 was...
> 
> I realize the 911T was the detuned, carbureted entry level 911, but it is
> not a fan of stop and go, idling, starting quickly in cold weather
> (California cold, that is) or shot distance commutes. The 912 handled all
> that well...

Same with MFI.
> 
> It also has the "sport" transmission with the dog leg first gear so I spend
> much too much time thinking about what gear I am in...
> 
> I find it to be much more of a single purpose sports car than the 912
> which had more (please pardon me) VW/356-ish driveability.
> 
> 2) The Targa top is extremely noisy. Can the top be adjusted to eliminate
> wind noise? My Haynes 911 manual does not discuss this...

I replaced my door seals and that made a big difference!  Sunroof in mine
so not sure about the targa top.
> 
> 3) The steering is extremely light...It has wider than original Dunlop tires
> and zero play in the steering, but the steering is "power steering" light.
> 
> I understand that this pointed to ball joints in very early 911's but that
> was resolved by 1970. What can this be?

Not sure what you mean by light...I drove a 1990 C2 and thought it was heavy
and no road feel...I have 205/60 tires all the way around and no complaints...
(*and no body mods*)
> 
> 4) It gets an optimistic 15 mpg in a mix of fast street and freeway driving.
> The "seller" (vague to protect the innocent) proclaimed it would get 20+.
> Is this due to short distance driving/cold running? In addition to this the tail
> pipe is fairly black. The "seller" is adamant that the Zeniths are in perfect
> adjustment.

I have similar experience driving in the city of San Francisco...10-15mpg in the
city, 20-25+(highest was 28mpg) on straight fwy driving.
