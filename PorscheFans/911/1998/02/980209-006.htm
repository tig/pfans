I have found the method described by Alan Caldwell in a past Pano article
inexpensive and very effective for 1-person bleeding. Get some lengths of
clear plastic tubing (about 2.5' long) from the hardware store. Its I.D.
should fit snugly over the O.D. of the bleeder screw. Place one end over the
bleeder screw and suspend it vertically somehow, you want the other end up.
Now open the bleeder screw and let gravity allow the brake fluid from the
master cylinder to enter and exit the caliper taking whatever air bubbles
there are with it. The fluid level will rise only to the height of the fluid
in the master cylinder. This part is very visual as you can see the bubbles
rise to the top of the fluid in the tube. Tap the caliper with a metal object
to loosen any bubbles that may cling inside. Using this method you can even
work the brake pedal to speed up the process. Want to replace the fluid? Curve
the end of the tubing over into a can and let it drip out while keeping an eye
on the master cylinder. With the reservoir almost empty, add new fluid .... or
start with a turkey baster and save a few minutes watching the fluid go down.
When you see fresh fluid exiting the caliper, close the screw. You can do all
4 wheels simultaneously or just 1 at a time.
