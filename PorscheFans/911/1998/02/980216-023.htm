Dan:
I would have a repair shop do the fronts.  With the rears, you will need
to secure the shaft from turning by using an allen wrench in the top of
the shock as you turn the nut off of the shaft.  Put some load on the
shock from below with a floor jack...assuming also that you have
loosened the bottom shock first.  
The top is a bitch to get at.  You may need to remove a few bits and
pieces to get to the tops of the shock towers, like the aux fan on the
heater.
Good luck.  Takes patience.
