For what application?  If you want a torque wrench for doing the wheel 
lug nuts, yes, a Sears click-type wrench is fine.  If you are 
rebuilding the engine, get a wand-style wrench - they are cheap, 
accurate, never go out of spec, and let you know continuously what 
torque you're making.

Randy Hubbard had, maybe still does, a great write-up on the subject on 
Raceware's web site http://www.wwnet.com/~raceware/
