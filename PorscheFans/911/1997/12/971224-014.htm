While the S02's are excellent "street tires," if you want maximum tire
performance, then you need to run "R" rated racing tires, like BFG R1's. 
As nice as the S02's are, R1's would walk all over them.  R1's are also
half the price, so even though they wear out faster, they're really not
much more expensive, if at all.  I run R1's year round, and could never
put any street tire on my car, including S02's, after running R1's, and
previously Yokohama 008 RS's, on my sports cars for the past 10 years.
The only problem is if you live back East - not practical to run R1's in the
winter, but then you would want Blizzaks or the like anyway, and not
S02's.
