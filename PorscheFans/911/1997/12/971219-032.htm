Cole,
    I have just completed having my case reworked. The 2.4 should hvae the
squirters already, but there are several things that are important. Have the
shop check the align bore to ensure that everything is straight, and within
spec. Mine had worn somewhat oblong. You might also want to have the mating
surfaces trued, this will make sure everything lines up and help prevent leaks
after assembly. 
    For modifications you may want to get the updated oil bypass. This will
reroute the oil to the pressure side of the pump rather than to the scavage
side which will reduce the strain on your oil pump. 
    I would also make a strong recommendation to have timecerts done for all
the cylinder studs, and for the internal stud near the main bearing. You might
not need it, but if you get it all back together and have a problem ....
    The only other thing I did was give the case a good cleaning, and then give
it a light coat of lubricant to prevent the magnesium from coroding.
    When you recieve the case from whoever does the work make sure you take the
time to clean out all the passages real well and blow them out with compressed
dry air. If not you run the risk of introducing contaminants directly to your
bering surfaces when you start the car. This could be unpleasent to say the
least.
    I should recieve mine any day now. I ended up using Competition Engineering
in CA. I will let you know how it goes.
