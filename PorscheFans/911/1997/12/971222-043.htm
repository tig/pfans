The "H4" really applies to the bulb type, not the headlight assembly.
An H4 is a single halogen bulb providing low and high beam.
Alternatively, two H1 bulbs are needed; one for low and one for high
beam.  The H4 bulb is used in both the Hella and in the so called Bosch
"H4" headlight unit.  The Bosch unit ($250/pair) was standard on euro.
cars, has the skinny trim rings, and looks much cooler.  These are not
simply new trim and lenses, but an entirely new unit that replaces the
US version.  The Hella "H4" ($80/pair) just replaces the sealed beam
bulb of the US unit, retaining the US look with the sunken headlight.
Both replacements use the H4 bulb, but I don't know which, if either,
uses better reflectors or glass for a better beam.

	Doc and Cy's, GPR, and AJ (San Diego) are some who had the Bosch
units at around $250.
