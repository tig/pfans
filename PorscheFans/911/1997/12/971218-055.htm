I just got done doing this swap it a 1970 911, and  on  Dec 27 it ran for
the first time in my car.
First you can not use a 1987 flywheel or clutch because it is far a G50 and
will not fit, you must use a 1984 -1986 flywheel.
Then you need a oil cooler if you car does not have one. I do not know what
you will need to do to the oil system on a 72 911. I had to make the "S"
hose longer and get some fitting made.
You can keep all of you gauges but the oil press sensor will need to be
change or the gauge will not read right.
You also need a return line for the fuel. I also had to make a small dent
on the back of the cross member to fit the fuel pressure regulator.
There are also a lot of thing that some be done when the engine is out.

This is a list of thing that I needed and the cost of parts

From the "school of hard knocks": a couple of years ago my '84 Carrera 
suffered from a dead alternator (failed in manner where dash light didn't come
on, so didn't have a warning).  Driving back from Mid-Ohio at dusk, turned on
 the lights and then started noticing that while sky was getting darker, so
were
 the lights!  Just when I figured the alternator was bad, the car died from
low
voltage; apparently there is a cutoff in the fuel injec. for low voltage.  Let
car 
sit for approx. 1/2 hour, turned key, it cranked and started, made it about 
another 10 miles when the FI quit again.  Finally had to get it towed.

Bottom line: if battery is very low, on at least on a Motronic system, car
will not
run.
