We use essentially the same process tire rack does for heat cycling. Our shop
has a car with a set of heat cycles R1's. The tires have been on for six
months now and have been driven by one to three drivers at five events, which
were autocrosses except for one two day time trial. The car took top time of
day at our autocross last weekend. The tires were hooking up great. Lack of
better brakes was our biggest problem, which we'll improve on by our next
event in February.

By the way, the tires still have tread left to start next season. Until they
start loosing grip we'll keep driving them.

If you've read the BFG report on heat cycling you'll see that their tests
included tires heat cycled by several different methods. Their testing
concluded that ANY heat cycling process achieved the same result. The key was
a minimum 24 hour curing period after the FIRST heat cycle of the tire after
manufacture. They also determined that curing over 48 hours had no effect on
the process.

There sure is a lot of conflicting info running around about tires. We're
trying to keep accurrate info on our customers and our own tire wear and
stick, especially among different brands.
