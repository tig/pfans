The rear speakers are easy to get out. The speaker grills unscrew, and the
screws holding them are also holding down the speakers. After removing the
screws, use a braod-blade putty kniffe to gently separate the grill edges
from the vinyl upholstery. Be careful, they do not want to part company.
The sun shining through the rear window ages them and they get very
brittle. The sun also ages the factory speaker cones, and they tend to
disintegrate when touched.

What is not so easy is getting decent replacement speakers in there. Using
the stock holes leaves you with only a choice among 4x6 speakers. 6x9s can
be made to fit if you use a build-up plate to raise them up an inch or so.
Larger than that requires an enclosure such as those from Moch One in CA.

Interestingly, the factory wiring harness can be re-used for most
applications. The wires aremarked where they go into the dash unit, so you
can ID them easily.

Good luck!
