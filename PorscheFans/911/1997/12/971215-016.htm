Just a few words regarding your post -
In a "wanna-be 911 owner"'s quest for a 911, take the cautionary stance that
what you think you see, may not be what you get.  If you are buying from an
owner who has reciepts and appears to have knowledge of what has been done to
the car while he/she owned it, and hopefully the same knowledge level of
previous owners, you are in much better shape.  What I wanted to get across
is that a previous owner may have installed either earlier or later versions
of the chain tensioners.  The later versions - beginning in 1984 - called
"Carrera tensioners" or pressurized tensioners, are obvious on inspection of
the chain cover area of the engine.  Earlier tensioners can only be
identified by removal of the chain covers.  Pre-1980 tensioners will fit
post-1980 911, so the VIN number alone won't guarantee what you will find
inside the engine, or other well-hidden places on your new purchase.  Of
course, I don't have any magic methods to uncover these hidden secrets, just
use good judgement, and other tactics I'm sure will be suggested to you by
other P-fans.  Using the chain tensioner criteria alone may steer you away
from a good buy in an earlier car which could have the later tensioners
installed.
