Jim,

You were quite correct in pointing out the warning about disconnecting the battery on a running car.  I know from talking to automotive electriccal designers in a previous job that this is a major problem in a car.  New cars have their electronics designed to withstand what is called an alternator dump.  If memory serves, this is a 400-600 volt spike on the ground (chassis) of the system.

In older cars, this will not only take out the alternator but will randomly go through the car destroying pieces of electrical gear.  I understand that it is a real mess to debug and fix everything.
