I used to be paranoid about my cars and bikes pulling to right, since I
definitely convinced myself the most common cause is the road crown.
Obviously you must be able to discriminate between that and a bad
alignment. The road induced pulling is always very smooth and progressive
(similar to a lateral wind), where the bad alignment induced could be
compared to a "spring" effect  (stronger and faster). I experienced also a
strong lateral pulling effect for the combined effect of braking, power
steer, ABS and a badly rough road surface, in my 964 turbo. A car severely
pulling to the left for LHD must be considered with more attention (after
routine suspension and tire checks  you can also consider a frame alignment
check)
