I don't think that's quite right.  The O2 sensor only operates
correctly if it's at a very high temperature, around 650 deg F, if I
remember correctly.  Two wire sensors are made to be used right up in
the manifold where the exhaust gases are hot enough.  The heater in the
three wire sensors allow them to be used used anywhere in the exhaust
system, even in the tailpipe where the gases are relatively cool.  It's
not an emission requirements issue.
