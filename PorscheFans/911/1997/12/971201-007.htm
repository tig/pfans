Tom,

You'll need to remove the two eccentric bolts to adjust the spring plate position on the torsion bars.  As soon as you do that, you'll need an alignment again.

As a trick that has gotten me through the short (very short) term, you can scribe a line on the spring plate around the arm.  When you put everything back together, you can spend an hour or two getting the arm back within the lines.  I've done this and driven the car for a week or two without noticing any difference in the handling.  Tire wear would be another subject.  I put on less than 100 miles before getting it aligned and did not notice any unusal wear patterns.
