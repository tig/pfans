On Fri, 12 Dec 1997, John Helgesen wrote:

> Anyway, I need to charge the battery back up, if it isn't shot.  It's a 750
> amp battery and I've got a 10 amp "automatic" charger I picked up at Sams.  I
> know when I charge Nicad batteries I charge them 1.5 times their rating (ie a
> 1000mh battery would be charged with a 100mh charger for 15 hours).  Does this
> work for car batteries, or should I just charge it for about 7.5 hours.  My
> fear is not charging the battery enough, then cooking my alternator when I
> start the car.

Your "automatic" charger should switch to a trickle charge as the 
battery reaches the correct charge - you can't overcharge. However, 
with a 10amp charger, you should be able to get the battery to full 
charge pretty quickly (I'm guessing about 20 min). The 750amp rating 
means something considerably different than the 1000mh rating on a 
NiCad. If you've got the time, just throw the battery on the charger 
and let it go for a few hours.
