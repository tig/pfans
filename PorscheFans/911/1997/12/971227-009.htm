At Trackside Products in San Diego, we've sold many R1's and AO32's for street
or street/track use. Pat Norris, a 944 driver, just ran the Starlight Rally, a
12-hour overnight rally from Santa Barbara to Las Vegas, on a set of A032's.
He was extremely happy. Although a little noisy, the grip was fantastic. One
point he was stopped on a patch of black ice. There were SUV's in front and
behind him. The road was a little off camber. When the line of traffic started
to move, both the SUV's slid sideways into the ditch on the side of the road
(no damage). His 944 slid a tad sideways and just drove off the ice. He's very
happy.

The AO32 looks like a better rain tire. I've had several people (including
myself) who like the R1 in the rain. I personally haven't driven the AO32 in
wet, but my customers are very happy. Comparison between the two in the wet? I
don't know. My gut says the AO32 is a better compromise tire.

As far as running tires on both street and track..... I used to wear out my
A009RS's on the outside edges (it was a compromise street/track suspension
setting). On the street the wear was more in the center of the tire. I enjoyed
the stick of the "R" tires around town and also enjoyed not changing tires two
to four times a month. Maybe I'm just lazy though.....
