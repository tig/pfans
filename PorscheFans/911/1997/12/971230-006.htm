Out of curiosity, were the manuals in the dark red binders with 4 hole
punching and printed (copied) on glossy paper?  If so, they were probably
factory.  If not, probably knock-offs.  The photos in the factory ones
sometimes look like they've been copied a few too many times.  You might try
microfiche because the price is MUCH cheaper, usually $15-30 a set if I
remember correctly although the quality is not great for some of the older
cars.  You'd save enough to buy the reader and some parts fiche also!   Buy
the stuff through a dealer that advertises in Pano or Excellence and ask for a
their published discount (20 or 25 percent for some).
