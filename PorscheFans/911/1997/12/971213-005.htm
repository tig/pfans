NiCad batteries have a lot of quirks like "memory" and (potentially)
thermal runaway.  Lead-acid car batteries are much lower tech, and don't
need much special care.

As far as frying the alternator, don't worry.  The battery acts as either a
load or [possibly] a voltage reference.  It can accomplish either without
being nearly fully charged.  As long as the battery can start the car,
you're safe.
