several students out in my car on pretty well worn R1's, and it did
surprisingly well.

Actually, I've had no such problems, and as I noted previously, I've been
running these tires on the street for 10 years.  The reason your concern
above is not germain is because driving on the street, the tires don't go
through "heat cycles" like they do at the track.  Just driving around, even
hitting an on-ramp every so often never gets them up to the kind of temps
they see on the track, that would be considered a heat cycle.  I know it's
not an issue because I take the car to the track at least twice a year,
when I run my driver training event, and I observe that there is little
difference in my lap times between fresh R1's and R1's that are toward
the end of their life, after having been driven on the street for about 6
months.
