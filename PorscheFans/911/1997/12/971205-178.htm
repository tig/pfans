Replacing the O2 sensor is easy. From inside the engine compartment,
unplug the O2 sensor, on the left side between the CD box and the fuel
filter, and push the wires and grommet down through the engine sheet
metal. Next, jack up the driver's side rear and remove the LR wheel. You
can then see the O2 sensor sticking up from the catalytic converter.
Spray a little penetrating oil on it and allow it to work, then unscrew
the old sensor. Install the new sensor in reverse order.

If you don't reset the counter behind the speedometer, when the counter
reaches 30,000 miles (or multiples), it turns on the red O2 sensor light
on the dash, which you might find annoying. It's easy to reset the
counter. I use a stiff putty knife or scraper, with a small piece of
wood (paint stirrer) to protect the dash, and carefully pry the speedo
out. It is held in only by the rubber trim ring. After the speedo is
out, shine your flashlight in there and you will find a small black box
connected to the speedo cable. The little white button on it is what you
want to push to reset.
