William Cline  wrote:

> There is no reason to downshift into any gear that you do not intend
> to promptly use.  On the track or the street.
> ....
> If you need third gear, brake until the speed of the car matches a
> third gear speed, then change gears into third.  Double clutch if
> you must, but there is no reason to go through any other gears to
> get there if you are more than one away, like 5th.

    William:

    Your gearbox will be perfectly happy shifting directly from 5th
    to 3rd so long as you properly match engine speed to road
    speed... But that doesn't mean that there's "no reason" to shift
    into 4th on the way down.

    I vaguely recall writing a message on the subject a couple of
    days ago... Did you miss the message, miss the points I was
    making, or just not agree with any of those points?

> Using the engine to slow the car is nuts and a waste of clutch
> material. Brake pads are cheaper.

    Um... Excuse me?

    I agree that using engine compression to slow the car is a bad
    idea, but how does it put wear on the clutch?

> It may sound and feel racy to go through all those gears, but it is
> a waste of time....

    Yes, it does "sound racy", but if it's done properly, it
    shouldn't "feel" ANY different than skipping gears... The whole
    point is to make the downshifts in such a way that the car's
    balance is unaffected.

> .... and puts unneeded wear on your gearbox and adds wear to your
> clutch which you do not need.

    Downshifting -- properly -- through the gears puts essentially
    no extra wear on the gearbox, and I still have no idea what your
    reference to clutch wear means.
