Garth_Gregoire@capgroup.com  wrote:

> Can anybody tell me the difference in weights between a 2.4 liter
> (71') engine and a 2.7 liter engine?

    Garth:

    I don't know exactly (although I'm sure someone else has this
    information), but it's not much... A few pounds at most.

> Will this aded weight significanly effect handling?

    No.

> Will I need to upgrade the suspension system significantly?

    Not for the weight difference.  You might, of course, want to
    modify your suspension to deal with the increased power.

> Lastly, has anyone built and run the 2.7 liter engine Bruce
> Anderson describes in his book "Porsche 911 Performance Handbook"? 
> I am speaking of the engine using the 95 mm European Carrera RS
> pistons with a 74 or earlier exhaust.  (Page 141)

    Must be page 141 in the revised second edition; my first-edition
    copy puts the horsepower/torque graph for that engine on page
    131.

    Anyway, the 2.7 Euro Carrera RS pistons are 90 mm in diameter,
    not 95... 95-mm pistons were used in the 3-liter cars.

    The engine Bruce describes has 2.7 Euro Carrera RS pistons and
    cylinders, E cams, Weber 40s, and the early exhaust with sport
    muffler; it makes 225 peak HP and 200 pound-feet of torque.

    The engine in my car is similar, although not identical... It has
    S cams and a different exhaust (the 911 exhaust, of course,
    doesn't fit in a 914), and it makes 250 HP and 220 pound-feet of
    torque.

    It's a great engine; the powerband is real wide, so it's easy to
    drive on the street.

> I'd be interested in any additional comments about it.  I'm
> seriously considering buying a 2.7 block and making this my first
> rebuild, and possibly (eventually) dropping it into my 71' 911T.

    Try to find a "7R" magnesium case... As Bruce says in his book,
    they're the best for building any engine up to 2.8 liters.

> Will I need to upgrade to a 915 transmision for this?

    I don't know... If you were putting the engine in a 914, I'd say
    no, but the 911s are heavier and may overstress your 901
    transmission.  You'd have to ask a 911 guy about this.
