	I'm not sure why you're blowing off your mechanic's idea of
something binding in the tunnel.  I suggest taking a careful look and
listen in the tunnel and begin eliminating causes and isolating the
problem.  For instance, try disconnecting the cable from the pedal-does
this make a difference?  If not, then you've isolated the problem to the
pedal assembly.  Continue this process for each component along the line
until you've cornered the problem.  Seems like you're trying to
eliminate the problem by replacing parts when you haven't identified the
cause, only the symptom.  This can prove very frustrating and expensive
and should be your last option.  Sorry for the sermon.
