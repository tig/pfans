Nick,
  I just thought I'd add my 2 cents to Bobs wisdom:

>2) You might want to have a wooden or aluminum rod of the right diameter to
>tap out the wristpins.
I heated the piston up using a heat gun. The theory is that is would
expand the hole piston. Seemed to help the wrist pin come out easier. 
When you get around to putting them back in, put the wrist pins in the 
freezer first. They slide right in then. (Applied physics!)
When removing the circlips that hold the wrist pins in, it's a good idea 
to put a rag over the hole(s) in the case. That way if the circlip goes 
ballistic it can't fly into the case.

>3) I find it real useful to have a small slide hammer to help get the
>rocker shafts out. You can make one pretty easily.  The shafts tend to
>stick in the cam housing, and it can be awkward trying to tap them out with
>a screwdriver.  Maybe someone else has a better idea.
I started at one end and used a long 3/8" socket extension (wrapped in 
masking tape) to tap the shafts out.  Those shaft bores tend to have crud 
in them. If you don't _really_ clean them out before you remove the shafts, 
the crud will scratch the bores leading to leaks. Then you need to resort 
to using those little O-rings on the rocker shafts. Don't ask me how I
know...
When you put the shafts back, start at one end and use the long extension
with a torque wrench. Failure to torque those nuts properly can allow
the shafts to move around and wreck your cam carrier. Again, don't ask me 
how I know...

>4) You'll need an appropriate set of hex wrenches to undo the gizmos that
>hold the rocker shafts in.  I think one is 8mm, the other 6mm, but check.
>For the larger one, I bought a simple 'L' shaped hex wrench and shortened
>the shorter side so that it would fit in the available space.  For the
>smaller hex wrench it's real nice to have a short, stubby unit that fits on
>a 1/4" drive.  If you can't find one -- Snap-on has them--you can always
>make do with a cut down hex wrench.  All this will be much clearer when you
>look at what needs to be undone.
Automotion sells the cutest little 6mm (?) hex drive for this. Its short and 
you can get it in there and turn it with your fingers.
