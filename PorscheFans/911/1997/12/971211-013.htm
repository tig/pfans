Yes, that's correct, if it's the same as my '87.  I recently installed an
interior light turn-off delay, which comes with instructions that describe this
method of removing the interior light (you install the delay unit behind the
light).  There are some spring-loaded clamps you have to overcome; once you
know where they are (I have already forgotten) you'll know where to apply the
force to pry the light out.  I used a medium-bladed screwdriver to gain
reasonable leverage without any damage to the plastic light enclosure, and it
just "popped out" when I reached the right force.
