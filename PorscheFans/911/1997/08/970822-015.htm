This is similar to a situation covered in the August '97 issue of Panorama (page 86) in Bruce
Anderson's Tech Q&A.

The oil pressure idoit light switch is used as a source for checking voltage for the brake circuit. 
To quote; "When the oil pressure switch goes bad it not only turns on the the idiot light for the
oil pressure, but also the brakes.  Put in a new idoit light switch.  That  is the one at the rear end
of the engine (the end with the flywheel) up by the engine breather back there."  The article
goes on to caution that a brake warning should be attended to until you are sure that you have
found the problem.

Hope this does the trick.
