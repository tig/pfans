In my own case, a seized doorstop led to busting the doorjamb--the
depressed part where the doorstop bolts in.  If your door isn't
staying in the intermediate places, but you can feel them somewhat,
you need a new door stop.  If your doorstop sticks and, all of a
sudden you hear a crack, you've broken the sheet metal.  And you need
a new doorstop.

Fortunately, there's an easy fix that doesn't show.  A piece of thick
sheet metal can be cut to act as a brace, inserted crosswise in the
(now cracked) depression that holds the doorstop.  Automotion sells a
repair kit that uses the same principle.
