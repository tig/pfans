I took all the parts off my 1970, and found a lot of minor rust spots that
would have ruined a new paint job.  This is on a relatively rust-free
example.  If you are sure there are no lurking rust spots, you can tape and
spray with good results.  The job will last a lot longer if you spend more
time getting ready.  The UV and humidity are intense in Jacksonville.  Be
sure to specify a good brand of paint.  Cheap stuff looks great the day you
pick the car up, but will leave you miserable within two years.  My Chronar
job still looks great eight years after the paint job.  Will probably go
another ten years before needing serious attention.  Well worth the extra
money.  BTY, my Florida friends with Porsches report that most shops are
rip-offs.  Shop carefully.
