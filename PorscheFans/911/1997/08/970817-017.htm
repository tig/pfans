> What is a "heat sink"?

Josh:

The word "sink", in this context, is used as the opposite of
"source"; a heat sink is just a device that absorbs (and then
dissipates) heat.

Ever seen the black, finned aluminum extrusions on the rear or sides
of audio-amplifier cases (or other pieces of high-power electronics
gear)?  Those are heat sinks, as are the little black U-shaped things 
that you may have seen bolted to the back of voltage reguilators or 
snapped around the processor inside your PC.
