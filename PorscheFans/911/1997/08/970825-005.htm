Concerning a recent inquiry about high oil temperatures on an early
911, one of the first checks should be the ignition timing.  Incorrect
timing can have a significant effect on operating temperatures.  The
spark on my '73 was retarded and I did not know it.  After installation
of a fender Earl's unit and correction of the timing, it reaches 200
only on the track, where it used to reach 250 in summer traffic here in
Houston.
