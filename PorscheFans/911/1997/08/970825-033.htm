    There could be a number of reasons why you are seeing such a wide
variation in pad thickness.
    The first thing I would check is the the flexible brake lines.
Deterioration of the inside of the lines will cause the pads to drag, eve=
n
though the pistons are retracting.
    Remove the pads and check the angle of the " step " on the pistons.
This is put there to cause the pads to contact in the desired manner. If
the pistons become rotated, the pads can wear unevenly.
    Calipers can be disassembled and new piston seals installed. It's a
nasty job, but you can be sure everything is clean. A buildup of crud
around the top of the cylinder can cause piston drag or hangup.
    For brake bleeding, use a system which relies on putting air pressure=

on the system. I use an adaptor which connects to a garden pressure
sprayer. Pump up the sprayer, connect the hose to the overflow tube on th=
e
reservoir. Place a piece of plastic bag under the cap to block the vent.
Pull the trigger on the sprayer and crack the bleeder. The air pressure
will push the fluid through.
