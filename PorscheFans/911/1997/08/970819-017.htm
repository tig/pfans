A response to this post noted that the body should be primed before new
undercoating is applied. I would go further, and recommend that at least
some (1-2 coats) finish paint be applied.

The reason is that most auto primer is porous, and does not provide a
watertight seal between the metal and the air. Although the underbody
coating will cover the primer and is waterproof, it's still the only line
of defense against water. Paint it, then undercoat it, and you'll have put
belt over suspenders.
