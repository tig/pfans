Brian Henry's question about color change is a perennial one.  Some
people care more than others about such matters, but a changed color,
unless it's done right as part of a ground-up restoration from a bare
body shell, will lower the value of a car.  This is just by virtue of
the fact that it will turn off a percentage of potential buyers and
hence reduce demand and therefore value.  If you don't care and the
car is otherwise sound, you can use this as a bargaining edge.

Just remember that the old color always shows somewhere in a
run-of-the-mill job.  If I owned a shop, I'd refrain from doing jobs
like this, since there's a good chance the customer won't be happy
when all is said and done.
