> Some possibly stupid questions about my '88 Targa's wheels:
> 
> The wheel rims are polished (chromed?  Is there a difference?) to a
> mirror-like finish.  The raised parts of the spokes are too.  The
> inset portions are black.  The center caps are also polished, and the
> crests are painted.
> 
> The questions:
> -These wheels are original.  Are they Fuchs?

They could be original Fuchs.  The easiest way to tell is
to remove them and look for a Porsche part number stamped
on the back.  Or the Fuchs emblem (a little fox in a
triangular border)

> -Was the polishing original, or would this have been done after the
> car left the factory?  I have no record of wheel work, so I was
> assuming they factory polished, but I've never seen any other 911s
> like this.

I can almost guarantee the polished or chrome finish is
not original.  Most Fuchs wheels came with matt finish
and solid black spokes.  There are exceptions like the
early unpainted spokes and the body color matched wheels.

> -Is polishing the same as chroming?

No.  Polishing is just that, buffing the aluminum until it
is mirror smooth and shiny.  Chroming is usually a process
of electrically depositing a layer of shiny metallic alloy
(don't know a typical composition, but assume it contains
chromium) to the surface of the item.

> -One of the center caps is missing.  How do they attach/detach?

They usually just pop in/out.  Although, you can get lock
rings for them.

> -I might want to trade these wheels for original equipment black
> anodized wheels.  Condition being equal, are the polished ones worth
> more than the black ones?  What are the prices of each type?

That's an interesting question because it depends on you
point of view and starting point.  If you have an original
Fuchs, then polishing adds a level of work and cost.  If you
have a rough wheel that you want refinished to original, then
(according to Wheel Techniques in Cambell, CA) it must be
polished first, then anodized, then painted, so it costs more.
