Cory Acuff wrote:
> 


> As explained to me, the increase in pressure as tires warm comes from the
> moisture in the air within the tires.  As the moisture warms it expands
> (steam).  This causes the increase in tire pressure.  Since bottled N2
> contains no moisture, filling the tires with N2 alleviates this effect.

Moisture conent will certainly effect tire pressures. The least
expensive nitrogen gas is not dry. If you pay for nitrogen gas for your
tires, make sure it is the more expensive dried gas. We have had
scientific aparatus freeze up when we used inexpensive bottled "pure"
nitrogen gas in very cold conditions.
