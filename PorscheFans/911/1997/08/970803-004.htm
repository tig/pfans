In response to a post on matching engine serial numbers to specific year's
chassis, the following may be of general help. PCA offers "The Porsche Family
Tree", a small pamphlet, for a nominal amount of money ( $5, I recall) that
goes through the whole model year range from 1948 to 1995. It lists the kind
of info we're after. The pocket size "Technical spec Books" for specific
model year ranges, give the same info, at about $10-12, for a smaller range
of models.
For 1974 911( Coupe) engine numbers are 6140001-6146625.
For Targa /Sportomatic- 6149001-6149517
For 911S-Coupe- 6340001-6342804
For 911S-Targa/ Sportomatic- 6349001-6349236
For Carrera-Coupe- 6340001-6340169
For Carrera Targa- (No list)
Matching series of chassis numbers are also given, but I assume the
interested parties have this part of the puzzle already. The above list comes
from the "Family Tree" book. To confuse the issue, the Tech/Spec book lists
this in a different and confusing way. It simply says 1974= 911- 6140001 to
6149000 (Sportomatic 6149001 to 6149999). For 911S, 6340001 to 6349999. For
Sportomatic, 6349001 to 6349999. No listing under Carrera. The code can be
deciphered as  first digit =6 cyl, second digit = engine type ( with 1=911,
and 3=911S),  next digit should be 4 (for 1974), and the remainder  is the
consecutive serial number. 
Hope it helps.
