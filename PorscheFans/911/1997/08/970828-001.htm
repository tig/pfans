Denny-

I commute in my 1992 911 Turbo S2 everyday on the DC beltway. I love the
car as a daily driver, but you have to be a little bit crazy to do this.
The 911 turbo is more expensive to maintain than a normally aspirated
variant. In addition, it requires more concentration to drive than your
standard automatic sedan - I never drive it if I am really exhausted or
even the slight bit inebriated.

Why would you want a 'turbo look' without a turbo inside?
