Collier,

I have a 1987 coupe with the Blaupunkt Reno SQR that you're talking of.  I
have recently replaced the front door speakers with Boston Acoustic units,
and I must say the sound difference is NIGHT and DAY! ... I'm not a
proponent of installing amps and all of the nifty gadgets, in fact, I would
rather have no radio and enjoy the sound of the car on spirited drives in
the country.  I am planning to replace the rear speakers and want to get a
set of 4x6" Boston Acoustics, but they do not sell the grill assemblies and
you must use the crummy stock units instead.

As far as the CD play goes, I would go with a single disc in car head unit.
 If you really like your music, a 6-10 disc changer in the trunk might be a
better option for you.  Rule of thumb for me is not to get carried away
with the audio stuff in the 911.

I'm satisfied with the sound and plan to keep the Reno to make it as
'original' as possible.
