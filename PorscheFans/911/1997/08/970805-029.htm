Way I seem to remeber is to jack up one rear corner (i.e, only one rear
tire on ground), put car in neutral, then try to turn wheel in air.  If you
can
turn it, you don't have limited slip.  Hope this is right!

Thre is an easire way by checking the codes on the label on the trunk
lid, but forget the LTD code.

Yes, you can change the oil yourself.  The biggest problem I found is getting

the new fluid in.  I used a long section of garden hose, had one end stuck
in tranny filler, ran other end thru rear wheel well and elevated it, then
slowly
added fluid via a funnel until it overflowed out the filler hole.

One hint:  untighten the filler plug first, then the drain.  if you drain the
oil 
then find out you can't get the filler plug out, it's flatbed time!
