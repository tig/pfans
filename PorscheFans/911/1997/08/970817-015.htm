A heat sink dissipates thermal energy. A heat sink usually has extended
surfaces sticking out from it, also called fins, to aid in the heat
transfer from whatever it is you're trying to cool to its surrounding
environment. The tubes Bruce Anderson is describing above are the extended
surfaces that will transfer thermal energy from the oil to the air so as
to cool the oil. If you look at old BMW motorcycles you'll see fins
sticking out of the boxer engine covers. If you open up your computer
you'll see funny looking things stuck to the top of the bigger chips.
Those are heat sinks...

To be precise, I don't think you can call the whole oil cooler a heat
sink, you can however call the tubes heat sinks...
