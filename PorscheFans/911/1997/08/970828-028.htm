Sanden used to call themselves Sankyo.  They make numerous AC compressors. 
All are swash-plate designs.  The model number seems to reflect the number
of cylinders.  Interestingly, the small units are more expensive.  My 1980
Mercedes came with a Sankyo 508.  Lasted 17 years.  I am putting a Sanden
507 on my 1970 911T.  They make a very small unit, the 505, which tempted
me since it is very light weight.  I was afraid I would regret it on a hot
Atlanta Summer day.

Sanden compressors are well-engineered, leak-free, smooth operating, and
durable.  You can also find them at a very good price (I payed $140 for the
507 three years ago.  I would recommend the 507 for a Porsche.  The 508
will cool any full-size car.  One last thing - they come with a bracket
that makes for easy exhange for the York four-bolt mounting system.  Makes
switching over as simple as replacing the York compressor with a new one.
