Tony:

The rule of thumb is:  When the engine's warm, the oil pressure 
should read 10-15 psi per 1000 RPM.

"70-80 mph in 5th gear" with a stock transmission works out to what?
3000 RPM or so?  I don't know, since my car has short gears.  Anyway, 
if it IS 3000 RPM, you should see 30-45 psi (or 2-3 bar, if that's 
how your gauge is calibrated).

If you never see less than 5 bar (approximately 70 psi) when the
engine's warm, something's wrong... Either the sender's faulty or
badly grounded, or the gauge is faulty, or your gauge and sender
aren't properly matched to each other.

-Andy
