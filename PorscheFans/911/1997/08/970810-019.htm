A trick I was taught years ago to keep from having to remove windows and 
avoiding the tell tale painted tip on the rubber trim involves a bit of time, 
but is effective.  Using a credit card lift the trim away from the sheet metal 
and stuff heavy cord or butchers' string up under the rubber.  This is to 
create some clearance between the metal and the rubber.  Then when masking 
wrap the tape around the tip of the rubber trim.  You can now sand the paint 
under the rubber and achieve a better than average repaint job.  The back 
window of a Targa presents some additional challenges, perhaps someone else 
has first hand experience with this.
