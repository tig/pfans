"William S.Cline"  writes:

> My adjustable Koni's fitted to my SC have mutely raised a question.  Just
>what setting is full stiff?
>As some of you know, this strain of shocks on the rear can only be adjusted
>if you take the darn things off and turn the center rod with an allen
>wrench.  The settings are from 0 to 3, with clicks that you can feel as you
>turn them.
>
>I always thought that 3 was full stiff, being the end of a full clockwise
>turn.  But, the other day I adjusted the fronts to stiff and (click) it
>dawned on me that their adjustment to full stiff was counter clockwise...so
>should not the rears be the same?

Full stiff on the rears is the "3" position (full clockwise).  My rears are the
type with the small "adjusting button" - I don't remember the "center rod
with an allen wrench" adjustent, but I assume the stiffness adjustment
is the same.

They are a PITB to adjust.  I set mine to "2" if I remember right.  It seems
there is enough weight in the rear to run them pretty stiff without affecting
the ride much.  R1s and the fronts on full stiff are great for the track -
but that
causes my vertebrae to rattle like castenets, so I have to set the fronts
to full soft for street use...
