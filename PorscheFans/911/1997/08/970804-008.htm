We replsced teh front pan on a '68 911 targa about 20 years ago. You're
right, it's a body shop job, and be sure they have a frame jig to maintain
the alignment. As well as the pan, you might as well replace the front
cross member, a formed sheet metal piece that attaches to the pan. Any part
of it near the battery will be affected by the leakage, too.

Costs: Well, these are 20 years old, but the pan part was about $225, the
crossmember another $75, and the body shop bill was around $300, I think. 

Good luck with the job, the early 911s are real sweet cars.
