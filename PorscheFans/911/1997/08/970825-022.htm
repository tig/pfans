that, since I would eventually replace the engine with something newer 
preferably a 3.2L,  putting in pressure fed tensioners would be a waste
of 
money, besides the 930 tensioners worked fine for 15 years.  I am
beginning to 
regret that decision.  He replaced the O-rings in the original
tensioners and put 
it all together. I put the engine and trans. back in the car started it

engine) it lasted about 5 min.  he purchased a new tensioner put it in -
same 
thing.  He ran the engine with the cover off to look at the way it all
worked as it 
ran, it looked OK.  He bought another new tensioner put it in and all
seemed 
fine, until yesterday.  All these problems have been with the right
side, the left 
side has worked fine. I don't want to put a lot of money in to a 80
thousand mile 
2.7L engine, I would rather save money to buy a 3.0 or 3.2 in a year or
two.
