"Holliker, Gene"  writes:

>     From prior posts on this list, I knew to look at the front sway bar
>when my '79 930 started making the squeaking bed spring sound. Unlike what
>others have found, my sway bar was not broken, but one of the bushings is
>bad. The prior owner had a lot of suspension mods done years ago but cannot
>remember whether bigger sway bars were installed (duh!). So, my questions
>are:
>
>     1. Did the '79 930 come with the 22mm front sway bar that is on mine?

I think the '79 came with a 20m bar from the factory.

>     2. If not, what years use a 22mm?

I don't know.  Carreras starting using 22mm front bars in '86, I believe.

>     3. Should I use stock or aftermarket bushings?

Depends on your application a little.  If you want all-out performance,
use the hard polyurethane bushings.  Otherwise the stick rubber ones
are OK.

>     4. Where would be a good place to get them?

Stock bushings can be had from the dealer (I've bought them from
Stoddard's in the past).  Most mail-order places have the poly ones.

>     5. What should be used to lube the bushings? The Haynes Manual says
>     to use  glycerine.

Glycerine's probably OK on the rubber ones.  I've used regular bearing
grease on the rubber ones and I'm fond of that Teflon based lube that
Tweeks, et al sells for the poly bushings.
