>There are a couple of takes on this topic.
>
>First, a leak down of 5% or less is usually accepted as good (2-3% is
>really good).  If the leak down numbers are better than this cold, they
>should only get better as the engine warms up.  One of the problems in
>doing a leak down on a hot engine is pulling the plugs out of a hot
>aluminum head.  Usually considered a no no.
>
>Second, there are stories (Bruce Anderson) of engines that have sat for
>extended periods of time (years) that had poor leak down numbers.  After
>the engines had been run a couple of thousand miles the leak down numbers
>were acceptable.
>
>Generally, we like to use leak down as a guide to engine condition.  If you
>check your specs on a annual basis you can get a good idea on the condition
>of the "seal" of the cylinders.
