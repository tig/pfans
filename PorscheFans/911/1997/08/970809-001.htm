Michael, strongly suggest you buy a shop manual fro the 911.  Factory
is the best, but expensive.  There is a company called "Haynes" that
to me puts out the best manual, and they are relatively inexpensive. I
would think get them, read up, then decide what else you might need.

Valve adjustment isn't too hard, but you need a special tool to measure
the valve clearance (regular gap aguge won't fit in confines of engine).
Biggest probelm is access and vision; hard to see & get at all valves.

Shock replacement isn't bad in back, but in the front you need a special
tool or a big hefty pipe wrench to unscrew the strut insert (fits inside
the strut housing which is the part you see).
