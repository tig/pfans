Chris Ketchum, c/o Walter Fricke  wrote:

> I have seen and ordered the ATE SuperBlue fluid, but recently noted
> that ATE makes two kinds of brake fluid.  Both have the same
> boiling temp, but one is DOT 3-4 approved.  Does this mean
> anything to me in a street car setup, or should Super Blue only be
> used for racing applications with SS brake lines?

    Chris:

    The two fluids have identical specs; the main difference is the
    color (Super Blue is blue, natch, and Type 200 is amber).

    Most people who use ATE fluid alternate between the two, so they
    can easily tell when all the old fluid's been flushed out of the
    system by observing the color change.

    -Andy

    P.S.  Stainless-steel brake lines, by the way, are no more or
          less compatible with the various brake fluids than any 
          other brake lines... I can't think of a single example of a 
          brake fluid that likes Teflon-lined stainless-steel hose 
          more than rubber hose.
