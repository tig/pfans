Vic, I would think you might have a case of the pistons maybe hanging up 
in the calipers due to dirt or wear.  Check the rubber piston gaskets; are
these
bad? (I believe your car has them; my '76 did).  You can try "exercising"
the pistons to see if that frees them up; put in the worn pads, pump brake
pedal till pistons fully extend and actuate the brake, pull pads out , push
back
pistons with a pry bar, reinstall old pads, then redo exercise.  Doing this
two or three times should free up the pistons.

Can also drive the car moderately on the street, usnig the brakes as little 
as possible, then stop and feel each wheel center.  If the brakes are
dragging
in the LR, this wheel should feel noticeably warmer than the others.
