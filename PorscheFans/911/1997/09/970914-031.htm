In case you're interested, SCCA rules don't allow any holes to be
made in body, chassis or interior to install -- save holes for bolts:

http://www.best.com/~mouton/sccasolo/car_class_stock_rules.html#s135

This bar goes through the area where the early 911 anti-roll bar went.
Unfortunately, newer cars (77 and later) have the boss for mounting,
and a nice clear area to put it in -- but no holes.  

I got the adapter kit installed, holes and all -- in about two hours.
Instructions are pretty clear and not too difficult.  You'll have to
grind the original anti-roll bar mounts from the A-arms to mount the 
U-Tabs.  Luckily I found out about the SCCA rule before I started
grinding.  The bar looks great -- VERY sturdy hardware.  The bar ends
are adjustable -- the upper end of the link slides in and out on the 
bar ends.  And, yes, I can just plug the holes and compete.

I feel a little silly, since I maintain the SCCA car classification
rules on my web site -- but I didn't see this in time.
