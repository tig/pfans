------- Chris Bennet asked -------

  >...could someone offer a thoughtful answer to the question:
  >Would bigger brakes actually stop an '87 Carrera any quicker on
  >the street?

---------------------------

The latest issue on "Road & Track" addressed the same question.  Their
reply was that bigger brakes require less physical effort to acquire
maximum braking.  As a result you have greater "feel" and can more
accurately modulate the brakes to achieve maximum breaking (i.e., the
incipient skid).

There is a ton of human performance data that supports the concept that
reducing physical effort increase how accurately one can physically control
a device.  So it does make sense.
