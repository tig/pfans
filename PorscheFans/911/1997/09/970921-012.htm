If you're referring to what I said about K&N's not flowing as good as foam
filters, you're mistaken. However, I did say you can't have it both ways. I
don't think a single filter type can be the best at both air flow and
filtering. And I don't think K&N's fit in that category. Maybe when they're
new, but that only lasts for a few miles until dust and dirt particles
accumulate. That's not a real world service situation. I think the only
scientific way to measure increased air flow gain (+HP) from just a filter
change is to test the engine/vehicle on a dyno. Check out
www.buschurracing.com/dyno for his dyno test on a 944 w/and w/o K&N's. Just
one person's experience.

BTW, what do electrostatic filters have to do with K&N's on a car? Yes, I
realize electrostatic home furnace filters are better than the $.79 filters
that your local hardware store sells. Are the K&N's electrostatically charged
filters? We're probably beating a dead horse here with personal and 2nd hand
testimonials. Perhaps one day an independent testing facility could put this
issue to rest.
