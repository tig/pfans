> When I got my C42 in '96, I read the manual, watched the videotape, and
> generally did everything I could to learn how to mind the oil properly. 
> Including visiting my dealer (Michael Schwartz at Paul Miller Porsche). 
> 
> Now Michael has been driving/racing these things for years, and was the
> number two individual salesman of 993's last year.
> 
> Here's what he told me: Forget the dipstick. Watch the oil-level gauge.
> 
> The gauge is a simple device.  Porsche has the technology to get it right.
> 
> The dipstick is (as you point out) tortuous.  In addition, the synthetic
> oil is so slick it will slide down the dipstick before you have a chance to
> read it.  You'll always be adding oil.
> 
> Michael went into the parts area and came back with some Mobil 1 and showed
> me how to add oil.  The car was already quite warm for the 30 mile trip to
> the dealer.  He raised the rear lid, opened the oil cap, and slowly poured
> the oil in while watching the level gauge through the rear window.
> 
> No muss. No fuss.  I've been doing the same thing for more than a year.
> 
> Michael says we should ignore other advice.  For that matter, that's
> exactly what the videotape from Porsche said as well.
> 
> By the way, Michael's a great dealer and has helped me several times since
> he sold me the car (like the time I let the battery go dead while on
> vacation).
