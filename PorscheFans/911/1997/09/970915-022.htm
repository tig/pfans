OK. Quick test. Given person A and person B...
Person A wants to talk to person B about person B's possesion C(regardless of
what it is), and person B doesn't want to talk to person A. Person A then
goes off and complains to another group of people about person B's behavior,
wrapping it the guise of "I thought people who owned C were better than
that." Who is the prick?

Now, my reasoning is that it is person A. For three reasons. 1: He seems to
think that person B is obliged to speak to him, regardless of how assinine
the conversation. 2: He's stupid enough to think that owning possesion C
means *anything* about person B other than he managed to acquire it somehow.
3: He's a whiner for going off and complaining to a whole bunch of other
people about it and expecting back-up. Particularly when he says "I don't
want flames, just people's opinions"(i.e. "I don't want to hear from you if
you don't agree with me.").

This self-congratulatory "Porsche owners should be better than that" garbage
is just as elitist as the "gold chain" set, and just as wrong. No matter how
nice a car a Porsche is, it's still just a car, and doesn't say a damn thing
about the driver. And that was part of the thrust of my original post.

And the rest of my point is that most people seem to think that owning a
Porsche is a lifestyle statement, or a major component of a person's
personality. For some folks that may be true, it's not for me. And it grates 
on me that people assume it is.
