Bill Fullerton  wrote:

> I've heard that you can get 15-20 HP by using platinum spacers to
> index your plugs.

    Bill:

    This is a load of crap.

    I'm hesitant to even dignify it with a rebuttal, but here goes,
    anyway.

> Something about the better heat transmission characteristics....

    Platinum conducts heat only about 1/6 as well as copper.

> .... as well as the null grounding induced by the platinum.

    "Null grounding induced by the platinum"?  What does THAT mean?

    If the idea is that platinum is a better electrical conductor
    than copper, it's wrong... Platinum is about ten times more
    resistive than copper.

> Supposedly much better than aluminum or copper which are just base
> metals.

    A "base metal" is one which reacts easily with oxygen, as
    opposed to a chemically-inert "noble metal" like platinum or
    gold.  The metal's "baseness" has essentially NOTHING to do with
    its suitability for use as a spark-plug washer.

> An added benifit is supposed to be better flame propigation.

    The benefit, if any, would come from the indexing of the spark
    plug; the material used for the washer wouldn't affect that.

    -Andy

    P.S.  And another thing... Sparkplug heat ranges are determined 
          under the assumption that you'll be using one new copper
          washer.  Using anything else (even a stack of COPPER
          washers) changes that assumption and may force you to
          change your sparkplugs to compensate.
