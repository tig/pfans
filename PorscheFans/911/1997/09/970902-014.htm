Actually, as I understand it, both have excellent high-end response
since both pressurize the intake charge.  The difference is that
the turbo runs off of exhaust gas and therefore needs higher rpm to
generate higher pressures.  The supercharger runs directly off the
crank via belt and gets pressure from zero to whaddaya want, so
it has no lag and just gets better as it revs.  The tradeoff is that
the belt drive does take away a little power, but hey, it's more than
replacing it, right?  Now that I think about it, the supercharger's
response is linear and proportional to crank speed so I guess you
could juggle the low vs high by playing with the pulley ratios?
Never driven a "supe" but that's my $0.02.
