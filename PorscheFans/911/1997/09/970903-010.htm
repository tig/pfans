I too had a similar experience with the seatbelt/airbag light.  When  
the Bosch Hammer was hooked up a high resistance fault for the right 
crash sensor was stored in the computer.  Cleaning the connections and 
clearing the fault fixed it.  I'm not sure if this is a latching fault, 
ie. it won't go away if the problem goes away.  IMHO it is worthwile to 
have the Hammer hooked up everyone once in a while as there can be lots 
of fault codes stored in there.  For example, I found out the climate 
control sensor blower was not working.  $125 fixed that (BTW a Nippon 
Denso blower!).

When I first purchased my car it was I took it into a local independent 
for a service.  What I didn't realize was that the Bosch DME on these 
cars has a proprietary interface and the mechanics Sun diagnostic 
computer can't talk to it so he couldn't reset the seatbel light nor 
could he do a system adaptation.  I ended up going to the local dealer 
for this.  What left a bad taste in my mouth was that since I asked for 
two procedures to be done (reset seatbelt light and perform system 
adaptation) I was charged the book time for each separately.  I was 
charged over two hours (excuse me, time units) of labor and the car was 
in the technician's hands for about 20 minutes.

If several of us went together the cost of a Bosch Hammer might be 
reasonable.  Sounds like a candidate  for a local PCA region to 
purchase and rent out.  Any of you EE/CS types out there know anything 
about the DME interface?  Any possibility of building an interface 
board and writing some software for a PC?  I'll volunteer to do the software.
