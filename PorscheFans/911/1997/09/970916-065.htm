>It seems to me that a lot of folks don't realize that just bolting on the
>latest "trick new" stuff (muffler, headers, spark plugs, wires,  inlet valve,
>air filter, fuel pressure regulator, etc., etc...)  is not going to do you
>much, if any, good if the system as a whole isn't tuned to take full
>advantage of that particular component change. We need to be more focused on
>a system approach rather than a components approach.  For examples:
>
>Decreasing back pressure through the exhaust system can actually hurt the
>torque and/or horsepower if you don't optimize the system via timing changes.
>
>Increasing inlet air mass flow via lower restriction filter element, larger
>throttle valve, boosting, etc... must be accompanied with timing and fuel
>changes to optimize the net effect.
>
>Spark plugs and various "spark altering" devices alone won't do good unless
>you tune to optimize their particulate characteristics.
>
>Any AMENS out there or am I just blowin' smoke (and unburnt fuel)?
