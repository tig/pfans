 Bob, 
 
I instruct with the Shelby Club and when attending events have been designated 
the rear engine instructor as I've raced front engine, rear engine, front 
engine/rear transaxle and mid engine cars. I've gotten in C2s and 993s that 
have showed up at our events with stock seat belts and instructed them with a 
very critical eye on safe driving as the cars are IMHO too damn fast to be on 
a track like Sears Point without more protection.  When someone does come to a 
Shelby event with a late model Porsche, I always recommend that they put in 
the minimum equipment and go the GGR Time Trial events as they are populated 
with rear engine instructors and are the safest events you can put a 911 on a 
track at. 
 
If you talk to other regions, you'll find that they lose cars at many Drivers 
Ed events.  GGR has a great safety record in the TT series, you don't have to 
run for time and the instruction is as close to professional coaching as you 
can get in any club, anywhere.  
 
Last year I watched an RS America with 4K miles on it get totaled in a drivers 
ed group at Sears at a NASA event. Happened on the same corner and in the same 
fashion that a new 993 piled up a club race.  The 993s are great cars but will 
bite you at the limit or if you find yourself off line in certain turns.  A 
993 out of the box is capable of turning faster times than many SCCA race cars 
and your's is without the benefit of a cage, fuel cell, and fire system. 
 
You can add a harness bar, bottle and harnesses without any intrusion to your 
interior.  Don't forget to add passenger side harnesses as the instructor will 
feel a whole lot better in your car and may walk you thru some of the finer 
points of getting thru corners.....
