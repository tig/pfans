> In my car the first to second up shift is a four step process - out
> of first, into neutral, move towards second and feel the syncros
> engage, then you  slide into second. What I'm trying to say the
> motion from neutral to second is really a two step process. When I
> pay attention, then the 1 to 2 shift works well. Under high rev,
> quick shifting conditions it never works well.
> ....
> Does the above make sense to those experienced with 901
> transmissions?

    Bill:

    Yeah, it does... Although if you double-clutch your upshifts and
    get the timing exactly right, the 1-2 shift can be performed VERY
    quickly, with no grinding.

    If your rev-matching's off, of course, very quick upshifts into
    second gear will grind REAL bad... So it's proably the better
    part of valor to make that shift relatively slowly.

    Or you can do what I do:  Every time you grind the gears, toss a
    five-dollar bill in the glovebox... By the time you have enough
    money in there for a tranny rebuild, you'll need one.

> The first to second shift is long and awkward. As Bob Williams said,
> first was just to get you out of the pits, and the early 911
> transmission is not meant for power shifting and quick 0-60 times.

    Power shifting (shifting without disengaging the clutch or
    lifting the throttle) is bad for nearly ALL transmissions...
    Even if you stab at the clutch between shifts, you shouldn't
    expect any transmission to enjoy being shifted with the throttle
    wide open.

    The only area where the 901 is weaker than most other
    transmissions is first gear; it's only supported on one side, so
    any attempt to put large torques through it may break it.  This
    means no drag-style standing starts, and no downshifting into
    first gear while the car's moving.

> My 901 was refurbishd to have new syncros and really needed to be
> properly aligned. No it works much better. Even with all the
> mechanicals correct it will still grind. But under low power (low
> revs) it does not grind at all.   

    There are two reasons for that:

    1.  At low revs, the difference between the engine speed in
        first gear and the engine speed in second gear is pretty
        small, so the synchros don't have to work very hard to
        equalize the rotation speeds.

    2.  If you're shifting at low revs, you're proably not driving 
        very spiritedly, so you're probably not trying to shift very 
        quickly, anyway.

> Finally, what is the downside of starting out in second if you
> really want a fast 0-60 time?

    The downside is that you can't GET a really fast 0-60 time if you
    start in second gear.

> Will doing this infequently cause excessive clutch wear and/or
> crank or rod bearing wear, or excessive transmission bearing wear? 

    You've got a '71 911E, which doesn't make a huge amount of
    torque... So you may not be able to get a decent launch in
    second gear without slipping the clutch.

    Slipping the clutch, even "infrequently", will cause wear.
    Whether that wear is "excessive" is for you to determine, but the
    clutch certainly won't last as long as it would if you NEVER
    slipped it.
