You may have a stretched cam timing chain that's catching when it's slack,
a collapsed chain tensioner or a combination of the two.

Do not run this engine! If you are a do-it-yourselfer, drop the muffler and
the rear sheet metal panel and pull the chain housing covers to check chain
tension. If the tensioners are not collapsed, and the chain is dragging on
the lower rails, you need to replace the chain. With a master-linked chain
this is difficult, but not impossible for the amateur. However, it's a bit
much for an e-mail post, so check the chain first (or have your mechanic do
it.)
