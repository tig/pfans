From my experience I would suggest the following:

1) '86 serial number should be like WPOxxx91xGxxxxxxx where "x" depends
on country shipped to and "G" stands for an '86.  Your serial no. has a "8"
in fourth digit, indicating a '78 (my '76 911 serial no. was 9116201918)

2) Factory Turbo-looks had the 930 big brakes (vented drilled rotors, 4
piston
calipers front and rear of same size).  Rear calipers are mounted behind the 
wheel center, rather than in front like on standard 911.

3) Have dealer go over body work, or else you can do it with a plastic
magnet.
There should not be any plastic Bondo around the big fenders (factory fenders
in '86 were welded up with no filler).

Don't have info on engine nos., but '78 used CIS fuel injection (can tell by
air
filter housing stretching across engine with air inlet on left side), '86
used
Motronic fuel injection ( air filter/inlet should be on far right side of
engine;
brain box for FI should be under left front seat - at least it is on US
cars).

I agree with dealer; car is a fake.  I don't remember the widebody being a 
factory option till '84.
