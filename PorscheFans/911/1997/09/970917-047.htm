From what I've learned, it doesn't matter how fancy your intake path is, as
soon as you put a restriction in the air path (air filter) you've thrown out
any potential gains in power and torque from preplanned turbulence,
pre-swirl, pressure waves or laminar flow to pack more air/fuel mixture into
the cylinders. I'd like to stress that the previous statement only applies to
the air upstream of the filter. Once pass the filter, however, air flow can
then be tailored for the desired torque and power characteristics via intake
manifold/port length, diameter, surface finish, temperature, etc.
