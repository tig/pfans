Indeed, most performance brake pads are noiser than the OEM ones. By
using stick-on shims on the back of the pad, putting a slight chamfer on
the leading edge of the pad material and making sure that your calipers
are clean on the inside so that the pads cannot drag against the ends,
one can minimize pad noise. There are lots of good pads, Pagid, Cool
Kelvar, Hawk, Performance Friction, Porterfield R-4's. All of them have
different charcteristics and their effectiveness depends upon factors
such as existing rotor surfaces, boosted or non-boosted brakes, caliper
size, auxiliary brake cooling and more. Some of these pads are harder on
rotors than others so; caveat emptor.
Ask around....
