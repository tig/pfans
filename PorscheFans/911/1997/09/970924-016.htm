Alan, I'm not close enough to your car to give you anything more than a
guess as to what your complete problem is, but if you changed to 134a
and did not change to barrier hose then the most likely source of leak
is the hose. You see 134a is made up of a very very much smaller
molecule and therefore can pass through the older freon hose like it was
made of cheesecloth. And since Porsches have ten times as much freon
hose as a 'real' car, the lose will be felt much sooner. For example
most G.M. cars hold 4 # of refrigerant and have about 6 ft. of hose,
whereas your 911 holds 3 # of refrigerant and has about 30 ft. of hose.
You might also have a leak at the seal of the comp. if this comp. is not
designed to handle 134a, because the rubber that was used to retain R12
will not seal 134a. Ask your a/c tech to check these things even if you
have to take the car somewhere other than a dealer.
