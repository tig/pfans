We have a member with such a vehicle and I've been around long enough to see
and drive a few 327/350 powered 911's. 

First off the 915 trans does not appreciate the speed torque curve of a V8
and 1st gear  dies rather quickly. Most just use 2nd gear as 1st gear and
live with a 4spd.  The weight bias becomes totally obscene really requiring
more spring rate than torsion bars alone can handle otherwise you have a
heavily squatting car that can't handle worth a Bevis and Butthead dump -
fine for the freeway drag race but don't try making an on or off ramp at
speed. Lift throttle oversteer is redefined to the utmost obscene.... 

The $4500 will go into the resale value. The $1800 will make the car
worthless to all but the high school buyer.
