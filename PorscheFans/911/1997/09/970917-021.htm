1.  What's holding the pins in place?  (They're solidly in there, and 
     I didn't see any holes through the pins where retaining clips 
     could/should be.)
     
     2.  How do I get the pins out so I can R&R the pads?

Dave,

The pins on some calipers are held in by a spherical piece of spring metal on
the pin itself. This is compressed when in the caliper housing and holds the
pin in place. Nothing is missing and there are no holes.

They just drive out with a punch. Look at both ends, one is a smaller diameter
than the others. Put the pin punch on the smaller end and drive it out.
