"I could get it fire up immediately after running; but only if it set for 5+
minutes."   -??? I assume you mean that immediately after turning the 
engine off, it would start.  However, if you leave it for >5minutes, it won't
start until it is close to stone cold?  If so read on.

I think that somehow you are not holding residual pressure.  One way to
check is to take off air cleaner, and lift up on the air metering lever with
the 
ignition on for a few seconds.  The fule pump should run.  Now, try starting
the car; I bet it will start.

These CIS vintage vehicles rely on the system to hold residual pressure
in order to start; the fuel pump doesn't begin to run until the engine has
caught
and is able to pull enuf air to lift the air metering flap and switch on the
pump.

You have replaced the standard items; only other things I can think of  are
the
fuel distributor or (maybe) the check valve on the fuel pump ( I think your 
vintage car has one).
