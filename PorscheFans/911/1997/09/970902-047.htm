> << We have a noise limit of between 90db and 95db (depending on

> noise limiting device) on an early 911 (71 or earlier) with regards
> to performance / noise characteristics compared to factory
> Megaphones or the 'sport' style muffler (twin pipes located
> toward the centre line of the car). >>
> 
> I am using twin supertrapps on SSI heat exchangers on my stock
> '72 S. They are the long stainless steel glasspacked supertrapps.
> They have a flange welded on to bolt to the stock early heat exchangers.
> These are 17" long and about $160 each in the Pegasus catalog.
> They also make an extra short 10" long one. Mine do stick out
> a bit and with my car VERY low will rub leaving steep driveways.
> 
> With a 2.4S with MFI they act very weird. I must run all disks (12?)
> in order to get good power. If I remove disks I get a severe bog
> coming out of slow turns. But with all the disks in it is too
> loud for the street or muffled track events (Lime Rock).
> Maybe about as loud as a home made factory sport muffler
> (stock muffler with 2 pipes added in the middle) which I have
> also run. I'd say over 95dB probably.
> 
> There are people
> building weird sound dams behind their mufflers to diffuse
> the sound away from the dB meters lately. One could be fabricated
> to bolt to the supertrap disk screws and surround the disk output
> so there is no direct sound path from the muffler to the meter.
> 
> The power with supertrapps seems slightly better than the stock
> muffler, though low to mid range torque is probably less.
> But I used the supertrapps mostly to remove a lot of weight
> from the far rear of the car which is more important to
> me than power.
