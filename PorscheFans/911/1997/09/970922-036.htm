On 9/22/97 4:39 PM, Gregory Hirsch wrote:

>In fact this whole variocam (is this the right name) on the new cars 
>changes the length of the intake path of air at different rpms to allow 
>ideal length for peak torque as rpms increase.

I think you mean "vario-ram", which changes the intake runner length.  
"Vario-cam" changes the cam timing to allow less overlap (and smoother 
running) at low RPM, but more overlap (and produce more HP/Torque) at 
higher RPMS.  

Porsche uses both (but not on the same engine, yet).  Vario-ram is found 
on the latest 911's, and Vario-cam is found on the 968 and Boxster 
engines.
