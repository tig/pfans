Joe West wrote:
> To recap...what specific 911 do I get for $9000?

Wait.  You could find a '66-'77 model in that price range, but it will
probably need more work than your budget will allow (mine certainly did
when I bought it, and I got a pretty good deal).  Looking at back issues
of Excellence (May and August of '97), assuming you want a car in
'excellent' condition, you're looking at a '66 to '73 non-S model.
However, you should be prepared to have to spend a couple thousand for
repairs (oil leaks, oil lines, brake lines, etc., etc.).

As for insurance, make some phone calls.  It'll depend on your driving
record and where you live, but I'd guess at around the $2000/year mark
for liability.
