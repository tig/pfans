I'm with Andy on this Fix-a-Flat business.  I have used this stuff on a
number of occasions (in lesser marques, to be sure) with at least a
modicum of success.  It is not apt to get on the rims much, as the scheme
is centrifugal force spins it up onto the tire where the pinhole leak is most
apt to be.  Never ruined anything that I could see.  Didn't always work,
either.  Flats or slow leaks never occur by a convenient tire store -
Newton's 5th law.  Weather conditions as likely as not will discourage
other repairs when needed, and the space saver spare doesn't inspire
confidence if you have a couple of hundred miles to go with nothing but
convenience stores between you and your destination.
