Remeber, the original post asked  for advice about a daily driver (90
miles per day). For a daily driver an early (pre-1974) S would not be
the most practical choice unless you  commute was at high speed. 

The cam timing of the S is not compatible with stop-and-go traffic.
Crusing at 70-80 mph (easonable highway commute speeds) the S has no
particular advantage. Any early 911 is powerful and nimble enough to
pass at highway speeds (but the S is the quickest and most nimble).

 You clearly get a lot of fun and pleasure from your S.Early Ses are
wonderful cars. They have the most aggressive engine you can get without
extensive modifications or extremely rare and expensive early cars. They
really get serious above 5K rpm (but you already know this!).

If the advice was what early 911 should I buy for a fun weekend car most
of us would recommend either an S, E or T. They are all 911s they just
have slightly different personalities.
