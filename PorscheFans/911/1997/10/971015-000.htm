I replied to this question individually, but there was a response on the
list that may be correct for the writer's '87, but not for the '75 that
was the subject of the original question.

If there was a change, I don't know what year--but as of '75, the 911
shift knob (a plastic cheapie, all one piece--the center doesn't pry
off) is a push-on item secured by a corrugated, wraparound metal ring. 
To remove the knob, pull and twist back and forth at the same time.  A
little WD-40 or Liquid Wrench might be desirable to make the job
easier.  In my case, the OE knob developed a hairline crack and came off
in my hand during an autocross.  I replaced it with a Momo knob, which
fastens with a set screw, and which I'd highly recommend (along with the
Momo steering wheel).
