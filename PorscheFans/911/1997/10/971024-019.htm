The switch on the throttle tells the DME control unit that the engine is
at idle and it should switch to the idle map (software data base and
algorithm) and turn on the idle stabilization valve.  The switch at full
throttle tells DME computer to use the full throttle map. And in
between...the part throttle map.  Simple no?   Well, as you guys have
found out the transition between the maps is smooth or full of
hesitation.  The timing of the throttle opening to the switch opening is
the critical part. Chuck, I can't tell you what the throttle position
should be for your 911 but for my 964 and Ross' C2 the manual says 1
degree of throttle rotation before the switch opens.  Hope this helps.
