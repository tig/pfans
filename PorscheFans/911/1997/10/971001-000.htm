OK, I"ve been waiting to try this before writing, but now you can test
and report on it.

In a Japanese Porsche magazine, I saw a simple solution that uses the
stock pedal without modifications. If this works, it will move the
bottom of the pedal rearward so your heel can reach it from the brake.
Cut a rectangular plate about the same width as the pedal base and about
2.5 or 3 inches long. Drill a set of holes at each end the same width as
the pedal mounting holes. Fasten the forward edge of the base to the
original mounts in the floor and the rearward holes to the pedal base.
When you are finished, the bottom of the pedal will be about 2 inches
closer to your seat. (You will probably have to re-adjust the threaded
rod under the pedal for correct throttle travel)

Another way is to take a new stock pedal, cut off the base and drill
screw the new pedal to the top of the original one to double the
thickness(but keep the bone-stock look). This does destroy two perfectly
good sacred Porsche parts, but...
