Yes the top of the evaporator case will come apart without disconnecting
the freon lines but it isn't easy and by the time you get done you may
wish you had. 

There are about 6 snaps around the case that hold the two halves
together. These are best removed with something like a cotter pin tool.
There is a hole in one side of the clip and you just pull it staight
away from the case with a hook in the hole or slide them along the case
until they clear the little boss on the case. Don't lose them to the
bottom of the well.  After you carefully pull the thermostat probe out
of the hole near the front and pry or slide the sticky insulation tape
away from the top, then simply lift the top off.

You will probably find the blower motor has gotten hot and distorted the
case and you may have to replace the top with a new or used one. Try
Aase in Cypress, Ca @ 1-800-444-7444.
