I'd be worried about fraying the sub-belt.  For a small
incremental cost, I'd go with a six-pt with the 2 sub-belts
mounted to the lapbelt mounting points.

I cut a length of 1" pre-drilled angle iron to fit across the seat 
rails.  Bent
back 2 ears flat and bolted it to the front flat part of the seat rail 
under the seat
using longer Grade 8 bolts.  Painted it gloss black, it's not visible 
unless you look
under the front of the seat but looks good.  The sub-belt eye-bolt goes 
through the
center of my 'cross-bar'.  No holes in car and it works well.  Of 
course, you have to
remove or at least loosen the front seat bolts to do this.  Cost: about 
$10 (I used a
Dremel cutoff wheel then since I hadn't invested in air yet).
