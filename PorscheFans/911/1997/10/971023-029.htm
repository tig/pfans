The light weight flywheel does allow the engine to accelerate/decelerate
faster than a dual mass, but also allows it to drop below the idle rpm
faster than the DME control system can respond to stabilize the idle.
In extreme rpm drops (from full throttle) the engine can stall.  You can
purchase a custom chip from the aftermarket (Autothority, etc..) to
increase the idle speed from std. to 1200rpm.  I suspect you can even
purchase one that doesn't remap ANYTHING else and ensure no increase in
power or wear :^).  IMHO, having lived with this setup, it's a pain for
the street.
