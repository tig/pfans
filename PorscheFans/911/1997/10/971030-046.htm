As to whether higher pressure reduces or accelerates center tread wear,
I think the best answer is "it depends on the tire." That is, according
to a tire engineer I met at the track, some stiff-sidewall tires, like
my BFGs and many others actually cup inward with higher pressure. 

The reason may be that they are designed to combat balooning as a result
of the centrifugal force of high-speed driving. For such a tire, lower
pressure reduces the tires ability to resist ballooning, which causes
greater center wear.

Note also, that optimum treadwear is just one characteristic the factory
considers when suggesting tire pressures.
