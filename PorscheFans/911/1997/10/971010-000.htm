In answer to J. Lehtinen, the black and red keys will both work in the
door and ignition locks.  The red will not work in the glove box lock
and, for open cars, the locking trunk release.  The keys are cut
identically, although the red key has a thinner cross section and an
extra groove.` These are early (70s) keys you describe.  Compare the
blanks with your existing key before cutting them--then you can, if
necessary, sell them to an owner of an early car.

As for someone else's question about the ignition switch, the advice I
got (once from the dealer, once from an independent) was to replace it. 
Then your door locks won't match the ignition unless you have them
rekeyed.

BTW, the most common cause of worn ignition switches in general (and in
my experience) is an overloaded key chain, which puts undue weight on
the lock!  Simple but true.
