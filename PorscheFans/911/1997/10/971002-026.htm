For that money, (were it mine) I'ld look for a 1971--73 911E, a 1970 911S,
or the like. That's for 911s. You can get a low-mileage '87 924s for that
money, or an 88-89 normally aspirated 944, although most have fairly high
milage.

Insurance depends on where you live. Figure on spending another $1500 in
maintenance the first year you own the car (no matter how great it looks,
stuff will need doing), and about $500-$700 annually after that if you
drive it year 'round.

Good luck!
