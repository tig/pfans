  Bob, I have been driving 911s since 1976, accumulated a total of approx. 
  250,000 miles in them - street, track and autocrossing.  I'm not bragging,
  I am not claiming to be an expert driver, but this is what I have noted
with
  the 993 vs. my previous 911s.
  
  With the power steering, I was initially overcompensating to correct the
(very
  rare) oversteer condition I got myself into.  This led to several cases of
tail
  wiggle (fishtails?), fortunately never to the point of losing it.  I have
noticed 
  that the rear suspension seems to try to correct almost immediately;
whether
  this is due to the fancy limited slip or the suspension geometry, I don't
know.
  Generally what I do in a oversteering situation, is only turn the wheel
slightly
  (1/8 turn or so); car then will stabilize.  At the same time I apply
neutral 
  throttle (no more or less gas).  Initially, I was applying more steering
and
  overcompensating.   Haven't had a problem in approx. 10 autocrosses and 
  >10 track events since learning the car.
  
  Does your region put on any general high-speed autocrosses where you can 
  do some practicing on oversteer correction?  By "high speed", I mean well 
  up in second gear, possibly third, and open enough where if you spin
  you won't hit anything?
  
  My 2 cents, for what it's worth.
  
  Ray Calvo (porsray@aol.com)
