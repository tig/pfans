What you get all depends on your budget -- but I think
the best bang for the buck would be a perfect, low mileage
1989 Cabriolet.

I've spent a lot of time in later models, and it's just not
the same driving experience as the 3.2 Carrera (for me). Porsche,
over the years, has become quite successful at insulating the
driver from the "driving experience." Cars are quieter now,
smoother, more forgiving. I'm sure the owners of 2.2L 911S's
say the same about the 3.2 Carreras!
