To those of you who have no difficulty with heel and toe in an 911, do
you drive a C2 or 993 with power brakes? If so, what is your secret?

I didn't notice a problem until I switched to a power-brake equipped C2.
Specifically before I can depress the brake pedal far enough to reach
the gas pedal the headlights are scraping on the ground. These are some
powerful and sensitive brakes! I tried practicing with the car stopped
in the garage and even then could barely manage a good throttle blip
with the brake pedal down as far as I could push. (Imagine what that
would be like while driving.)

Sure, if you overheat your brakes on the track, eventually the pedal
height will fall far enough to allow good heel and toe, but you only
have one or two laps left before they fade completely--guess how I know!

If you shorten the brake pedal rod, you risk running out of travel when
they overheat, so the only safe way is to raise the pedal height (or
wear those 4-in stilleto heels you've been saving for that special
occasion :-) -- Spearco are you listening?).
