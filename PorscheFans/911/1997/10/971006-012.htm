I'm one of those nuts who drives his 993 in the snow.I tried Blizzaks 
and they are the best in snow and on ice. Too squirmy on dry pavement 
though.  Best all around in my experience is the Yoko Guardex 600. 
Make sure to buy them in the narrower sizes Porsche suggests for a 
911 in winter (see the owner's manual).  If you drive mostly on 
pavement with occasional snow, any winter tire will do and you would 
be better off with a high performance like the Toyo which trades off 
performance in snow for better dry pavement (at cold temps) handling. 
Toyo build hi perf winter tires especially for 911's ask your dealer.

Do not attempt to use summer tires in cold temps. The rubnber freezes 
and you will be driving on ice even if the pavement is bare and dru.
