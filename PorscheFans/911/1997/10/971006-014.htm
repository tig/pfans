Some time ago, someone posted a message expressing concern about
pressurized brake bleeding, and he asked what the Porsche Manuals said.
As I recall, he was mostly concerned that 30-35 psi from the tires was
too much for the reservoir.  Well I just found my reminder note and I
don't recall anyone answering the question, so...

The factory manuals for my 1971 say to "Fill the tank with original ATE
blue brake fluid (ATE Blaue Original Bremsflussigkeit) by following the
manufacturer's instructions, and pressurize with air to 2.2 atm (32.3
psi)".  I assume this "tank" is from a "bleeder stack (such as ATE Nr.
3.9308-0600.2)" which is mentioned later on the page.

So it looks like getting 30-35 psi from the tires should work fine.
