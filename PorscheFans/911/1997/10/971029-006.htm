Jim Bauman wrote:
>Porsche,
>over the years, has become quite successful at insulating the
>driver from the "driving experience." Cars are quieter now,
>smoother, more forgiving. I'm sure the owners of 2.2L 911S's
>say the same about the 3.2 Carreras!

That is true: I myself used to have a 1980 SC Targa, and I loved the
car. After a while I sold it to buy an 1968 short wheelbase model, and I
must say: THIS is it! I just love the way the car perfoms and wants to
be treated (with respect, that is). You have to work with the car, but
if you have mastered it, it is the most rewarding way of driving: pure,
historic, real.
Don't get me wrong, I also enjoy comfortable cars for every day, long
distance driving (may I say: Minivan driving. Please, go ahead and flame
me ;-), but this is a very special experience. I would even say: Nobody
would ever need MORE car than this.
Yes I am a bit crazy, but aren't we all?!?
