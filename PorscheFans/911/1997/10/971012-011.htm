> Collyer:
> 
> There are two problems with mounting a camcorder; both relate to the 
> fact that you don't have a rollcage in your car.
> 
> First, the camcorder's tripod mount is pretty feeble... While it'll 
> hold the camera forever if your car remains stationary, it'll have a 
> hard time restraining the camera in the unlikely event that you crash 
> into something solid during one of those driver's-ed events.  You 
> have a cabriolet, so a flying camcorder may also pose a hazard to 
> other cars on the track.
> 
> Rollcages generally provide a more-secure mount (usually with a 
> redundant restraint strap or something, too) that's more-or-less 
> guaranteed to keep the camcorder from flying around or out of your 
> car in the event of a crash.
> 
> Second, even if the camera DOES stay in place, it may pose a hazard 
> to you in a crash or a spin... Camcorders have hard, sharp edges, and 
> you don't want to bang your head or arm against them.  Again, a 
> rollcage could help here, since it might allow you to mount the thing 
> far enough away from the driver's seat that you wouldn't have to 
> worry about hitting it.
> 
> I don't know how you could securely mount a camera in a stock 911, 
> especially if -- as I assume from the fact that you don't have a cage 
> -- you want to do it in a completely-reversible way that doesn't 
> require drilling holes in your car.  Whatever you do, though, 
> please keep the above two points in mind.

A harness bar will work and is reversible.  I bought one for about $80
from Automotion http://automotion.com or if you would like a real pretty
one for more bucks, try getting a Brey-Krauss.  One source for a B-K is
Engine Builders in Reno/Sparks, Nevada.  There are others.  See Pano.

Then for your camcorder mount, there are a number of makers.  I have a
IO Port that I bought from Engine Builders.  I can't remember the price
but it was a bit more than the bar.  It comes with a strap to keep it
secured.  Again, B-K makes them too.  I cannot see any issues with this
setup regarding safty.

And I think that my setup has already paid for itself.  At my first
timetrial, Hurley Haywood asked me if he could drive my car and demostrate
how to drive an all wheel drive correctly around Thunder Hill.  I said
noway, nobody drives my car. Yeah right!  He was almost the first driver
to drive my car since I bought it -- only missed by an hour.  And I caught 
HH on video.  It's priceless.
