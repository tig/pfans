In fact, Aichele's book indicates "These are the 911SC, whose letters are a
combination of the previous year's S, with it's "Super" equipment, and the C
representing the more powerful Carrera engine."

From the postings, it would appear Aichele is just perpetuating the
widely-held "myth" on what SC stands for.  If you read the introductory
publications of the time (1977-78), I agree with the school of thought that
it really means "nothing" unlike the 356C and 356SC (of which neither Lothar
Boschen or Jurgen Barth found the need to comment on the obvious "Super"
 engine variant of the 356C in their 1977 book "The Porsche Book, a
Definitive illustrated History")   It is just a way to distinguish the 1978
models from the previous 1977 911S without particular inference to "comfort"
or engine.
