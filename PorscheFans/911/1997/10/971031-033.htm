Chris, from what I have been told (including B. F. Goodrich), the old theory
of center wear meaning high tire pressure is no longer valid with modern 
low aspect ratio tires (60 series or smaller, ;i.e., 50, 45, 40, 35 etc.
series).
Now, apparently what happens on really wide tires is that if the pressure is
too
low, the tire flexes too much and the center is forced out by centrifugal
force,
therefore wears faster.  So, surprisingly, underinflation and overinflation
might
both result in center wear.

Recommendations I have heard is to go with the car manuf. values; I think
Porsche still has values for "high speed" use?
