Tad,

I use drill bits and thick coat hanger to remove the radio.  You are not
trying to remove the face plate, you will only succeed in removing the
entire unit.  Put a piece of hanger or a drill bit in each hole.  Slide
each in at an angle away from the center of the radio (i.e., the tip in
your hand should be towards the middle of the radio).  When all are in
place, apply slight pressure to the outside and remove the radio.  I
generally do one side, then the other , . . .  This way you can rock it out
going back and forth.

About your oil puff.  I've heard many people describing this and I think it
is extremely common.  I have exactly the same experience, some times it
does and other times it doesn't.  Some have suggested that it happens when
the gap in the oil ring is at the bottom and that oil seeps into the cylinder.

Hope this helps,
