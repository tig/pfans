Great story on the oil change.  My first 911 was a 1970 T purchased in
1977.  After proudly driving home, I left it overnight in the garage.  The
next day I checked the dip stick.  No oil!  Panic!  Have no oil at the
house.  Decide to crank it anyway, and see if there is pressure.  There is.
 Drive to K-Mart, eyes glued to the pressure gage.  Buy case of oil.  Start
dumping in quarts.  On quart 3, start to Exxon the K-Mart parking lot. 
Finally understand dry-sump design, and purpose of the scavenging pump. 
Now I watch the level gage.  As long as the needle is moving in the normal
range, all is well.  Still have the car.  Still love it.  Bare-metal
restoration underway.
