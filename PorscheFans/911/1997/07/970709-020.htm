George,

The same thing happened to my 1971 911S about ten feet from the rally
starting line at the San Antonio parade.  It turned out to be a simple
fix.  The clutch cable is attached to the clutch by a plastic part
(about 4-5 inches long) which has dog ears to hold it in place.  After
26 years, the dog ears sheared off and it began to slip in its holding
bracket.  It only takes about 5 minutes to remove the part.  As I
recall, the replacement plastic part is only about $8, but bought the
aluminum replacement part for about $30.  It was a pretty simple fix,
especially given my panic when the "clutch failed".

After overnighting a replacement part from Automotion, my son and I made
the parade autocross the following day.  I hope your repair is as
easy/quick.

Greg Skalla
