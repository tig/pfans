1)	Premium unleaded works fine.  Stay away from oil and fuel additives. 
Believe it or not, the better brands are fine as is.  The changes make for
cars to run on no-lead fuel are better alloy for the valves, and Stellite-6
(cobalt alloy) valve seats.  Porsche 911s have had this from the start. 
Early 911 owners often ran "white gas", which is the name for Amoco premium
unleaded.  Before the unleaded gas requirement, Amoco was the only mass
marketer for premium unleaded fuel.

2)	As far as modifying the car, or getting it painted, that is a personal
preference.  Early 911s are collectable, but if you plan to drive the
thing, it will continue to get more ratty.  I would do cosmetic maintenance
to keep the car nice.  Enjoy.
