To your questions:
1. Yes, altitude cuts power dramatically, but the Dynojet corrects power
to sea level at a standard temp & humidity 
     You will get an uncorrected and a corrected reading for the above.
2. Turbos are MUCH less prone to this issue, but again, you will get a
reading corrected to sea level, so it's not      relevant. You may be
interested in the delta between the two to see the loss that altitude
gives you.
3. We use a loss factor of 18% for FWD, which should be similar for
Porsches. Divide you wheel HP by 0.82 to get      an approx flywheel HP
rating.
