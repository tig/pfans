>Here's what I do:  I have a brass bar that is about 8-10 inches
>long.  Using the bar and a hammer, I gently tap around the
>periphery of the cap by angling the bar back towards the
>rotor as much as possible.  Rotate the rotor a couple of times
>while you're tapping and it comes right off.  The brass bar
>doesn't leave any lasting impressions on the cap, either...
>

>together without making the cap look like someone used channel lock pliers

>--------------------------------------
>Here's how to adjust the front wheel bearings (  from Clymer's service
>book) :
>
>- Tighten front clamp to about 11 ft.lb while rotating the wheel
>
>- Loosen the clamping nut just to the point when the thrust washer ( behind
>the nut ) can be moved when pried lightly with a screw driver.
>
>- Tighten clamping nut here being careful not to move it.
>
