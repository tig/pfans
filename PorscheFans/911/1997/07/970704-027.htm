M Campeau  wrote:

> What is the differance between a long and short block engine?

    Marc:

    A long block includes the cylinder heads; a short block doesn't.
    These terms have nothing to do with the engine's displacement or
    physical size.

> What is the need for a heat exchanger on  a 911?  I assume it is to
> cool engine as no water and if so where are they located.

    No; they're used for heating the interior of the car. 
    Water-cooled cars use the heat from the car's cooling system for
    this purpose; air-cooled cars like the 911 use heat exchangers
    wrapped around the exhaust pipes.

> What are the reactors that people refer to?

    "Thermal reactors".  They're emissions-control devices; think
    "catalytic converters".

> Is there a comprehensive poster available that shows the 911 thru
> the years and shows the 901 911 930 ... differances.

    Yes, there are a number of posters like that... But you need more
    information than they can give you.  Get a copy of Bruce
    Anderson's "Porsche 911 Performance Handbook" or "Buying a Used
    911" (I forget the author's name).
