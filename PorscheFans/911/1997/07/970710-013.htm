Hi Marc,
Up untill they called all the '84 and on 911s carrera (as a model update to
the SC) Porsche used the term carrera for special racing/racy cars, not
necesseraly the 911. There's a 924 Carrera and probably the most famous are
the early '70's 911 carrrera RS and RSR, the late '70 3.0L 911 Carrera and
a number of pre 911-era models that got the carrera tag.
As far as I know all 911 carrera's are non-turbos, the 924 however was the
LeMans version of the 924 turbo.
'84 and later carrera's can be either coupe, targa or cab. The older 911
carreras there were no cab's (cab's start from early '80's SC's) and I'm
not sure about targa's (someone else will know this for sure). Anyway, the
carrera doesn't say anything about the roof of a 911…
So except for '84 and on 911s carrera means you're talking about a special
car (not that regular 911s ain't special, but you know what I mean here…).
Anyway, I guess there are many other P-fans on the list that can give you
more details, but I hope I gave you somewhat of a general idea…
