	You can splice and replace sections of the oil cooler lines that go to
the front using standard copper tubing and fittings from the plumbing
section of any hardware store. Just solder them using regular acid-core
solder.

	Don't lube your clutch cable. They are plastic coated and that would
just gum it up. If the plastic wears through and starts coming off, it
can make your pedal stiff. In a pinch you can strip the rest of the
plastic off and reuse the cable. I wouldn't run it long though, or it
will start wearing through the tube.
	Everyone should have a "Porsche Technical Specifications" book for
their car. You can get them from your dealer, or you can mail-order them
out of the mags for less than $10. They will answer a lot of your
questions and are interesting to read. It covers serial #'s for the car,
engine, and tranny. It also list detailed engine specs, gear ratios,
torque values, alignment specs, tune-up specs, fuses, relays, bulbs,
etc.
	When refinishing anodized wheels, they have to be polished to a mirror
shine because the anodizing dulls that to the original look.
