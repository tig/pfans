All bleeding the brakes does is to get rid of the air bubbles which are
in the brake system. There are bleed valves at the top of each brake
caliper which can be opened to let fluid/air out. Basically you push
through a bunch of non-aerated brake fluid by using either the brake
master cylinder (pumping the brake pedal), or you pressurize the system
from the reservoir end by using a compressor or similar. In the latter
case you then go to each caliper and open the bleed valve which allows
the brake fluid/air to be pushed out of the system. When you you see
there are no more air bubbles coming out you then shut the bleed and
move on. Once you have done all 4 brakes....voila...no air in the
system, no spongy feeling brakes etc etc.  

Hope this helps....just did mine today so it is fresh in my mind...
