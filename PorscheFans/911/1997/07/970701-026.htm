You stated that you had pressures of 27 psi in your a/c system but this
is not the way to charge your a/c.  If when you connect the gauge to the
comp. with the engine off the pressure should read between 75 and 90 or
100 on both sides (high and Low)Suction and Discharge. Route the hoses
so as not to get into the belts, then Close the Deck lid. Start the
engine, The pressures should go to 25 on the low side and about 200 on
the high side. But this still doesn't tell you if the system is full or
partially full. Raise the RPM to 1500 and hold it for one minute, then
look in the sight glass located under a cap on the Rec/Drier in the left
front fender. The white ball should be floating at the top of the
opening
if the system is full. If it is not floating add JUST ENOUGH freon to
make it float. Putting too much freon in an a/c will only cause higher
pressures and not make it cool better.
