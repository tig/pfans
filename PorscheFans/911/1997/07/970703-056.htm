It sounds like the problem is shift fork alignment.  This is rare, but one
of the forks may have loosened, and is preventing the selector finger from
selecting the correct fork.  I wish I could tell you there was a quick fix,
but if that is the problem, the tranny will have to come out of the car.  I
would carefully check the gear shift linkage.  Take the coupler off at the
transmission, and see if you can manually put the transmission though the
gears.  If you can't, the problem is internal to the box.

The early 911, and the 914 gear boxes are the same, except for some
differences in the clutch cable and the shift linkage.
