Just a note to add to Rich Brown's excellent writeup.  After removing the oil
filter, particularly if it appeared to be overly "tight", make sure the
sealing gasket in the filter mounting face has not separated and bonded to
the oil line mating surface in fender of the engine compartment.  Some
"cheap" non-OEM oil filters, non-lubricated gaskets, or overly tightened
filters (by dealers or "other" oil change "specialists") can separate and
leave the oil gasket still on after filter removal and when you put the new
filter over the top and start the engine... instant oil mess!  Hand tighten
only!

And check the oil level, engine fully warmed at idle as noted.  Even after 25
years of 911 ownership, I have never seen even a dealer add oil to bring the
car to the correct level (usually overfilled).  Moral...change it yourself on
pre-993 911's at least.
