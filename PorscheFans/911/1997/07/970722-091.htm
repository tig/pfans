Not knowing the year model of your car, it could be the starter solenoid
that is sticking. When the engine/gearbox/starter is hot from driving
the solenoid sticks. Does your car push start easily? My car would
always start cold but not hot. I had my starter and solenoid
rebushed/furbished and the problem went away. It is a similar problem to
what is described below in the fuel pump.

> Your hot start problem *might* be caused by a problem with the fuel
> accumulator gizmo.  It is located somewhere between  the fuel pump and
> the engine.  Its job is to maintain fuel pressure in the fuel line so
> that the gas will actually spray when you re-start the engine.
> There's
> some kind of diaphram (or something) inside the accumulator that wears
> out over time; when this happens, fuel pressure to the engine is no
> longer maintained when the motor is off; hence, hard re-starts.
