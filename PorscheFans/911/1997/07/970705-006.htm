> The comment about dyno testing a few days ago perked my interest. I
> am taking my built up twinplug 74 911S in for a FREE chassis dyno
> this week. I just have a few questions about the tests.
> 
> 1.The test will be done at 4800 feet elevation. I have been told
> that you lose roughly 10 HP per thousand feet above sea level. Is
> this reasonable?

    Not exactly; you lose a PERCENTAGE of your power, roughly
    proportional to the loss in air density.

> 2.Are turbos less prone to this phenomenom?

    Yeah, they can be.

> 3.Is there a multiplier to use to figure actual engine horsepower? 

    Yes, and here's the important part:  The dyno incorporates a
    barometer, so it corrects for air density automatically.  That
    is, the numbers you get off your high-elevation dyno will be
    adjusted so that they're equivalent to the readings you'd get at
    sea level.
