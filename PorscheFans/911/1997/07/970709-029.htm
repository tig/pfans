Yes! I just replaced the wires on my 1980 911 SC Targa with a set of Fyrebraid
ignition wires that I picked up from Performance Products for $119. They are
red color and rated to 1400 degreees F.  They do not have the stainless steel
sheath around them.  The  OEM stainless ones are rated to 600 degree F and
cost ~$250, so I think I got a better deal.

 I had a bad connector, and probably could have gotten away with just buying a
new connector, but I was so jazzed that I found a set that didn't cost me
$250, that I decided to just replace the set.  
I have not noticed anything different about the way the car runs, nor do I
have a "High power stereo"  so I couldn't tell you if I was getting any noise
into the radio.
They look cool though as they are red, although have some oil spots from
installation.
Their are other "standard" sets for generic 6 cylinder engines that are even
cheaper.  I have no experience with those.  Maybe someone else does.
