Brian St.Denis wrote a whole bunch of neat stuff about the oil change.
There is one thing that is implicit in his experience, but should be pointed
out.   The expansion of the oil in a 911 as it heats from room temperature 
is substantial.  After initial filling and running the motor for a few
seconds,
the level should be no higher  than half way between the marks.  It will
do the rest when it warms up.  You can see this on the dash guage, 
looking at it when the car is idling and level.  When cold, the needle will
be low in  the range. As the motor warms up, the needle will rise.
On my 3.2 the total oil to do a change is 12 qts.
