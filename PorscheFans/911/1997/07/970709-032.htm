Hi Steve,

For removing adhesive/stickers and such, best not to use any hard or sharp
objects. It'll leave scratches that the paint/bodyshop will have to fix
(which will cost you an extra buck). I doesn't work any way (yes, I did
find out the hard way).
There's a special tool for this that works just great! It's a little
rubbing disk that fits right into your drill machine. It's some funny kind
of plastic (more like a pencil eraser) that'll rub of any adhesive easily
without causing major damage to paint or bodywork (at most it'll dull the
paint - but hey, it's going to be repainted right). I think it's from 3m or
Wurth. Just stick it into your drill machine and ATTACK! Works a whole lot
better/faster/easier than any liquid adhesive remover (and smells better
too - kind of caramel). Ask your bodyshop or prof.toolshop for it.
Good luck on the paintjob!
