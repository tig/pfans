> 	It's axiomatic in racing that speed coming out of a turn is more
> important than speed coming in, particularly on turns before the
> longest
> straights.  That's because the speed you have coming out of the turn
> is
> the speed you will carry all the way down the straight.  On the other
> hand, the speed you gain by braking late you will have for all of a
> few
> hundred feet or less.  Therefore, it's much more advisable to brake a
> bit
> earlier going into the turn to make sure that your car is well under
> control,
> so you can get on the gas as early as possible in the turn and be at
> full
> throttle at or slightly past the apex.  I see students again and again
> trying
> to pick up that extra little bit by braking late into a turn, get the
> car all
> squirrelly, and lose seconds down the next straight because they lost
> 5
> mph or more on the exit speed.
> 
> 	On the other hand, if you are a very experienced driver,
> obviously you are going to want to pick up every tenth, and it becomes
> improtant to brake later, even trail brake into the entry of the turn
> where
> appropriate.  But my guess is that over 95% of the people on this list
> fall
> into the "not particularly experienced at race driving" category, so I
> think
> my advice in the previous paragraph is much more generally applicable.
> 
> 	As usual, just MNSHO.
