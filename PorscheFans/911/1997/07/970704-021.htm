Eliot, very good, if very good means:
  - Low noise (no rumblings/whines)
  - Low harshness (no worse than Yokos, feel a little more compliant)
  - Good wet grip (no problems in our local monsoons; then again, the fronts
    see the worst and I have not gone to the T/As in the front yet).  Did
    bring car out to a very wet Mid-Ohio race track driver's education
    event and a very wet drive from Pittsburgh to Columbus OH and competed
    in an autocross with them; they felt just fine on the road, on the track,
    and in the autocross.  I don't make a habit of using them
"competitively",
    but figured if I tried to drive out on my Comp T/A R1s in the rain they'd

    probably be scraping me off a bridge abutment.
