Walter et al,
One thing to consider is the stock Flywheel bolts have a lot of
clearance with the holes in the Flywheel so the only thing resisting the
vibrational torque is friction between the flywheel and the crankshaft
mating surface. This is a problem at extended RPM if everything isn't
mated up perfectly! As soon as you have motion, you mess up that
"perfect" interface and it gets worse...as you noted about the material
transfer from the flywheel to the crank.
And then the motion of the flywheel relative to bolts allows them, or
maybe a better description is, causes them to back out and loosen up!!
So The CAUSE of the bolts backing out is flywheel vibration relative to
the Crank/Bolts, the EFFECT is the bolts back out, (they can't resist!)
and rub on the clutch disk, that's when you finally notice the damage!

One solution that apparently works is to dowel pin the Flywheel to the
Crankshaft so that there is no opportunity for motion between these
parts. This comes form an old 911 racer
Ralph Meaney, who finished second in class at Daytona in 1970 driving a
privately entered 911.
Increasing the torque of the fastening bolts is potentially a bad
solution , the torque spec take into account the variation in material
allowed by the specification, so you don't know how close you are to the
elastic limit when you over torque these bolts, and you can potentially
make it worse, i.e.. reduce your clamping force.
Locktite only stops the bolts from backing out, it doesn't prevent any
motion between the Flywheel and Crank flange. Again as soon as there is
motion, the bolts will back out!

If you can reduce the clearance between the Bolt's diameter and the
Flywheel holes to zero then you have a solution. Maybe our friends at
Raceware should make Flywheel Bolts, and a reamer kit!! Boltmeister
where are you?
