I use a claw hammer.  Pops the cap off with out a scratch.  Takes about two
trips around the wheel, getting a little bit at each stop.  Drive it back
on with a wide brass drift.  Don't get greedy, and you will get it back
together without making the cap look like someone used channel lock pliers
on it.
