> I am formally pleading ignorance.  What is "limited slip"?  Thanks . . .

Most cars have open, or non-limited slip differentials. When you make
a turn, the inside wheels spin slower than the outside wheels. To
compensate for that, the open differential provides power to either
wheel. One wheel can spin freely, while the other wheel can remain
stationary. Normally, this is fine.

A Limited Slip Differential (LSD) is a device that allows the rear
wheels (or front wheels on a front wheel drive car) to only turn
in some percentage of the other wheel. In other words, one wheel
CANNOT remain stationary while the other wheel turns. 

This is a good thing for racing, because with an open diff, you
can spin the inside tire exiting a corner. All the power then goes
to the inside, spinning wheel, and you lose speen. With a LSD, 
if one wheel sping, the power gets transmitted to the other wheel,
so you don't lose speed.

LSD also helps in the snow, but who cares about that?
