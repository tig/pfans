    Well, since you asked so nicely and all...

    The NSX is an awesome car, but the tall gearing really IS pretty
    stupid; a friend of mine replaced his stock ring-and-pinion
    with a lower (numerically higher) aftermarket R&P, replaced the
    stock 2nd through 4th gears with shorter gears from the
    Japan-market-only NSX-R, and instantly gained two to three
    seconds at Laguna Seca.

    Even with the short gears, though, the NSX will never be a
    competitive autocross car.  Its wheelbase (longer than a 914's
    and MUCH longer than any 911's) and its very wide track were
    designed for stability at very high speeds; on tight autocross
    courses or on VERY tight, twisty roads, a 914 or 911 in the
    hands of a skilled driver will do better than the NSX.

    On the other hand, you're right about the BSX's abilities at
    higher speeds; right out of the box, it's faster than almost any
    other car on the planet.

    More importantly -- and this really can't be emphasized enough --
    it's EASY to drive the NSX really fast... Almost anyone can do
    it.

    I don't want to start an NSX-vs-Porsche thread here (I've
    participated in enough of that on the NSX mailing list), but I'd
    like to repeat a couple of comments I made on the PFans-944 list
    about nine months ago when the same subject came up there:

        My Porsche has the same personality as the mid-engined

        "They're not friendly.  They don't say, 'Let's go for a
        ride.' They holler, "Hey! Wanna go for a ride?  Me, too! Let's
