Todd wrote to Mike:

>If you plan to disassemble the entire motor
>you should get a standard American engine stand that has the
>swivel bracket to mount to the engine.  Then purchase the 911
>half circle bracket to mount to the engine.  This allows you to
>rotate the engine and get to all the parts easly.

That's one way, but you can do it without purchasing the half
circle bracket.  With some engine stands you can manipulate the
four arms to fit a 911 engine, and with others you might have to
drill a hole in one of the arms to allow it to be mounted to the
stand, or the engine, in a slightly different position.

(Sometimes both happen...I twice mounted my 911E engine on the
stand without drilling holes, and once mounted my 911T engine
without extra holes.  The second time I worked on the T, however,
no matter how I manipulated the arms, I couldn't get them to fit,
and finally, in dispair, and feeling not a little stupid, I drilled
a convenient hole.  Don't ask.)
