When Porsche came out with the 3.0 liter engine they used the 930. part
numbers for a reason. If you look at the insides of the 2.7 and 3.0 liter
engines you'll see a big difference. I'd forget about the weight advantage
of the 2.7 case and not settle for less than a 3.0. Not only is the case
far stronger, in general you have bigger ports and valves, and lots of
other improvements. About the only disadvantage of the stock 3.0 liter is
that with the exception of the Carreras, they all came with cis. This
limits camshaft wildness if you stick with the stock pistons. And if you go
with Webers, the car will look quite authentic. 

Plus, as they say in Detroit, there's no substitute for cubic inches.
