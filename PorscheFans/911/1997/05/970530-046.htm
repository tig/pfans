In reply to the painting of p-cars, at my shop, aptly named Series 900 , 
The removal of the doors is a must. The pins are easily punched and
the right config of tape on the door/fender seams for protection of paint,
therfore the loss of door alignment is eliminated. Occasionally I find stub-
born pins which leads to bolt removal. after the door is off, pop the pin,
clean it and the bore, reassemble for alignment purposes, pop the pins
and paint those door jambs the way the factory did?off the car!!! This is 
the only way i know to eliminate tape lines on the jambs. I also paint lids
off, standing upright to eliminate any surface dust. Ofcouse every Pcar
paint job is topnotch but, the customer always says "its not a show car"
$$$ is directly related to hours and the care and craftsmanship of the
artist, choose an enthusiast to paint  yours. As far as the color, your a 
big boy now :<) !! what ever turns your crank, Remember somewhere
there is a perfect 911 out there in original no mileage condition, yours
is not that one and unless steve is an alias for Ralph Lauren, meaning
$$$$ is no object for the absolute, chill out, make it yours and do a 
nice job. As far as " not a show car" , one of my customers then 
entered a local pca concours, forgot to enter as novice first timer, yet
scored 100% on paint points. Matter of fact I do not believe the judges
even knew it was a repaint! That was a 77S choc. brown w/77k.
