Ed Mellinger  writes:

>On a similar topic... are the braided wire shields on some 911 plug
>wires necessary for engine performance (ie airbox longevity), for radio
>interference reduction, for both, or neither?  From catalog prices, it
>looks like about $100 worth of extra metal in the engine compartment...
>is it worth it?

I don't think the braided wires do anything for you except make your
wallet lighter.  I believe they were originally supposed to be one of the
"fixes" to keep air boxes from blowing, but the wires weren't the cause
in the first place.

I would suggest that you buy a set of the "un-braided" factory wires (the
ones usually advertised for the early models).  I bought mine from Zims
for about $80.  They work just fine.
