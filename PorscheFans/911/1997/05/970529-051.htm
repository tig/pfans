To remove the heat exchanger (my car had the SSI ones, so I hope this works for you) I purchased a 13mm "distributor" wrench
from Snap-On.  It is the long handled 90 degree wrench.  I still
had to bend the wrench a little bit to get it to clear under the 
heat exchangers.  If you plan to disassemble the entire motor
you should get a standard American engine stand that has the 
swivel bracket to mount to the engine.  Then purchase the 911
half circle bracket to mount to the engine.  This allows you to
rotate the engine and get to all the parts easly.  You may be
able to purchase one of these used, by checking PANO, and Excellence, sometimes this part shows up in the classifieds.
