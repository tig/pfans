> Well, I've done the B&B TriFlo primary muffler bypass - it reduces weight 
hanging over the rear (maybe thirty pounds!) in the worst place on the car 
to be carrying dead weight.  It has an acceptable effect on the exhaust 
note (nothing so sweet as a full Borla or Ruf) but it also cost only about 
300 clams.
>
>
> I noticed that after a few hundred miles, the secondary muffler has 
changed its behaviour (no doubt due to the change in gas flow) and the car 
has a better note (even a fun 'crackle' on high RPM trailing throttle.)
>
>
> Thankfully, it's not an obtrusive noise in the cabin and it's a pure 
bolt-on operation which can be reversed in about 30 minutes.  It also 
leaves the car looking stock.
>
>
> I have the thought that a cop walking up to your car from behind might 
look at a fat four inch race exhaust tip hanging out of your car and make 
some different decisions about the ticket he writes (or doesn't.)
>
>
> I also did the K&N filter as a 'no-brainer.'  Another easy change under 
the hood is to replace the heater/blower unit with a simple, factory, 
plastic pipe that runs from the fan.  It gives the engine bay a clean 
appearance and reduces another few pounds of junk that's just not needed at 
the rear of the car.
>
