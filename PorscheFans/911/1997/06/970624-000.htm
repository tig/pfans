In the latest European Car magazine there was an article on modifying the
aluminum S calipers to accept the later style '84 up 25mm thick rotors. 

Has anyone had experience doing what was described in the article to modify
these calipers to fit these rotors? They milled 1mm off the mounting ears of
the caliper and an additional 1mm from each side of the caliper's inner walls
(for rotor rotation clearance).

It sounds like a relatively low cost solution for better braking, but I'm
thinking that removing metal from the caliper might contribute to more brake
flexing during braking.  

Is this modification a valid way to improve braking? I have not heard of this
done before. Any thoughts on this? Thanks.
