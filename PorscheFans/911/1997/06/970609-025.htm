Matt,
It's your idle circuit. You can start by trying to adjust the idle . The
idle compession motor controls a valve in the line around the mass flow
valve on the intake to the engine. It tries to find a happy medium by
opening and closing the valve to add more air to the system to increase the
idle or to control the idle. If it can't control the mixture it cycles open
and close hunting. Thus the 500rpm to 1500rpm surges. 1.You can try to
adjust the idle and get rid of the problem, ( turn the idle screw in
quarter turn increments open to increase the idle speed, 2. Take the motor
out of the circuit (2 clamps on air hoses) and try cleaning it out with
carb cleaner.  3 Look and see if the motor is in the line with the air flow
the correct way. Sounds dumb, but if this happens it runs swell when warm
but not worth a darn when cold (it surges) 4 If your good you can carefully
take the motor apart and clean it.
 A new motor is about $150 -$180 if it's shot.
I took mine apart and cleaned the shafts  and cleaned the crud out of it
and it runs great no more surging or dieing when cold.
