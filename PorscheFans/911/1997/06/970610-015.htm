>	I am just in the process of fitting a bump-steer-kit to my 76 911s. When
>raising the steering rack I found it hits a small bracket where the fuel
>lines run through. Apparently there is not enough room for the fuel lines
>to run above the steering rack with the bump kit fitted. Do I disconnect
>the fuel lines and run them under the steering rack and then bash the
>bracket flat? Are the fuel lines OK to be hanging under the rack or do they
>need securing to prevent rubbing on the protective steel plate under the
>rack? Anyone come across this before? Any experience/ ideas appreciated,
>regards Dean.

Hi Dean:

You shouldn't have to move any lines.   Are you loosening the U-joint
located under the access cover in the trunk?  You need to loosen the
5mm Allen bracket and 2 13mm nuts.   It's tight in there so I cut down
the short end of the Allen key instead of taking out the blower.  Be
careful of popping off the nuts/bolts when you pry the rack up to
squeeze in the spacer.  I also found the supplied spacer bolts were too
long and needed to be trimmed to 42mm using a Dremel tool.
