following.

1.) What would the net effect be of putting 2.2 S pistons on the car

components (I could make adjustments to the port sizes if that maximises

2.) Has anybody out there done this and what experiences did you have?

Some of you may ask why I would want to do this instead of doing a full
S conversion.  The main reason is that for events like the 'Targa
Tasmania' (fantastic bitumen rally for GT and sports cars of all eras)
in Australia, you really need an engine which  you don't have to be
constantly revving the stuffing out of get the desired performance for
varying terrain as opposed to the predictability of a closed circuit.
This is why the 1970 2.2 Porsche rally cars were of the 'S' spec with
180HP and the 'GT' versions of the same cars had a 2.3 motor producing
around 230 HP (8000 rpm)
