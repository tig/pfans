I have been using 110 octane leaded fuel in my CIS 2.7 911s on race day. It
has stock 8.5:1 compression. (Really Harry, it does!) If there is any
increase in power, it's slight, but in racing a couple horsepower can mean
a position at the finish so I buy the expensive stuff on race day.

Last weekend a knowledgeable Porsche wrench told me, based on his
experience, I was actually decreasing my power output as this engine was
designed to run on lower octane, preferably 87. He said with this low
compression I needed the shorter more intense burn of the lower octane.

Anyone have any data on this?
