being a prior participant in this topic, i've had time to think things
over.  here is my current mindset:

what is the commodity that is more valuable?  the mechanic's skill or the
part?  it would appear to me that the mechanic's skill is far more
valuable than the part, especially when the part can be gotten from
various places at competitive prices and the skilled machanic cannot. 

wouldn't it be common sense then, that the smart thing to do is to take
advantage of the fact that skilled labor is a scarce resource rather than
getting into all sorts of needless confrontations about part price markup? 
why not let the market forces take care of the situation? 

if you want to sell a burger for 59 cents you better make sure that
everybody will want your $1.59 fries!!  no use crying foul if
everybody buys your burger and not your fries.
