>How would I check the expansion valve?  And where is it?

Thanks for the help.


You really need access to a set of gauges to diagnose it, but almost all
expansion valve problems you'll notice the lines freezing up.  Typically if
the expansion valve is stuck open you'll see ice on the low pressure hose
of the compressor (the larger diameter hose, normally labeled "S" on the
compressor)  If the expansion valve is blocked you'll notice heavy
condensation or icing on the input of the expansion valve.
If the receiver/dryer is icing up it means a blockage in the high side line
or dryer itself.
Any of the above could cause insufficient cooling.

On my car (73 T w/dealer installed a/c) the expansion valve is located in
the small compartment just behind the gas tank.  I'm not sure if this
applies to all 911s.
