I had a strange problem with my seatbelt light coming on in my prior 911
(an '87 Coupe).  Turned out it was my alternator!

The seatbelt light would light briefly - typically between 1-2 shifts.
Here in San Antonio TX, we have the Jone's goup, so I whipped down there -
with less than 30k on the car, I wondered if this was "normal".

They told me that it was a "little known" indication that the diodes were
going in the alternator.  I trusted them, and the rebuilt alternator they
put in solved the problem with the seatbelt light.

On my 993 C4, I have another warning light problem - my brake (pad) warning

- after teardown and reassembley -  says "C4s do it, C2s don't.  ?  sound
logical?
