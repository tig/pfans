A couple of months back I posted a plea for help on a problem I'm having with
the open/closed door sensor and the interior light on my 1993 C2 Cabriolet. 
Most of the recommended solutions told me to check the mechanical switch in
the door, however, this did not solve the problem. So I'm asking for help
again.

I'm sure of this part:
Here's how the circuit works: The interior light that's mounted between the
sun visors has 3 positions: (1) Always ON; (2) Always OFF; and (3) ON when
doors are open, off otherwise. There are 3 leads to the light ass'y: (a) 12
Volts, (b) grounded directly to car body, and (c) switched ground.  [Lead (c)
is not switched 12 volts as many told me].  When the switch is in position (1)
lead (a) and (b) are in the circuit cross the bulb. In pos (2) only lead (a)
is in the circuit; and in position (3) leads (a) and (c) are across the bulb.

The switched ground works like this: When the car door is open the plunger
switch mounted in the door jamb springs out making a connection between the
lead and the door, grounding the circuit. Back at the light, this places a
ground on one side of the bulb and with +12V on the other side the bulb
illuminates. When the door is shut the plunger is depressed and the connection
between the lead and door is broken essentially placing an open in the circuit
and no current flows thru the bulb. 

According to the owner's manual there is a delay relay built into this circuit
so when you first get into the car and shut the door the interior light stays
on for awhile (automatically goes out when car is started, doors are locked,
or timer expires). This complicates the circuit somewhat. 

My problem is that the light is always ON in position (3). This is caused by
the fact that the switched ground lead (c) is always grounded, even when the
doors are shut. This tells me that somewhere between the door and the light,
the circuit is grounded. I don't have the circuit diagram, but I think this is
all that is between those two points:

1. the delay relay
2. some wire
3. the alarm/door lock control unit (see below)

Here are some other problems that may or may not be related to this:

  o The factory alarm/door lock control unit under the passenger seat that the

that has killed my battery.  I currently have to unplug the two plug

related to the light problem.  I don't know all what this part does but I do

connector hooked to it so it's not a simple device.

  o My after market car alarm system is also wired into this circuit for
automatic door opening and sensing of door status. It's also getting screwed
up by the door being sensed as open even when it's shut.
