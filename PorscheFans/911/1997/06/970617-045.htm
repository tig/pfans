Jeff, my 2 cents:

I have a 1990 Audi Coupe and 1995 Porsche 993, both with ABS and both
I have bled successfully, the Porsche several times.  The Audi has another
bleed port up by the master cylinder which has to be bled (I believe do
this first, then start on the other wheels).  The Porsche has to be done
in a careful sequence (RR, LR, RF, LF) to get a firm pedal and good 
track brakes.  The manual recommends hooking in a special Porsche
diagnoster tool and apparently triggering the ABS; I believe this is what 
your mechanic is implying.  I have had to bleed the Porsche two times in 
succession once or twice to get good brakes, so I believe he is partially
right.

I watched an Audi mechanic try to bleed the Coupe the standard way one time;
just by going to the wheels.  He couldn't bleed the front wheels;nothing
came out until as I remember he turned on the key and switched off the ABS
(possible on an Audi). 

By the way, I use a pressure bleeder; approx. 20 psi air pressure at the
brake 
reservoir to bleed at the calipers.
