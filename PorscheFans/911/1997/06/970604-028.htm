Mike -- I don't work on 911 engines enough to spend big bucks on a stand,
and when I do work on them I work on them alone, so I need to be able to
get an engine on/off the stand without 3 husky colleagues.  I mounted the
cheap VW-style bench-mount yoke on a low stand made out of 4x4s.  The 
stand is at the highest point my transmission jack can reach.  I sit to
work on the engine.  The cheap yoke won't hold the engine up alone (altho
it does an ok job of locating the engine and holding a set angle) so
depending on what I'm doing I either have the jack under the engine, or
a comealong to the garage wall, holding up the rest of the weight of the
engine.

I figure this way I save about 25 cents an hour over the life of my 911
work.  Since I pay myself 39 cents an hour for what I do in the garage,
this is a significant percentage savings.
