Steve,

I think replacing the O2 sensor is easier than the Haynes Manual describes.
 Jack the and remove the LR wheel.  Pull off the spring loaded protector,
and use an open end wrench to remove.  Hopefully if anti-sieze was used it
will come off easily.  Install the new one, and splice the connector.  

And yes, the universal fit ones work fine.  You will have to splice the
connector though.
