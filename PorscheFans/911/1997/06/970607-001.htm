As the owner of a small mail order company specializing in parts, tools,
hardware and storage systems, I do a fair amount of UPS shipping.  If you
have any question regarding whether or not a company has shipped your
package (via UPS), ask the company to tell you when it was shipped and get
the UPS tracking number.  You can then go to the UPS web site (www.ups.com),
punch in the tracking code, and verify the status and location of your
package. Red, blue and orange (next day, 2nd day, 3rd day) label packages as
well as "ground track" items will give you status on day-by-day
basis....regular ground packages will merely say "in transit" until the item
reaches your local UPS distribution center.  This is a great use of the web.  SD
