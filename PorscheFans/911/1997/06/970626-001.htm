Some suggestions from Car Theft Land:

Due to the frequency of armed hijackings South Africa now has
a couple of tranponder systems, i.e. they don't prevent theft
but instead aid recovery. Some systems use the cell phone
network to tell the control room "hey I've been stolen"
& can be commanded to flash the lights and stop the car.
I went for the cheaper Tracker system which uses a simple
satellite-activated transponder. If the car's stolen you
let them know and they turn it on. Many police vehicles
have receivers which pick up the signal. The advantage
of this system is that it is totally hidden and involves
minimal butchery of the wiring harness.
