Hi All,

I found a means of making the job a bit easier.

Before I removed the pin that holds the lower part of the strut in
place (not the little retainer), I inserted a piece of string (3 feet
or so long) through the hole where the little retaining clip goes. Pull
about half the string through the hole. Next, push the pin out with the
string attached. Now remove the strut; it should have the string
passing through the lower mounting hole.

Cut the string and thread it through the replacement strut's lower
mounting hole and tie the two ends together. Carefully move the new
strut back in place while keeping some tension on the string. When the
strut is in position pull the pin back through the strut. I used a
hemostat to replace the small retainer. Use very strong string.

Good luck!
