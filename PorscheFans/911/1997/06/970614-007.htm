>I do think that there is a left and right caliper difference.  Beside the
>part number and bleed nipple difference the piston that contacts the back of
>the brake pads are different.  The cylinder that presses the pads together
>are not perfectly smooth.  They are recessed due to uneven heat distribution
>along the brake pad(leading edge versus trailing edge).  The uneven heat
>distribution causes the pad to expand differently so the cylinder is
recessed
>to achieve even pad wear.  I think i explained that right....anyway there is
>a diagram in the Hayes' Porsche manual for the exact location of the
recessed
>portion of the cylinder.  If you have any questions let me know and i'll try
>to explain a little more clearly.

I always thought (without really thinking that is, although it's
problematic as to whether really thinking would make much difference) that
the caliper pistons were  'recessed' to accomplish two things
simultaneously.  The recess results in applying slightly more force to the
trailing edge of the pad which prevents the pad from grabbing at the
leading edge, thus evening pad wear by preventing excess wear at the
leading edge, and also preventing squealing and chatter.  I hadn't thought
about heat.
