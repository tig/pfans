    Eric Brown, at   mailto:kool@nwlink.com (home) wrote:
>I have Weltmeister 22mm bars front and back. They were on the car when I
>bought it. If I had to buy new bars, I'd get the Vision Motorsports
>bars. They, and the Charlie Bars, are works of art. The speed shop folks
>I know are leaning towards the Vision bars because they consider them
>the equal of the Charlie bars, but are less money. The Weltmeister is
>pretty crummy in comparison to these other two brands. First of all,
>they use rubber bushings at the body in the rear whereas the others use
>metal bushings (I believe they are bronze). The Weltmeister bars use
>hardware that is too light for the stresses they encounter. The rear
>drop link attaches to the spring plate with a 1/4" bolt. One of these
>bolts sheared off on my car. This bolt attaches to a replacement camber
>eccentric that Weltmeister supplies (maybe it is the toe-in eccentric, I
>forget). These camber eccentrics don't appear to be properly hardened. I
>did not take it up to the factory specified 60 foot pounds of torque
>because it did not feel right. I know that this is subjective, but I
>have tightened enough bolts to know what it should feel like. Also, and
>this is just a nit, the Weltmeisters don't use metric fasteners.

Like Eric, I have 22mm Welt f/r.  However, mine came with poly bushings
not rubber.  The hardware is grade 8 (5 slashes) so it's adequate.  Sorry to
hear of your bolt breaking (it's attached to the toe-in eccentric) but 
I'm happy
with mine.   They may not be the best bars out there but I wouldn't 
rule them out
especially if you can find a set used.
