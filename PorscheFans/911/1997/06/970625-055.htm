Yeah, that bolt can be a pain...

Biggest trick I found is to make sure to support the trailing arm (and
thus take the weight of the arm off of the bolt you are trying to
remove) using a hydraulic jack or similar. I just put the back of the
car on jackstands (on rear torsion bars) and then use my jack to support
the arm....

Originally I had to use an impact wrench to remove...I globbed on
antisieze and used a new replacement bolt and whenever I have had cause
to remove it it has been easy IF I support the arm....
