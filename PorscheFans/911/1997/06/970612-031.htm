I DO think that there is a left and right caliper difference.  Beside the
part number and bleed nipple difference the piston that contacts the back of
the brake pads are different.  The cylinder that presses the pads together
are not perfectly smooth.  They are recessed due to uneven heat distribution
along the brake pad(leading edge versus trailing edge).  The uneven heat
distribution causes the pad to expand differently so the cylinder is recessed
to achieve even pad wear.  I think i explained that right....anyway there is
a diagram in the Hayes' Porsche manual for the exact location of the recessed
portion of the cylinder.  If you have any questions let me know and i'll try
to explain a little more clearly.
