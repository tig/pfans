Something which I have used for rounded-off Allen-headed CV joint bolts
 and brake caliper bolts is a small 6" pipe wrench.  Wrap the head 
around the bolt head in an unscrewing direction, then clobberr the end of 
the wrench with a hammer.

For bolt removal where the heads are too rounded for a socket, I use the
appropriate sized EZ Off socket....this is a hardened 3/8" drive socket with
teeth that bites into the rounded bolt head.  Rap on the socket and use a
long handle 3/8 ratchet/breaker bar to remove the fastener (make sure you
have some Rost Off or other penetrant on the fastener ahead of time).  For
stud removal, I like the roller type stud drivers as they are much easier to
get into tight spots.  For Porsche (and most other European cars), you'll
only need the 6, 8 and 10mm drivers.  This tool has saved me more than a few
times.
