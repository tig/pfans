The following is something I originally posted to PorscheFans about a year
ago and which may be of interest to newcomers; it relates to car-related
bookshops on the UK side of the pond.


The two best that I know of are:

Chater's
8 South Street
Isleworth
Middlesex
England TW7 7BG
Tel: +44 181 568 9750
Fax: +44 181 569 8273

(This is just outside London but is a *pig* to get to by public transport.
Take a tube to Hounslow East on the Piccadilly Line and then a H37 bus to
South Street from Hounslow Bus Garage. They do overseas mail order and
take all major credit cards.)

Motor Books International

(can't find address details :-( but it's just off Charing Cross Road in
London)

There is also the world's most chaotic large bookshop, Foyles. If you can
manage to find the car section, you'll be amazed at the sheer number of
books which you just don't see anywhere else. The downside is that some of
them have been on the shelves for so long that they are quite dog-eared.

Foyles
Charing Cross Road
London
WC1

(Right bang in the centre of London. Nearest tube is either Tottenham
Court Road or Leicester Square, I think)

(Brooklands are publishers, but they sell direct to the public. They have
a hugh range of road test books, each containing about 100 pages of road
tests from magazines - there are about 8 or 10 covering the 911 alone. They
also do 356 and 912 workshop manuals.)

As far as I know, all the above produce catalogues, ship worldwide, and
will take credit cards.

The Automobile Club in Milan (Italy) has the most *amazing* selection of
car books I have ever seen. I counted over 100 different Porsche books in
a variety of languages, but I don't have the address :-(
