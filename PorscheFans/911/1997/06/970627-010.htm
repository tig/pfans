The usual breakdown of 911 "generations" goes something like this:
66-68: 911, 2.0 ltr, short wheelbase, no fender lip flares
69-73: 911. 2.0-2.4 ltr, 2" longer wheelbase, small fender lip flares
74-77: 911, 2.7 ltr, more or less similar body to earlier group
78-83: 911SC: 3.0 ltr, more weight, wider body, more fender flare, better
engine management systems
84-89: Carrera: 3.2 ltr, improved suspension, more creature comforts
90-94: Carrera 2/4: 3.6 ltr, some body & engine mods, heavier still
95-97: 993 Carrera: 3.6 ltr, new body style, better colors
98+: 996 Carrera: water-cooled boxer 6, different body.

A number of folks will argue that some of these distinction are too fine
(i.e., 66-73 should be one group) but I've always though of them this way
over a couple of decades of enjoyment. Good luck in your debate!
