Chris,
   I own a '91 C2 and had an identical problem such as yours that is why
I can speak from personal exp. What has to be replaced is the thermo
valve insert inside the thermostat behind the round outside cover. This
insert part # 930.107.155.00 (1974 and up) costs around $60. This valve
opens @ 180 degrees and allows the oil to flow forward to the front oil
cooler and when temp goes up to 225-230 degrees the fan starts up for
increased cooling capacity.To change this insert you just drain the oil
and remove the  right rocker cover,oil hoses need to be tightened.
   When the thermo valve inserts does not work, the oil ( engine )
overheats!!!!!! This will usually happen in the spring when it starts to
work again after having the winter off or at least working part time.
I'm
sorry you had to change & remove the  entire thermostat needlessly.
