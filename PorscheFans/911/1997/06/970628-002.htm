Gentlemen:

  FWIW, Eric Brown's advice about using the RSR suspension components
to restore order to a lowered 911 is correct. Without these pieces,
you have: no suspension travel, bump steer, excessive roll center drop,
and perhaps destroyed shocks not to mention a terrible riding car.

You do not have to spend $4K to have these pieces. You can have your
Bilstein struts modified to RSR spec, and purchase the RSR shocks
for the rear. They are shorter and maintain travel without bottoming.
We have performed these mods for years. A bump steer kit is also 
required, as Eric said. A lowered car handles very well after having the
corner weights adjusted and aligned. Driveways, etc are definitely a 
challenge.
