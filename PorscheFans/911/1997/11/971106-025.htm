>> 3. I recently purchased one of those Smartproducts wrenches
>>   for adjusting the rear ride height. Has anyone else  used one 
>>   with any success?

>Absolutely YES!  I spent a few weekends tuning the rear ride height and
corner
>balance.  The first weekend was w/o the wrench and I'd never do that again:
it
>is hard to loosen and tighten the front bolt (of the two adjustment bolts)
at
>all without it, let alone develop the torque needed to get it to spec, and
it
>lets you do very well controlled adjustments on the eccentric bolt (the rear
of
>the two).  The wrench pays for itself in 10 minutes if you're going to do a
few
>ride height iterations.
Some friends and I did  a friend's car and the wrench spread so much that
I sent it back.  Turning the eccentric took a lot of force under some
conditions.
It seemed like we were working against the force of the torsion bar.  I don't
know if they didn't heat treat it correctly (or at all) or if we shouldn't
have
had to pull on it as hard as we did.
