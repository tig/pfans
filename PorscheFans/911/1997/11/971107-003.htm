Nick, 

I recently refurbished the S calipers on my 1971 911S and it was easy.
There was enough surface corrosion that I also replaced the caliper
pistons.  After taking the calipers off the car, it was almost a 10
minute job once I figured it out.

*  I had read several warnings about using a metal tool to remove the
o-rings, so I used a cap from a bic pen -- worked great -- but I doubt
the extra care was necessary.
*  It took 100psi of air pressure to pop the pistons out (with mild
corrosion).
*  Apparently you cannot hone the cylinder wall with these S calipers
because of the plating (?).  I understood that this plating should not
be sanded/etc., but I did it anyway (very lightly) with very fine emory
paper to minimize the old corrosion.
*  On the advise of Jason Burkett at Paragon Products, I coated
everything with silicone paste before reassembly.
*  Everything I read said not to take the calipers apart unless they are
leaking, so I did not take the "small side panel" off.
