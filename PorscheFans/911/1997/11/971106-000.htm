Armorall is apparently loaded with silicones.  As long as they stay in 
solution (as delivered) things are OK, within reason.  If you stop applying
Armorall, the solvents dry up and the silicone precipitates out as a
whitish stain.  I don't think the silicone is doing the leather any good,
but that's just an opinion.  What may be helping is the oily solution
in which the silicones are delivered, and Hyde Food will do this better.
Absent the Hyde Food, I'd use Lexol (thin coat, well wiped down).  My
sense of the Armorall is that I don't like it anyway (too wet or slick an
appearance when used on rubber or vinyl) and that leather is a pretty
special commodity and needs its own stuff.  Same for WD-40, which
appeared later in the thread, I think.

Also, I'd think the Armorall would make the seats too slick, no?
