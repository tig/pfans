As you indicate, it may be only be a problem 'in the heat of battle', but
that grinding noise is usually  the sound of catching reverse in the 5-4
shift. I never had the problem on the street, but found it happening on the
track. Automotion sells a spring kit that is supposed to prevent this
problem in 915s, but I was unable to install it in a '72 shift tower.
Instead, I bought an SC shift tower and it was a 95% solution. I suspect
the rest of the solution is stiffer engine mounts...

>I've also occasionally experienced the lever seeming to stick in 5th.
>This hasn't happened often enough for me to describe it any better.
>When it has happened it's been a pretty firm stick, to the point
>where rather than forcing the lever I have simply let the engine
>wind down until the lever decided to come loose.  Has anyone else had
>this happen?

I had that trouble with a G 50, and ended up having the transmission taken
apart to solve it.  Some monkey had put an incorrect retaining pin in the
transmission. (The correct one was found at the bottom of the trans case.)
You are wise not to force it. the leverage of the shift linkage is
enormous, and can accelerate transmission damage if you get too heavy
handed. I know this from e$perien$e.
