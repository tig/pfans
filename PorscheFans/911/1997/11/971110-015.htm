I had a similar problem with my 85 911.  My conclusion was that the
idle was set too high.  The PO had set the idle high because the
car would stall.  I fixed the real problem.  Dirt and vacuum leaks.
Remove and clean the idle positioner.  Remove, disassemble and clean
the throttle body assembly (special attention to the idle air bleed
screw).  Find and fix vacuum leaks.  I attached my shop vacuum to
the air box and used smoke to look for leaks.  This took a long time
but the result is a nice stable idle and no $$$ spent.
