Scott,

	I recently had the same symptoms with my '72. 

	What it ended up being was a poor seal between the body and the top of
the charcoal canister in the trunk. I popped the top off and did some
quick work with clear urethane sealant. This seemed to do the trick.

	Fumes from the gas tank are forced to pass through this canister. The
activated charcoal within adsorbs the stinky stuff. When the car is
running, air from the cooling fan is blown through with the resulting
stinky air going to the engine via the air cleaner for burning. Result -
less emission of gasoline vapors into the atmosphere.

	If there is a leak in this system, downstream of the canister, gassy
fumes are blown out into the trunk or wherever the leak is.

	Hope this helps.
