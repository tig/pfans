needs of the real world out there which unfortunately includes very strict
emission laws and WAY too many lawyers (usually found driving Porsches or
BMWs).  Look at it this way...we're lucky to be able to drive these
wonderful automobiles, and Porsche is lucky to still be an independent car

don't think it's as nice looking as the 993 and so I decided to buy a 1997
911 C2.  I've always wanted a Porsche, and wasn't sure I liked the 996,
so....but I still think Porsche did the only thing they could do to
survive.  I'd say let's all hold back on our criticism until we actually
have a chance to see one and, more importantly, drive one.  Then at least
the criticism will be based on our own experiences, not pictures and
articles out of car magazines.
