> Mike, I strongly suspect the master cylinder is going bad; this is similar
> to my experiences on my old '76 when I had it.  I think I did 2-3 MC 
> replacements in the 150,000 miles I had the car.  I, too was using the 
> brake bleeding technique you described, several times per year due to
> running PCA track driver's education events.  I beleive that this bleeding
> technique pushes the MC piston seals past their normal wear limit, and 
> prematurely wears them out.  I switched to a pressure bleed technique in 
> early '90s and have not had a problem yet with MC seal damage.

>  Adnan-     It is very, very easy for a novice to wreck his first 911. Many 
> do. I almost did when I got my first rear engined buggy 15 years ago, but I 
> was very lucky. If an inexperienced 911 driver lets off the gas and/or brakes 
> in mid turn, or even applies so much gas as to break loose the rear wheel 
> traction, the back of the car may well pivot around like a pendulum, and then 
> you and the car leave the road backwards. This can happen very quickly. 
> 	This actually happened to me on a rainy night less than two weeks after I 
> bought my first 911. At about 45 mph, I came around a curve, and I saw a 
> branch down in the road; I swerved and braked to avoid it. In a split second, 
> I was spinning 360 degrees, and all I could do was wait for the outcome. I was 
> much more fortunate than many, as I slid across someone's lawn and came to 
> rest in his hedge... I had to paint a fender and door,   and buy eight new 
> hedge bushes... pretty lucky, wouldn't you say? The more recent cars such as 
> you are considering buying, are somewhat more forgiving, but they are still 
> rear engined 911's. It can still happen. Be careful, but have fun. 
> 	One can still cut safely with a sharp knife, or go skeet shooting with a 
> loaded shotgun; for me, the awareness of some danger makes for a more 
> exhilerating experience. If not, I would drive something competently dull, 
> like the  Nissan Altima you mention. I recommend getting some advice and help 
> from experienced 911 pilots. Join the PCA and go the driver's ed classes, 
> maybe? Yes, you will indeed find a 911 drives differently than a Nissan 
> Altima, Adnan.... Warren.
