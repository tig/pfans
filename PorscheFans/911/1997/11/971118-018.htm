> 	- The oil pressure gauge.. Ahem... Cold engine, it registers
> zero and stays there for at least 10 minutes as I drive at constant rpm
> (fwy). If I stop, after 5/110 minutes, if will register middle to 3/4
> full in neutral... Is that OK ? Yesterday at sustained 80mph , it was in
> the bottom third after the car got warm. And it bounces a lot ! 

I sure hope you mean "oil level gauge"!!!! (judging from the context, this
is what you mean).

It's normal to see a low or nonexistant reading when the engine is cold. It's
also normal to see a low or nonexistant reading when you're underway. The only
time the reading is close to being valid is with a warm engine, car idling
on level ground (after idling for 30-40 seconds or so). Nonetheless, don't
believe the gauge - check using the dipstick (again with the car idling and
on level ground same as above).

> 	- Druck ?  Is that like instant pressure ? That was pegged at
> 3.5 the whole trip. 

Druck is German for pressure. Sounds like you've got good oil pressure. THIS
is the gauge you don't want to see at 0 while running! ;^)

> 	- The steering wheel seems to vibrate a bit too much ...
> (slightly lowered car, no pwr steering, hard shocks). Could it be that
> plastic thing on the steering column the list was discussing a while
> back ?  I'll balance the tires anyway....

Vibrate over bumps or in a straight line? If the car's lowered, you will
probably get more bump steer than normal (and the 911 is known for having
a moderate amount of bump steer anyway). This can be partly alleviated by
installing a steering rack spacer (and re-doing the alignment).

If it's not bump steer, then the "normal" causes are all possibles:

- alignment
- tires
- wheel bearings
- brakes

etc etc.

Dunno about damage, but when cold, I try to keep mine between about 2500 and
4000 rpm. It's highly inadvisable to lug or really rev the engine with the
oil cold. I doubt you'd damage it at 2000 rpm, but high loading at very low
speed with cold oil is a bad thing.
