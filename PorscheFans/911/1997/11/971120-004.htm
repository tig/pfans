Chuck,
 I think the Bosch hammer is used on '89 and newer C4's and '90
and newer C2's. An '84-'89 Carrera doesn't use one to the best of 
my knowledge. Sorry if I'm stating the obvious.
  The Hammer is connected to a (big?) connector somewhere near
the passengers footwell I think.
 About that port on the left side of the engine compartment:
Baum Tools sells something (looks like a relay) that plugs
into it. It has a switch (for setting the idle adjustment
probably) and a couple of LED's. This is Baum's description of 
the tool: 

"9246 FOR IDLE SPEED ADJUSTMENT AND FAULT FLASHING CODE
Output for knock adjustment. 911 after 1985. 944 turbo/928"

I didn't think the 911 had knock sensing till '87 and I never
noticed a knock sensor on my '86. I'm really curious about this
one so please post what you find out.
