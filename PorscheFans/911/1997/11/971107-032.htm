Thank you Henry Watts and Larry Correnti for your contributions to the
thread on double clutching.  My 2 cents added to double clutching.

In typical street driving heel-and-toe/rev matching using synchros is
easy enough to get the hang of.  Double clutching for me produced 2
distinct scenarios.  The first is that the only difference between
double clutching and rev matching/downshifting from a driving result
perspective is that double clutching is much harder to realize with my
transmission.  The second observation(s) is that double clutching  makes
getting into first gear much, much easier.  I use it when slowing to
exit a main road into, for example, a residential street or a shopping
center parking lot to give noticeably easier shifting effort.  Blipping
with the clutch out before shifting into first at a stop also makes
getting into first gear very buttery.  Wwithout blipping it is a night
and day experience.  It may be different with the hydraulic set.
