This would scare me to death, if you are really referring to the pressure 
gauge, but I think you are talking about the oil LEVEL gauge, which
measures the top 3 quarts of a nominal 12 quart system.   Look at your
owner's manual, learn how to deal with oil levels.  VERY important.


>
>	- Druck ?  Is that like instant pressure ? That was pegged at
>3.5 the whole trip. 

Druck is the German word for pressure - OIL pressure.    The rule of 
thumb is at least ONE bar of pressure for each THOUSAND rpm.   If
you were showing 3.5 bar at 5000 rpm, I would be worried.

>
>	- Ok, the car's got 114 K miles, purrs like a cat, what's the
>deal with hydraulic chain tensioners ? If it lived this long without
>them.... ????

If it does not have them, get them YESTERDAY.   All other tensioners
are a TIME BOMB which will cost you a complete engine rebuild if they
fail.   If yours are the originals, they are WAY overdue.

>
>	- Is there a manual for climate controls in a FAQ somewhere ?
>;-) 
Don't know.   Get an owners manual.

>
>	- I can shift straight, no double clutching, no rev matching,
>(the tranny is new). Should I do the previous anyway to preserve it or
>is it OK to just shift it normally as long as it lasts ? 

911 synchronizers are not Porsche's best work.   Learn to double clutch
for downshifting, and just do it as a habit.   The transmission will last far
longer than if you don't.

You won't damage anything.    The older carburetted cars, running on leaded
fuel had a plug fouling problem.   SCs have no such problem.

>
>	- Saw a recommended maintenance section in the car's latest
>paperwork : Blower motor (something unlegible) and injector leak (more
>unlegible). Trouble ? 

Blower motors regularly fail.   Injectors must not leak, in order to hold system
pressure and prevent vapor lock when the engine is turned off and will be 
restarted when hot.
