Didn't mean to mislead you.  I too don't make it a habit of lugging the
engine because it really does stress the engine in ways it wasn't designed
to handle.  The only time I've nailed it from idle speed in various gears
was in looking for a mixture problem.  If it stumbles, it's a good
indicator that something is wrong with the fuel delivery/ignition system.
Otherwise, I just idle around without even touching the accelerator in
parking lots and when I get on the road, accelerate easily until around
2000rpm when the fun begins!

I'm continuously amazed at the quality of Porsche engineering.  I just ran
my first driver's ed this weekend and my 18-year-old car with 130k and
engine never opened up, ran 50-60 laps of Road Atlanta like a champ and
came back begging for more.  And I was most definitely NOT babying it!  All
hail Dr. Porsche!
