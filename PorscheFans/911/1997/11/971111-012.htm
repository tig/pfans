You may or may not be aware that Porsche actually produced a certificated
aircraft engine for several years in the '80s. The 3.2 litre unit was rated
at 200 HP at 5000 RPM. Mooney Aircraft produced the Mooney PFM with this
engine, and  several aftermarket shops offered conversions for Cessna 172s.
Like the one you mentioned, this one was geared down 2:1 to reduce prop tip
speed.

Mods for aircraft application included complete dual ignition and fuel
injection systems (inclduing the DME control computers, wiring harness and
A/C batteries, not just twin nozzles and plugs). The key advantage was
single-lever power operation (no mixture control) and a much smoother
engine feel.

The product was killed due to slow sales caused by the high price ($27,500
for conversion, and a $15-20k add-on to the Mooney's base price). I think
that PFM parts are still available from Porsche, however.
