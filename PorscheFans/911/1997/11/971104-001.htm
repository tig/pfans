If you find you have blown a fuse on one of the footwell auxilliary heater
blower motors (in '84 there is no fuse at all for these, so it just burns until
something melts enough so the burning stops!), consider well WHY the
fuse blew.  Fuses, being inert sorts of beings, seldom just blow of old
age or too much jostling.  Earlier this year someone favored the list with
an explanation:  the cheap plastic fan part, which is only a press fit on its
steel shaft, can creep closer to the plastic housing.  Under provocation
(like long use?) things can start to touch.  These devices cost quite a bit,
considering their size and mass produced old style technology.  Don't
know quite what one might do to fend off the inevitable.  Take it out (not
hard), spin it, look at clearances.  Maybe you could pry or push the
rotating squirrel cage a tad in what appeared to be the right direction.
