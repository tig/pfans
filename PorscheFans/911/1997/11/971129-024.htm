Depending on the change, yes you will screw up your alignment as the two
eccentrics adjust rear toe in and camber at rest, both of which change a
fair bit as the wheel moves through its suspension travel. Raising ride
height significantly (like more than 1/2" or so) will make a noticeable
change.

Correct procedure is to set ride height/corner weights first and then
set toe and camber. Ray Scruggs has a neat little pamphlet which has
good ideas which can be used to adjust things yourself. It is not hard,
but takes time and precision. Only major problem for the do it
yourselfer is setting the corner weights correctly without the benefit
of 4 scales.........
