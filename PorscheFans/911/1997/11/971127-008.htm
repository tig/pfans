You DO have an air box and the second paragraph was my explanation of the
airbox and what
happens if it is blown.  The pop-off valve costs somewhere around $12-15,
the air box is around 
$350 (the last time I looked).  Installation of the pop-off valve is
relatively easy with the engine out
of the car, but is a pain with the engine in due to the lack of clearance. 
Detailed instructions should
come with the valve itself, but basically involves drilling a roughly 3"
hole down from the upper 
chamber to the lower chamber and gluing the valve into the box with epoxy
provided with the 
installation kit.

> If you've replaced the "air box" with a K&N cone, you don't have an air
box
> and don't have to worry
> about it.  The air box referred to is on Bosch K-Jetronic injection
> equipped cars (pre '84).  It sounds 
> as if you have the Motronic (EFI) system.
> 
> The air box in the K-Jet cars is subject to being blown because
> all air being fed to the intake runners is routed through the bottom
> chamber of the air box after the
> throttle.  In the event of a backfire, the pressure gets pushed back up
the
> intake runner into the
> sealed plastic box.  The pop-off valve is spring loaded and allows the
> excess pressure to be vented
> to the upper chamber (where the air filter is).  If the box is blown (not
> literally in most cases) unmetered
> air leaks into the lower chamber causing the mixture to run way too lean
> causing the engine to run
> poorly, at best.
> 
> Hope this helps...
