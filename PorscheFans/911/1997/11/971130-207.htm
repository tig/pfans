Answer:
Hi John,
I have experience with both units. The Whipple is excellent! I use it on a
Carrera 4 as a daily driver and track driving. I plan to do Car & Drivers
One Lap of America with this car in 1998. Fairly pricy installation for the
3.6 engine but a bolt-on system for the 3.0 and 3.2 engine due to starting
with a lower compression ratio. My wife had a Mercedes 300E with a Paxton
system from Mosselman. It made 330 hp the engine and was fine but the
supercharger itself was not reliable. I've heard that Paxton has improved on
the design. Also a friend of mine had a 944 with the Paxton system from SoK,
it ran great as well. No probs with supercharger but he was a little too
free with adjusting the timing advance and burnt a piston. So, these systems
can be very reliable but must be respected.
I had a 1979 911SC for several years. One thing I've found in all the
Porsche techie info is the 3 liter engines have the strongest crank of any
model. The pistons are excellent but very close tolerance. Most owners
won't pay the extra to lower the compression so the boost level must be kept
low at 5 - 7 psi. This is plenty and yes you can get 300 hp safely from the
911SC engine.
In talking to Mike Parker at SoK they have tested the Whipple on the 3 liter
for quite a long time to be sure of the quality and reliability. This would
be my choice. Low boost but plent of torque!! The Paxton would require a
Turbo tail and the Whipple should not.
Any more questions please let me know or if your serious call Mike at
Supercharging of Knoxville.
Later,
Zane Dobson
