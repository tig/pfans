Bob Tindel wrote:

I don't like the way the air hose that goes from the heater blower to
the right side of the engine is squashed by the AC compressor. Has
anyone found a solution to this? I'm thinking of using a section of
rubber or plastic hose to replace the paper/wire hose where it goes
under the compressor.

As an alternate, if I replace the AC compressor with a rotary
compressor, would this allow me to cut the AC bracket to accomodate the
air hose?

	The best solution to this problem IMO was done by the factory, I
saw it on a '85 Euro 911. The fan output connection was different than
the US versions, the hose for the right side went forward around the
front side of the engine and then to the rear and through the same hole
in the sheet metal to the right side heat exchanger. The left side went
the normal way to the left heat exchanger, both sides used the 50 mm
paper hoses. I don't know if that euro fan output part is available, but
it would not be too hard to make one.
