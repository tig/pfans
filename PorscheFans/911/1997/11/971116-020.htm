Replace the rear window seal.  It's simple, it's relatively cheap, and
takes two hours.  Some advise:  use the factory seal, not the
reproduction.  I tried what I was told was a factory piece only to try
4-5 times to install it without success.  I sent it back and ordered the
OEM part from Stoddards and it went in the first try ( although I had
lots of practice with the 1st seal).  My experience is that the rear
window is much trickier to install than the front, which I can remove
and install in 30-45 min.

Start by carefully removing the aluminum trim from the rubber seal.  I
use a bicycle tire iron (made out of high strength plastic). After
sliding each center trim clip to the side, get the tool under the trim
and draw it towards you.  As you do this the trim will lift right out.
Be careful not to bend or kink the trim as it is expensive.  Once the
trim is out use a utility knife to cut the rubber holding the glass in.
Careful not to damage defrost harness, if you have one, located within
seal. Now, lift glass out and remove seal from window hole.

Working on a rubber trash can, install new seal on glass as noted when
you removed old seal.  Then press aluminum trim (2 halves) into seal and
don't forget center clips.  This pressing uses alot of thumb strength.
The next step is the "trick" for the whole installation.  Into the inner
flap of the seal that eventually fits over the lip in the window frame
(where the headliner material wraps around), press a piece of smooth
rope (1/8" diam) around the entire perimeter of the window assembly and
overlap the two ends of the rope.

Next, drop the window in the hole and pull the rope from the inside of
the car while a friend presses the window and seal down from the
outside.  You'll see that as you pull the rope, it draws the rubber flap
into the car and over the lip of the window frame.  You may want to use
some soapy water to help lubricate this process.  The installation can
be tricky and may take several trys, even for those who have done it
before.  The common problem is that the aluminum trip pops out before
the window is fully installed.  Just take your time.  E-mail if you have
more questions.  Good Luck.
