I don't know the "Northland" oil either, but 11 quarts is the right number.
Oil expands as it warms, and the owner's manual will tell you to check it
when the car is idling and WARM. Sounds like you checked it right after
start-up. 

Normally, adding an extra quart isn't such a big deal, since highway oil
temps don't get all that high. However, if you go to a driver's school
event, that extra oil could contribute to an overfull condition (much
hotter oil occupies higher volume) and dump out through the breather. Oil
on track = REALLY upset event workers......

Doubleclutching: Yes, but... Usually used to downshift only. After
clutching to disengage, "blip" the throttle to bring the RPMs up nearer the
level they will be in the lower gear, then clutch to engage teh lower gear.
Save wear on the syncros, add wear on the clutch. Take your pick, pay your
$$$.
