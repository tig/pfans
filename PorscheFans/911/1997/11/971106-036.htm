Here is an excerpt from the April 21, 1989 Road & Track, Special Series,
"44 Enthusiast Cars, Road Test Annual."

"A road car with racing pretensions, that's what the 911 Carrera Club
Sport is.  Painted Grand Prix White, it has no fog-lamps, engine- and
luggage-compartment lights, radio, air conditioning, automatic heat
control, rear seats and belts, lids for door-storage compartments,
clothes hooks, sound insulation (except in the engine bay and roof),
interior trim panels, passenger sun visor, Carrera badge on the rear
deck lid or electrically actuated windows, door locks and seats.  All of
these niceties--plus switches, wiring, latches and geegaws too numerous
to mention--are missing from the 911 Club Sport, which weighs in at a
fit 2600 lb, 155 lb less than the 911 coupe.

"What the Carrera Lite does have is manually adjustable seats with sport
(bolstered) backs and normal (unbolstered) bottoms, carpeted rear parcel
deck with cubbies, shortened shifter, special tachometer with higher
redline, sport shock absorbers, 16 x 6-in. front/16 x 7-in. rear
forged-alloy wheels with Dunlop SP Sport D40 tires (205/55VR-16 front,
255/50VR-16 rear); stiffer 911 Cabrio engine mounts, Digital Motor
Electronics control unit recalibrated for higher maximum permissible
engine speed (6840 vs 6520) and hollow intake valves (to accommodate the
increased rev range of the engine that develops 214 bhp, the same as the
911 Carrera)"

Hope this helps.

Juergen
