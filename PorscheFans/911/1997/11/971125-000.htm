Replacing the channel felt will require removing the glass and as I
recall, I removed the window frame from the door to install the felt.
If you haven't done so, this is also a good time to replace the 1/4
window seals, squeegee, and the inner and outer fuzzy strip at the top
of the door shell.  All of these parts, except the 1/4 seal, are
inexpensive.  As you disassemble the frame and remove the old seals
you'll see that assembly is pretty straight forward.

After I replaced the channel felt in my '68 912, I found that the window
was difficult to role up, even after adjusting the frame.  This will
result in worn window regulators and handles.  I believe the difficulty
is due to the later style channel material, which is a soft flocked
rubber on all three sides of the frame.  The original channel material I
removed was a flexible metal section lined with cloth. My advise, which
I hope to follow soon, is to use something close to the original.

In the November 1997 issue of Pano, on page 74, a member asked for
advise on this very subject.  According to the article, there is a rigid
cloth covered channel available from BD Company (303/744-1405) that is a
better substitute for the original.  If you have any questions about the
details of this project, feel free to e-mail and I'll do my best to
help.  Greg
