> Mark:
> Well fire maybe, but the reason to disable the pump is to keep the
> engine from starting without disturbing the rest of the wires plugs et
> all and perhaps inducing another problem along the way.  BTW your
> description of the small back fires is exactly what I had.  When you
> find the offending cylinder and check the wire lead and plug, I
> suggest swapping the injector.  They pull straight up and out and are
> held in via a rubber doughnut seal.   Leave the fuel line attached at
> first as the injectors are not cylinder dependent and you can check
> both injector and fuel distributor port in one shot.  If the problem
> moves from one cylinder to another you could have either a bad
> injector, or fuel distributor delivery problem (what I had).  If the
> problem doesn't move my next move would be a pressure and leak down
> check to the offending cylinder.
