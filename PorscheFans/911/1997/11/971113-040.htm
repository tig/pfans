Joel,
I just installed these same shift coupler bushings today in a '69 911T. The
pin that inserts into the donut-like holes of the bushings can be driven
out. If you look at the coupler housing, you will notice that there are four
equally spaced reinforcement splines on one side. These allow that side of
the coupler to be placed on a solid surface like a vice or anvil. The solid
surface also should have a hole or gap in it to allow the pin to pass as you
drive it out. A drift punch and a hammer work well for driving. Once the pin
is out, the rest is easily taken apart. Do the reverse to reassemble and
remember to lubricate. Shifting should feel tight again.
