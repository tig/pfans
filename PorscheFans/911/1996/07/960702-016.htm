Yeah, I already did this.  I cut out two "plus-sign" shapes from a
square piece of tin, and bent the four flaps around the four sides of
each of the rectangular openings.  I then slid hose clamps (big ones)
over the rectangular openings and tin sheets, and tightened them down. 
This gave me an excellent seal that will hold up to the beating the
underside of the car takes.  How critical is it that the seal between
the engine tin and the sides of the car are good?  I've got good sized
gaps there...
