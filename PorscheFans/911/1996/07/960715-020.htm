Thanks for the response.  In your opinion, is this the only one I should
go for?  I have seen others in Tweeks, Performance Products, et.al., for
prices ranging as low as $94.  But, I have come to realize that with
lower prices comes compromise in some sort of performance (usually). 
For me, it seems that I want a muffler that does its job--reduces
noise--but does not hinder engine performance (excessive back
pressure).  When I start going for lower-priced mufflers, what am
compromising?  Is it decreased life (early failure due to corrosion),
higher failure rates (poor quality), decreased performance (does not
adequately muffle), detrimental impact on engine performance (high
back-pressure), or a combination of them all?  I certainly want the most
inexpensive muffler, but am not willing to compromise in all these
areas. The examples I have found are:
