Hi Joe,

The oil return tubes are not easy to replace, but it is something that you
can do. It is much easier on a lift. You take the old ones out by crushing
them with a big pair of pliers, twisting and yanking and out they come. It is
not quite so easy to get the new ones in, it requires a lot of fiddling and
swearing. 

You have to take a lot of stuff off to get to the timing chain cover. Support
the engine and remove the muffler, the rear sheet metal and the engine mount
and then the cover. The gaskets go on a clean surface with no sealant. 

So what oil are you using?
