Bart G. Denys  wrote:

> it's my understanding that sway bars are best adjusted with "no
> preload". Hence adjustable drop links. I am confused, howvere, as to
> how to do it: it appears that my bars are adjusted to have "no load"
> with the car on the lift. So when the car is lowered back to the
> floor and the suspension arms go up, I assume that a considerable
> preload is put on the bars, effectively working as an additional
> spring. Is this so and is this the way to do it ? 

Bart:

If your car's corner-balanced and your springs are of a decent
quality (i.e., they have equal rates left-to-right), a swaybar
adjusted with no preload when the wheels are in full droop (on the
lift) will ALSO have no preload when the wheels are pushed up; the
swaybar will just rotate in its mounts when the car's lowered to the
ground.
