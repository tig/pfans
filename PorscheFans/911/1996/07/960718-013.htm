Hi Scott,

I bought a Makita 12V contractor grade cordless drill for this purpose. The
9.6V standard cordless drills didn't have enough torque.

I use a 12" long 3/8" extension in the drill's chuck, but had to "turn down"
the female end using a lathe to fit in the drill's 3/8" chuck. I suppose you
could just hacksaw the end off, though, as the extension's shaft is small
enough to fit.

Barry L's comments about a "T" wrench are true but as my car has (had) 12" deep
offset rear wheels, the wrench didn't reach far enough. Plus, any labor saved
at the track (in 105 degree Willow heat!) is very much appreciated!
