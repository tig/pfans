Steve,
   You're really straining the grey matter on this one. If I remember correctly,
the microswitch was used to turn on a smog control device which allowed
additional air to be introduced below the throttle plates. The transducer kept
track of how many RPMs you were turning. If you slammed the throttle shut above
a certain RPM range extra air was let in to cut down on  unburned  fuel going
out the tailpipe.
   Once your throttle reaches a certain point you hit the microswitch and the
circuit is opened. Doubt if any of this stuff is still working on your engine. I
removed it from my 2.2 carb motor and plugged the holes.
