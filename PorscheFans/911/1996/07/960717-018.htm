Andy,

When you lower a car by one inch, it is pretty normal that the 
springs are one inch shorter than the original ones. When you lower a 
car by one inch, you have one inch less suspension travel. Also one 
inch less ground clearance.

Based on the 911's I'ver seen with stock (US spec) springs, the 
perches are at the very bottom of their travel. The springs are lnger 
than the Euro ones.  When you put on a euro ride height spring it is 
normal that it be loose, that because the perches are at the bottom 
to handle the longer spring. T'aint nuttin to worry about. If you 
forget to adjust the perch you'll have so little ground clearance in 
the front that you'll have trouble getting out of the driveway.
