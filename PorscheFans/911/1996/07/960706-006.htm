Howdy,

I'm not an "expert" on engine work but have been adjusting my
911S valves for 11 years with no problems.

Bob Spindel wrote :
 
>>Congrats on trying to adjust them yourself.  It is pretty
 hard to slip the feeler gauge in there, as others have
 mentioned, and simply loosening them a bit and re-setting
 them seems to be what most folks do.<<

Using the automotion type tool, you can usually get it in there
after getting the oil out of the gap by moving the cap a bit.
Then if it feels good, no need to loosen the nut and adjust.

<<  I sometimes "cheat",
 in that I set one valve and then feel how much it moves
 simply by grabbing it and moving it.  I then feel the movement
 on the others, and if it's about the same, I don't adjust
 anything. >>

Very interesting, I'll try it next time!

<<  It's also really amazing how when you use
 a feeler gauge on the valves you almost always wind
 up with a loarge setting than 0.004".  I've experimented with
 a feeler gauge and then checking with a depth gauge that could read
 to 0.0001", and the feeler gauge method always resulted in
 an actual lash of 0.005 or more. >>

If you go for the "STIFF sliding fit" of the feeler and can just
barely slide it, it should be .004". You can really feel the
difference in slideability when you adjust it a bit.
 
<< I think the bottom line on all this is that the
 gap, while important to set right, isn't critical
 to ten thousandths of an inch. >>

I hope so, because when one of my valves feels close enough,
I note it ("a bit tight/loose" in my log book) and go to the
next one. Then next time I may adjust it if it's worse.
