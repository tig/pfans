Subject:Microswitch and RPM Transducer on MFI 911's
Date:Thu, 04 Jul 1996 08:34:13 -0700
I have studied the MFI too much for one life time and enjoy my Webers now.  
The microswitch is to sense when the throttle is closed, and the RPM 
Transducer gives power the microswitch between 3-4k RPM.  I the revs are up 
and the throttle is closed a fuel cut off valve is closed in the front of 
the MFI pump.  The RPM Transducer is located beside the rear window de-fog 
box in the engine compartment.  (it has 4 tabs)
