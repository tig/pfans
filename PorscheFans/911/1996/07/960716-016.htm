    As I said in that message, it's unlikely that the guy will

    Springs that uncouple at full droop are WRONG.  Limiting the
    suspension travel to "correct" for those short springs is also
    wrong.  If you think too-short springs or limited suspension
    travel is in any way an ADVANTAGE, please let me know why... I'd
    really like to hear your reasoning.

> 2) I like the clutch engagement down near the floor for a lot of
> reasons:
> 
>     a) It is faster, you can feather the clutch in with a minimum of
>     motion. There is less uncertainty as to where engagement begins,
>     it begins almost as soon as you lift your foot.

    I have no experience with the new 911s (you drive a 993, right?),
    but none of the older 911 clutches want to be slipped at all.  If
    you're properly matching engine speed during your shifts, you
    should be able to treat the clutch as an on/off switch...
    "Feathering" it is neither necessary nor desirable.

>     b) I have better "fine motor control" with my leg close to
>     extended than when my knee is bent a lot. I think this is a
>     quirk of the human body.

    Actually, it's a geometric, not physiological, thing... For a
    given shortening of the distance between your hip and ankle,
    relatively more change in knee angle is required when your leg's
    extended than when it's bent.  Since you gauge the position of
    your ankle from the angle of your knee, this means that you can
    sense it more precisely when your leg's extended.

    Although you're right about this, my comment above still
    applies.
