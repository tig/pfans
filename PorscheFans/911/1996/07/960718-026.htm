You need more than an alignment.  Any change in springs should accompany a
complete corner balance.  You will need to seek out a shop that has a
complete set of corner balance scales.  Make sure that they have 4 scales and
not just some crude lever on a bathroom scale method of weighing the car.

After this is done, you can have the car aligned.  Yes, specialty shops
charge more for this than the local "well if it drives straight it must be
done" alignment shops.  There usually is a good reason.  

A quality corner balance and alignment will not only make the car drive
better (and faster through the corners), but will save a small fortune on
tires.

If you do mostly street driving, you will want to be very conservative on the
camber.  We generally set up the street 928's with about 10 to 20 minutes of
less negative camber in the front than the factory calls for.  This generally
gives good tire wear.

If you are going to use the car for track and street use, set the front
camber at stock specs.
