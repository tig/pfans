>I was all set to tackle a valve adjustment on my '88 911, but found that I
>was unable to get a spark plug socket on the #3 (??I think??) plug.  It's
>the most forward plug on the right engine side.  My 6" extension with spark
>plug socket was too long to fit in the spark plug hole without comming into
>contact with the surrounding body work.  It would be even longer with a
>spark plug on the end.  I was going to try two 4" extensions with a swivel
>in the middle, but that seemed a bit precarious.  I guess my question is,
>is there a preferred tool or a "secret" to getting that plug out?  Also, is
>it recommended to use a "locking" type socket to hold the spark plug socket
>onto the extension?  Thanks for your help!

Use as short an extension as possible.  I'd avoid joints if you can, they only
make it easier to cross-thread the spark plug going in, and they definitely
muddy the feel as you try to start the threads.

My personal weapon of choice for a 911 engine is a miniature Snap-On 3/8"
ratchet, the smallest extension in my set (maybe 1"?), and a double-jointed
Snap-On spark plug socket.  Yep, I'm not crazy about item #3, and would
rather be using a straight socket with a slightly longer extension.  The
socket, like most, has a rubber insert which does a good job of grabbing
the plug.
