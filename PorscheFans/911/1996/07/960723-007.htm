To all fans of sports car racing from the late '50s/early '60s:

We just bought a really cool video of sports car racing in California from the
late 1950s through the very early '60s, called _Hay Bales and Asphalt_.  There
are lots of Porsches (356 ,
 RS60, RSK, etc.)  There are also Scarabs, Jags, Mercedes, Old Yellers, MGs, 
Corvettes (including the 1st Stingray and one of the CERVs), etc.  Drivers
shown: Dan Gurney, Lance Reventlow, Stirling Moss, Jo Bonier, Jim Clark, Phil
Hill, Max Belchowski (sp?), Carroll Shelby, etc.  Cameo celebrity appearances
by Jill St. John, David Niven, Jayne Mansfield.  My husband's uncle, Ted
Conrad, raced in that series in that era, driving Carrera Speedsters (and 
other cars).  Not sure if we saw him or not, it wasn't always easy to see the
vehicle numbers.  Race tracks shown: Laguna, Vaca Valley, Pomona, Riverside...

The race footage has been nicely restored.  Much better than other old racing
footage I've seen.  Intelligently narrated.  All in all, a really enjoyable
video.  It's really cool to see many of the cars that are still being run on
the Historic race circuit.  Now I'm madder then ever that I can't make it to
Laguna Seca this August!!!  The guy who made it, Clark Smith, seems like a 
really nice guy, too.

It's available from Inside Line, (415) 595-3580 (San Carlos, CA).  The video
cost $40.  Even though it is short (30 min.), I think it was worth every penny.

I'm not connected with Inside Line, I'm just a happy customer.

Enjoy!
