Craig,

        I used the aftermarket plastic bushing sold by Performance, and
their kind.  Remove the horn pad, and steering wheel (5 minutes max.)  Then
slide the new bushing onto the steering shaft.  When it reaches the bearing,
it will be tight.  You will have to drive it on.  This requires a sleeve of
the correct diameter to just clear the steering shaft, so that you can drive
(with a hammer) the new bushing into place.  Don't use a drift punch, or you
will probably destroy the new bushing.  I used a 12 pt. socket of the right
size, and it worked fine.  Replace the wheel and horn pad.  Note the wheel
orientation when you remove it so that you can replace it in the same
position.  The nut that holds the wheel on is largish - I don't know the
size.  I used an appropriate english socket w/o complication.
