> I've hooked up an external oil cooler to the front bumper of my '76
> 914 2.0 and had amazing results.  My oil temperatures are less than

> never crossed the half-way point no matter how hard I run it.  Now,
> I realize that 914's and 911's are air cooled, but to some extent
> they're also oil cooled, right?  Does the oil temp accurately
> represent "engine temp", or is there a chance that even though my
> oil is cool, my heads are ferociously hot since there was no
> modification made to the fan or air flow mechanisms? 

JJ:

Assuming that your oil pump, sender, and gauge are working properly
(and that your sender is properly calibrated to your gauge), the 
oil-temperature reading DOES accurately represent engine temperature.

On a 914/4, which normally holds about 4 quarts of oil, the addition
of an external cooler (especially one mounted up front, with long
lines to it) adds 20% or more to the total volume of oil.  It's not
surprising, therefore, that you've had such amazing results...
Significantly more oil + cooler oil = A 914 that runs almost cold.
