Hi Steve,

Most 911 pistons have the flywheel end marked on the pistons to save this
confusion. I think that the big bump goes toward the inlet valve and the
hollow or recess goes down toward the exhaust valve. 

I think that you should save your manual for when you run out of toilet
paper, it is a very poor misleading document. 

Measure the clearance when you put it together and make sure you are going to
have clearance and avoid the pink, pink, pink, thud. After you time the cams
try adjusting the intake valve adjustment in one and a half turns from zero
lash. Then very slowly and carefully turn the engine over 720 degrees. If it
goes around you have over 0.060". The valve lash screw is a 1 mm per thread
pitch so if you turn it a full turn you have 1 mm clearance or 0.03937", so
one and a half turn is 0.060" clearance. You can also check the exhaust
clearance the same way.
