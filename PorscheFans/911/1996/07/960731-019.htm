Mark,

I'm surprised your shop is surprised about doing the rebuild yourself. When I
did a rebuild of my right rear caliper, it turned out much much more easier than
I had imagined.
My tips:
If you don't get a piston out, put some wood between the jaws and use compressed
air. Just hold the "air-pistol"  close to the nipple and blow away (the wood (+-
3 cm. thick) avoids the piston from becoming a deadly projectile).
Getting the pistons all the way back in can be a real pain. Don't use force but
make sure the rubber ring is in the right place (take the piston back out to
check if you're not sure) and use a twisting motion.
For the rest, the rebuild is pretty straight forward.

Good luck,
