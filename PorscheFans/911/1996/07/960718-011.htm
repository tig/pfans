While what Mike says is true about it being the sellers responsibility in CA
(as it is in MA), it is still worth your while to have the test re-done when
you buy a car from a private party.  They could have gotten a positive result
from a friend who does smog tests, or by slipping the tester a $gift$.  If you
assume the sellers smog test is legitimate, but it really wasn't, YOU will have
to pay for repairs, etc. the next time you have to get the car smogged.  If it
fails when you have it retested immediately after buying it, you have can go
back to the seller with a justified complaint, and a good case if you have to
pursue it further.  I think an immediate retest is an excellent idea.
