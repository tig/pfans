  The answer is:  maybe.  (imho, of course)

  On some 911's (CIS?) the fuel loop pressure is
5 bar (75 psi). . .and oil pressure is what? 5 bar?
  and on 914's the pressure is 2.5 bar (45psi) with
oil pressure peaking around 4 or 5 bar.

  Since the sensor is built to withstand this application
perhaps this would work.  One potential problem
might be fuel pulsation in the loop either shocking
the bejeebers out of the mechanism, or giving shaky
numbers.   But if your fuel pressure fails you will get a
better indication first felt in the seat of the pants. . .

  Oddly enough, this might even register correctly.
