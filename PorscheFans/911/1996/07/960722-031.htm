The guy seemed responsible, sincere and honest, was forthcoming with
stuff about the car, even drew my attention to spots of rust I might
have missed.  Sadly, he never kept records. Has owned the car since its
first 100 miles, and has driven it only on western mass roads -- ie.
not bad bad boston stop-and-go traffic. 95K miles.  Never rebuilt.
Life and familiy has gotten too busy to enjoy his "porsh", so would
like to sell.  He dislikes "oil additives and junk like that" too
(cool!).

Questions:

1. My main plans for this car is to let me find out what this
"autocross thing" is all about, maybe in what is left yet of this
season :) So the plan is to buy a car that is OK enough, and just
update tensioners right away and struts if need be. I can afford to do
a pre-emptive top-end rebuild sometime if need be.

That the car has no A/C or CD, and no show spoilers and show wings and
other junk (I dont feel any inadequacies in me that I need my car to
mask :-), and is bone-stock original steel-work -- all this is a great
plus!

2. Of course, there is some rust. (Not unless you really looked, will
still impress the neighbours as is, for what that is worth :-)

   a. Driver side door sill, at the very rear end, a 1in square hole
   b. Bubbles under paint, one on edge of hood and one on rear right
      quarterpanel. Total two. Each the size of a quarter.

No rust at all that I could see anywhere on the underpan, undercoating
seems clean and continuous.  No rust in battery compartment.
Suspensions seemed solid -- the usual "bounce the bumper" test was no
use :-), as it should be with strongly suspended P-cars.
