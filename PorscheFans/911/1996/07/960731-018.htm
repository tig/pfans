Don't go to sleep to fast:
I think the temperature you measured is NOT your "engine-oil"  
temperature but just the temperature of the oil in the tank.
The engine temperature is a result of the relatively cool oil which goes in to
the engine, and the heat added to it by six hardworking cylinders.

So: the temperature in the oil-tank is always lower than the actual engine
temperature.
I don't have any clue what is the normal reading for the temperature in the oil
tank but I'm sure someone on the list does (or tells me my opinion should
