> Friends,
> it's my understanding that sway bars are best adjusted with "no preload".
> Hence adjustable drop links. I am confused, howvere, as to how to do it: it
> appears that my bars are adjusted to have "no load" with the car on the
> lift. So when the car is lowered back to the floor and the suspension arms
> go up, I assume that a considerable preload is put on the bars, effectively
> working as an additional spring. Is this so and is this the way to do it ?

Remember, sway bars only experience load when one wheel moves more
than, or in the opposite direction of, the other.  It functions
to counter the "relative" movement of one wheel to the other. If
both wheels move the same amount in the same direction, then the
sway bar just pivots in it's mounts.  So, as long as both wheels
drop the same amount when the car's up on the lift and you install
the bar ... then when you drop the car, each wheel should
move the same amount (ie. no "relative" movement of one wheel to
the other) so the sway bar should still be unloaded.
