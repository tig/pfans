 Hi Steve,

I quote from the factory manual regarding CIS piston installation.
 Unfortunately I can't reproduce the accompanying photograph, but I'll
interject my own descriptive notes.
"Pistons for K-jetronic equipped engines have a turbulence (whirl) cavity in
the piston top (crown).  This provision ensures a particularly thorough
blending of the fuel/air mixture.  The piston must always be installed so
that the flat spot (which is opposite the "whirl cavity") in the piston crown
faces upward (towards the intake valve).

In other words, the "whirl cavity" faces down and the "flat spot" faces up.
 In reality when the piston is oriented correctly, the depression is more
toward the lower right, and if you look more closely, you will see that the
depression (or whirl chamber) is below the spark plug which, if you are
looking at the engine from the side, (and the head is installed correctly)
will always be on the right side of the combustion chamber.
 Installing the piston upside down, (whirl cavity oriented towards the upper
left) will result in contact between the piston and the intake valve :-(
