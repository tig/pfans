> However, they are worn mostly on the INSIDE.  Do I need to do
> something other than having new tires mounted?  (Okay - I am pretty
> clueless about this - but I would like to be able to communicate
> with my mechanic)

    Unless there's something drastically wrong with your front toe
    (which I'd doubt, given that the tires have lasted so long), the
    tires are wearing only because they have lots of negative camber
    (that is, they lean inward).  Negative camber (up to a point)
    helps cornering performance by arranging things so that when
    you're cornering hard and the outside tire leans outward, it
    ends up essentially vertical.

    If you only (or mostly) plan to drive your new car on the
    street, you'll find that the inside edges of the tires will wear
    more rapidly than they should.  You can correct this by aligning
    the car with less (that is, less negative) camber.

> For summers, I would like some good performance street tires.  I was
> planning to stick with stock 205/55 and 225/50's.  However, I would
> like some feedback on the pro's / con's of wider tires.

    Pro:  They look really bitchin'.

    Con:  They may not fit in your wheel wells.

          They're more expensive.

          If you try to squeeze too wide a tire onto your existing
          wheels, you may end up with a SMALLER tire footprint,
          since the sidewalls may have to be pulled in so far that
          the tread shoulders lift off the ground.

          Unless your wider rear tires also have a lower
          aspect-ratio than the stock tires (that is, unless the
          second number in the tire-size designation is lower than
          50), your speedometer will read low.
