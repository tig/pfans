>Another quick question, the pop-off valve in the airbox --
>is it to be installed such that the lid opens outwards, or
>such that the lid opens inwards to let _IN_ air?
>
>I thought the pop-off valve protects the air-box from an
>explosion by venting _OUT_. Or should I re-take Physics 101?
>
>Why do I ask ? The '74 911 I am looking at has it installed
>such that in will open inwards, inside the airbox. Hmm,...
>I got to be very very alert with this thing now.

The pop valve should be installed so that it opens "out".  Its
purpose is purely to vent the expanding gases that would other-
wise make their own vent in your airbox.  If it is installed
correctly, it will open up and rotate toward you as you are looking
at it with the air filter removed.

How in the world did someone actually install the thing back-wards?
