Vic-
It is possible to replace the thermostatic element in the oilcooler
valve, I did mine several years ago.   I think that I got the new
temperature sensitive cartridge from Engine Builders Supply, but I am not
absolutely sure of that.  I suspect that the cartridge is an off the
shelf item.  It is the standard design wax cartridge that you see in a
lot of other applications where a valve is controlled by temperature.
It is similar to, only larger than, the temperature element in a coolant
thermostat in a "water pumper".  The biggest problem is getting the oil
pipes loose from the valve body, but once you do that, the valve is easy
to get apart with a drag link socket.
