Bob Spindel says

>Another opinion.  I don't think it much matters what you
clean the pistons with.  Clean pistons are important because
as Kevin Gross points out, the crud can ruin something else, but the
important part to have clean is the piston bores.  
>>The pistons
themselves don't actually touch anything.  It's the rubber
seal on the piston that rides against the bore, not the
piston itself.  It's the bore that needs to be clean and
pit-free.

No, it's the other way around. The pistons DO touch something. The rubber
seal is installed in a groove in the caliper, and rubs against the piston. If
the piston has flaking chrome plating in the area where it passes the seal,
it's trash, get another piston. The flaking will cut the sealing surface of
the piston in no time.
