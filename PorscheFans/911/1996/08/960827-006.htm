>Marc Gelefsky  wrote:
>
>> I have been contemplating changing the wheels on my 911, I have an
>> 86 Carrera and would like to put the 5 spoke style that is found on
>> the 993 and later 964's on my car. I know the offset is differant
>> and I could use spacers but does anyone have experiance with a good
>> aftermarket repro with the proper offset?
>
>    Do what you like, but keep in mind that:
>
>        1) the offsets are WAY different; putting positive-offset
>        wheels on the front of your car is likely to negatively
>        affect the car's handling.

I don't agree. If you use the right combination of studs and spacers, you
end up with the wheel in the same position, regardless of offset.  It seems
like a cheesy way of doing things, but remember, Porsche did exactly that
with the 930 Turbo.

>        2) your existing Fuchs wheels are among the best in the
>        world; if you trade them for repro 993-style wheels, you'll
>        be paying good money for inferior quality.

Nearly all of the newer Porsche wheels are inferior to the Fuchs, because
they are cast rather than forged.  There are many aftermarket wheels
superior to Factory Porsche wheels now. Just ask anyone who has genuine 993
Turbo wheels that won't hold air.

>        3) the long studs you'll need are very expensive, and
>        installing them is a royal pain.

>        4) spacers aren't cheap, either.

Long studs are neither expensive nor difficult to install. For instance:
the 993 comes with the wrong offset on the front wheels, and a common
modification is 21 or 23mm spacers and 66mm studs. I did this conversion to
my car in about an hour and a half, and the price was about $140. The rear
is probably more complicated, but the process is simple, and even a pricey
mechanic probably won't charge more than $200.

>> Should I stay with my current size? 16" 7's and 8's?? What other
>> size options are there?
>
>    993 wheels, as far as I know, are 17" in diameter, so repros are
>    likely to be that size, too.

993s come with 16" wheels. 17" are a common option. Around here, the 16"
versions are fairly inexpensive.
