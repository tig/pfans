Kevin:

You don't need anything as complicated as a Java-based calculator...
For a tire size A/B-C (e.g., 225/50-15), A is the nominal tread width
in millimeters, B is the sidewall height expressed as a percentage of
the tread width, and C is the wheel diameter in inches.

Therefore, the formula for tire diameter in inches is:

     AB
    ---- + C,
    1270

and the diameter in millimeters is:

    AB
    -- + 25.4C
    50

Your 225/50-15s have a nominal diameter of 23.86 inches.  The
215/60-15s from your 944 have a nominal diameter of 25.16 inches, so
if you put them on your 911, you'll raise the axle by 0.65 inches. 
Is that enough lift for your car to pass?
