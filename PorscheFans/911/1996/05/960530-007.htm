On 5/30/96 Robert H. Albrecht replied:

>Scott,
>
>This is a pretty common occurence, and does not necessarily indicate a
>problem.  My '83 SC has been doing this for the last 5 years/50K miles
>(since I've owned it.)  Oil consupmtion is nil, and it runs great.
>
>I've heard various explanations, the most recent being that the rings rotate
>in the piston grooves, , and that when the gap in the oil ring is on the
>bottom, oil seeps past.
>
>It's all related to the dry sump horizontal boxer engine.  When the sump is
>dry, as when running or shortly after being turned off, most of the oil is
>in the tank (and coolers, and lines).  When stopped, the oil will slowly
>seep past the oil pump back into the engine sump.  When it's deep enough to
>be sitting in the bottom portion of the cylinder sleeves, you are subject to
>the start-up smoking syndrome.  If this is the cause of your start-up smoke,
>it is not a problem.  Doesn't mean there can't be another cause that is a
>problem, but it sounds pretty normal to me.
>
>Bob
>
>Bob Albrecht, San Diego Region

Obviously this is also correct and possible. But if the occurence is after
sitting for only 1 night then my experience is that the guides or seals are
bad...but usually you get smoke on the overrun as well (at high RPM). Not a
serious problem unless the car is an older 911 with non-CD ignition (which
can't handle any plug fouling) or the smoke gets to the "bug killer" range.
