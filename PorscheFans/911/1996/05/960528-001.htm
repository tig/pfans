Actually, all may be generous.  How many of us are there now? :)

Thought I'd relate my weekend experience with a 951 crank as my first post.

Helping a friend prep his 944 Turbo for the Silver State coming up.  Bigger 
turbo, suspension, chips, exhaust, roll bar, etc. etc.

Apparently, the 944 has a nasty habit of starving the #2 rod bearing under 
certain circumstances, which I understand to be something like: Sweeping 
(left?) high speed turn, RPM >6000.

A remedy involves an oil pickup and pan modification (Bauer in Oakland) and 
cross-drilling the #2 rod journal.

In order to remove the oil pan, one must drop the front crossmember.  It 
took about 2 hours to cross-drill the crank.  That sucker is way hard.  The 
last 20 thousandths were the toughest, since, I suspect, Porsche nitride 
 -treats the crank.  A vacuum device and positive pressure through the oil 
system keeps wayward metal out of the system after the process.  New bearing 
while I was down there, and it looks like we just might make it to Nevada.

Anyone else going?
