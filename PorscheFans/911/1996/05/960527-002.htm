It's certainly not a bad idea if the economics don't get in the way.  Two
things to consider.  First, the pad material mass stores up heat generated
during braking.  Half the mass means twice the temperature rise, which can
always be an issue even with carbon brakes.

The other thing to consider is that Cool Carbons have a ceramic layer at
the base of the carbon/kevlar bulk.  You can see it if you dress one of the
pad edges before installing (as is often necessary).  While this layer
continues to afford pretty good stopping power, it wears quickly in comparison
with the carbon/kevlar layer.
