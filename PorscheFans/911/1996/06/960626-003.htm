Here is what my good friend and mechanic recommends.

If the car is only going to be used for street, or occasional drivers 
ed., then stick with the rubber lines.  These lines do not require the 
owner/driver to check the lines two or three times a year, like a track 
driver would.  For street applications, brakes do not get hot enough to 
cause the problems that track evenst do.

If you are going to be doing a lot of track events, and possible club
racing, then you should switch to the braided lines.  The braided lines do
require careful attention and occasional maint. to make sure they are
clean, not fraying, crimped, or other such things.  The advantage of the
braided lines is when the brakes are being used very hard, I.E. track
events, the brake fluid heats up and the hot fluid causes the rubber lines
to flex under hard braking.  This gives the driver a feeling of a spongy
brake pedal.  The steel braiding does not alow the lines to flex,
therefore, the brake pedal has a more consistant feel.
