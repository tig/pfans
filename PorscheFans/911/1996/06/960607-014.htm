Stephen I Jagernauth  wrote:

> After an autox at the Meadowlands in May, I took off my air cleaner
> and found some engine oil in the back of the plastic housing.  There
> was maybe 5 tablespoons.  This seems to be an odd place for oil to
> be collecting.  Is there some sort of crankcase breather hose which
> connects to the air-cleaner?

    Yes.  Since your car has a dry sump, the breather's from the oil
    tank, not the crankcase.  Same effect, though.

> Also, saw my oil temp climb up to 250^ with a RF fender Terbatrol
> oil cooler.   I thought maybe my thermostat was bad but both oil
> hoses were hot to the touch.  Are there any sure-fire ways to test
> the thermostat? 

    If the lines between the thermostat and the oil cooler (if the
    thermostat's a separate unit) or the oil cooler itself (if the
    thermostat's incorporated into the cooler) are hot, the
    thermostat is allowing hot oil to flow to the cooler.
