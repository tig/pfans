There is no such thing as a stupid question.....

My understanding about leaning the mixture as the engine warms up is that the 
engine requires an overly rich mixture (far higher than what is optimum while it is 
warm) to get started and run smoothly when cold. If I understand correctly it is to 
compensate for some fuel condensing on the intake manifold walls, to overcome the 
"chilling" effect of mixture coming into a relatively cold combustion chamber and 
stuff of that nature. Once the car warms up, then the mixture can be leaned to the 
level which is optimum for normal operation...obviously if this goes to the overlean 
stage, you have other problems including overheating and eventually the fire goes 
out and the engine quits.

If you ran around on the startup mixture, it would have other problems like 
washing the oil off of the cylinder walls and the attendant wear as well as lousy 
fuel consumption and high emmissions...
