Dirk Bergstrom  asked :

> I've been told that I should replace my spring plate bushings before I do 
> the corner balancing.  I'm wondering if there is a pressing reason to 
> make the move to adjustable spring plates now, rather than waiting until 
> I upgrade the torsion bars next year.  New bushings are $50, while the 
> Sway-Away spring plates are $300, so it would have to be a pretty 
> pressing reason....

There is a very good reason, and may actually SAVE you money!

You will need to re-index your torsion bars several (dozen?) times
to do the weight balancing. The sway-away spring plates
have a removeable adjuster end that will come off the end of the
torsion bar for re-indexing without removing the whole
spring plate from the suspension. Bad explanation, but it
takes a long time on the stock ones and a few minutes with
the sway away. I was even able to re-index the bars without
removing the wheel.

Since time is money, it will be much cheaper to buy them
now.
