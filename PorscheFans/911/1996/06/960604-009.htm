OK, now I'm confused a bit.  My Porsche MFI Adjustment Manual describes the 
following in its section on Checking of Warm-Up Thermostat:

The thermostat enriches the fuel/air mixture whan a cold engine is started.  
Enrichment stops at a temp of approx. 53degC.

The thermostat can be checked by measuring the CO emission.  Cold engine content 
is high.  As the engine temp increases, the CO content decreases since 
enrichment is reduced.

Enrichment is over when the needle of the CO measuring instrument no longer 
moves in the direction of lean.  The CO now should be normal value for idling 
speed or medium speed.

My question:  So does the thermostat enrich the mixture or lean the mixture?
