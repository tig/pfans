>  On a reminder note:
>* * *  Please share this with your VW friends  * * *
>  Its hot and now your 914 wont spool up the starter. . .
>
>  Why?  (this is common on most VW designs)
>  Its not the battery, its not the starter, it is the keyswitch
>terminal that sends current to the starter solenoid.
>  It gets a hefty arc across it each time you let off of
>the keystart position because the starter solenoid is
>sorta like an ignition coil, but without the secondary
>(high voltage) winding.
>  That solenoid behaves just as electromotive theory
>says it will. . .a collapsing magnetic field generates
>a counter EMF pulse (>350v on the 914) that zaps the
>switch contacts.  After a while those contacts are
>pretty grody, and on a hot day where more current is
>needed to make things go. . .the switch poops out.
>
>  Those nifty fix-it relay kits; all that does is substitute
>a lower current relay coil for the heavier solenoid
>coil.  It will patch the situation, but the burnt keyswitch
>contacts are still there (waiting to get you) and now
>another layer of electrical rubbish is in place waiting
>to poop out because the installation was poor.
>
>  How to fix this (for now):  Pull the keyswitch and
>carefully open and clean the contact section of the
>switch.
>
>  How to fix this (for ever):  Add a 1N4004 diode across
>the solenoid coil (I dont recall which end to ground).
>  It is best to place the diode as close to the solenoid
>as practical to shunt the pulse at the source.
