  If you are changing to a 74 or later engine in an early 911, you will want to
change the tach to the later version. The early tachs work off a sending unit (
that little cylindrical doohicky on the electrical console ).
   Late distributors send out a signal which doesn't require the sending unit,
but you need the correct tach.
   For you 70-73 911 drivers who would like to get rid of that " bouncy "
needle, send your tach to North Hollywood Speedometer, and have the needle
damped. 
   I sent my early Tach to them and asked to have the needle damped, an "offset"
10K face, and MSD conversion done. Sent it out UPS on Monday, got it back on
Wednesday. Now that's service !
