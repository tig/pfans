Fred Emmerich asks

>I only have 25K on the car so I am thinking I do not need the
>rotors turned.  But if I did, I have a question.  Is there anything special
>or
>ticky when you have drilled rotors?  Can a local shop do it or does it
>require
>special machinery?  Thanks for any reply.

I don't know of any reason why turning crossdrilled rotors should be any
different. 

If your rotors are still smooth, don't have any scoring, you don't need to
turn them. Normally if you didn't run the pads down to the metal backing
plates, you just put new pads in and they quickly conform to the tiny
irregularities of used but not scored rotors. Rotors will always have some
"texture" but only really deep scoring and unevenness needs to be turned out.
