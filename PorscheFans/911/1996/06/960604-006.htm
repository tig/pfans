>I'm wondering what difference I should expect in the 
>dle speed or warm up of the car now that the hoses are 
>back in place.  I think that the thermostat adusts the 
>leaness/richness of the pump, right? 

If your pump is adjusted right, the car will tend to run real rich without the 
hoses, until the heat in the engine bay is sufficient to operate the thermostat, 
thereby leaning out the engine.  (Actually, I don't know if it even gets  hot in 
there to do that.)  You may not notice any real difference in the way the car 
feels since engines can run pretty much OK when rich.
