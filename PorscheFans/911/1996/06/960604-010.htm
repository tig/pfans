> OK, now I'm confused a bit.  My Porsche MFI Adjustment Manual
> describes the following in its section on Checking of Warm-Up
> Thermostat:
> 
> The thermostat enriches the fuel/air mixture whan a cold engine is
> started.
> ....
> As the engine temp increases, the CO content decreases since
> enrichment is reduced.
> 
> Enrichment is over when the needle of the CO measuring instrument no
> longer moves in the direction of lean.  The CO now should be normal
> value for idling speed or medium speed.
> 
> My question:  So does the thermostat enrich the mixture or lean the
> mixture?  

Steve:

It enriches the mixture, to a varying extent dependent upon 
temperature.

On cold startup, it enriches the mixture A LOT.  As the engine warms
up, the thermostat slowly decreases the amount of enrichment until
the mixture is at the "normal" ratio.

Perhaps that "enrichment is over when...." paragraph in your manual
fooled you.  All it's saying is that the CO needle will move in the
lean DIRECTION, not that the CO needle will ever show a
leaner-than-normal condition.
