>1) I've been told that it is possible to lessen the spring force on the door
>of the Air Meter by moving the spring back a few notches, which may
>somewhat lessen the throttle lag issue.  I've tried to get to the spring by
>removing the bottom plate of the meter, but apparently access to the
>spring is from the top.  My question....  How do you get the black plastic
>top off without destroying it in the process?  I'd also be interested in
>hearing from anybody who has experience with this modification.

You will not correct a "throttle lag" problem, but you will mess up the
driveability.  The springing was chosen to have the right damping/filtering
effect.  It's also a coarse adjustment to the enrichment -- if you cannot
get the basic mix right by adjusting the allen in the meter, you can
reposition the spring.  Damping also comes from the air mass trapped behind
the other "leg" of the flap, which sweeps into a closed chamber as the
barn door flap opens.  Again, by design.

Uh huh.  And when you make that back up, I'd recommend the extra security
