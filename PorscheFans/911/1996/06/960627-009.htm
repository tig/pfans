Todd-

You can use a conventional engine stand, the kind you
can pick up in any autoparts store or from one of
the mail order places like Harbor Freight.  The kind
that's self-supporting and has 4 attachment points.

You have to fiddle a bit with the 4 arms, but you can
get them into a position that will allow you to bolt the engine on.  You
might need a few washers to act as little spacers
so you don't press the arms against the little ridge
that runs around the case where the transmission is attached,
and where the stand attaches. (It'll be obvious when you
look at it. The explanation falls short of clarity.)
