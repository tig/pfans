We have been using both of the Redline gear lubes in our 914-6 with the
901 gearbox.  We usually use the MTL for qualifying, anf the other for the
race.  Since the MTL has a lower viscosity, it lets the gears spin up a 
little quicker. At the SCCA National level of compitition, every little 
bit helps.  The hoursepower we are generating is very close to the limit 
of what the box can handle, so it generates a tremendous amount of heat, 
and the higher viscosity gear oil last a little longer.  We have not used 
the swepco gear oil, but I prefer to use the swepco grease for the CV 
joints.

I have been running the MTL (Manual Transmission Lubricant) in both my 73
911 street/track car and my 91 Eagle Talon AWD.  Going to the MTL in my 
Eagle made the shifting significantly better then the stock gear oil.  I 
think you should use what ever make you feel the most comfortable.
