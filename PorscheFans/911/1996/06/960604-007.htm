I may be misunderstanding your question about the
calipers, but there is no self-adjusting mechanism
on disc calipers.  They are inherently self-adjusting
in that every time you step on the brake they are
pushed against the disc, and then, when you let up,
the rubber piston seal exerts a slight force that
pulls the pistons a tiny bit away from the disc.  Thus,
every time you apply the brakes you are automatically
compensating fro any brake pad wear that has ocurred
during the last application.

The 20 degree piston position thing is, I believe, a
method to prevent brake squeal.  Some calipers use
little thin metal anti-squeal plates, but Porsche,
being Porsche, has a more elaborate method.  I think
the idea behind it is to ever so slightly cause the
pressure on the pad to be non-uniform in just the
right direction to keep the pad from being totally
flat on the disc.  Bad explanation.  Don't have
time to edit it.  Hope you get the idea.
