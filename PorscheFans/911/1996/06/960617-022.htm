> You should use anti-seize on the threads, not too much.  Look at them when
> you remove them.  You should see the anti-seize in the groves of the
> threads.
> You should also put anti-seize on your alloy wheel nuts.

I think the consensus has been that you should NOT put anti-seize on the 
plugs, contrary to the wheel nuts.  I used to anti-seize my plugs but 
stopped.  I started doing it on my 912 which was slowly losing helicoil 
inserts somebody had installed (ever heard a sparkplug eject itself from 
the plug hole while cruising down the highway at 70?).  It probably saved 
me some time before I had to get the heads redone, but everybody says not 
to do it.
