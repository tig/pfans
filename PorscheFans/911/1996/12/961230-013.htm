I just recently read a good description on the Porsche list
about using anti-seize on car parts.  It does an excellent
job of explaining the need for anti-seize and I thought I
would throw in some things I heard.  

My Porsche mechanic (a racer) says all of the lug nuts he
has had to cut off were not due to thread lockup, but due to
the nut friction against the wheel itself.  He said many of
the ones he cut off had anti-seize on the threads, but not
on the face.  The lockup can occur because of the different
types of metals between the nuts and the wheels.

Moral:  put anti-seize on threads and the face of the nuts.
