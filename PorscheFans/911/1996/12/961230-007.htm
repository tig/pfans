Before removing the old tensioners, Bruce Anderson rightly pointed out
that the timing chains should be secured - use some wire to keep some
tension in the chains. Also, make sure that number one cylinder is at
TDC ("Z1" aligned with mark on crankcase, and rotor arm in distributor
points to #1 cylinder, not #4).
 
BTW, a PO had fitted tensioner guards to my car - these were an old
dodge to prevent the tensioner collapsing completely in the event of
tensioner failure. They fit around the piston rod, and if you find them,
consign them to the trash can, along with the old tensioners.
