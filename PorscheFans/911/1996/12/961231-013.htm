You are changing the over/understeer balance a LOT by using
26 mm rear bars with stock 19mm front bars. I would recommend
using tires and wheels on the rear that are MUCH wider than the
front, like 205s on the front (6 or 7" rims) and 245 or more on the 
rear on 9" rims.

Also you will need an adjustable sway bar on one end at least to
really tune it. An adjustable rear is easier on the late model (post '73
is late model???) 911s.

As for shock adjustment, start with the rears near full soft
to help the rear balance with the big bars. You can tighten
them later if needed (or for wear which they will).
