>Mike - Last summer I was faced with the same dilemma. < How to drop a 911
>engine w/o much help. > A friend sent me a copy of an article from a VW
>magazine on building an engine trolley.  I built one it worked great.  It
>slides under the engine on wheels and your hydraulic jack fits between the
>supports.  You end up lowering the engine to the trolley, removing the jack
>and using it to raise the car.  You place the jack with a plank for
>additional support under the rear part of the body pan just fwd of the tranny
>to raise it.  I think Dave Cooley described this recently.


>The trolley is simply two pieces of 4X4s about 24 inches long parallel to
>each other and held together by an 18 - 24in (you'll have to measure) piece
>of 4 in channel iron under the front edges.  I used some three inch wood
>bolts to hold the iron.  I put 4 in swivel wheels under all four corners.
