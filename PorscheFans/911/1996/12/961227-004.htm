On the OUTSIDE windows I use a powdered cleanser named Bon Ami that can be
bought in your grocery store. It removes road film, etc without scratching
the glass . I do this as part of washing the car, then use car to
make sure all the residue powder is rinsed off the finish. Then I finish off
the 
in and outsides with an ammonia based cleaner like Windex. It has "always
said" that newspaper is the best product to use (instead of paper towels)
because the inks are mildly abrasive enough to keep from streaking. Old
wives tail?

>There are auto window cleaners available that are Alcohol based, instead

>works well...cuts right through the film and doesn't streak.

I wonder about the long term effects of repeatedly using alcohol products
