Dion-

It's best to remove the grease before sandblasting.  Unless the grease is really 
hardened, the sand will simply stick in it, and it won't come off easily.  I've 
found that an engine degreaser, like Gunk, works fine.  Be generous, and 
wire-brush areas that have real build-up.  Use a pressure washer if you have 
one, but a hose and nozzle work fine.  Once the engine bay is clean and 
grease-free, you can decide whether or not to sandblast before painting.  You 
can also remove the old paint with paint remover instead of a sandblaster. 

Wear gloves.
