Bob:

I didn't say anything when similar advice showed up on the 924 list
a month or so ago, since 924s don't have enough power for this to be 
a problem, but...

On a 911 or any other powerful car, halfshafts should NEVER change 
position once they're installed.  That is, the left one must STAY on 
the left, and the inboard and outboard ends must not be switched.

The reason, of course, is that the halfshafts are VERY highly
stressed (more than any component except maybe the crankshaft), and
they take a torsional "set".  Swapping them haphazardly will reverse
the torsional stress, thereby weakening them and possibly leading to
failure, especially if you beef up the CV joints to the point where
they're no longer the weak link in the chain.
