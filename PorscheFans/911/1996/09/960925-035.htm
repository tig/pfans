> Bill 78 SC (sans tail)
>           ___________________________
>           The tail may provide some engine cooling capacity, but by
>           far its primary function is aerodynamics.  I found this out
>           when I first took by former '85 Carrera on the track in 1986
>           when I started racing.  The back end of the car was wild,
>           and wouldn't stay stuck at what I now realize are relatively
>           low speeds at Willow (and the then functioning Riverside
>           Raceway).  After 2 events, and having made a decision to
>           actively pursue time trialling in the POC, my first purchase
>           was a tail, which made a tremendous difference in handling,
>           adding a lot of downforce to the rear end of the car.  In
>           fact, it's my understanding that the downforce effect
>           provided by the tail comes into play at speeds as low as 50
>           mph.  It's not really surprising, when you think about it,
>           since the shape of the rear end of a 911 is completely
>           contrary to the ideal aerodynamic model - low in the front
>           and high in the back.  Without the tail, the rear end of the
>           911 drops downward, providing essentially no downforce.
