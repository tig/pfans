The engine has a temerature sensor on it that acts as a feed to the Motronic
brain; if the sensor is going such that the brain senses an overheating
condition, it will kill the engine.  A friend of mine had his act up at a
track
event at watkins glen; after 1-2 laps it would suddenly kill the engine, and 
wouldn't start until the car cooled down.  I would suggest have someone check
out the sensor and connections. Can't be more specific since I sold my '84
Carrera and the associated shop manuals in February.
