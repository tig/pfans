I have to agree with Bruce on this issue, especially on late model FI
motors.  I've looked into this for both of my Motronic-equipped cars, an
'86 911 and a '91 Alfa 164.   After discussing this with a German
engineer recommended by Bruce (thanks!), he suggested that there is
(purposely) precious-little metal to bore away from modern throttle
bodies.  Apparently this is "suggested" by some of the governing bodies
(EPA, DOT, etc) so that we cannot mess with the throttle body as we
are all discussing.  With earlier cars (70's), there is more metal to cut,
and thus more value to undertaking this upgrade.
