According to Paul Frere who relates Porsche testing in his book "Porsche
911 Story"

An H series 911 (1975) without any aerodynamic aids has a Cd of .423,
CLf (coefficient of lift - front) of .112 and a Clr (coefficient of lift
- rear) of .246.

With the rear duck tail but without the front air dam these figures
change to a Cd of .414, Clf of .112 and Clr of .062. 

As can be seen the duck tail reduces drag slightly, has little effect on
the front end and a significant reduction in rear end lift, aka tail
happy behaviour at speed.

I don't have the effect of the front air dam with the duck tail, but
with the tray spoiler, it reduces Clf to .010.

I would imagine the effect of the two is to essentially get rid of
aerodynamic lift  and get a bit of a drag reduction to boot.

Since this is what I have done to my 911 (an H series) I am obviously a
believer....
