>One says 16 inch wheels give better ride, the other says 17 inch is 
>better.  Reasons are that 16 inch is not so low profile, so that's better

Correct!
 
>and 17 inch runs at lower pressure, so THAT's better!

Not true, either that the ride is better (it's usually at least somewhat
harder because of the shorter, stiffer sidewall which is th reason to use
17, for crisper handling) or because of tire pressure, which should not
necessarily be lower.

I speak from direct experience, having recently replaced the 16" stock rims
(7" f, 8" r), covered with Michelin MXX3 tires in stock sizes (225/50x16 f,
245/45x16 r) with 17" rims (7 f, 9.5 r) covered with Michelin MXX3 tires
(225/45x17 f, 255/40x17 r). Since the new equipment is stock for a newer
model of my car (the GTS), the factory provides recommended pressures,
which are identical in front.

I'm delighted with the change, because I'm getting MUCH more crisp, linear,
and positive steering and cornering with remarkably little extra harshness
of ride.
