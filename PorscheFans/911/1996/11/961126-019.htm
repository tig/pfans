Frank-

It's hard to give advice about a car one hasn't seen.  

That being said,  it's been my experience that a $4K 911 of that 
vintage is going to be a project, lots of work,  and unless you are 
willing to do it yourself, it's going to cost.  Engine overhauls can 
run $10K nowadays (so I'm told), and even if you do it yourself 
it'll be $1500 or so.  I've rebuilt two '72 911's, so I know.  I'm 
always looking for another project, so I have a fair idea of what's 
available for $4K. Unless you're very lucky, like stupendously 
lucky, you're not going to get a reliable daily driver.  As long as 
you're prepared for that, $4K isn't a bad price for a 911 with little 
rust and an engine that actually runs.

>I know nothing about the MFI system - what sort of
>things cause poor idle/cold start?

Lots of things.  Just like in any other car.  Not enough gas.  Too 
much gas. Erratic fuel supply, improper timing adjustments, 
ignition problems, vacuum leaks...the list goes on..

>Any other gotchas I should look for?

Rust is the biggy.
