I would be careful increasing the CR on the VW type IV engine because of 
the poor valve layout and combustion chamber design. Also, the heads will 
run much hotter, causing cracking and rapid valve guide wear. In stock 
form, the heads already run too hot, since I've heard that most of them 
get cracks by 100,000 miles. 

Also, increasing valve diameters is a bad idea for these heads, since the
distance between them decreases, causing severe stress on the metal
between the seats. There just isn't much that can be done to these engines
without heavy penalties. 

The only areas that Iv'e thought about addressing are the machanical
efficiency of the engine and the cooling of the heads. The mechanical
efficiency can be improved by using lighter rods, counterbalanced
crankshaft, and needle bearing rocker arms. Better cooling can be had by
adding an oil cooler, and using thermal-barrier type coatings on the
pistons, valves and combustion chambers. 

Porting and polishing the heads would help too, of course.
