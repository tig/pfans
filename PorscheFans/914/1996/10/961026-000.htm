Fred:
You say your Bursch has a leak. If it's just the muffler part and not
the header part that bolts to it (at least on my 72-74 Bursch 2.0) you
might want to look at replacing the muffler. It shouldn't be too hard to
duplicate the flange on the existing muffler. 

I've been dying to try this but my muffler is still good. I have already
been following the catalogs and talking to a friend with a dyno who
tunes 914s for a living. It works out that Borla makes a neat stainless
muffler (for under $100) that will fit perfectly (a couple in fact).
Also the Walker dynoMax Turbo series (which look suspiciously like the
original Bursch Steelpack muffler) tests well for flow and they even
have a very good Stainless knockoff of the Borla design (for about the
same price, unfortunatly). Any of these should flow better that the
original Bursch Steelpack muffler.

If you or anyone else go(es) this route, please let me know the results,
I'm a sucker for bolt-on power upgrades.
