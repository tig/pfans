It's really a matter of money, like someone else said. If it was me, and 
I had some cash to spend, I'd get the Keith Black hypereutectic pistons 
and jugs, light weight rods and counterweighted crank from FAT. Then I'd 
call up Summit Racing and get the TechLine coatings and coat the piston 
tops, combustion chambers, ports and valves (after getting the heads and 
intake runners extrude-honed, of course). I'd use the stock sodium-filled 
exhaust valves and aftermarket ss intakes. Then I'd install a new wiring 
harness, and needle bearing rocker arms from Pauter Machine. (I think 
we're well over a thousand dollars by now). But, most people don't bother 
and just get a 911 motor instead. It's a much better engine anyway, some 
people say "why try to turn a potato cart into an indy car?" It's really 
just a matter of money.....
