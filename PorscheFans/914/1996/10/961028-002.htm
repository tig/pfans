>He he said drilling a hole in the end-plate will give reasonably low
>backpressure and better performance (I don't know what that does for the

Better yet, Supertrapp makes a different end-plate (aluminum, not steel)
for jet-ski applications which has a one-inch diameter hole in it. It has
the same bolt-pattern and fits nicely. You can buy it separately at many
water-sports stores.

I used it with the Supertrapp megaphone attached to a Bob Garretson Phase-9
header system on the '74 2.0L AX-car I once owned. Had it coming out
diagonally to the right-rear corner with a notch cut in the rear valance
panel for clearance (didn't like the idea of an exposed stinger directly to
the rear). Was a very subtle look.

It gave great sound and performance.
