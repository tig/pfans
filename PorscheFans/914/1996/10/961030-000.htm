I am trying to improve the driveability of my '74 914 2.0. The engine has
8.0 CR, standard bore, mild cam, ported/flowed/cc'd heads, dual 40mm Dells,
and an Allison ignition. At this point it is used only on the street.

I have the carbs adjusted pretty well except for transition, i.e. going at
a steady speed and give small increase of throttle, result is hesitation
unless I give it more gas. Same kind of thing when starting from a stop -
hesitation. Steady speed is smooth. On the gas response is strong and
smooth. Idle jets too lean normally have a big effect on this. I'm using
#65 jets which should be on the rich side to get things as good as they
are.

I have read some about is MSD ignition. One article spoke about smoothing
out idle and increasing low end power. Also about eliminating "off idle
flat spots". Would this help my problem? What model would I choose? What
experiences have list members had?
