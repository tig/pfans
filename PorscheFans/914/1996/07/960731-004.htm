Hi Jim,

I use the Autopower mount that has a reasonably sturdy attachment
to the cross brace on the bar/cage and incorporates a rubber damper
into the mount to cut down on vibration.  They also supply a tether cord
to attach to the camera in the event the camera were to somehow work
itself loose.

As far as the camera is concerned, I use a 7-year-old Sony 8mm without
all the fancy bells and whistles.  Getting it aimed properly took some
practice, but it's a no brainer now.   The picture quality on this unit
is great with the only problem being that the tape lost its track once 
under high-G loading as it photographed a 360 degree panoramic view
of the turn 1 exit at Las Vegas.  

Drew S.
