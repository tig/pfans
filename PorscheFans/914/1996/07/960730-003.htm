At 13:58 7/30/96, Ehler, Kyle wrote:
> Furthermore, an aux air valve could be adapted
>from a 944, or some years 914 (not sure).

        Yes, it can.  The 1.8L ones are still available.
However, it wouldn't be the right part.  The amount of
air that is allowed through is a function of the temp-
erature, and is determined by the shape of the hole in
the rotating piece (see Probst's Bosch FI and Engine
Management).  So there would be not enough extra air, or
too much extra air, depending on how the holes compare
with each other.  Not a huge deal, but it is a difference
in the way they work.

>  All it does is leak air past the throttle body until
>the thermo time switch tells it to stop. . .

        Actually the thermo-time switch is for the cold-
start valve.  The AAR has its own built-in bimetallic
thermo-time thing.
