Ok, now here's where you want to be careful of you go out and buy for
the car. Some things which seem fairly innocuous can bump you up into
a more competetive/expensive class.

I would first go with shocks and or tires. I don't remember the stock
wheel sizes, but unless you want to enter C Street Prepared, stay with
the stock wheel sizes. If you're running 14" wheels, go for the 205/55
race tires. I'd recomend the Yokohama A008Rs. They're not a sticky as
the BFG or the Hoosier, but they will last longer and aren't as
sensitive to bad alignment settings, driving styles, etc. It's a great
race tire to learn on. Shocks, go with Bilstien or Koni. They are
tried and true shocks and will improve the way your car drives
remarkably, especially if your shocks are old and worn out. Stick with
those for now. There's LOT'S of things you could do, but when you're
starting with a bone stock car, keep it simple and keep it cheap. Make
changes incrementally, that way you'll be able to get a feel for what
changes had what effect. You'll also gain a much better understanding
of your car in the process.
