> I've started eyeing the rest of the car.  From some pictures that
> I've seen, I noticed that the GT cars got rid of those ponderous
> headlight motors, and used cables instead.

    Mike:

    If you're building a track-only car, there's generally no need
    for the retractable headlights at all; if you ever find yourself
    running in a night race, you can just bolt big rally lights to
    the hood.

    If, on the other hand, you're planning for this car to be usable
    on the street, the cable-actuated headlights are going to make
    your driving experience pretty horrible (in my opinion).  As far
    as I know (as usual, I could be WAY wrong about this), the cables
    only raised the headlights; when dawn broke over the Sarthe, they
    were pressed back into place by the pit crew.

> And while I'm at it, there looks to be some rust in the w/s cowl
> area, near the cowl/fender junction (covered by that black plastic
> strip). I'll be cleaning that up when I do the bodywork.  But while
> I was in there.....I noticed in the 914 Authenticity (XXX XXX) book
> that some early cars were seam finished there.  Seems like a good
> way to prevent future rust problems. How did Porsche do this?  I
> assume not via bondo....  Not likely a small strip of metal welded
> in either.  Did they just put down a large bead to fill to bridge
> the gap, and how do they manage to prevent the enclosed area from
> becoming a potential rust trap?

    The very early 914/6 that I helped prep for the class win at the
    concours at the San Diego Parade a couple years ago (my "help",
    by the way, was exceedingly minor... I'm in no way an expert on
    914 authenticity) had that seam welded shut.

    It was done just as you suggested, with a large bead filling the
    gap. It WAS, as you suggest, a potential rust trap, which may
    explain why the factory didn't fill the seam on later cars.
