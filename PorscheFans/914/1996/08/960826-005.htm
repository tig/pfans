        As Gary said, replace the inner o-rings, too.  Dry--no goop of any
kind.
        If this doesn't fix the leak, the leak may actually be in the runner-
to-head seal.  There is a plastic block between the two runners and the head.
Each side of this block has a paper gasket.  Frequently, the paper deterior-
ates over time, and if disturbed, can then leak.
        Solution 1:  Tighten the three nuts (you said 2.0L, right?) that hold
the runner onto the head.  About 3/4 grunt is good, I think the 10 ft-lb spec
in The Book is too low...
        Solution 2:  Remove the runners, put a VERY LIGHT layer of goop (the
non-hardening gasket sealer is good) on both sides of the plastic block.  And
I mean *very* thin layer.  Then put it all back together.
