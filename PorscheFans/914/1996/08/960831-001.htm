Before the results here is a little side story that is so typical of the
kind of people that make up club racing:

Saturday practice; one of the most enthusiastic racers in the country, Steve
Valentinetti, experiences a mysterious mechanical failure in his new  GT2
993. It just dies. He spends the whole day diagnosing the problem. Finally,
the problem is identified as the fuel pump. It rins but won't pump. But
Steve has no spare and none can be found. 

Enter Jeff Gamroth. Another of the countries most enthusiastic racers and a
competitor of Steve's. Jeff, a mechanic at Marque Motors, calls one of his
customers that owns a 993 and convinces her to allow a transplant of her
fuel pump into Steve's race car. Not only that but Jeff performs the
operation personally. Saturday night he removes the customer's pump and 7:30
AM Sunday he is under Steve's 993 installing same.

Steve qualifies Sunday AM one tenth of a second behind Jeff. 

Ultimately Jeff defeated Steve in the afternoon race but not before they
swapped the lead several times to the absolute delight of the spectators.
Now that's club racing.
