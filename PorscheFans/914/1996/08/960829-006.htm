Actually, an early mk.I RX-7 GSL-SE puts out about 140 HP, and weighs in
at about 2500lbs, maybe 2600. This yields approximately 18lbs/hp.
Whereas, the 914 if it's puting out say 105 hp (being very generous

either. Electromotive FI, and alot of time and money...

> Now, I understand that there's a lot more than goes
> into 0-60 times than power to weight, but I would figure that the 914
> would be capable of beating the RX7.

It has the potential. Weight distribution is the best reason. It can get
more of its weight over the drive wheels in a dragstrip launch. The RX-7
has the advantage of having a limited slip available as an option. That
is big plus. Also, the RX-7 can basically wind out forever. The redline
on a stock RX-7 is simply dictaded by how much air it can suck in and
how much exhaust it can push out. The 914 on the other hand can only rev
so far before the cam simply renders the power nonexistant. the RX-7 can
just leave it in 2nd gear for an eternity, whereas the 914 will need to
shift into third _much_ sooner. OUt of the box, the RX-7 has it. With
equal amounts of money invested, the RX-7 still has it. The 914 has the
advantage of handling. The live axle of the RX-7 is a big detriment to
it's handling characteristics. And the RX-7, although a very well
balanced car by itself, still tends to push a bit.
