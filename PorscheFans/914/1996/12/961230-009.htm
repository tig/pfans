Yes, using bolts instead of studs in aluminum is really bad. You can bet 
that's why you're having problems. I've bought a bunch of SS set screws 
that I'm going to replace my rusty studs with. They have a allen socket 
at one end, but that's just to get them in and out. Stainless is a good 
choice in this application because of the high temperature and low torque 
requirements. It also has a lower thermal conductivity, which should make 
for cooler head temperatures. You can speacial order them from Totally 
Stainless, they are M8x50mm set screws.
