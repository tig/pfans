Since the original 914/6 is a rare and collectable car, restoring one in 
any condition would be worthwhile, in my opinion. As long as you don't 
expect to use the car for awhile (you've got other wheels to get around 
in), then the first thing to do is to completely diasassemble the car and 
take it to trusted body shop. You'll save money at the body shop by 
disassembling the car yourself. JCM transmissions in Colorado will 
rebuild the transaxle for about 500$, I believe. Depending on your skill 
level, you may or may not be able to rebuild the engine yourself. It'll 
be expensive either way. These cars will always be valuable, and I'd 
expect their value to go up in the future, moreso than the four cylinder 
cars.
