>"jjwilson" asked about the values of the markings on
>911 temp gauges.
>
   >While to the average driver, it may APPEAR that
>the gauge is unnumbered, there actually are numbers
>on the gauge (a least if your Carrera is the same as my '82
>SC).  Unfortunately, the numbers are WAAAAY over to the left
>and minuscule.  They are mostly covered up by the gauge
>fascia.  If you have a double-jointed neck and 20/10 vision, 
>they are trivial to read.  Otherwise, I have provided
>the following translations:
>
>Mark         Centigrade      Farenheit     Anderson
>
>Top of        60             140           ?
>"cold" band
>
>2nd mark      90             194           "normal" 
>(~8:00)
>
>3rd mark     120             248           "too damn hot" (TM) 
>(~10:00)
>
>Bottom of    150             302           "I dunno, I suppose
>red zone                                    you might try 
>                                            PartsHeaven for a used 3.2"  
>
