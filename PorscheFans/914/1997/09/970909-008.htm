Get the R1. If you're going to be entereing parade events, then you may
want to get the 205/60-15. Otherwise, get the 205/50-15. Parade seems to
be the only rules set that specifies rolling diameter (I could be
wrong). The advantage to the R1, and the reason why it's the most
popular tire in the SCCA stock classes, is twofold: One, the asymetric
sidewalls allow you to get an optimal contact patch with significantly
less negative camber than other more conventional radials. And two, a
practical side effect of the sidewall construction is that the R1 can be
squeezed onto unusually narrow rims. A 205/50-15 can be pinched onto a
5.5 inch rim, and a 225/50-14 can be shoehorned onto a 6 inch rim. The
basic rule is that R1s can go onto rims 0.5 inches narrower than
recomended. But remember, that this is not endorsed by BFG or anyone
else for that matter. Well, except for probably every stock class
autoxer on the planet...
