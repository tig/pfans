The reason for this part is the difference between a fog light and a driving
light.  The hourglass metal piece cuts off the top of the light beam, and
lets only the lower part of the beam shine through.  This makes for better
illumination in foggy conditions (ever try driving through fog with your high
beams on???).  As you could expect, fog lights should be aimed much lower
than driving lights, to illuminate the roadway during marginal visibility.
