> What about the bearings/bushings on the ends. Any particular lube I
could
> use or just leave alone?

  Depends on what they are... ball bearings, use a good grade of
grease... I prefer moly base... if bushings, these are most probably
oilite, which means that they are heat-soaked in oil, and have a felt
oil reservoir in their immediate proximity.   In this case, straight
engine oil is appropriate.

  C A U T I O N ! !    DO NOT get oil or grease on the comutator!  
If you do,use your contact cleaner to remove all of it before placing
the motor into service.  Be sure not to over-lubricate so that
lubricant migrates onto the commutator from the adjacent bearing
after the motor is placed in service.  Oil on a comutator quickly
turns into carbon, making a low-resistance connection between the
segments, which severely degrades motor performance and can ruin the
beast over time.
