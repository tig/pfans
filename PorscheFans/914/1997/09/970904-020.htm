> 1) I was experiencing some bump steer, but the height had not been changed.
> Could it need new front shocks, an alignment, or some other attention ?

Had you had any bump steer before storage? How was it stored? It could
be a number of things, but before we get into a big guessing game, I'd
say give the front end a thorough once over. Check the tires for flat
spots, check teh shocks for response, inspect the bushings for wear or
tears, check the steering rack for excessive play, get your alignmnet
checked too, but that's not likely to change if the car was well stored.
 
> 2) After a few stops I started to hear a bit of a "clunking" noise from the
> rear of the car on the drivers side moving forward from a stop.  I suspect
> the CV joint is loose or is in need of a lube job.  Is this a proper
> assessment or should a be looking at something else ?

Could be a CV, but I'd say it was more likely to be a tranny mount. Do
you have stock mounts or the Poly mounts? Either way, a broken or loose
mount will result in a clunk under initial acceleration. Sounds almost
like something heavy in the rear trunk? Also look at your shift lever
when you accelerate, if there's a tranny mount problem you'll probably
see the lever move by itself. BTDT again, and again, and again...
 
> 3) Even after warm up, under hard acceleration the exhaust tone was quite
> LOUD.
> When cruising at steady speeds it was considerably quieter. Could it be that
> I need to check the mounting nuts and studs for the headers, or is the
> muffler (an after market, could be a Monza) shot and in need of replacement?

I'd suspect something near the back end of the exhaust system. If you
had a loose exhaust nut near the heads, you'd probably hear a ticking
and getting the occasional backfire too. But a 914, even with a stock
muffler is not terribly quiet.
