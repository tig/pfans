For those of you that have used Kjell Nelin's D-Jet article on Tim's 914
site (http://www.estinc.com/porsche), there is a small correction that
should be noted.  At the end of the article, he discusses adjusting the
fuel mixture by turning the screw inside the manifold pressure sensor.
He says that clockwise is lean, and counter-clockwise is rich, the
opposite of the adjustment knob on the ECU.  At least on my '76 2.0, it
is the SAME as the ECU (clockwise = rich, counterclockwise = lean).
After figuring this out, I've got my mixture within the ball park, and
the car runs like a champ at all temperatures!  Now, it's off to the
mechanic to see if I can't fine-tune the mixture and pass the dreaded
e-test!
