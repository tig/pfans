Hello all,

I have just stumbled over some forgotten information.  I have an old
Chevy bulletin on cyl. head temps for Corvairs.

The skinny is as follows:

200 - 300 degrees (F) at idle
350 - 475 degrees (F) at 30 to 60 MPH cruise
460 - 575 degrees (F) full throttle from 3000 - 5000 RPM

Also incase anyone wants a cheap piece of insurance they have warning
units that can be screwed between the fins of a cyl. head to warn of
doom.

P/N 1993599 or P/N 1993547 both provide a warning at close to 575 degree
(F).  The operate buy grounding at that temperature.  So wiring your
warning is a pretty simple item.

12v fused Power (+) ------ (Light or buzzer or both) 0 -------(to sensor)
---S ----|||| Gound

All in all pretty simple, now where to get them, well I would try NAPA
first, if that fails then there is always Clarks, sorry don't have the
number.
