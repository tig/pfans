Hi Stephen,  I have had no problems with the plasma on dirty or painted
metal at all.  Maybe there is something wrong with his unit.  I did notice 
that on some metal that's really rusty it will have trouble keeping the arc
going.  But I assumed that it was normal because it cannot conduct electicity
that well on the real bad rust.  It's just a matter of stopping and
re-starting it 
more often.  Sometimes you just have to break down and cut it some other
way if it is really bad.

So far I've cut apart two parts cars (ie: rusty) for junk & salvage, and cut
out various 
rusty sections of other cars for repairs.  Last weekend I took out a right
rear
suspension console out of a friends 914 and replaced it  -  all with no
problem
as far as the plasma was concerned (it was a pain in the A#$ though).

I feel it was a good investment and has saved enough time to warrant the 
cost.
