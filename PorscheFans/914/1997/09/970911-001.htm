At 01:06 PM 9/10/97 -0700, Dave Darling wrote:
>>	One of the black wires (the thinner one) is actually black
>with a very old and faded purple stripe.  Look carefully for the stripe.
>That is the one that goes to the tach.  It should be hooked up to the
>same side of the coil as the green wire, which comes from the points
>through the condensor.
>	The other thick black wire is 12V from the relay board.  It
>goes on the other side of the coil from the two other wires.

If you're changing coils, the +12V goes to the + terminal on the coil, and
the tach and points wires connect to the - terminal. BTW, it *is* possible
to connect these backwards and have the car still run. What you get instead
of a high voltage positive pulse to the plugs, is a high voltage negative
pulse. The tach will still work, too, as I recall from my 2002, which was
wired that way when I bought it. So, that's one other thing to check when
you're diagnosing those "mystery" problems.
