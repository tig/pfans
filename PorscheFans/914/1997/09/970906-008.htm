> 3)      Both of them were adamant that the center console (with the gauges) was

O.k. So what, more leg room. I don't think the center console is really
worth going out of your way for. And the lack therof is a further
bargaining point. Especially since that would mean either their car has
been stripped down, or they're missrepresenting it. All '73 2.0s (model
year) had both sport and appearance options, that includes sway bars,
fucks, chrome, fog lights, center console, and a few other things.

> 4)      Neither knew how to tell if they had the actual Porsche 2.0 or a stroked
> and poked 1.7/1.8.

Look for a GAxxxx serial number just in front of the oil filler cap
embossed on the engine case.

> 5)      One said that the car was maintained by Otto's, a very well recommended
> Porsche shop.  The owner told me to call Otto's and that they knew more
> about the car than he did.  To which I answered, "Is it your car?" "Of
> course," he said.

So you've got someone who throws money at their car without knowing too
much about the car itself. So what, it's not as if people like that
don't exist. We're a minority in the car world, we actually _want_ to
know what an oil galley plug is. The good thing is that Otto's is a
great shop. There are a handfull of _real_ 914 gurus out there in the
US, and Otto is one of 'em. If the car has been maintained by Otto, call
him up and ask about it. Tell him what you want to do with the car
(competition) and see if he recomends your buying the car. He's a good
source for knowledge and information.
