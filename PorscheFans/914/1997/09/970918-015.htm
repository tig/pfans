At 7:31 PM -0000 9/18/97, JJ Slowik wrote:
>Has anyone been able to successfully test their fuel injectors for both
>flow rate and pattern while out of the car?

	I diconnected all the fuel injectors from the wiring harness.  I
also unbolted all the injectors from the intake runners, so that I could
pull them and the fuel rails up away from the engine.  I held one injector
at a time into a jar, after re-connecting the wires to that ONE injector.
Then a friend cranked the motor over with the starter, and I watched the
squirts.  They're slow enough that you could easily count (say) six or
ten or whatever pulses and measure that.  If you've got a graduated
cylinder, you could use that instead of the jar.  Use something clear so
you can see the spray pattern.
	So, no--they weren't really out of the car, but I did test them
for pattern and function.  (I didn't care about flow rate at that point.)
	Easy 'nuff?
