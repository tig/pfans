At 4:52 AM -0000 9/15/97, RD Rick wrote:
>Seems like you guys have either forgotten or left off the aux air valve.
>That valve keeps vacuum less than about 22" at all times.

	The piece in question is the Decel Valve, which Haynes in one
or two places calls a Pressure Regulator.  It lives on the RHS of the
car, below the battery.  On D-jet cars it shares a bracket with the
MPS.
	The Aux Air Valve is the piece that lets more air past the
throttle on warm-up, providing a (slightly) higher idle when cold.
