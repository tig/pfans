I made a small booboo here. The life expectancy for the above racing
conditions were for stock engines. The expectancy based on the sustained
engine speed is for thier precision remanufactured engines.

>Also this assumes that all engines are built alike. I'm pretty sure that

Yeah, they are able to run 10:1 compression ratio on street gas because of
the many modifications they make, including some patented technology. The
highest they recommend for "regular" unmodified engines is 9:1 these days.

But, it's not the CR that is the major factor in determining engine life,
it's engine operating speed. Sure, CR is a contributing factor, but the main
thing is that the speed that you run your engine at will certainly effect
it's life. Of course, I'm sure everyine knew this. I just wanted to lay out
some numbers for eveyone's horror. I would guess that the life expectancy
for the VW air cooled motor would be worse becuase of the higher operating
temperatures, but I have no data to prove this.
