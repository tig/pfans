Craig Zaplatynsky-CCZ001 wrote:
> 
> I'm sure this has been mentioned before.  I'd like to know a safe way to
> jack up the front end of a 914 without causing damage to the underside of
> the car.  It is not obvious to me that there is a structural jacking point
> in the center/front of the car.  I know this group, I'm sure there are
> comments and suggestions.

1] Just use the jack point at the center of the car if you have a jack
fitted to use it. That's the rectangular hole just under the door on the
rocker panel. 

2] If not, then use the jack points that look like metal 'doughnuts'
that are inboard of the wheel wells. In the front, these points are just
behind the wheel well on each side, and in the rear the points are just
forward of the wheel well. You can then place jackstands in other
places. In the front, use the torsion bar caps, follow the axis of the
A-arm forward untill you get to a tapered 'cap' that merges flush with
the front pan. You can place a jack stand there. In the rear you can
place a jack stand under the trailing arm pivot arm. Not the rearward
part of the trailing arm, but the pivot axis of the arm.
