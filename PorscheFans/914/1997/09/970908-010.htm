At 5:07 PM -0000 9/8/97, Jerry Brady wrote:
>The AAR is going to give lots more air when the engine is cold as well
>which would help to "lean-out" a rich condition that wasn't caused by
>a simple mistuning of the FI (leaking injector, cold-start valve, etc.)

	The aux air regulator does NOT affect the mixture.  It is a
simple by-pass of the throttle plate, just like the idle passages inside
the throttle body.  More air is let into the manifold, but the MPS does
sense this extra air (as a higher pressure) and commands more fuel to go
with the air.
	Jerry is quite right that a cold engine needs a richer mixture
than a warm one--that's why we have head temp sensors.
