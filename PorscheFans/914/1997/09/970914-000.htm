> Also note: the Automotion rebuilt MPS units appear to all come with the
> air vent facing up where it could fill with water. The simple solution

A very good suggestion, as water in the MPS could lead to excessive
corrosion and premature failure.

> I would stronly advise against pumping more than 10 in hg vacuum when
> testing your Manifold Pressure Sensor. I found out the hard way.

10 in. hg is pretty small, but not a bad number to use for testing
purposes.

> Here's where the warning comes in. Ordinarily, the engine pulls no more
> that 10-in. Hg at idle (highest vacuum).

Bzzzt. I'd tend dissagree with that. Lift off the throttle at 5000 rpm,
I'm pretty sure you're generating ALOT more vacuum than at idle. That's
why when you have worn valve guides (like mine) you suck oil down the
valve stem and blow blue smoke when you lift at high rpms. Although it
is a sign of a tired engine (and don't I know it!) it is an amusingly
helpfull indicator at an autox. You can have someone watch you and
comment on your driving without ever sitting in the car. They can SEE
when you lift. Poooff. You can also see when a car shifts, or doesn't
shift (shift dave, shift dave! SHIFT DAVE!!). ;)

> When you (or I) connect a

Don't discount how much fatigue 20 years of oscillating vacuum behind a
