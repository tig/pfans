JJ Slowik wrote:

> Sorry to keep pestering you, I just want to make sure I'm really on the
> right track.  If the engine is running too rich, would it make sense
> that it would only misfire when warm, or do you think there's something
> else that I'm missing that is resulting in a drastically different fuel
> mixture between warm and cold engine temperatures?

With the cold start valve bypassed - if your car is tuned to a generally 
too-rich condition, you won't notice the effects until after the car has
come up to normal operating temps.   A cold engine needs a richer
mixture,
which is probably why it runs well until it warms up...

The AAR is going to give lots more air when the engine is cold as well 
which would help to "lean-out" a rich condition that wasn't caused by
a simple mistuning of the FI (leaking injector, cold-start valve, etc.)
