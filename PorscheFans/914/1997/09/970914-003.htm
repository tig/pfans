I was driving around last night and had an odd thought.  (Be warned-
these happen with some regularity... ;-)

The Honda Goldwing has a liquid cooled 1500cc horizontal 6 cyl (boxer)
that is newer and (arguably) more reliable than the 25 year old mill
in my 914.  Has anyone ever looked at putting one into a 914?  It'd
be nice to bring the tranny, too, except for two issues:  the transaxle,
and reverse.  (Goldwings have a reverse now, but it's kind of funky:
you have to turn the motor off, put the tranny in first, and push a
special button that (I believe) turns the starter motor backwards.)

Other than that, the transmission would be a dream- push forward
to downshift, pull back to upshift, clutch optional once you're
rolling.  (All bikes have full-synchro transmissions.  If you're
any good, all you have to do is pick the right RPM range, preload
the shifter, and lift off the throttle for a fraction of a second
to take the load off the tranny long enough to change gears.  You
probably don't want to do it all the time, but for autox or other
racing it'd be a nice feature...)

The radiator space seems to be minimal, which helps some of the
problems with other liquid-cooled motors...

Just think- we'd have a whole other series of /6 conversions ;-)

OK- I've got the nomex shorts on.  Tell me why it won't work...
