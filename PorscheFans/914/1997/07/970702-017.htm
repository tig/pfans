>Check: Pad thickness,bleed lines, free caliper piston(?), cracked rotor, if the brake lines are original, you might have the dreaded swollen line, not allowing fluid to pass freely. If you get a good volume of fluid on the pressure stroke when bleeding, it's probably OK, but the other side front brake line may be restricting the release of fluid pressure. If the lines are original or very old, you probably want to replace them anyway.
