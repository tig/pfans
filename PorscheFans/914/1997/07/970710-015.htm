For Mike Lee and other interested parties:

  Mike asks:

> 1) Is there an easy way to externally identify the type of chain tensioners
>    on a 911 engine.  I'm playing around a bit with the engine I plan to plug
>    into my conversion, and I notice some oil lines running to the tensioner
>    covers; I'm hoping that means they are the late style Carrera hydraulic
>    tensioners.  Is there anyway to visually distinguish these from the other
>    older types?

  A 911 expert I ain't, but I do have a suggestion that MIGHT be helpful.  The latest 
Automotion catalog has a writeup on the various chain tensioners, with pictures.   
They appear on pages 168 and 169.  Perhaps you can deduce what you have 
from this.

> 2) I was trying to remove some spare exterior door handles from a parts car
>    so I could get at the locks for re-keying.  In the process, I broke the
>    stud that holds the door handle "tang" to the door skin; this is the stud
>    that is right near the edge of the door.  The nut is still tightly fastened
>    to the bolt, but any attempts to turn it just result in turning the stud,
>    which appears to be just spinning in the tang (must be some weld or casting
>    that broke).  Any ideas on how to get the handle off?

  The stud is, as I recall, a flat-headed thing like a rivet, that goes through the tang 
that you speak of and is welded on the back side.  I would assume that rust got to 
the weld, and it has given way.  I am not sure what to do about this.   The only things 
I can think of would be either to use a nut-cracker on the nut (if you can fit it in there) 
or do some sort of violence to the door so that you can get at the back of the tang.   
Hopefully there will be someone out there with a better suggestion than mine.
