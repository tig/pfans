guitarsmith@juno.com wrote:

> The new guage had two connections (+ and ground).

This doesn't make sense - most VDO gauges will have 5 connections:
	+/- backlight,  +/- gauge, and signal

If you have two connections (no light in gauge) for power and ground
where is the gauge going to get its signal?

> My "old" gauge, which is virtually identical in face, size and housing,
> has three connections (+ and ground and "S"). Have you seen this style?
> The ground connection is a spade connected to the screw in the center
> between "S" and +.

This is how all my VDO gauges look on the backside.  The "S" connection
is
for the signal wire from the sending unit (in your case the CHT sender).

> I am also wondering how the sender can attach to a bolt or spark plug and
> not be grounding out? 

It's a feature.  The CHT sender is a termisitor which is attached to
chassis
ground (by screwing into the head) and its signal wire.  The resistance
can
be measured by attaching a multimeter to the signal wire and any place
on
the car you can find a good ground.  Check it with the car warm.  The
resistance
should go down as the tempurature goes up.
