I'd suspect a leak somewhere upstream of the calipers. Possibly the hard
lines connecting to the master. Seeing as how you claim to see this much
air in all four caliper lines, suspect whatever they all have in common.
I suspect either the hard lines, the soft lines to/from the resevior, or
the master itself. What else would there be? If it's just the rear, then
maybe the proportioning valve is a possible culprit.

But before you tear apart your braking system. It might behove you to
re-eximne your bleeding technique. No flame or insult intended, but if
you're finding that much air in you lines, it's possible you're letting
air back into to lines between pedal pumps.

> quantities of fluid involved, I am running Castrol LMA which is a bit
> more reasonable in cost.

I used LMA for a while until I started running with race pads. Boiled
the fluid in 20 minutes of spirited driving in the hills (NO BRAKES!
Definate shorts check there). 914s are notorious for hot brakes, and at
the time, brake ducts were still not legal in my class (they are now),
so I did all I could to prevent boiled brake fluid. If you're looking
for a high-temp brake fluid, Ford heavy duty brake fluid is a cheap way
to go. It's the high temp castrol stuff repackaged for ford truck use,
and it's alot cheaper than the actual castrol SRF fluid.
