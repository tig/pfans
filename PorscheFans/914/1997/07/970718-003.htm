I was doing some research on recommended oil temp's in race applications
and
got the following advice from a technical contact at Valvoline.  I
though
the list might find it useful, so...


>From:     Walter H Edmonds
>Date:     07/15/97 03:04:46 PM

>If you are using the Valvoline SAE 20W-50 Racing Motor Oil, 225 deg F
>should be no problem.  Most cars on the road today have a 190 deg F
>thermostat.  The oil temp is usually 30-50 deg F higher than the coolant
>temperature that the gauge in the automobile is measuring.  The oil has to
>reach 212 def F to burn off condensation and other contaminants in the oil.
>If 225 deg F is all you are seeing, no problem.  Keep on going.  If the
>temperature get above 260-270 deg F, back off.  Thank You.
>
>Technical Specialist
>Walter H. Edmonds
