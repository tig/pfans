Hi, I have been running a J-stock racer for the past 4 years (914-2.0 
Stock) and I can tell you it is fun and frustrating at the same time. I am 
in the east and there are people here with money, and J is an easy target. 
The 914-2.0 runs against 68-69 -911's and 924 (not S). The 911's should 
beat you hands down, and the 924 is faster on the long straight, but you 
can beat them in the corners so it is a fun class.

Most of the cars out here are well prepared, and you should plan on 
spending $1k per race on the whole deal, more if you screw up.

The next class up is I and you don't have a chance there. (911's. 914/6, 
etc.) The winner should be a well prepared lightweight 911, and there will 
be lots of them.

To keep the car in J you will need to keep the Fuel Injection on it, make 
no changes to motor other than balancing it and making sure the heads are 
in good shape. (You can make no changes other than lighting for balancing 
from intake to exsaust ports. The tires will need to be 205 x 60 - 15's. 
The weight must be over 2139# and you must have all the interior that is 
not required to be removed for the roll cage/bar.

I recommend the auto-power cage from O-G racing, it must be a bolt in type, 
and it weights about what the spare tire, jack and sound suppression on 
fire wall combined come in at. Given the cost difference between a crossed 
braced roll bar and a cage I will take the cage and walk away from what 
ever I hit.

I drive my car to the track and back, so it is street legal but has no 
heat. I have yet to win a race but have lots of  seconds and thirds. (BTW: 
second is first loser)

Get the rule book and stick to it !  Many people "cheat" (oh my mechanic 
must have done that!) and though they win sometimes, I view them as losers. 

All in All the class is fun and the compitors are good. Come on in the 
tracks fine.
