The unit that Bob Hoover mentioned is probably the least expensive one I've
heard of after the Pertronix/Compufire  point replacement units.   I recall

transformer, they were getting close to the cost of a built one.   Just

Joe,

It would cost less to buy one from Universal Corp. Their address & phone
number is in one of the 'sermons'. Present cost
of the units I use is about $90. The problem with building a unit yourself
is availability of a proper transformer and heat-sink.
By the time you've worked up a suitable circuit board, tracked down a
transformer and figured out how to keep the
switching transistors cool, you will have invested far more than the cost
of a purchased unit.

Heathkit, Radio Shack and others sold the identical module in both kit and
assembled form. Common brand names were
Delta and Archer. All used the same circuit and components, the most
critical of which was the special transformer used in
the inverter.

If you can obtain a back issue of "Nuts & Volts" magazine for February,
1995, you'll find a schematic and parts list for the
Delta Mark 10 CDI module on page 43.
