Sorry, but inspectors in California aren't as dumb as we would hope them
to be. And unfortunately, they are very strict. Simply a single
miss-connected hose may get you failed at most stations. And I doubt
you'd be able to get a 3.6 or 3.8 to look like a 2.0 or 2.2. And why
would you carb it when you've got a great FI to begin with. The will
look at the engine numbers and determine its vintage most likely, and
then the engine'll fail because it's indiction has been modified
significantly.

> This would include original fan shroud, aircleaner box, heater ducting.
> Twin ignition  could be explained away as an emmisions improvement,

Sorry, they won't buy it. They will fail anything, ANYTHING that is not
_stock_ for the model being tested. And if you're trying to pass a 3.6
as a 2.2 motor plan to get failed for illegal modifications. 

> while a late a serial number on the case would be
> attributable to replacement of old and weary mag cases that were beyond
> remachining limits.

They won't buy that either. Best case scenario, they'll fail you and
send you to a referee station. And those are a real pain.

However, if you do a complete transplant with _all_ of the original
emissions equipment intact (including catalytic converter) turbo or
otherwise, then you can have the car tested as a 911 with serious body
work modifications. _That_ they won't care about. Their criteria on
transplants are whichever is NEWER, the engine or the chassis. Whatever
has the more stringent limits smog wise.
