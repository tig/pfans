Stephen,

i had no problem getting in my car, when the trunk lock failed, by
unbolting the rear trunk lid (4 bolts).  the bolts are accessible through
the engine compartment.  after these bolts are out, you can GENTLEY move
the trunk lid enough to get a socket with long extensions (or a
"combinations" of extensions) and a flashlight in there.  at this point
you can unscrew the two 10 or 11mm (?) bolts attaching the lower trunk
part of the lock.  it sounds funny, and is tricky, but it works...and
there is no nasty cutting or drilling of parts or, heaven forbid, the
body.

of course, this is if your key still works.  you might be able to clean it
and regrease the mechanism, but you can try it out of the car.

good luck,
