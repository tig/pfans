Kieth,

	Get yourself onto an empty open road and start swerving at 40 mph. If
you hear the noise go away when you swerve right and get louder when you
swerve left, it's very likely the bearing. The load on the bearing
changes when you swerve or turn and that will result in less howl from a
bad bearing. Beyond that, you need to get your hands dirty.
