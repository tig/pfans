This probably will work, but boy does this sound like the hard way!  Go to
your nearest autoparts store and buy a roll of 3M Window weld.  It is a
continuous roll of round window bonding material specially made for this job;
it bonds and "caulks" in one step.  You just have to make sure the glass and
the pinch weld in the body is clean.  This is what the factory recommends in
the supplement to the shop manual.  No messy silcone or cleanu to worry
about.  This is the same stuff you use to replace windshields.
