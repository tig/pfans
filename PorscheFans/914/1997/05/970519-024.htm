Our steering wheel had developed about 3/16" vertical movement, so I 
disassembled the casting this weekend which holds the upper end of the shaft 
(the same part which also holds, for most of you, the ignition switch).  I 
expected a bad bearing or that I needed to use a bushing/shim between the 
shaft and bearing, but the problem turned out to be a crack in the casting on 
the ignition side of the bearing which was letting the entire bearing move up 
and down.

Luckily, I had a spare with rusted bearing, so I transferred the good bearing 
into the good casting, and VOILA! - only about 1/32" vertical slop now.

Just another "while I was in there" story.
