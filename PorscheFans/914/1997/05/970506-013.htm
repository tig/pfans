On Tue, 6 May 1997, Steve Howitson wrote:

> I have a 72 914 with a tail shift.  The shifting is pretty sloppy.  I
> added a short shifter and its still all over the place.  Is there a
> better transmission option?  Is the swap a hard activity?  I'm up for
> the job if it will help the slop.

You need a side shifter conversion. AA has a kit, others sell parts, too, 
I think. You'll need a '73 or later transmission, of course. The tail 
shifter *can* be converted to a side shifter, but you need to take the 
transmission apart and replace the shift rails with the side shifter type, 
pull out the shift selector at the bottom of the case, and obtain a side 
shifter-type selector and tail piece. If you find a junker side shifter 
and you know that your transmission is in excellent shape (unlikely, but 
there's always hope), you can transfer the parts. The gears, shafts, etc 
are identical, as afar as I can tell. You'll probably find that when you 
drain the oil out of your existing transmission, it'll be black and gross 
(hopefully not). (remove the fill plug first, of course)
