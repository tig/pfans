> It is not adjustable - at least not as far as I know and is buried
> against
> the firewall in the engine compartment.  You probably have to go
> aftermarket
> but someone may correct me...

It is adjustable. There is a screw adjuster on the body of the valve.
It's sealed by the factory with epoxy, but if carefully chip it away,
you can get to it. 

DO NOT do this haphazardly. If you go to far on the adjustment you could
end up with too much rear bias, and that's bad. If the rears lock up
before the front, you can find yourself going backwards entering a turn.
