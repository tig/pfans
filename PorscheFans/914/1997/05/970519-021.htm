Well, since your car is a '75, I believe the front calipers are
_identical_, each with two bleed nipples, the lower of which is useless.
So, if an upper one is sheared off, how 'bout just swapping the calipers
side-to-side?  I think the only problem you may encounter is the "angle" of
the pistons, which will need to be rotated to ensure correct pad wear --
check the Haynes manual for the proper orientation.

That said, unless you have the right tools, rotating a piston in a caliper
bore, especially if it hasn't been rebuilt recently, is a hopeless
proposition. ;-(
