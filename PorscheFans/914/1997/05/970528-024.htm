At 01:54 PM 5/28/97 -0700, you wrote:
>> Anyone have any advice on rear springs for my 914? There are so many
>> differerent ones listed I am not sure which ones to buy. My car is a
>> stock 2 litre with sways front and rear. It is driven on the street only
>> and I would like to get the stock ride height and feel back. Also, does
>> anyone have any idea's about bilstein's with adjustable spring plates?
>
>If the car is street driven machine and you don't intend of competing
>with it, the adjustable plates are a waste of money. Get a set Bilstiens
>or Koni Reds, and probably 100# coil springs in the rear.  That should
>set you up just fine.

My opinion is forget the 100# "stock" springs and go for a set of Weltmeister
PROGRESSIVE RATE 140# springs.  The PROGRESSIVE RATE will give you as nice a
street feel as lesser 100# springs, but will give a little more performance
for those spirited drives.

As Josh said, don't go to the expense of the adjustable plates unless you
plan on corner-balancing for serious racing.  By the way, the Koni spring
perches require a different spring with smaller diameter coils.

Sean at AA suggested to me that the Bilsteins are a much better "all around"
shock than the Koni's.  His reccommendation, if I recall, was based on the
fact that Bilsteins have much less fade and longer overall life expectancy.
