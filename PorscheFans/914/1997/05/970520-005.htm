	Good suggestion.  If this doesn't work (or if the cold idle
is ridiculously high when you do this), start the regular FI diagnotic
procedure.  An idle mix that gets too rich will cause the idle to bog
down, often with a fuel smell.  (BTDT, fighting it right now...)
	In my own case, I have a potentiometer in series with the Head
Temp sender.  If I leave it at zero resistance, the car warms up
pretty poorly, with a big lean hunt from about 1 minute of warmup to
3-5 of warmup + 3-5 minutes of driving.  If I tweak the pot to
eliminate the hunt, the idle bogs down as described.
	I checked my ignition system (always check it before trouble-
shooting the FI!) and now my main suspect is the head temp sensor
itself.
	Other things to check include the fuel pressure (28-29 PSI),
manifold pressure sensor, and cold start valve/thermo-time switch.

	Good luck!  I'll let everyone know if/when I get this fixed!
