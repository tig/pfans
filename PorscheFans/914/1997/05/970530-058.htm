The points intallation is very straight forward. Just look at how the
points are installed in your car before you remove them. There is a
notch on one end that you can use to push it with a screwdriver in order
to adjust the dwell. Step one, replace the points. Step two, adjust the
gap between the points when the cam inside the distributor is peaked
against the points rubbing block. Use your feeler gauge to set it at
..016 in. Then put the whole thing together and start the car. Make sure
you tightened the points down before closing it up. Not too tight, you
don't want to strip it. Measure the dwell off of the green wire that
connects to the coil. If the dwell is out of range (44 to 50 degrees)
then turn off the car, open the distributor and adjust the gap. More gap
equals less angle, less gap equals more angle. Once you're done, set the
timing and away you go!
