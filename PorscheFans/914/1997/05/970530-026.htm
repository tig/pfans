At 08:31 AM 5/30/97 -0700, you wrote:
>Ken,
>        Corner balancing is weighing each corner of the car (while the
>surface is perfectly level) and trying to get the corner weights equal.
>This is an impossible feat in a 914.  There is just too much difference
>between the front and rear weight wise.  What you can do is adjust the car
>so that the diagonals are equal.  That is add the RF and LR and the LF and
>RR and they should be equal.  This gives you a good balance.  You can also
>increase the rake of your car to move more weight forward, but you'll never
>get 50/50.  We did this exercise (with someone seated in the driver's seat)
>and were able to get about 47/53 with 5 gals of gas on board.
