> Anyone have any tried-and-possibly-true methods of setting
> toe-in at home?  I put turbo tie rods on when I installed rack
> spacers last year and never got the toe-in set yet.

You should really get a four wheel thrust alignment. Than you'll know if =
your car is straight, too.

Anyway, John Muir, shows a simple method using two sticks and a pencil =
in his "Keeping your Volkswagon Alive" book. With the car on level ground=
, take the sticks and put one end of each at the rear edge of each wheel,=
 pointing inward. Mark a line where the end of one stick overlaps the oth=
er. Do the same for the front. The difference is your toe-in (or toe-out,=
 as the case may be)
