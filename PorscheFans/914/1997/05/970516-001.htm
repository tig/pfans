It's a tricky issue. But constant speed is the easiest. The concern I
have is a situation like a long high speed WFO straight. And then snap
the throttle closed for a time. Shock-cooling comes to mind. With the
crank driven fan, air is delivered with revs, more revs, more heat, more
air is needed. Certainly there is lag due to the thermal mass of the
heads. But they were designed to radiate heat as efficiently as they
could (at the time they were designed), and can probably dump alot of
heat in a hurry. Shock-cooling is one reason that people can loose valve
seats in Type IV motors.
