> In the Quaife, as I understand it, there are 3 pairs of worms arranged

Yes, and I just recently learned that the only type of diff to deliver
power with only one wheel loaded is a "detroit-locker" and is not a good
diff for road racing applications. In other words, most every type of
LSD requires a reference torque to drive the slower wheel. Even the
salisbury clutch type diffs. I just talked with Gary Taylor at Tylor
Race Engineering , they make leightweight adjustable salisbury type
diffs. And when I say light, I mean 7lbs light! And he told me that all
LSDs require a reference torque. His recomendation for the 901

stupid. Not only that but he can get 'em cheaper than advertised prices.
