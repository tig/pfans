Jerry R. Brady wrote:
 
> As far as compression goes, the going maximum compression ration
> for premium-pump gas(94) is somewhere between 9.0 and 9.5:1.
> Bumping your compression up would have a minimal inpact on most
> other aspects of your engine, but you will sacrifice longevity
> in the name of HP.

I'd like to add to Jerry's comments and say that increasing the
compression ratio also increases the temperature of your cylinder heads.
IMHO, if you are considering higher C.R., spend some time studying
external oil coolers and even aftermarket air cooling systems ( like FAT
performance's trick upright system).  The added cooling will help your
engine from deteriorating from the added heat, but it won't last as long
as a stock rebuild.
