        Mark:

        I believe your salesman friend is overstating the problem a bit.  What 
you're describing is the well known 'Groove of Doom," and occurs when R1's are 
rather severely underinflated.  Some racers were using them at pressures in the 
teens and low twenties, and BFG went to great lengths to explain why pressures 
this low were causing the problem.  As long as you keep the pressures in the mid
to high 20's or above, you'll be fine.  Hundreds of us on this list run R1's 
without encountering the groove of doom problem.  I myself run them all of the 
time, track and street.  As for crashing, I think it's unlikely, unless some 
yutz was simply not paying attention to how much his tires were worn and simply 
wore the centers down into nothing.

        Hope this helps.
