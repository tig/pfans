I would skip the no name, but 'specially for a 914' type speakers. =
Making or finding an adapter and putting in speakers you know are a good =
brand is a far better choice.=20

Just make sure the speakers are not too deep -- there is not a lot of =
space behind the stock grilles. Get a Crutchfield catalog. They have =
great technical info on the dimension limits for mounting speakers in =
virtually any car. I think they also sell lots of adapter plates.

I put a set of MB Quart 4" rounds in and they work great. To do this, I =
used a set of homemade plywood adapter plates left by a previous owner. =
I also used black sheet foam from a craft store as a front for the wood =
-- looks nice through the grille.=20

The biggest limitation to the stock 914 speaker mounts is that they are =
fairly thin rigid plastic -- so are prone to vibration problems if given =
much bass. By chance, I ran into a MB Quart product manager on the 'Net =
who is a former 914 owner. He suggested reinforcing the backs of the =
plastic frames with bondo. I have not had the chance to do it yet, but =
it makes a lot of sense.
