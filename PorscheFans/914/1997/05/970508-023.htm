The main reason the rear bar is ommitted on most autocross cars lacking
a LSD, is that the cars are running stiffer rear springs (generally
140-165 lbs). The stiffer rear springs will add to the roll rate of the
rear of the car sufficiently to cause wheel lift under hard cornering if
there is also a rear sway bar installed.

On your car, with stock rear springs and stock torsion bars, I would say
leave it all as is. Maybe add a stiffer front bar, but definately leave
the rear bar hooked up if you're running stock (or near stock) weight
rear springs.
