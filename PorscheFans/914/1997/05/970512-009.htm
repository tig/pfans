	Hey, I resemble that remark!  ;^)

	I got a lot of advice about break-in on my motor back when.  The
2K-3K RPM for 20-30 minutes is supposedly mostly to break-in the cam, cam
bearings, and lifters.  It may or may not also be good for the rings.
You'll have to choose if you want to do this or not.
	Anyway, Jerry covered a lot of it, but I'll forward what Stan Hanks
told him (which he forwarded to me back when I was facing the break-in):

>Make *SURE* that everything is installed correctly. Check and double check.
>Fill with SAE-40 oil, pre-fill the oil filter, disconnect the coil wire,
>crank for about a minute after the oil pressure light has gone out.
>Re-connect
>coil and start it up (you may have to screw around with lots of things at
>this point 8{) ), bring it up to about 2500 RPM and let it run there for
>about
>half an hour. Bring idle down to about 800 RPM, adjust timing. Stop engine.
>Let sit overnight. Change oil and filter (still SAE 40). Adjust valves. Check
>torque on the exposed fasteners (including head studs). Drive car for
>about 30 miles, running through a wide range of RPM (accelerate from say 20
>to say 70, back all the way off the throttle, repeat, etc). DRIVE NORMALLY
>otherwise. Return to home base, change oil and filter. Drive normal for 300
>miles (another 270), again being sure to run through a wide range of RPM
>(please stay under 5000). Let sit overnight, change oil and filter, adjust
>valves and check fastener torque. Next oil change/valve adjust is 3000
>miles, then oil every 3000 and valves every 6000 after that.
>
>And stick with SAE-40 oil. I like Valvoline, even though I no longer
>have a sponsorship deal with them... *AND* do *NOT* use Fram or AC or K-Mart
>or any oil filter other than the OEM filters. Mann. Mahle. Nothing else.
>You can get these  at good prices from Zims, etc.

	Tim Radsick had a slightly different procedure:
> 1) Like Mike W said, remove coil wire and turn engine over until oil
>pressure
> light goes out (make sure oil filter is FILLED before you start this) Do not
> use synthetic yet, use 30Wt
>
> 2) Reinstall coil wire, make sure all hoses and stuff are hooked up
>right, and
> start the car.  Immediately bring revs up to 2000-3000 and hold there for an
> honest-to-god 20 minutes.  Use a watch!  I have done this on a few Type 1
> engines and trust me it seems like a LONG time!  This helps to break in
>the cam
> and lifters, plus get the oil heated up and circulating everywhere.
>
> 3) Drain the oil.  Replace filter.  New oil (not synthetic yet).  Start
>car and
> take it for your first drive.  Never go beyond 3/4 throttle and never stay
> cruising at a constant speed/RPM.  Sort of let the engine rev up and then
>rev
> down.  Vary your rates of acceleration and deceleration.  This seats the
>rings
> properly for various engine demands.  Make this trip about 40-60 minutes.
>Do
> not test out the RPM capacity at this time.
>
> 4) Drain the oil.  Replace filter. New oil (nope- still no synthetic!) Let
> engine cool overnight.  Have a beer and sleep.  Wake up. Brush your
>teeth.  Set
> the valves properly.  Now your car is sort of drivable as a car.  Just
>try to
> vary engine RPM and don't rev too high.
>
> 5) At 300 miles, new oil (Maybe synthetic?) and filter, reset valves.
>
> 6) When oil consumption drops (rings have seated) swap over to synthetic and
> change oil and filters every 3000-5000 and set valves every 6000.
