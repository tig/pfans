JJ Slowik wrote:
> 
> If everything goes well (and we all know the likelihood of that), then
> I'll have my 914 running by the end of this weekend.  What is the
> recommended immediate break-in process?  I know that I'm supposed to
> take it kinda' easy for the first 500-1000 miles (nothing over 4000
> rpm), and change the oil and adjust the valves at 500 mile intervals for
> the first 1000 miles at least.  But my question is, once I'm ready to
> turn the key for the first time in 6 months, *if* it starts, what should
> I do?  Let it idle?  Hold the RPMs up?  Drive it around?  I don't want
> to screw this up...


If it is a complete rebuild, as mine was, with new bearings, P&Cs, 
valve work, etc.  The advice I received was to start the engine, 
bring it up to 2000-2500 RPM and run it there for 20 minutes and
shut it off.

Let sit overnight - change oil and filter.  Adjust valves, timing,
and any other tuning.

Then run for about 300 miles making sure to spend lots of time at
various RPM levels during driving. (don't just get on the highway
and cruise at 75 the whole time)  Change oil and filter, adjust
valves.

Then resume normal maintenance.
