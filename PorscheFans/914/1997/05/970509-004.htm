> ATROSAA (at the risk of starting another argument), SOFM (speaking only for
> myself), and IMHO (in my humble opinion), I like the feel of about 28# all
> around.  Again, ATROSAA, SOFM, and IMHO, tire pressures are for getting
> maximum tread on the ground.  With radials, in hard cornering, you'll see
> scrub around the corner of the tread, but not as far as the sidewall when the
> pressure is correct.

I've heard that tire pressure adjustments for AutoX or racing are 
only effective if you're running with bias ply tires.  With radials,
as mentioned above, the sidewall is so stiff that pressure adjustments
won't do the trick or will have mimimal effect on your handling.

The recommended practice to increase your 'contact patch' would then
indicate suspension adjustments with radials vs. adjusting tire 
pressures.

One other note, if you use a pyrometer to check your tires after 
running your car - use the probe type.  And with radials you want
to make sure you get the probe deep enough to read the temp near
the radials and not of the outer tread itself.  That's where the
tire will wear/fail first.

Just what I've heard...
