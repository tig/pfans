> Since I'm not stuck to a certain displacement, what are experiences with
> the merits of each? I've seen the 2.7RSR spec (ala Andy's 6), 3.0SC
> (Rich J. and Al J.), 3.2 Carerra, and even heard of squeezing in the 3.6
> 964 (twin plugs would sure be nice). I was also intrigued by Bruce A.'s
> description in the May Excellence of a "3.2 Carrera RS" engine based on
> MFI with SC case/crank/rods with 98mm P/Cs and 911S cams.

My personal bias is probably always going to be towards an MFI engine, I
just think they're way cool. Also, a 2.7 case is a good bit lighter than
the 3.0+ cases. It depends on what you want out of it. Reliability and
lower maintenance, or the fun-factor. If reliability and maintenance are
the priorities, go for the 3.0 or 3.2 stock motors. They're pretty much
bomb-proof, and are much more tractable. As for a fun-factor motor, my
bias is towards an RSR engine. Of course other people will have differnt
prefernces, and some may say that the RSR engine is just all wrong. But
then, I'm not other people... :)
 
> While I don't mind investing time into putting together the powertrain
> [it's a hobby afterall ;)], I'd like the install to be very clean
> (minimal mods to body to make it fit) and durable (it's my daily
> driver). From a "bang for the buck" perspective, more CC cost more $$,
> but for the same power, the various displacements appear to even out
> somewhat (e.g. the 2.7 requires hot cams, headwork, etc. where the 3.0 -
> 3.6 have good/excellent power/durability in stock form).

It's not really a total wash. The smaller displacement motors have to
make sacrifices to gain the same power as the larger displacement
engines. Things like low end torque, power band width, ease of
maintenance, and overall lifetime. Food for thought. There's no such
thing as free power, and almost every mod you do one of these motors
will be a trade off somewhere. Things like cams will give you lots of
kick at higher rpms, but rob it of alot of bottom end power and possibly
make the idle difficult.
