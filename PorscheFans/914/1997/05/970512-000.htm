On Fri, 9 May 1997, JJ Slowik wrote:

> If everything goes well (and we all know the likelihood of that), then >
I'll have my 914 running by the end of this weekend.  What is the >
recommended immediate break-in process? 

Check out the "Hoover Sermons" for a good overview of this. They are on 
the Web somewhere.

One of things he says to do is to cycle the revs up and down from idle to
about 2,500rpm for sometime upon intial startup. This is to keep the oil
pressure up, and it is done with the engine unloaded (ie, in neutral). He
does it on the bench, but in the car wouldn't make anydifference. Also,
immediately inspect everywhere for oil leaks and anomolous sounds. Adjust
the valves often, also, they will drift. Also, change the oil after the 
intial run to get all of that assembly lube outa there.
