JJ Slowik wrote:

> I plan on cranking with no fuel
> or spark until I get pressure, and then *hopefully* starting it.  I
> think I'll use a non-synthetic 30w oil... do I really need to change the
> oil 3 times in the first 300 miles?  I heard this was to get all of the
> assembly lube out, so maybe I'm missing something here.  The only
> pre-lubing I did was a little on the wrist pin, in the piston where the
> wrist pin slides in, and on the rod bearing where the pin slides
> through, and I only used motor oil.  Have I screwed up again?  The motor
> seems to turn over by hand just fine.  Also, is it possible that I could
> do more harm than good with the 20 min. 2500 rpm thing since it's not a
> new cam?

Your main goal then is to help the new rings get seated.  I think
your correct in avoiding the 20min@2000RPM burn-in and subsequent
oil-change as you didn't crack your case and replace any bearings,etc.

Get it started, get it timed and tuned and follow the next step which
is a short 30 mile trip at *varying* RPM levels to help the rings seat
then back to homebase, let sit and oil change (it can't hurt).  

You may want to check for valves at this point, but since you didn't
mess with the valve train you may be ok in this department; it's your
call.

Then do 300 more miles the same way to finish seating the rings.  Change
oil and check valves.  This is what *I* would do...
