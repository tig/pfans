On 5/30/97 12:08 PM, Dirk Wright wrote:

>Why would anyone want a 50/50 weight distribution? I think it's a marketing
>fallacy.

>When a car goes around a corner with all four wheels sticking, the car 
>turns about the point between the rear wheels. When a car is sliding on 
>all four wheels around a corner, it turns about the center of mass. If the 
>center of mass is in the exact middle of the car (as in this theoretically 
>ideal 50/50 weight distribution), then as the car transitions from 
>sticking to sliding, the center point of rotation moves from the point 
>between the wheels to the middle of the car. This will upset the cars 
>attitude (direction of pointing).

My car sure doesn't turn about the center of mass when it starts to slide 
(that would be spinning, not sliding!).  In my car the center of mass 
begins to move radially outward from the center of the turn.  If the car 
has 50/50 weight distribution then both ends slide the same amount 
producing nuetral handling.  If the weight bias is more toward the rear, 
then the rear will likely move more away from the center of the turn, 
leading to oversteer condition.  (BTW, this is the condition most race 
car drivers like, they can feel the rear slide and can correct with the 
steering and throttle to maintain control.)
