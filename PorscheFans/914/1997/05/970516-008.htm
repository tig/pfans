right,  I think I saw Stan's car with just the fan on the cooler.   That
means the air circulates all around trunk area (heats up) as opposed to
being forced through.  (I don't have a fan on mine.)   If it is not
enclosed,  I recommend directing the right to the cooler.     OOPS,  just
re-read your mail,  guess it is ducted.

Other than that,  I don't really have any ideas.  It should work.  I run
with my rain tray off the engine lid.   Could the oil cooler flow be
blocked in any way?   Is your sandwich plate thermostat so high that it is
not letting oil into aux. cooler until it is TOO hot already?

I read your post with interest.
Re:
>3.) Put it up front.  ...  It works great!!!   For those

Perhaps youo can shed some light on a problem I'm having.  I have Stan
Hank's old car (belonged to Brad Davis previously). It came from Texas. It
is set up in a manner that sounds very similar to yours:
  1) stock cooler and thermostat in place.
  2) 10AN lines from spin-on adapter to front trunk and to/from cooler,
etc.
  3) All holes unplugged in front of car (w/mesh in front of them).
  4) 18"x12" cooler on 1/2" square tubing "frame", flat, above cutout in
trunk floor.
  5) Large (16") fan above cooler mounted to "frame".
  6) Very nice ducting to ensure all air blows over cooler and out floor.
Unfortunately, this setup doesn't do beans for me.  The fan has little or
no effect, oil temps (from the stock sender in the sump) reach 275-280F on
20-minute runs on a 90-degree day. Thermocouples _on_ (not _in_) the input
and output hoses register a 6-degree difference from what goes in vs. what
comes out, and an absolute temp of around 230F.
Do you have any ideas as to why this works so poorly? The motor is a
bone-stock 2.0 (FI), BTW.
