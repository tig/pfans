> 5. Still no mention of an alternator bracket.  At the least, buyers would want
> to know where to get one (recommended supplier) or would want a blueprint they

That looks to be a little more difficult. For one, the alternator
recieves cooling air from the fan housing. In addition a pully for the
alternator would need to be created, and it can't be too small or it'll
burn up the alternator.

> 6. No mention of possible downsides or applications to be avoided.  For
> example, it's obvious it can't be used with stock FI, but does its racing
> function imply it shouldn't be used on the street for some reason?  Is there
> such a thing as too much cooling air going over the engine?  What about using
> it in the rain - any problem there?

Rain? I doubt would be much of a problem. You drive through deep puddles
don't you? And the bottom of the engine, cylinders and heads are
splashed every now and then. Rust however, from sitting in the rain may
become more of an issue.

As for over cooling? I think that could be a problem. But not at high
RPM, if anything it's insufficient at high rpm. The maker claims 1,200
cuft/min output from the fan. That's alot of air, but the original
factory system delivers just over 1,700 cuft/min at 4600 rpm. Now that's
just at 4600 rpm, and they claim that this is for a racing engine, which
runs at higher rpms than a street car, and as such will run hotter, and
need more air. I would not recomend this system for a street driven
competition car. The air supply at higher rpm would not be enough. It'd
probably be good for engines that run in sprints. And don't spend too
long at really high teperatures. But for say a track car that runs a 20
minute race? I don't think it'd work. At the low rpm range, the fan
would be delivering too much air, it's not regulated by engine speed
like the crank driven fan is. But that could be compensated by adding a
small feedback circuit that takes its input off of the head temp. The
cooler the heads, the less voltage it delivers to the fan. It's a DC
fan, so that's easy to control. Which could also aid in rapid warmup if
one so desired.

> 7. My current accelerator cable bracket would mount inside the fan housing, so

That's something that should probably be left to the buyer too. Any car
that has a non-stock setup (like this requires) will probably be set up
a little differently from each other. But a hard point or two on the
shroud for mounting accessories would be nice.

> 8. They show a degree wheel where the impeller was; again, there's an

> 9. On the secondary gains side, it would sure be a lot easier to change (late

You said it!

> 10. They claim "%HP" gains, but aren't clear how much is due to the fan
> change, and how much is due to the removal of the alternator.

I'd say that the fan certainly produces a substantial amount of drag on
the engine. It takes energy to move that much air, and that energy is
coming directly from the engine. But I know that the alternator has some
impact on power. Not enough to be blatantly noticable though. I've
thrown my belt numerous times and never noticed a sudden increase in
power.
