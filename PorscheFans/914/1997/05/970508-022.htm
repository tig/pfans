> Rear [anti-roll] bars are usually only recommended for use with a
> limited slip differential (because the LSD increases "push" so
> much).

Michael:

I'm probably just nitpicking, but the main reason for only putting a
rear swaybar on an LSD-equipped car is this:

The swaybar will necessarily lift the inside wheel in a corner.

If you have a limited-slip dfifferential, this is no big deal... It
will direct the engine torque to the wheel that's still on the
ground.

If, on the other hand, you have an open (i.e., non-LSD) differential,
all your engine torque will go to that inside wheel and it'll just
spin uselessly.
