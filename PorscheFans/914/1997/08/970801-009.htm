I agree with JJ.  The platinum plugs take less to make them fire, so if you
have a marginal ignition system you will see an improvement since you get
more spark and fewer misfires.  But if your ignition is basically sound, or
you have a newer electronic ignition system like an MSD, Jacobs, CDI, etc.
you shouldn't need anything other than the regular plugs. Plus they are
cheaper.

The  info I got with the Jacobs EI recommends against the platinum for the
above reasons.  They recommend the regular type of plug.

Well, I can't say I've heard of cracked heads as a result of them, but I
can say that I've heard other 914 owners claim better performance with
the Bosch Copper Plugs.  FWIW, I have a hunch that it's more
coincidence.  When people see commercials advertising the virtues of
platinum plugs, they tend to use them more often.  914 heads tend to
crack around the spark plug holes naturally, so put the two unrelated
items together, and it superficially appears that there's a connection.
