Yeah, dont' expect much out of the vw motor. It's already somewhat on the
ragged edge of reliability, so the motor probably wouldn't last too long
without extensive modifications. Due to the poor valve train layout, the
exhaust valves run hot, limiting compression. If you increase the effective
compression by usng a turbocharger, then you'll probably get detonation. The
things that I think would have to be done to make a turbo type 4 reliable
include: ceramic coating of the valves, combustion chamber and exhaust port,
some kind of plating on the outside of the cylinders to prevent rust and
enhance heat removal, strengthening of the crank and rods (including
precision balancing), increased oil pressure and flow, external oil cooler,
some kind of bigger cooling fan, etc. With turbos, you have to get serious
about heat managment, such as providing a cold air snorkel for the intake,
insulating the exhaust system from the motor, etc. It could be done, but it
probably would be better to just install a 911 motor.
