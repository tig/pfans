Sure you have options, take out the old and pick up a set of Hella or
Cibie H4s from one of the many catalog companies, any 7" round will work.
 Buy an extra H4 bulb and you and stow it in the glove box.

I have used both Cibie and Hella, I prefer my Cibies, but they are darn
expensive compared to the Hella's which are now DOT legal.  You will want
this, every now and again you can run into police trouble if you run none
DOT lights.  Also means any local mechanic can aim them since they have
the little nubs to support the aiming instrument.  I got my aiming unit
from Meijer for $11.99 works great.

The difference in lighting is not the actual wattage, but the focal
points of the lenses, and the lenses design.  You get what you pay for,
and no way will a $12.99 Halogen sealed beam beat a H4 light with a
proper design.  The difference is phenomenal, you can see.  If you want
more check out Brian Ngs web page, (Brian I need your URL), he has a
diode setup that works wonders.  Thanks Brian!!!!  So simple I passed up
the idea several times in my head.

>
>How about the fog lights... Can I make these
>high powered driving lights with a new bulb 
>and lenses?

Ottos sells a kit that converts these to an H1 light and will let you
pick bulb wattage.  The big disadvantaged here is that you are stuck with
the original factory reflector and lenses design, which was set up as a
fog light.  If you are looking for a driving light a fog light lenses
will not do, completely different focal design.  So if you want driving
lights go pick them up.  The Europe lights are nice, there are some new
generic types out there in catalogs, AA will have one soon.  Some careful
measurements and you may find one that fits.  I have a set of Cibie
driving lights that are a perfect fit, but allas the part number is long
gone.

Once you have upgraded lights, you can also pickup higher wattage bulbs
if you wish, but this will make your lighting illegal for street use.
