Hi, I took one of those steering rods that go from the small u-joint down to
the steering rack, and cut a notch about 3/4" from the end with an angle
grinder.  (it was from a parts car obviously =8^) )

Leave the sharp edges where you grind it out and it grips the torsion spring
better,  it still takes two people because you have to have one push on the
lever (doing this with the rear trunk lid off) and the other person guide the
spring
onto the pulley/roller - with a wrench or something (NOT FINGERS PLEASE).

There probably is a better way, but this worked fine for me.
