>         Powdercoating is (more or less) another way of painting
> something.
>         [Massive oversimplifications:]  A paint is basically a
> pigment suspended in a liquid.  The liquid dries, leaving the
> pigment.  (There are also other things in modern auto paints
> that are left, but for this explanation they count as "pigment".)
>         Powdercoating omits the liquid.  Pigments are applied
> directly to the parts (as powders), then the parts are heated
> and the pigment melts together and adheres to the parts.
>         It gives very good uniform coverage when done right, and
> you can put different stuff onto the parts than you can by using
> a liquid medium.
