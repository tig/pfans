> But the red ["empty" fuel] warning light works fine. Same circuit?

	Different circuit.  The light is turned on when a metal piece
in the float bridges two contacts at or near the bottom of the tank.
It is usually much more reliable than the gauge.  When it comes on,
you only have about a gallon left, so find gas ASAP.
