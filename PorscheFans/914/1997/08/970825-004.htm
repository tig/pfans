At 10:42 AM 8/25/97 -0600, JJ Slowik wrote:
>O.K., now I've got to try to figure out what the mechanics couldn't.
>Here's the symptoms:  The car starts instantly when cold, idles a bit
>rough, and apparently, the fuel mixture is different every time the car
>is stopped and started.  

It sounds like a temperature related bad electrical connection in the fuel
injection system. It very well could be inside the ecu ("brain") box. As the
components inside the box get warm, either some part changes value or one
(or more) of the transistors fails to function (stays either full on or full
off).

> The car is equipped with an electrical
>ignition, so the points that control the spark aren't an issue, but the
>points that control the injectors could be, right?  How do I check
>these?  If I pull the distributor out to do so, do I risk knocking the
>timing out of whack again? 

Put a mark on the distributor and the engine so that you can line them up
again when you pull the distributor. There are 2 screws on the side of the
distributor near the bottom where the ecu harness attaches. Undo those
screws and the points slide out the side. The contacts should be clean looking.
