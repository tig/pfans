Joshua raised some good questions about the EFI system.

More in the D-Jetronic Replacement:

The original manifolding is for those who want the least modifications
and want to retain the stock appearance.  Dual-dual throttle bodies can
also be used.

The system is sequential with individual fuel and timing on an
individual cylinder basis for optimal tuning.  Of course fuel and timing
maps with configuration will be part of the system.

A O2 sensor input for optional closed loop operation is provided.

The system will use the modified distributor to sense engine position. 
A crank only system is a compromise unless it has a cam sensor. Most
crank systems are batch inject or twice inject.  With twice inject the
the timing error related to on/off errors are doubled.  With batch
inject manifold pressures and timing creates errors on a cylinder by
cylinder basis.
