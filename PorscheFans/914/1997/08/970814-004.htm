Sagging spring does not change its spring rate, until one of the coils
collapse.  Check the formula close.  There is no variable for distance
between the coils or overall height.  Allot of progressive use spring
spacing to collapse and then to increase the spring rate, by limiting the
number of active coils.  In theory you could be better off running a
collapsed spring in a stock class auto-x.  Just use spacers and or
adjusted collar (Koni / Blistien).  Then you may end up with a cheap
progressive as the coils collapse on one or another.

The higher rates where optional, I never seen the P/N for them.
