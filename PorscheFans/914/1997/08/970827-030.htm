> As explained to me, the increase in pressure as tires warm comes from the
> moisture in the air within the tires.  As the moisture warms it expands
> (steam).  This causes the increase in tire pressure.  Since bottled N2
> contains no moisture, filling the tires with N2 alliveates this effect.

That's basically it. And I thought I'd seen the end of this thread. But
the heated moisture does not turn to steam. Actual steam is supreheated
watervapor and would melt the rubber in no time. It is simply that water
vapor has a greater expansion rate than dry nitrogen. Not that N2
doesn't expand, it does, but it's expansion is more predictable when the
moisture content is controlled.
