If it can in any way help... I recently upgraded my entire stereo system
after many months of planning.  Here's what worked for me: I added a
subwoofer box into the footwell of my '74 2.0 similar to what Brian did
(I wish I would have known about his install before I did mine) I used
an 8" Boston RS8 and built a 3 sided box totally out of 3/4" MDF and
sealed it onto the interior wall (which I added a layer of Dynamat to). =20

**Mike M.-Depending on how big those cables are you may be able to seal
them within the box. The weatherstripping around the outside of the unit
would certainly accomodate a "normal" thickness of cable.**

The box takes up 1" of legroom but the sound improvement is HUGE! I then
added seperate tweeters on top of the dash pointed towards my big ole
ears.

The Pioneer Premier GM-X404 4 channel amp is mounted upsidedown,
underneath the spare tire panel in the front trunk. This way you don't
lose any room or even notice it until you pick up the panel (it fits
nicely into the space vacated by the spare tire rim). It is also easily
removeable for Auto-X events.  The Motorola AM/FM radio was replaced
with a Pioneer Premier DEX-P88 which is a AM/FM single CD player that
has provisions to control either Pioneer multi CD box and has a built-in
alarm! Just remove the face plate when exiting the car and it arms the
alarm.  If the alarm is tripped a horrible noise is sent through the
speakers.  You can wire up an external (traditional alarm type) speaker,
but I think that would be overkill (you can also disable the ignition).
Installation of all components was straightforeward with one exception.
The amp has a diagram on it for the wiring of the speakers, it's wrong.=20
The correct diagram is in the manual.  Strange but true!
