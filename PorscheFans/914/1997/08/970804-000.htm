Why not do both (ie, shuffle pin and raceware studs)? I assume you're using
a counterweighted crank. I read somewhere that in these vw motors the center
main journal suffers from a precessing motion from the crank shaft flexing
under load. I think the extra metal in a counterweighted crank makes the
crank stiffer and thus reduces this problem. So, do all three:
counterwieghted crank, raceware bolts (and studs), and shuffle pins. Another
thing is to get better rods, which several machine shops on the left coast
manufacture.
