Dave,
I recently removed and cleaned up the (stock) mirror.
Actually pretty easy and a good idea - if it doesn't move freely the
glass can break if bumped. I've heard you can even have a local glass
shop cut a new mirror if needed (Tech tips?). I recall it went like this:

Remove from car.
Remove nut from inside bottom of stalk (note configuration of pieces).
Remove mirror from frame by (gently) prying out the (black) plastic
        retaining seal - it *does* comes out and will be reused.
Clean everything and lube lightly.

Assembly is the reverse of the removal procedure.
