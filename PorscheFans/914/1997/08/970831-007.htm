This is a pretty straightforward job. You will need to remove the
alternator from underneath the car, so you must raise it up on jacks.
Unplug the three wire connector from the alternator on the relay board,
and the heavy wire to the starter. loosen the adjusting screw on the
slotted alternator bracket, slid the alternator over and remove the
drive belt. Go under the car, you will have to remove some of the heater


ducting, (I think the J pipe) and anything else that's in the way. (not
the heat exchanger). Loosen the remaining bolts and pull the cooling
duct off the alternator. Work the alternator around until you get it
down. I may have overlooked something, doing this from memory, but it
was a fairly intuitive job, no special tricks, except for the unexpected
cooling hose to the alternator from the fan housing. Thorough German
engineering.
