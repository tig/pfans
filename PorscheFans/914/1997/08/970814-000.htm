> What the heck is a side shifter?

Something you should already have if the car is/was a '75. On early
914s, they used the very same tranny case that the 911s used and as such
the shift linkage entered on the very end of the box. On the 911, this
was great as the box pointed forward, but the 914 is set up in the
reverse direction and as such they (Porsche) needed to extend the shift
linkage all the way to the back of the car and it had to go though a few
bends and joints. The unfortunate result was a VERY sloppy shifter. In
'73 they resolved this problem by moving the linkage forward to the side
of the case. The shorter linkage and fewr direction changes drastically
improved the accuracy of the shifting.
