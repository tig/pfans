> Anybody know the point gap and spark gap setting for 1973 914-4.
> Also the ignition timing setting.

  The point gap is given as .016"; Haynes cautions that this is a
preliminary assembly setting only; final adjustment is to be done
with a dwell meter:
 dwell angle = 44 - 50 degrees.

 Ignition timing for 1.7 and 2.0 litre engines = 27 degrees BTDC @
3500 RPM vacuum advance disconnected.   For 1.8 litre engines, timing
= 7.5 degrees BTDC @ 800 - 900 RPM; vacuum advance disconnected.

 Beware... timing marks can only be seen in a mirror; they are on the
base of the fan which is the edge closest to the engine.  Original
timing marks are stamped "0" (on 2.0 litre engines only), or white
(1.7 litre engines only) for TDC (no TDC mark present on 1.8 litre
engines),  black for 5 degrees advanced  (5 degree mark present on
1.7 litre engines only... this is the static timing mark), red for 27
degrees BTDC (1.7 and 2.0 litre engines only).   1.8 litre engines
have only one timing mark at 7.5 degrees.
