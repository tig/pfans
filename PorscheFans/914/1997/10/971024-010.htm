Nope... The 4-cylinder cars have different ratios than the sixes:

            914-4:              914-6:              916:

    1st:    A - 11:34 (3.091)   A - 11:34 (3.091)   sub-A     (3.18)
    2nd:    F - 18:34 (1.889)   GA- 18:32 (1.778)   G - 18:33 (1.833)
    3rd:    N - 23:29 (1.261)   O - 23:28 (1.217)   N - 23:29 (1.261)
    4th:    V - 27:25 (0.926)   V - 27:25 (0.926)   U - 26:25 (0.962)
    5th:    ZD- 31:22 (0.710)   ZA- 29:22 (0.759)   ZA- 29:22 (0.759)

    Final Drive:  7:31 (4.429:1) for all models.

The 901 transmissions used in the early 911s, by the way, were
differently-geared than the 901s used in the 914-6.

There was some variation, especially among the 6-cylinder cars.  I'm
not sure whether it was destination-specific, year-specific, or just
random chance, but some had "N" third gears instead of "O", and I
think some may have had "G" (or even "F") second gears instead of
"GA". 

Nearly all 4-to-6 cylinder conversions have "F" second gears, even if 
all the other gears have been changed, because the "F" gear is bonded 
to the 901 mainshaft.

If anyone's interested, my car's gearing is:

    1st:    AA- 12:34 (2.883)
    2nd:    F - 18:34 (1.889)
    3rd:    L - 22:30 (1.364)
    4th:    S - 25:26 (1.040)
    5th:    X - 28:24 (0.857)
