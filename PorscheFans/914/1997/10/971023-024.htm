	In  my professional capacity as sometimes electrical troubleshooter,
the last thing you changed is 50/50=90%  the culprit.  That is if you put
the old coil back in, then there is 50-50 chance that the new coil is bad.
If you don't put the old coil in, then there is 90% probability that  the
new coil is bad.  Either way you are worse off.  Of course just the fact
that you worked on the system could have  broken something.  If it ain't
broke.... :'}     Then there is the possibility that whatever fried your
old coil fried your new  one.  I'm never happy when I replace a fried
component.  You have to cover all the bases.
