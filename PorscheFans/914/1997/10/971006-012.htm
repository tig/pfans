In a message dated 10/6/97 10:50:42 AM Central Daylight Time, you write:

<< When replacing the push rod tube seals witht the engine in the car, is it 
 helpful / necessary to remove the exhaust system?   >>

Not necessary.  It will help to remove the heating plumbing from the heat
exchangers.  You will likely NOT be able to completely remove all pushrod
tubes, but you can get them out far enough to easily replace the seals and
then push them back in.  I recommend cleaning the underside of the engine
VERY well with gunk or some other engine cleaner, then make sure the grooves
in the push rod tubes are immaculately clean, and the seal surfaces in the
head and engine block are immaculate as well.  The late factory seals are
very good; don't know if viton will help much.
