At 12:36 AM -0000 10/15/97, andylv wrote:
>... how high does the bottom of the car need to be off
>the ground to get the engine out with the thottle body still in place?

	Put your jack-stands on their highest setting.  Then go out and
buy some stands that are about 2" taller, and put *them* on their highest
setting.  That might do it.
	Seriously, my own jackstands aren't tall enough.  I put them
under the round jacking reinforcements at the front corners of the engine
compartment.  I use a floor-jack with about a 1" thick wood "cradle" to
move the engine.  I usually wind up stripping off everything above the
manifold--air cleaner, throttle body, oil filler (and dipstick, right
Josh2?  ;^)  and all that.  Lots of rags/paper towels to keep crap from
getting into the motor.
	If you use cardboard or something really low, you may be able to
drop it with the throttle on using regular jack stands.
