I am going through the same process now. I will NOT use the pvc lines
(always felt a little uneasy with them anyway), or copper for that
matter. I have been tossing around the idea of steel (hard) lines or
ultimately stainless teflon lines. The braided stainless
is what I'm leaning on the most. I feel that do it once and be done with
it. Cost isn't that great of a factor. When purchasing the steel or
copper or whatever lines you choose, try using one of the area hydraulic
line shops in your area. They have the knowledge of dealing with fluid
lines, and can get the correct metric sizes if needed. I used a local
distributor for my cloth braided rubber lines for the engine compartment
and saved $$$$ over ordering through the catalogs.
