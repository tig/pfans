> JJ, and all,
> The OTHER thing to look at which hasn't been mentioned in quite awhile
> are
> the grounds for the FI at the back of the engine.  Your problem sounds
> like a
> classic ground problem; works fine when cold, and resistance increases
> as the
> engine gets hot, and injectors connected to the bad ground cease to
> work. .
>  There are at least 4 ground connections attached to one of the bolts
> on top
> of the engine attached to spade connectors.  Take off each one, clean
> it,
> squeeze the terminal together slightly, and put it back on.  Make sure
> there
> is zero corrosion between the wire and the spade connector; if there
> is,
> remove the connector, and crimp a new one on.
> 
> Grounds are the bane of a 914's existance.  If they aren't PERFECT,
> the car
> won't run right.
