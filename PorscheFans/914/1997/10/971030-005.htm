At 09:09 AM 10/30/97 -0700, JJ Slowik wrote:
>Does a Bosch Super Blue coil have a resistor?  Is it inside the coil
>somewhere?  Can I check the resistance of the coil between some of the
>contacts to get more information about what condition it's it?  What
>exactly does a resistor mean to the ignition process?

Accoring to my information, all bosch coils have an internal resistor. If
the primary resistance is 3-4 ohms, then you have a resistor in there. The
primary resistance is measured between the + and - connections on the coil.
The primary resistance of a regular high performance coil is about 2.5 ohms.
Also, if you have the coil hooked up backwards, the car will still run, but
you will get a weak spark. Check that also.
