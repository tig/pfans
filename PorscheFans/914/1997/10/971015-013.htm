Gary Wheeler writes:
> 8. Support engine with hydraulic jack.

	Remember that hydraulic jacks can sag.  You may want to use
something more solid.  My mechanic, BTW, wired up the ends of the
heat exchangers to the tranny mounting brackets to keep the rear
end of the engine up.  Evidently they can bear the stress, as long
as the transmission isn't attatched.

	Jim S. adds a bunch of good stuff, including:
>Basically, that's it. the starter upper bolt is one of the four that
>holds the tranny on.

	That particular nut and bolt comes off from the engine side,
not the tranny side.  You remove the nut from inside the engine com-
partment, and the bolt stays with the starter.  The recess the bolt-
head sits in has flats to hold the bolt steady.
