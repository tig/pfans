>Just got through that problem myself. And that was *after* having a brand
>new starter put in!
>
>Yes, all you should need is a starter relay to make sure you get enough
>voltage to the solenoid. Not enough voltage and the starter won't even kick
>in. Because the problem is often due to the aged wiring in the car, the
>problem is worse when the car is hot (heat reduces the conductivity of the
>wire running through the center tunnel).
>
>To verify, check the voltage on the yellow lead at the starter before and
>while the problem is occurring. To be thorough, you could check the
>electrical part of the ignition switch to make sure it is not corroded and
>adding too much resistance to the circuit. But even if that is the problem,
>the relay should take care of it.
>
>But you don't need the $30 relay kit. You can put together your own kit a
>lot cheaper. Or if you want to spend a few more bucks to have it assembled
>for you, Tweeks has a kit for only $15. Your choice.
