JJ,
With new MPS, trigger contacts, ignition wires and CHT sensor to spec,
my D-jet 2.0 would lean hunt without a 500 ohm resistor in the CHT lead.
In fact it was so bad without it I could not set the timing
correctly. Now, my local mechanic says the need for a resistor to
richen the mixture is not unusual - while another expert says
this means there is a vacuum leak somewhere. Well I've looked
long and hard for a vacuum leak and found none, the only place
I have yet to look is beneath the throttle body.

So, you might try installing a variable potentiometer in the CHT
lead to play with richening the mixture. However, if it's running lean
now, seems all plugs would indicate this (although all mine were light
tan before reinstalling the resistor).
Hope this helps.
