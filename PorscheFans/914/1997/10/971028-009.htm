Michael S Tarter wrote:
> 
> I had a couple real  bad boys when removing those bolts.  Even with the
> correct 12 point allen wrench type bit, pounded in place, some of these
> bolts are in so tight so rusted , etc, that they just don't come out even
> with a breaker bar - they strip out.  (Many unhappy noises heard from
> underneath car)   Two suggestions, in case the other ideas don't work,
> both of which will lead to having to replace the bolts (probably a good
> idea since they may be stripped/rounded out):
> 
> 1. Try gripping the outer round body of the bolt head with a good pair of
> vice-grips really crunched on, then whack the vice grips with a hammer.
> (This even works on engine sheet metal screws that are rusted in and
> rounded off at the screwdriver slot.  Replace the sheeet metal screws
> with small stainless bolts.)
> 2. The ultimate problem solver is take out yer high speed (10,000 rpm)
> right angle grinder (borrow or rent one) and grind off the whole head of
> the bolt.  Once you remove the CV joint, you can then remove the bolt
> stub left, with vice grips, etc.
> (You can get a grinder like this from some tool suppliers as cheap as
> $30; Harbor Freight 1-800-423-2567, item #31135-7AVB.  With a couple
> cutting wheels and grinding wheels you can do a lotta metalwork & repair.
>  Put on a wire wheel or cup and you can strip paint & bondo right down to
> clean steel.  Be careful, wear eye & ear protection.)
> 
> Kinda destructive, but effective, and bolts are cheap (any good VW joint
> will have em').  I think the 914-6s used 6 point Allen head bolts as
> stock instead.
>
