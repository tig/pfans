  As far as rebuilding a solenoid, it depends on what the problem is.  
The solenoid in the 914 does two things: 1, it drives the starter pinion 
into engagement with the ring gear on the flywheel, and 2, it closes a 
set of high-current contacts to switch on the starting motor.  If the 
solenoid is hanging up, it could be due to wear or lack of lubrication on 
the motor shaft or pivot points, or possibly in the solenoid armature 
itself.  If there is an electrical problem, it is probably due to wear and/or 
burning of the high-current switch contacts.  Anything but dried-out 
lubricant calls for replacement parts; the best solution is a rebuilt starter.
