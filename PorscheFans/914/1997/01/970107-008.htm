Just so *everyone* knows.

A '75-'76 914 will NOT pass "Smog-II" with a Bursch muffler.

If the Cat's missing, it no pass.

The technique is to lean it out enough to get the HC down, and
let the Cat take care of the CO.

And yes it would be "rude" to put a stock muffler on once every
two years.  And never, never think about pooling a few bucks
with your friends to get one to share.

> Until recently I also believed the way to pass was to lean until it 
> almost won't run.  It turns out that the lean mixture results in 
> unburned, or half-burned oxygen - CO.  You have to be near stoich for 
> ideal emissions.  Too lean, you get CO; too rich, you get unburned gas 
> - HC.  If you have both high CO and HC, then it is too lean and 
> misfiring.
> 
> You can richen the mixture most easily by putting a 500 ohm 
> potentiometer (rheostat, really) in series with the Cyl Head Temp 
> sensor.  You can go lean by reducing fuel pressure.  I would set it up 
> by ear, then pay a gas station for 20 minutes playing while their 
> machine reads out the numbers.  At too lean, the rpm hunts up and down.
> Get it just richer than that.
