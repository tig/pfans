I took full advantage of the spring weather we had over the weekend. I
think it got up to the 70's yesterday. When the weather is good, it's
painting time! I repainted the driver's side footwell floor (I did a
bad job the first time), the rear control arms near the hubs (under the
brakes and discs), and one rear caliper. I use rustoleum products because
they are easy to find, and work well if the surface is properly prepped.
(step 1 - wire brush, step 2 - sand paper, step 3 - wipe with mineral
spirits, step 4 - wipe with dry paper towel, step 5 - prime with rusty
metal primer, step 6 - top coat)

The rear calipers are really rusty. (the pads had rusted to the body and
the pins) I could use some new hardware (pins and cross spring). Anyone
have a source, or are these "pure unobtainium"?
