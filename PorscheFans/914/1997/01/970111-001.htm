>   Anything I should look for that makes a rear cover unusable?  I recall
>   looking at one briefly at a previous meet before I needed one. Was asking
>   $50,  I recall it had a rusty looking "idler shaft" (I think that's the
>   name.  Large tube shape sticking out from the cover plate in toward the
>   tranny).  I don't know much about trannys, got the idler shaft name from
>   Haynes 914 manual.  Would this being rusty make it worthless even if
>   cleaned up?  If deeply pitted?

The mating surface is the most delicate, and has to be in excellent shape 
to prevent leaking. The reverse idler shaft can be replaced with your 
old, good one. There's a roll pin that holds it in. Other problem areas 
are the exhaust bracket studs and the speedometer drive setscrew. They 
can be fixed by a machine shop, but it's better to get a good one, of 
course.
