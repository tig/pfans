In this price range, you will find one of two things, a rust bucket, or a steal.
I have tracked down 3 914-6s in this price range.  No.1 did not have serial
numbers or any build plates, but did have title.  Also was missing all lids a
fender and a door.  But it was all there, right.  Second, was a rust bucket,
which was supposed to be salvageable, yeah right.  This one had a tree growing
up where the cockpit once resided.  Get the car, needed to fall the tree, thus
destroying the car.  The third, was and still is a rust bucket, but then I knew
that going in, and don't mind the entry price.

You are correct, some can be impossible to find.  Brake parts, rear calipers,
and the hub.  Other than that the brakes are 911T parts.  Don't forget oil lines
and oil cooler, also washer bottle, and pump, and a few other small items.  Dash
section, and the other small idiosyncrasy parts.  The tranny, has a lower fifth,
to keep the engine cooler, and slightly better shift linkage, plus the throttle
linkage.

Don't get your hopes up, minimal rust, means something different to each person.
To some it means a spot about 1mm big, to others you don't find a car, just a
bunch of bumper stickers that resemble the shape of a 914.
