> Question about torques for wheels.  Didn't get an owners manual
> with 'new' car.  It has 911  alloys on wheel adapters.
> 
> What are the standard torques for steel wheels?
> What are standard torques for stock alloys?

    94-100 pounds or so for both.

> What torques would be used with the adapters, if different from 
> above. Adapter to hub? Alloy to adapter?

    Again, 100 pounds or so.

> Since I recall a discussion about dry/ wet torques awhile back, are
> these figures for torques based on antiseaze or something on the
> threads?

    Yes... Antiseize is required.

> With alloys, what are some do/don't  items I should know about?

    If you're using steel lug nuts, it's important to put antiseize
    on the FACE of the nut... You don't want to tighten a bare steel
    nut against an alloy wheel.

    Also, those adapters are really an accident waiting to happen...
    Around here, you can't even get them approved by the local PCA
    tech inspectors for use at an autocross.

    Have you thought about properly drilling your hubs for the
    five-spoke wheels?
