The 2.7 can, if properly re-built, can be a very potent and reliable
motor. The advantage of the 2.7 over the 3.0 is the weight savings. The
2.7 is significantly lighter.

> If I were ever to go the conversion route (with a 1.7 with a blown engine),
> I'd either go with anearlier 2.0 or 2.2 six and keep the 901 gearbox, or go
> power crazy with a 3.0 or 3.2 and a converted 915 (??) gearbox.

My choice would be a '73 1.7 or a '74+ 1.8 donor car for the better
widow winders, side impact beams, and movable passenger seat. For the
motor, I'd either go for an MFI 2.4S motor or go for the grunting torque
of a 3.0 or 3.2 engine. I'd prefer the 2.4S because of the MFI. Yeah,
it's a pain to tune, but the throttle response is simply amazing.
