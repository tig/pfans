Addco (the sway bar people) have a neat little book about this. 
Basically, they say the samething, ie, use relatively soft springs, good 
shocks, and big sway bars. They say that it's best to maintain suspension 
compliance in order to keep the tires on the road. I guess you might 
consider hem a biased source, however, (Eibach probably says to use stiff 
springs, mushy shocks and a wimpy sway bar, Koni probably says use a 
stiff shock, mushy springs and a wimpy sway bar - just kidding :) ).
