Brad, 

	I would recomend against forcing lubricant into the cable housing. The
throttle cable is intended to be self-lubricating. If you use any kind
of lube I would recomend graphite powder. Grease of any kind will trap
dirt and grime and will eventually ruin the cable. More likely, there is
something retarding the pedal or throttle-linkage travel. Check the
motion of the cable with both ends disconnected, then with one end
connected. Try and isolate the problem. I would sooner replace the
cable, they're cheap, rather than try and force grease into the jacket.
If there has been any welding work done recently in the tunnel then the
cable jacket may have melted. BTDT.
