I've seen everything from straight up over the rear axle line, all the way
around to hanging off the back, parallel with the trunk line.  Huge and small.

What I'm thinking of?  In reference to what I DO have (books mentioned
previously and a litlle more)  my supposition is that a full width wing,
multiple-element, large full fence, starting just above the end of the rear
trunk lid, LOOKS like the logical correctness.  Remember this Andy, paradigm
shift.  I understand the rules, but when I make the decision to bump up to a
more modified class, restrictions tend to go away, and the
wallet-filling-material becomes the restriction (not saying that mine is
limitless).  I'd also like to see what corner turning vanes mounted on the front
corners of the bumper area would do.

I'm obviously going to have to do some tuft testing with video camera and chase
car, to find actual effects on my car.  Especially in areas of low pressure
relief, when cutting relief slots in the WIDE fenders to stop the stall and move
the boundary layer along.
