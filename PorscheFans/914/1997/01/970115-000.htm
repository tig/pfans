Hi Barry et al,

Check your hose clamp connections, and look (or feel) for fuel coming from
the
injector body.  If you can feel fuel leaking from the plastic body, you
need a new injector.  

If the fuel drips from between the short hose and the injector, you can
replace the hose.  Factory connections are crimped, you just need a new
hunk of hose, and 2 small hose clamps.  

20 minute procedure:

1.  remove both injectors (10mm nuts + wire terminal) on that side
2.  remove injector from rail--cover with rag, fuel will spray out
3.  remove hose--cut factory crimp with side cutters, saw or whatever.
4.  replace with high pressure hose
5.  re-install injector on rail
6.  check for leaks--turn key on, listen for pump whirr, and
     repeat (2-3x total)
     - look for fuel drips
7.  replace hardened O-rings if required
7b. check spray pattern (optional, but it just takes a second)
     - have somebody crank the engine over several times, watch for 
       pulses of fuel spray.  pattern should be a cone-shaped spray, not
       a stream or spurt.  equal amounts of fuel should spray from both 
       injectors.  either use glass jars or a rag.
8.  re-install injectors in manifold
9.  pat yourself on the back.

cheers, B
