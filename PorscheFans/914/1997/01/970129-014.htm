Not easy to do. Even if you get all the stuff off on the back side, the
case is held on by the black bezel holding in the glass. I took my old
broken clock and used a sharp object to carefully pry the bezel away from
the case. When I got more than halfway around, the case finally came off.
Now I have a better appreciation of what special tools are needed to work
on instruments. I think that when I have the $70-80 available, I'll send
the clock to one of the places that repair VDO instruments to fix.
