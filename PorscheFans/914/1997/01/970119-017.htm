Craig Crease wrote:
> 
> 1. I am allowed "soft" plastic pushings as replacemnts in the class I run (BI),
> If I replace the problem side with the "soft"  bushings, will it be comparable
> to the other side or will I also need to do the otherside at the same time?  I
> know Weltmeister sells "street" plastics which I assume is the same as "soft",
> does anyone have experience with these? are there others out there that are
> superior?

The performance plastic bushings are stiffer than the "street" bushings.
But not like the street bushings are stiffer from stock. The difference
between the two plastic bushings isn't that much compared to the
original rubber bushings. The material is essentially the same, but the
compunds are slightly different. I went for the perfomance bushings.
They are noticeably stiffer than stock, but it didn't make the ride so
unpleasant. And it improved the handling noticably.

> 2. Last time the arms were off I had the heat exchangers, half shafts etc off
> also so it was not hard to do, this time I am trying to save a few steps but
> cannot see a way...... can I get away with leaving the heat exchangers on and
> just disconecting the half shafts from the transmission and take them along with
> the arms to the machine shop?

Leave the heat exchangers alone. Disconnect the half-shafts, the e-brake
cables, the brake lines and remove the arm. I took the arms w/shaft to
the machine shop and they had no problem at all.

> 3.  A week or so someone was talking about the grease used to install the
> plastic bushing (elephant snot!), what is the real name of this lovely stuff?

The stuff called plastic bushing grease, it's silicon based grease. But
the nick-name involved a somewhat different substance...
It's really a mess to work with and it's really sticky.
