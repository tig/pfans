Joe Davis wrote:
> 
> I somehow think I'm going to regret asking this but
> given certain criteria what are the good and bad points about
> a modern (say v8 either a Porsche or a new dohc Americian v8) vs a
> standard pushrod v8 vs a Porsche 6 I'm thinking 3 liter.

If it's a choice between a 3.0 six and a 350. The six wins hands down.
Here's why.

> Givens
> 1) this is a toy to go out an have fun with I'm not that concerned
>    with resale.

O.k. So resale maters not. That's not a factor.

> 2) ease of maintance is a good thing

Yes, chevy parts are cheaper. But the 3.0 is basically a bullet-proof
engine, and if it's cared for, it'll last quite a while.

> 3) I can drive a car at a reasonable rate but since I'm entering
>    middleage I don't need a car to scare the hell out of me.

Either engine will launch a lightweight 914 into warp speed pretty damn
quick.

> 4) I realize there will be a price difference to do each but Really how
>   much.  (this isn't a big concern)

The initial cost of conversion will probably cost a little more for 3.0
six than for a 350. But there are differing problems involved in each
conversion, and each have their expenses.

> 5) I don't want to destroy the handling of the car.

This is the BIG one here. The 350 weighs quite a bit more than than the
3.0 six, and will have a very noticable effect on the handling
capabilities of the car. In addition, the water cooling causes problems.
The radiator has to go somewhere in front if it's going to be usefull,
and as such you will need to remove a fair bit of sheet metal. This can
reduce the integrity of the front of the chassis. There are acceptable
ways to mount and plumb the cooling system, but it is not cheap and will
wind up shortening the lifetime of the chassis in the long run. Also the
massive torque of teh V8 will tend to twist the chassis pretty badly
unless it's reinforced somehow.

> 6) I've already made an number of upgrades (911 suspension brakes
>   strengthing the chassis ect. 5 bolt patterns and fenders flairs (that
>   I made myself with a English wheel a hammer and a weilder brag brag..I
>    am really proud of those flairs they were my first sheet metal
> project.)

Great! If the car is gonna go fast, it's gotta stop first.

> 7) I can rebuild an engine or can afford to have one built.

The 350s are cheaper, heck you can pick one up for cheap at almost any
FLAPS.

> 8) I have a fairly complete machineshop (Bridgeport Mill S/b lath mig tig
>    ect.)

Cool! Wish I had a setup like that. I'd never leave the garage.

> 9) I realize I'll probable have to get rid of the orginal trans ???
>    The car was a 75 so the sideshifter is nice.

Nope, keep the 901 gearbox. It'll handle up to about 250-300hp but you
do have to be carefull with first gear. Kennedy Engineering makes
adaptors for any engine to any transmission. Not a factor.

Good luck on your decision.
