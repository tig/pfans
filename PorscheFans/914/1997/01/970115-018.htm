Dion,
	A pal of mine races a 1.8 in E-prod. and uses no head gaskets.  He
has no problems at all with clearance or leaks.  If you do this, you must
be sure to follow the correct procedure, first, it is a good idea to "lap"
the cylinder and the head to make sure that you have a good seal.  This is
done the same way you lap in your valves, only different.  Use a good
quality lapping compound and when you finish, you should lightly spray the
mating surface in the head with an aluminizied paint.  Don't worry about
overspray...it will obviously burn off.  Finally, assemble like you
normally would.
	In theory, the reduction in volume caused by removal of the head
gasket will decrease the volume of the combustion chamber at TDC, but due
to the thickness of the gasket, (I can't recall the size, I just remember
it's kinda' thin) you might get some extra power (and HEAT).  I doubt this
increase will require you to switch to a higher octane, though.

Hope this helps,
