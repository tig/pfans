> 
> P-fans,
> 
> I talked to the mechanic re-doing my 2.0 liter heads today.  He's doing the
> usual: 3-way valve job, new guides, bead blast, etc.  I also want him to
> bump the compression a little, maybe something like taking it from the
> stock 7.6:1 to 8.5:1.  Nothing radical, but at least something.

Start with Euro Pistons. They will give you 8.0:1 by themselves. A full
point of CR from cutting the heads is a pretty healty cut.

> He says the easiest way to bump the CR will be a combination of shaving the
> heads and shaving 10 thousandths off the cylinders.  The way I measured the
> pistons and barrels gives me .0030 to work with.  He says taking .0010 off
> of that plus the usual spacers will give me the optimal burn spacing (?).

There is a BIG difference between .0010 and 10 thousandths. By about 10
times. 10 thou is alot to shave off of anything, that's .010". Be
carefull.

> He does a lot of racing motors and seems to know what he is doing, but I've
> never heard of shaving the cylinders down.  Is this an acceptable method of
> upping the CR?

The main issue is to make sure that there is enough room left for the
pistons to complete their full travel and that valves will not be around
when that happens. I have heard of 914 race motors running as high as
13:1 or more CR. Call around to other motor builders and see what they
do. Just keep in mind that the higher CR, the higher the engine temps
will get. No way to avoid it, just try and draw more of the heat away
with better air flow and more oil.

Also, the more you shave, the more you need to consider the pushrod
length. If you shave alot off, the stock pushrods will be that much too
long.
