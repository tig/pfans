I dont' think there should be much of any problem driving without them. 
They only do  coupleof things: first, the thermostat closes them to heat 
the engine up faster in the winter. This is important because it reduces 
cylinder wall wear (a cold cylinder wall wears fast). second, the 
passenger side flap directs a portion of the air flow down into the oil 
cooler. If you had an external oil cooler, and lived in a warm climate, 
you wouldn't need the flaps at all. 

You can't get the fan box off with out undoing the engine tin, and I 
wouldn't try getting the tin off with the engine in the car. Hey, it's 
only four bolts, right? :)
