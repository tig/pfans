What you will probably find is that your calipers will be sticking from
lack of use. You should verify that they are functioning by removing the
pads and attempting to push the pistons all the way back into the calipers.
If the pistons retract nice and smoth, you're in luck. If you do this one
caliper at a time and replace the pads and tromp on the pedal, you most
likely will not overflow the resevoir and spill brake fluid down into the
nether regions of your 914 (and you don't want to travel to those nether
regions, either. Trust me :)

Bleeding them is straight forward in my experience. Nothing special, except
in your case the master cylinder may have rusted internally in the areas
not covered by a seal. pumping the pedal during a bleeding operation may
damage the seals enough to cause them to fail, requiring a
rebuild/replacement. I like the Mighty Vac brqake bleeding kit. 23$ from
Walmart.
