It is common to have this happen on a car that has been sitting. 
Solution, pop the bads loose like you are replacing them.   You can take
the pins out and tap the pads only the pads use a hard wood stick and
then a hammer on the stick.  All you need to do is break the bond between
the pads and the rotor.

After this I would check out the caliper (rebuild it if needed) you can
damage seals this way.  Replace the pads and clean the rotors up.  3M
makes a rotor surfacing kit that mounts on a drill, makes the same
pattren as the pros machine does.
