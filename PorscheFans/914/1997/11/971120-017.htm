>AMP makes electronic connector terminals *big* time. . .they probably have
>a web-site, and are based in Harrisburgh Pennsylvania.  A trick I like to 
>use
>is schmoozle a local rep and ask for samples, say 5 terminals.  These will
>be delivered within a week (usually a couple days) and is almost faster than
>ordering from a parts house over the phone.  Oh, and they be FREE!
>I suppose this could be abused and then you would be SOL, so be forwarned.
>
>If memory serves, the ECU harness has wire terminations that are crimped,
>not soldered into each terminal, and they are secured in the connector 
>housing
>with small tangs formed by the terminal that engage in the housing.  I have 
>had
>these apart, and unless there has been damage of some sort, are *very* well 
>made.
>If anything, vibration and age would cause the terminal to circuit card edge 
>connection
>to degrade.  The circuit board (the system Brain) uses gold-plating in these 
>connection
>points, but it is possible for a buildup of grunge at the contact point.  In 
>this case, just
>remove the connector block and use an Ink type eraser (it has a small amount 
>of grit)
>and rub the connector fingers to buff the gold clean again.
>If you are replacing wires, I have had some luck with opening the terminal 
>crimp and
>removing/replacing the wire, but afterward, you have to solder the joint to 
>restore the
>connection. . .solder is kinda tricky in an environment such as an engine 
>compartment,
>but once the terminal has been disturbed, you cannot re-crimp the wire 
>because the
>metal fatigue will cause a failure later *Dont do this*.
>Before I go;  to remove a terminal from the ECU housing you have to use a 
>small strip
>of stout (spring) steel (properly sized -I dont have dim's) and insert it 
>into the connector
>housing to press the tang to disengage and gently persuade the terminal out 
>of the housing
>by pulling on the wire at the same time. . .This is not easy unless you have 
>the correct
>tool for the job.  Sorry I cant be more detailed, but sometimes the tip of 
>an X-acto knife
>blade can do the job.  Caveat:  your mileage may vary depending on your 
>dexterity.
