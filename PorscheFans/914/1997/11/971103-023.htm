>Carefull, this 3/16 glass is probably single layer_Tempered_ glass (common in
>European countries for windsheilds, also used in USA for side and rear
>windows ).  Tempered glass is designed with lots of internal stress, when the
>glass is struck, the entire windsheild will break into ~ 1/4 inch pieces. 
>
>A windsheild approved for use in USA (going back at least to the 50's) is
>made with _three layers_.  The outside and inside are regular glass with a
>layer of plastic in the center.  ( Look at a piece of american glass)  When
>this glass is struck it only cracks on the side that was struck.  The plastic
>layer prevents cracking on the opposite side and prevents medium size objects
>from entering the passanger area.  
>
>So. . . A car useing _tempered_ glass will shower the driver with small glass
>pieces and the object that hit the windsheild.
>
>A car useing _three layer glass_ will only have a crack.
