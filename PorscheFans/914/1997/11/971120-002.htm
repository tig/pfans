Re: Questions about PCA racing classes for turbo/super charged 914s

PCA Club racing has a class for everything, including the above. What is
done, is the displacement is multiplied by 1.6, and the resulting number
maps to a class, either in the stock classes (C-K), or the GTs. For
instance, if you had a J class 914 (say 1973 2.0 L), and "charged it, it
would move up into the 3.2 L range where 84 up Carreras run in F class;
I believe it would move into GT-3 in the GT classes.

The problem with this scheme is that in the stock classes what you can
do to the brakes and transmission is extremely limited -- think about
it.
