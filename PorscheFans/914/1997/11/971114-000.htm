Dion,
I must admit that my interest in the Beck 550 Spyder replica is based
primarily on the aesthetics and the ability to have a NEW one, not any
slavish (IMHO) desire for authenticity.  Who in their right mind would
actually run a Porsche 4-cam motor?  Sure, they sound great, but compared to
a well done Type 1, 356/912, Type 4 or small 911 engine, are the 4-cams
really worth what must be, after maintenance, running costs of over (real
wild guess!) $100/hour?

Myself, I wouldn't give up the advantages of reliable modern disc brakes just
to have authentic drums, or forego modern bushing materials, shocks,
electrics, etc.  I would not, however, go with very wide, square-shouldered
low-profile tires, as these would truly change the original balance of the
design.  Ditto for power assists on brakes and steering.  Transmissions --
modern 5- or six-speeds, synchromesh and closer &/or different gearing are a
thornier issue on the "feel/function" front, but something I would do.

I view this as consistent with the American hot-rodder spirit, with tactile,
kinaesthetic "beauty" added to the visual... separate but (IMHO) equally as
valid as the European "originality" aesthetic.  How many 914's -- which the
second vision would dismiss and not worth the restoration -- live on because
of the first vision?
