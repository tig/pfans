> To remove and replace the seat rails, first remove the seat, then undo the 4
> 10mm bolts.  Installation is the reverse of removal.  Put the rails back
> first, then slide the seat back on.  It's much easier to install the rails
> without having to jiggle the seat at the same time.
> Be sure you realign the seat rails when you reinstall them so they fully 
mesh without binding with the cog plate in the front center of the 
assembly.  This will prevent the seat from slipping and damaging the cog 
plate.
