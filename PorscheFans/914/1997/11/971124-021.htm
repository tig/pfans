ED CARLSON wrote:

> . . . how to measure backspacing . . .

Lay a straightedge across the inner side of the wheel rim.  Measure from that
line to the center where the wheel goes up against the brake rotor (where the
studs/bolts go through).  That's the backspace.

Offset, by the way, is different. It's measured from the center of the rim to
that same part of the center where the wheel goes up against the brake rotor.

Backspace + frontspace equals the true width of the rim (outside edge to inside
edge); it's typically an inch more than the stated rim width.  For example, a
5.5" rim (measured tire bead to tire bead) has about a half inch on each side
of the bead part of the rim, so its backspace + frontspace = 5.5 + .5 + .5 or
6.5".
