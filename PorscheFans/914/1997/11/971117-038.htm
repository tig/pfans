I vote for the CV joint. And i support Dave's theory of geometry-related
failure.

The only times I lost a CV joint were on the same damn hairpin at the
track. Once on cool down and the other on the warm-up lap (this is
worse).

In both cases I was cruising at a slow speed in a higher gear than usual
because of the slow speed. Also because of the slow speed I applied
throttle a LOT sooner before the apex than I would when racing.  Instant
big bang and no more forward traction. Both times, the CV near the wheel
on the inside of the corner failed. I invite others to verify this
experiment :-). My guess is the car was leaning too far and the CV
locked up when all that torque hit it.
