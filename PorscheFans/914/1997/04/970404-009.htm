> All of the suggestions for possible culprits make sense, but I
>can't see why, if the head temp sensor was bad, or if the cold start
>valve was leaking, re-connecting the fuel pump would cause it to die?

	The idea is that, if the connection to the head temp sensor
is bad (high resistance), the ECU will tell to injectors to stay open
longer to richen the mixture for the impossibly-cold engine.  But, if
the fuel pressure is low enough, the actual amount of fuel delivered
could be close to correct.  Raise the fuel pressure (in this case, to
its correct value), and more fuel goes in with the same injector pulse
length, flooding the engine.
	Ditto the cold start valve.  When the pressure goes to its
correct value, there is *way* too much fuel going into the engine, and
it stalls (floods).

	Mostly, we're guessing.
