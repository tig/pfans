J. J.,
By definition, compression ratio is the maximum cylinder volume divided
by minimum cylinder volume, or [(Vd + Vc)/Vc] where:
Vd= displaced or swept volume, and
Vc= clearance volume

Now, does the TED include deck height, dish, and combustion chamber
volume already?  I don't remember the 2.0L size (93mm X 71mm I think)
but by figuring piston displacement [(3.14 * (93/2)^2) * 71] I get 1929
cc engine displacement which leads me to believe 1971 cc may include
piston dish/deck height already.

Any comments?
