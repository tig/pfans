  I'd like to offer an explanation for the rusting cylinders noted. After
  the engine shuts down, things begin cooling off, and as the engine
  cools, a convective action is set up, whereby fresh cool air is drawn 
  in, heated and radiated away. The fresh air also brings along moisture
  which condenses on the cooling fins of the cyls. The cooling tin holds
  the moisture in, and since the cyls are raw steel with no paint to inhibit
  corossion, they rust....quickly.The heads, being aluminum, don't. Simple.
