> 'Burping' isn't the baseball bat procedure. It involves cutting a slash
> in the top of the fender well, approximately 50 cm long, beginning
> about the same distance behind the junction of the fender and the wing
> panel. The fender is then pushed out [by whatever appropriate means,
> including a porta-power and a wooden 'form'], and a slice of metal
> is butt welded in the ensuring gap.
> 
> This allows the fender to expand outward along the part that usually
> contrains monster tire introduction: the rear 1/3 of the fenderwell.
> It also allows the stock trim line to the tailight to be maintained.
> 
> Most cars that have been rolled with a baseball bat to gain as much
> clearance as you desire will crack the paint, with or without 'plastic';
> at least from those that I've seen. Of course, the selection and age
> of paint will determine your mileage.
