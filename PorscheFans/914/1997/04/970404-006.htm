First of all, the fuel pump is controlled via the relay board in the
engine
compartment and has it's own relay.  Second, the pump itself has an
internal
valve which keeps the fuel system pressurized for sometime after the
power
has been cut (to permit easy restarts after short stops, etc).  So, it
is
natural for your system to remain pressurized until a) enough time has 
gone by for it to bleed to 0 or b) you run the car with the fuel pump
relay disconnected and eat up all the fuel in the system.

Could be that good old cold start valve that's flooding your engine. 
The
cold start value is controlled by a thermo-time switch attached to the
top
of the crankcase between the heads.  If either goes bad, it could be 
possible for them to fail open.  It's one thing to check.  You can
bypass it since the valve itself may be stuck open.
