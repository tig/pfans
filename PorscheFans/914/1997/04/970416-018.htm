On Wed, 16 Apr 1997, Jeff Lorentzen wrote:
> Anyone using one of these  rev limiters with an electronic ignition like a
> Jacobs OmniPak or MSD? 

Shorting the coil HT lead to ground is a bone-head way to interrupt the 
spark, if you ask me. You're basically shorting the coil + to ground, 
which will heat up the coil and the ballast resistor. If you use an MSD 6 
or better, you can get a MSD rev limiter add-on module for not very much 
money. I'm sure some of the Jacobs products have a similar feature. The 
MSD rev limiter is a "soft-touch" type so that your engine doesn't 
experience the shock of complete spark cessation. This can be a big 
problem with high compression engines at high revs.
