JJ Slowik wrote:

> I've had a very small nail in one of my tires since I dropped my valve
> seat last fall.  Throughout the winter I re-inflated the tire every
> month, noticing that the leak resulted in a loss of about 10 psi per
> month.  I took the tire into a local shop and had an internal patch put
> in, and asked what kind of speed I could safely take the tire to now.  I
> was told that the tire wouldn't be safe over 65 mph.  I talked to a
> friend of mine, and he said that if an internal patch is used, than the
> tire retains its original speed rating ("V" in my case).  Since these
> two bits of information seem to contradict each other, I thought I'd ask
> the list.  I've had flat repaired in the past, and I've taken them well
> above 65 without any trouble.  Was I playing with fire?

Interrnally patching a radial tire is perfectly safe. For a street tire.
Personally, I have had no problem with patched tires, and have had many
done over the years. If the limit was indeed as low as 65mph and it was
fully expected that the car would be travelling _at_ that limit, I'd
toss the tire and get a new one. Now that is probably what the tire shop
wants you to do. And as for the 65 mph limit, they can't legal tell you
to exceed the speed limit, so they say 65 mph. 

Now, race tires? I don't think I'd trust my life to a patched tire on a
race track. 65 mph on a freeway is one thing, 100 mph on a track is
another story.
