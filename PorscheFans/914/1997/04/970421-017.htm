Actually, it is 25% difference between the 17mm and the 19mm.  We are
dealing with the area of a circle which is proportional to the square of the
radius. It ought to be (19/2)^2/(17/2)^2 which is 1.249.  That sounds like a
pretty big difference to me.

 At 11:46 AM 4/21/97 -0700, Joshua Hadler wrote:
>Dirk Wright wrote:
>
>> So, using the 911 master only makes sense to me if you upgrade the
>> calipers to ones that have larger pistons (as in the BMW 320i upgrade).
>> The larger pistons require more fluid to move the same distance, and the
>> pedal effort would go down because the calipers would require less
>> hydraulic pressure to achieve the same clamping force.
>
>No. Not neccessarilly. This starts coming down to driver preference. I
>like a very firm brake pedal, and the amount of travel I had with the
>17mm master was not satisfactory. Heel-toeing for me was too difficult
>if the brake pedal was below the accelerator. The 19mm does require more
>pressure than the 17mm, true enough. But it's no where near as bad as
>you make it out to be, and the pedal feels much firmer. The 17/19ths
>difference comes out to be about 11%. That's really not that much. In
>addition, the 19mm (IMHO) provides the driver with much better pedal
>feel than the 17mm. 
> 
>> So, since I've already purchased the 911 master cylinder, it lookslike
>> I'm committed to the BMW brake upgrade.
>
>Try the master by itself first. Bleed well and install good pads. You'll
>find it's probably it's acceptable if not quite nice.
>
>> I've learned that I shouldn't recommend the 911 master cylinder upgrade
>> unless larger calipers are being installed at the same time.
>
>If this were so, why do so many people upgrade the master and not the

>experience rather than supposition. This is not a flame, just a better

>world says is the right thing to do. Like 5-lug wheels in a six
>conversion. Havn't done one myself, but I've found it's pretty much
>agreed to be the way to go.
>
>I havn't heard any complaints about a 19mm master upgrade yet. So either
