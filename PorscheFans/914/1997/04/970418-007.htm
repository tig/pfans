The impression that I'm getting is that placement of the shims doesn't
affect deck height, but it does affect performance.  The mechanic that I
spoke with out here said that none of the VWs (or 911s, if I remember
correctly) used head gaskets, only the 914.  Bill at Mark Stephens said
that this was a mistake on Porsche's part, and they (Porsche) recalled
the head gasket.  If I am to believe these two people, then it would
seem that shims under the cylinder (providing a deck height of at least
..026) and no head gasket are the ideal way to run.  Several people on
the list, however, have said that running without a head gasket would be
a mistake.  I have a feeling that I'm going to end up running with no
head gasket due to a point that Mark Stephens made.  They claim to have
much better success with high compression engines and no head gaskets
than they do with low compression engines with head gaskets.  Now, maybe
Mark Stephens is doing something wrong, but I can't see them lying about
a Porsche factory recall.
  If Porsche is willing to admit that they made a design error, and a
professional engine builder is telling me that I should acknowledge that
error to receive better performance and reliability, then I really can't
argue too much.
