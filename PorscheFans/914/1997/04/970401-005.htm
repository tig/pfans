> I have the following questions regarding an oil cooler in a 914/6

 Hello, 
 I will try to answer from what I've read, seen and what my mechanic has
told me. Im putting mine in the front trunk.

> 
> 1. What size cooler to use for front mount?

 There are many different types, but one of the cheapest in the Mazda
RX-7 modified (Mazda Comp) when its modified it looks just like a 930
cooler. I have to buddies that build P-Race cars and they use these for
oil coolers and trans coolers.


> 2. Mounting before or behind the front trunk sheet metal ?

Behind sheet, inside the trunk. Mount it on the flat spots where the
suspension bolts are inside, across the front of the apron.


> 3. I have a GT shroud, how big a hole did these cars have in the trunk
> floor and where was it located ?

 Depends if you want to notch the bumper and valance ? I have seen a ton
of pictures and kinda guessed the size, since the original GT car where
race cars , Im sure the size varied. At this point I have only notched
the valence and pulled the plugs into the trunk, I will be cutting out
the holes in front of the notched valance. This way you get the air and
you dont have to cut the front bumper and its a little more stealth.


> 4. Is AN12 appropriate for oil line size ?

 Thats the size Im welding onto my coolers today. also the size the Race
buddies use.

> 5. Where did the oil lines run ? Under the car or in the rocker panel
> space ?

 Rocker panel space. the original GT car had the oil lines run into the
car down by your feet and out the back side of the peddles ? must have
made for hot feet ! I think they did this so not to rub the tires on the
oil lines ? But just run the oil lines high in the fender well and it
shouldnt be a problem

> 6. Anything else I should know ?

 I have heard A.J. Racing sells a cooler kit using a Mazda modified oil
cooler for $800.00 but I have not seen there catolog. I wanted one and
didnt want to pay my mechanic $300.00 to modify for me so I had my
racing buddie show me how its done. to date I have made 6 Mazda Comp oil
cooler ( 3 will be done today,but ones mine) If you buy one already made
make sure they have cut out the stock RX-7 thermostat and they cut off
th stock fittings and use #12's.

 BTW, I will have a Mazda Comp cooler to sell by tomorrow if you/anyone
interested. I cuold make more if needed, but it would take me 3 or 4
days. Another time monster!

 They are easy to make , but make sure whom ever makes it knows what
they are doing !!! 




 Just my take on the Cooler issue, Good luck,
