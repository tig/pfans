At 10:10 AM -0700 4/28/97, Daniel B. Freedman wrote:
>... you could use High Temp RTV
>to lock the tubes in and keep any leaks from happening, then you would
>not need the springs.

	Please, no!  Do not "glue" your pushrod tubes in.  The motor
expands when it gets warm, and the seals at the ends of the tubes are
supposed to slide inside their holes when this happens, as the tubes
themselves expand at a different rate than the rest of the motor.
	Install the spring correctly, and lubricate the o-rings when you
install them.  I believe that the manual calls for engine oil for this,
while some people evidently like to use a silicone lubricant.  If the
tubes don't get bent out of shape or out of round, and the seals seat
correctly, they should not leak for a good long time.  If you glue them
in place, eventually the seal will leak--and you may get chunks of solid
silicone floating  around in your oil system, which is not a happy thing.
