> Dear Mr. Confused,
>
> The points act as a short to ground for the Coil, and the Black wire if
> you follow it goes through the Dist Wall to the Coil (Black or Blue Can)
>
> When the points close they compleate the circuit allowing the coil to
> make the spark.
>
> BUT!!! the points sit on a sliding base plate so you don't get a solid
> ground, So what they did was to put a copper braided wire connecting the
> plate to the Dist Wall. That way the burst of power whould not spot weld
> the sliding part of the plate to the base.(The power burst is alot, thats
> why the points burn up).
>
> When you run without the "plate Ground" the two plates get little spot
> welds that cause the plate to stick, as they build up it gets to the
> point when the Vac. advance can not move the plate easly, and then the
> car acts just like you described.
>
> This is a real easy and informtive fix.
>
> 1. Get a new plate AA has them for about $25
> 2. Pop out the dist. (Don't be afraid)
>    Take it apart, clean it up, put it back togetter and use some Dist
> grease on the plate & the lower points
> 3. Put it back in, (It only fits one way)
> 4. Re-time the motor 28 degrees at 3000 rpm
> 5. Have a good time
>
> 1st time it should take you 1 - 2 hours, second time 30 mins
