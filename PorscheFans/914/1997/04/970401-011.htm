	I'd take the 2.0.  Not only do we have a 2.7 with some known problems,
but the 2.0 comes with some good stuff--the engine mount and headers are not
exactly small-ticket items.  (I think that's around $1K stuff new, prolly $500
+- used.)  And old, tired 2.7s tend to turn into nightmares.  No, not all of
them, but enough of them.  How soon did you want to do a complete overhaul on
your new engine?  (With case savers, Raceware studs, yadda yadda yadda...)
	Finally, the 2.0 won't necessarily require an external oil cooler.
The original 2.0 914-6 didn't have one, so we can assume at least a moderate
lifespan for this motor without one.  The 2.7s, from what I've heard, just
about require an external cooler in the 911, which gets more air than the
cramped space of the 914 engine compartment.
	Either way, though, sounds like a fun car!
