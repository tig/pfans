Chip Stratton wrote:
> 
> I have been incrementally 'improving' the suspension on my '74 914,
> and would like a bit 'o advice.
> 
> Currently the car has:
> 
> Bilstein shock/struts all the way around
> Weltmeister 19mm adjustable front anti-roll bar
> 140 lb springs rear
> Stock torsion bars

That's a decent setup for a daily driver/autox car.

> The car has been lowered on the stock torsion bars to match the 140lb
> springs, but now has a tendency to oversteer. I suspect that the
> proper approach would be stiffer torsion bars, but barring that for
> now, would it be reasonable to set the anti-roll bar to a stiffer
> setting to help control the oversteer. What handling quirks might be
> introduced by such an approach?

First off, where is the sway bar set at? Is it full soft? That would
explain your oversteer right there. Stiffen up the bar and if that
doesn't solve the oversteer, then start looking at other factors.
Second, what is your alignment set at? You may not have engough negative
camber in the rear. What kind of tires are you running on?
