>   It seems that many of the mail order companies sell the Bursch 914-6
>headers. Has anyone had any experience with these? How do they compare to the
>other brands out there? If I use these headers, is it possible to use
>SuperTraps, or will they stick out too far? Would it be better to use a
>stock,
>or Bursch, muffer anyways?

I'm not 100% certain, but I believe if you look at your 914-4 exhaust,
you'll get an idea of what the 914-6 exhaust length will be.  Then
you'll realize you don't have much space from the end of the header
to the rear of the car.  What you don't want to happen is to have the
muffler sticking out beyond the bumper so it'll get hit by a low air
dam if you are rear-ended....you won't like the damage to your
engine.

By the way....SuperTraps aren't very good mufflers.  They are loud
and not very efficient.

>   I've followed the recent thread concerning the bar vs. original mounting
>style for the engine, and believe I am going to use a "bar". How do the
>different brands compare (i.e. AA's "Quick Six", MotorMeister's, Patrick
>MotorSport's, GPR's)? They all appear to be pretty high quality, should I
>simply opt for the least expensive?

A friend of mine bought the GPR mount....it is well built and I doubt
it'll bend with your engine, but it's too tall.  It'll reduce your
ground
clearance.  At least it was this way a couple of years ago.

After seeing bent bars, I'd probably buy a cheap one and weld
reinforcing plates all over it....making it UGLY, but strong...  :-)
This rules out the PM bar (for me anyhow)....making mods to
chrome moly isn't for the average guy.

>   Has anyone had any experience with Kennedy Engineering Products (name?).
>They quoted me a price of $500 for a 9"in clutch and flywheel ring that will
>mate my 901 and a 3.0. Do the 3.0 and 3.2 use the same flywheel/clutch?

KEP has a good name in the kit car industry and they've been in
business for years.  I've only heard good things about them.

>   Is GPR (German Parts & Resto)'s price of $425 the best I can do for sheet
>metal? Should I wait until I can find the original tin, or is repro ok?

IMHO, it's just pieces of metal to stop the exhaust heat from frying
your engine so I'm sure repro is just fine.  However, DON'T BUY THE
FIBERGLASS STUFF!!!  Odds are you'll break it trying to install
your engine.

I've heard of one guy who took a set of old 911 sheet metal, and
a set of 914-4 sheet metal and cut and welded them together.  A
little ugly, but very functional and inexpensive.

Good luck!
