>I too am wondering about the proper cylhdtemp gauge.
>Hate to be a bonehead-- but how do you hook it up to the Head Temp Sender
>in #3?

	It's probably easiest not to.  Your CHT gauge is most likely a
voltmeter in disguise.  It would send a current to the temp sender and
measure the voltage drop across it.  Unfortunately, the ECU is already
doing just that--and having the gauge's signal going to the ECU will
tell the ECU the wrong info.  The mixture will be *way* off.  Ditto with
the reading in the gauge.  You just can't have to things measuring the
same thermistor.
	If you put another stock sender in the *other* head, you still
have the problem of trying to calibrate it.  I have no idea where you
can find the temp vs. resistance characteristics of the stock sender
(but if you find it, please forward a copy to me!), but I serously doubt
your gauge matches it.  Or rather, matches any of them, because there
are at least two different stock senders.

	Talk to VDO, and buy a spark-plug washer sender that matches
your gauge.  Far fewer headaches, and probably a lot cheaper, that way.
