Opinions:

	Dealer installed air deducts from the value.  I've seen the damage
	done by installation.

	Dual Webers is a bad thing.  In California, they would cost me
	almost a grand to get rid of.  Which I would have to do if I ever
	wanted to drive the car.

	It may not be the shift coupler.  I've driven (owned) 914's with
	almost NO linkage, and was still able to make them go.

	Checy valves in a 914 do NOT give me a warm and fuzzy feeling.

> The car was painted about 2 years ago and I can see some rust coming up in a
> spot or two and the battery tray area was repaired. The paint is 7 out of 10
> and the interior has a couple small tears and non-original carpets and is 7
> out of 10 condition also.
> 

More opinions:

	Carpet is cheap.  And easy to install.

	These cars always have a little rust.  A lot of rust is a bad
	thing.  Was the rust repaired properly?

> I've never owned a 2 liter, always 1.7s, so any advice you can give as what

> strictly be a third car in our house fun autocross and weekend driver.

Bottom line:

	This is a "fair" price, but not a great, (or even good) price.  There
	will be another.  The biggest test would be the "smile on the face"
	test, which you can only get with a test ride.

	It may be a good thing that you can't test drive it.  The first time
	I drove a 2.0, I was hooked, and bought it.  "It" was a POS.
