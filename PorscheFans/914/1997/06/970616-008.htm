Andy Lester  writes:

>Nothing against nifty gadgets, but there's an easier way to install rear =
>wheel bearing..... Put them in the freezer for a while (days)

Acutally it doesn't take days. This is a 'standard' method for installing
large bearings, like spindle bearings for machine tools. The rule of thumb
is to freeze the bearing for one hour for each .001" of shrink you need.
..015" is generally enough to get a slip fit for just about anything.

It works quite handily for bearings approaching two feet in diameter [BTDT].
Smaller are easier. If you're pressed for time, you can also throw
the trailing arms in the oven. You might want to remove the undercoat,
rubber bearings and brake fluid first, however.
