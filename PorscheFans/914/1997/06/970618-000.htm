Dirk,

That IS the custom there. I spent most of last summer in Egypt (the
Sinai peninsula) as a flight surgeon to support the MFO there. I was
able to make it to some of the tourist towns while there to pick up some
some jewelry and perfume on the local economy. Gold and perfume are
really cheap there. Haggling is the rule and a pass-time there. They
chat with you, bring out cokes while you banter over price. There's
always a sales clerk along with the shopkeeper and all get involved in
the transaction. Meanwhile they ask you where in the U.S. you are from,
what you do, etc. None of my purchases was made in less than a 1/2 hour.
It reminded me of day trips to Tijuana  when I lived in San Diego.
