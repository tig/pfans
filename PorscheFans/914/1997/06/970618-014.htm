>I saw a comment once, whether true or not I don't know, that the clamshells
>can rust from the inside out.  They were talking about the tube inside the
>clamshell for the heater and if the heater was used a lot moisture could
>condense inside the clamshell and start rust.  

That's the usual way, I think. The inner rockers have drainage holes at the
bottom the often times get clogged, resulting in the collection of dirt and
moisture lead ign to rust. 

>If I had the bucks to blow
>I'd get one of those couple hundred $ inspection probes like Eastwood
>catalog carries now. Make a small hole into the end of the clamshell and
>look inside.  Then use an undercoating wand to spray inside there.  Anyone
>ever done this?
>

Get the body cavity wax instead and make sure the drainage holes are clear.
Since you have a california car, most likely you're OK, so I would both
squirt cavity wax up into the clamshell from each drain hole, and also if
you remove the carpet on the inside you'll see a couple of black oval covers
that if you remove you have a little room to do some more squirting. You can
get the wax from Eastwood. Wurth also makes the stuff.
