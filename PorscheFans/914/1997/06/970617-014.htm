>There seems to be a little confusion here. First, the outer decortive part
>is called the "rocker panel" and the inner structural part is either
called >the "inner rocker" or the "clamshell." You can get the outer rocker
panels >in either steel or fiberglass. Fiberglas is fine for that part.
But, the >structural inner rocker is only steel and must remain only steel,
and yes it >is a really big job to repair it. If you can find a good
bodyman at 40$ per >hour, then you can figure at least 500-1,000$ per side.
The car has to be >put into a jig to hold it true while the old part is
removed and a new one >is welded in. The outer clamshell rocker can be had
for about 100$ each. But >that's just the part. The big part is making sure
the car is straight both >before and after. At this point, your car
probably is bent from the lack of >structural support. Only putting the car
on a frame jig will you really know.

Okay; I've asked this question in various guises before, but since someone
else brought it up this time, I'll feed the fire.....

I'm working with a '73 that's being converted to a 6.  I've inspected for
rust, and I've found some in the engine shelf, and lower outer rear
firewall (near the control cables); the firewall area was patched by a PO.

This, with vigorous rapping/testing with a screwdriver.  However, when I

reasonably stiff (it have heavy springs/shocks on it) while the car was

getting reinforcing rockers and a GT-type rear stiffening kit, but only if

competent MIG welder).

Is the amount of deflection I am seeing normal, or is reinforcing the

not willing to pay the labor rates I've been quote to do this.  Surely
there must be *someone* out there who has tried to do this at home?  I'd
like to hear what the results were, and any tips/suggestions!

as just fitting up the new plate and then welding into place, can it?
