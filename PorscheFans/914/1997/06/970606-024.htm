Joshua Hadler  wrote:

> the whole [911] front end is a bolt-on swap. It's one of the
> easiest ways to beef up the 914 suspension, and it's fairly stright
> forward too.

    Joshua:

    Yes, but it requires 911 front wheels.  Once you install THOSE,
    you'll probably want 911 wheels in the rear, too.  That, of
    course, requires 5-lug hubs.

    With 911 brakes, you'll probably want a 911 master cylinder and
    a Tilton proportioning valve, as well.

> > I've done a little reading on the engine swap from four to six
> > and it seems like no troble at all .
> 
> Woah! Be carefull what you say there. I don't think anyone who has
> done a 6 conversion would say that it's "no trouble at all". It's
> alot of work, and will require some special hardware in addition.
> Engine mount and oil tank for starters.

    ... And sheet metal, and a throttle linkage, and an exhaust, and
    oil presure/temperature gauges, etc.

    And...  Once you put the engine in the 914, you'll want stiffer
    rear springs to keep it off the ground.  Installing those springs
    will necessitate larger front torsion bars, and then you'll want
    to change out all the shocks to match your new springs.

    Next, you'll probably want to box the rear trailing arms and
    stiffen the chassis to keep the car from flexing and breaking in
    two.

> It's a [1967] 2.0L engine, and depending on the model it will have
> either 130, 160, or 210 hp. That is for the 911, 911S, and 911R
> models respectively.

    It's extraordinarily unlikely to have the 210-HP 911R engine;
    Porsche only built about two dozen of those cars.  If it's a
    911S, it WILL have 160 horsepower, but it'll also have a 9.8:1
    compression ratio... Which will require the use of high-octane
    gas.

> Good luck. It is a major project.

    You can say that again.

> But usually well worth it.

    True, although it's best to have an idea of its scope BEFORE you
    start, so you're not surprised halfway through when you run out
    of money/energy and still have a "project".
