At 7:51 PM -0800 3/11/97, Mike M. wrote:
>  I purchased Koni front & rear sport shocks

>tackling the fronts as far as what is involved,  I would appreciate it.

	Just did that two weekends ago.  It's easy.  There are some
good instructions on Tim's Fan Page, http://www.estinc.com/porsche
under the Technical References section.  Check 'em out.
	Having a buddy there for the extra hands is highly recommended.
(Also feet; you'll have to bleed the brakes when you're done.)
	A couple of additions to the procedure:  When you loosen and
then tighten the big nut that holds the insert into the strut, put the
top of the strut back into the car, have your buddy hold onto the brake
rotor or hub (or the wheel, if you need more leverage) while you tighten
or loosen the nut.
	Depending on the alignment of the front end (how much negative
camber it has), it may be a good idea when putting the strut and insert
in for the last time to 1) grease up the hole that the top of the strut
goes into, 2) grease the top of the strut just below the threaded portion
(lightly, for both), and 3) collapse the strut, jack the wheel up with
the strut collapsed, then let the strut expand up into the hole.  On my
car, the geometry of the front suspension at full-droop would not allow
the strut to go through the hole easily.
