JJ wrote:

>I spoke with Dave at MSHP again today, and tried to express my concerns
>and achieve a little more insight.  Here's the deal:  I ship them my
>heads, my intake valves, and the springs and retainers for all of the
>valves (intake and exhaust).  First, they inspect the heads for any
>damage and perform a (two-step?) chemical crack test.  Then, they call
>me to let me know exactly what they think should be done.  Depending
>upon what I authorize, they'll repair any cracks (~$12/crack,
>~$25/cracks @ valve seats), and pull the old valve seats and replace
>them.  They replace the valve guides with some "top-of-the-line"
>material (I can't remember what kind), and clean up my factory intake
>valves and re-install them.  Finally, they install new stainless steel
>one-piece exhaust valves (not the kind you'd get from the dealer, those
>are two-piece).  All of my springs and retainers are tested, and if any
>of my intake valves are damaged, they'll use new ones (no extra charge).
> Dave said that the stock intake valves can last forever on account of
>the cold intake charge, so they hardly ever have to put new ones in.  I
>expressed my concern about the heat transfer, and he said that the
>sodium filled valves rely upon transfer through the guide.  He said that
>in order for effective transfer to occur, metal-to-metal contact needs
>to be made.  Obviously, this is somewhat hindered in the guide since
>"actual" contact never takes place.  He explained that these special
>stainless steel one-piece valves, combined with the new seats, transfer
>heat just as well (if not better) than the stock setup.  He then queried
>me for a currently manufactured aluminum engine that uses sodium-filled
>valves (as opposed to stainless), and I was unable to provide one.  So
>
>
>my big question was, "If I believe you and use the stainless valves and
>one breaks, will it be covered under the warranty?"  He said that the
>warranty explicitly covers only the guides and seats, but MSHP
>appreciates the reputation it has received, and in the few instances
>where a valve has broken, they have taken care of any and all head
>repair, no questions asked.  He explained that even though a broken
>valve isn't their fault (they don't make them), they don't want to
>install shoddy products.  If an item routinely fails, they will stop
>using it.  All of this made enough sense to me, so I'm going to have my
>sodium filled valves replaced with the new stainless ones.  I'll keep
>everyone updated as to the outcome of this "experiment".


I to purchased a set of 2.0 L heads from Stephens. My engine has been put
back together but has not yet been installed in the car yet.  My worries
over exhaust valve temp were explained away as above.  My engine is a
little more than "stock" and will keep the group apprised of ANY problems
that are associated with these heads.
