Chris Kolstad wrote:

> O.K., so this is probably a really stupid question, but here goes.  I just
> finished passing smog in my '73 2.0, hopefully for the last time if SB42
> passes ;-).  I have no resitor installed right now and adjusted the CO2
> using the pressure sensor adjustment screw for the high rpm part and the ECU
> idle adjustment screw for, obviously, the idle part.  I was able to get the
> high rpm CO2 to read 2.5 % (I want to run a little rich) and the idle to
> 0.18% (full rich on ECU).  The idle is way too low.  It hunts like crazy and
> goes into a high rpm idle every so often.  So, the question is, will adding
> a resistor to the head temp sensor fix this?  And, if it does, does it just
> effect the idle mixture or also the high rpm mixture?  If it effects both, I
> guess I need to take the car back to the smog station and pay them a bribe
> to put it back on the machine so I can adjust both again.

Not a stupid question.  To properly adjust your mixture for idle 
the basic procedure is to get a variable resister (pot) from radio
shack in the 200-1000ohm range or so.   Solder some leads to it 
so you can place it in line with your head temp. sensor.

Play with the dial on the pot until you have the desired mixture
at idle (if you can measure CO yourself) or until the idle stops
hunting.  Remember the factory put a 270ohm resistor in-line with
the temp sensor for this vary reason.  

When you have the setting you like, read the value from the pot and
get a fixed resistor from radio shack as close to that value as
possible,
wire it up in-line with the sensor and you're done.

Need to pass smog again?  Take it out of the circuit...
