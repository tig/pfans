On Mon, 3 Mar 1997, Raymond Jang wrote:
> 
> I have a 19 mm master cylinder out of my 914, and an ATE rebuild kit
> for same. The brakes were good, but after letting the car sit for a
> few months, I've had loss of brake fluid.  So that on resumption of
> driving, I'd find a low pedal.  The brake reservoir would be drained.
> Topping it up, which was the path of least resistance, would enable
> me to pump up a hard pedal, and the car is drivable.  Obviously, not
> something to leave unattended.  Right now the car has a 17mm master
> cylinder from a parts car, and it too seems to going out.  Maybe the
> grommets from the reservoir supply lines are bad.

You've got to find out where that fluid is going before doing anything 
else. A resevoir of fluid should be easy to spot (then again maybe not 
:-)).  

Since you've decided to do the rebuild, a point to remember is that the 
seals go on the pistons in specific directions. You shouldn't hone the 
bore, but do check it for scratches. If it's scratched up, toss it and 
get a new one. Better to be safe than sorry.
