> Here is what I look for:
> 1) VIN r S/N starts with 914 - the rest of use got stuck with VW numbers

That of course is an obvious start. I was thinking of body work. But
certainly there are interior parts that can be transplanted. But a tag
can be removed from a dead 6 and riveted in place.

> 2) The gas tank never had an expansion tank mounted to it.

Hmm, that's a good one. That one I didn't know.

> 3) the washer bottle is different, only an outlet.  And they had an
> electric washer pump

Yup. Easy to transplant too.

> 4) Ignition switch is on the dash

The easiest to see. But a dash swap can do that too.

> 5) Hand throttel lever  by the shifter

Uh huh. Again, transplantable.

> 6) The tail shifter is slightly different on a "6" than a "4" the casting
> for he selector rod is not as long.  And on the other side is a bell -
> crank mounts for the throtel cable.

Ah! Bingo. That's a tough one.

> 7) the shifter mechinism bars are different.

Still swapable. But tougher I guess because of teh transaxle
differences.

> 8) the relay board is different in the engine compartment, and also
> mounted different.

ditto.

> 9) The orginal "6" engine lid has different sized torsion bars.

Ooh, good one. But swapable.

> 10) Real sixes use only a center engine mount. - I have an extra one, but
> will probally use a 911 style dual mount.

Swapable, but probably hard to find. Senseless to fabricate.
 
Great list. Thanks, I was hoping someone would set me straight on that
account. I should have been more observant the last time I had my head
in the engine compartment of a 914-6GT.

I guess my intent was to try and spot an attempted forgery rather than
just a well done conversion.
