Well, my motto is "if it ain't broke, don't fix it.". Unless you don't
trust the rebuild and want to redo it yourself. 

> There is oil leaking out of the hole at bottom of bellhousing, assume a
> poorly installed crank seal,

What does the oil smell like? It could also be transmission oil leaking
from the input shaft seal.

> The PO didn't know much about the rebuild other than it had a 'big bore'
> kit, non stock cam, weber 44's (I'll be putting back the FI courtesy of
> Ralph).

Unless you plan on splitting the case and installing a stock (or FI
designed) cam, forget the FI. The D-jet FI can't handle agressive cams.
Too bad, but 'dems the breaks with a 30 year old FI design.

> I've seen honing the cylinders mentioned, with the type 1 engines I know
> people sometimes do this but it is usually not considered worth it,
> effective etc.  I assume this is different with the type 4 engines?? Most
> of my aircooled experience to date has been with the type 1.

Not too much. The cost of new pistons and jugs aren't terribly high, and
there really aren't overbore kits available. There used to be big-bore
kits, but I don't know if they're available anymore.
