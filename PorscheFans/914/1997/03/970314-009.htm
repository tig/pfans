          Allen,

          Here is some brief background as to my 914. It is a 1972
          2.0. I have used it as a daily driver ever since I bought it
          in 1992. The inner rockers have been repaired so there is no
          rust there. The car could use paint but in general is not
          bad. There are some rust bubbles in the left front fender
          panel down low near where the hoses to the fuel tank go. So
          thats it for rust as far as I know. The fire wall seems to
          have no rust. I have had the panel out of the interior and
          the sound deadening material out of the engine bay.

          The 2.0 engine was rebuilt last year (it developed rod knock
          at 144,000 miles) by me. I had to do it because I needed the
          car.

          Next came money saving. I then bought 911 SC struts rotors
          calipers for the front. I then bought 914-6 stub  axles and
          rotors for the back. I plan on using standard 15X6 wheels
          under stock fenders.

          Next step as I see it is the stiffening kits from someone?
          Any suggestions? 180lb rear springs with konis and hieght
          ajusting collars. At this point I think I would also paint
          the car and decided on flares. Any thoughts on flaring. Yes
          no Fiber Glass or steel and so on.

          Finally the engine there are really three routes  as I see
          it. One and most expensive but appealing to me. Build a 2.4s
          motor from a 2.0 motor. This would allow me to use carbs
          since 914-6 with this engine came with carbs. I like the way
          carbs sound and look I know FI is better. Next choice is a
          2.4S motor with FI and potential expensive FI repairs. A
          peaky racey engine that I like because it is lighter than a
          3.0 and makes more power. Next is a 3.0 with FI. I like
          this engine because it is reliable and makes good power and
          torque. This car will remain a daily driver (everything else
          bores me) so the reliable nature of the 3.0 will probably
          win me over.

          Anyway I would appreciate any guidance from someone who has
          done this before.

          Nick
