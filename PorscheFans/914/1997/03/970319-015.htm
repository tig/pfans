1/4" of front toe in, the car will strongly resist turning.
 
> Good point about the rear toe....I'd rather the shop screw up on

Toe out on the rear should be for a car that in trully intended for
autox, or for a car that understeers so badly that other suspension
changes aren't sufficient. I would not feel safe driving my car on the
freeway if I had toe out in the rear. BTDT. If it were say a dodge neon,
that's another story. But we're talking about mid engined rear drive
cars right now.
