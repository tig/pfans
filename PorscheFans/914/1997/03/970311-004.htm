How did you determine this?  Did you drive the car with a vac gauge?
I used one gauge at different times, but noted the conditions, and 
found that the two are never operating simulataneously.  The retard 
port may be drilled at an angle to cancel venturi effects. 
Here's my estimate:

1) at idle:            retard=max, advance=0  = retarded
2) hi rpm/light load:  retard=0, max advance  = max advance
3) hi rpm/cruise load: retard=0, part advance = part advance
4) at WOT:               retard=0, advance=0  = no advance/retard     

The amount of vacuum advance is a function of both rpm and load.  
It could be plotted on a graph.
