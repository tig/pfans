Don  wrote:

> Now I bummed, I really like the way crossed drilled vented rotors
> look behind a Fuchs wheel and the fact that I paid $400 for them.
> They will crack why ? from the heat ?

    Yeah, Don... Even if they're stress-relieved to remove the
    stress-risers from the drilling process, and even if the holes
    are chamfered, the metal right next to each hole cools faster
    than the metal further away.  The uneven heating/cooling makes
    them crack.

> They are from Troutman and that hole next to the edge was
> eleminated for that reason,

    Troutman does good work -- I just recommended them in private
    e-mail to another list-member who was looking for slotted rotors
    -- but even the best shops can't overcome the basic laws of
    physics.

    In fact, even the wildly-expensive Porsche racing rotors that
    had the holes CAST in (rather than drilled) crack... It's
    unavoidable.

> So what will happen when it breaks ? oh, dont tell me, I probibly
> dont really want to know.

    Ok, I won't tell you... But if you carefully inspect each rotor
    periodically, you MAY be able to see cracks just starting to
    form and replace the rotors before they do any serious damage.
