TMW Inductin states that a single 48 mm throttle body will flow:

460 cfm at 30 in H20
450 cfm at 27.5 in H20
425 cfm at 25 in H20
400 cfm at 22 in H20
380 cfm at 20 in H20
365 cfm at 18 in H20
340 cfm at 15 in H20
300 cfm at 12 in H20
275 cfm at 10 in H20
195 cfm at 5 in H20
125 cfm at 2.5 in H20

All numbers are approx from TWM graph.

Sorry I dont have my chem. book handy to get the conversion from in Hg to
in H20.  Let me know if you cannot find the conversion and I will figure
it out for you.

Plot this data out in an excel spreadsheet and get a curve.  Use this
curve to determine the flow at 1.5 in Hg.  If I was at work I would be
able to get the mathmatic formula which describes this curve but I hope
this information helps.
