One tip I have picked up on concerning "tappets" on type IV motors is to
use the "elephant foot" adjusters used on the 911 engine. These
adjusters have a pivoting foot with, I believe, a completely flat
surface which touches the valve stem. Sounds like a plan to me, though I
think I remember that they can't be used on 2 liters because thouse
engines use a different style adjuster. As for valve adjustment issues
being discussed, I am not sure whether I'm following the procedure in
question fully. I have been told when using the fifth gear-block one
wheel method that you should rotate the wheel until the cylinder
opposite the one you want to adjust is just closing on one valve and
opening on the other. Take with a grain of salt, y'all. I hear plenty of
advice but not many reasons.
