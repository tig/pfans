First of all, the idle mixture pot on the ECU works only when the idle
contacts on the throttle position switch are closed.  It should have a
large effect on idle mixture - none when throttle off of idle.

Adjusting fuel pressure is an easy way to affect full range mixture. 
You need a good gauge.  Many I have are off as much as 5 lb at 30.

Lean hunt can be expected to cause fluctuating CO and HC readings, as
the mix goes lean and rpm goes down, at which point the ECU calls for
extra fuel, and on into an unstable cycle.  Don't forget that partially
clogged injectors will cause lean hunt, as will every other item in the
chain.  

A manifold pressue sensor that does not leak when sucked, and is of the
correct part number, should not need to be adjusted on a stock engine. 
Yes, I have done it for my hiperf 2.1.
