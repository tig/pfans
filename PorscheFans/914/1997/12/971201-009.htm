Hi Mike.  Usually this is caused be the brake pads sticking to the rotor
or the brake caliper's piston sticking inside the cylinder or the
e-brake sticking on that side and not releasing.  If you can't get it to
break free by rocking it and you've looked underneath to make sure the
e-brake is really releasing that side, you'll need to jack up that side
to remove the wheel.  Then just remove the brake pads.  You'll probably
have to use a screwdriver to pry the pads out (after removing the
retaining hardware of course) or use a big pair of pliers to grip the
ears of the pads.  Then you should be able to see what's what.

Good luck.
