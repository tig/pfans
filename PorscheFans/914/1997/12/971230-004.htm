>An Idea I have been toying with (only sort of seriously) is swapping the
>throttle body from a 3 liter Mercedes (or something else with a D-jet and
>slightly larger displacement) over to a 2 liter 914.  Is this simply not
>worth thinking about?  Why do it?  Presumably better flow.  Also, it
>would be nice to find a throttle body that has a throttle stop screw
>since the lack of one on the 914 is a cause for rough operation (at least
>it is on mine).  The throttle switch should be compatible.  The MPS
>should be able to compensate for the extra flow... maybe.  Need to alter
>the airbox (??) and the filter housing would have to change.  So, has
>anyone done this or heard of this?

No way dude, this won't work. Well, the motor will *function*, but it'll be
much less than optimum. One reason is that the larger throttle body will
alter the manifold vacuum at part throttle from the oem setup. Also, the
throttle switch may not be the same, altering the enrichment function. The
oem throttle switch may not attach to the larger throttle body. The OEM
Djet setup is calibrated for the stock displacment, vacuum, ignition
timing, and cam timing. Altering any of these (and maybe other things as
well) will change the mixture from the original. While the calibration can
be changed, the only way to do it is to use the "black box" approach, which
is hokey if you ask me.
