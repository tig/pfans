This doesn't sound like a bad deal if the motor has been updated. It
should have Carrera pressure fed tensioner update, and needs the updated
valve guide material. The 2.7 motor is somewhat notorious among P-6's
mainly due to the original configuration in 911's where the engine was
an early emission design and ran very hot, used poor material for the
original valve guides which was usually shot by 50 or 60 K mile and had
the notorious "thermal reactor" exhaust . If the top end was ever
rebuilt, the guides and tensioners have probably been done, but it pays
to ask. Conversion to 914-6 type carb setup and exhaust should satisfy
the other concerns, Good
luck.                                            Jim S.
