Oh yeah, another thing that's good about mechanical injection is that you
can use your stock pump and fuel lines from the 4 cylinder. The mechanical
injection has 2 pumps, one is electrical near the tank, the other is driven
by the crankshaft. There's a timing belt for it. The nice thing about
mechnical injection is that the fuel pressure at the injector is very high
(100's of psi), for the best possible atomization. Before delving into
setting on up, I would find every friggin' book on the subject I could get
my hands on. 

Leave CIS for the VW and other low priced, low performance crowd. (yes, I
know, CIS can be *made* to make better power and have better throttle
response, but it still a kludge in my opinion).
