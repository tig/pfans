Hi Rich:

The five-lug conversion seems like something Porsche should have done
themselves from the beginning.  My opinion is that the 911 parts swap is the
best solution since 911 disks and calipers are stronger than stock 914.
There are too many compromises with drilling or using adaptors -- and the
only thing you are upgrading is the wheels.

There are 2 things stopping me from making the "proper" converion:
(1) price;
(2) It would move me out of CSP.  (yea, yea Dion.  I know you'll write back
"Welcome to AP!")

>Since I've been 914ing (and I'm sure a lot longer) the options for front 5 lug
>conversions were: Drill existing hubs and insert studs, Install adapters or
>Use a 911 strut setup.  Each process has its strengths and weaknesses.  Hub
>drilling is OK, but when the front rotors wear thin they have to be replaced
>and done again.  Spacers don't work well on stock bodied cars and the quality
>of some of these pieces are not up to most of our standards.  Complexity for
>us do-it-yourselfers and high cost keep many from doing the 911 stuts.
>
>Question 1 - Do you think there interrest for a reasonably priced 5 lug
>conversion kit for the existing front 914-4 strut?
>
>Question 2 - What drawbacks are there to the idea?
>
>Question 3 - Would used parts (brake pads and bearings excluded) in good
>condition be a problem?
>
>Technical Details: Kit would have 911 vented rotors, aluminum hubs, large
>caliper (54 mm piston set and almost turbo size brake pads).  Other than a
>dust seal collar (good for a couple hundred years or so) none of the parts
>would be custom which means servicability would be easy.
