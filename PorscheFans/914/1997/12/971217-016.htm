          My thoughts on this would be to choose a engine designed by
          porsche or use a combination of porsche parts that work
          together to improve HP. Instead of hot rodding your 1.7 you
          are probably better off buy a used 2.0 ~$500 and having it
          rebuilt to factory specs with a good muffler and euro
          pistons figure 100hp. If you decise to go the six route
          which I had always planned on doing I would say rebuild the
          1.7 as cheap as possible and enjoy the car. Now spend the
          next 2-3 years buying parts and planning decide what engine
          induction system an budget time and money for project then
          thriple the time and double the money and you'll be in the
          ball park. 

          I went this route when I rebuilt my 2.0l. I wanted to do a
          six, but did not want to stop driving the car, so I rebuilt
          the 4 stock with euro pistons and FAT FI cam. Now I drive
          the car and do a major upgrade each winter it seems.
          Rustoration, breaks wheels & suspension etc. I did did rust
          first then suspension & brakes. I was going to do stiffening
          kit next but I lept at an engine oil tank mounting bar when
          they were available as a package. so the stiffening kit may
          have to wait another year while I play with the engine.

          Anyway I guess what I am saying is the 914 is a bit of a zen
          garden. You change one thing you have to change a whole lot
          of other stuff brakes frame etc. Change the part of the
          engine have to change other stuff to compensate more cooling
          better rods etc. So plan to address the whole system not
          just one aspect or you will end up with a car you don't like
          to drive much. Too much power not enough breaks or a frame
          that twists when you step on the gas.

          WOW sorry for the essay

          Nick
