>>jug and fill it with warm water and add a half box

>>and engine compartment with this solution.

>Please do not do this. The reaction of an acid and a base is water and SALT!
>Nope, just flush with regular water, lots of it.

	It is actually OK to use baking soda and water--but
you will need to flush the area with clean water afterwards.
Lots of it.  (Maybe less than you'd use on just acid, maybe
not.)
	Of course, you can always save that middle step (the
baking soda solution) by doing as Dirk suggests--using lots
and lots of clean water.
	But the baking soda solution *will* tell you when the
acid is gone--by when it stops foaming up.

	There are good and bad points to each process.  On
your car, you decide which you want to do.
