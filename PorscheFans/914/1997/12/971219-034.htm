	One thing (of many?) I forgot, in my irritation with what I
percieved as Dirk's incorrect information.  When you replace the
tensioners, make sure to replace all the chain ramps at the same
time.  You need all the ramps there, with no pieces missing, or
bad things can happen to the chain.
	There are several different ramps needed, some of them are
unique, while most share a common design.  As always, get the latest
revision of the part that will fit in your motor.  The later parts
will hold up better than the earlier ones.
	Buy Bruce Anderson's book.  Read it.  Most of these sorts
of questions will be answered in it.  It is pretty close to being
the Bible as far as 911 motors are concerned.
