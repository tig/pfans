At 8:12 PM -0000 12/16/97, JJ Slowik wrote:
>... ("hiccup" at constant RPM) ...  Can someone
>explain how a bad tach would cause this?

	You said it.  The tach is connected to the coil.  The way
that the coil sends out a spark (in a stock car) is by the contact
breaker points closing, which grounds the one "side" of the coil.
The tach hooks up to the same part of the coil, so that it can use
those 12V/ground/12V/ground pulses to determine engine RPM.
	A flakey ground somewhere along the path can cause "extra"
sparks, or mess up the formation of a spark.

>...what's the easiest way to remove the tach from the equation...?

	To remove the tach from that whole circuit, unplug the con-
nector from the coil.  It is a black-with-purple-stripe wire, that
usually looks just plain black.  It plugs into the same terminals on
the coil as the green wire from the points (stock ignition), or the
black wire from the Allison/Crane gizmo (on yours and mine).
