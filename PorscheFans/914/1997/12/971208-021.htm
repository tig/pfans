> Greetings and Salutations.

>         I'm looking to re-tire the Fuchs on my '73, and was
> trying to find a fairly grippy street tire that's not going to
> fry in 10K miles of reasonable (i.e. not sliding around) drving.
> I was thinking of getting the Yokohama AVS Intermediates (since
> they seem to be decent when autocrossed, as well), but would
> appreciate the wisdom from people who know better ('bout
> everyone, I suppose).  Or is it just better to get a tire
> for high mileage and good wear, and swap to my Yok-032's for
> auto-Xing (and get my butt kicked anyway in Bi)?

If you don't plan on driving in inclement weather, then either the Yok
A509 or it's pricyer sibling the AVS-I would be good choices. The A509
is, IMHO, the best bang-for-the-buck tire on the market. I ran the AVS-I
as a street tire and really liked 'em. Had to swap 'em out last month
for some Gislaveds to get me to and from autoxes in the winter though.

The Bridgestone RE-71 is also a great tire, and has better wet weather
performance than the A509.
