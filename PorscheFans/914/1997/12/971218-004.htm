>>If you use the factory-style centermount, you can use any shift
linkage that fits the tranny.<<

The 914-6 shift linkage is different because it goes between the heat
exchangers and longer because of course it is a tail shifting trans. If
you want to install a side shift trans and use the side shift linkage it
will hit the engine and heat exchangers, you need to custom make the
rear shift bar. Mine was made by Perry Kiehl and it works wonderful!( I
do believe the "Perry Bar" is still available from Mr. Kiehl
>a914man@aol.com<). You will also need to tap the intermediate plate for
the
accelerator pivot.

>>As far as I know, the tranny mounts are identical.<<
The body end of the trans mounting area is the same piece and in the
same position in 4 and 6 but the trans mounts themselves are different.
the 4 cylinder have open areas around the rubber while the 6 cylinder
mounts are solid rubber.
It is best to replace with 911 sport mounts.
