Now that you've gotten a few of the "shoe polish" responses, here's my
$.02

When shoe polish fades to the leather's natural brown color, many people
will re-apply black leather dye before applying the polish.  If you're
going to experiment with shoe polish, you might as well try leather dye
too.  All easily available from a leather goods store like Tandy, etc.

The last time I did a 914 top, Garold, I cleaned it with de-waxer &
preparatory solvent cleaner, then re-sprayed it with a Ditzler product
called HP-440 Black.  It is a semi-gloss black, formulated for black-out
hood treatments like on the old Dodge & Plymouth muscle cars of the '60s.
 Looks like the original new paint color and sheen, when done.  Lay down
several coats and it lasts a long time.  Many folks have sanded down the
original texture nubs and tried everything from flat black, to high gloss
black to color matching the body color.
