	Very true, especially if you can lift both sides of the car and
block the rear wheel on one side.  I lift one side of the car at a time,
and let the ground block the other wheel.  In fact, I also remove the
wheel on the side I am working on, to give me better access to the tops
of the valves.
	I marked the flywheel while the engine was out of the car.  I
do not think it is necessary to have it out, but that was when I did it.
Now that the painting is done, there is no extra effort on my part.
	Since I have a stock motor, I figure that I'll follow the pro-
cdure laid out in the manuals.  If I see what I consider a compelling
reason to change, I will--but I'm used to this particular method, and I
have (just a little) more confidence in it.  I know that the only reason
I have to be at all confident is "because the book says so", which is
sometimes not a very good reason.

	As I said, I'm just trying to provide info so people can make
an informed decision.  If it's your own car, you make the choices and
deal with both the good ones and the bad ones.
