        Over the past few days, I've seen some very sketchy 'shoot from the 
     hip' advice posted to inquiring minds on some very basic repair 
     procedures.  I'm not interested in starting any flames, so I'm not 
     naming names or specific emails.  Any Haynes or Chiltons manuals will 
     cover the topics easily, and the best advice (if you haven't done the 
     procedure yourself) should be to guide the inquirer to that source.  
     All too often, it is evident that the person offering "advice" has no 
     experience with the topic at hand.  Often, I see someone describe a 
     process that is dead wrong, and would result in creating more work to 
     fix the 'goof'.
     
        Naturally, it is "buyer beware" of (in this case) advice.  This 
     list can be an excellent resource if we all take the time to craft our 
     replies with care and quality.
