At 7:36 PM -0500 12/19/97, Dave Darling wrote:

>	There are solid "tensioners" available for racing applications, but
>they are usually not recommended even in those cases.  The 911 motor, like
>so many others, expands when it heats up.  The mostly aluminum motor expands
>more than the steel chain.  The tensioners take up this slack when the oil
>and the motor are cool, and then give it back when the motor and the oil
>are warm.  This action needs to happen, or you have lots of chain slap when
>the engine is cold (which leads to failure of SOMETHING eventually) or you
>have the chain too tight when warm, which leads to Bad News in a BIG hurry.

I was referring to these solid tensioners when I said "mechanical". I'm
fully aware that the original style ones fail. But, what I neglected to
consider was the point you raised about the engine expanding and
contracting, thus changing the tension on the cam chains. I guess then that
the carrera tensioners are the only real choice. Sorry for the confusion.
