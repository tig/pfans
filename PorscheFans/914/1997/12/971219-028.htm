Since I've owned. worked on & maintained all three intake/fuel systems
you guys are talking about, here's what happens...

On cold start, raw fuel is dumped into the intake manifolds. This fuel
sometimes exploded in the intake, and the back-pressure  from the
explosion (opposite of a "backfire") would blow apart the airbox - about
a $350 problem. So many after-market suppliers supplied a spring-mounted
valve (drilled into the airbox) which would open in case this happened -
saving the airbox. It's about a $20 part..
