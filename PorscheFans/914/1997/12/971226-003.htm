The first time I adjusted my own valve clearances I had alot of noise. 
Since I was relatively new to the 914 mysteries at that time and I was 
doing alot of business and getting a lot of free advice from George Hussey 
at Auto Atlanta, I asked him what I might be doing wrong, and that I was 
following the Haynes manual. George said that the Haynes manual for the 
valves clearance settings is WRONG.  He said that they both should be .006 
for stock 1.7 and 1.8 engines.  I believe that the .006 and .008 is OK for 
the 2.0, but don't quote me on that.  My car doesn't have a stock CAM (and 
several other things) and the last time they were adjusted they were at 
..004 and .006.  By the way, AA may have high prices, but their advice 
(good so far) is free if you're willing to pay for the call.

From: JJ Slowik 
To: thomkuby@ridgecrest.ca.us, 914@porschefans.com
Subject: RE: Brakes and Valves

If you bring the car up to about 5000 rpm under load, and then let it 
coast
back down (leaving it in the same gear the whole time), do you hear the
backfiring?  Did you at any time remove your exhaust to do the valve
adjustment or any other work?  If so, I'd suspect a small exhaust leak
between the heat exchangers and the heads.  This can sometimes be verified
by inspecting the tops of the pushrod tubes for exhaust residue.  It's
really critical that the heat exchangers get seated in the heads correctly,
and always use new copper gaskets.  As far as the brakes go, have you 
tried
"seating" them?  I had an Rx7 that squealed a lot when I put on new pads,
but several very aggressive stops from about 50 mph put a stop to that.
Basically, you've got to get the surface of the pad to match the surface 
of
the rotor, and there's only one way to do it... use.

From: Gary Helbig

Subject: Re: 4 lug wide wheel question

Ben,

What you need to know is the wheel OFFSET.  Offset is the distance
between
the mounting plane and the center of the wheel.  It's fairly easy to
calculate from measurements on a bare wheel, and not impossible with
a tire mounted.

You want the offset to match, independant of rim width.  (The correct
"backspace"
will change according to rim width.)  Changing the offset will change
the suspension geometry.

Measurements on my car indicate that a 6.5 or 7" width rim will fit with
the stock offset.

Gary.

Subject: Re: Brakes and Valves

The traffic is low today for some reason, so I'll jump in despite my
limited experience.

I strongly suspect the pad compound. And the short answer is you may
have to learn to live with some noise. I tried an early (orange) version
of the carbon pads on my 914 a few years ago and loved them at the
track, but very quickly decided to swap them out for the street. Try all
the tricks first, like champfering the leading edge, cleaning the
caliper surface that the pads slide on and lightly greasing the metal
backing plate where they touch the caliper, etc. but you will probably
have to experiment with other pads until you find the performance/noise
compination that works for you.  Have you tried the Hawk HPS pads? They
were pretty quiet on my C2, but still noiser than stock.
