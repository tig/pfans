What do you want to do with the RX-7? ("Uh oh" says everyone, "Here goes
josh on his rotary kick again...."). If it's for C-Stock in SCCA autox,
then don't even think about the GSL. If however, you really like the
idea of the new spec RX-7 class for SCCA raod racing, then get the GSL.
The GSL-SE has the 13B motor, and while it's considerably more powerful
than the 12A in the GSL (Hence it's high demend for C-Stock in Solo2),
it's not allowed in the spec RX-7 class. This also helps keeps the costs
down for the budding road racer.

But first off, decide what you want to do with the car. Then when you've
picked a club and a class to compete in, READ THE RULES. If you buy a
car with "all the autox goodies" it may have one too many goody and bump
the car up into the trailer-riding-firebreathing-monster classes.
