I'm entirely with you on the complexity/reliability factor here.  I've
run dual Webers (and still do) on my 73 VW bus for nearly 7 years and
ran them on the 914 for 3 years (90,000 miles combined) with 0
breakdowns due to carburetion and 0 dollars for carb repairs (other than
experimenting with different jets/venturis and an occasional gasket).  I
have yet to accumulate enough miles with my recently installed FI to
make any statements about reliability, but the complexity is
substantially higher.

The only problem I ever had/have with the carbs is leaks between the
manifolds and heads.  I have to remove/reseat at least one of the ones
on the bus about once a year and I never did seem to get the ones on the
914 completely sealed for any length of time.  I typically check the
balance on them twice a year and very rarely have to adjust them (I
STRONGLY recommend the Weber brand 'X'-bar linkage for the setup).
