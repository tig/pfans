Jim,

You mention that the fuel pressure is 29 psi.  Is it actually 29 psi
when the engine starts to die?  It still sounds like a fuel delivery
related problem.  Will the symptoms occur if the car is just idling?  It
could be the fuel pump dying.  My VW Jetta used to do something like
this when its pump was worn out (it wouldn't necessarily happen when
idling, but would under load).  I didn't investigate to see if it was
actually the pump or the pressure regulator as I got a used set for too
good a price to pass up and installed it.  Problem went away.  If you
have a fuel pressure guage try hooking it up and checking it out with
the car running and watch it when the symptoms start.  If you have done
all this please ignore the above and carry on with someone elses
(probably) more relevant suggestions.
