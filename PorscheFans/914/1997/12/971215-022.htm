>I can't help you there. I assume the stock cooler is adequate for street

	That may not be a safe assumption.  The stock cooler is adequate
for street use in a 911, which has a larger oil tank which is better ex-
posed to outside air (which means the tank also cools the oil).  Not to
mention a straighter path for the cooling air into the engine fan.
	The stock cooler should be adequate for street use in a 914-6
which uses the stock 2.0 L 110 HP motor.  No guarantees at all for a
larger, more powerful motor, or one that sees high RPMs for long periods.
	Consensus seems to be that many or most 2.7s in 911s require
external coolers even for street use.  A high-po 2.4 will be putting out
more power than the stock 2.7, so it might be good insurance to get the
external cooler mounted *somewhere*.
