At 02:57 PM 3/3/98 EST, Jon Lowe wrote:

>Rick,
>You need to disassemble the tranny.  The 914/6 uses different gear ratios than
>the 4, particularly 5th.  The longer 5th from a 4 with a six cylinder engine
>can cause the engine to run hot; I know, I tried it!  Move the shift forks,
>etc. from the 4 cyl box to the 6 cyl box.
>As far as drive shafts, you can use either.  only difference is the number of
>splines in the CV.  Balls and everything else are the same size.  I confirmed
>this with Bruce Anderson last year.
>

I think you'd be better off converting the '70 tail shifter to a side
shifter using the parts from the '73. You need at least the following from
the side shifter:

rear housing (with speedo drive)
shift rails 
side mounted selector

As far as I can tell, everything else is the same between the two
transmissions. You need to keep the intermediate plate that goes with the
ring and pinion, as well as the tunnel housing. You'll need a gasket kit, of
course.
