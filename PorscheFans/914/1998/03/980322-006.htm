	I'm back, after a week off.  I wandered around New Orleans for
a while, and had a great time.  I spotted one 914 there--parked in the
driveway of a house near where I was staying.  A black car with a
chrome rear bumper--appeared to be a 70 or 71, but hard to tell from
that vantage.

	Jeff, in his search to keep his MPS properly hooked up, asked
about a tool for removing the pins from the electrical connector.  I
talked to the good folks over at Global Metrics, and bought a tool from
them.  It looks like a small piece of U-channel, with each end ground
down to a smaller tab.  The tab is about 3/8" long.  The two tabs are
of different widths, presumably for use on different-sized pins.
	By the way, you *can* flood a 914.  The wire to my thermo-time
switch broke, and grounded itself.  The car would not quite start when
warm.  Lots of fuel smell from the tailpipe, lots of cranking, but no
starting.  I pulled the FI Trigger wires, ran the car for 10-15 seconds
(I am not kidding!), let it die, then it would start fine after re-plug-
ging the trigger points back in.
	For "Kaminiki", check the article on the 914 fuel pump control
system on Tim's 914 Fan Page in the Tech Refs section.
  It will tell you which electrical sig-
nals are used to keep the fuel pump running.  You may not need a new
relay board at all.
