OLNEY G  wrote:

> i have recently considered turning my 74 2 litre 4 cylinder into
> something like a 2.2 or a 2.4 6 cylinder.  What has to be done to
> the car for a process like this?  I know the gear ratios need to be
> changed.  Im specifically looking for the draw backs of such a
> project.

    The big drawback is that it costs a lot of money... Expect to
    pay $10K+ to do a decent conversion.

> ive heard the big 6s are pull a hard number on the standard 4
> cylinder transmissions.

    A 2.2 or 2.4-liter engine isn't a "big six", and it won't hurt
    the 901 transmission.  In fact, the 901 transmission can handle
    up to around 300 HP, so long as you don't do drag-style standing
    starts.

> Another curious question along the same topic is smog.  I wouldnt do
> something like this if i have to worry about california smog laws. 
> i know 73s and previous are smog exempt, but what about 74s?  What
> is the deal on how that works?

    The '73 and earlier cars are exempt from smog inspections.  The
    74's will be exempt starting in the year 2004.
