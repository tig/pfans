     These sound related.  If you don't have any fuel
pressure, then the car won't run very well.  Is this
what happens:  The car starts when cranking, idles and
then dies; fuel pressure when checked is zero.  If so,
I'd guess you ahve a problem with the pump control cir-
cuitry or the pump.  There's an article in the Tech Refs
section of Tim's Fan Page about the pump control system.
     If that isn't what's happening...  Hmm...  Time
to start checking the components.  MPS is a biggie; if
the diaphragm is broken, the car runs *very* poorly or
not at all.
     If pumping the throttle helps, then you may not
be getting any fuel from the "regular" injection pulses.
You can keep a 914 running for surprisingly long with the
trigger points disconnected by pumping the throttle
quickly (the enrichment pulses are enough to keep it
going, sort of).
     That's the idea dump for now.  Good luck!
