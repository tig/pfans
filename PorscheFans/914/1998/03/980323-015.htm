> 2.  Piston Dish Volume:
	"  Almost nothing, 1cc? pistons are on the rods so it's just a
guess.  "

  I have Kolbenschmidt Euro pistons in my engine; their dish volume
was measured at 4 cc's.   If you have Kolbenschmidts also, then yours
are probably close to that value, but the only way to be sure is to
measure.  I have included calculations for your guess of 1 cc versus
my measured 4 cc's, and you will be astounded at the difference...
for the 54 cc chamber volumes, changing the piston dish from 1 to 4
cc's makes a .3 difference in compression ratio; while on the 49 cc
side, the same change in assumptions results in a .5 change... this
is more than the difference between Euro and USA configuration
engines!
  I don't see any help for it... you are going to have to stand the
engine on end and measure that dish volume to better than 1 cc
accuracy, or you're still guessing...
