  It depends on what year car you have... there are a few '74 year
cars, like mine, that have '73 instruments (two turn signal arrows)
but '74 wiring (one indicator) so that they blink together.  The
earlier cars had seperate arows for left and right, and the later
'74's and later cars had a single round indicator in the tach at
about 10:00 o'clock, as I recall from my brief stint behind the wheel
of Dave Darling's car.
  It sounds as though you have a '73 or earlier car, which should
have independent signaling.   The usual checks apply:  check all four
corners of the car to be sure that all the external lights are
working correctly; most turn signal systems produce all sorts of
screwy results with one or more lamps out.   Be sure to check that
all of the light fixtures have good grounds, and that all the bulbs
are seated correctly, with no corrosion in the sockets.
