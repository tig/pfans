>Now, the 1.7 and 1.8 liter cars are on a separate line from the 2.0 liter
>cars. This distinction makes it much more expensive to autocross a 914

Ultimately there are some subtle differences. And those differences could
amount to maybe 100# weight savings. Thinking about the lack of side impact
door beams, different window winder mechanisms, and a non-movable passenger
seat could probably save a moderate amount of weight.

>914 owners are not allowed near as many modifications as other cars
>because the heating system is part of the engine cooling system and the
>exhaust system. So, no headers, no cooling tin mods, no electric fans are
>allowed in a 914 Street Prepared car. Link that with the limit of
>separating the 1.7 & 1.8 cars onto a different line effectively makes the
>914 uncompetitive in SCCA Solo II competition in CSP.

Yup, but it's not exactly -no- headers. It's just that the heater hardware
(any of it!) cannot be modified to accomodate tuned headers or anything
else for that matter.

Consolodation of the 914 could still allow a DSP move as an X1/9
consolodation (along with it) actually yields quite a noticable benefit for
the italian imitation . The 1300cc X1/9 has a 4.43 final drive and the
1500cc X1/9 has a 4.11 final drive (I think), and this could help even out
the balance a little. But the 914s have noticeably more torque and are
capable of running larger diameter wheels and tires that the X1/9. So,
while _I_ personally feel that the smaller 914s should be moved to DSP, I'm
hesitant to think that the SPAC will. But we should all keep pushing it
anyway.

>I think the best thing to do would be to put the 1.7 & 1.8 cars into DSP.
>But your input is needed by the SPAC to help them along whatever path you
>would like to see followed.

It's not unheard of, to split the cars between classes, but the
displacement of the 1.8 is a pretty big edge over the 1.5L X1/9 (with or
without 4.43 final drive). I will still be pushing for a DSP move, but
untill that day, I'll be doing my darndest to make the most kick-ass 914
possible under the current SP rules. But in CSP, it's a tough crowd.
