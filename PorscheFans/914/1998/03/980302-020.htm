	Speaking of the rev-limiting rotors, I think I've found an
interesting side-effect of the way they work.  On an on-ramp on the
way back to work from lunch, I noticed that the rev-limiter seemed
to kick in earlier than it did when I was on the freeway.  (I ran
it up in third when I got on the freeway to see.)  The difference was
only a couple hundred RPM, I think.
	My speculation is that the lateral G's from my turn (on-
ramps of course curve) pulled on the weight in the rev-limiter enough
to get it to make contact with the ground once it got pretty close.
	Or maybe my particular rotor is just inconsistent about when
it grounds the spark...
