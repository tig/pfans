Saw you got some good answers from Josh so I'll just add my .02

I got the initial numbers from a guy on the VW bus list.  I'm going to be
using a Cyberdyne gauge, about $30 from Jeg's catalog.  It's a simple LED
bargraph.  I'll put my Fluke DVM across it when I first get it wired up
since I want to see what the actual numbers are to compare with the LED's.
I'm using a generic Bosch O2, #13950 3-wire sensor.  It was $69 at Kragen's
Auto, heard they can be found for around $60 but I was there so got it.

For the bung (which I brazed into the 180deg bend in my Bursch, after the
4-2-1 merge) I got one of those HELP packs that had 2 anti-fouler spark
plug adapters.  Suppose to be used to help a plug in an oily cylinder.  Has
threads to go into the head then the interior of the adapter has threads
for the spark plug.  Suppose to shield the plug in the cylinder.  The
interior threads are 18mm and match the O2 sensor.  $4.99 for 2 adapters.
Just cut off the bottom threads and shape the end to match the curve of the
exhaust pipe.  I'm going to put the other bung on my VW Thing ( has carbs,
that suck ;-) to check the jetting.
