>   I'm still trying to find out the suggested compression
>reading range for a 2.0l stock? engine.

	Compression (as read on a gauge) has lots of variables.
You might see 150# or more, at sea level with a stock motor.
But a cam with more overlap would tend to lower the pressure, as
would being at a higher altitude.  Other things also play a role.
If you're down below 100#, it's not a good sign.
	Cold vs. warm also makes a real difference, and most
numbers that get thrown around are for warm engines.
