>For the last few years, I have been having trouble with my seat belt grabbing
> when trying to extend it making it almost unusable.  I finally got around to
>fixing it.  I donít know if anyone else has this problem, but I thought Iíd
>tell you how I fixed it.
>
>The retractor mechanism consists of a spring to real in the seatbelt when
>released, a centrifugal latch that catches when the belt is jerked on, and an
>acceleration sensor that latches the seat belt when acceleration/deceleration
>is sensed.  In my case, everything worked fine except the acceleration
>sensor.
>
>The acceleration sensor consists of a pendulum that forces a pawl into the
>real  teeth when it is displaced and any direction (fore/aft or
>side-to-side).  Wear of the bearing surfaces for the belt real over time
>allows the real (with the ratchet teeth attached) to lower into the pawl
>casing the pawl to interfere with rotation of the seat belt real.  The
>pendulum and pawl are mounted on a bracket held in place by two screws.  The
>fix for the problem consists of loosening two screws and lower the assembly
>just enough to allow the pawl to clear the ratchet teeth.   The above
>description may not be real clear but if you open up the mechanism, I think
>it will help.
>
>To fix the problem, first remove the back upholstered panel to get at the
>retractors.  You may be able to make the necessary adjustments without
>removing the retractor from the car but it may be easier with it out.  I
>suggest taking it out so that you can check to make sure that
>acceleration/deceleration is properly detected.
>
>Anyway, with the retractor out, remove the WIDER endcap.  The narrow endcap
>contains the retractor spring.  If you try to remove this one, you have a big
>problem trying to get the spring rewound and stuffed back in the endcap.  I
>know, Iíve done it!
>
>Loosen the two screws holding the pendulum and pawl bracket and move it down
>just enough to clear the ratchet teeth.  With the mechanism held level (as it
>is installed in the car), test the new position by slowly pulling the
>seatbelt out while checking for interference.  When it is working to your
>satisfaction, tilt the assembly  fore and aft to make sure that the when the
>pendulum swings (due to gravity), it causes the pawl to engage with the
>ratchet teeth.
>
>This simple fix make driving my 914 much more enjoyable.  If  you are having
>retractor problems, I hope it helps you.  Remember that the seat belt system
>is a safety device that probably should only be serviced by a qualified
>technician.  The fix described seems to work but there are no guarantees --
>youíre on your own.
