   I don't know about other brands, but my Kolbenschmidts were
advertised as "Euro"  pistons, have a pronounced dish (4cc), and with
stock 60cc combustion chambers, calculated out to be 7.9-something to
one, so I presume that it is possible to have 8 to 1 (Euro) pistons
with a dish... although it may also depend on whose pistons you are
talking about.   I have a set of Mahle's that came out of the engine,
and these also have a pronounced dish, but I believe that they are
stock 7.6 to 1 (USA)  pistons.   The only way that I know of to tell
them apart is to measure their deck height without shims and dish
volume, add in standard head gasket thickness, assume 60cc combustion
chambers, and run the numbers.   If the results come out to be 7.6 to
1, then they are USA pistons.   If 8 to 1, then Euro. 
   Someone out there must have a way of interpreting the codes
stamped in the bottom of the pistons, which would be a much easier
way of doing it; maybe they will read this and post.
