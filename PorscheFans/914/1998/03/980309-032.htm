I've heard another gray area trick (depending upon class (as in whether one has
any or not)) is only for those with sideshift motor mount bars.  The inner
(rubber) mounts are replaced with fabricated metal ones (!) and the outer
(metal) mounts are replaced with early-style rubber ones (and the mount bar has
to be cut to reestablish correct hanging height).  The idea is to reduce engine
twist under acceleration by lengthening the lever that attaches it to the
chassis.  Anybody on the list admit to having done this?
