 an original '70 914/6.  I am wanting to use the '73's side shift tranny.  I
 have notice there is no mounting boss on the intermediate plate for the
 throtle linkage pivot arm. (As on the '70 tail shifter)  Are there any
 alternatives to throtle cable/linkage routing?  I didn't want to
 dissasemble the tranny.  Also, if I am to use a '73 914/4 tranny with 914/6
 wheel hubs, do I use 914/6 drive shafts or 914/4 drive shafts?  What is the
 difference between the two?  Any ideas are appreciated. >>

Rick,
You need to disassemble the tranny.  The 914/6 uses different gear ratios than
the 4, particularly 5th.  The longer 5th from a 4 with a six cylinder engine
can cause the engine to run hot; I know, I tried it!  Move the shift forks,
etc. from the 4 cyl box to the 6 cyl box.
As far as drive shafts, you can use either.  only difference is the number of
splines in the CV.  Balls and everything else are the same size.  I confirmed
this with Bruce Anderson last year.
