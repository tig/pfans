Question: What exactly is the mechanism of a knock sensor, and what
>physically is it detecting. In other words, what exactly is making the
>sound/shock/vibration that the KS is picking up? 

It's just a vibration sensor, tuned to detect vibrations with a sharp spike,
which would be charatistic of a knock. MSD sells one that fits in a drain
hole. What's making the sound is the hammer-like blow to the piston face
when detonation occurs. 

>	On another note, the mixture obviously goes _way_ lean when the
>throttle is fully closed above idle. 

If you have your over-run bypass valve hooked up, it opens under these
conditions, thus drasticly reducing manifold vacuum, thus causing the MPS to
lean the mixture. It is an emissions control device. I think it is designed
to alter the vacuum so much that the fuel shuts off. I also seem to remember
from reading the patents that the ecu infers an engine speed from the f.i.
trigger points (or something).
