	I've said it before; I'll say it again:
	Always always always check the ignition system first.  All
FI and carb diagnostics (all of em!) start out with "First, make
sure that the ignition system is in good shape."
	Make sure your timing (and dwell, if you've still got points)
are OK.  Check the condition of the spark coming out of the coil.  Is
it big, fat, and white?  If it's small, orange, and pathetic-looking,
you have an ignition problem.  It may be the only problem, or it may
be contributing to a larger problem.
	Also check the spark out at the plugs.  You should still see
a decent spark there.
	If all that checks out OK, then start troubleshooting the FI.
