>O.K., I'll bite. Hey Dirk, what's the faraday cage effect?

Powder coating uses a high voltage (low current) between the powder and the
object to be coated. This electrostatic charge insures that the powder is
attracted to the metal object and adheres well. But, the downside is that
the electrostatic field around things like inside corners makes the powder
be diverted to one of the side walls instead of going into the corner. This
would also be true of the deep fins on the cylinders. The electrostatic
field would cause the powder to collect on the ends of the fins and not down
deep between them. Hope this helps.
