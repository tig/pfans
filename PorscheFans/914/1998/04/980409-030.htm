	PCA Club Racing rules require a *kill* switch, not just a
battery cut-off switch.  The switch has to kill the motor when it
is running at 2500 RPM.  We have checked this every Club Race in
the N. California area.
	The most popular place to install it on a 914 is one side
or the other of the side engine covers.  The horizontal parts that
are next to the engine lid.  Most cut a hole in the mesh for the
switch.
	The red-white-blue "Zap" decal is required, as well--to
show the emergency worker just where the cut-off switch is.
