Some people will say you can yank on the shaft after removing the 4 CV bolts
(8mm triple square). The roll pins can be tight, and the gasket tends to
glue the parts together. I prefer to remove the stub axle. You'll need a
1/2" drive 30mm socket, a breaker bar, a 4 foot cheater pipe, and a big
hammer (3# is good). I'm working on getting a puller to use instead of
hammering the stub axle. Does anyone know of a correct puller for this? Also
renew the cotter pin when you get it back together. You must make sure to
re-use the roll pins when you re-assemble, sometimes they will stick in
opposite halves of the joint. Rolling the car without the bearings will not
destroy the bearing per se, but the seals will be shot, effectively ruining
the bearing. If you need to move the car with the axles out, temporarily
install a spare stub axle.
