Some answers to the list: Mike Cooley asked about painting chrome door
handles. Mike, I did mine 10 years ago and they have held up great. I
wetsanded the chrome with 400 grit sandpaper. Then I primed with a metal
etching primer, and painted with SEM (brand name) Trim Black. It is satin
black, not gloss or flat. These have not chipped flaked or scratched. The only
part you can not paint is the round stainless water shield where the key slot
is. You could also glass bead the chrome to give it some tooth to hold the
paint. The Eastwood Company has a self etch primer and SEM Trim Black in their
catalog in aerosol cans. You can try them, will probably cost about $15. Good
luck.
There was also a question on syncronizing carbs. When I HAD carbs, I used the
4 tube mercury sticks from my motorcycles to balance the carbs. This tool
works very well.You can watch all 4 barrels at one time as you adjust. I had
installed vacum ports on my manifolds under each throttle plate.
