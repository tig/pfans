I've used the stuff to install a rear window; it works well, and isn't
THAT hard to use.  It's a rope-like stuff made by 3M.

You will want everything to be warm when you start.  A nice heated
garage is a good start.

You will need to get both the frame, and the glass very clean.  Then
an accelerant that is used on both surfaces: frame and glass.

Uncoil the rope into the frame, leaving the paper on where the glass
will go.  Press the rope into place with a small roller; a wallpaper
"seam roller" works nicely.

Remove the paper, and press the glass into place.  Gently, or you
will crack the glass!

The roller and the warm room are the only real "tricks".  I got some
of the accelerant from a local glass shop; they filled a small jar
filled for free.....
