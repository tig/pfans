Hello all,



I just wanted to share my experience in with dropping a tranny last weekend.   I
don't know if this is standard procedure, but as per instructions from a friend,
I was able to drop the tranny without dropping the engine, or even removing the exhaust.
 



What I did was…

· Remove clutch and speedo cables.

· Remove wires to Starter

· Remove ground strap.

· Remove shift linkage to tranny

· I used some big zip ties to suspend the exhaust.  While I didn't have to remove
the exhaust, I did have to remove the bracket that attaches to the tranny, and supports
the exhaust.  This was so the tranny could be worked back off the engine.

· The drive axles required a little pounding with and rotating, but they came off
rather easily.



After that, it just a matter of working the tranny off the engine with a jack bit
by bit, and it came off nicely.



I checked out the chutch plate, and I could still see some "imprint" on the face
(Sachs), so I figured it's pretty new (later the PO confirmed that he just replaced
it).



The reason I'm doing all this is because I blew out first gear (on my driveway no
less!).  So I got a used tranny from a friend.



Question:  Are there any items I should check out for wear (pilot bearing?, throw
out bearing?) before putting the tranny back on?
