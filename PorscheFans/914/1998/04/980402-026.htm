My reply here is I repair them but anyone else can do the same. NO EXPERT.
The following is my general thoughts and theory which may be questionable.

Seriously, there is always light around the butterfly and was that way when
it left the factory. Not always like you describe. I would think that
somewhere along the way, a different butterfly plate was installed in your
TB by someone. The numbers mean nothing to me except the manufacturers part
number. Have you tried to install with numbers facing the other direction to
see how throat and butterfly plate mate? Is it the same or worse?

The butterfly plate has a 1 degree taper around the edge. It was sheared
with the flat brass plate set at 1 degree so as to mell with the circular
throat in fully closed throttle position. There are straight sections on the
edge also which fit precisely into the flat area of the throttle shaft.
Generally I find most numbers on butterfly facing opposite direction of the
idle air adjustment screw, the screw with the spring that is normally frozen
by neglect.   
 
Check your throttle body and you will see that there is some lateral motion.
<- -> on the throttle shaft but not really a problem unless the nut is loose
on the shaft itself.
 
The hole in the butterfly is for vacuum venting of the suction that
developes down in the throat and without some venting the vaccuum would be
very difficult to overcome and I suppose would contribute to excess fuel
flooding the compression chambers, since closed is the choke position. Some
actually solder these holes closed. ????

The swinging rotational motion of the butterfly on the throttle shaft is for
intake AIR that rushes down into the throat, mixes with the fuel drawn into
the throat by the rush of air and atomized into properly proportions for the
required combination needed for proper combustion in the compression chamber
which some use one word to describe all I said as metering.

The 914 was supplied with 2 different types of throttle bodies. The one you
have is the one everyone would like to have. The early models had 2 vaccumm
ports on it. I won't go into this reasoning since all I did was repair them
when someone sent them to me, but pollution reduction was a factor in later
years.

Hope this answers your questions.
