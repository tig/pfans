Lawrence,

Unless the surface "pebles" are totally trashed, i would try a
minimalist approach first. This is what worked on my faded top: Remove
wax etc. with strong cleaner like Fantastic. Use a stiff brush or
scotch-brite. Touch-up chipped coating around the edges with body putty
to approximate surrounding finish. Re-tint with vinyl dye (Griot's
Garage sells it and maybe "Black Again" will work, too.) My top has kept
it's new look for the last two years after this treatment.

BTW. The vinyl dye I found is water-based and rubs on like liquid shoe
polish, but it resembles thin paint in practice. I even use it to
restore the look of all the engine and trunk vinyl stuff and rubber
bushings. For a less shiny finish, I appied it with a rather damp towel.

Hope this helps.
