My suggestion is not to go with the AA Clamshell but rather, the Brad
Mayeur (sp?) piece...  Brad's piece is structurally sounder with a
heavier gauge.  Do both sides at the same time, 'cause if you don't, you
are only delaying the inevitable....

> #2. Where the inner rocker meets the inside of the wheel well there is
> a
> creatively  Bondo'd hole.

Front or back wheel well?  Fronts are no problem - take a piece of 18
gauge and bend to fit.  If it's the rear, well, then you have a
structural problem  The longitudinal wrap around the rear section...(See
below...)

> #3. The panel under the front motor mount is showing it's age. Some
> rust and a
> couple of small holes.

There are a couple of pieces in there - if your referring to the engine
tray, replacement parts are cheap and plentiful.. If you are refering to
where it ties into the body/structure, well, get ready for some major
welding and re-work.  Keep in mind that these areas tend to rust from
the inside out - so most of the major rust you *can't* see, but will
disintegrate when you go to weld them (the torch will blow through the
metal when you strike it.).  This is also a critical suspension area, so
be prepared for some time, money and swearing...

> #4. The front motor mount has a Definite cant to the rear of the car.
> Looks
> like a backslash, kinda.

OK, so finish your first beer?  Start your second - now!  The mounts tie
into the rear longitudinal near the suspension console (just forward of
them).  My best guess is that if the mounting bracket is gone, then so
is the inner longitudinal and probably the suspension console on that
side...  Parts?  $300 - $600 per side depending on what's good versus
bad.  Time? Count on 20 - 30 hours per side (cutting out the rot, prep,
welding, finish, etc.)

> Welding isn't a problem as
> I have a cousin whose a welder by trade and loves the car as much as I
> do.

Suggestion - if you like your cousin at all, tell them beforehand what
they are up against....  But, having done all the stuff you are
referring to recently, yep, it's worth it..
