   Well.... therein lies the exact problem that I am trying to solve!
  You see, both my old Mahles and my new Kolbenschmidts pass the long
feeler gauge test on the low (tight) side of specs:  I bought two of
the long feeler gauges: .002"  (.051 mm) and .004"  (.102 mm)... I
don't remember which one I used now, but I remember that they were
very tight on drag.   Even if the gauge that I used was the .004"
gauge, this is well within spec for used pistons  (I just don't
remember whether I used the same gauge for both the new and used
pistons, but I do remember that they were within spec using both the
long feeler gauge trick and measuring the bore and the piston and
doing the arithmetic.)

   The problem is, that both my used pistons and my new ones passed
the clearance measurements with flying colors, and both new and old
could be rocked back and forth in the bore to what I considered ( no
real experience here ) an alarming degree.   I don't see how it could
be otherwise, given the degree of taper that I observe.   Even the
tightest fit, if it is only over the range of a centimeter or so,
will allow a thing 7 to 10 cm in length to rock back and forth in a
cylindrical bore.

   Now we come to the crux of the matter:  Both piston sets pass the
micrometer and long feeler gauge tests easily, so the spec book says
"keep 'em".... yet you said (and I agreed with you to the tune of
$400. until I got my new pistons): "if you can actually feel the
piston rock in the bore, i'd say you have JUNK!!.. TOSS IT!  "

   Now, the issue that I'm trying to resolve is which spec is
critical, and which is not.   I can think of no way of doing this
other than gathering data on the taper of a whole bunch of pistons,
both new and used, and doing a statistical analysis.   Logic says
that if nearly all pistons are tight at the bottom and loose at the
top, and all of them (even new ones) rock back and forth in the bore,
then this is NORMAL, and don't worry about it.   On the other hand,
if most pistons do NOT have much taper, and DO NOT rock back and
forth in the bore, then excessive taper must be grounds for rejecting
the pistons as unusable, even if they are NEW.   Since I have only
one set of used ones, and one set of new ones (now installed and
unavailable for measurement) I have only two data points (8 actually,
since there are 4 pistons in a set, but you get the idea).

   Hence the request for people to mike their pistons and send me the
raw data.
