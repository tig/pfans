Like I tell everyone that is having odd carburetor troubles (if I had
listened to this advice a few years ago, it'd have saved me 50 hours
worth of tinkering), make 100% certain you don't have any intake leaks.


Start up the car and get it idling nice and smooth then carefully spray
carb cleaner around the manifold/head gasket area (both top and bottom)
and listen/look for any change in idle speed or engine sound.  Also
check around the gaskets between the manifolds and the carbs.  Any
changes?  That means a leak.

I tried for years to put my manifold-to-head gaskets on dry and get them
to seal.  I succeeded in pulling out 2 head studs and cracking 4
isolator blocks (and 2.0 blocks are $25 each!) by over-torquing the
nuts.  Finally got smart and used gas-resistant, high temp gasket sealer
and everything started working better.

Just my thoughts on it.
