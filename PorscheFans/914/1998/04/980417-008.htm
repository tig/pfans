Yeah, the passenger side is usually the first to go because battery acid
mixed with rain gets into the body cavities on that side. Just for a thrill,
remove your backpad (and seat of course), pull up the rubber cover and poke
real hard down where the floor meets the rear bulkhead. Mine was very weak
from rust (caused by battery acid getting into the double wall there). Also,
on the rear of the shifter tunnel is an access panel, remove it and take a
good look inside. The shifter tunnel is open to the cavity formed by the 2
firewalls, so battery acid can freely migrate up into the shifter tunnel.
