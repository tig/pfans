I think only the "California" 75-76 914's had catalytic converters... my '76
doesn't.  I think the biggest "power robber" on the '76 was the smog pump,
which is powered by the crank.  By loosening two bolts and removing a belt,
you get that power back.  I think someone might have said something about a
different set of heat exchangers that weren't as effective as the earlier
ones, but the earlier heat exchangers bolt on without a problem.  I can't
think of anything else that would make an early 2.0 more powerful than a
75-76 2.0 with no smog pump and an early exhaust.  The only other thing that
I can think of would be the bumpers (which weight about 40 lbs each), but
those too can be replaced.  Mine have been replaced with 2 lb aluminum
frames to support the rubber cover, which I figure weighs about 4 lbs, so my
6 lb bumpers might actually be a little lighter than the earlier chrome
bumpers.  FWIW, I saw an early 914 in the Rocky Mountain News with a
professional V6 conversion for $7000...
