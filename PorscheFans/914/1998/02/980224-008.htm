After adding a new battery (it was time) and a rebuilt starter (maybe
not necessary), the 2.5 popped into life and settled into a rough but
steady 1950rpm idle!  I let it run 2-3 minutes, then shut it off, topped
off the oil, and checked for leaks/drips (none!).  The second time, I
let it run about 5 minutes until the oil temp got up to about 150F.
That is enough for tonight; even with the garage door open, the fumes
were building up (exhaust, "curing" of the ceramic exhaust coating, new
cooling tin paint, etc.).

It still barely cranks prior to firing, but the combo of HI-6 and PS-2
apparently gets the job done even with the tight new engine.

Next will be setting the timing, the idle screws on the throttle bodies
(where's that Unisyn?), and and idle knob on the FI ECM.  Another 20-30
minutes of break-in @2000 and I'm looking for pylons!

So have a E-beer with me to celebrate!
