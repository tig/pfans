Dave and Josh-- If you recall, your lock is held in with a small screw right
down the center.  In almost all cases, you can insert a small drill bit into
the lock, centering it on the end of the screw, and when you turn the drill
slowly to the right, the screw backs out to its left, tumbling the locking
mechinism into your trunk and releasing it for you.   Give it a good try, its
better than a fire axe which someone used on our 74 some 20 years ago.   Also
am a SWEPCO dealer, and it all comes from Deano the Dino.  --  Mick  Michelsen
