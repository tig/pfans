Hi there.  We have two items on our website dealing with the master 
cylinder switch.  You can replace the switch without rebleeding the system. 
(I've never done it myself, but I've been told this many times).  You don't 
need to take it out from the master cylinder either.  The switch is not 
really apparent from looking at it that it actually is a switch.  There is 
a plastic little 'nipple' that extends from the switch (where the 
electrical cable attaches).  You need to make sure that the nipple is 
pushed in.  New switches are unreasonably expensive (around $40-50) 
considering that a new master cylinder is only $100, and includes a new 
switch.  For more information and some pictures, check out the following 
pages on our Pelican Parts Website:

http://209.6.145.21/914/914QA/914Q_brakes_e_light.htm
http://209.6.145.21/techarticles/914_replace_master_cylinder/914_replace  
_master_cylinder.htm

Hope this answers your question...
