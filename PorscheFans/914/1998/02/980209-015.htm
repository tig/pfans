	Someone mentioned this earlier, but I'd like to hit it again.
Is your car a 74 (or very early 75)?  If so, you may have the dreaded
"seatbelt interlock relay" down under the passenger seat.  Check for
it--it's a big (3-in?) silver rectangular cannister labeled "logic-
relais".  If you have that, it may have failed, stranding you.  You
can check by using some FAT wire (as thick as the original!) to jumper
the FAT yellow wires to each other.  If the starter and pump now work,
you've got a dead relay.  Either get a replacement, or (the preferred
method), permanently connect those two yellow wires together.
	If this is not your problem, then start troubleshooting.  I'd
suggest the point of commonality of the two systems (starter and fuel
pump):  The ignition switch.
	Good luck!
