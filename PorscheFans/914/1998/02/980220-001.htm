I have narrowed down the "buggish" sound to the diver's side carb 
(Dell 36).  The motor has now gone to studdering upon acceleration and

I 
recently tore the carbs down to clean them and replace gaskets, but I
didn't 
mess with the jets.  There are no vaccuum leaks (checked with the
starter 
fluid trick).  Any ideas?  The timing is dead on and the valves have
been 
recently adjusted.


  Sounds like an idle circuit problem. First, clean all the jets, make
sure you can blow air thru them. Then get an air gauge, (I like the 
J C Whitney gauge better than a uni-syn). Then make sure your air flow
is the same at all 4 barrels(or 2). Your car should idle at 950 to 1000
rpm after warmup. Make sure the idle is balanced for both sides, if you
have a duel setup.
