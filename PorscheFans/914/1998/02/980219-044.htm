> Both of the latter systems are affected by shaft wobble.  If
>severe enough, either may misfire.  Of course, if it's that bad, your
>distributor is probably already in pieces and you'll have heard it
>long ago.  But the wobble will change your timing, as it would with
>points.  The Hall effect sensor is more sensitive to up-and-down play
>in the shaft.  The Hall effect varies as the fourth power of the dis-
>tance (if I remember correctly), so small changes in distance can have
>large changes on the signal from the sensor.  But again, this is most
>likely not a problem unless your distributor is REALLY badly worn.
