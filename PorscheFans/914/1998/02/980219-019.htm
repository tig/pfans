Hi fellow "g's" freaks 

A guibo  is the rubber piece you find in nearly all Lotus cv joints
it is the cv joint.   3-5 bolts thru 3-5 holes in the rubber/flex steel
to the wheel side of the axle
3-5 bolts thru 3-5 holes in the rubber/flex steel to the tranny or
differential side of the axle. simple and very effective.

BMW used them too

Lotus 7 ( autocrossers know ) use these items also most other older
Lotus' use them. 

Also formula 1 cars of 50s 60s and 70s vintage.

I think Alfa Romeo was first to use guibo   hence the Italian name 
for the active component in a cv joint.

Please   all those interested in nice suspensions and where they started
investigate  the Alfa Romeo section in the local Barnes and Noble or
bookstore in your town.
