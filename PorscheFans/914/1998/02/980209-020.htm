> plug back and closer to top of the combustion chamber. Anything to this?

That's not what they're for. If that were the case, you should just buy
shorter plugs. What these are for is called "indexing". The basis is to
shim the plug such that when it's torqued down properly, that the spark
is facing the larger region of the combustion chamber and hence reduces
the effect that the electrode has when it 'shrowds' the spark. This is a
very common drag racer tweak, and can indeed be effective in increasing
power. But keep in mi9nd that this is a _tiny_ increase in power, and
should not be bought into as a super tweak.
