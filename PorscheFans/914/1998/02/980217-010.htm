> Can it be removed easily, or does it involve
> pulling the gas tank to get at it?

Hi JJ;

I don't think you can get it out without removing the gas tank first. 
The base of the blower needs to swing out first. this allows the top of
the blower unit to clear the lip where the trunk weather seal rides. 
With the tank out, you'll also have to remove the rear gas tank mounting
strap - and even then it just *barely* slides out.  (BTW: be careful
when you pull it out, as there is a rod along the bottom of the box
which controls the air flaps. It bends easily if it gets hung up.).

However, the tank is a snap to remove...

Hope this helps,
