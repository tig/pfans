Well I guess if it makes you happy, go ahead and use a sealant on these plugs,
which I've never had leak, but hey it's your car do whatever you want.  (even
a V8 engine if that makes you happy)  My experience only goes to the 8-12
914's I've owned.  The extra slick teflon may also allow you to possibly over
torque these plugs as well.  I just don't want to use something I don't need,
or borrow trouble.  If you've ever done any actual plumbing you'll remember
upon dissassembly that the teflon tape is usually very shredded and difficult
to completely remove.  Aircraft sealant however, comes off easily with solvent


The problem I have with using teflon tape on anything near my engine
lubrication system, (oil lines, drain plugs, etc) is I really don't want to
find out how small a scrap of teflon tape it takes to plug up a piston
squirter, or the pin holes in the cam spray bar.
