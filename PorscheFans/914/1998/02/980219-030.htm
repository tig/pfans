Teeners,
        I appreciated the overwhelming response on the rear caliper rebuild. 
 When I pulled mine off of the car, the passenger side unit had no fluid at 
all in it.  Upon disassembly, I found that the inner adjusters on both 
calipers were frozen solid.  The inner pistons would not budge.  After a 
quick trip to my mechanic, we determined that both calipers were shot.  $200 
later, GPR sent me two good used units.  They went on the car with no 
problem, but when I tried to bleed the system, it would hold no pressure.  
My suspicions were confirmed when I took the MC apart and found rust and 
worn out seals.  Another $100 to GPR for a 19mm unit, and the car is finally 
back on the road after a 2 1/2 week hiatus.  It is amazing how a simple pad 
and rotor change can turn into such an expensive odyssey.  
        The jumping out of first gear problem has been narrowed down to 
either a chewed up gearset or selector shaft.  Given the fairly high price 
of these parts, would I be better off replacing the whole tail-shift trans 
with a good used side-shifter? What would be the cost of this transplant?  
Anyone have a good used tail or side shift unit for sale cheap?  Are the 
gearset or selector shaft available used for a reasonable price?
        I have narrowed down the "buggish" sound to the diver's side carb 
(Dell 36).  The motor has now gone to studdering upon acceleration and comes 
close to dieing when I let off the throttle and the car is out of gear.  I 
recently tore the carbs down to clean them and replace gaskets, but I didn't 
mess with the jets.  There are no vaccuum leaks (checked with the starter 
fluid trick).  Any ideas?  The timing is dead on and the valves have been 
recently adjusted.
