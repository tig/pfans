> And is probably running really rich! Operating temperature should yield
> only a couple hundred ohms, not thousand. The resistance of the HTS
> drops as it heats up and will range from about 2.5k ohms cold to about
> 100-200 ohms when warm. If you had to crank up the pot to that high a
> level to get the engine to run at normal temperatures, then you probably
> have big leak in the intake plumbing somewhere. What you're doing is
> fooling the brain into thinking that the engine is actually very cold
> and the ECU compensates by running rich. The extreme of this situation
> is an open circuit on the HTS which will force the ECU to go to full
> rich and consequently flood the engine. Ask me how I know... :)

> Yup, first off make sure that your Auxiliary Air Regulator is working.
> Make sure that the cold start valve (if you have one) is functioning
> properly (turning off when it should) and that it's not leaking. After
> that, the regular leak check routine on a D-jet car. Get a can of brake
> cleaner or carb starter fluid and start spraying it in small controlled
> bursts on any joints that could be responsible for vacuum leaks. The
> intake runner to head interface is a common source of frustrating vacuum
> leaks. The 'socks' that join the intake runners to the plenum are
> another possible problem place. But don't worry, there's _lots_ of other
> places to check too. :)
