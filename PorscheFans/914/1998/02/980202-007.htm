Brett Miller wrote:

> Does anybody know of any companies in southern California that will widen
> steel wheels?  My 2.0 has 5.5x15s on it now and I would like to widen them
> to 7x15 to get a sleeper kind of look.  What offset should I ask for to fit
> 7x15s under stock fenders.  Will they fit.  I would like to run 225/60/15
> tires or there-abouts.  Any suggestions?

_Stock_ fenders? I don't think it'll work with the combo you're looking at. I
run 225/50s on 6 inch rims and they BARELY fit. I'm not kidding, that extra .2
inches cross-section you'll gain with the 1 inch wider rim may very likely be
too much.

As far as getting steel wheels widened? Just get a whole new set. Steel wheels
are pretty damn cheap. Custom steel wheels can be had for as little as
something like $60-$80 per wheel. And you'll probably have to pay like $50 per
to have a wheel widened.

> On another note.  I managed to scavenge a front and rear sway-bar set-up off
> of a 73 2.0 in the local yard.  The rear is a snap to put on but I dont have
> the holes drilled in the body for the front one (it has the tabs on the
> control arms).  Is this something I want to get into at home or should I let
> a place like Ottos handle this.  This is kind of a pressing issue since I
> also got a pair of 140lb rear springs out of the yard.  Since Ive put them
> on the car's a little tail happy and I believe the sway bar should restore
> some balance.

It is something that you can do if you have patience and and good set of
instructions. Templates are key, if you don't have some kind of template (like
the Weltmeister plates), and aren't comfortable with pulling the gas tank and
all that (yes, the tank will need to come out), then maybe giving it to a pro
isn't a bad idea. Otto's does good work from what I hear, so that might be a
good place to try.
