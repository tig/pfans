Sorry, but I can't resist adding aa few items of interest  - and you
bet, the blower housing is a *PIA* to repair, but, to make it easier,
IMHO here are a couple of tips.....

> The problem
> is those spring steel clips that hold the clamshell
> together. . .

Don't try to unclip them - yes they will break the mounting tangs (did
on one of mine). The solution is to "slide" them off their tangs with a
screwdriver...

> and the actuator cables with those snap
> clamps.

Loosen the box and pull it out a couple of inches.  That allows you to
actually reach the clip and mount.  The metal retaining clip is held
onto its metal base as it clamps the wire.  Take a jewelers screwdriver
and pry the clip between the retaining tang and it's metal base.  If you
try to just pull the clip off using the wire, you'll break the box....

> The motor in these is an open frame type and uses
> those 'permanently lubricated' bushing bearings.

Yea, that's why I re-lubricated mine - I figured it would give me
another 10/10  of lifetime lubrication (10 seconds or 10 tries to get
the thing working again - whichever comes first:-)

> In relative terms, if you can fix the fresh-air blower
> in your 914, you should have little difficulty with
> rustoring the body.

Obviously, you haven't seen my 914:-)  I'd rate them as about equal...
