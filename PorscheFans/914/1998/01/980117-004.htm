I haven't seen any aluminum valve covers first hand in several years.  I
purchased a set aprox. 15 years ago.  These were advertised as "no leak"
covers, and required unbolting two ends of the rocker shafts and installing a
nut/stud.  They did require some "massaging" of the sheetmetal.  They looked
great and worked poorly.  "No leak" was a bogus claim for these.  I've since
seen some advertised with O rings instead of gaskets and I would like to know
if these are any better.  The bolt holes are off center (top to bottom)
because of how they attatch to the rocker shafts, so I wonder if you could
ever get equal pressure on the gasket or O ring.  After more valve adjustments
than I can remember, I've got pretty good at putting on the valve covers, and
this is much less trouble than fooling with the bolt ons.  I purchased the
alum. ones to trick up my engine, but took them off when they leaked.  Oh yea,
I forgot that there wasn't a very good method for sealing the bolt holes
either.
