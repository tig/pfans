You're not kidding about the cost of rust repair. I spent 3,500$ on my 2002
over the last month and a half, and that was with me doing all of the
assembly and disassembly. No paint job either, except on the repaired
sections. My body guy did an excellent job and I highly recommend him to
anyone. It is just extremely time consuming to do a good job. It was at the
end of the second day before the first rocker panel went on the car, that's
how long it took to rip out the old one, repair the inner rocker (including
fabricating the parts because they are NLA), clean and rustproof the inner
rocker, and fit and weld on the new rocker. The other side took just as
long, plus I had lots of rust holes in bad places like fender wells and
floors and shock towers. I was also extremely fortunate that he allowed me
to "help out" by allowing me to do all of the disassembly, re-assembly
(removing bumpers, fenders, lights, etc.), rust proofing and general stupid
things that dont' require any skill. He said I saved a lot of money doing it
that way. I took a week off from work and went to work on the car out at his
shop. I had a good time, but it is extremely time consuming to do a good job.
