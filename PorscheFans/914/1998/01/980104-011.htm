Cole, Nicholas wrote:

> What welder would you buy to begin with.

Lincoln make an excellent arc/mig welder in both a 110V and 220V series.
The cost is less then $300.  Without the Argon/CO2 tank (the attachments
is another ~$100), they have a core welding spool that can be used
without the tank attachments - and will do a great job for most  body
work.  My experience with traditional arc and oxyacetylene is that is
difficult to  get the temps/volts low enough to create a tack or run a
seam without burning holes in the sheet metal.  With the Lincoln 110V
model, I use the lowest power setting with a wire feed of about 2.5 for
20 & 22 gauge steel (most body panels).  Plus, a good MIG will last for
years and helps even the most un-practiced novice create good welds (I
should know - the 914 is my 5th P'car restoration :-)

One last thought - PRACTICE helps a lot. Practice tacks and seams on a
22 gauge sheet (~$10 for a 2'x3' sheet) before going to  work on your
car.  The sheets are cheaper blowing holes through factory sheet metal
replacements.  Also, take your time to prepare the surfaces properly -
the extra prep time will show in the final product ..
