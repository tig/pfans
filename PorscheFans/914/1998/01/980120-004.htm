	There is a procedure in the factory manual for aligning H4's. It is
really pretty simple.  If you pull up to a wall, you will notice that there
is a distinct light pattern.  The top of the bright-lit area will be flat
(horizontal), then abruptly angle upward to the right.  When your car is
straight and level, the point where this turn happens should be level with
(and directly ahead of) the center of your headlights.
	BTW, this is not exactly legal in the US.  I'm not sure exactly why,
but it may be that US headlights are supposed to point down by a certain
amount.  To do the aiming properly/legally over here, you need one of those
special gizmos that hangs off the bumps on the surface of the light.  If
you don't have the bumps, then your headlights are (technically) illegal for
street use.
