> is running so bad, I have only gone around the block once in 2 months of

Change the fuel filter, and cut open the old one. If there's alot of
junk in the filter (brown rust) then it's time to pull the tank and
either get it cleaned and fixed or replaced.
 
> The 914: 1970 1.7 with a Webber 32/36 DFAV that PO had been rebuilt. He

Wow! A gift? You lucky dog! 

> 914 starts after about 20 seconds, after sitting over night. Once warm it
> fires up on 1st crank. Battery is new, a Diehard that I bought. Oil and
> filter has been changed, plugs and points are new and timing is correct.
> Fuel: not enough when started cold after sitting overnite, but excessive
> after cranking and running. This is evident by excessive fumes. Not oil, but
> gas fumes. The fuel gage is a Mallery attached directly to the fuel line
> that reads from 1 to 15 pounds. Fuel pressure is a constant 8.3 psi as near
> as I can estimate between the marks. The fuel filter is also new.

First off, I'm an FI guy primarilly. My only personal carb experience is
with side-draft Mikunis on a Kawasaki. Amusingly enough, _they_ were
36mm too! But first and foremost, get a fuel pressure regulator. That
sounds like your fuel pressure is too high. It also sounds like you've
got a leaky carb. The 20 second period is probably because the gas has
either leaked or evaporated out of the float bowl, and it takes time to
refill. Once the bowl is full, the car will start just fine. But you're
probably running rich too. Check the spark plugs for fouling.

It would be a very good idea to clean the carb. If it sat for six months
or more, it's quite possible that the gas has varnished in the bowl or
jets and is adversely affecting it.
