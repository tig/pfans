>I've done it.  Twice - both times on cars that had never had them.  No
>problems, but a little work.  Both cars had the U-tabs on the A-arms 
>so
>I didn't have to mess with welding them on.
>
>First off, buy the Weltmeister mounting kit ($16).  This eliminates 
>the
>need for any welding and makes installation much easier by providing
>detailed instructions and a template to make sure you drill/cut in
>exactly the right place.  You'll need a 'key-hole' drill bit - I don't
>remember what size, but you can get it at your local discount hardware
>store - it'll tell you in the mounting kit instructions.
>
>The gist of it is, remove the gas tank and put the front of the car up
>on jack stands so its real secure and remove the front wheels.  Using
>the template, mark your 4 holes (1 for the bar, 3 for bolts) on the
>outside of the inner fender wall.  Drill them out (watch out for the
>wiring harness on the driver side -ask me how I know...).  Now the 
>PITA
>part.  Where the inside plate mounts to the inside of the inner 
>fender,
>there's a metal seam.  You have to clean that off so the overlap is 
>gone
>so the plate will sit flat against the inner wall.  I used a 
>combination
>of a drill, pliers, grinding wheel, file, and sweat to do mine.  On 
>the
>second car, we used a grinder and, in my opinion, made a messier
>installation.  Pre-fit the mounting plates to make sure all the bolts
>will line up.  RUST-PROOF THE METAL that you have drilled!!!  Bolt up
>the plates and run the bar through.  That's all there is too it.  Took 
>2
>guys about 2-3 hours to do a nice job of it.
>
>As far as what bar to run?  That's pretty much pocket-book dependent.  
>I
>bought a factory (16mm) bar for $75 (also get new bushings if you buy
>used).  I couldn't believe the difference!!  I still get a little body
>roll on tight corners, but compared to running w/o a bar, its 
>incredibly
>flat.  I daily-drive the car and do local autocrosses monthly - works
>great and is NOT at all uncomfortable around town.  (I run 140lb 
>springs
>in the rear.)
>
>I suppose the 22 or 23 mm adjustable bars are even better, but for the
>$300, I can buy a pair of R1s.  If you want to go the stock-bar route,
>post an inquiry to the list - I'm certain someone out there's got a
>spare sitting around.
