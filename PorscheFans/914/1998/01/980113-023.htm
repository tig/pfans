 > There are a number of studs attached to the back of the dashtop, and 
> plastic nuts are used to secure the top to the dash frame.
	
	There are also some metal ones.  The plastic ones face down and the
metal ones unscrew towards the front.
 

> I would begin
> by removing the (1) center console; (2) dash bottom pad (bottom 
> screws and side screws) (3) steering wheel: (4) guages; (5) glove
> box (6) radio, (7) ashtray.;  
	
	Don't really need to remove the steering wheel, guages or glove box. 
You will need to pull the tach and the speedo forward - be careful not
to scratch the paint on the bezel and get some slack in the speedo cable
before trying to pull it forward.  My experience has been that most/all
the nuts are only hand tight!

>  I would also suggest removing the targa
> top for 
> additonal light and room to work.

	An excellent way to make room to contort your body in such a way as to
get your hands behind the guages.  

 
> There are studs which hold the dash
> top down and others which hold it in to the metal dash frame. There
> are 
> both types on each corner and some in the middle as well. The most 
> difficult to access are the two nuts which are located in a very
> narrow 
> space in the dash frame behind where the guages are typically
> located.

	To ease in getting these two nuts reinstalled you can take a pair of
needle-nose plyers and bend the sheet metal flatter and up a little to
get your fingers back in there.  Also, I put a little grease on the
washer and try to "stick" it up on the stud first.  That way you only
have to worry about getting the nut started on the threads. 

>    I have found no alternative to using a 13mm wrench (almost
> everything 
> on these cars is either 13mm or 10mm) to loosen the nuts and then 
> squeezing two fingers into the gap to continue turning the nuts. If
> you think
> this is ridiculously difficult, wait until you have to reinstall the
> nuts and
> tighten them. Keep a sense of humour and put aside a whole day for
> this project.

	These two nuts you probably will need a short 13mm wrench to get
loose.  All the others I can usually loosen by hand with a socket.
