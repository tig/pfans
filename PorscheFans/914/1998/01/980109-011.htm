Hi Ben and anyone interested,

I can only tell you a tiny bit.  I had never welded before, I bought the
MityMite welder and both the attachments you mentioned from Eastwood.   I
don't think much of the MityMite, very hard to control and especially for a
beginner.  I had a friend who was a professional welder try it and he had
problems also.  He said he hated these little buzz box type units.  He
recommended a small MIG unit for most car work and especially for a
beginner learning to weld.

So I haven't tried the spot or stitch attachments.  They may work better
but I don't have the skill to evaluate them.  The quality of the units
looks good, they are made in England.  If I can find the manuals that came
with the attachments I'll look and can send you what they say are the
requirements for the control unit.  I don't know if they would work with
your large Lincoln arc welder.

I'd been looking at the small Lincoln 100 MIG welder. Then I found that
PriceCostco had started carrying the Century 130 unit which has a few more
features than the Lincoln 100, like totally adjustable heat range.  It is
normally more expensive but at Costco it was $399 (with the gas regulator)
which is about the same as the Lincoln.  So I got one.  Now I just need a
gas bottle and to clean up space in the garage to start practicing.
