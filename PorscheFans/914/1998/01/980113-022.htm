   I am pretty sure that I saw some limit (or position sensor)
switches when I last had the plastic covers off the motor drive area
inside the front trunk.   As I recall, there were some leaf-type
switches attached to the side of the gear cases on the motors, but I
don't recall for sure.  At any rate, they are pretty obvious by their
appearance and the wiring going to them (as I recall).   It is now
dark when I get home from work, and I won't have a chance to take my
own covers off until I finish my current long work week, which should
be Jan. 23.   If you want to wait until then, I will try to take a
look for you.  
   However, I think that once you have the black plastic side covers
off, the mechanism is pretty self-explanatory, with the exception of
the wiring details.   I am still not sure how they manage to
accomplish all of the logic functions that they seem to with only one
relay, but I think that they are helped out in this by the fact that
the motor only runs in ONE direction to drive the lights both up and
down.   In other words, I believe that the gear mechanism is a sort
of crank, that goes around and around, with stop switches at it's two
extremes of travel.   This might make what you propose to do somewhat
difficult;  you may end up having to change the lever arm length or
fulcrum position to change the length of travel.
   I hope that this is of some help.
