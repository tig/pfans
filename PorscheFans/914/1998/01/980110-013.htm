Unless, someone else from the list has better advice - here's mine:

Follow Dirk's recommendation and save your money for the correct
longitudinal pieces and then have them professionally welded in. I tried
originally on a '65 911 (that was the first P'car restoration 11 years
ago) to braze pieces in with an oxyacetylene torch. I ended up cutting
out and re-doing it right (wasted time and $) - my $.02 is that brazing
does not have the structural integrity of a good MIG weld (at least mine
didn't).

The trunk (unless it's the cross-member) floor isn't a problem and
either is the floorpan - unless you want a dry car on those rainy days.

Wish I had a better solution (maybe I'm having a bad day since I spent
all of it rebuilding the front suspension of the 944 Turbo - and not a
single minute on the 914..)
