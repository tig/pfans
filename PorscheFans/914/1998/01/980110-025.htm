I'm definately in the BTDT group.  Couple of comments.

1st:  The number I have for Parts Werks is 800-366-9147 or 815-462-3000
They're in Chicago.

Mine had the rust you describe (and a pristine trunk)  I thought that
all
I needed was a new battery tray.  So I got one of those, and tried to
fit
it in.  Finally figured out that I needed a new engine shelf.  When I
tried
to fit THAT in, I found that the inner fender had rotted away.  By the
time
I had cleared out all the rusted pieces, I needed a small section of
firewall,
the top of the longitudinal support, new upper inner fender, new engine
shelf,
and the batterey tray.  For me, adding the stiffening panels didn't add
that
much to the job.  Of course, once I was in there, ship-wrights disease
set in,
and the trunk mounts got changed, the engine lid hinges were replaced,
the rear
sway bar mounts were beefed up, etc., etc.

But back to your project.  I don't think your firewall rust comes from a
leaky
rear window.  That's just not how these cars rust.  More than likely,
the holes
are from where the engine shelf _used_ to be.

What you need to do is find a good example of a car, and look closely at
it.

It there anyone in the Seattle area that will let Nick sniff around at
their
car?


Gary.

car.
Your repair idea will probably work, but for how long?  Purists will
hate it,
but heck, it's not that rare of a car.

PPS:  My rust repair cost more than the purchase price.

> Since it seems like more of us are trying to tackle the rocker rust devil, I

> My passenger door will not pass the dd Big butt test or even the no butt test!
> The door needs some serious muscle to open. The top squeeks like hell on the
> right rear mount. There doesn't appear to be any problem with the suspension
> mount. The battery tray is gone, I presume from rust. The motor support there
> is intact. The lower edge of the firewall (outer) is full of holes (1"-3").
> This appears to be caused by the water dripping down the rear window. The only
> place this goes thru to the inner firewall is the lower corner behind the
> passenger seat, which actually lets water in. I patched this with some bondo
> over the summer as a stopgap measure until the big repair. The RR jack plate
> is still workable (the square tube one) but the corner triangle is pretty much
> gone. Unlike many cars in condition like mine, I have a beautiful trunk with
> not a spot of rust ouside or in or under.
>      The car runs great and everything works--good interior, newly rebuilt and
> detailed brakes, stereo, etc. I believe it is worth the rescue. I don't have
> that much in it ($1200) and I use it as a daily driver. I have yet to find a
> better 914 available for the price, locally (seattle).
>      I've cleaned the junk from under the rockers by removing the three screws
> that were rusted tight. Couple of pounds of crap in there. Driver side shows
> no serious rust. I will install new rockers (fiberglass? ABS?), so I'll POR 15
> or EXTEND the driver inner at that time.
> As my friend and former boss runs a custom gate factory, he has volunteered to
> donate a day or weekend to weld for me, if I do the grinding (which I did
> there) and wire brushing. What I would like to do is:
>     1. Use the "donated" steel from the gate shop and save the cost of buying
> clamshells, if at all possible.
>      2. Jack the car up with the door closed at the jack point with a floor
> jack until the door gap is correct. With a piece of flat bar (about 18") on
> the floor jack, the gap corrects under very light jacking.
>      3. Weld a 1x1 or 1x2 in place from the front fender well to the rear over
> the good existing metal, properly prepared. I am not after concours and the
> rocker cover can hide any strange looking appendages.
>      4. Weld some 20 gauge sheets over the properly prepared holes in the rear
> outer firewell.
>      5. Weld a small 20 gauge piece behind the passenger seat on the inner
> firewall. Back pad will cover this, along with the seat.
>      6. Weld a new corner triangle for a good jacking point (those guys at the
> tire place can be hell on VW's--I have horror stories from my 65 Bus!).
>      7. Install new outer rocker covers--for cosmetics. Mine may be reusable,
> but their bottom edge is pretty ugly.
