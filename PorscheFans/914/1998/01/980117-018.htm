I've added a GIF to my 914 Lite page (see below) to show the
Bursch-into-turbo-muffler that's so hard to explain in words.  It
connects to stock heat exchangers (with, or in my case without,
outsides).  Instead of a U to the left after the collector, it has a
2.25" (for 2.0 engines) turbo muffler bolted on via a flange and a
90-degree tailpipe on the far right end.  It's a little hard to see, but
the muffler is offset (bottom) in and center out.

I'm replacing the muffler piece with a 2.5" turbo muffler (FlowTech) for
the 2.5 engine.  The only catch in selecting a muffler for this
configuration is that the case can't be longer than 13" if you want (as
I do) a hanger at the beginning of the tailpipe.  Also, this system
precludes use of a non-modified rear valence as the tailpipe would need
to go through the right side of the valence (not the back/middle
section, the part aimed at the rear tire).  The little colored part just
left of the muffler is the sender for the air/fuel ratio gauge.

Clearer?
