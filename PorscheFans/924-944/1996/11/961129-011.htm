>shape, and I don't punch the throttle *that* aggressively coming  
>out of turns.
>Is this normal for this car, or might I have a suspension problem?  I've
>noticed that the camber in the rear is greater than in the front,

>this to counter oversteer.  Any wisdom would be welcome.
>

With enough power, you can induce oversteer in any car. While your 944S2
is neutral to the bitter end, you can still convert the tendency to 
understeer to an oversteer condition with enough throttle input or 
enough braking.

Perhaps your shocks are not up to par, your tires are not good enough,
or you might have encountered hydroplaning or a bit of oil on the water.

In order to learn about the characteristics of your car at the limits of
adhesion, I would suggest joining the Porsche Club and attending the 
driver education sessions that the local regions make available.

Good Luck!
