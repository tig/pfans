You wrote:

>The subject line states the problem.  I have tried several lubricants 
>without success.  What is the best lubricant for the job and what are the
>proper points to apply?  It's kind of a pain to open the door everytime 
>for my passengers.

I've found that powdered graphite is still the best.  I would lubricate both 
through the key hole, and directly on the mechanism.  The workings of the 
handle are easily accessed.  Remove the 1 Phillips screw just ahead of the 
door latch, slide the handle forward about 1/4 in. (there's a hook on the 
front portion that latches on the door shell), then gently swing the handle 
away from the door shell.  You don't need to detach the handle, and the 
lubrication points should be quite obvious.
