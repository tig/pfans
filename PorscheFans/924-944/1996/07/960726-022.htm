> What causes the gears to strip and how to best avoid this from happening?

I have an opinion but George probably has more experience! The plastic 
gear is the weak link in the roof drive-train. Any misadjustment that 
caused the motor to attempt to overdrive the cable beyond roof limits 
will weaken the gears over time. I think that my original problems with 
the roof and the length of time I took to fix it properly, planted the 
seed. One gear stripped completely, the second was still driving but 
looked well worn with a few teeth missing.

Having said that, this does seem to be a high replacement item.
I noticed from the Automotion catalogue that there are aluminium gears 
available. IMHO this is a bad idea. The plastic gear is an easy to 
replace mechanical "fuse" if you have a significant problem with the 
system. If the gears won't strip something $$expensive$$ will probably 
result.

just my $0.02 (plus shipping & handling)
