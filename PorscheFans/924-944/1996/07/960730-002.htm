Could be ground strap. But before that, where did you measure
the battery voltage ?

If you measured it someplace in the car wiring (fuse-box etc.)
then repeat same measurement with BOTH the DVM terminals right
on the battery terminal posts. Do you still see the voltage
drop? You can stick the DVM cables under the gap near the wiper
blades so you can read the meter through the windshield, -- now
rev up to some 3500rpm, and now if you see the voltage rise, if
you now get more than 13v, it is surely the battery.

First rule out the possibility that your battery needs water, or
acid, or ... just lacks speed and is headed for the pot.
