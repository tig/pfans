There's always some confusion when talking about boost levels. 

The gauge in the 944 Turbo reads from 0 to 2 bar. When the gauge reads 1 bar,
that means there is NO boost from the turbocharger. That is normal atmospheric
boost. When the gauge reads above 1.0 bar, the turbo is generating boost, and
increasing the power of the engine. The maximum boost a stock 944 T will
generate is a reading of 1.8 bar on the dashboard gauge, or .8 bar boost over
normal atmospheric pressure. 

If the chip in the 944 T is changed, more boost may be generated. The FAQ states
that the 944 turbo can safely (?) use up to 1.1 boost (2.1 on the dash gauge)
before you risk damaging the engine. 

Hope this clears up some confusion.
