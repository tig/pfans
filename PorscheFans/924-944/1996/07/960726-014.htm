Hi Peter,

>> > The gauge in the 944 Turbo reads from 0 to 2 bar. When the gauge reads 1 bar,
> > that means there is NO boost from the turbocharger. That is normal atmospheric
> > boost. When the gauge reads above 1.0 bar, the turbo is generating boost, and
> > increasing the power of the engine. The maximum boost a stock 944 T will
> > generate is a reading of 1.8 bar on the dashboard gauge, or .8 bar boost over
> > normal atmospheric pressure.

> as a non-turby trooper, I am interested
> 
> in normal trundling along the freeway motoring, what does the guage read?
> 
> ie if you are in traffic rolling at 40 in 5th gear what is the pressure.

Under normal highway driving, the gauge reads around 0 boost. A little
pressure on the throttle will cause some slight boost (1.2 on the gauge).
Letting up on the throttle and coasting will cause the gauge to drop
(.5 or so).
