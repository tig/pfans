I have now done over 100k miles in 2 944's, and have not noticed any
blackening of the brake fluid.

UK spec cars have a separate reservoir for clutch fluid, I have not seen
any appreciable change in the fluid in either system.

If you are getting ANY wear on the rubber seals, I would expect to find
problems with fluid leakage - aka break failure.

The most common contaminant of brake fluid is water - non silicon fluid is
hydro-scopic, the effect of water contamination is to cause vapour locks,
as the water - even under pressure - will vaporise at <200degC this tends
to force fluid back into the res. leading to very long pedal travel as the
water vapour is very compressable.

I have no idea what can cause blackening of the fluid - that seems quite
odd to me.
