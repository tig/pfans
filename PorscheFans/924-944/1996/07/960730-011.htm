Well as if y'all hadnt heard
enough outta me. . .

  I'm still wrangling with my 84's head doings.
  To-nite I might get the intake side buttoned
up and refill her carcass with oil and coolant
before I attempt to give fire.

  I spent considerable time cleaning everything
up before reassembly.  I discovered the 3M
scotchbrite 'cookies' for polishing mating surfaces.
  These chuck in a drill and are quick-change.
  -might even beat chemical paint stripper. . .

  I completely disassembled the throttle body.
  The throttle shaft sits in a pair of needle bearings
that are pressed into the housing, and the bottom
bearing was so full of crud that the throttle shaft
was sticky above 1/2  throttle.  The lower
bearing gets submerged in oil fumes and cleaning
grunge, and then cant void.  It gums up and stalls
the shaft.  Beware.

  -kyle

  It took a lot
of solvent to clean the bearings out, but the
results are like new action.
