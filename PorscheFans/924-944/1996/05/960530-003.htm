        The technique for using grease to remove a bearing is a neat one, 
     however it does rely on a space between the bottom (i.e.crank) and the 
     bearing.  If this space isn't there, then your mechanical advantage is 
     essentially nil and you will eat a lot of grease before going to the 
     store and buying the proper tool.  Check using one of those nice 
     dentist picks with a right angle at the bottom.
