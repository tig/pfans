Depends on the Snap On guy.  The one who services the shop-I-hang-around
is *very* well stocked on metric/euro tools, all the time.  It's quite
surprising how much stuff fits in a Snap On truck.  For that matter, given
the weight of my own little toolbox, it's even more amazing that the Snap
On truck even moves!

Most of the aftermarketers stock and sell 32 and 36 mm wrenches for working
with 911 oil lines.

For the 944-series spring plate eccentric, you don't need a lot of force
to turn the adjuster.  You can certainly get away with using a fractional
wrench, or even an adjustable wrench.  Unless for some reason the adjuster
is really frozen up... in which case dose it with something penetrating to
begin with.
