>Does this mean that the 3.0L block is not the same block as the 2.5?  I'm under
>the assumption that the same block was used throughout the series.  Only bore
>and stroke were increased, not the actual engine block.  In '89 the 944 had
>2.7L of displacement, the 944s2 had 3.0L displacement.  The 944 had a larger
>bore (over the 2.5), the 944s2 had same bore as the 944, and a longer stroke.
> It seems that it would have been more cost efficient for Porsche to simply use
>the 944 and 944s2 block (2.7L, to gain 3.0L the 944s2 had an increased stroke),
>instead of having to bore out two different sizes into the blocks.

Yes the 3.0 Liter and the 2.7 Liter motors have a bore of 104mm, vice 100mm for the 
2.5L earlier cars.  Radically different block, completely different in the buttressing
of the cylinder walls, near the crankshaft.

Using the 3.0 liter crankshaft to stroke a 2.5 liter motor to 2.8 liters without changing
the bore is one improvement that is being made around the country for some club
racers.
