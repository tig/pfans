The dust shield is manufactured and shipped with that split in it.

>
>-Zims will only sell you a pump rebuilt on a core identical to yours, i.e early
>for early, mid for mid, late style for late style.  Mine is mid style (gasket on
>thermostat sealing surface, but no casting feature to prevent cam belt contact
>between top and bottom courses).  What *came* was early style, so I had to send
>it back.  The purpose of the gasket is apparently to speed up heater warmup when
>the thermostat is closed.  The casting feature would be nice to have as a "risk
>management" item, but my favorable experience with Gates belts *not* slackening
>reduces the value of that.  The only time the previous belts were tensioned was
>at installation and 1000 miles later.

Jay Pineau will rebuild your core pump for you, so that you don't have to worry about
improper substitutions. His ads are in Panorama and Excellence.

>-My biggest anxiety upon startup was that the lifters wouldn't pump up.  In the
>event, I got what I guess was a rod knocking for perhaps a second, as the oil
>pressure came up.  There was some lifter-sounding noise but it gradually
>subsided to what I best remember it sounded like before I took it out of
>service.
>

A simple procedure is to prime the oil pump with oil through the filter flange hole.
You can then crank the motor with the DME disconnected, until the oil pressure comes
up to normal.
