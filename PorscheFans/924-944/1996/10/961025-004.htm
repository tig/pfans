> Everything was working just fine before you replaced the turbo coolant pump,
> right? Just make sure you have the system properly bleed and watch it for
> a couple of days. If you find that periodically re-bleeding the system
> restores proper operation then you are either loosing coolant or the head
> gasket is leaking and pressurizing the system.
> 
> I would be surprised if the thermostat and the pump decided to go south at
> the same time, but here is the answer to your thermostat question.
> 
> You can remove the thermostat without major trouble, but it ain't a lot of
> fun. The key is to get a pair of snap ring pliers with the 45-degree tips.
> These are available from Snap-On, Craftsman and the other places. The ones
> I bought included a variety of replacable tips and the 45-degree ones were
> part of the set. If the tips are very small you might have to take a file
> to them. If I remember correctly the holes in the ring are about 2mm in
> diameter, but you have probably seen this during your inspection.
> 
> Removal will be easier from either the top of the car or the bottom,
> depending upon where the end of the snap ring happens to lie.
> 
> I do not think you will have much success trying to rotate the snap ring into
> an ideal position as the ends will dig into the aluminum and prevent this.
> 
> You just have to work by feel but it is possible and should only take you
> 10 minutes or so once you have the correct tool. If the car hasn't been
> draining coolant long you will get a big splash in your face if you happen
> to be under the car when the thermostat comes out so be careful of that.
> 
> Good luck with it,
