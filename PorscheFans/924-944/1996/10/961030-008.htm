     OK, last night I test fitted one of my rear Turbo calipers and its 
     associated rotor. They did seem to fit, but the offset for the caliper 
     relative to the rotor was biased slightly toward the wheel.
     
     That bias could be compensated with two washers (one for each mounting 
     bolt) as spacers between the caliper and the mount of the trailing 
     arms.  I only need to shift the caliper about 1 mm, so I would think 
     that the structural integrity would not be compromised.  Am I missing 
     anything here?
     
     The other difference between the 87 944 and 87 Turbo trailing arms 
     would be the ABS sensor which I do not need for this car.
     
     I understand that there could be things that I have not accounted for, 
     so I am open to your suggestions.  Breaks (and steering) are the two 
     most critical components of any car.  My mission is not to compromise 
     either of those two, but to enhance both.
