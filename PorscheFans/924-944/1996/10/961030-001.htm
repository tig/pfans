regarding engines > 3l, the general perceived wisdom is that the optimal
cylinder size is 300cc, when the capacity gets much bigger fuel burn
efficiency tails off - hence 10 & 12 cylinder F1 engines.

I am uncertain about US fuel efficiency regs - as I understand it
manufacturers have to meet certain fleet average fuel consumption figs -
is it CAFE ? bigger 944/68 engines will make it more difficult for
porsche to meet the regs.

going from 3l to 3.4l probably means you need a new block, and block
design & tooling is very expensive, so there is not gonna be a lot of
interest from porsche for such a venture. This is especially true as
porsche can already get all the oomph reqd by adding a turbo to the
existing engine.

Regarding turbos and 4 valve heads - porsche in common with most other
turbo car manufacturers ( renault, rover ) used 2 valve heads - as the
intake is under pressure, you can just fiddle with waste-gate settings to
get the reqd mixture at max revs.
