     I had a BMW that had a similar problem.  The dealer replaced the fuel 
     injectors (it was a long time ago, I think it was the injectors).  
     After replacement the car started instantly.  How are your injectors?
     
     I don't know if you have a problem though.  Seems to me a lot of 
     things could contribute to the slower starting syndrome, but I would 
     only worry if this is symptomatic of a component slowly failing, or 
     de-tuning.  If this has been this way since you got the machine, and 
     there is no degeneration in running over a long period of time (how 
     long have you had it?), then I would not worry.
     
     As for any benefits; yes it might help prime the oil pump.  Some 
     people I have heard talk about adding a pre-lube oil pump (electric I 
     think) to circulate oil before ever starting the car.  I never heard 
     of one on a Porsche, but it seems like a good idea.  Most of the wear 
     on an engine is during the first few seconds of starting.  After that 
     the wear is much much less when running under normal conditions.

>      If your starter spins the engine at 800+ RPM you should be able to 
>      stop using gas and run your car on batteries!
> 
>      Ideally I would believe you should hand coat every moving part in the 
>      engine prior to starting. However, I disconnect the coil wire before 
>      starting the engine after oil has been changed.  Both of my 944 cars 
>      will hardly register on the tach. when cranking away.
> 
>      There is an advantage to this low speed rotation.  It allows the oil 
>      system time to prime and get oil moving.  Even a relatively low
>      running RPM (900 RPM) there is a substantial risk to dry metal parts 
>      wearing under this initial start.  Starting the car at idle to prime 
>      the oil pump represents about a 10 times faster rotation than the
>      starter motor will generate.  That is 10 times the wear and tear on 
>      your engine.
> 
>      Turning the  car over with the coil wire disconnected is about the 
>      best compromise I know of.
