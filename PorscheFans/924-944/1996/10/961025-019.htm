> I have two questions, first when bleeding the clutch/break lines on a 
> '84 944 which do you do first, clutch or break and also which wheel 
> comes first and so on.

Doesn't matter as far as the hydraulics are concerned, since the clutch
is fed by a separate line from the reservoir.  BTW, to contradict what
Barry said (sorry!), you can get a 7 mm wrench on the bleeder w/out
removing the starter.  However, there's a large, unfused +12 line going
to the starter -- be careful or disconnect the battery first.

> Second yesterday I had ALL of the belts replaced on my '84 944 and 
> when I got it back it now has a high pitched "whur" to it when I 
> drive, not so much at idel but I sounds like a cake mixer right about 
> 2000rpm.  Is this normal for new belts that just need to be broken in 
> or have they over tightened them and I need to take it back.  

Sometimes new belts will make this noise for a while, sometimes they
will make it forever.  Be sure that it's the belt, and not a bad roller or
overtensioned belt; get your mechanic to give it a listen if you're not
sure.
