Take  a long drive in both before you make up your mind.

> 
> What are the pros and cons of these two cars compared to each other?

I went thru the same decision process before I bought my S2. Depends what 
you want to use the car for. For a daily driver in the city I prefer the 
low end torque of the S2. But in Germany on the autobahn I would prefer 
the Turbo. For racing in autocross S2 is clearly superior, for road race 
tracks go Turbo.

> Which of these cars is more susceptible to wear (Average maintenance
> costs)? (I expect to use this car as a daily driver)

IMHO S2 is cheaper to maintain than the Turbo and less susceptible to 
engine wear. If you want a sporty ride/suspension and will be doing any 
competition or driving schools be sure the car has limited slip 
differential (option M220) and Sport chassis (M030), both are options on 
the S2 and standard on the Turbo.
