     I have removed them using the method I described and it doesn't take 
     too long.  The point is, not to have to use a tool (vise grips) that 
     was never intended for that job.  The vise grips do not fit well, can 
     cause further damage to the rubber boots and associated hardware, and 
     not to mention damage to flesh and bone when they slip.  Many a fine 
     mechanic has uttered words not sociably correct under that kind of 
     duress.
     
     If you have taken all the time to clean and pre-lube everything else, 
     go the remaining step and get it right.  Besides, those bolts should 
     not be too tight anyway, but play it safe.
