What is the magic trick to successfully bleeding all the air out of the
clutch hydraulics?

I have tried every thing I can think of except turning the car upside down
to get the air out of the system.  This includes raising the car up at an
angle (the feline position - butt up); pre loading the new slave cylinder
with fluid; use of a vacuum device with the clutch pedal up; using a vacuum
device with the pedal down; pumping the pedal, then holding it down,
release bleed screw, watch bubbles, tighten, repeat sequence, repeat
sequence, repeat sequence, repeat sequence, repeat sequence...

I have replaced both the master and the slave with new components (both
leaked) and the system still seems to have air in it (clutch pedal will go
limp over night and requires pumping to get it to work at all).  I have
dumped about 4 - 6 pints of fluid through this system.  First to get the
master bled, then I replaced the slave after it leaked.

The only thing I have not tried is to force fluid (via pressure) through
the fluid fill tank, but a vacuum should, in theory, do the same.

Any tips?
