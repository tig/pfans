Subject:Belt Tension: 4.0 versus 5.7
Date:Fri, 27 Sep 1996 20:46:38 -0700
OK everybody.  I know we've all been through this, but the local Porsche
Mechanic insists that the latest tech bulletin states that new belt tension
is 5.7  --WAIT WAIT WAIT-- but WITHOUT doing this rotating backwards 0-10
degree thingy.  I did a little informal test and-- get this-- a belt
tensioned to 5.0 without reverse rotation measures about 3.7 once you do
this reverse rotation thing.  In other words, if you do it to 5.7 without
reverse rotation, it should work out to be roughly 4.0.  Next time someone
changes a belt, let us know if you can confirm this.

Also, I know that this 0-10 degree thing is supposed to have a reputable
source, but I still think it's just plain WEIRD.  I mean, 0-10 degrees is
quite a bit of crankshaft rotation and can introduce a variety of readings
in the belt, especially considering the narrow range of the tension spec.
