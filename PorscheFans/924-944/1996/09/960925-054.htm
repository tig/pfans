My 86 951 had a SACH's clutch in it as standard equipment.  The clutch plate 
had a steel center, four or five springs, and I shattered the hub on a normal
shift from 3rd to 4th at Sebring under full acceleration (except during the
shift).  Rubber centered clutches were in 944s, but not 951s.  Porsche split
the cost of the repair under warrantee even though track driving was disclosed
and clutches were specifically excluded from warrantee.  That led me to
believe there was a manufacturing defect.

During that service discussion, slipping the clutch was the number 1 way to
ruin it.  The material separates from the clutch disk.  Apparently a number of
people had tried that approach.  I was given the impression that just one time
could do it.

It cost about $1800 to fix in 1987.
