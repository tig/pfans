>I also own an '89 951 which I have been autocrossing within our local club.
>The club has adopted a new rolling start philosophy in an attempt to lessen
>the wear and tear on driveline components.  This usually consists of a small
>diameter skidpad with the start timer at the exit of the skippad.  This
>approach makes it unnecessary to launch a car because the timing does not
>start until crossing the timing line.  Cones in the skidpad do count however
>and you must stay on course.  For a Turbo driver this makes a big difference
>in times.  The lack of low end torque with the Turbos makes them less than
>ideal autocrossers against the 944 S2s and 968 that I typically compete
>against locally.  Case in point:  Of the 5 or 6 autocrosses I did this year,
>I finsished 1st or 2nd with the rolling start, but no better than 3rd
>starting from a standstill.  Maybe you guys in the Great White North should
>look into adopting similar rules, but it may put some Porsche mechanics out
>of business.  Good Luck, have fun and may your clutches last forever.
>

I autocross my 87 951 in the Suncoast Region in Florida. The track we
normally use is MacDill Air Force Base. Our autocrosses are usually on
*large* tracks, but we always use a flying start. The timing equipment is
probably 30 to 50 yards from the launch point. IMHO, it is the only way to
go for saving clutches.
