Hi Ken:

I'm not an expert on this chemistry but, last month, I chaired a conference 
here in Tuscaloosa, AL, and one of the speakers gave an extensive 
presentation on long-life coolants for industrial applications.  I recall 
his stating that the phosphate additives break down quite quickly, causing a 
loss of corrosion protection in about a year.  He said the greatest worry 
was with aluminum parts.

Well, even though I've been following the phosphate-free advice, this 
presentation confirmed it was likely the best thing to do.  Regardless, 
you're probably correct that annual replacement of "regular" coolant is 
safe.

I found that Walmart carries Prestone phosphate-free here in the States, and 
this is now my source.  I don't know about BC (Canada).
