>A question. I just got a 944 S2 that is very persistent about letting me
>know when the oil level drops 60% of the level from max to min on the
>dipstick.  Appropriate lights in instrument panel light up. I was curious
>as to what sensor was involved. The oil pressure is at max when it lights
>up, so I assume it's not the oil pressure sensor.

Why, the oil level sensor of course!  It was slip-streamed into 944 production
during 1987.  In fact, there was a tech bulletin warning dealers that they
will see cars with various incomplete bits of the circuit, that that
was normal, and that PCNA did not consider finishing the implementation to
be a warranty matter!

The sensor is installed into the right-hand side of the oil pan.  It has
a triangular mounting plate with three M5 bolts, and a large yellow wire
to its single connector.
