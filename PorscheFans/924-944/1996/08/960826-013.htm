At 12:06 PM 8/22/96 -0400, Doug Donsbach wrote:
>Given that the triple-square or "cheesehead" bolt in the end of the
>camshaft is somewhat difficult to remove, would it be bad thing to replace
>it with a normal allen bolt?
>
>It is not the normal 8.8 grade you find all over the car, but 10.9. Maybe
>this would be difficult to find in an allen fastener?
>
>I already sourced the new triple-square bolt, but was just thinking that
>maybe I could make it a little easier if I need to get it apart again...

My understanding is that you are at least as likely to tear up the socket
head of a hex allen as a triple-square, insofar as it is only a question
of design strength.  I don't think that it is quite true that the head always
strips on removal, and I believe that if you treat them as single-use parts,
you're going to generally be OK.

If you do want to go with an allen, make sure the grip and thread lengths
match the original part, as well as material strength matching (10.9).
