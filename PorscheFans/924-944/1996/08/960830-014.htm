Does the car start to smoke when the power falls off?

It sounds like you may have an intake air leak.  When the boost hits .2BAR,
one of the fittings or bellows make begin to leak air causing the engine to
go rich.  Usually you will lose power and begin to smoke (amount of exhaust
smoke depends on the size of the leak).  Check all air intake fittings and
hoses between the air flow meter and the intake manifold.  The rubber
bellows will tend to deform slightly over time causing a slight leak at the
area with the hose clamp.  The added pressure from the turbo will aggravate
the leak.  The injection system meters the fuel based upon measured airflow.
If that air leaks out before making it into the engine, the engine will go
rich.   The rich condition will reduce combustion efficiency, providing less
force on the turbo and possibly dumping unburnt fuel into the exhaust.  The
car will run reasonably well off boost unless the intake leak becomes very
bad.
