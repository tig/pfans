If I remember things right:
Your clock has 4 wires: +12V continuously (otherwise the clock would
stop when not                                              driving the
car)
			Ground
			+12V from the ignition-switch to light the light.
			+12V for detecting that the you switched on the lights
			     It dims the light of your clock in the dark.

Why your clock is blank? I don't know. But it could be the rubber
connector between the display and the printed circuitboard. You may try
cleaning it with some tapehead cleaner. (video-head cleaner)
In my clock there's missing one piece of the most right digit.
I've had the clock apart once, but I cannot find the cause. Sometimes
though, when it is very cold outside, the part is working. It quits the
job when the interior heats up.
