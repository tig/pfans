Someone asked about Dual Points or Distributors. Dual points first. The
theory is that by having the timing slightly off you get an overlap
during firing that makes the switched pulse longer, especially at high
rpm. This improves the ignition energy availble to fire off the mixture
(increased dwell)

Dual spark does the same basic thing, though twp spark plugs and two
coils firing them give better flame in the combustion chamber. The
hemi-head of the four cam and later 911 engines neext a lot of spark to
get the mixture fully burned, especially under lean conditions. This
also reduces emissions. 

Both add mid-range and top-end power. Most DME applications use altered
pulse width to do the same thing. MSD uses a higher frequency (about
10X) to charge the coil and produce a higher energy spark pulse when
turned on.
