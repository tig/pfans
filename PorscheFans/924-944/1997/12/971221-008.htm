Marshall,
One other CRITICAL element to successfully bleeding the clutch, besides
getting the back of the car way up in the air, is to keep the fluid reservoir
topped up while bleeding.  You'll notice that the hose from the reservoir to
the clutch master cylinder is mounted high on the side of the reservoir (it's
a safety feature -- prevents a clutch system hydraulic leak from draining all
the fluid from the brake system).  If you have two people to assist you, one
can keep it full while one pumps the pedal (pulling it up by hand) and one
bleeds (that would be the one under the car tearing up knuckles on the starter
motor!).  With only two people doing it, you'll need to stop every few cycles
to refill the reservoir.  By the way, with respect to the MityVac, I've found
it very usefull for purging fluid, but less effective than the old "pump-the-
pedal" method for final bleeding.  Others have had more success with it.
