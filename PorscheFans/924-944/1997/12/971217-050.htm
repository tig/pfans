I second the S2 idea, M.R.  I went through the same thought process this
summer and decided the S2 was the way to go.  With the supercharger, you will
need to upgrade suspension and brakes to have a balanced package.  By the time
you do all this, plus rebuild the engine, you will have likely invested more
than a nice S2 will cost.  Also--from my years of drag racing 5.0 Mustangs,  I
learned a lot about superchargers, both Paxtons and Vortechs.  They are far
from simple bolt ons, and have a tendency to blow things like head gaskets
with regularity.

Now, good luck finding a good S2--they're kinda rare.
