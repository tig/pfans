When I get a 951 in the shop that does not boost, I first listgen to the
engine for rough running and give the engine a visual glance to see if
any lines have become unplugged, blown off, etc.  The next thing I
usually do is clamp (I use a fuel line clamp, but there are other ways)
off the line from the cycling valve to the wastegate and drive the car. 
If the car has a DME related problem (bad cycling valve, Control Units,
knock sensor, etc.), they will then be out of the loop of problems.  If
the car then boosts ok (actually, it should overboost, so be careful in
driving...use common sense.), then it narrows my search to troubleshoot
and find the problem.

If the car still does not boost correctly, then you look for a
mechanical problem with the turbo, the wastegate sticking open, a leak
in the intake system (exhaust system leaks should be apparent), os some
other mechanical problem.

Yes, I do realize that it could still be a fuel problem, etc...but
generally, this little test will normally put you in the right
direction.
