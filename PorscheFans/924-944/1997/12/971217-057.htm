 Hey P-Car fans!
Merry Christmas  to an interesting bunch of guys ( and Roxanne !) One thing to
keep in mind when trying to decide if furnishing your own parts is a good
idea. If you furnish a mechanic with defective or wrong parts, you can't
expect him to remove and replace them for the original labor charge. I saved
about $20 each on turbo motor mounts and had my guy install them( $200 labor)
Two weeks later one went out. Automotion replaced them @ no charge but  I had
the labor to pay again. If the shop had furnished them, there would have been
no additional charge. I guess what I'm saying is ... Weigh the amount of money
saved against money risked. Also , this may sound obvious, but find out in
advance what you will be charged for a part.  Because of my previous experence
with the motor mounts, I let my mechanic supply the parts for the repair of my
automatic transmission (rubber dampener). I assumed that because I could get
one for 3-4 hundred dollars that he could ( and would)also. WRONG! $800 wrong!
 Due to this and his keeping my car 7 weeks!! ( never say "Take your time") I
have a new mechanic. I also do many little things myself. I'm lucky in that I
have acess to a complete shop, Lifts, strut compressors, Hunter  infa-red
alignment machine, etc. but 12 hours is the longest time I can tie up a stall.
