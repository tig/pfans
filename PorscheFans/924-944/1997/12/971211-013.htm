The stock compression is about 1/2 point shy of spec. That explains how these
cars could have basically the same Motronic system as the earlier 2.5 8 valve
engines with no knock sensors.  To up the compression, I had .006 taken off
the deck and the head was shaved .020.  The retarded timing was corrected with
a adjustable cam sprocket. I calculated 11:.2 compression.  Almost a full
point from where I started on this motor.  I feel the small, stock airflow
sensor is the major impediment to making power on this engine. 
 
>> What octane
did you have to use? And the airflow sensor --  replaced with a hot-wire
sensor? Sounds interesting.  Can't recall if that model had knock sensors.
How long did the engine last?<<

This is a dedicated track car that runs race gas. The MoTec engine management
system does not use an airflow sensor. The throttle body goes straight to the
air cleaner. It is a speed density engine management system that allows the
fuel/ignition to be mapped via laptop computer.

>>How long did the engine last?<<

3 years and still going strong.  This car has proven very reliable, with the
exception of a mis-shift (3rd instead of 5th), which bent all the valves, no
engine failures.  The car runs competitively with non-chipped 944 turbo's and
911 SC's.  In fact, friday a group of us are testing this car and some other
944/951 racecars at our local racetrack, Willow Springs Raceway.  Kind of a
pre-holiday speed fix.
