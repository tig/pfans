Dear Ray:
        I see Marv answered most of your questions.   Just for reference,
though, here are more data.  I have not had much of a chance lately to
really open up my car ('89 951, 75k miles) on the open road, and as a
result am only using about 1/3 quart every thousand miles, including a
minor leak waiting to be fixed. A buddy of mine with another '89 Turbo
(55k) lives out on the windward side of Oahu and has more chances for fun;
he uses almost a quart per thousand miles. This, I believe is quite normal
if you hit the boost often.
        My oil pressure gauge drops to about 3.5 at idle. I am using Amsoil
20W50 synthetic.
        In several recent articles in Excellence magazine, it is
recommended that a quality synthetic be used in turbocharged 944s because
of the high engine temparatures.  In Honolulu, because of the climate and
heavy traffic, I change the oil and filter every 3,000 miles to be safe.
Seems horrifically wasteful of oil, but hey, engine and turbo rebuilds can
be hell on the wallet.

Best wishes,
