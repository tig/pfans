	Check your Spark Plug Wires for the missfire. Take some glass cleaner or wet
them down with an old sprayer bottle. Look closely to the inner hood foam -
usually the foam loosens here and the spark plug wires touch and arch or
ground out there. It helps if you wet down the plugs in total darkness - ei at
night. If the tach needle stays steady when you feel the "miss" then it
usually a primary ignition side of things. But - If the "miss" is felt and the
tach needle move down slightly with the miss - it is usually caused by a fuel
problem. ie: spray pattern, carbon build up ect... Do not forget to check the
"set screw" that holds down the rotor to the distributor shaft - you should
use low strength loctite (Blue) on the set screw's threads.
