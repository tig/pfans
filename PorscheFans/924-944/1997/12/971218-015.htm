>I second the S2 idea, M.R.  I went through the same thought process this
>summer and decided the S2 was the way to go.  With the supercharger, you will
>need to upgrade suspension and brakes to have a balanced package.  By the time
>you do all this, plus rebuild the engine, you will have likely invested more
>than a nice S2 will cost.  Also--from my years of drag racing 5.0 Mustangs,  I
>learned a lot about superchargers, both Paxtons and Vortechs.  They are far
>from simple bolt ons, and have a tendency to blow things like head gaskets
>with regularity.

Just to back up Terry's comments, I did blow a head gasket on my
supercharged 944 at high speed, which resulted in destroying a couple a
cylinder walls.  If you want to supercharge, you should go into it from the
perspective that you are essentially developing a new engine, and be
prepared to make all components work together.  The supercharger may be
relatively easy to bolt on, but that's only the beginning of the process.
Wish I had been aware of that when I embarked on that fateful journey into
uncharted territory.
