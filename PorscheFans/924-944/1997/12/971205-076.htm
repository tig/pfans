Beware!

The speedo's on the three hole cars (early 944, and all 924) are prone to
odometer failures. I have seen more that a few of these cars where the
odometer has stopped working. The nylon drive gear for the number wheels
strips, or cracks, rendering the odometer inoperative.  The speedo still works
fine. 

I would not necessaryily skip this car, I would just be wary of the claimed
actual mileage. Maybe service records will prove/disprove the low mileage. 

As you should do before considering any Porsche purchase, have a
disinterested, qualified third party repair facility perform a pre-purchase
inspection. 

These cars (924S) are good values and get you a late 944 drivetrain at bargain
basement prices. . If you can verify the low mileage is correct, go for it!
Low mileage is good mileage.

Good luck!
