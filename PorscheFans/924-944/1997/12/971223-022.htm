Always looking for a bit more power for the "I" stock club racer, I queried
some 944 ITS folks about taking the balance shaft belt off and running
without......they agreed that there was an increase in performance as they
had run this way for a while.  They put the belt back on because they
started cracking fuel rails and couldn't keep a starter bolted in.   These
were the most obvious prooblems from running without the belt.  They figured
the problems caused were not worth the power gained.......

Hello Jay & P-Fans,
	When you performed the compression test, what was the reading on the first
compression stroke? Did your gauge read 180 psi on the first compression
stroke? Or did the gauge start out low and then pump up to the high reading of
180 psi. 
	ei: 140 - 160 - 170 - 175 - then 180 psi ? If your engine takes several
compression stokes to get to the highest reading - that means your rings are
worn! But if the readings start out high ( say 170, then go up to 180 psi)
that would indicate the rings are ok. You can always add a say 8 oz. of oil to
the cylinder before doing a compression test and see if the compression
readings start out higher than the previous readings.
