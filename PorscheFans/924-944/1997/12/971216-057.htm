Next, the facts on the Chevy are, the V-8 weighs in at 505 pounds,
versus the 310 for a 924, 360 for a 924Turbo/944NA and 410 for the 951
engines. This can be compensated by upping the fron springs to 250
pounds at a ride height equal to delivered, not lowered. True the weight
distribution is now 55/45. Still better than one would expect. I would
highly recommend the 944 body as a starting point, if only for the
reason to have enough space for wheels and tires required to get it all
to the ground. The Chevy LT-1 engine with all the stock FI will fit in ,
though there isn't much room for twiddling around under the hood.

But, if you really want a Porsche with a V-8, either buy a 928 or wait 2
years or so.
