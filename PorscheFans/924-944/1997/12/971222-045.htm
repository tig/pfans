Hello P-Fans
	You wrote:  Now the car has a very strange cut off. Whilst driving the engine
cuts of above 4000rpm in all the gears. Having the car in no gear and reving
the engine it goes all the way to 6500rpm where the rpm limiter cuts in.
Any suggestions what may be the problem?
	I believe if you can rev the engine above 4K rpm's not moving but the engine
does not rev above 4K in gear - you have a ignition problem. I would suspect
on the primary side of the ignition. If your problem was fuel - I do not think
the engine would rev above 4K all the time. Fuel flow is fuel flow wether it
is at idle not moving or in gear.
	I would say that the primary side of the ignition is the culprit. The energy
from the coil is either not getting to the spark plug (ie: distb cap & rotor,
plug wires worn, coil wire shot ect...) or that the coil is not producing
enough power or voltage at high RPM's. The fuel is putting out the flame or
spark under load - this is why the engine will rev past 4K while not under
full load. 
	I would check the resistance of the plug wires and the coil wire. If the plug
wires have more than 5K ohms and the coil wire should have no more than 1K
ohms - if there is higher resistance replace the wires. Check the cap & rotor.
Look for carbon tracing on the inside and outside of the distb cap.  Last I
would test the coil - there is a way to check the resistance along the #1 &
#15 terminal - but I do not have the specs, look in your shop manual. 
	I do nat think your problem is too hard to pin point just keep at it and let
us know what you find. 

							Good Luck, DavidJalai@aol
