Happy Holidays, all.

My holiday project was replacing the clutch in my '88 944S.  It took about 
40 hours (good thing I'm on vacation) which I guess wasn't too bad since it 
was my first time to replace a Porsche clutch.  It definitely had it's 
moments of frustration and panic.
I replaced the old clutch w/a Sachs spring-centered one & honestly the new 
one is much smoother than the old one contrary to what I thought would 
happen.

In the last 2 months of the old clutch's life I would get clunking and 
whirring noises.  The clunking would occur when I let out the clutch. 
 After examining the old clutch plate I understand that the clunking was 
caused by the plate essentially functioning as 2 separate pieces.  The 
rubber center had completely separated and was missing a 3 1/2" chunk which 
caused some "slack".
On to the whirring noise.  Whenever I would let off the throttle in the mid 
to upper RPM range I would get a whirring sound from the transmission.  The 
noise sounded like dry, rattling gears & several people on this list 
suggested to me that it might be a bad bearing in the tranny.  However, 
when I took it to the dealer for an expert diagnosis they said the whirring 
would go away when I replaced the clutch.  Sure enough it did, but I don't 
understand why.  I was pretty sold on the bad bearing idea & I cannot 
figure out why a separated clutch plate would cause such a noise.  Could 
someone please explain?

By the way.  Does anyone have a good use for a used pressure plate?  As far 
as I can tell the old one is in great shape but I replaced it as a matter 
of course.

Thanks
dave randolph.
