Although I autocrossed both cars, my track driving was mostly 911.  In auto-x,
the 911 was great in tight turns because you could use all that mass to swing
the back end around.  Also, in slaloms, you could lift on turn in to bring the
tail out quite nicely.

For predictability and stability, the 944 wins hands down, at least at my
skill level (which is intermediate).  In a 911, once you commit to the line
through the turn, you'd best not try to change it, unless you enjoy travelling
backwards at high speed (I tried this: a guaranteed Immodium moment.)  The 944
will forgive errors far beyond the point at which the 911 is uncontrollable,
though it will also be happy to spin if you run out of ideas and talent at the
same time.

 My non-spoilered '88 911 was very scary on bumpy track at 135+.  It also ran
out of brakes pretty quick at Seattle International Raceway.  I now have a
944S2, which has big brakes, and am eager to see how they do.  My guess is
that I'll be quite a bit quicker in the S2 because of the increased
comfort/control level.

All that said, there is nothing quite like the sound of the 911 engine at
redline in third gear coming out of turn two at SIR, holding on for dear life
until the car settles down enough to grab fourth and shoot off down the hill
toward the hairpin at 3A. . . . 

Bottom line:  I love 'em all.
