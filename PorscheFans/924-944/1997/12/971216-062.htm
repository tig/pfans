Yes, there are all kinds of tools for this application, and you can 
spend all kinds of money.  If pads are sufficiently worn, I generally 
find that using the old pad (ie, its backing plate) with a large 
screwdriver or small pry bar works well and does not damage the piston 
or seals.  Before moving the piston back, though, clean the seals and 
piston with rubbing alcohol or whatever.
