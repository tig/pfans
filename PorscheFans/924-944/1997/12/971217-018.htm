>the engine it goes all the way to 6500rpm where the rpm limiter cuts in.
>Any suggestions what may be the problem?
 
The first thing I'd check is the boost pressure sensor.  It's screwed in
to the front of the pressure manifold (metal pipe to throttle body).  It
serves its function as a ground cutoff for the fuel pump relay.  You can
disconnect the wire and ground it to the block, then load the engine by
driving around on boost.  If it works fine, the sensor's your problem-or
your car is overboosting.  The sensor is supposed to open the ground at
something like 15 psi.
