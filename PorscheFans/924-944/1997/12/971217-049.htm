>This is an incorrect analogy. I mentioned myself:
> > If the sum of vertical forces on the wheels is decreased -- such 
> > as when the frame of the car is going up on a hoist -- negative 
> > camber will also decrease rapidly.
	
which is exactly the same as your analogy of jacking up a car, which
will of course result in decreasing negative camber. However lifting a
car by the wheels and lifting it by the frame are nowhere near similar.
Try putting a couple of jacks under the wheels and lift, and see what
happens to the camber! That is the correct analogy to increasing
pressure in a shock absorber, and will lead to increased negative
camber.
