 On the turbo's I have been replacing that puzzling extra radiator that the
intake charge is forced to go through with a nice piece of PVC pipe. Way less
restriction then that stupid and heavy radiator. I'm sure it's at least 75hp.
I Can't understand why Porsche left that one on the table? Maybe an attempt to

 Farrazan is right about the normally aspirated cars, it's though going to get
more than a modest gain on these cars. My experience,  like Farrazan's, is
supported with dyno time.  I find that establishing a baseline is sometimes
more relevant than actually finding more horsepower. "Seat of the pants" is
deceiving... 
 
 The best improvement I have seem on a non-turbo 944 engine was a very high
compression 2.7 8 valve with Motec engine management.  That car came in at
just over 190 horsepower at the rear wheel. I attribute the gain to high
compression and removal of the restrictive airflow sensor.   
 
 A fellow racer with a 968 engined 1983 944 got 231 hp on the same dyno. We
dicked around with three chips, crazy fuel pressure, and big exhaust to gain
22 horsepower.  We did manage to flatten the powerband by adjusting the fuel
pressure. The DynoJet dyno we use has a very fast reacting $4000 Motec lamda
sensor that we screwed into the stock location to read O2 content. 
 
 On all these runs we assume a very conservative 10% drivetrain loss.
