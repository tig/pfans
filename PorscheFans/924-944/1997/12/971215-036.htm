I found a low tech, 0 damage way of spreading the pistons apart when
changing calipers. Get one of those free paint stirring sticks from the
paint store, break into 1 to 2 inch lengths, insert at least two, or
however many will fit between the new, or old pads, or the pad and rotor,
depending on how your changing pads.( some try to pull the pads out the
top, some loosen one caliper bolt and pull them out the bottom) 	
 Take a screwdriver with a large flat head, insert between two of the
peices of wood, and twist 90 degrees. As the pads seperate, insert more
peices of wood and repeat until you acheive proper seperation. The wood
keeps the rotors, pads, and pistons from getting damaged. I know it sounds
kind of hokey, but it gives great leverage for spreading the pads, doesnt
put sideways torque on any parts, is free, and causes no damage. Just try
not to splinter wood to much.
