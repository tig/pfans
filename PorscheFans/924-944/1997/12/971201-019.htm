I have made polyurethane swaybar bushings for all locations on the front
sway bar on both a na & 951 for several size bars. Measure OD of bar and
convert to Inches. Grab your handy JC Whitney Cat. and find their
bushings. Order the ID size closest to but larger than your bar. They
are easily "turned down" on a bench grinder to any shape you need. Don't
go smaller than the bar OD on the bushing as it is difficult to grind it
out and it might bind. Also, I have sawed groves in the inside of the
bushings to allow teflon spray lube into the bar/bushing surface because
they can squeak. Be ready for a big pile of pink dust.
