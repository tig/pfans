I'm a bit late responding to this but..  Try a product called Dry Z Air. 
It is sold in home improvement stores for removing moisture from closets
and the other small damp spaces in a house.  Just set it on the floor with
the car sealed up tight, and it should suck all the moisture out while the
car is parked.  Be prepared to pour the collected water out on a regular
basis.  I've never tried this, but hopefully it should bring the humitity 
down enough in the car so your guages clear up.

This is a great item for cars that are stored for extended periods as 
well.. keeps mustiness, mold and corrosion at bay.
