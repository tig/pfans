The Porsche owner's manual specifically prohibits the use of any oil
additives.  Put nothing into your crankcase except motor oil.  Motor oils
already come with a complex additive mix included at no extra charge. 
Additives in a bottle provide no benefit except to suck money out of your
wallet, and can possibly interfere with the additive package already in
your oil. 

If you want to improve your lubrication, you can try synthetic oil.  It 
has excellent flow characteristics when cold, and better shear resistance 
when hot, as compared to natural 'dino' oil.

I prolong my engine's life by changing the oil and filter every 3,000 miles, 
in defiance of Porsche's recommendation of every 15,000 miles (!) (7,500 
miles for the 951). Of course, this assumes 'non-severe' service.

Even after 98,500 miles, the engine oil is still transluscent when I drain 
it, and I have no oil consumption between changes, FWIF.  No track or 
autoX driving, however.
