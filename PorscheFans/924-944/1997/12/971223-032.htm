>How can bad valve seals adversely affect compression? They're off in
>the intake and exhaust portions of the head, not in the compression
>chamber. I think what everybody means here is the valve seats, correct?
>
>Namely, if you pressurize the cylinder at TDC, if you hear air hissing in
>the air filter box, your intake valve is not seating properly, and if you hear
>air hissing in the tailpipe your exhaust valve is not seating properly.

Sounds logical to me, Vaughan.  Assuming even if a cylinder was pressurized
off TDC, and you were forcing air past the guides or seals, where would you
listen for it? 

What happens if you're forcing air into the crankcase, either past the
rings or valve stems? Would any make it into the crankcase ventilation
system?  If so, then pressurization at TDC might result in the sound of air
flow at the air filter box (or wherever it vents), even if there were no
sound in the intake manifold.  

The bottom line I'm getting is a leakdown can only tell you whether the
seating of your intake or exhaust valves are bad.
