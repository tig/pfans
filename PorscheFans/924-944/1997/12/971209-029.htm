Gary,

it would seem that the trick to the perfect alignment (or at least what has
worked for me) is to loosen the 10mm (I think) nuts that hold the release
widgets to the body.  Make sure they move freely, but arent "floppy" (ie-
loosen a turn or two, so they can slide around of their own accord) and
then lower the hatch  gently a few times to make the mechanism locate
properly; don't make them engage, just lower the pins enough to move
catches them to the proper spots. Lower the hatch a final time, going as
far down as possible without engaging the locking feature (to get maximum
lineup effect) and then crawl inside and tighten the nuts.  Also, it is
fairly important to have both sides sharing the job of holding your hatch
down to avoid rattles (as much as is possible anyhow). My $0.02.
