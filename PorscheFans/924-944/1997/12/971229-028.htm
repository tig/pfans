>1. How do solid engine mounts affect the 944T.  In my Talon, you had to

IMHO, not as much as you would feel with a FWD-like chassis setup of the
Talon. In FWD sideways-mounted engine cars, solid poly mounts get rid of a
lot of, well, driveline slop. In a 924/44, there just isn't that much
drivetrain "windup," but if you wanted to kill it, solidify the entire
driveline, the rear axle mounts and the motor mounts. I suspect doing the
usually recommended 944 suspension bushing work would prove more useful for
lap times.

I have poly mounts on the Swift and it makes a world of difference. I also
can't drive the car longer than two hours without becoming jello. I've been
in a 944 with aluminium mounts and just plain would not consider them
unless they were the "unobtanium" advantage (which I doubt unless you had a
tied-to-suspension mounts roll-cage put in). People can go on and on about
how smooth the 944 motor feels, but with a solidly mounted drivetrain it
feels to me like the whole car is going to vibrate apart. (Then again,
there are those that feel a racecar should feel like a racecar... :-)

carbon fibre can you afford? :-)

Just pull the axles, repack the CV joints, and install the axles on the
opposite sides.  Make sure the outer joints stay on the outside.  The spacer
or covering or something is slightly different sized between the inside and
outside, but I've heard people say they've put them in backwards with no ill
effect.

If you drive the car in and tell them to repack the CV joints and swap the
axles (the swapping axle thing should have NO effect on the price.  They're
out anyway.) I'm GUESSING at probably $100-$200, and I'm posting this to the
list instead of just responding to you to see if anyone has a better idea on
the cost.  I pulled mine off and brought them in and let my mechanic repack
them.   If I would have had a few extra hours, and lots more patience, I might
have repacked them myself.
