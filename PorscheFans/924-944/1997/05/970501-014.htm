Sometime I'll get around to upgrading the shocks in my S2.
I wanted to get some info while the following question is
fresh in my mind.  My understanding is that shocks should
affect transient behavior rather than steady state.
Hence they figure in response during turn-in and
turn-out.  Does stiffening the back shocks promote
quicker turn-in (i.e. fight the initial understeer
involved in getting the car to rotate)?

Further, in getting the car to rotate - say to the right -
the front tires must generate force to the right, but the
rears must initially generate a force to the left, i.e. opposite
the direction of the cornering force they'll play when the
car is in steady state in the turn.  How does one drive to get
through the transition quickly?
