For the type of r-d I described, yes.  But read further.

Okay, there are two types of receiver-driers used in the industry (not
sure about cars specifically though).  There is a r-d that can be used in
the liquid line side (between the condensor coil and evaporator coil) or
the suction side (gas side) between the evaporator coil and the
compressor.  Now that I think about it some more, the one most prevelant
in car systems is probably the one between the condensor and evaporator
coils.  I say this because most car manuals talk about looking into the
sight glass and seeing if you see bubbles (gas) present in the liquid
line.  If so, most likely you are low on refrigerant.
