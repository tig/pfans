My original Blaupunkt cassette/radio does the same thing.  I have narrowed it
down to 2 problems that I was able to find:

1. bad cassette tape, or the tape is wound too tight - try another tape, or
   put the "bad" cassette in a home stereo and fast forward and fast rewind
   it all the way to the end a couple of times (to loosen up the tape a little)

2. excessive humidity or condensation/dew formation on the cassette tape head
   (causes tape to eject automatically) - run AC to dry up the air in the
   cabin or wipe the tape heads, rollers, etc, with liquid freon if available
   or just regular rubbing alcohol
