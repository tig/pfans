Blueprinting:
Blueprinting as I understand it, is the specifications that the engine was
originally designed to.  In the real world, these specifications sometimes
change due to manufacturing constraints and costs.  A case in point is the
intake ports on the head and manifold.  In accordance with the blue print,
they should match the gasket.  In reality, Porsche included, they do not. 
Hence, we have a cottage industry doing porting and polishing, port
matching and flow bench porting.  How much good it does probably shows up
better on a drag strip than a track but I still vote for blueprinting.

$troker:
For those of you who asked, my explodeo 951 is on the road to recovery. 
The only good part about this is it is being fixed under warranty.   I'll
write a detailed post shortly.  In a nut shell, the headgasket blew at
about 7000 rpm (big oil slick on the track).  It vented into the oil galley
rather than the usual water jacket.  The welch (oil galley) plug blew out
of the back of the block.  The handy dandy Accusump helped pump about 13
quarts of oil onto the exhaust pipe and tires.  A quick spin and the engine
was saved with two quarts left in the pan.  Tech Tip:  The experimental
machined o'ring on the cylinder liners appears to be no bueno as a fix.  To
date the head has been machined and stainless o'rings installed.  The stock
oil galley plug has been replaced with a threaded plug.  The freeze plug in
the back of the head has been replaced with a 90 degree fitting which runs
to the water pump for increased flow to #4.  If the new custom copper head
gasket arrives next week, I plan to run the red beast at an auto-x on the
18th.  No,
I'm not getting any smarter.
