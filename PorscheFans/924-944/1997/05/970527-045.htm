Sounds like it might be brake fluid leaking out of the clutch slave
cylinder.  Look underneath at the bell housing and see if you see any
drips on anything.  Slave sylinder leaks are quite common, but not a
difficult thing to repair.

If you have not had your belts replace before, it dfefinitely should be
done.  The 968 manual says to replace at 60K (tech bulletin Book H, page
12), but if I owned a 944S or 944S2 or 968, I would replace them at
30,000 just like a 944.  The belt does indeed have more surface area,
but on your car--which is 10 years old--IF it has not been done--do it.

Labor in the Mitchel manual shows 3.8 hrs.  The Porsche microfiche shows
2.8 hrs.  Be sure that the balance belt is replaced at the same time (no
additional labor time), and check for oil leaks at the cam, balance
shafts, and crank seals (additional time if needed).
