>>BTW, Matt, do you know what the thermodynamic purpose of the drier is?
>>I've forgotten it, if I ever knew...
>
>The purpose of the drier is to trap any non-gas refrigerant and any water that
>may be in the system.  Since it sits between the condensor and the evaporator,
>it's the last stop before the passenger compartment.  If any liquid
>refrigerant
>were to make it to the evaporator (where it expands, getting even colder), it
>could possibly freeze, blocking the evaporator and effectively damping the
>cooling process.  Another factor is efficiency since this whole system is
>based
>on the heat of transformation (conversion from liquid to gas and back),
>having
>liquid already in the system would reduce the amout of cooling power.

In my last post, I mistakenly stated that the drier blocks non-gaseous
refrigerant, but after some consideration, this doesn't make sense-- the
compressor forces the refrigerant from gas to liquid and then lets it
expand back to a gas form in the evaporator inside the passenger
compartment.  That only leaves the water moisture issue for the
receiver-drier to handle.  Les Hatton also caught this error (thanks!).
Sorry for the confusion.
