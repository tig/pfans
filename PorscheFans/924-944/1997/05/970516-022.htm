There are several reasons for the BMW to have higher specific output
than a 968. One of the most basic is that it is a 6 cylinder. Each of
those 6 cylinders is smaller than the Porsche, wich give less 'pumping
losses'. The BMW, as a 6 cylinder does not need the two balance shafts
to counteract the 4 cylinders vibration - and those balance shaft rob
the engine for 4 HP in addition to the vibration losses itself. Then the
BMW use a different system for air intake, with (if I remember rightly)
have one throttle per cylinder instead of one throttle servicing 4
cylinders. Add the variable exhaust cam drive on the 3.2 and see the
specific output go even higher. I think the BMW:s revv higher than 6800,
too - but I dont know.  =


Some 'facts': Specific output for the 968 is around 80. For the BMW 3.0
it will be 95 and for the 3.2 around 108. The current top value for a NA
(this nice acronym...) may be from Ferrari, with 116 HP on a current 8
cylinder model. In the european 'standard' car racing series 2 litre 4
cyl engines have around 280-290 HP available, so there is no *real*
reason that the 968 cannot be made more powerful. =


Any noticeable power upgrade will require major hardware modif=EDcations
and a new DME chip too.
