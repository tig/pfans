> If the camshaft TDC marks are lined up with the marks on the camshaft
> cover, the clutch housing marks are not necessarily lined up correctly? I
> mean, do the camshaft marks line up more frequently than the clutch housing
> or is one rotation of the camshaft = one rotation of the engine and all of
> its associated  parts?
> 
The deal on that a precise and accurate setting is easier to see on the
flywheel than the cam sprocket. This is mostly because there is a heck of
a lot of ambiguity in the sprocket alignment marks because they are fairly
wide. Since the engine makes two revs to each camshaft rev the width of
those marks get multiplied by two at the flywheel. Have someone turn the
engine (or turn it yourself if you can) while you watch the flywheel marks
line up. Then look at the cam marks while you turn it a little more. The
cam marks will still be lined up but the flywheel mark will be long gone.
>
