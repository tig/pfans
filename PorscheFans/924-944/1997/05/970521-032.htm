Just about all these questions are answered in your owner's manual.  Did 
you get one?  If not, you really must get one.

You should read the 944 FAQ (Mandatory for all 944/51 owners!) at 
http://www.connactivity.com/~kgross/944faq.html

Our fabulous cars do have a few weak points that must be addressed right 
away, especially if you don't have records from the previous owner.  Most 
critical are the timing/balance shaft belt changes and retensioning, 
along with the water pump.

> - what oil weight is recommended for this vehicle (10w30, 5w30, 20w50)? 
> ... I live in PA/NJ/NY area

I use 20w50 year round - but in Oregon it rarely gets below freezing in 
winter.  The manual gives an outside temperature range for that viscosity 
from about 18 degrees F to off the scale hot.  A 5w10 synthetic is good 
year-round in any climate.
 
> - I see that this car doesn't red line till about 6500 rpm ... what
> optimal RPM should I be shifting at for normal non-aggressive city/country
> driving? 

Whatever feels comfortable, but probably between 3000 - 4000 to prevent 
lugging in the next gear, and leave the engine with enough revs to give 
you reasonable power.  Remember the 16v engine really comes alive above 
4000.

> - There is a button on dash (defroster I think) which automatically starts
> up a fan (this is right below a button with a circular formation of arrows
> which I think activates cabin air recirculation).  Is this a special
> defroster (like is it electrically heated)?  Also, should air be blowing
> out of the dash vents, or only the defroster vents? (air does blow out of
> my dash vents when this is activated).

That button overrides all climate control settings, and gives you maximum 
defrost power.  It will also engage the A/C compressor to help dehumidify 
the air and clear the windows.  Interior fan goes to full speed, and 
defrost flaps open all the way.

> - At all times, there appears to be a fan running in the vent .... a very
> slight breeze is always coming out (even when stopped and yes the fan knob
> is on the 0 position).  Is there some way to turn this off completely
> (could be quite bothersome in the winter)? 

The fan runs all the time.  This is normal, as (1) keeps ventilation 
going in case you have an exhuast leak into the cabin and (2) pulls air 
over the interior temperature sensor for the climate control.

> - If I use the center console button to lock the doors, and I then turn 
> off the car, the center console won't unlock the doors (I have to turn it 
> back on). Is that normal?

Yes.

> - On the far right hand side of the trunk, there is a piece of carpet
> which can be lifted which reveals a cylindrical weight bolted behind the
> rear right hand side quarter panel.  On the opposite side of the car there
> is no weight - the previous owner used this space on the left to put some
> radio amplifier and car phone equipment which I don't plan on using. Is
> the space on the left supposed to be empty, or did the previous owner
> remove another cylindrical weight to fit the radio equipment? 

Porcshe added this weight to counteract resonances in the body structure 
of the cabriolet, due to loss of body stiffness.
