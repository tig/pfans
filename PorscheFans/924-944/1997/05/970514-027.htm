Another source of hot feet is the heater air ducting.  In my experience
they are difficult to get back together in the proper alignment, let me
explain:  Behind the heater controls is the main air ducting manifold. 
Attached to it are two angled heater duct elbows.  If these are not
installed exactly right there will be air leakage.  There is enough air
pressure (even with the fan off) to push air out through the misaligned
elbows to cause warm feet.  Of course we know that the heater control valve
is mechanical but if it is old enough there should be enough bypass that
the heater coil is warm enough to warm the old feet.  Take a look behind
you center console with the fan on and see if you can feel any warm air. 
Be careful though, last time I worked on this I got torn up by panel screws
with sharp points.  Ouch!
