> I only have the early 944 wiring diagram at my disposal.  I don't recall
if
> you mentioned what year your car is.  I'm sure the wiring for the 1985 &
> 1/2 cars and up is different since they have fully integrated A/C.   
> 
> For the early cars here's the deal:
> 
> There are two relays that come into play; the A/C relay and the cooling
fan
> relay.  With the A/C off and engine to operating temp, the temp switch
(the
> one screwed into the radiator) closes providing a path to ground for the
> cooling fan relay.  The relay closes and this sends full (12V) voltage to
> each fan.  Bad contacts, etc. here can make one or both fans inoperative.

> There is another circuit with a resistor to one of the fans that bypasses
> the relay.  I believe this is the low speed fan noise you sometimes hear
> when you shut down a fully warmed up engine.  
> 
> When you turn on the A/C, the A/C relay closes and the temp switch is
> bypassed.  One side of the A/C relay provides the ground path for the
> cooling fan relay, thus, as long as the A/C is on, both fans should be
> running full speed.  If this portion of the A/C relay were inoperative
(so
> the cooling fan relay would not stay on), I would guess that the engine
> would tend to run hotter since the fans would presumably cycle on and off
> as if the A/C were not on. I'm sure the factory must have had a reason
for
> those fans to be on all of the time that the A/C is on ( i.e. the
condenser
> is dumping its heat load right in front of the radiator).  As I recall,
the
> cooling fan relay is located with the others on the relay panel up under
> the drivers side of the dashboard (see your owners manual for which one).

> I think the A/C relay is located behind the center console somewhere.  I
> don't think either one of these relays are all that expensive (I was able
> to fix a bad solder joint on mine), so you might try replacing them. 
Also,
> I have seen replacement cooling fans for sale in Panorama, so they must
go
> bad at some point (mine are original and at 160K).  Do both fans appear
to
> be turning at the same speed?  One other thing:  these cars must have the
> cooling system bled when anti-freeze is changed.  Other wise, air will be
> trapped and poor/uneven cooling will result.  Hope this helps.
