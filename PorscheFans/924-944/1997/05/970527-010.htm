>My wife borrowed my '92 968 coupe this afternoon to go to the mall.  On the
>way home, she called me from the car to say that the exclamation mark light
>had lit up while driving.  The car was driving fine, she said.  I explained
>that that light was just designed to call attention to another specific
>warning light, but she said everything else on the dash was normal.  I
>thought for a minute, then figured that the light must have malfunctioned and
>told her to drive the 15 minutes home.
>
>When she got home I looked at the dash and immediately saw that the temp
>needle was in the red and no fans were on.  We turned off the car and let it
>cool.  Coolant bubbled around and some dripped out, but it eventually cooled.
> I don't think anything was damaged, since the car could be started later (no
>warped aluminum block).  Some coolant was lost, of course.  I swapped the fan
>relay from her 92 968 cab, but it made no difference (I did this while it was
>still hot enough for the fans to go on).  The fans spin freely.  What else
>could this be besides the relay?  A switch?  The car was just in for a new
>clutch.  Could the mechanic have loosened something which disconnected a few
>days later?  The temp gauge had been acting completely normal until today (in
>other words, I hadn't lost one fan before, and now the other went, as far as
>I can tell).

Try the thermo switch in the radiator. There are two wires connected to it,
and if you short them the fans should come on. I don't know where it is
located on your 968, but on my 924 it is in the upper left corner of the
radiator (left=drivers side).

If it works, perhaps the thermostat won't open.
