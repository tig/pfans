Wrote about epoxy the vaccum venturi.

You may well have "temporarily" fixed you problem by expoxying the venturi.
However, I for one would not have messed with "gluing" such a critical
component. In all likelyhood (from experience) the booster feed line, which
wraps from the venturi to the 2nd booster pipe is "cooked". As you know
this hose comes in contact with the head and is very heated. A 10 year old
car will have this hose near failure. (very brittle).

The new venturi pipe is $8 and a new booster feed is $30. Cheap insurance.
