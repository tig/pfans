>But, I now recall one summer a few years ago, when the battery was
>completely shot, and did not crank the engine at all. It took me a few
>days to get my act together and get a replacement (standard WV battery -

>my own a few times, and I can assure you that the cranking speed was not
>even close to 200 RPM when the engine fired at those times.

When you push-start a car, you don't need a lot of speed at the rear wheels
to get over the 200RPM at the flywheel, due to the gearing and
differential.  When my car had this trouble, I would sometimes drop the
clutch too soon and the engine would *not* fire.  I had to push just a
little faster.  I'm uncertain as to how you can know that you weren't at
200RPM or more.  The other difference, of course, is that when you dump the
clutch, it's possible to get a different sort of velocity curve than with
the starter.  That is, the RPMs can come up much more quickly than with the
starter and produce a spike just over the minimum RPM.

Regardless, this is only my observation combined with the info from that
book.  I have not yet seen any Porsche documentation on the subject, but I
would sure like to!  All the rest of what we've been discussing really
falls into conjecture.
