>I wanted to get some info while the following question is
>fresh in my mind.  My understanding is that shocks should
>affect transient behavior rather than steady state.
>Hence they figure in response during turn-in and
>turn-out.  Does stiffening the back shocks promote
>quicker turn-in (i.e. fight the initial understeer
>involved in getting the car to rotate)?

Increasing the stiffness (compression damping) of your rear shocks does
indeed reduce understeer, or increase oversteer, depending on how you look
at it. It's a trick I've used on front-wheel VWs (for autocross) to make
them rotate. You can also reduce the stiffness of your front shocks or go
with a smaller front sway bar.

Be careful, though -- adjust in small increments, especially if your car is
driven only on the street and in the wet. It *is* possible to turn your
neutral-handling car into a wild, oversteering beast ala more
infamous-handling Porsches. Sometimes it's more fun that way, but if you
need to make an emergency maneuver in the rain you might get a 360 degree
view of your surroundings.
