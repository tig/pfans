Here's one to tuck under your hat:

We had spinning pinion bearing syndrome in my '86, which seemed uncurable
even though we tried loc-tite, buggering up the race-holder, etc..

Eventurally we pulled the bearing race, put a small slot on the outside
of the race , and drilled the trans case, then inserted a roll-pin int
he hole.  The roll pin then prevented the bearing race from spining in
the case.  40 or so track weekends later the trans is still happy.
That tranny will be going back into service in wife's track car soon.
