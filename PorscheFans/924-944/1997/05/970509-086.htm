>1) Maybe someone has experienced this and can lend some insight as to how 
>to fix the problem.  I have a 1980 924, and after a day of heavy rain I >
>can hear water sloshing around behind the dash board.  If I make a heavy 
>turn I am libel to get a decent amount of water dumped on my feet.  Any 
>idea as to how to get the water to drain properly, where the hole may be?

That's and easy one as I had to do the same for mine.  First, pull out the
battery.  Clean out all the rust underneath it.  Once you have done that
You will probably find a hole.  That is where your water is coming in.  I
patched the hole I had with fiberglass.  I recommend a couple of layers to
add strength to hold the battery.  Once the glass has cured protect it with
battery compartment sealer or like I did, use some rubberized undercoating.
 I haven't had any leaks since I applied the treatment to my car.  To help
it from coming back, make sure the drain is clear (front of the battery
compartment) and try not to park uphill when it may rain.  The water stays
in the compartment that way. 

>2) As well, any insight as to the complexity to changing the rear drum 
>breaks and emergency brake cable?  Is it as easy as take off the old 
>parts and put the new parts on in reverse order?  Any special tools 
>needed?  What tools should I have.

Not sure on this one as I have never done so myself (on a P-car).  I
suggest getting the Haynes manual and follow the directions.  When changing
rear brakes always jack both sides and only disassemble one side at a time.
 That way if you make a mistake you have a guide to go by.

>3) My front defroster doesn't blow air with enough force.  Connecticut 
>Inspection will not pass until the front defroster blows a piece of 
>paper!  There is some air coming out of the vents now.  Any ideas on 
>where the problem may be?

Hmmm, had this problem too.  I had limited success fixing it.  Two things,
make sure that all vents are connected together and there is no air leaks. 
Second make sure that the fan slider is making full contact with the high
position on the controls.  The disassembly is a major pain.  Good luck.
