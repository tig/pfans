> Ratch Hattle is usually caused by the little pins that hold your hatch down.
>  The pins (the two items that extend from the hatch end, and go into the back
> of your car) can be adjusted by loosening the lock nut, and twisting the pins
> with a screw driver inserted into the slot.  If the hatch is loose, you shorten
> the pin...
> 
> Identify which side needs adjusting by tugging on the hatch at either side (be
> careful of the spoiler and press down and up on the hatch end).  You can
> usually feel which side needs adjusting because that side will move up and
> down.
> 
> As often is as often not...on both 944s I've owned the pins are rusted into
> their nuts.  If you take the mount for the pins off (torx 30 or 35 I believe)
> you'll find that the pins are in a metal sleeve inbedded into a plastic nut.
>  Either get a new nut/pin combo, or carefully freed the pin from the nut.
> 
> OR if your hatch is adjusted properly, the rattle could be the glass separating
> from the hatch itself, in that case, take the car to a glass shop and have them
> re-attach the glass.
> 
> Have fun, and hope things work out!

One more thing, when troubleshooting this one:

The latch mechanism for the hatch (hatchlatch?) is prone to collect dirt
and dust. This, over time, transforms any grease on and in them to
slow-motion grease and it seems that sometimes the latch grips unevenly,
on one side only or not as positive as one would wish. It may be a good
idea to check this area (hatchlatchwatch?) condition, and if suspect,
clean them. This would be a place where dry teflon lubricant may be
useful.
