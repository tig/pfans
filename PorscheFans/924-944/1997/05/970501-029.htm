> After my recent theft of my stereo I was figuring on laying the wire for a
> rear subwoofer, should I ever decide to add one in the future, while the
> new head unit is out of the dash. What's the best or easiest or neatest
> way of routing the pre-amp line level or speaker output wires from the
> head unit to the rear hatch area? TIA

Wes-
The easiest way is to remove the plastic rocker panel trim. there is enough room to run a set of patch cords (line level output) and some decent size power cable. In front of that you can slip it under the kick panel carpeting. Behind the rocker area you can remove the screws at the bottom of the quarter panel trim and run the wires behind there. It is a relatively straight shot over the wheel well to the side storage areas. If you really want to fix it up replace the rear speakers while you are doing this. If you need the instructions on how to remove the rear side windows(!) and quarter panel trim let me know. Its not a bad job, takes about 3 hours to remove and replace windows, speakers and trim.
