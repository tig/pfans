I recently completed a similar exercise on my 86 951. I decided it was =
easier to pull the engine and work on it in comfort (a great idea). I =
replace every single rubber hose, gasket and pipe in the front end. =
Initial cleaning was done with a power washer, followed by degreaser, =
toothbrush and hot water. Hundreds of ziploc bags later, and thousands =
of dollars, the engine is running like a champ. New bearings, rings, =
head job, pupm, clutch kit, gaskets, AC producing cold air (repaired all =
AC hoses: fairly inexpensive to have them remanufactured), PowerSteering =
no longer leaks (new rack, and hoses, and pump), new turbo. Oil pressure =
is great (surmized that presssure was dropping thru the balance shaft =
half bearings, after seeing their condition), not exhaust leaks, oil =
smoke drip on exhaust, no vac leaks, no coolant leaks, no fuel injector =
leaks (new injectors) etc etc. Suspension is now tight (rebuilt arms and =
tie rods and rack, new bushings etc)
On belts, I acquired "The Tool" as part of the budget for 'The Project". =
Do replace all the bearings and idlers in the belt system. Follow the =
factory manual instructions and pictures (for gear installations. Triple =
check the tool readings.
By better half was equally sceptical (trade it in etc: Oh dont worry =
about that oil pumping out onto the driveway...) But 4 months later and =
tons of fun, it feels great to drive a 97 86-951. Currently considering =
a paint job.

Phil Harris
