replied about the anti-lag system and some are close, but not totally

their actual duties:

Pop Off Valve:
A pop off valve bleeds intake air pressure to atmosphere to control
boost.  This is what is used on Indy cars to prevent a team from 
cheating.  You can set the pop off valve at 1 bar of boost.  The turbo
can pump out 1.5 bar or whatever;  as soon as it gets to the pop
:
:
Compressor Bypass Valve:
This valve is that whoosh you hear on the rally cars when they get off
the throttle.  This is the valve that goes between the compressor and
the throttle body.  When you let off, it releases the air pulses into
atmosphere.  This device is only supposed to open under vacuum, not boost,
