The air intake tube will sometimes take in water if you drive through
standing water.  The worst thing that can happen is that you suck water
into the engine and it dies (forever--rebuild city).  If you are very, very
lucky, it could be that the baffling in the air box and the air filter prevented
the water from getting directly into the engine.  In which case, you may
be able to restore normal functioning by replacing the air filter, looking for
dampness in the box and drying it out.  FWIW--For future reference, you
do not want to drive this car through deep standing water if you can at
all help it.  I imagine you will get more informed responses from others on
the list who are a whole lot more expert than I.

Good luck--James
