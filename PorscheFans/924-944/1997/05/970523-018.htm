> Hi Porsche fans!
> Yesterday i put 2 new speakers into the doors (JBL GTO462D). That
> was a 10 minute job. Now i want to replace the rear speakers.
> Reading some older mails i realize that i have to remove the small
> rear windows first before i can remove the panels which cover the
> rear speakers. So far so bad. So can anybody tell me
> 1. What is the factory rear speaker size (is it 4x6" as in the doors?)
> 2. What is the maximum speaker size that will fit into the rear location
>    (without major modifications, sheet metal cutting etc.)
> 3. Is there a way to cut holes into the rear panels, remove the stock
>    speaker through these holes and mount new speakers there so rear
>    panels and windows do not have to be removed? Any experiences?

Answer (which are derived from an 86')
1. 4x6 
2. 4x6 is the max with no mods.
3. Sound messy. It really isn't that hard to remove the windows and do the job the right way. If it really scares you a good car stereo shop shouldn't charge much more than a couple or hours to do it for you.
 I also think that it may be possible to do the job with out removing the window. You will need a really short screw driver to unscrew the speakers while bending the interior panel a little. (Need to remove all panel screws, obviously) When I did mine I removed the window but I think in retrospect that you may be able to do it this way without damage.
