On Sun, 4 May 1997 SamuelsMA@aol.com wrote:

> 
> I plan to attend autocross school a week from Saturday followed by an
> autocross on the next day (Sunday).  The event is run by the Akron Sports Car
> Club, so I have no idea of there will be other Porsches there.  A few
> questions before I go (and I'm sure I'll think of more between now and then):

I  was there last month for the first time.  It was a well organized event
with about 100 drivers.  The site is on 4 connectd lots with 1 serving as
the pit areas and the course on the other 3.  Not much shade, so bring sun
block and water.

> 
> 1.  What tire pressures should I run in my 92 968 coupe?  It has Dunlop D40
> M2 tires on standard 16" cup wheels, 205/55 in front and 225/50 rear.

I started with 40 lbs front and back on my 944 and it worked fine for me.

> 
> 2.  I need to bring some things with me including a cooler, a tire pump, etc.
>  I assume that these things should be out of the car when I race.  Will there
> be a place to put them?
> 
a tire gauge might be useful for pressure adjustments between sessions.

> 3.  Should I make sure that the gas tank is only, say, a quarter full?  Or
> will the lower weight be offset by the fuel pump having difficulty sucking
> gas as it sloshes around?
> 
Whatever you decide to do with the gas level, do the same every time.  It
did make a difference for me.  The car handled different when I went from
an almost full tank to about a quarter tank.

> 4.  Is there any other specific preparation for the car before I autocross
> it?  There will be a brief tech inspection at the track.

Make sure the car is emptied out including floor mats, garage opener,
mobile phone, etc.   They tend to fly around and get stuck under the pedal
you really want to use at that paticular time.

They start early in Akron, around 8am with the first car off by 10am.

Talk to the other competitors.  They're very friendly and will be glad to
help out.  Have fun
