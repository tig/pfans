>Does anyone know whether it is possible to change the transaxle output
>shaft bearings of a 944 with the transaxle in the car?  If so how is =
the operation
>performed?  Are any special tools required?

There are two taper roller bearings for the pinion shaft, and two for =
the differential carrier.  None can be replaced with the transmission =
even close to the car, let alone in it.  The pinion shaft bearings =
require a complete tear-down of the transmission, the diff bearings =
require that you at least remove the gear carrier (center steel section) =
from the case before beginning the work.

Setting up either pair of bearings (and they should be replaced in =
pairs) requires someone who knows what they're doing.  In particular, =
setting up the "S3" pinion bearing (in the case) is a bitch job, done =
right.  The pinion bearings are also pricey.
