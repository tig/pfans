While a spotless and perfect car may a beauty, there is considerable
charm in a well maintained but used car. The nicks, dents and scratches
each tell a story - see them as the scars of experience.  

The difference between a 'perfectly restored' (old) car, and the
slightly worn, but not abused one is very much a question of charachter.
I my mind, the artificial and shallow feeling, when seeing (driving is
of course completely out of the question...) a perfect (virtually
unused) 10 or 20 year old car on a show does not compare favourably to
the feelings one may get for a visibly used, but cared for car.
