     Well...
        I bought my '86 944 with an engine that had the timing belt 
     replaced by a Porsche DEALERSHIP at 138k miles.  Two months later the 
     engine bent 8 valves; nice new belt did not snap, but slipped about 
     three teeth every revolution to give ALL the valves a good chance to 
     get hit, which they did.
        Turns out the tensioner was not replaced and the bearing had 
     disintegrated, allowing the belt tension to let go.
        
        The guy then sold the car; without raising all kinds of hell at the 
     dealership when they quoted him $3500 to do the work. Go figure.
     
        Not that I'm complaining, I LIKE my car a lot; cost me $380 to get 
     this baby on the road; including new valves and gaskets.
