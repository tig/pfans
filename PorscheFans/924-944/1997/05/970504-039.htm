>can anyone tell me what kind of tool or how to remove the hubcaps from my
>87'
>944, with phone dial rims, the hubcaps have two very small holes in them

There is a special tool for removing the center caps that fits in the 2
holes.  They are ridiculously priced (not super-expensive, just over-priced
for the material needed), and you can make your own tool for like 4 cents.
Just take a metal coat hanger (you may want to use one with the vinyl
coating for protection) and bend the hook to a tighter curve or right angle.
To maximize leverage, get 2 modified coat hangers (or cut one coat hanger
into 2 "tools"), and use both at the same time.  Stick the wires in the
holes, twist or adjust them so that the hooks catch behind the face of the
center cap, and then PULL!  They are in there tight, so it may take a lot of
force and patience (twisting the cap may help, but be careful not to damage
the crest by making the holes larger!).
        Another way to get the center caps off involves removing the wheels
from the car and pushing the caps out from behind.  Hope this helps...
