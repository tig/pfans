> 1.  My salesman/autocross fanatic/PCA member suggested that the dealer
> install a "Sachs clutch" rather than a "Porsche clutch."  He said that the
> parts are identical, that Porsche buys them from Sachs and then puts its name
> on the box with a big markup.  Is this correct?  I don't want an inferior
> part.  And I have read all about the perils of "rubber-centered" clutches on
> the list.  Can I assume that the part for a 968 would have been updated to
> eliminate this problem, or do I need to specify a special part?

I thought 968' used rubber centers. If you don't plan to run SCCA events
which require OEM style clutch, maybe the single spring turbo cup sachs
disc will fit. Anyone tried the "Racemaster" clutch sold by Automotion?
The 968 version lists for $1154 (ouch).

> 3.  Once I put the new clutch in, how harmful are those "rev to 5000 rpm and
> quickly engage the clutch to make a little wheelspin" autocross starts? 

OUCH! Once in a while (a few times per yr) should be OK but I would'nt be
making a habit of it for every auto-x run. On sticky R tyres I normally
launch at around 3500 rpm and can still get enough spin on the tyres to
clean them off the line. But then most courses around the Northwest are
not straight shots, they generally lead directly into a short turn at the
start.

> Will
> I just ruin the next one? 

Yes, you would see acccelerated wear or failure of the clutch unless
you have a specialized race clutch (which are'nt to happy with street
starts) if you do consistent 5000 rpm drop clutch starts. Does centerforce
make a clutch for 944'?

> And how hard is autocrossing on the brakes.

I don't see much accelerated wear on the pads from auto-xing.

>  Hopefully, new brake pads won't be nearly as expensive as a new clutch, in
> any case.

A good set of front pads for auto-x can run $70-140. And it's an easy
do-it-yourself job.
