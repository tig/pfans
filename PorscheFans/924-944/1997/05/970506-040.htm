>If the camshaft TDC marks are lined up with the marks on the camshaft
>cover, the clutch housing marks are not necessarily lined up correctly? I
>mean, do the camshaft marks line up more frequently than the clutch housing
>or is one rotation of the camshaft = one rotation of the engine and all of
>its associated  parts?

Start with the flywheel/clutch housing marks, and get them DEAD ON.


>Next question(s):
>
>If the camshaft notches are lined up, should the flywheel be lined up? What
>if the flywheel is off an 1/8 or 1/4 inch like I think mine is?

Line it up as close as you can after the flywheel is aligned.   If you end
up being 
a small amount off (less than a tooth), you will be fine.

>
>If the camshaft notches are lined up, should the balance shaft notches be
>lined up? What if the balance shaft notches are off a couple of belt teeth,
>like mine are?

After the timing belt alignment and tensioning are DONE, then return the
engine to tdc (flywheel perfectly aligned), and tackle the balance shafts.
They should be essentially DEAD ON, not one or two teeth off or you will
definitely feel it.   Balance shafts will sometimes fool you by look fine until
you rotate the engine, and then be off.   I f that happens, go with the one
that is aligned, loosen the belt, and spin the other into alignment.   Don't
settle for a tooth off.



>
>Am I correct in using one set of notches as the mother of all notches? In
>this case, the camshaft notches.

NO.   Use the flywheel.   It is more precise.
