>I may be wrong but it was my understanding that most cars with EFI have a
>safety feature that shuts off the fuel pump if oil pressure drops too low
>(as in zero).  I was told that this was to prevent the fuel pump from
>feeding a possible fire/accident situation in which the engine has stopped
>but key is still in the run position (and a fuel line might have been
>ruptured).  Now with the DME, there are certainly more sophisticated ways
>to accomplish this (i.e. RPM sensor reads zero).  However, is it possible
>that DME looks at oil pressure too, and should the relief valve be stuck
>open, insufficient pressure will develop for the DME to say 'yes' start?

This would make sense, except that even if you blow the oil filter (as I
did), you still have at least some pressure available (for a short time
anyhow).  The piece of information I have states that the DME uses the
engine RPM as its input.  If your oil pressure *gauge* (not the oiling
system itself) goes bad, you can still run, but if your engine speed and
TDC sensors go bad, the computer is blind anyhow, so it would only make
sense to shut off the fuel pump.  The oil pressure sensor tends to be a
rather inaccurate device anyhow-- it's not meant for accurate readings
(imagine the cost if it were!).
