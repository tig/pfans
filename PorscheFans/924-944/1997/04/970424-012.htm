To remove the steering wheel (4-spoke non air bag design):

Put your two hands behind the horn pad (kind of curl around it) and pull, hard!
The horn is held on by three spring loaded clips (I don't know what else to
call them).  Once that is off, you'll need a 24mm socket.  Don't get the
deep socket ones because they will rub on the steering wheel when turning.

While you have it off, consider getting a Momo replacement.  It makes a huge
difference.  Paul, myself and Darren swear by them.
