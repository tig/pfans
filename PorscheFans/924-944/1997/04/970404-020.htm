First, let me qualify my experience.  I have replaced valve seals on BMW's
and water-cooled VW's.  I haven't on a Porsche Typ 951.  (but I bet this
part's the same)

Two options I know of, one's a guess:  

1)There's a tool made for this express purpose.  It "holds" the valve by the
grooves in the top where the keepers were before you removed them.  About
$30.00

2)I've seen a buddy wipe the valve stem dry, and proceed to wrap, double,
wrap, double, and wrap (you get the picture) a rubber band around the valve
stem to hold valve up.

3)  Since the 944 is not a freewheeling engine, why not carefully rotate the
engine until the piston is at TDC for that cylinder, and let the piston do
the work?

All assume, of course, you have a "pry bar" style valve compressor, and not
the big "C clamp" type.
