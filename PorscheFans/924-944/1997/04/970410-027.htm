I guess I was the lucky one then, found my flywheel lock in one of the two 
side storage spaces in my trunk when I purchased the vehicle.  Didn't know 
what it was until I saw it in a catalog for ~$60. Just remove the starter, and 
bolt it in!   In speaking to people at various repair shops, they have seen 
many vehicles with teeth stripped off the flywheel due to people not using the 
correct tool. Some people had even tried placing their vehicles in gear, and 
blocking the wheels, causing all sorts of clutch damage.  This is not 
surprising considering the torque required to remove these crankshaft bolts..
