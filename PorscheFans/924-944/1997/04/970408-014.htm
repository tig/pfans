Russ,

The switch for the rear hatch on my car (89 944 turbo) is part of the shocks
that hold that hatch up.  I haven't really studied it, but the shocks
themselves act as a switch, when the hatch is open, then the contacts at the
top of the shocks get grounded, thus closing the circuit, and the lights light
up.  When closed the contacts don't touch the shock, and the lights go out.

You might want to check these.  Don't get them confused with the heater element
wires which also run up and down the shock.

Maybe someone could give a little bit more info since I haven't really looked
at how the rear hatch switch is set up.
