> I've got a cylinder that is fouling its plug, and my oil consumption is
> exceeding the factor spec.  I suspect that I have a valve seal going
> away.  In looking at the shop manual, it seems like it should be
> possible to replace the seals without pulling the head.  The main trick
> looks like it would be to keep the valves from dropping into the
> cylinder while pressing on the new seals.  Has anyone tried this, or
> know for sure if it's feasible?
> 
Why not? If you lock the flywheel, put an adapter in the spark plug
hole and connect it to 120PSI of air, you're going to have each valve
pushing back with over 90 pounds of force while you try to compress the
spring. It should be enough.

The deal will be finding / making a good spring compressor for use
without access to the valve face.
