I just spent the last year looking for what I considered to be a =
reasonable deal on a good 89 944T.  I did not find one.  What I found =
were a large number of these cars being sold by private owners for what =
I felt were unreasonable prices.  Let me clarify.

I started by check both NADA and Kelly Blue Book for average retail.  =
What many fail to realize is the prices listed in both the NADA book and =
the Kelly Blue Book are an average of the price dealers sell the =
particular car for.  Since typically dealers sell cars on the high side =
of the price model I felt this was a good place to start.  The on-line =
Kelly Blue Book lists the 89 944T at a retail price of $15325 USD.  I =
never found a car in what I would expect as average condition for this =
price.

What I found was many of the club racing guys were snatching these cars =
up at prices in the 19k - 25k range.  I'm sorry, but I don't think this =
car is worth that kind of dough.  Even the dealers I spoke with felt =
those prices were too high.

I ended purchasing a 93 968.  A car I had never even considered until I =
went and test drove one.  Following the test drive, I looked up articles =
on it in past Excellence and European car (no access to pano) issues.  =
The reviewers all had good things to say about it.  More or less the =
same performance as a stock 944T. =20

So, unless you have to have a turbo, consider a 968.  I have begun to =
think it is the most under-rated Porsche to date.

Thanks for your indulgence.
