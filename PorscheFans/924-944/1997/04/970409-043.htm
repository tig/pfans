I recently took my 924S back into the local tire dealership which had
installed 4 studded snow tires onto my 15x7 phone dials last fall. In
the process of removing the rims, they stripped out the locking lug nut
"key" for the Porsche (not after-market) locking lug nuts.  There were
two (2) rims left on the car. They had two men, one on each side of a
star wrench, one pushing and one pulling just before stripping out the
lug nut "key" since the impact gun couldn't remove the lug nut even
before the key stripped.

They then drilled through the two remaining locking nuts, both wheel
studs and into the lug nut seating area of both rims. They also tried to
pry off the lug nuts and thoroughly marred the face and inset area of
the rim where the lug nut meets the wheel stud (very visible),
cosmetically ruining both rims.

The shop is denying any responsibility for damages (2 rims, 1 locking
nut set, and replacement of the 2 studs) since they claim that "I am at
fault" for having locking alloy wheel nuts on the car. They were able to
remove the "regular" wheel nuts without incident. They also add that
there is some chemical process involved between alloy nuts and steel
wheel studs which in effect "welds" the two unlike materials together.
The manager claims this is quite common and known among industry
professionals.  It had nothing to do with over-tightening the lug nuts.=20
