>      Leaks at the oil/water intercooler are not uncommon - I've suffered on
>      my last car, a 944S, and my current S2.  The  thing you normally
>      notice first is oil in the coolant, rather than water in the oil.  Are
>      you sure you don't have oil in the coolant?  If not, it may be
>      something else.  Do you do a lot of short journeys, creating
>      condensation which doesn't get chance to evaporate?
> 
>      The oil/water intercooler is a small matrix (like a heater matrix)
>      which fits under a cover on the side of the engine under the exhaust
>      manifold.  It's connected into the oil system via two stubs fitted
>      with "O" rings.  The whole thing is immersed in coolant.  When the "O"
>      rings fail you get oil in water and/or water in oil.
> 
>      You can get a kit containing all the gaskets you need for the job for
>      about $50. You also need an alignment tool (which you can get from
>      Automation for about $16). The job itself takes several hours, and is
>      no fun because access is so difficult.  I say this with feeling,
>      having done mine 2 weeks ago.
