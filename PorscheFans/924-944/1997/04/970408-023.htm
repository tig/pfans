Well, the last time I posted to the list (6 months or so ago), my car was a D. 
O. G..., and I didn't know why.  So, I drove on up to AutoSports and had them 
take a look.  Come to find out, my local shop, which had rechecked the timing 
twice on AutoSports' suspicion, had grinched up some parts, (cam bolt, etc.), 
and failed to notice that the cams had been re-installed (at some point in the 
past), with the cam lobes separated by 101mm instead of the proper 113mm.  
Seems they were off a tooth or so.  That being corrected, and a chip being 
added, (and a veritable slew of other little things like factory alarm being 
fixed, gauges being re-aligned, broken starter replaced, new chain tensioner, 
etc.), the car runs W. E. L. L.   I could best define it as solid, everywhere 
in the RPM range, and any time I want to step on the gas!  The drive back from 
Maine was "a 6 hour grin"...

All is well with the world.
