> ...So what's the most convenient device(s) to aid bleeding brakes

One clever device consists of a tube (that attatches to the air valve of
a wheel) and a screw on lid to the brake/clutch fluid reservoir. This
contraption pressurizes the system, and as soon as any bleed nipple is
opened, fluid escapes. Obviously, the reservoir needs to be topped up
before starting this procedure, and checked during bleeding, especially
when doing brakes. However, its really convenient and effective.
Properly handled, there will be less residual air after bleeding than
with any other method - due to the constant pressure. 

You may want to use a spare tire (not the space saver - 1. ever tried to
re-fold one of these? 2. Pressure may be too high) to be sure of not
emptying a road wheel.  

This is also in the finest Porsche tradition - the windshield washer on
erly 356:s was driven by spare tire air pressure.

Price? In Sweden it costs ca 200 SEK - about 30 $.
