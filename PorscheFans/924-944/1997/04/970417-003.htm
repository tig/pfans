On Wed, 16 Apr 1997, Edward Mathers wrote:

> 1) Exhaust leak: When starting the engine completely cold, there is a small
> exhaust leak on the right side of the engine that goes away in about 30-60
> seconds. Could this be a leaky waste gate? 

Could be a manifold leak, which goes away as the metals swell with heat.  
When you first start the engine, run your hand along the joints to feel 
for exhaust spurting out before the engine heats up.  Gasket replacement 
recomended.

> 2) Shocks: After 50k plus miles, I would like to replace the shocks. What
> works and what doesn't? The rear end tends to jump around under moderate to
> hard cornering - the 'wash board' effect.

Listmembers?

> 3) Overheating: When at highway speeds the indicated guage temperature is
> normal, at the first white mark. But around town the guage will frequently
> climb up towards the red mark. The motor never boils over, or feels
> unusually hot. Does anyone know what temperatures correspond to the
> different guage marks?

Sounds normal.  The first mark is where the thermostat just starts to 
open.  The fans should come on at low speed when the gauge indicates 
about halfway, and in high speed (or second fan on older cars) when the 
pointer hits the 2/3 mark.  I would be concerned if the pointer is over 
the 2/3rd mark for an extended period.
