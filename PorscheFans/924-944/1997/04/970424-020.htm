Michael, 

If you are going to replace the brake and clutch fluid here are a 
couple of ideas that work for me:

1)	Raise the rear of the vehicle and place on jackstands - this gets 
the bleeder valve on the clutch slave cylinder into a better position 
to bleed all the air from the system.  The higher you can get the 
vehicle, the better.

2)	Remove the starter to gain easier access to the slave cylinder 
bleeder valve.

3)	I have tried those one man self bleeder kits, but the best method I 
have found is to have someone pump the pedals.

4)	When bleeding the brakes, start at the driver's side rear and work 
counterclockwise around the vehicle.

Hope this helps.
