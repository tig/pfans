> What exactly are the legal implications of removing or bypassing the
> catalytic convertor in a 951?  

That depends on the state.  In Massachusetts, it's illegal, and no shop
will do it for you.  Plus, it almost certainly won't pass inspection.

> In an article I read about 951 mods, the
> author mentions that "Tests show that 944 turbo's are dirtier, but still
> relatively clean even without the cat."  Will a 951 pass emissions tests and
> inspection with a hollow or bypassed cat?  Do all 951's have the 3-way cat,
> or was that only installed on the "S" models?

The problem is that most harmful emissions are reduced to a negligible
level before even entering the exhaust pipes under the car.  (Actually,
that's good.)  But nitrous oxides, IIRC, can't be re-combusted as
efficiently as carbon monoxide or dioxide and other emissions.

This means that you'll pass the CO tests in flying colors, but flags will
go up everywhere in the system if your state tests NO content -- as it
almost certainly will.  However, if the 944 *still* passes... well, that
would surprise me, but it *might* happen.

The rule of thumb as I've heard it applied is to either replace the
catalytic converter right before inspection and take it off again right
after, or only do it on a car that's been grandfathered.  (I think the
current magic number is 1982.  Can anyone correct me on that?)
