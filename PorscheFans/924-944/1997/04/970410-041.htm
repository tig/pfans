Doug,

	You might want to try some 'impact' on the stubborn little so & so's.
From much experience up here in the "rust belt"(Buffalo,NY), a
combination of pen. oil, heat, tapping & persistence usually frees up
the offending hardware. In the case of bleeder nipples the problem is
compounded by a massive caliper which renders heat pretty much
ineffectual & a flimsy(sp.) fitting that precludes any great amount of
force being applied. This limits your plan of attack severely, so some
LIGHT tapping along w/pen. oil should help it penetrate deeper, followed
by LIGHT tapping while applying force(in both directions). Preferably
you'll be using a six sided wrench & tapping repeatedly(& LIGHTLY) on
its side. Don't forget the persistence part, it'll seem like it's not
doin' a darn thing & just when you're going to give up, bingo! (At least
that's how it happens some of the time!) [Is this discourse major
overkill on my part or what - sorry about the length, I'm just feeling
quite verbose tonite.]
