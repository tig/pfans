From some reading I have done (Bruce's and Smith's books):

The fluid doesn't absorb moisture, which is good, because the boiling
point doesn't degrade like conventional fluid. But it allows moisture
to pool in the system, where it might cause more corrosion than moisture
in suspension.

Also there have been some complaints of a more spongy pedal than with
conventional fluids.
