It still might be your emergency brake, but not actually the brakes
themselves - When you release the e-brake handle and push it down, it comes
in contact with a small "button-type" micro switch.  Sometimes the bracket
holding the switch gets bent downward, so that when the brake handle is
lowered it doesn't completely depress the micro switch, which would in turn
not open the contacts fully to allow the light to go off.  All you have to
do is raise the handle and adjust the height of the bracket a little until
the button is completely depressed when the handle is in the down position
(you should be able to hear the contacts open and close inside the switch.
This used to happen to me frequently in my first P-car, a 1983 944.  Hope
this works for you.
