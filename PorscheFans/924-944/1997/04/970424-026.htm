>Brake pads are the subject of much debate and personal preference.  You will
>get many different opinions.  I happen to also be fortunate enough to own an
>'89 951 like yourself so I will give my own experiences.
>
>I started autocrossing and tracking my car the day after I got it using
>stock pads.  The stock pads worked well, did not squeal, but dusted heavily.
>The word at the track is that stock pads will warp your rotors if you do not
>add cooling ducts.  I switched to Repco (now Axxis) pads thinking they would
>improve my track performance.  These pads last longer and do not dust,
>therefore seem well suited for everyday use.   At the track(Summit Point), I
>experienced more brake fade and required more pedal effort to slow down
>heading into turn 1 where braking from 130+ mph to about 40 mph is required.
>I switched to Pagid pads for the track.  They squeal like crazy, dust less
>than stock pads and require less pedal effort to stop, but I still boil the
>fluid at the track.  I can not stand them for everyday driving due to the
>squeal so I use the Repcos on a daily basis.
>
>Other people will swear by Cool Carbons, Performance Friction, Hawke, etc.
>I would not buy the Pagids again because of the cost, but I also will not
>use Matalmasters for the track.  I think next time I will probably try the
>Perfromance Friction pads because AI can get a good deal on them.  I really
>believe that brake pads are a matter of personal choice and budget.  I do
>not think you can go wrong with any of the major names.  I think the stock
>pads work well for all around activity if you can stand the brake dust.
>Otherwise I recommend Metalmasters for everyday driving and a high
>performance brand for the track.  Good Luck.
>
>Tracy Meyer
>
I agree with Tracy and my experience has been similar on an 87 951. I use
Metal Masters around town with shims to stop the rattling and I use Hawk
pads on the track. I haven't used Performance Friction pads ($$$) but I
have been very pleased with the Hawks. But, for the same reasons Tracy
cited, you can't use them around town, plus they are *very* dusty.

By the way, the shims are not a big deal. I bought a simple aluminum sign
at Home Depot and I cut shims out of it with tin snips. They don't require
perfection!
