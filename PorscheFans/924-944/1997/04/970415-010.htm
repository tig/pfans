they are almost the same and although i really have respect for chris cervelli,
i am not quite sure where he sees the advantage of a turbo.  a supercharger gives you power all the way through the power band there is NO ,NO, lag.  when you put your foot to the floor it is making boost all the way until red line.  thusly there is no peak. a supercharger puts approximately half the strain on an engine that a turbo does if the engine is a good one.  there is NO reliability problem and a supercharger NEVER has to be rebuilt.  One problem with them is that if the belt that runs it is not checked and it breaks then you are looking at catastorphic meltdown!!

that being said i think since the Porsche cars tradionally rely on turbos they are probably the way to go.  leave the superchargers to the american muscle cars, like my stang!!  

just another point of info. a supercharger and turbocharger all make different amount of boost depending on size, intercooler, etc, etc.......
