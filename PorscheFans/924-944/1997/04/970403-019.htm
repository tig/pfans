  Most american cars have a "device" that when the throttle is suddenly
released delays the return to full closed position for a few seconds.  I
believe the purpose is to prevent your very symptoms.  On american cars
the "device" is somewhat of a shock absorber right at the carb or
injection unit.
  I am not sure but believe porsche has something similar because when I
suddenly release my throttle it returns initially to 200-300 rpm above
idle for 3-4 seconds then settles into normal idle rpm. I believe the
theory is to allow extra air in to prevent a over rich condition.
 Others more knowing on porsche specifics may be able to elaborate.
