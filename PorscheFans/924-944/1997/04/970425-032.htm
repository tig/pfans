Should I run with my AutoAuthority chips at the track?


I replaced the AutoAuthority chips with the original chips in my car today.

Without the chips the throttle response at idle is faster and smoother.
 However the car has less torque in the low rpms.  AA must richen the mixture
at idle and when the throttle is pressed (richer than stock).  When I press on
the throttle with the AA chips (at idle), the rpm drops before rising.

Boost in the upper gears is limitted.  I noticed in third my car gets up to
about 1.75 bar (absolute).  With the AA it got to 1.9.  In the 1 and 2 gears
boost reaches to 1.8  Previously it reached just about 2.0.

This is all I noticed driving to work tonight...
