On Fri, 11 Apr 1997, Barry Lenoble wrote:

> I haven't seen a real compelling reason for SS brake lines. One of my friends
> has them, I don't. His pedal is stiffer than mine. So? His brakes don't work
> any better. 

> I suppose that when the times comes to replace my brake lines I'll probably
> go to SS. I can't see replacing perfectly good rubber lines just for the
> sake of SS. If I'm missing something, can someone enlighten me?

IMHO SS lines advantage is in racing (that's were they are used most)
because it allows you better feel/response/feedback through the brake
pedal while braking at 10/10'ths when trying to shave 0.20 seconds off of
your lap time. It should be easier to make micro adjustments in your
braking while at the threshold of lockup (even with ABS).

Anyone know what is different about these newer DOT approved SS lines? Who
are the manufacturers? I remember "Russell" lines advertises DOT approved
ones.
