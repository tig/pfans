From what I understand, Cross drilling rotors does the following:

o Reduces Surface area otherwise available for cooling (BAD)
o Reduces Thermal Mass (BAD)
o Provides a better escape path for gas,fluid, dust, etc.. between the
rotor and Pads (Good)

So, unless you increase the diameter of the rotor, which would require
new calipers, etc..  you will most likely lose by replacing OEM rotors
with cross drilled units.  I have no personal experience with this, but
seem to remember a posting to that general effect some time ago, and it
makes sense.

Any other opinions?
