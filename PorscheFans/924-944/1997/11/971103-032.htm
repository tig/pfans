Sean,

Nice detective work.  It is very likely that your DME relay is indeed 
bad.  Buy two new ones: one to install, one to keep as a spare in the 
glovebox.  You can test the DME relay by checking for primary circuit 
voltage when the engine is switched on.  I think there is a drawing on 
the side of the relay detailing, by number code, the pins.

It may be that your problem lies elsewhere.  Once in a while, a bad 
factory or aftermarket alarm disables the DME relay.

BTW, unless you abuse them electrically, 944-series DME's almost never, 
ever go bad.
