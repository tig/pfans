I think the best bet is to remove the radiator.  If your oil cooler is
removed you can probably get everything out from below.  If not, pull it
from the top.  You'll have to disconnect all the front water hoses and
remove the aluminum pipe that runs across the engine, in addition to the
typical air box/ distributor cap removal.  Remove the fans, then loosen
the top rubber radiator mounts, then pull the radiator out.

Once out, you should be able to remove the plastic from the upper
radiator hose connection.

As for anything to do?  I guess you could clean...
