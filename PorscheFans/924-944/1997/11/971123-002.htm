Tom:  Here's some free advice from someone who has been there - go to
17" wheels for the track.  The tires are not much more expensive and
the amount of rubber on the road is much better with 17" sizes
available.

Depending on your budget, you can spend between $900 to $2000 for the wheels
(new) or you can look for used 17".  If you are buying new weels anyway, spend a few extra
bucks and get a serious upgrade!

On your car (930)I'd suggest the following:

Cheap: 7.5+9x17" borbet or similar replica wheels  $950  >245+275/40/17
Mid   8.5+10x17" OZ or similar 1-peice wheels $12-1300  >255/40/17+315/35/17
Ultimate 9.5-10 + 11-12x17" 3-peice (like HRE) $1800-2200 >275/40/17 + 315 or 335*/35/17

(I use the HRE 504 and as a dealer can sell sets in almost any width offset for under $2K.)

*335 is pushing it  and may require fairly serious fender tweeking.

315/35 is a bolt on.

I beleive in this to the extent that even my street tires are moving to 17"
when upgraded.  I run 245/40/17+275/40/17 on my turboS with pirelli 7000SS
tires and the difference in handling between that and the other turboS (225+245/16
on 7.5+9" wheels) is dramatic.

You can run 8+10x17 in stock class, if that concerns you.

Just a thought.
