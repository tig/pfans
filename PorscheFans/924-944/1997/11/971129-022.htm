enough thru Interstate traffic).
  As a side note to everyone, I saw an 83' 944 for sale at the show. Had 
about 150,000 on the OD. Inside was clean, motor was clean, and the paint 
job was impressive. Black on black. You could stand back 15 feet and still 
see your reflection clearly! The only problem was the front spoiler. Whoever 
did the paint and body work neglected to use proper materials to fix the 
road scuffs and digs. It looked like they just slapped some Bondo down there,
 sanded it, and went on their way. That fatal mistake ruined an otherwise 
beautiful car. The owner was asking $7900 for it. Needless to say, the car 
had very few nose prints on the windows....
  What he might have tried insted, was to have the spoiler send over to a 
shop that does remolding work with automotive ABS plastics. They can recast 
the part so it looks as good as new. I have seen it done. It is worth it. I 
plan to have mine done when I start on the body work on my 944 (when and if 
I can get the car to start and idle right, hot or cold).
