Dear Kent & P-Fans
	A internal combustion engine needs two things - air & fuel.  Next time you
engine will not start, bring and extra spark plug, attach it to one of the
plug wires and ground the sides/threads of  the plug.  You could also buy a
test plug with one of those aligator clips attatched to the thread area - a
test plug I think they are called?  See while cranking if there is spark. If
so move on to check the fuel side. You will need a fuel pressure gauge,
attatch it on the end (oil filter end/side) of the fuel rail there is a
fitting that the gauge can be hooked up to. The regulator is near the fire
wall on the other end of the fuel rail.  I suspect that the fuel pressure
regulator is sticking - cuasing an overly rich condition. If you have a
freind with the same car you could swap fuel pressure regulators. Also check
the 10 mm  bolts that hold down the inductive pick up sensors above the
flywheel - sometimes they loosen up causing strange things to happen.
