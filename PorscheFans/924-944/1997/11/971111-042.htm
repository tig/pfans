John Pohl wrote:
> 
> Just wanted some advice on my plan for handling improvements on my 88-951.
> I have decided not to lower the car, I dont want to have clearance problems
> and the cost of reseting the rear torsion bar is high. I replaced the Comp
> TA's with RE71's. I will upgrade the springs with 220# Eibach's with stock
> ride height. To help balance the rear after this, I'm going to replace the
> rear sway bar with a stiffer Weltmeister one. I want to reduce sway, but
> cant afford to do everything at once. I think this first step will leave
> the car balanced and handling a little better.
> Later, I will do the front sway bar and upgrade torsion bar, but this will
> have to wait for my wallet to catch up.
>
John,

If you aren't going to do it all at once you will probably be happier in the
interim doing the anti-roll bars in pairs and the springs and torsion bars in
pairs later. Some savings by only having to worry about one alignment after
doing the torsion bars and springs at the same time (although if you are
careful and don't change ride height you might get it pretty close for the
front).
