I would try using heat on the case first.  Probably not practical with 
the transmission in the car, but then again neither is drilling it out. 
 So, remove the transmission, heat the case around the plug with a 
plumber's propane torch (MAP gas an optional upgrade), and try to get 
the plug out.  Use a long breaker bar on the socket.

On reinstall, make sure the threads are clean.  Torque spec is like 24 
Nm / 17 ftlb -- which is not a lot of torque.  Everyone overtightens 
these plugs, big mistake.  I've seen a few that look like they were 
install with an air tool.
