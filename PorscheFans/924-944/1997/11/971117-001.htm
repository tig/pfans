I understood that one should NEVER disconnect the battery when the engine is
running because this can cause fatal damage to the semiconductor regulator
pack. If you think about it, there are large inductances in the alternator.
If you suddenly break the flow of current through an inductance, then a big
voltage spike will appear across it. Such a spike could easily fry a diode
in the regulator pack. Worse, the output voltage of the alternator could
become unstable, with potentially damaging consequences for the
engine-management system.

Disconnecting the battery in a car with a dynamo won't harm the equipment
(unless you have replaced the old control box with a semiconductor
equivalent). Just make sure that a spike does not appear across you!
