Hi Bob;

I assume you are referring to the Bosch adjustable fuel pressure billet
(regulator).  If so, and your car is chipped (don't know which chips you
have), you will notice a slight increase in throttle response.
Particularly, you'll notice a difference in the 2200 - 3200 range,
before the Turbo begins to dial in. I have not dyno'd the effects, but I
would also imagine there is a slight increase in HP/torque.

Anyway, installation is straight forward, but you will need a fuel
pressure gauge to attach to the fuel bar to set it up. As I'm running
these on both my 951 & 911 3.2 track car, I believe the stock fuel
pressure at the rail is at 28 +/-1 PSI. While the Bosch billet's  are
adjustable to +6 PSI over stock (up to 34 PSI +/-1), I set it to 32 +/1
PSI.  If you are interested, I'll post the installation steps for this.

Hope this helps you out,
