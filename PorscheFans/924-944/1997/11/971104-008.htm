Think about gettting a "battery maintainer" & leaving it on constantly for
the winter if you have power handy. Get it at Wal-Mart for $24.95, same
thing in a slightly different package as the $60 ones you see advertised in
the catalogs.

I always pump the tires up to 40-45 pounds so they take less of a "set"
from sitting in one place over the winter.

If you decide to jack up the car to get the tires off the ground, be sure
to do it in a way that does not expose the shock absorber shafts for a long
time. After jacking, lower it on jack stands set under the wheel hub to
compress the shafts. If they rust they can freeze in the extended position,
or the rust will quickly abrade the seals come spring.

Happy winterizing.......
