Hello.
I have been lurking for a couple of months & have come up w/my first 
questions.

Background:  I have an '88 944S w/about 90,000 miles.  It runs quite well 
(I use it for a daily driver) & I have been very happy w/it.  It does, 
however, have the rough-idle problem.  So, this afternoon I got under the 
hood to check for vacuum leaks & noticed that I had some oil leaking out 
from the plugs on the top of the camshaft cover.  I got out the trusty 
Haynes manual which said the torque on the plugs should be 29ft/lb. 
 Naturally, the first one I tried only had about 3 so I began to tighten it 
and it snapped at about 7.  Forget 29.  My question is: Is 29 ft/lb 
correct?  The other plugs all had between 5 and 15 on them (I didn't apply 
any extra, just checked them out) and I'm hoping that I had a bad plug.  I 
guess as soon as I can get a new gasket I'll pull the cover and drill out 
the broken plug.
Second question:  The transmission has recently begun making "whirring" 
sounds when I let off the throttle, sort of like gears that are not seated 
firmly.  I think the clutch is about to give as well (it's beginning to 
grab a little) and I'm wondering if the whirring is related to the grabbing 
clutch and if a new clutch will solve both problems.

Any and all advice is appreciated.

Thanks
dave randolph
Denton, Tx.
