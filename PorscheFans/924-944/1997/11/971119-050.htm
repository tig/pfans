OK, here's some food for thought on altitude.  Standard barometric
pressure at sea level is 29.92" Hg.  At Denver's 5280 ft elevation,
pressure is 24.64" Hg, and at Colorado Springs' 6035 ft it is 23.95" Hg.

Calculating the ratios for Denver/Sea Level and C-Springs/Sea Level we =
get
0.82 and 0.80.  Therefore it would not be unreasonable to get cranking
compression readings of 162-171 psi at Denver and 157-166 psi at =
C-Springs
(Example, 0.82 x 18 x 10.9 =3D 162 and 0.82 x 19 x 10.9 =3D 171)

Therefore, my conclusion is Terry's result of 170 psi is right-on and
indicates excellent compression.
