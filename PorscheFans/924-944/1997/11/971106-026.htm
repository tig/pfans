Yeah!!  Get to the track, guys.  Then your 944 will ALWAYS feel slow on a
straight.   You're not driving a straight-line Chevy or Grand National. 
Straight-line speed has only two dangers -- a bird (or god forbid an elk)
cracking the windshield, or your date eventually getting bored and falling
asleep, and going out with a track driver next time.  Oh yeah -- three
dangers -- guess a blow-out wouldn't be fun.  OK, four -- you could get
really embarassed because the cop's Crown Vic goes faster in a straight
line.

Anyone have an opinion on when straight-line speed DOES get scary?  +200
mph? Or is it just a question of habituation? Regarding top ends for the
various 944s, my S2 will do 150 at 7000 ft altitude w/ a slight tailwind.
Otherwise it reaches 145-147.  I sometimes do this (on a very straight road
with no traffic where you can see other cars coming for at least 2 miles,
officer) just before a track event, just to get rid of the feeling of
speed. When you slow down to 100, it feels like 55.
