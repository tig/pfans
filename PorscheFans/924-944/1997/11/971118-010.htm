There might ba a couple of things you want to check while you are under
there...
- Check the rear bushing where the A arm attaches to its mount.  This
mount seems to collect oil and takes heat from the exhaust, so it tends
to wear out faster... To check for play, lift the right side of the car
and see if you can move the A arm with a large lever (yes, like a
screwdriver) - the bushings get "mushy" (sorry for the technical jargon)
before they disintergrate..

Also check the ball joints (you didn't mention these in your post) -
jack the car, turn the wheel all the way left and right and check the
play where the strut connects into the ball joint. A failure here is NOT
fun...

Aside from the other stuff you mention, check the strut and the top
strut mountings...
