Carbon deposits:
	Carbon deposits occur when you shut off the engine, and the fuel flow does
not stop right away, there will be a small amount of fuel that is sprayed
onto the intake valve or that drips off the injector due to a leaking check
valve, these fuel drops will build up over time. Eventually you will have
large chunks of carbon on the intake valves and pistons - "carbon build up".
These carbon deposits can starve the engine of fuel when first started in the
morning. The reason is the carbon deposits dry out and act like a sponge when
the fuel get sprayed on them, instead the fuel flowing past the intake
valve,into the cylinder. The fuel gets absorbed by the carbon build up. The
deposit on the pistons can increase the compression ratio and cause
pre-ignition before the spark plug actually fire - causing "engine knock" or
"ping".
	There are a few ways to remove the deposits: You can physically scrape them
off with a pick. You can use chemicals to reduce the build up. Or you can
blast the carbon away with a walnut shells blaster - the walnut shells burn
away once the engine is started up after the procedure.
