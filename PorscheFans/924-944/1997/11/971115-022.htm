Jay,

Yes, this is done without removing the oil pump.  You need to take off 
the cam belt, then the crank pulley bolt and pulley.  The seal you want 
to replace is a radial oil seal installed in the front of the oil pump. 
 While you're in there, check the condition of the metal sleeve on 
which the oil seal rides.  If you can catch your fingernail on a groove 
worn into it by the seal, you should replace the sleeve.  This is 
normal, btw.
