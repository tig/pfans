>"Walter W. Spector"  wrote:
>One way of mounting a smallish antenna in a non-destructive way
>is next to the hatchback.  If you lift up the hatch and examine
>the side rain channels near the top, you will see that there are
>two holes with hex bolts in them on each side.  I've heard that
>these were apparently provided for some sort of ski rack.
>
>Bend a thin piece of moderately stiff aluminum to curve around
>the hatch and drill it to match the two holes.  Once the plate
>passes out of the way of the hatch, bend it to level off and drill
>to match the desired antenna.  Paint the aluminum black for proper
>'Euro' look.
>
>Using a bit of cardboard to make an initial template is highly
>recommended.  Note that it might take you two or three tries to
>make one that you like the looks of.
>
>I, and at least two other 944 owners I know, use a mount like this
>for VHF/UHF ham antennas.  It should also work well for cell phone
>antennas.  A small active antenna for the AM/FM broadcast radio,
>like the one you mentioned for the Golf, should also work ok.
>Though you must do something attractive with the amplifier (it
>must be mounted as near the base of the antenna as possible in
>order to work correctly.)
