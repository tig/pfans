Dear BadBob & P-Fans
	The 968 DOES NOT have "Vario-Ram" system like that of the '96 & up 993
models. The 968 DOES HAVE "Vario-Cam" - if you do not believe me look at Pano
- Up-Fixin' Vol IX, page 15.  There are no moving parts of this "Dual Inlet
Path System". The 993 "Vario-Ram" system does have moveable parts - change
the path the air must flow to the cylinder head - and - effecting the air
flow characteristics. 
	The 968 does have a "Dual Inlet Path System" which I think you have confused
with "Vario-Ram" system. A "Dual Inlet Path System"- where intake air enters
the manifold from the front as well as by a divider and bypass tube from the
back/rear of the manifold. This in-effect gives a "harmonic pressure
pulsations" - a sort of super charging effect base on the speed as which the
piston pumps/move air flow. On the 968 the speed in which this comes into
plat is in the 4000-6400 RPM range. The one draw back of the split manifold
is a loos of power in the mid-range, which is more than offset by the "Vario-C
am" feature the 968 employs.
