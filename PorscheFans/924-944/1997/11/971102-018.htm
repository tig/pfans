Pfans,

A while back there was some discussion about converting an S2 engine to
that of a 968, and getting the variable cam timing of the 968 by exchanging
as few parts as possible.  Jim Pasha had some comments on this topic that
might be of interest.  I believe George B. is also of the same opinion. 

>Well, this is going to stop your project a bit. First, the head will bolt
>right on. It is different, though, enough to be another casting. The DME is
>totally different as is the block for the hydraulic tensioner. Sorry. The
>computer controls all valve timing based on the perfomance map in the
>computer. It takes temperature, rpm, etc. to decide what to do.
>
>Making your car lighter would help as the 944S2 coupe is about 3000 pounds.
>My 1983 944 coupe weighs in at 2750 pounds, so the power from your S2 would
>make the 83 a killer. It all bolts in.
>
>The 968 drivetrain bolts right into you S2. The engine wiring harness from
>the 968 must be used for the additional electronics from the DME. I'd buy a
>whole package and do it that way. You need the electronics to get the
>hoprsepower.
>
>But you still have to overcome the weight issue. The Carerra Cup Turbos
>weighed about 2800 pounds, though there were some Escort series 951s at 2600
>pounds with fiberglass panels and no insulation. Those cars were built for
>purpose and many things were left out when they were built.
>
>It's not a dumb idea at all. Enthusiasts have been doing this since the first
>2-cylinder Oldsmobile was built. You get about 30 horsepower with an engine
>swap here.
>Jim Pasha
>
>

Would appear that, once more, seemingly straightforward approaches to power
cost more than you would hope.
