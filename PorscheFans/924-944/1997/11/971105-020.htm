> The other thing is my rear defrost: the relay keeps fusing the contacts
> together whenever I switch it on. I then have to unplug the relay, open it
> up, break the weld on the contacts, and retry. It is acting like there's a
> short to ground, but I'm still getting +12V back at the hatch (on left side,
> right side is properly grounded), and with the defroster grid unplugged,
> and the relay shorted out, I have no problems. Does anyone know what
> the correct resistance of the rear window defroster grid should be?

If you are not blowing fuses, and the rear defroster works when the 
contacts weld themselves together, then you need to replace the relay.  
The contact points are plated to increase durability.. if the plating is 
worn away the points can stick together, as the base metal is soft.
