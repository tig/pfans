I picked up the car yesterday from Manhattan Motorcars.  They had kept
the car for almost two weeks from the date of purchase going over
everything carefully.  I made quite a pest of myself with 'phone calls
and faxes passing on suggestions from PorscheFans about what areas to
check on a Turbo that has seen so little use.  

The dealer seemed genuinely sincere about making sure everything about
the car was right and I drove away feeling very satisfied.  I am
scheduled to return the car at 2,500 miles to readjust the timing belt.
At that time, the dealer will replace the tach with a back-ordered unit
since the boost gauge is stuck.  They will also replace the fuel
gauge/sender unit which is not reading accurately, although this may
correct itself after a few refuelings.

It turns out the car has a very sophisticated after-market stereo system
installed by the first owner.  Not my thing, but the younger mechanics
were quite effusive about it.  The radio/cassette unit was a removable
Sony that replaced the original "Reno" radio and was either not
delivered by the first owner or mislaid.  The dealer installed a new
Alpine unit with detachable face plate, remote control and CD changer
control (for a remote unit if I ever want to add one) at no charge.  It
does sound nice.

After thinking seriously about putting my S in storage and preserving it
as a "museum piece", I decided "Naw, this car deserves to be driven". 
The shakedown drive was from Manhattan to Newport, about 200 miles,
mostly on I-95 I'm sad to say.  However, I did detour on a nice open
stretch of Route 1 from Westerly to Saunderstown.  What a wonderful car
to drive! No problems at all and I was impressed by how the car planted
itself on the pavement in a sweeper at 120 mph.

Next week I will take a longer trip to northern Maine by way of Lake
Winnepesaki.  My three year old son will join me.  Austin lives in
California and already is a car fanatic (ever since he figured out the
TV remote, he surfs the channels for motor races.  He likes the midgets
the most now, but I am sure this is a phase he will out-grow.  When they
flip over, he shouts "Oh-oh").  I think he'll approve of the 951S.

Jim Foley
