According to Jeff Stone, at Kelly-Moss Racing, the milling/machining process
provides a superior surface for the pads.  He suggested finding a place that
turns flywheels and getting them to do this.  For street use and AX/driver's
ed, I don't think it would make much of a difference.  For true club,
semi-pro and pro racing, I'd take Jeff's advice.  Kelly-Moss is one of the
most successful Porsche racing outfits in the history of IMSA/Firehawk
endurance racing.  Incidentally, Jeff always suggests (and sells) Pagid pads
for the track.  They are what Porsche AG and Porsche Motorporsports North
America use. I alternate between the Pagid orange compound and Performance
Friction 90 compound (used by Winston Cup cars), depending on the track.
