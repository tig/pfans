Tim, 
 
Toss them and by new ones. 
 
The internal grain structure has been distorted and turning the rotor will
not 
solve this problem and the next time you get them nice and hot they'll warp 
again due to the distorted structure. 
 
If you wish to keep them,  you must have them annealed to relieve the
internal 
stress (distorting) then have them trued (turned).  This will be more 
expensive then the cost of replacing them. 
 
If you check pano or excellence you will be able to find new rotors for
under 
$40.  If you factor the cost to turn the rotors and the risk of warpage
again 
as well as the down time,  replacement is is relative inexpensive. 
 
My $0.02
