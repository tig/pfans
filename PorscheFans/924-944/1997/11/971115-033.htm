On Sun, 16 Nov 1997, Whittaker Hamilton wrote:

> my question is what is a leakdown test i hear people talk of so often.
> is it not a compression test?
> what are the benifits? how is it done?

Simply stated, a leakdown test is performed by pressurizing the cylinder 
with compressed air via the spark plug hole.  The loss of air is 
expressed as a percentage.  Also, the point of leakage can be pinpointed 
by listening in appropriate areas for air loss.

It is not a compression test, as the engine is not turning.  You are 
merely measuring air loss in the cylinder, which better pinpoints the 
source of the compression loss.
