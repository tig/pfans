Michael,

Don't drilling it because of the possibility of putting metal shavings
into your transmission.

Probably the easiest thing to try is to get a manual impact wrench.  A
good impact wrench to try is the craftsmen hand held one.  It basically
looks like a screwdriver handle with a 1/2 inch drive on it.  This is
nice because you hit it dead on with a hammer.  The combination of the
driving force of the hammer and the torque force of the wrench loosen
bolts while keeping the tool in the socket.

I realize there isn't much room where the filler plug is, so this may be
moot.  If that is the case try to go up a size in allen heads (18mm).  

If things are looking really grim, reverse thread the inside of the bolt
(you might have to bore it out), and screw in a bolt and use the bolt
head to loosen the bolt.

And remember, penetrant, penetrant, penetrant...

Good luck!
