Actually, "dry sump" is mis-leading. 

Easiest way to think about it is "where is the majority of the oil
stored"?  For a 911/914, it's in an external (outside the engine) oil
tank.  For the 924/944/928 it's in the engine itself (actually in the
pan). More accurately, the "sump" is the oil pump pick-up area. If the
"sump" area is fed from an external "reservoir", it's commonly referred
to as "dry".
