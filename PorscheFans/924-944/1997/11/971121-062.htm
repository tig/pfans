	If you enjoy the theory of "whys and how's of horsepower tuning I highly
recommend the following books:

"Performance Tuning in Theory & Practice" 
by A. Graham Bell,  ISBN#0-85429-275-6

"Tuning - New Generation Engines for Power & Economy" 
by A. Graham Bell,  ISBN#0-85429-609-3
	
	These two books shed some light on mysteries of "making horsepower", they
are a great read for the tech-ies out there on the list. 

"Bosch Automotive Handbook" by SAE (society of Automotive Engineers) lots of
tech information. Tech-ies prepare for overload!
available thru book stores & SAE, ISBN#0-89883-518-6

	Speaking of making more horsepower for turbo charged cars - I was wondering
if anyone out there has eliminated the PCV system. PCV (or Positive Crankcase
Ventilation) re- routes oil fumes and/or blow by back into the intake
manifold system. I believe that the oily film cuts down on the inter coolers
efficiency to dissipate heat. Bob's water injection idea - would also do the
trick but removing the PVC and just installing a breather I think would
really improve the heat transfer of the inter cooler. I would also remove the
inter cooler and thoroughly clean out the inside of it.  What does everyone
else think?
