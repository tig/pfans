Mark wrote a few days back:
>2). With the new chips I notice a little improvement in low end torque and 
>driveability and smoother acceleration but when in 3rd or 4th gear on the 
>highway accelerating from about 4000 RPM the car feels much more sluggish. 

There is definitely something wrong there.  Perhaps the car already
had chips?  The car should feel much more responsive in the bottom end
and it should pull like a 10-ton truck rear ended you when you get on
it.  Did you figure this out already or are you still having the 
sluggish acceleration problem?  With turbocharged cars, when something
doesn't feel right, it's NEVER a good idea to keep on driving it that
way;  there are too many variables that could destroy your engine in
quick order to just ignore the problems.

> I changed the Plugs with the chips and the car has 90K on it.  In general 
>there seems to be a flat spot in the torque curve at 4000-4200 RPM.  Can 
>anyone else comment???? Farzaan - the Cup car computers you spoke of and 
>tested, are they just chips or would I have to buy the whole units????

The flat spot is generally normal with the boost bolt setup, but it should
not feel sluggish.  The boost bolt is the best method for beginners or
people who don't want to worry about reliability so I recommend for now
to stay with that setup.  The cup computers are quite expensive, but a few
list members have sourced them out.  One even managed to find the so-called
"head-gasket" computers which pump out over 300hp, but run so lean they
used to pop head gaskets in the other make series all the time.  Luckily
for that individual, his local area has 98 octane gas so he should not
have a problem.

>3). My front tires are now gone after about 13K miles.  They are Dunlop 
>SP8000.  I have been happy with the traction and in the back of my head I 
>knew that they would not last long but 13K went by much quicker than I 
>expected.  Has anyone who used SP8000s tried anything that works as well or 
>better and lasts longer?  These tires have never seen the track.

I'm in the same dilema.  The S0-2 is the best tire around, period.  But,
it's expensive and isn't holding up very well on Paul's car.  In fact,
at a high speed auto-x (3rd gear) we did in Bremerton, Paul's tires chunked
where mine did not and we both ran the same times and have similar driving
styles.

I've been blaming the tires as my limiting factor, but the more I race
at high speed events, the more I feel it's the chips.  I just don't have
the low-end grunt with the cup computers that Paul has with the APE chips.

As long as I can get the revs above 4000rpm, I can match Paul's times
with my SP8000's.  But, if it's a low speed event, I just hang back about
1 second.  Maybe I'm just better at high speed stuff or maybe the car is,
I really don't know.

I think I'm going to try the SP8000 ULW version next.

     9. Gas tank - smells like leaking while filled to the top. Any 
     potential weak places?


There's a big hose that connects the filler "funnel" to the tank itself.  
It's held by clamps, which may have come loose with time and allow fumes to 
escape.  Fortunately, you can fix/replace the hose and clamps by going 
through the filler assembly from outside the car.  Unfortunately, you can't 
tell if this hose is causing the problem until you go in there to check.  
It's a couple hours labor, but is not as nasty as having to drop and 
replace the entire fuel tank!  While you're in there, might as well replace 
the little ducts that vent fumes (pressure?) too.
