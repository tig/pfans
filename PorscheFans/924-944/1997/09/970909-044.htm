There is no secret to changing a timing belt. The trick is to take it to
a REPUTABLE SPECIALIST OR DEALER. Thus avoiding problems. The belt
requires tensioning by a special Porsche manufactured gauge that is
almost prohibitively expensive. I'm sure you have been made aware of
what happens if the belt breaks. Find a good shop and build a rapport
with the service manager and head mechanic, it will all help in the long
run.
