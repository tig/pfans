Stu, this is the first I have ever heard about removing the clutch housing
without dropping the transaxle.  I find it VERY difficult to believe,
having replaced a few clutches myself.  In fact, if you look at the
relative position of the aft end of the transaxle and the spare tire
'bump', there is very little clearance between the two.  The transaxle
mounts don't have bolts long enough to allow the tx to drop enough to clear
this.  The torque tube/drive shaft must slide out several inches for the
clutch housing to clear the end of the driveshaft.  I would guess that if
someone is somehow doing this, the are most likely damaging something in
the process.
