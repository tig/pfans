     Gary,
     
     I saw your post about the seat switches. I've had the same problem 
     with mine. All I did was to remove the seat from the car and uplug it. 
     This gave me more slack on the cable. 
     
     I was then able to take the switch apart. CAREFULL there are a lot of 
     pieces that spring out. Then I cleaned the contacts within the switch 
     as they were burnt and replaced then using a electrical contact spray 
     to keep them clean. (Sorry can't remember a brand name). Test the 
     switch before you put the seat back in properly. Everything is now 
     okay. But I did take the precaution of getting the part number off the 
     switch just in case I had to buy a new one.
