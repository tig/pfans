As it turns out there were several problems.  

The story goes like this.  The shop tried replacing the DME relay, still
nothing.  They tested the ignition switch, as I suggested, and found
that terminal 15, which apparently supplies the DME relay, was not
energized. After they replaced the ignition switch, still nothing, so
they tried a new relay again.  This time the fuel pump started cranking
and the injection system pressurized, but still no spark.  They popped
the distributor cap and found a cracked rotor.  After replacing the
rotor and cap, the car started.  The O2 sensor was dead, so I had them
replace it, too.  While they were at it I had them tension the belts for
good measure.
