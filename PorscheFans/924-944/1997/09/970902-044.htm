On Tue, 2 Sep 1997 ItzEd@aol.com wrote:

> My '88 924S has developed a strong fuel odor, and I cannot seem to
> locate the  source.  The odor is not present while driving, but appears
> gradually after I  shut off the car.  If I close my garage door soon
> after parking the car, it  becomes unbearable within about 10-15 minutes. 

One idea to check out is where the fuel gauge sender screws into the fuel
tank.  This is accessed from under the carpet in the trunk.. there is a
square piece of white pad covering the the sender and its electrical
connection.  Remove the pad (it is glued on) and sniff delicately.  Next,
open the fuel filler door and check to see the grommet for the overflow
hole is in place.  Otherwise, overflow will leak into the interior instead
of onto the pavement via a little plastic hose.  However, this problem 
would only cause gas smell right after filling the tank.

Keep a fire extinguisher handy until you solve the leak.
