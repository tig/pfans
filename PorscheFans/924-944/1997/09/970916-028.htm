Karl - Don't feel bad.  My car does this every other time I drive it.

- My weird solution -
I found that if I push the defroster switch (lower button to left of fan
switch) until the relay clicks, then wiggle it while in the "out" position,
I can get another relay under the right side of the dash to click.  At this
point the air begins to cool off and all is well.  I'm assuming that the 
second clicking relay controls the hot water valve, as it takes about 30
seconds after it clicks for the air coming from the vents to completely 
cool off.  It sometimes takes ten tries wiggling this &%#@* switch for
the second relay to finally click.  The REALLY CHINTZY design of 
the HVAC panel switches is to blame for this - anyone who has 
disassembled the A/C switch because it refuses to stay in the "ON" 
position knows what I mean - a tiny plastic pin inside the switch (easily
broken) is all that keeps the A/C on.  I'm going to take the panel apart 
again to see why the defroster switch does this.
