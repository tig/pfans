Page 21-10 of the 944 turbo workshop manual states:

Maximum boost pressure of 1.75 bar (absolute value) is reached at
approx. 3,000 rpm.  At higher engine speeds (engine type M 44.51, non S)
the boost pressure drops again and reaches a boost pressure of 1.52 (+
or - 0.03bar) absolute value at 5,800 rpm.  With engine type M 44.52 (S
and 89 turbo), the boost pressure remains constant at 5,800 rpm (1.75
bar absolute value) and drops only after this value has been passed.

All pressures have a variance of + or - 10%.

The gauge in the car reads absolute pressure, which is your normal
atmospheric pressure plus the engine charging pressure.  Most pressure
gauges read pressure above atmospheric pressure, so an external gauge
reading .8 bar of pressure would actually be at about 1.8 bar absolute.

If normal atmospheric pressure is 14.7 lbs. (1 bar) at sea level, then
1.75 bar on the gauge would be a total of 25.72 lbs. of pressure.  Your
porsche would be running about 11 lbs. of boost (.75 bar).  With proper
fuel, ignition timing and engine cooling, an intercooled 944 turbo
should be able to run slighlty moore boost in stock trim (Please, don't
anyone swamp me with boost opinions).  Boost is horsepower. and it adds
up quickly.

For reference, the TAG Formula 1 Porsche V6 engine, at 1.5 liters, ran
up to 3.2 bar (47 lbs.) of boost and put out over 800 hp.

Hope this clears up the boost mystery.
