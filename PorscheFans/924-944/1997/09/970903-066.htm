     I have recently switched to Mobil 1 synthetic in my '86 951's 
     transaxle, and it does in fact shift easier.  I won't know if it's 
     better in the cold until I drive the car this winter, but I anticipate 
     only a slight improvement, if any at all.
        I have heard that all synthetics in general can "cause" leaks in an 
     older engine that has been drinking natural oil all its life because 
     synthetics are better at finding all those nooks and crannies (i.e. 
     weak and potentially leaky areas that naturals rarely reached anyway).  
     I don't know how this may apply to transaxles, but I haven't heard of 
     anyone complaining about springing leaks after switching to synthetic.
        As for the hype about Mobil 1 being corrosive on the yellow metals, 
     I don't believe it.  I know several people who race 951's and 944's 
     with Mobil 1 and haven't had any problems.
