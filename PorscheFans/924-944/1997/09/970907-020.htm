> Subject: TDC Inspection Hole on 951

> First, on the 86 944 T, does anyone know where to look for the
> inspection hole for the flywheel TDC mark? I can't find it, but found
> the TDC and rev sensors where I thought the inspection hole would be?

The hole is next to the TDC and rev sensors.  If you look at the sensors from
the driver's side, (leaning over the fender) the hole is directly above the
sensors.  The hole is a small rectangle, with a metal marker, the marker goes
over the line that is above the OT on the fly wheel.

>
> Second, the balance shaft TDC marks are about -5 degrees (about 1 tooth)
> off at TDC on the camshaft, is this normal?

Are you sure your at TDC on the engine?  Find that out, your camshaft could be
off by a tooth.  In general, everything aproximately lines up.  Do to the
length of the belts (they sometimes stretch a little) someone might not be able
to exactly line them up.  If you don't notice any odd vibrations, it is
probably okay.  But first find TDC with the flywheel.

>
> Third, for the balance shaft idler pulley, how far from the belt should
> this pulley be set? Should it be touching the belt?
>

The idler pulley should be .5 mm away from the balance shaft pulley.  Turn is
counter-clockwise.  The idler pully seems to touch the upper portion of the
belt regardless how you set it.  I'm pretty sure this is where the hideous
whining comes from on these cars.
