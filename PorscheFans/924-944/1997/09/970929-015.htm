The amount of travel you need depends on your spring rates.
I'm not sure how much you would need for the stock springs.
Bottoming out on the rear would definitely cause a very sudden
oversteer.
The easiest way to see if the suspension is bottoming is to place a ball
of prestick (putty) on the bump stops. Then go drive around some hard
corners and check to see if the putty if squashed, if it is then the
height of what is left is the amount of travel you have left. (I think
this came from Fred Puhn's 'How to make your car handle')

Good luck
