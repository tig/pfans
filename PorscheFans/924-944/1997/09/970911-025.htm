Not having had the pleasure of grit under my fingernails
for quite some time, I bled my brakes last night.  Over
time I've used several methods: i) traditional pedal pump w/ partner,
ii) Mity-Vac, iii) homemade pressure bleeder (EZ-Bleed knock-
off).  The only method that ever gave me a hard brake pedal
was traditional pumping.

Last night, lacking a partner, I hooked up my pressure
bleeder AND pumped the pedal as well.  Very good results -
the pedal is so hard and high now, that I have to re-train
for heel-toe shifting.  I speculate that the caliper (and
master cylinder) pistons need to be moved back and forth
in order to release gas/moisture.

Taking the car out for a test drive, I noticed that the ABS
threshold is higher than previously.  This is really curious
to me and I wonder if anyone has a plausible explanation.
