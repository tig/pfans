>      Has anyone had problems using the higher wattage bulbs?

Possible problems would be: current draw too high for wire size, and
hence voltage drop that lessens output, too high heat in reflector
(especially fog lights and high beam projectors) and hence shorter lamp
life length, too strong light and (possibly) uneven light projection (as
80/100 W may be slightly worse aligned as they are not 'legal' even in
Europe. A relay will fix the first problem, cold weather will fix the
second, oncoming traffic will tell you about 'too strong light', and a
badly aligned lamp will throw the beam adjustment off.  
> 
>      Did the euro cars use them, if so are the harnesses different?

Standard Euro (E-marked) is 55/60 low/high. Illegal 80/100 or 50/100
bulbs are popular here, and is sold 'for racing use only'. HArnesses are
not different, but current draw must be dealt with. I recommend a relay.
> 
>      Does anyone know how many amps a 100 watt bulb draws?

100W/12V = 8.3 A. 12V is nominal, and the real value could be up to
13.2V (7.5A).

Fuses will blow at between 10-50 % overload, so a fuse of 7.5 may hold
on for longer than you think. It may also blow when you do not want to.
Melting the switch is not the broblem (the relay takes care of that) but
melting a piece of wire between the relay and the lamp (and possibly set
fire to the wiring harness and more) is a real concern. That is why a
relay solution is recommended. A 25 % increase of fuse value is not a
big deal, but be advised that there is a (small) risk for problems.  

IMHO, the difference between standard sealed beams and H4's is much
greater than the difference between 60 and 80 W, especially if the
higher current indusces a voltage drop. A noticeable difference will
mean relays and 100 watt lamps.
