Hydraulic lifters are supposed to balance out the force from the valve
spring in such a way that there is no lash at all (ideally). Oil 
pressure is used for 'pumping' the lifter up, and pressure will release
slowly. Lifters will compress if there is pressure on them, but on a
thight lifter, that takes some time. 

Basically, the clatter heard sometimes comes from a lifter that have
been sitting for a while, with the valve open, and hence the lifter
pressed together by force from the valve springs.      

So it is safe to say that finger pressure alone cannot compress a good
lifter.
