Anthony,
        Unfortunately, I do have some experience with this. Right before the 
UPS strike, I had new tires put on my Fuchs, wouldn't you know it, they 
incorrectly mounted the 225/50-15's on the 7x15 rims, and the 205/50-15's on 
the 8x15 rims, and scored grooves around the periphery of the rims. I showed 
the damage to the manager, and after some discussion, he agreed to have the 
damage fixed. I did a bit of research, had a local shop give me an estimate on 
repairing the damage (~$550), but decided to have a place that specializes in 
Porsche wheel repair (Rennsport) do the work. Of course the tire shop deals 
with UPS only, so they insisted on waiting until the strike was over. I think 
every other person in the US sending their wheels to Rennsport must have done 
the same because I have had the car on Jack stands for the last three weeks. I 
should be receiving the wheels back today (shipped last Tuesday). I will let 
you know how it all pans out. Make that tire shop that damaged your wheels pay 
for the damage! It certainly is a struggle to get quality work done. Make sure 
the next place you have tires mounted at uses a contact-less tire changing 
machine (they are expensive ~$10,000).  I am going to the dealer, ~$80 to 
mount and balance 4 tires, ouch..

Good Luck,
  Ezra
