Hi Michael;

46 PSI front and 42 PSI rear should be a good starting point for your
Hoosiers at the track.  Watch them closely after each run session.  If you
start to see a ring forming on the outside edge of the tire add 6 pounds of
pressure before going out again.  The Hoosiers are lighter than the R1s and
do not have a stiff sidewall, therefore you use more pressure.  I hope you
have your car set up with about 2 degrees of negative camber in front.
After trashing my two front tires a rather short order I learned that a car
has to be set up with lots of negative camber to work with Hoosiers.  I
completely corded the front left outside and right inside edges because of
low pressure (42 PSI cold) and not enough negative camber(-1.3 deg).  They
are fast though!  Have fun.
