I think $2,500 is quite a fair offer for what you describe, particularly if
you live in an area where A/C is really needed for a significant portion of
the year.  Depending on what's wrong, you could spend upwards of $1,000
getting a major A/C component and related hoses replaced.  Getting a used
exhaust manifold should not be too expensive and labor wise should not be
all that bad.  Figure $300 to have someone change timing and balance shaft
belts.  The leaky P/S reservoir is no big deal.  Mine's been doing it for
years and the leak is so slow I only add fluid about once every 2 years. 
If the mess bothers you, I dont imagine the offending hose is terribly
expensive.  Good luck.
