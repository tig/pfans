In some cases, the rubber 'donut' will shear away from the metal spline
(that engages the driveshaft) without completely coming apart in chunks. 
If this happens, you will still be able to drive the car but will
experience severe lurching and it will probably sound quite noisy when
coasting down in a low gear (i.e. if you abruptly take your foot off the
gas at 4,000 rpm in 2nd gear).  The other scenario I have seen is more
troublesome in that you probably won't be able to disengage the clutch
sufficiently to shift.  In this case, the rubber donut does come apart and
some of those rubber fragments interfere with the pressure plate releasing
properly.  If your car has the original clutch then I think you may very
well be ready for a replacement (my original went at about 50K; the second
lasted 100K so maybe Sachs is improving them).
