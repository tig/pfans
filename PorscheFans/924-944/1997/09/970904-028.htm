I had this same problem last summer in Middle Tennessee. Not only is it hot here,
the humidity amplifies everything.  After about a week of this anti-freeze expulsion
routine, I noticed that my car's (86 951 79K miles) cooling fans were still running
after the car had been sitting for about three hours.

The problem was the cooling fan switch in the top left side of the radiator. By the
cooling fan switch being in it's final stages of life, it would stick in either an
always on/off position or sporatically turn on/off making the temperature guage race
to the redline at idle and return to normal, when driving. It took me about an hour
to replace the $9.55 thermostat

switch. 



1) Remove the battery ground terminal (never know when    the cooling fans are going
to come on and remove    fingers)

2) Remove Air Filter Box

3) Remove top cooling fan screws



From the bottom of the car

4) Drain Coolant

5) Loosen the oil cooler 

6) Remove bottom cooling fan screws

6) Remove the cooling fans

7) Replace cooling fan switch.



8) And of course, put everything back on.



Good Luck!
