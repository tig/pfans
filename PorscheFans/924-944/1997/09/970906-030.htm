I worked at a auto repair shop for about a year before I quit, and the
technicians there used a propane torch with a 2-3 foot section of vacuum
hose attached to it. They turned the gas on at a low level then ran the
other end of the hose around the area that they suspected the leak to be.
The RPM's would then pick up if there was a leak. ( just a reminder..
propane gas is heavier than air so, the leak may be lower than you think.)
