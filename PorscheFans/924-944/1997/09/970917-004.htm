Gary et al,

I have a 90 944 S2 that I purchased one year ago with 42k mi.
The car now has 58k mi. I auto-x the car regularly during the summer.

My repairs in this time include:
  i) Windshield wiper motor replaced
 ii) broken AC mounting bolt replaced
iii) wiper relay/timer replaced
iv) recharged AC (I consider this a repair ... these things
    shouldn't leak down as they do.)

The previous owner replaced the clutch disk (rubber center
disintegrated).

I've had no engine-related breakdowns.  The starter motor
may be on its way out.

Add to this regular maintainence -- timing/balance belts
and rollers at 45k, new tires, plugs, filters, fluids, pads etc.
I replaced the shocks & struts at 55k.

The performance improvement relative to a standard 2.5L 8 valve 944
is easy to feel, and S2s have done very well in
SCCA Solo II A stock.  So I believe it's fair to say they're  
significantly
faster than a standard 944.

Of the three Porsche mechanics here that I've dealt with, none
of them have remarked about specific problems with the 16 valve
heads -- of course I didn't ask either ...

This is my experience -- just another datapoint for you.
