The 944/968 shift rod is a narrow beilce of metal (perhaps 3/16x1" in cross section)

Over this are two rectangular rubber bushings, one at the top and one about 3" down.
They are perhaps 1/8"" thick and 1/4" tall.

The shift "knob" is placed over this assembly, which acts as a damper.

Early (though '86) shift knobs are aluminum, and in 87 they went to  a resin
knob.  Over this is a leather covber perhaps 1/16"

The combination of the rubber and leather is quite effedtive at
damping, since there are NO rubber bushings in the entire linkage and
it is much more direct than a 911, with the shift lever attaching to
the torque tube rathern than the body.

I can attest that the lever without knob is painful to drive with.  I have done this after clutch replacements or while recovering thefactory knob.
