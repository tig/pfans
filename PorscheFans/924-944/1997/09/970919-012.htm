I hope this is not sacrilige, but here is a tip included in the iPD
catalog (aftermarket Volvo part supplier).

For the polyurethane and polygraphite bushings that they sell, they
suggest that before installation, one should fit the bushing where it
belongs with no lubrication and then rotate it.  Then remove the bushing. 
Any prominent points where the bushing or the control arm/sway bar/etc rub
will be evident and should be filed down.  Repeat until the parts rotate
freely without "catching".  Then lubricate as usually and install.  iPD
claims that this will greatly aid in reducing noises.  To me this makes
sense as the friction will be reduced by spreading the pressure over more
area as compared to just the prominent points.

Now don't go crazy filing everything down to nothingness :-)
