 >OK, let me quote from the 944 Turbo  Information Technik Book (WKD 491 =
321 - available from your
local Porsche Dealer - about $5.00)...

"Precaution Against Excessive Charging Air Pressure"

"If the charging air pressure leaves the nominal curve and reaches or =
exceeds the safety curve (C)
for more than 3 seconds because of damage on the charging air pressure =
control valve or in the=20
knock and charging air pressure regulating system, the injection signal =
(t-sub-j) is switched=20
off immediately - the injection of gasoline is stopped.

"Injection starts up again only after the sensor plate in the air flow =
sensor has gone back to approx
3/4ths of the full deflection travel by way of the engine speed and or =
accelerator pedal position.
Full acceleration and driving is then possible after about 1 minute."

So, what I think is happening is that you have too much boost and the =
KLR is turning off your fuel...

I can't show you the graph (it.s on page 35 of this manual), but I can =
give you some data points of
the graph and you can draw this safety curve (C) for yourself...

1000 rpm           1.25 bar (absolute)
1500 rpm           1.55 bar
2000 rpm           1.82 bar
2500 rpm           1.96 bar
3000 rpm           1.95 bar
3500 rpm           1,94 bar
4000 rpm           1.91 bar
4500 rpm           1.88 bar
5000 rpm           1.82 bar
5200 rpm and higher flatlines at 1.8 bar absolute

If you are using the stock DME and KLR, then this is what the engineers =
used as a safety net to keep
from blowing up the motor if the rest of the boost control =
malfunctioned...
