The headlight switch on my '84 has failed.  The other night when I parked it,
the switch felt a little funky, and the next morning (I leave in the dark) it
wouldn't move *at all*.  I mean, that rocker would not budge a millimeter.
Tonight I disassembled it.  What a wonder of complexity! I counted at least
eight tiny springs that load the sliding contacts, a spring-loaded detent ball
for the rocker, and eleven terminals (counting the one for the integral
dashlight dimmer rheostat. The plastic had melted around one of the power
terminals, both on the switch side and in the connector, and it had oozed into
the sliding parts and resolidified.  It's highly doubtful I can resurrect the
switch -- I *am*  working on it  -- and the connector is iffy. 

Is these VW-Audi parts (if so, what part numbers)?  If they're Porsche parts
(what numbers?), where's a reasonably priced source?  I suppose a used switch is
too risky....especially *this* used one!
