Barry, congrats on removing the cv joints. I stripped 3 12 point bolts
and 4 bits when I did my clutch but they were all on the pressure plate.
Keep your drill  handy. 

The two things that were difficult for me were the speed sensors (broke
one which was stuck - the bakelite doesn't take much force) and the
speed sensor bracket - it has to be unbolted and is held on with a pin.
Also beware of the ground bolt on the top of the bell housing, its hard
to get at and almost impossible to see. If the bell housing won't come
off check under the heater valve(?) at least I think its the heater
valve.
If you haven't already,I would recommend you buy new bolts for the
pressure plate, flywheel and a new rear engine seal.

John Lowe


> Hi Gang,

> Well, that part of the clutch job (removing the CV joints) is done.
> 
> Total CV joint bolts stripped: ZERO! The 8 mm 12 point tool shows
> no signs of stripping.
> 
> As mentioned by a number of people, there were two keys to a successful
> removal of the bolts. The first is to make sure the bolts are clean.  I
> spent about 30 minutes with a can of Carb cleaner, and a wire brush,
> scrubbing and spraying. The second was to use a hammer to make sure the
> 12 point tool is completely engaged. Then I used a long 1/2 drive ratchet
> to smoothly break each bolt loose.
> 
> Total time to remove both joints (including cleaning area, but not
> jacking car up, etc.) was about an hour. I saved a lot of time by using
> the ratchet to loosen all 24 bolts, and then an air ratchet to spin the
> bolts all the way out.
> 
> I had planned on using my air impact gun to remove the bolts, but there
> isn't enough room down there to get the run in. As it was, I used various
> length socket extensions.
> 
> Thanks to everyone who offered advice on this subject. Once again, this
> list saves the day.
