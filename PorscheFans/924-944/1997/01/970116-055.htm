I too have done the clutch job from hell. I did it myself except for mating
the transaxle which required 2 people. It was more like a 40 hour job but I
repacked the CV joints, changed the rotors and replaced all the hoses at the
same time. You really don't need the worthless Haynes manual to do the job,
you just take everything off the bottom of the car from the front wheels back
(including the whole rear suspension). I would agree that it can be done by a
resonably talented  indivudual, but it is not a job that should be tackled by
a novice.  Anyway, congratulations on getting it done and welcome to the "I
survived the Clutch Job from Hell" club.
