> I have the same problem with my steering rack-- I can't seem to keep fluid
>in it and it's because the rack seals have aged to the point that they
>leak.  You're probably in the same boat as me-- in for a new steering rack.
>I would NOT run the car without power steering fluid for very long-- the
>rack was intended to be lubricated and power assisted.  Also, if it's
>leaking fluid out, there's a chance that dirt can get in and make the rack
>more difficult to eventually rebuild (among other problems).

My power steering unit was in need of servicing when I first got my car.  The
PO had neglected it long enough to require me to purchase a new (recon) power
steering pump which was not too expensive...    The  "other problems"
 however - as I just found out yesterday - 8 months later is that the leaking
fluid caused my swaybar bushings to rot away on that side of the engine
compartment.  Hence I feel it is a good idea to fix the problem as soon as it
is detected - A tell-tale sign of mine was the loud groaning noise the
steering made on cold days! - the leaking fluid was subtle enough be non
visible at first.
