>Any oddball gotchas to look out for?

Timing Belts and rollers must be replaced on time which for a 968 is
45,000
miles max. even if owners manual says 60,000.  Also water pump checked
and belts checked and retensioned every 15,000 miles.

Check A-arm/ball joints well before purchase.  Has been a problem on
lowered
and tracked cars. (~$500 in rebuilt parts)

You will need a new battery soon if it is the original.  

Rubber clutch disk centers can be a problem (~ $800 parts + 8 hr)

>The clutch in this car seems to grab close to the top of the pedal
>travel. Is this a sign of wear, of a necessary adjustment, or the nature
>of the beast?  It didn't slip at all on me.

968 has a hydraulic clutch which isn't adjustable per say.  Clutch pedal
can be
set for height adjustment so may have some effect on when things happen.
Have seller pressure bleed clutch slave before you buy and recheck to
see if
clutch releases high.  Mine is perfect but took a little getting use to
at first.
I have to rev higher to start in 1st gear than I was use to.  Everyone
who
has driven my car stalls it on the first try.

>At what interval should the timing belt be changed?

See above

>Do you 968 owners have trouble finding parts?  I've looked through
