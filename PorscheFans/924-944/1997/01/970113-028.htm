On a ten year old car, it is reasonable to presume that not all is in perfect
order.  Rising emissions would be good evidence of this.  I would not mess
with the mixture, per se, but rather find out the reason they are off.  Most
probable is a head temperature sensor that is sending a inaccurate reading
to the brain, and giving you a slightly rich mixture, but you might also have
worn valve guides, worn rings, or other things which result in slightly
faulty combustion.  If you pass the test, even though imperfectly, I would
not worry about it.
