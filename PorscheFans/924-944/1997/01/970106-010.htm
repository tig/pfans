Hello to the group.  If Everyone remembers a while back I posted that I
had just bought a 85.5 944 with very loud collision problems while the
motor was running.  Well I took it all apart, found the cam had slipped
1 tooth and polished the piston tops.  The slippage seems to have been
due to an improperly tensioned belt from the previous rebuild which
judging from lack of carbon and incredibly good shape of the cylinders
was performed very recently.  Upon further disassembly of the head I
found some post factory stampings looking like someone's initials and
7-95 which I hope means July of 95. Anyway I just did the valve job and
we are good there.  My problem (and Questions) now concerns the camshaft
assembly.

a 16 point allen head.

thread?  I don't want to go Blazing away with the impact in the wrong
direction. 
3.  Can anyone give the headbolt torque for my year? (85.5) It seems for
every aspect of this rebuild the manual (haynes) has been useless.  It
just says see Porsche Dealership. It is a Fel-pro gasket if it matters.
