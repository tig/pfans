I'm guessing it's a 12 point allen head bolt, also known as a Triple
Square. It looks like a Torx bolt, but has 12 points instead of 8. (Why
do we need Allen, Torx, AND Triple Square?)

I know those are used on the CV Joints, pressure plate to flywheel, and
flywheel to crank locations. I didn't know they were used anywhere
else. Snap On makes the tool, so does the Lisle Corporation.  The Snap
On parts are about $15 each. The set from Lisle (6mm, 8mm, 10mm, 12mm)
is about $15. I bought the Lisle set. After removing all the CV bolts,
and then the pressure place bolts, the head was pretty shot. So I
bought another 8mm tool ($4.00). 

You can also buy the tools from Performance Products, Automotion,
Tweeks, and other mail order places.
