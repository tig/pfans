Last year, European Car magazine ran a series of articles about bolt-on power
for the early 924 (Audi engine). I am sorry I don't have the issue dates at
my fingertips. The bottom line was that the only significant power increase
came from a new high performance head. The stock head simply can't flow
enough for ANY power increase, regardless of exhaust changes, cam changes,
throttle body changes, air cleaner changes, etc. It is a very expensive
proposition to make more power from an early 924. If you really like the look
of the 924, check out a 924S, which has the 944 engine and better wheels,
brakes, suspension, etc. BTW, on track tests, they were able to drop lap
times most significantly with suspension, wheel, and tire improvements.
