>I've been having a few problems with my 77 924.  First, it seems to idle OK
>when cold but once it warms up it idles way too high (1800 RPM).  I'm
>guessing it is the warm up regulator

Try the auxiliary air valve, which is tasked with controlling idle speed.
It should have battery voltage to it while the engine is running.  If it
does, replace it.  If it does not, check the source.  You describe a common
failure mode.

..  Also once it is warm the oil
>pressure seems to go much lower.  My guess there, rod bearings.

It would depend on how low, I guess.  All engines will show lower pressure
once warmed up and the oil has thinned out.   The most important measure is
what it does when pulling high rpms.  If it is ok there, then it is ok.
