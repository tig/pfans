Jerry Easley wrote:

> I've had it explained to me that [removing the thermostat] is a bad
> idea because the water begins to flow so fast without the
> thermostat in the way that it's no longer in the radiator long
> enough to cool properly thus causing the temp. to increase rather
> than decrease.

and Ted Smith  replied:

> Yup, good general rule for all water cooled cars.  Some restriction
> is necessary.  There are restrictors available in different temp
> ranges for various thermostat housings that slow down  the flow of
> water enough to keep it in the radiator to long enough to cool, but
> don't close when cold like a 'stat to force rapid warmup.

Ted and Jerry:

You've got it wrong... The problem with removing the thermostat is
that it cools the engine TOO MUCH, not too little.  Your engine was
designed to run at a certain water temperature; running it colder
than that can decrease its power and shorten its life.

The restrictors that Ted mentions exist in order to keep the engine 
from running too cold, not to prevent it from running too hot.
