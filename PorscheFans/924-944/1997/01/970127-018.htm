>Can any one help?
>
>I have recently restored a 1978 924 (LHD 2Ltr Injection) the car had been
>standing for 4 years prior to me purchasing it. The fuel/gas tank was flushed
>as the fuel was brown in colour. I have had the injectors cleaned, new warmup
>reg, new fuel filter however, there are still problems with the running of the
>car. The petrol is now clear and free from contamination.
>
>The problems are as follows:
>1) Difficlt to start when cold - the engine turns over and starts, runs for a
>few second and stops. This happens a few time and eventually it will run but
>ticks over at 400 rpm. If you touch the accelerator, it will stall. As it gets
>warmer it runs better however, it is very sensitive to the accelerator being
>pressed. When warm it ticks over at 950 rpm.

It sounds like your warmup regulator is not functioning.   Pull the plug and
check for battery voltage.   If not present, replace the appropriate relay.  
The low idle indicates that the auxiliary air valve is also not working.   Same
relay, same test.

>
>2)When warm the car runs quite well however, there is a lack of power. The car
>will not rev above 4,500 rpm (1st gear) 4,000 rpm (2nd & 3rd) and 3,500 (4th)
>It seems that there is a miss fire - like a rev limiter cutting-in. I do know
>there is not a rev limiter on the car and suspect there is a fuel starvation
>problem somewhere.

Sounds like old injectors fouled with old fuel.   Pull them and check the
spray pattern.   You are looking for a clean fog , not little streams like from
a squirt gun.   Be sure to replace the O-rings.

>
>3).The fuel pump some times make a strange noise and the reading on the voltage
>meter increases to 16 volts. This occurs every five/ten minutes and lasts for
>about a minute.

Do you get this reading at the battery?   Sounds like an alternator headed
south.
You should get a steady 14.5 volts from a fresh alternator.   Any more and
you will
eventually cook the battery.   Any less and you will chronically undercharge 
(something I wish my parts suppliers would do.

Overall, you are having the typical experience of taking any CIS car out of
storage.   Just a little more work to do.

Best of luck.
