pejones@pop.dn.net (Peter E. Jones) wrote:

>I'm not accumulating water in the rear wells, but in the last week have
>started  getting rainwater under the passenger's side seat and into the
>footwell behind the passenger seat (83 944). Problem is it occurs any time
>the car is in the rain, even when it's not moving... just sitting in the
>driveway is enough. Floorpan is tight, no holes.
>
>My son theorizes that the drain lines from the sunroof drain is clogged and
>water is overflowing the rim and leaking inside. Problem is that the seats,
>back deck, all else is dry. Only the floor under the passenger is wet. I've
>tried checking the lines from the sunroof drain... they *appear* to be open
>(I can blow in the end going through the wheel well and hear the hiss of
>air at the sunroof drain).
>
>Question: Any ideas? Other places where I could be going wrong? If I have
>to check the drain lines, is pulling the headliner off difficult? It
>appears to be molded plastic....

You should check the battery tray. It is directly above the passenger side
footwell, and the metal there has a tendency to rust, making holes water can
flow through. Stick your hand up the footwell, and see if the insulation
directly behind the glove box is wet. If it is, you probably have a hole in
the battery tray. I would check the metal even if it looks fine, since it
seems to rot from the inside instead of rusting, at least on my car.  The
metal has become soft, but it looks perfect with no rust spots.

Strange that nobody mentioned this already, though.. This problem is also
listed in the 944 FAQ, perhaps you should take a look at it.
