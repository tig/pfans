> Hello everybody,
> Since a few weeks I own a '83 "rauchsilber" 944 coupe with about 100K miles
> on the clock. The car appears to be well cared for, everything but the rims
> looks excellent ! Engine starts like a dream (even in the extreme cold past
> weeks) and runs very smooth. Nevertheless there are a couple of things that
> bother me a bit:

	Welcome!

> 1. "Knocking" sound when idling. Disappears above 1500 rpm.

	Uh oh. Actually, could be a lot of things. Where's it
	coming from in the engine compartment?

> 2. "Tick" from somewhere beneath/rear when driving away in 1st.

	The "tick" is probably a "click". Check your CV joints.

> 3. Headlights sometime fail to lower, but will do so spontaneously after a
> while.

	Check the headlight motor and gearing system. After 14
	years, you can sometimes get dirt and grit in them which
	will cause the system to "hang" occaisionally. Try a thorough
	cleaning. Your owner's manual should show you where the
	motor is at.


> 4. "Running" sound and slight vibration apparently from right rear wheel.
> Sound comes at about 45 mph, disappears at about 70 mph.
> 
	Sounds like a wheel bearing or bad tire. What do the tires
	look like? Are they the original ones? :)


> Since I intend to go back to the shop where the car came from with my
> little list of complaints, I welcome any comment from you 944 buffs !
> 
	Check the water pump and belts while you're in there...
