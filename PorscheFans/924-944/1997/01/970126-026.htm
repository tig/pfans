>First, try this:  When you're decelerating and the rattle occurs, put your 
>foot on the clutch pedal and put a bit of pressure on it.  Not enough to 
>disengage it but just enough to engage the pilot bearing.  If the rattle 
>stops, that's likely the problem.  If it doesn't stop I'd suspect something 
>else.  Try another mechanic.  My cars 944 and 944TS make some strange noises 
>on occasion from the driveline and bell housing and it's pretty hard to track 
>'em down.  Then the noises go away....... 

This is a fine proceedure, but it only serves to test for potential throwout
bearing troubles, not pilot bearing problems. The pilot bearing is located
at the terminal end of the driveline shaft (the end of the shaft that goes
through the clutch disk). Often, the pilot bearing is neglected during a
clutch change. I didn't see the original post, but if this is a fairly high
mileage car (or if the cluch has previously been changed) and if the noise
is continual at some level, it could potentially be the pilot bearing. My
advice...get another opinion from someone qualified to diagnose Porsche
problems.
