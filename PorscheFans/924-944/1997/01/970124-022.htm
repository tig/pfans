Steve writes: "So what is the result of a pilot bearing failure?  Will it 
destroy the clutch or just make a rattle and not be a big problem?  Can I  
keep driving or is it a FIX IT NOW sort of thing?" 
 
First, try this:  When you're decelerating and the rattle occurs, put your 
foot on the clutch pedal and put a bit of pressure on it.  Not enough to 
disengage it but just enough to engage the pilot bearing.  If the rattle 
stops, that's likely the problem.  If it doesn't stop I'd suspect something 
else.  Try another mechanic.  My cars 944 and 944TS make some strange noises 
on occasion from the driveline and bell housing and it's pretty hard to track 
'em down.  Then the noises go away....... 
 
I wouldn't worry about fixing it if it is the pilot bearing.  Unless it drives 
you nuts. If it gets worse, it will make more noises and possibly start to 
squeal.  Then it's replacement time. If you have to go to the trouble of 
pulling the driveline out to replace it, unless your clutch is very recent, 
you may as well replace the clutch too. 
 
Let me know what happens, I'm curious also about who your mechanic is?
