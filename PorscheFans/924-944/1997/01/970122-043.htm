is
what I wrote on the Hond Perf mailing list a while ago.  Note: this
equation does not predict top speed, it only relates speed to engine rpm.

To figure your approximate true speed use this simple formula:

rpm x tire_circumference/(trans_gear_ratio x diff_gear_ratio x 1056.0)

where:

rpm =3D engine speed, revs/min.
tire_circumference =3D rolling circumference of tire, inches.
trans_gear_ratio =3D transmission gear ratio, depends on gear.
diff_gear_ratio =3D differential gear ratio.
1056.0 =3D conversion factor to convert units to mph.
Note: for circumference in cm, use 2682.24 instead of 1056.0

=46or example, look at the theoretical speed of a 1996 GS-R with the
following properties:

rpm =3D 8000
tire_circumference =3D 73.7 inches (approximation based on 195/55-15 =
tires)
trans_gear_ratio =3D 0.787, 5th gear
diff_gear_ratio =3D 4.40

We get:

8000 x 73.7/(0.787 x 4.40 x 1056.0) =3D 161.2 mph

At 8000 rpm in 4th gear:

8000 x 73.7/(1.034 x 4.40 x 1056.0) =3D 122.7 mph

Obviously, a GS-R does not need to worry about running out of rpm in 5th
gear as it would reach its top speed of about 141 mph at 7000 rpm.

Hope this helps.
