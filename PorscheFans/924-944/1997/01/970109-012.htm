>Hello all,
>	My '88 944S is acting funny when it is cold outside.  I get a searching
>idle, and a slight misfire.  When I try to drive away, the car feels
>sluggish, and when the clutch is disengaged (ie. when I am about to
>stop) the engine drops to about 0rpms and sometimes stalls.   This
>disappears after about fifteen minutes of driving (about when the oil
>pressure drops to 3 bar.)  
>	I am guessing that I have a leak in one or more of the o-rings that
>seal the fuel injectors.  Without replacing them, how would I check
>this? 

I would agree that you have some sort of vacuum leak.  While the engine is
running, spray carburetor cleaner around everything from the air flow meter
on back.  If you get an RPM change you have probably found the problem.
This presumes that the plugs are clean and you don't have another problem.

 I also assume that I need a new O2 sensor since it is 140K miles
>old (as far as I know), and any vacuum leak will make an old sensor hunt
>like crazy.
>	Also, my steering wheel always angles left when I am driving in a
>straight line.  I also hear a clunk (a single clunk) when I have the
>wheel in total left lock.  What is this?  Tie rod ends?  Ball joint? 
>Both?

Take it to an alignment shop, which is where any of these things would get
you anyway.  They are capable of diagnosing anything in the front
suspension, steering, even if they are not a Porsche shop.  Then go from there.
