> feel.  I use the car only on weekends and not on the track but the tires 
> feel that they are not sticking to the road and have been advised that 
> they are too hard a compound for the cabriolet.  I am considering 
> putting on 16" 993 wheels and would like to know if Bridgestone SO1 
> tires would offer better performance (205/50 front and 245/55 rear on 7"

You're talking big bucks for S01 and new wheels. Have you considered
trying road race compound DOT approved tires for the street on your
existing wheels. They will wear out quicker than regular street rubber but
you did say it was a weekend car. If you spinout the car on R tires on the
street your license should be revoked. Bonus is they are cheaper,
typically around $135 each for 245/45x16. I'm partial to BFG R1's. I drive
R tires on the street and hate switching back to the regular stuff.

> Although the owners manual offers a sport package with 
> larger tires for the couple, can the cabriolet take a 245/45 wheel in 
> the rear with the stock suspension (I just had all new stock struts and 
> shocks installed)or will it put a strain on the suspension?

You can run 245/45's on all 4 corners with 7" & 8" rims on a S2 coupe so
should
work for you. I don't think it will wear out your suspension. A bunch of
us race with this setup all the time, no problem.
