I use the factory jack points halfway down the rocker panel, just
underneath.  The location is marked by a diamond impressed into the
sheetmetal.  The factory jack fits into a hole in the jack pad, but I just
roll my 2-ton hydraulic floor jack under there and hoist away.  Being
halfway down the car, both wheels are lifted off the ground.. very
convenient.  I use jackstands on the frame rail that runs from front to
back, so I don't get permanently flattened if the jack fails.  I like to
use a flat piece of wood under jacks and jackstands, to keep the
undercoating from getting damaged. 

I never considered lifting the car by the oil pan - all I can imagine is 
a massive crumpling action.  Aluminum?  Forget it.  Having the car's 
weight supported by the motor mounts must certainly ruin them.
