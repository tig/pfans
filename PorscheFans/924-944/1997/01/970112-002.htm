I also have the "poor man`s Porsche" being short of a few sheckles### meself.
The car is vastly underated and in my opinion has better lines than the
944`s steroid look. I had a similar high and surging idle which was
corrected by new injectors and a 02 adjustment by a local Bosch garage (the
motor was running way too lean). My car is an "82, Euro spec. 125 bhp
version, has covered 150k miles! I have covered 25,000 hard driving miles,
just checked valve clearance perhaps for the first time, no adjustment
needed! Car starts cold at 6 to 8 bar depending on outside temperature and
lowers to 2 or 3 bar at hot idle, up to 6 bar under load. Water temperature
does not rise above half way on dial unless there is a problem. When the
cold waether started this winter, -15 C. temp. raised to red and no hot air
from heater. Fitted new thermostat and enough anti-freeze for -50 C. no
problems since.

How do we get more power from our practical, dependable 924`s?

I asked the same question to "The Porsche Shop" in the UK and was advised
the following:
a)      Straight through middle exhaust box with 944 rear box.
b)      K&N air filter.
c)      Adjustable fuel head.
d)      Larger carburator body.
e)      Performance cam (out of stock! no name, sorry)
I have no personal experience of any gains achievable with these
modifacations and would be grateful to hear from anyone who does!
