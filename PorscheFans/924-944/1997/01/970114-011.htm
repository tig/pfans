I don't know what you mean by "cold".  It has been beyond thirty below 
here...

To answer your first question, as long as the car develops oil pressure
and runs smoothly, go ahead.  A secondary suggestion, however, is that
you should subsequently drive far enough for the car to warm up fully.
This has less to do with running than it does with driving off moisture and
condensation completely.  Therefore, if you have a five minute drive to 
work, warm it up for a while.  If you drive thirty minutes, get in and drive.

Regarding the stalling, it might indeed be that the battery was down, 
and Motronics are sensitive to low batteries.  It might also be your charging
system.  If you have enough poop to start the car, but the alternator
does not kick in, the car could do this on a cold morning.  Sadly, it
needs to become more chronic before you can be sure.

All of this points towards having the electrical system in top shape for
cold winter driving.
