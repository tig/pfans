On Fri, 3 Jan 1997, Dave Cooley wrote:

> Two things come to mind.  First, 944/951/928 valve lifters will often hang
> up after prolonged storage.  The vast majority of the time they will
> eventually pressurize and quite down.  You can accelerate the process by
> adding one of the top oils that have solvents that can remove the deposits
> that supposedly contribute to this.  Stuck lifters clearly make a clicking
> sound, rather than a deep knock.  If you had a stuck valve you would have
> serious miss associated with the noise.  Second, the dreaded #2 rod bearing
> demon lurks out there, and this sure sounds like it.  A deep knock, directly
> related to engine speed, and nothing affects it.
> An experienced mechanic should be able to hear the difference.  I would not
> drive the car until you have it sorted out.  A sticky lifter is no big deal,
> but a rod bearing can quickly lead to a tossed rod.


	I hope this doesn't come too late to help you out (I'm 1000
	messages behind, literally) but I can vouch for the difference
	between your noise and a sticky lifter. I have one of the sticky
	lifters and the sound is just a light tapping almost as if you
	were to tap on your window glass with a pen. Mine only taps on
	start up when it's very cold and it goes away after 2-3 seconds.
	Your sound seems entirely different. Hope it's not the worst
	case scenario. Good luck!
