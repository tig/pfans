Guys, here's the cold truth. The state of PCA Club Racing is INHO not good.
It is a lot different then it was in its early years. It has gotten to be a
jump off point to pro racing and the competition is fierce as is the
technology. It doesn't matter which car you buy or which class you are
planning to race- it's going to cost of bundle to make and keep your car
competative.
That being said I hope that anyone who has the urge does get involved because
it is more than a thrill. It's the most fun you can have in your car even if
Pamela Anderson is involved. Get an inexpensive car and make the necessary
mods. If push comes to shove, and it will, you can junk your initial
investment and put all those racing goodies into another cheap base car. Do
not take a car that you love and start Club Racing. Consider the car
disposable and you will have the right attitude. Not that Club Racing
approaches a destruction derby but be realistic, it is true racing. It's a
whole lot different then Drivers ed events and the wrong split second
decision can lead to damage. Stuff happens.
Have a great time!!
