>      My friend has an 84 944 that has been sitting for about three months
> (long story).  She recently started it up and the brake pedal felt "soft" but
> pumping brought it back just fine. Problem:  the clutch pedal has
> resistance for 1/2 the travel length, goes to the floor, stays there, has to
> be toe-pulled back up, and exhibits resistance for the top 1/2 of the travel
> length.  Gears, however, cannot be engaged at all (no pressure plate
> movement).  Everything was A-one when the car was parked.  Any
> ideas?
> 
James,

Sounds like the clutch hydraulics decided to go south while the car
was setting. This would be the master and/or slave cylinders. You
might try bleeding all the old fluid out and see if that helps. The
fact that the brakes were initially soft indicates that the fluid
is probably overdue for a change even if the clutch can be bled back
to life.

Good luck,
