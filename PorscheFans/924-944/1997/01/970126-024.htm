Hi, John,

You're right.  That's about what it takes.  I changed the seal yesterday and not without 
difficulty.  You just can't get the screwdriver to grip the old seal enough to pry it 
out.  I finally took a sharpened screwdriver and drove it into the side of the seal 
through the slot near the bottom of the flange.  The screwdriver then wouldn't slip off 
the seal and I could pry it out.  Also, I used a 2" long piece of 4" diameter PVC 
plumbing pipe and a block of wood to drive in the new seal.  The 4" pipe is just about 
a perfect fit.
