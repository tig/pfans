>My silver '84 944 has very few resident Porsche gremlins and no serious
>problems. Never let me down. It is well cared for and has just crossed
>the 100k mile mark. 
>
>Last winter for the first time the motor developed an intermittent hiccup
>in -12F weather. It felt like a fuel pump problem or electrical problem,
>with the revs falling and coming back up very quickly. This would never
>happen at startup (still amazes me how a 12-year old car, at -10F can
>start up at the first crank after sitting in the snow for a month), only
>after driving for more than 20 minutes. And equally promptly it would go
>away. At the time I checked the cap and rotor for water, plugs and the
>fuel pump and filter, and came up with no explanation. 
>
>The problem disappeared come spring, and appeared once in March on a 5,000
>mile drive to California from Washington state. Since then the car has a
>new cap, rotor, plugs, fuel filter. The hiccup reappeared this winter,
>very short, jerky losses of power on the highway. Never actually died,
>more like somebody was squeezing the fuel line for nano-seconds at a time.
>The only thing I can think of are the ignition wires, which are 100,000
>miles old. Since there is negligible loss of HP in a test, would the old
>wires cause this strange malady. Are the hiccups a precursor to worse
>things? Is my trusty 944 finally about to roll over and play dead?
>

I would say that replacing the plug wires at this point is not a bad idea,
but still a stab in the dark.

is 
causing the problem...
