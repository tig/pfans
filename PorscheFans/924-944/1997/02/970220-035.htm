Woo hoo.  I finally received a new rear timing belt cover today.  After a few
shipping snafus, I've finally got it.  Of course if I'd known it was going to

Tip:

To install the cover into a turbo 944, install it from underneath the car.  It
won't go in from the top, even if you remove the upper radiator hose.  To get
it in from underneath, you can unbolt the oil cooler mounting bolts, and push
the cooler and it's hose enough out of the way to squeeze (and I'm talking
about squeezing baby) the cover into place on the engine.
