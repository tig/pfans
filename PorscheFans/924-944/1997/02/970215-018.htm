I fitted one on my previous 924 1983... it had a hole too (I don't think
it's worthwhile when there isn't a hole yet...).
Anyway, there are only 3 contact points on it: one for a permanent +, one
for permanent mass and one that runs to the switch in the console and is
connected to + too when you turn on the switch. It's very simple...

And while we're at it, does anyone know why the original switch has 3 wires
connected to it (instead of just 2) ?? I recently replaced my rear wiper
switch with one of a 911... that's one that pops back automatically, you
can't leave it on... Since I only want the wiper to wipe once when I press
the button, I found this more logical. Anyway, the 911 version has only 2
contacts, the seller said it wasn't possible to install the switch in my
924S... He was wrong, it's real easy ! 1 of the wires simply seems to do
nothing at all... I simply let it hang there.
