> I could just as easily say this about virtually ANY Porsche.  In many ways

> Maintaining them is a lesson in frustration and big dollar repairs.  Some
> of the engineering, or lack thereof, in these cars is simply amazing.  For
> instance, the infamous 911 chain tensions... 30 years in the making... 951
> clutches that self destruct in under 30K miles and cost $2K to replace.

	You could just as easily say that about anything that breaks
	and requires maintenance. You have to consider the car and what
	it was designed for. Apologies to the racers aside, the Porsches
	are racing cars straight from the factory. How many racing cars
	are there that are designed to go 100K on the engine/clutch/tranny
	without any kind of rebuild? I admit, the Porsche clutch is
	weak in this area, though. Now, on to your Honda, what is it
	designed for? To go from point a to point b slowly and cheaply.
	If you track your Honda, you will quickly find that you have
	misused it and now all you have is a pile of scrap. But, that's
	your fault because you used the car for a purpose it was never
	intended to be used for. The Japanese car companies would like
	you to think that the Miata, the 300 ZX, the Supra, and the RX-7,
	etc., are in the same class as the Porsche. Well, they're not.
	How many tracked 300 ZX's have the original engine and tranny
	in them? Not many, I'll bet. If you're gonna talk the talk, you
	better walk the walk and that means building a racing car that
	is really a racing car instead of an econobox in disguise.
	I know they can do it, just look at the NSX.
