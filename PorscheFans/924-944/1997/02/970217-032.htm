	I think I just answered these questions. Generally, it's as
	much of a committment as you want to make of it. At the very
	least, it's more of a committment than one normally makes
	to a "car". Let's face it; if you just needed some transportation,
	you wouldn't be looking for a Porsche. So you obviously have
	at least a little something else in mind. Repairs and maintenance
	follow directly from how many miles you put on the car. If you
	don't drive it much, your bills will be cheaper. The one year
	warranty would be great as 100K seems to be the time when lots
	of things like shocks ($1000), water pump ($900), clutch ($1300),
	etc. like to go out. Those things could put a real bummer into
	your Porsche thrill if you had to pay for them. If you put 10K a
	year on it, I'd say your maintenance should average out to about
	$800 a year averaged over several years.
