Thore Bjerklund Karlsen wrote:

>The other day I noticed my 924 was leaking fuel, in the back near the right
>wheel. It was dripping quite fast when I first noticed it, and when I reached
>the repair shop it seemed to be dripping even faster, so I don't suspect it
> be something that has been going on for long.  Anyway, my mechanic told me
>_not_ to drive it any further, so it's in the shop, due for repair some time
>next week. I was just wondering if this happened to anyone else, and what the
>was? I recently filled the tank for the first time since the summer,
>maybe that has something to do with this?

Definitely the fuel accumulator.  It's stuffed way up behind the right wheel
and leaks when the diaphragm fails, or if you're lucky, when the fittings are
not tightened properly.  It's probably a bad diaphragm.  Mine burst after
the car had set unused for a couple of months.  A new accumulator cost about
$100 in the US (if you shop around) and takes about half an hour to install.
