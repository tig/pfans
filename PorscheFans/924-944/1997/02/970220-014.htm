You do not need a belt tesnioner for the Timing Belt - there is an
autotensioning device.

You may need it for the Balance Shaft - whihc has to be removed before
the timing belt can be removed...or you canb do the balance shaft by
feel, since it is not critical and does not turn anyting hard to turn
(like a camshaft).

THat's what the Porsch Dealer Mechanic told me anyway....

You need ot remove the tensioning mechanisms 0 3 13mm head (8mm) studs
hold it on.

Also - be REAL smart and paint the timing mark on the flywheel BEFORE
you inadvertantly move the crank!

Just did it.  Not fun.  But I saved $$$ so I'd do it again.
