> My wife can do pads.  If you can unbolt a caliper you're done!  You 
> will need to bleed the brakes.

I changed rotors on my '84 944 last summer and did not need to 
disconnect any hydraulic lines at any time, ergo no brake bleeding was 
necessary. You do need to be careful with the calipers though, and hang 
them out of the way on the axles with some wire, not by the hydraulic 
lines. The job took me 4 hours (with new pads) but it was my first time 
with the wheels!
