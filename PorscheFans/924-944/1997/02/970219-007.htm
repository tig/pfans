> I've recent noticed (today in fact) that the macpherson strut assembly is
> threaded on the bottom and allows for adjustment of spring compression (is this
> standard on turbos?).  The nuts (lock nut, adjusting nut) on this assembly are
> fairly big around, and have indentations in them (they are not standard hex-nut
> variety, but round) as if some sort of toothed tool is used to adjust them.

You have a turbo S, and they have adjustable front spring perches. This does
not adjust the spring compression, but does adjust ride height. With those
perches, you can easily corner balance the car, and / or adjust the front
ride height. 

I have heard that Porsche added the adjustments to make the car more comp-
etitive in racing, as the rules didn't allow modifying the front suspension,
and this way the car could be adjusted without changing parts.
 
> I noticed this when I was looking for some sort of ID on my front rotors.  I
> need to know how large they are, and what type they are (I don't think they are
> stock, they are large, and look greater than 12 inches)

They are probably stock. The 951S has BIG front rotors. According to my spec
book, the stock front rotors for the 951S is 304mm front, 299mm rear.

FYI, someone else posted about brake rotor thickness.
From the spec book:
Brake disk thickness new:
 Front  Turbo: 28mm
       TurboS: 32
  Rear  Turbo: 24
       TurboS: 24

Brake disk thickness Minimum
 Front  Turbo: 26.6
       TurboS: 30.6
  Rear  Turbo: 22.6
