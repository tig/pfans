Wheel offset is the distance form the hub mounting surface to the wheel
centerline, front to back.

A 7" wheel with zero offset will measure about 3.75" from the back rim
to the hub since the outer rim is generally about 1/4" thick, and wheel
width is measured at the INSIDE surface at the tire bead.

Wheel offsets are as follows (in mm, even though wheel widths are inches,
that's how porsche does it).

911 to '89  10.8 to 23.3 mm (9" is 23.3mm)
944 to '86  23.3mm
944 87+, 928, 911-89 C2 and on  53mm

944 up to '86 (pre ABS) and all 911/930 wheels are pretty much
interchangable.  

911C2 and later and all '87+ 944/968 and all 928
wheels are interchangable.

Hope that ccovers it.

INTERESTINGLY ENOUGH THE '86 944TURBO WHEELS (7+8X16") are actually
preferable on a 911 as they move the wheel center IN 12.5mm (1/2")
allowing clearance for larger tires.
