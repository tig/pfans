One note, I do not understand what the big debate or discussion is on oil
changes and oil change intervals, seeing that the price of regular blend
20W-50 is so minor and even synthetic is very reasonable, and the price of
a new engine say is so very, very expensive, what exactly is the advantage
of going a long time between oil change intervals.

My '44 has 98,000 miles on the clock, it unfortunately sees the open road
about 6 months of the year, May to Oct. When I take it out of the garage
in the spring, I start it up with a light weight oil IE 10W30, I run it
for roughly 20 minutes around my neighborhood and than drain and replace
with 20W-50.

It sees a fairly intense summer, just ask the RCMP officer who caught me
with the cruise on 190KPH. I put about 4-5000miles on it in the summer and
have no problem doing 3 or 4 oilchanges.

I do not think that ensuring that your "baby" is full of clean oil, is
excessive, I like to think of it as piece of mind.
