Perhaps someone else can confirm this, but I think the dash temp gage is 
from a sender in the head and is decoupled from the temperature of the 
radiator.  If that is true, the water cooled from the radiator may not 
be reaching the head.  The heater core is connected to the head, so 
running the heater cools the water in the head. Turning on the AC runs 
the fans that only cool the water in the radiator further.  

If the water in the head is truly warmer than the water in the raditor, 
then water must not be circulating correctly.  It may be that the 
thermostat is stuck, the water pump is not working well, or there is air 
in the system.  I'm sure there may be other causes of this problem.  Try 
bleeding the system first - its real cheap.
