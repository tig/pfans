At 10:33 PM 2/26/97 -0500, Gary Windham wrote:
>I have an '88 944 Turbo S.  The throwout bearing in the clutch is starting
>to fail, and with 94K on the clock I suppose it has every right to.
>Still, I'm surprised it is failing before the disk.  Here's a few
>questions I'd love to hear comments on:
>
>1.  I've done plenty of heavy work on my hobby cars before, but this is my
>commuter vehicle.  Is a clutch job horribly difficult, or just a pain?
>Are any special tools required?

Time-consuming and a real pain. You could look at Michael Kehr's excellently
documented procedure faq for guidance (probably better than the factory
manual, and certainly better than the haynes manual).

>
>2.  I've heard there are some good sources for genuine parts, but the only
>ones I have phone numbers for are the two big catalog companies.  Can you
>folks give me some alternatives?
>

Check your copy of Panorama or Excellence. I am hearing prices of $400 or
so for new Sachs clutch packages.

>3.  I gather my clutch disk uses rubber rather than steel springs.
>Catalogs pitch the steel springs as the "hot" upgrade.  My local Porsche
>repair shop is unimpressed.  What difference does rubber vs. springs make?
>

Your clutch has steel springs. Replace it with the OE parts.

>4.  What other parts should be replaced at the same time with clutch,
>pressure plate, and throwout bearing?  Pilot bearing?
>

Yes, all of the above...
