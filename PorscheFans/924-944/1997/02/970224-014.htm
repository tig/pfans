BFauv@aol.com wrote:
> 
> I just dropped my torsion tube to change my torsion bars, and reinstalled it
> (without the help of a manual), and I am in a quandry. I neglected to mark
> the position of the spring plate (attached to the torsion tube cap) relative
> to the tube. Is there a standard position in which the cap/plate should be
> installed? If i get it wrong, the ride-height will be off, and the geometry
> will likely be screwed up. 944 manuals are on back-order, so I'm a bit lost.
> If anyone knows, drop me a line, otherwise it will take me about 20 tries to
> get it matched up with its prior position...
> 
I think you have a couple of choices:

1. Find someone who has similar-sized torsion bars and ask them to make a
   cardboard template which shows the unloaded angle of the spring plates.

2. Do the math on the spring rate of the torsion bars. Knowing the weight
   of the rear of the car, the spring rate, the spring plate length and the
   desired loaded plate position should get allow you to get within the
   range of the height adjuster.

I am away from home now but I _think_ I saved the template I made when I
changed to the 28mm bars (from 23.5). Yell if you're interested.

No matter what you do you'll have to have the car aligned when you're done.
