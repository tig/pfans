> Contrary to what others might suggest, I'd start with the shocks set to the 
middle of the adjustment range and go from there.  Think of the shocks as 
springs which only add stiffness during initial turn-in.  The adjustment in 
single-adjustable Konis is a rebound rate, not a compression rate.  My 
experience has been that with mine set full stiff, I got turn-in push.  I 
backed off from full stiff, perhaps roughly a full turn, and found the 
handling much improved.
