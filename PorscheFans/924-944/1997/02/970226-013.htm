>Is this normal?  Or should I start looking for an internal oil leak?

If the car has been sitting or a while, oil settles all over the place,
and will need to come out.  I think all 911s also do this when sitting
for some time.  What you may be seeing is also rich smoke which is usually
a dark gray/black color that comes on under boost.  If it continues to happen
on a daily basis, then I would start to look at rings.  It's unusual for
a turbo to blow blue on acceleration because when the seals go, the oil
comes into the turbo under vacuum and puffs it out, unless it sucks it into
the compressor side in which case you could have it under boost too.  So,
check rings and turbo.  That wasn't of much use now was it :)

[Smokey wrote again]
>Let me put some forth some more info.  Since I've had the car there always has
>been a small trail of smoke coming from the car under full acceleration.  There
>are no external oil leaks (I checked this winter), and there is no coolant/oil
>mixing.

I can almost guarantee you that it is rich smoke.  I would think that Andial
would run the car rich with that kind of a setup with the K27 and all.  Even
my friend's 1 bar chipset runs really rich and spits out black smoke under
WOT.  Under part throttle, your 02 sensor takes care of the mixture, under
WOT, the O2 sensor is ignored and voila, black smoke.  It's pretty much
harmless, *but* excessive richness can wash the cylinder walls down and reduce
the ability for the oil to lubricate the rings, leading to ring failure.  Both
of my race Talons had this problem, but we had to run them that rich to prevent
detonation.  Excessive richness can also clog a catalytic convertor pretty
quick (RX-7TT are notorious for this), but the 944 Turbo uses a very cool 
cat bypass under WOT through the external wastegate so I wouldn't think there
would be a problem there either.

>So either oil is leaking from the turbo, into the exhaust (hmmm, hopefully not
>into the intake).  Or residual oil is being burnt off the cylinder walls, and
>the pistons go down.  Or oil is getting past the pistons into the combustion
>chamber.  Am I missing some places?

Get someone to ride behind you when you mat it and watch the color and tell them
to smell (make sure they are a good friend because if it's oil, they'll 
probably be sick for a while).  Richness has a very distinctive smell,
almost like something from a different planet.  Oil has that telltale smell
which is easy to see and smell.
