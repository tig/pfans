"Wolfgang questioned if the air filter on the S2 was in the nose of the car"

Teutonic logic strikes again Wolfgang, however  neither your German heritage or
mine allow us to see the logic here.  Yes, you do have to remove the sheet metal
to access the air filter.  If I remember correctly, raise the headlights, pull
out the rubber pieces by the headlights,  and take out several 8mm and 10mm
bolts and two hidden screws.  Slide off the front of the car and you will find
the air filter.  Replace it with a K&N,  you will not have to mess with it as
often and it will give you better air flow.

The Porsche mindset seems to be to leave no space unfilled with no consideration
for access or removability.  Spending half a lifetime trying to remove the
underneath turbo bolts is a Zen experience at no extra charge.  Still there is
nothing that will replace a Porsche.  I had the pleasure of throwing my friend's
S2 around the track this weekend.  Fantastic car, you can love and hate them at
the same time.
