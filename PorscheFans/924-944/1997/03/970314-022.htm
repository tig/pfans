> Sorry I'm late on replying to this one-- I'm still catching up after a
> move.  There is a more delicate solution to your problem than wire, but the
> problem in my 944 is that there are two solenoid-controlled vacuum valves
> underneath your passenger-side dash.  If you remove the glovebox (only
> about six screws or so- not a bad job, really), you can see both valves.
> They are marked Nippondenso.  I'm pretty sure that the lower one in my car
> is bad since this is the one that controls the vacuum flow to the actual
> plastic valve in the engine bay.  If you bypass the lower valve by directly
> connecting the two vacuum hoses, you have full-time vacuum to that shutoff
> valve and absolutely zero heat in your interior.  It's a lot less work than
> wire and a lot less likely to break something else in the process.  My
> advice would be either swapping the two valves to see what results you get
> or just getting a new solenoid (they were a whopping $70 at the dealer, but
> perhaps the prices are less now with Porsche's new pricing policies.
