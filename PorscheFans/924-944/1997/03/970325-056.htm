>I thought I should relay to the list my experience of last weekend with the
>sunroof.
>This will hopefully save frustration to the sunroof users and may also contain
>the
>answer to the question above.
>
>I had retracted the rear "catches" of the sunroof so that I could remove it and
>popped
>up the rear of the sunroof, but not removed it.  Then in my infinite wisdom
>;-), I started 
>the car and moved it a foot.  Notice that Porsche has installed this little
>trick that figures out when
>the "catches" are not engaged to the roof and the roof is still in.  It knows
>when the roof
>is in by the micro-switch that gets depressed by the tongue on the front of the
>sunroof.  
>Thus, the "catches" re-engaged the sunroof when I released the transmission
>clutch.  Thus I
>had to turn the car off again and tried to remove the sunroof again by first
>lowering it (key
>position 2) and then retracting the "catches" (key position 1).  During the
>lowering of the
>roof I heard some funny sounds, as the roof was now out of adjustment.  I had
>managed to
>crack the heck out of the plastic gears that motivate the "catches".  
>
>It makes no sense that trying to engage the sunroof while it was raised in the
>back 
>would throw the system out of adjustment and trick it, but it seems like it
>did.  The morale
>of the story is "Do not leave the sunroof partially in if you are going to move
>the car".
>
>Paul, take the mechanisms that contain the rear sunroof "catches" apart and
>check the
>state of the gears.  The part number is 944.564.430.00 if you need to replace
>them, they
>cost about $3/each.
>
>1987 944S (In need of two 944.564.430.00)

I think that by some coincidence that your sunroof gear cracked when moving
the car.  Mine has engaged on several different occasions.  This happened
mostly due to tinkering with the air deflector with the roof out.  The gears
are quite fragile.  I even found a broken one under my seat where the
previous owner had replaced it.  I don't see any possible way that the
latches attempting to engage  without the roof in place could damage the
mechanism.
