cwhanlon@eai.com wrote:
> 
> I changed my rear brake pads today.  In the process I checked out the underside
> of my car (89 951).  I noticed that I don't have any rear springs.  On most
> cars there are rear springs that support the vehicle and shocks that dampen any
> rebound from the springs.  I'm assuming the rear shocks on my car serve a dual
> purpose, to support the car, and to control rebound.
>
The next time you have the wheel off, follow the plate attached to the control
arm forward. You'll see where this spring plate is attached to a torsional
spring or torsion bar. A popular upgrade is to supplement or replace the
torsion bar with coil over springs, more like the setup you expected to find.
> 
> My car has been lowered, how is my car lowered when there are no rear springs?
>  When I bought the car the PO said that the car was lowered and it was done
> correctly...what does that mean?  Are there different methods to lower these
> cars than new springs? (obviously)
> 
He probably means he replaced the front springs and re-indexed the torsion bars
to get a lower ride height. But the rear of the car can be height-adjusted to
some degree by an eccentric bolt on the spring plate. So he could just mean
that he has lowered the rear of the car to make it level.
