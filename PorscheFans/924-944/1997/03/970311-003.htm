> It appears that on these calipers, the pads are not held
> in by pins, but only by retaining springs.  Is this correct, or am I
> missing something? 

Correctamundo. A neat racing setup, lets you change pads much faster than
with pins. Brembo calipers you know.

> Second, do I need a special tool to compress
> the pistons?  Due to the rounded shape, there's hardly a
> place to get purchase on the caliper body with pliers.

BTDT, no special tool required. Open the bleed valves makes retraction
easier. If you're scrapping the existing pads like I did I just used a
large screwdriver wedged between the pad and the outside edge of the
rotor. Be gentle and put a rag on the rotor so you don't mar it. Some have
used C-clamps on the calipers but say it mars/scratches the nice black
painted finish. There is a special spreader tool if you wanna spend $.

I used vise-grips on the clip to compress it enough to remove. Pull the
pad wear sensor off. Rest is normal procedure in Haynes manual. Overall
real easy, even for a non-mechanical person like me.
