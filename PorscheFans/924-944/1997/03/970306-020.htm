>I know that they started to come out with 83 degree
>thermostats, instead of the 80 degree, to allow
>the cars to run warmer. I guess they feel that is
>being more environmental aware.

That is correct.  Last time I had my car in for water pump replacement (when
they replaced the rebuilt junk one with a new unit), the new unit came with an
83C thermostat.  That really means nothing because your gauge is all over
the place and your wondering what's going on.  This is what my gauge was doing:

Action               Gauge
-------------------- -------------------------------------------------------
Start car            Bottom (obviously)
Idle for a while     3/4 mark (thermostat opens)
Radiator gets hot    Drops down to 1/4 mark once fans turn on (this is where
                        it previously used to run)
Radiator gets cold   Rises to 3/4 mark (themostat opens again)

I didn't like this so I took it in and Weissach Performance did a bunch of
pyrometer tests on vairous hoses, etc. and that's when they realized the
thermostat was opening at a higher temp than another 944 Turbo at the shop.
They decided it was a bad thermostat so they opened it up and found that it
was actually a higher temp thermostat.  We stuck in a normal usual thermostat
of 80C and all was fine again.  The cycle is now as follows:

Action               Gauge
-------------------- -------------------------------------------------------
Start car            Bottom (obviously)
Idle for a while     1/2 mark (thermostat opens)
Radiator gets hot    Drops down to 1/4 mark once fans turn on
Radiator gets cold   Raises to 1/2 mark (themostat opens again)

When driving, both thermostats would drop down to the 1/4 mark.

One thing I did notice is that the high speed fan rarely comes on with this
thermostat, but then it is pretty cool out here right now.

>I was wondering if there was a way to tell if your water pump is starting
>to go out before it actually fails, besides the obvious, higher temp, water
>leaks, etc.  My 86' 951 has recently started to make a squeaking noise
>during startup when the engine is really cold.  Almost like a bearing
>starting to go out, it will squeak then stop then repeat again until the
>engine gets to operating temp.  Just wondering if anyone had any hints for
>me before I take it in?

This sounds like a belt slipping.  When my water pump failed, it started
to leak.  When my water pump in my Talon self destructed, it sounded like
square marbles being tossed around a bowl.  I would look at your voltmeter
on the dash when the squeeling happens.  If it drops and comes back up when
the squeeling goes away, then you know it's the alternator belt.  I had this
and everytime I'd turn on the rear defroster, my car would do it's opera
imitation.  Got it tensioned and all is fine now.
