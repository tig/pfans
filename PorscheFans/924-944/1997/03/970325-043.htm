It is normal for just about any cold vehicle to temporarily dribble a bit
of water from the exhaust in cooler weather.  A main ingredient of the
combustion process is water vapor, which will condense on the inside of
the exhaust system and dribble out as liquid, until the system heats up
above the boiling point of water.  Then, there is no condensation, and the 
vapor leaves the system and you never see it.  Don't worry!

Just be sure you take long enough trips to warm up the car completely, so 
you don't have condensed water sitting in the exhaust system, which will 
accelerate internal corrosion.
