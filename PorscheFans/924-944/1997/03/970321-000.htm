Steve_Vojkovic@discreet.com.sg wrote:
> 
> I have a 1988 944S (sixteen valve). Normally, the rpm idles at 850-900rpm.
> However if I rev the engine only a bit, the revs go up BUT then will drop
> to around 600-700 and the car almost stalls (but never does) then they
> return back to normal. This ONLY happens when the engine is cold. When
> warm, the rpm always settles around 900 - 1000rpm.  It's a real nuisance
> when I reverse out of the driveway as you don't need many revs but the
> engine rpm's are constantly fluctuating. Could it be a faulty air-sensor?
> All replies appreciated.
 
First, try injector cleaner ! Then check the engine warmup sensor
connections. As this sensor signals cold or hot engine via a varying
voltage, just making sure there is no oxide on contacts *may* help.
There is also a throttle positioning device that may interfere -
especially if the idle is rock solid if you don't touch the pedal. But
try injector cleaner first.

And for nuisance when reversing out - why not reverse in instead?  :-)
