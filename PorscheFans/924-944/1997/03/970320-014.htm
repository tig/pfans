If you would like to install long studs in the rear hubs follow this procedure:

1) Remove the wheel, caliper and rotor.
2) Remove the emergency brake shoes and unscrew the cable.
3) Push the cable out through the back.
4) Using a tired lug nut screwed on to the old stud, give
the stud a few sharp taps with a small sledge (1.5lbs dies the trick).

You'll find that the hub and spindle assy as enough mass to damp out this sharp blow
without damage to the hub or bearings.

5) Remove the stud
6) Insert the new long stud into the hole where the emergency brake cable was,
then pull it through the hub with fiunger pressure.
7) Using washers and a steel lug nut, pull the stud home using an air gun.

This has worked for me in the past, and just on friday, and I have not experienced bearing failure.

The forces transmitted to the bearings are minimal as impact is damped out through the mass 
of the hub/spindle assy.  I have ddone this with 72mm studs and have seen it done with 100mm studs.
