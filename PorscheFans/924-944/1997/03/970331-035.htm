>I am having a hard time removing the crankshaft bolt on my '86 NA 944.<

We had the same problem with mine.  The solution was to heat the bolt to a
dull red glow, apply a 6 point 3/4 drive socket and breaker bar.  Add a 4
foot section of pipe to the breaker and give it a big pull.  I would of
never twisted as far as my engine builder did.  He cranked about 180
degrees before it broke loose with a bang.  Do not be afraid to heat the
stubborn bolts.  It is cheaper to replace a few bolts than to have to have
the bolt holes Heli-coiled.

On your stripped radiator drain, either try Locktite thread restorer or
drill and tap to the next larger size.
