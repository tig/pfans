The "other" conditions are very, very relevant.  For example, Paul and
Darren both run on street tires, but Paul has about three years more 
experience, as well as the fact he is a very talented driver (3 Regional
wins out of 4 entered in E/SP down here last year).  Darren is a completely
new driver and is improving very rapidly.

When comparing the two tires in auto-x, I would say the Dunlop is faster
in the wet and the Intermediate is faster in the dry.  The reason why it's
faster in the dry is not because of traction, but because of response,
I personally find the Dunlop a bit mushy on turn in, but it sticks, just
feels like it's not going to.  You can elleviate this a bit by turning up
the pressure to around 38-40psi instead of the factory recommended 36psi.
