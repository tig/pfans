>What is the proper procedure?  We're getting a bit tired of all the brake
>dust from the stock pads.

This is what finally worked for me and was recommended by long time MM
users:

1. Drag brakes for 1 mile on highway, let brakes cool, drag again for 1 mile,
   let brakes cool, do it one more time.
2. Slowly slow down from highway and go down empty side street.  Get up to
   60mph and firmly slow down.  Repeat this 10 times.
3. Go up to 60mph and brake hard activating ABS, etc.  

If the ABS activates and the car stops fast, you've broken them in right,
if the pads just slide, you've done it wrong.

The Hawk pads I'm trying right now are really something else.  I can't seem
to brake as smoothly though so they may not work out for me for auto-x
use, but MAN DO THEY STOP.

I installed pads by myself and was quite proud.  It's real easy on these
cars, really, really easy.  I installed the Hawk pads and found out some
interesting things about my old pads.  My rear's were Textar pads!  I love
these Hawk pads, they are hard as rocks when you stand on the brake, have
tons of feel, stop like crazy when hot, but I keep activating ABS even with
slight pedal pressure when hot.  I'm gonna give them some time to get
a better feel for them, but I can't seem to modulate them well enough to
make them work for me in auto-x.  On the street, these are stunning for
those panic stops.  If I can't get the hang of their all or nothing stopping
power, I will try a new, full set of Metal Masters again.

Also, I got an alarm installed in conjunction with the factory unit.  I'm
really happy with the install.  Ralph's Radio in Vancouver, B.C. did it.
The installer's name is Jim.  Total cost was C$580.  I wrote up a whole
sheet on what I was hoping they could do and they pretty much met all the
goals.  The car has no external sign of having this alarm (which is what
I wanted as wierd as it sounds).  The remote is a very nice unit with a good
tactile feel (yes, feel matters now that I have a Porsche).  It uses the factory
blinkers and will set off the factory alarm too when it goes off.  I'm using the
silent arm mode so it looks like a factory alarm.  The alarm is a VSE Raptor.
