On a related note, I performed a "cooling fan operation experiment" today 
to
see how my car acts. Some of you were saying that you hardly ever noticed 
the
big fan coming on and I knew mine was on quite a bit. Here's what I found:

   1) When I turn on the A.C., both fans come on. I hardly ever use it,
      but I checked for completeness' sake

   2) At highway speeds, the gauge settles just under the first tick mark,
      no fans on. If you drive a little slower (35-40 mph), the temp rises
      somewhat, to the center mark or so (still no fans).

   3) At idle, starting with a cold engine, the gauge SLOWLY creeps up to
      just below the last (third) tick mark and both fans kick on until the
      gauge reaches the first mark again.

   4) Once the engine is hot, it will cycle through the above number three
      condition much more quickly (back and forth between the first and
      third tick marks) just sitting at idle. It takes about 4-5 minutes
      for the fans to kick in and they run for about a minute until the
      gauge hits that first mark again.

I can find no condition where only one fan runs. I think this is wrong. At
least the car never hits the "hot zone" on the gauge. How do you guys' cars
compare? If you don't mind, please e-mail me directly as the bugs don't
