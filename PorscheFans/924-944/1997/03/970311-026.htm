Regarding Automotion Adjustable Boost:

My question seemed innocent enough.

I don't think adjustable boost or boost which is higher than stock are 
automatic head gasket killers.  On my '89 951, I have the Autothority set-up 
which runs just under one bar.  On my '87 951, the Milledge race chips use 
waste gate shims as opposed to the jetted banjo bolt of the Autothority.  I 
think this car has a drilled cycling valve and a higher pressure fuel regulator 
as well.

Jon Milledge told me that because the four shims are agressive and the 1- to 
1.1 bar of boost is pretty "high," and that I should avoid heavy use of full 
boost without race gas, or at least a 50-50 mix on the track.  He also advised 
colder-than-stock plugs.

Recall that I bought this car in the Fall of 1995 from the original owner, an 
ex-Indy Car driver and friend of Jon's.  The mods were performed in the "early 
days" of 951 street development (circa 1988/1989) and included the Turbo S K-26 
turbocharger.  It had 61k miles at that time.  I think the original owner drove 
it VERY hard.

Still, it took me 1 1/2 years to blow the head gasket.  That's 30-40 AX runs 
and ~6 track events (times two drivers) in the California summer heat.  The 
failure mode seemed to be "simple" old age.

Anyway, I remember an article on Curt Erikson's 951 in Excellence which had the 
Authority MFS and an HKS adjustable valve (boost) controller.  I think he is 
now going whole hog, and having a race motor built with Motec.  His is the only 
951 I know of which tried an extensive adjustable boost set-up.

Milledge sells an adjustable boost mod which doesn't use the HKS system, but 
requires changes to the wastegate, I think.

This Automotion thing sounds somewhat "compromised" in that it seems too 
simple.  The boost control system in these cars is electronic, and I can't 
imagine that you can simply put in a vaccuum/boost line valve to control the 
boost.

At any rate, as these cars are so popular, the will probably sell a bunch of 
those gizmos to street drivers who would like to turn a knob...

- MM
