Back in my track days there were many warnings issued about the street use of Stainless Steel brake lines. I was told that the lines had a much greater bursting strength and would not 'swell' under great pressures, however the stainless braiding did not age well and that it did not cope well with many cycles of flexing or tension. The other problem is that abrasion from rocks, ect. could result in frayed braiding which makes a very efficient saw!! 
As a result of this info I never used these on track or street. I still have yet to see a 'stock' flexible brake line fail without being cut first.
