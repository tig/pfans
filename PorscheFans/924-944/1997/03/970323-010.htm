Normally the 10A fuse should be connected to the battery, but as I didn't
find a way (didn't really look for it either :) to connect the stereo to
turn itself off automatically when removing the key from the ignition, *and*
be able to turn it on manually with the key out of the ignition (I think
this is the normal method of operation), I just connected the main power to
some wire on the ignition switch. Or did I? I'm not sure anymore (read
below, I'm starting to doubt my memory.) If you have a voltmeter, you can
measure the various wires coming from the ignition switch and see which one
is high when the key is in.

As for the CD changer, I connected this to the battery.  The way my stereo
works now is as follows: Without the key in the ignition, I can only change
CDs in the changer. If I stick the key in, the stereo immediately starts
playing from exactly where I left it, with the same settings for
balance/bass/treble etc. This means the stereo gets power even when the key
is out of the ignition, since the settings are not lost.. Hmm..  I obviously
must have wired the fused wire directly to the battery. Argh, I did this a
year ago, so my memory is fading..
