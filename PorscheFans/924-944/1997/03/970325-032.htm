> 4> is there a correct place to put the jack stands so that the underside
> of the car is not damaged???
> 
Behind the front wheels, slightly inboard, are a couple of round
supports about 2" in diameter. Pad the jackstand with a circle of
1/2" thick plywood and use those.

Just ahead of the rear wheels, on the outside near the body, are a
couple of square supports about 2" square. Pad the jackstand with
a chunk of 2x4 and use those.

Also at the rear the alloy torsion-bar attachment to the chassis
works well. Just be sure to use a wood block on top of the jackstand.
That is a natural load point for the weight carried by the torsion
bar assembly and would be a better choice than the torsion tube
or the bar ends, IMHO.
