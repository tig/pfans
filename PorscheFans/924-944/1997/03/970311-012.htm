At 07:27 AM 3/11/97 -0600, you wrote:
>Has anyone tried the stick on things from places like tweeks?  They sell
>a thin pad that sticks to the back of the brake pad and prevents
>vibration between the caliper and pad (this will often cause squeal or
>squeaking and not the pad's contact to the rotor).  On American cars new
>pads come with some Blue Goo that you spread on the back of the pad to
>achieve the same thing.  Comments???  Advice???
>
There is a product called "Disk Brake Quiet" (or something like that) that
you can buy from just about any autoparts store. It is a blue liquid that
goes on the back of the pads. This works fairly well for *routine*
squealing. It doesn't work at all for competition pads like Cool Carbons,
Hawks, etc. It also doesn't work on rattling pads which is common in 951's.
