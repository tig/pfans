everything I can tell, I'm not doing anything wrong -- no braking through

If I understand correctly, you are lifting off the gas half way through the
turn.  If so this action is what causes the rear end to slide.  You should brake
before the turn and get your speed down so you can accelerate through the turn.
You smoothly start your acceleration as you turn toward the apex.  Increasing or
holding the acceleration through the turn, unwinding the wheel and accelerating
as you come out of the corner.
