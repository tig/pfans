Subject:Advice on chassis mods for a 951
Date:Sun, 2 Mar 1997 22:25:28 -0800
I need some advice on chassis mods for my '86 951.  I recently took my
car to Santa Barbara and drove the heck out of it along the mountain
roads I used to terrorize when I called that city home.  The car was
amazing in that environment (much more fun than my friend's Kormann
modified '88 M3, which felt like a stiffly sprung front driver on those
same roads), however, I did sense some problems in the chassis balance.

The PO performed the following mods:  Eibach 250lb. front springs, 968
front anti-roll bar and Koni Sport shocks (the springs & bar have about
12,000 miles, the Koni's are of much older [and unknown] vintage).  The
car definitely needs added stiffness in the rear (although it
understeered much less than I suspected with so much done to the front
end), but I'm not sure which route to take -- springs, or a thicker bar?
 My concern really isn't a matter of too much understeer (although less
is definitely preferred), but rather a sensation when transitioning from
left to right that the rear is too soft and wallowy.  During this
transition, the front feels really tight and responsive, the rear
feels...slow.  I don't know if this makes sense, but I don't know how
else to describe it.

As a first step, I am going to check the rear shocks and see if they are
set at full stiff.  If they are not, I will adjust them in that
direction, but my question is what to do next.  I had originally planned
on replacing the factory rear bar with a Weltmeister this summer, but
now I'm not so sure.  Am I better off buying thicker torsion bars to
offset the stiffer front springs?  As a side note, when I push down on
either end of the car, the front feels STIFF, the rear, relatively soft.
 Oddly though, when I drive over a speed bump, the rear of the car feels
like the stiff end (even considering the fact that my butt is closer to
that axle).  Any opinions on what to do?
