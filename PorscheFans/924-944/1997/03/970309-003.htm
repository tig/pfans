Every time.  No matter where the camshaft is positioned, there is always
at least one valve open if the camshaft is stationary relative to the
crankshaft, sad to say.  It's just not the old VW Golf that harmlessly 
comes to a halt if the belt fails.  High compression dictates the 
interference-design engine.

Being attentive to coolant/oil leaks in the belt area and tensioning/
replacing the timing belt on time will eliminate any worries of failure.
