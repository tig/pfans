> If anyone's got any sound advice for me about this car, I'd love to 
hear
> from you.  I'm interested in buying a 1985 944 from this guy, he's 
selling
> for around $5000.  The body is immaculate, so's the interior.  The 
car
> looks and seems like loving care's been taken.  There are 101,000 
miles on
> it.  Only thing is he says that it needs a new fan switch.  Anyone 
know the
> price of this repair?  And the timing belt will soon be due - how 
'bout
> that?  And how about 1985 944's in general?  I know embarrisingly 
little
> about this car, aside from basic specs.

     Timing belt jobs will usually run 300-400, but then they'll tell 
you it needs (and maybe it really does) new belt rollers (another 
200), and maybe new oil seals (another 300). Timing belts are a 
regular thing--every 45,000 if there are no oil leaks in that area. 
 But has the clutch ever been replaced? ($1200+)  When was the water 
pump replaced (and "updated")?  (Can be done in conjunction with belts 
with a minimum of additional cost (300), and may be a good 
preventative measure.)
     I have an (early) '85 that I bought with 87,000 miles on it 4 1/2 
years ago (now has 140,000 miles) and have had very few problems.  But 
if you read this list you'll find that not everyone has been so lucky. 
 I've done all my own maintenance until lately, and now I'm realizing 
how expensive they can be to repair--even little things (prices above 
are for a shop, not do-it-yourself).  And I haven't yet found a 
competent 944 mechanic for any price, including the dealer.  (Most 
Porsche mechanics around here specialize in 911s and look down 
somewhat on 944s.)   I have found that the early cars (83-early 85) 
are usually a bit less expensive to repair than the later ones.  Learn 
as much as you can so you don't get taken.
     Despite the increasing cost, I love my car and wouldn't trade it 
for anything--except maybe another 944.  (Anyone have an S2 or Turbo 
for sale?)  For the price of admission, nothing else compares.
