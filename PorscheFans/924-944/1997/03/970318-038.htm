> On a (sort of) unrelated note, can anyone tell me what a CV (constant
> velocity?) joint is?  Or maybe a CV universal joint?  Well, I know what it
> is.  I've seen it on my car, and I've seen Perf Products advertising one,
> but I don't know what it does.  The Haynes manual just says "...and the CV
> universal join attaches to the [blah blah blah] and this clearly gives
> better handling, as anyone with half a brain can see." 

The purpose of the CV - Constant Velocity -- joint is to transmit the torque from the transmission to the half shaft and the half shaft to the wheel at various angles without undue friction. This allows the transmission / transaxle to be mounted to the chassis / body and the half shafts (axles) to follow the motion of the suspension. It actually does a similar job as a universal joint. (If you look at the older corvette suspensions you will see that they utilize universal joints where Porsche has CV's). I just repacked my 109k mile CV's and they showed little or no ware. The one thing to watch out for is the condition of the rubber boot. Once you develop a crack or leak and the grease leaks out and the water leaks in the CV will not last long!
