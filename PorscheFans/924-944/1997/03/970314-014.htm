At 01:33 PM 3/14/97 -0600, Mark Leeber wrote:
>The idle on my '89 951 is jumping from 700RPM to 1100 RPM like someone 
>is pumping the accelerator.  This just recently started and I 
>understand that it could be a bad O2 sensor.  I also understand that 
>the O2 sensor is connected to the wiring harness at the back of the 
>intake manifold.  I was going to disconnect the sensor as a test but 
>there are two electrical connections held by a stalk mounted to the 
>rear of the engine.  Which one is the O2 sensor, the rearmost or the 
>one closer to the front of the car?  Also, if the problem is not the 
>O2 sensor what could it be?  Throttle sensor???

If you look at the exhaust manifold crossover pipe, you can find the O2
sensor, then follow the wire up to the connection. It is a round connector
with a total of 3 pins (on yours - early cars have one one pin, since
the sensor is not heated)...

If the sensor has "gone lazy" - you will feel this loping oscillation 
in the rpms...
