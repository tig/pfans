Tim B. states that the drive shaft is rotating when the car idles, I
assume he means the car is stationary! (Clutch depressed or shifter in
neutral as opposed to idling in gear with the clutch out.) The clutch on
these cars is still bolted to the rear of the engine & that's where all
driveline activity stops in an idling car(stationary)!! As to whether or
not it SEEMS to be coming from the back, that's another story. Possibly
some clutch/ T.O. bearing problem would transmit sounds along the drive-
shaft to the back. I personally would go with your instincts that it's
NOT coming from the rear & give your alternator a good hard look. Remove
the belt & check for worn bearings in it. It almost sounds as if it's
getting ready to quit completely (voltage variation) soon. Let us know
how you make out!
