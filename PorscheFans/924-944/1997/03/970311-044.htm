On Wed, 12 Mar 1997, Whittaker Hamilton wrote:

> In the past i have suffered cam shaft wear on my 924.Could this be 
> caused by the oil pressure dropping off when warm?
> on initial start up (cold & driveing) pressure is 7-8 bar and idle is 5 
> bar.
> When the engine warms up pressure drops to 5 bar (driveing) and down to 
> as low as 2 bar at idle.

Depends on the oil type you're using (of course).  20W50 gets you about
5/2 at 3K/idle, other oils will (of course) change those numbers.

> The reason i mention this is because i read a test report on a 924 which
> at idle (engine warm) had a oil pressure of 6 bar.
> Could my 924 be getting tired at 100,000 miles on the clock?I have had 
> the cylinder head reconditioned 15,000 miles ago however.Am i just 
> worrying about too much and the pressure is fine as long as it does not 
> drop right off...

Yeah, the Porsche manual says that for 10W30 oil, the pressure should stay
around 6 or 7 bar at 3K, 4 or 5 idling.  I'd suggest that at 100K miles,
you switch to a heavier oil; no real reason to (unless you're noticing
problems), but it lasts a little longer.  If you're as anal as some of the
people on this list and change your oil every 50 miles, you won't notice
it, but...

> Any advice would be appreciated especially if it is only say a oil pump 
> and not internal to the engine.

I don't think it's an oil pump.  Don't think it's anything.  If this is a
recent thing, someone who serviced the car probably put in heavier oil.
Your camshaft wear could have been due to hundreds of things, like a worn
clip or something.

I think you're just worrying overmuch, but if it really bothers you, by
all means ask someone who knows something about cars.  I just sponge off
the list and occasionally offer random 924 advice. ;p
