Darrin wrote:
-- I have a 928S4 with the slotted alloys (not phone dials).  The wheels
-- are covered in disc dust.  How do I clean the damn things.  My phone
-- dials on my 944 Turbo appear to be clear coated and come clean very
--easily.  The slotted wheels on the 928 just don't come clean!!

I've had good results with the P21S wheel cleaner.  It appears to be the same 
stuff that Porsche sells as their wheel cleaner.  Just spray it on, wait a few 
minutes, then spray it off with the hose.  If the deposits are as bad as it 
sounds, you might want to invest in one of a soft "wheel detailer's brush".  I 
got one at Pep Boys, and it really helps around the balancing weights.  Of 
course, a toothbrush would probably work as well, it's just smaller.  I etched 
the clear coat on my phone dials with Simple Green, so avoid that.  The P21S 
bottle advises that for heavily soiled wheels, you can leave it on for a 
couple of hours before you hose it off (just don't let it dry), so if it's a 
cool, cloudy day or you can put the car in the garage, a combination of that 
and some gentle brushing might be the way to go.

-- If I do get them clean, is there anything I can apply to the wheels
-- (without hurting them) to make them easier to clean in the future?

I've been having pretty good luck using Sonax hard wax.  It's a spray, so it 
goes on pretty easily.  I usually apply this in the spring when I first get 
the car out, and then again mid-summer.  It seems to make the cleaning easier.

Cheers!
