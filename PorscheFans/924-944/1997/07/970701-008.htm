The point about antifreeze raising the boiling point is good but in doing
so it also ends up reducing the ability of the coolant to transfer heat
away from the engine. And going much over a 30% concentration of antifreeze
produces a minor increase in the boiling point anyway. A similar situation
exists with pressure in that the negatives (mostly cooling system
reliability) associated with using pressure much over 1 bar to control
boiling outweigh the gain from the reduction in boiling point.
