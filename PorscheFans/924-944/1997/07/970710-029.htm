Tony, I second your cautionary advice for potential '44 buyers. The selling
price of the car is only part of the story. I thought I was prepared - I'd
read and re-read the 944 FAQ, I had the car checked by a reputable shop
before buying it, etc. I was *still* caught by surprise when the bills
started coming in.

I know all you future 944 owners out there are shaking your head your head,
saying "Yeah, but that won't happen to *me*!" So, since you are going to go
ahead and buy one anyway, a few observations.

1. Murphy (if it can go wrong, it will) must have been a black sheep of the
Porsche family.
2. Read the FAQ. Contrary to what you might think, the 944 FAQ is not a
list of things that might happen, it is a prediction of your future. Know
that everything on the FAQ will happen to you - it's only a question of
time! (And not very much time, either!)
3. Read the Pfan list. At least you'll know that fate hasn't singled you
out to single-handedly support the car parts industry.

The bottom line? I still love the car. I'd buy another one in a heartbeat.
Go figure!
