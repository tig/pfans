I have a strange problem when starting my 1987 944S. When the outside temp
is about 20 degrees C (70 F) and the car sits for 2 - 3 hours it won't
start right a way. Normally 2 - 3 revolutions and it fires right up. Under
these conditions it fires then quits. It sometimes takes 5 - 6 tries to get
it started. The local Porsche dealer said it was a fuel pump check valve
and wanted to change the fuel pump. This doesn't make sense to me as it
only happens under those exact temp conditions. Hotter or colder and it
starts fine. I have left the car sitting for 4 days with an outside temp
range of 15 - 30 degrees C and it started right up. If it doesn't start and
I let it sit longer, 5 - 6 hours, it starts right up like normal. If it was
a check valve where did the fuel come from?
