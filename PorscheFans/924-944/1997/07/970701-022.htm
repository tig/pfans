Thoughful posts on coolants.  I'll just add some empirical evidence to
support what Marv mentioned.

>If the system is operating well, and you add Waterwetter, I would be surprised
>to
>see much difference except that you might not see the same high temperature
>spikes.

I think this is what I'm seeing with the addition of Waterwetter.  Under
track and traffic conditions (fast/slow, stop/go), the average temperature
may be about the same, but the maximum temperatures are lower, and
fluctuations are smaller.  I'd feel more confident about assessing effects
if the system had several temperature sensors spread along the coolant
path, but if the flow is fast enough and similar throughout, perhaps the
average seen at one sensor is enough.
