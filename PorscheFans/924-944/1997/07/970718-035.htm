Thanks to all for responding to my question about the the 924s and its
advantages/disavantages compared to the 944.  Following is an abreviated version
of the many responses I've recieved since posting that question.

The 924s is comparable to the 85 and lower 944.  Minus the fender flares, it
doesn't accomodate the wider tires that the 944's do, resulting in less
cornering ability (mostly needed only in autocross or track use).  The flares
are also a much contested asthetic issue, like all questions of taste will be
left up to the individual owner.  The 924s has a performance advantage based on
lighter weight, shorter gearing and several listers claimed higher compression,
though others contested that.  The 85.5 and up 944 has improved/updated
interior, better seating, better air conditioning and front suspension
improvements.  Some listers complained about the steering wheel on the 924s
cutting off circulation in their thighs.  While others called the air
conditioning useless in climates like mine in Orlando.  Still, several owners of
the 924s told me that this was simply not true, so who knows.

Every 924s owner that responded loved their cars,  several are now 951 owners,
but remember their 924s fondly.   So the moral of the story is......Like
everything else in life we each must make up our own minds, but like everything
Porsche in life, they're all nice cars.
