I didn't pick up the original thread, but I would suggest that head gasket 
problems on both 944s and 928s are VERY possible if the car has been
run hard, perhaps on the track, and has gotten hot.   These heads do not
like high heat and will warp if mistreated.   Been there, done that.

Dave


>Allen, no I would not expect a head gasket problem on a non-turbo model at
>any milage.  However, if you have chipped the car and/or have heard any
>pre-ignition (not sure if the 944S had a knock sensor), particularly under
>track conditions (i.e. high rpms, high load), then you may very well have a
>head gasket problem.  This scenario happened to me.  I guess head gasket
>problems on the turbo are not uncommon due to the generally higher
>combustion pressures/power output.  The aluminum and plastic radiatiors are
>not very durable (I went through 2 of them; failed at the plastic/aluminum
>crimp joint) so I am not surprised to hear that you have a leak at 75K.
