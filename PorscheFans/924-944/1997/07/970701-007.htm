One more real-world implication of the Water-Wetter/cooling discussion:

In formulating our coolant mix, we must also consider preventing
corrosion (as mentioned by Anders) and deposits, and lubricating the
water pump. This is normally taken care of by the glycol mix added to
the water, except for deposits. Using distilled water should minimize
deposits. I believe that Water-Wetter has components in it to inhibit
corrosion and provide lubrication for the pump. In our case, where the
water pump appears to be a sensitive area, lubricating the water pump
would seem to be especially important...
