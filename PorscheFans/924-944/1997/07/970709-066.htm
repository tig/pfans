     I just put a set of Borbet C2 Cup wheels (17" X 7.5" and 9") on my '86 
     951 and they look great!  I've compared them to the real thing and I 
     actually like the look of my Borbet's more!  The Borbet's are softer 
     and rounder, and seem like they are "brighter" too.  However, I've 
     seen other brand replicas and their finishes don't look as nice as 
     those that are on the Borbet's.
        Tire Rack will know which offset is necessary for your car--they 
     obviously had to match it for my '86 951 (23mm?).  There are some 
     web-pages with offset info but I can't find their addresses anymore.
        By the way, for those interested, I weighed my new and old wheels.  
     I was afraid that my new Borbet's would be heavier than my 16" (7" & 
     8" wide) phone dials, but I was wrong.  THE BORBET'S ARE LIGHTER!  The 
     phone dials with inflated tires were each over 40 pounds (the rears 
     were about 1 pound heavier than the fronts), and the Borbet's with 
     inflated tires were 13-14 pounds lighter per wheel!  (I have to admit 
     that I never actually weighed the Borbet's, but the UPS shipping tag 
     indicated the total weight of the package, including wheels with 
     mounted and inflated tires, so I ignored cardboard packaging and just 
     divided by 4 to get a rough estimate.)
