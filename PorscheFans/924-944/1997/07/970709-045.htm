For those of you still entertaining the idea of fitting a K&N to your
car the only benefit I can see is you will never have to buy another air
filter. Replacing the stock paper filter with a K&N will have no gain
mostly because of the limitations in the design of the air box and
induction system prior to the turbo. Although we all still wait for the
dyno results from the K&N 'cone' style I'll go out on a limb and say
there will be no HP gain that you will feel is worth the $100 they
charge for the kits.

Hansman wrote...
>Here are my feelings on a K&N airfilter for turbo charged cars :
>
>It won't improve horsepower.  Even if you didn't have an air-filter, your not
>going to get any more horsepower.  The turbo (or KLR) controls the amount of
>air being fed to your car, it will acheive it's max boost regardless of what
>filter is installed (providing the filter does allow for airflow).
>
>A K&N airfilter might decrease turbo lag.  With non-restricted airflow, the
>turbo impeller will be able spool up faster (i.e. the turbo doesn't have to
>fight to pull the air through the filter).  However I don't know if anyone
has
>done any tests to see if their turbos spool up faster with the K&N.

If it decreased turbo lag, we would have seen it on the dyno.  The
only aspect that wasn't measured was throttle response.  This is
something
we will be testing next time.  We are going to take the car to 3500rpm
and floor it and see what happens.
