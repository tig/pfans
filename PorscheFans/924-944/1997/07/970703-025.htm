Steve --

>* This clutch is not as stiff as my BMW and the whole car seems much
>easier to stall. (I never did) but I always felt like more gas was needed.
>Is this normal? Also, if I didn't make a shift pretty close to perfect.
>(matching RPM's right) when I let the clutch out completely the engine
>would seem to "buck" and the tack would be oscillating. Is this a clutch
>problem? As I said, it only happens when I was grossly off on the shift.
>Anything pretty close and there was no problem. 

If you're talking mainly about a standing start or shifting at low rpms, I
had the same experience w/ a late '85 944.  It seemed to bog on a standing
start.  Two things that helped were (1) the circular cam throttle that
makes the same throttle pedal depression open the throttle plate faster,
and (2) an Autothority chip.  The chip doesn't do anything (or very little)
for overall power, but it does seem to help torque below 4000 rpm.  Plus it
gives you a soft rolloff at higher rpms.  If you want the chip, check
around for prices.  EBS gave me a better deal than Autothority.

If your problem is a real shutter indicating a clutch problem, ignore this
advice until you get that problem solved.
