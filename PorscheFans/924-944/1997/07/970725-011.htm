So, in other words we can use just about anything we want!  There has =
been
much debate in this list about whether to use GL4 (which the owner's
manual says) or to use GL5.  It now seems that either type is OK.

> I use Swepco 201, others use Redline Synthetic
>  MTL, or Mobil 1 75W/90 gear oil.

If I were putting in Redline I'd opt for either the 75w/90 gear oil which
is GL5 or MT90 instead of the MTL.  MTL is 75w/80 (equivalent to 10w30
motor oil), which may be fine, but for my son's turbo a little extra
protection couldn't hurt.  MT90 is like 10w40.  I use MTL in my '92 Honda
Si and it has improved the shifting greatly.

> 7. While the old fluid is draining, I recommend you heat the new
>    transaxle fluid in a water bath. This will make it flow faster.

You don't need to do this with the synthetic varieties.  They pour like
water and are very easy to install.
