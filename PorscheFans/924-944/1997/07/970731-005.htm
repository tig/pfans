On my 951 there is the following:

There is a tube connected to the top of the plastic cylinder that branches off
the oil fill pipe.  This tube connects to the intake boot (before the turbo).
The tube also has a small branch that ends at what appears to be a spring
loaded valve.  (I'm assuming that valve is there to relief crankcase pressure)
 I can not seem to get air to flow out of this valve by blowing on it (I
probably can't apply enough pressure on it)


Now, why is there a tube that connects from my crankcase (indirectly) to the
intake boot?  This would mean that the turbo not only pulls air from outside,
but air from the crankcase.

Why would you want to pull air from the crankcase?
