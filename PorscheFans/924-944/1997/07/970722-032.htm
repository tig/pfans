Martin,
       I recently replaced my master cylinder, I used the factory workshop 
manuals and the Haynes manual for reference. I can check my factory manuals to 
see if the info is there, but the procedure for master or slave cylinder 
replacement is not terribly complicated. The most difficult part of the Master 
Cylinder job is reaching the bolts on the engine side of the firewall. I used 
a 3/8" drive ratchet, with various extensions, and a u-joint. The slave 
cylinder is much easier to reach. If you are bleeding the system, make sure 
you disconnect the Battery! I didn't, and had a nice fireworks display in my 
face as my little wrench touched the starter terminal which connects directly 
to the battery with very heavy gauge wire!
 
Good Luck, 
           Ezra
