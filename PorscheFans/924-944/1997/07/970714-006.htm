>Does anybody know the difference between a Series 1 931 turbo and a Series 2
>931 Turbo???? I have been told that its just the intake housing, If so could
>I 'bolt' on the housing from my trashed Series 2 turbo to make a series 1 turbo
>fit?

Richard,

If the "series" you are talking about is the turbocharger type (there are 3),
then I believe the main difference was the addition of ring seals to the
turbo-to-manifold and turbo-to-exhaust joints.  I heard somewhere that the
impeller blades and housing are a little smaller on type 2 and 3 turbos, but
I can't see any obvious difference.  Type 1 has no ring seals, type 2 has the
ring seal on the exhaust manifold joint and type 3 has a ring seal on both
the manifold and exhaust joints.  As far as the intake housing and compressor
blades, I don't know of any differences.  The factory manual says the different
turbo types are not interchangable, but my 1980 model has the type 3 on it now.
It should have been manufactured with type 1, but the previous owner may have
had the exhaust manifold replaced.  Mine has an additional "05" added to the
standard part number.  I've had a type 2 on the bench next to the type 3 on my
car now, and they looked identical except for the rings seals.

IMHO, you can swap them at will as long as you use ring seals where the 
exhaust housing can accept them.
