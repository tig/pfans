I'd be interested in a thourough discussion of convection coeficients
on rough cast aluminum with and without waterwetter.

The claim form them is that it "wets" the surface by reducing surface
tension.

I would venture to guess that the whole of the cooling system is pretty
damn wet. I'm not too sure I buy the localized boiling scenario, but if
that's the case anti-freeze also raises the boiling point...  (big
news)...seems like boiling, even to a localized degree would cause
coolant overflow, since the systems are generally only pressurized to 1
bar.

I wonder what effect this has on the velocity gradient (through
viscocity) in the boundary laver of the flow....and how that effects
convective transfer.

Another thing to consider is whether or not the car is allowed to run
Anit-Freeze.

I'm not sure of SCCA, but I'm fairly certain water is used in most
racing venues due to the very slipery and non-evaporative nature of
anti-freeze.  There is definatly a movement underfoot by the air-blowers
to get anti-freeze outlawed in the waterpumpers for PCA club racing.
