     Hi Guys,
        Well there it is; the people who put the more closely spaced coils 
     at the top are the ones with the correct configuration.  If you have 
     the closely spaced coils at the bottom then you need to have them 
     changed...
        It's all to do with unsprung weight, more weight which is unsprung 
     causes suspension problems when travelling fast over uneven surfaces.  
     Alloy lug nuts, alloy wheels, all of these items are being used to 
     reduce unsprung weight.  Alloy calipers and brake rotors are being 
     researched for exactly the same reason.
        
        I knew about these aspects but was not aware of any other criteria 
     used for coil-spring configurations.  Happily there aren't any; 
     suspension is a complex enough subject already.  I *LIKE* my new 
     adjustable Koni's on the 951, and the progressive springs have made 
     the car a lot stiffer.  The Weltmeister torsion arms at the back will 
     make the transformation complete; just need a free day to fit them!
