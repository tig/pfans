>This is a fairly simple job, and no special tools are required
>(well, you might think a 17mm Allen hex key is special, but it
>isn't). Here is what you will need:
>
>1. Floor Jack (bigger is better)
>2. Jackstands (2)

>To change the transaxle fluid:
> 1. Drive vehicle for a short distance to warm the transaxle.
> 2. Remove negative lead from battery.
> 3. Raise rear of car and support on jackstands
> 4. remove the driver side rear wheel (not absolutely necessary,
>    but it makes filling the transaxle a lot easier.

If you buy a small hand pump, it's actually quite easy to drain and re-fill
the oil without raising the car. Compared to the front, there's plenty of
room to get underneath and work at the back of the car. As long as your
drain pan is short enough to fit under the exhaust and you have a way to
pump oil into the fill hole, you shouldn't have any problems. I've done it
twice this way.

One thought: Does raising the rear of the car affect the amount of oil the
transaxle holds before it spills out of the fill hole? I've been able to
get 2+ quarts in with my level-ground method, and when dealers/shops change
the oil I'm sure they do it on a lift where the car is level.
