Folks,

Recently Barry Lenoble posted the procedure for
transaxle oil change.  Thanks Barry, and some comments -

          > 3. 17mm Allen hex key (available from Sears, Snap On, or
          > you could   borrow one)

The fill plug is close enough to
the muffler that using an allen key was not convenient.  A 17mm
hex socket is a good idea -- I had a friend of a friend cut
a couple of inches off my hex key and weld it into a 6 point
17mm socket - several extensions and a ratchet works like a charm.

          > Mobil 1 75W/90 gear oil

I've been very happy with this - smoother shifting than
whatever was in there to begin with.

          > -I recommend that you use a heavy duty floor jack and heavy
          > duty   jackstands. Sears frequently has a combination of
          > floor jack and   jackstands on sale for about $100.

The Sears combo is what I use - great deal and heavy (i.e.  
over-engineered
to protect your head ).

          >  7. While the old fluid is draining, I recommend you heat
          > the new      transaxle fluid in a water bath. This will  
make it
          > flow faster.

Good idea - remember this stuff is VISCOUS and will take a very
long time. Note, contrary to a later post, this IS very helpful
even for Mobil 1.

          > One thought: Does raising the rear of the car affect the
          > amount of oil the transaxle holds before it spills out of
          > the fill hole? I've been able to get 2+ quarts in with my
          > level-ground method, and when dealers/shops change the
          > oil I'm sure they do it on a lift where the car is level.

I put the car up on 4 stands adjusted to insure it's level.
