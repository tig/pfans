> The biggest problem with the earlier years is head gasket failures.  What
> we noticed in Darren's car was the APE allows the computer to run 14psi
> boost and then it immediately drops to 10psi and stays there to redline.
> APE allows the "S" model cars to hold their boost longer, but still not
> much longer.  Since my car has the drilled valve, the computer has no
> say it anything and the car runs to a max boost of around 15psi and then
> the turbo runs as hard as it can against its own efficiency.
> 
> >where are either the "drilled bolt" and the "drilled cycling valve" and how
> >is this modification done,or where do i look to find the information on doing
> >it? Any information you provide would be much appreciated, please either
> 
> The drilled bolt is commonly referred to as the boost bolt.  On your
> black intercooler pipe just above the air box is a banjo bolt.  Undo
> that bolt and you can tell if you have the soldered version, the weber
> jet version or the stock version.  The weber one has three large holes
> on the top and a weber jet in the stem of the bolt.  The soldered one has
> two of the three holes completely soldered shut and one is soldered with
> a small hole drilled into it.  If it's stock, it'll have the three large
> holes with no weber and no solder.  The drilled cycling valve is the
> brown colored plastic cylinder under the intake manifold that controls
> the boost through the KLR.  If it's drilled, it no longer has any
> control.
