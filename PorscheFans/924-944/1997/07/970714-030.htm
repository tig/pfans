My advice, if you want to be frugal is a 85.5 (later style dash is first
indication) or later 944.  They are generally bullet proof (sometimes
transaxle problems, the usual water pump problems, maybe a motor mount).
 There should be lots around at good prices.  Parts cars are cheap too.
 Turbos can be real good or bad.  I have a 88 TS that has only ever needed a
water pump (and now a power steering hose).  It's been tracked lots.  Others
have head gasket problems (blown), pinion gear problems, etc.  Problem is
usually overexuberance and too much boost.  (I have a hot chip and its caused
me no problems.)  944S's were a 1987 and later phenomena (2.7L).  S2's are
even better (3.0L) but more expensive.  Both can cost more to fix - if the
head is damaged -  than they can be worth.  They are great cars.
