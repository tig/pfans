I'm sorry to say, but you just don't understand what modern really is.
I look at key areas of the 944T that still haven't made it into other
production turbo cars that make it more modern:

1. The front mount intercooler is absolutely huge in this car.  You need to
   spend US$1000 to install a front mount on most Japenese cars *and* you
   will then have overheating problems with that setup, whereas with our
   ancient cars, the airpath for the rad and intercooler are seperate.
2. The exhaust ports are ceramic coated to keep the heat in the ports to
   give the turbo more efficiency.
3. The turbocharger is on the other end of the engine to promote turbo
   longetivity and to cut down on the amount of heat transferred from the
   turbine shaft to the compressor shaft.
4. The 12" rotors on the front of my car are still larger than a comparable
   RX-7 T and the calipers are 4-piston on this car compared to dual on
   almost every other comparable car.  
5. There are still no other production cars in this range that has height
   adjustable suspenion from the factory.  I guess those things are just
   too ancient for new car manufacturers to install.
6. The turbo cooler is the best engineering idea I've ever seen and the
   other manufacturers still haven't copied it.
7. The 50/50 weight distribution with the poler moment of inertia is now
   being copied by the Corvette.  Wonder what those engineers were thinking
   when they copied an ancient design!
8. The exhaust system design is a work of art to a power hungry person like
   myself.  Go and try sticking a broom up a Supra's exhaust and see how
   far in you can go.  Can you say backpressure?

I can go on and on, like the way my sport seats hold me in place or the 
dash board's nice layout, the clear dials, the smooth shifter, the perfect
placement of the pedals (only when going full-out), the late activating ABS,
etc, etc.  Just to be fair to you and to the other readers, the 944T is
not perfect, no car ever is.  There are small things that would need updating
because the technology just wasn't there back in the 80s.  For example:

1. The CBV does not seem to have the capacity to handle the higher boost
   of the modded cars.  This is not Porsche's fault, turbo cars in the 80's
   didn't even have CBV's!
2. The flapper door should have never been considered as an air metering
   device, but they really didn't have the hot wire system back then, so
   once again, just a technology change.

That's about all that I can really feel is wrong.  The water pump and
other trouble areas like that are based 100% on owner maintenance.
