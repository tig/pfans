>1.  Do you own a 951 or 951S?  I believe it is a 951.

Paul and mine are 89 944T which are not technically called "S" models,

>2.  How do you drill a cycling valve?  Where is the cycling valve?  I thought I
>read the other day that it is under the manifold.

It is this ugly brown cylinder under the intake manifold, but before doing
that, get the right chips from John Milledge or anyone else that uses
that system.

>3.  How do you adjust you boost so easily?  Do you have adjustable boost inside
>your car?

The car, based on exhaust temp, etc moves the boost around a few psi, so
during our runs, we were able to see how much 1psi boost change made
a difference in the HP and TQ.  For comparison runs, we would keep
doing the run until the boost came up to the same level (usually took
a few minutes to let the coolant temp get past the first mark on the gauge).

>4.  Please share with us your simple mods to get to your stated goal of 268 hp
>to the rear wheels.

I actually want 300hp at the wheels now :)  So far, I can get up to 265hp
at the wheels using APE chips and the drilled valve, but I'm going to
continue developing the setup I have until I run out of fuel.  As my
car stands right now, it has 258hp at the wheels.  I'm hoping the test
pipe adds another 5-10hp and opens the torque curve up a bit.  Once that
goes on, I'm going to try a conical K&N.  If those two items don't give
it, I'll go for the MAF.  I'm sure that by the time I reach my goal, I'll
want more, that's when the really cool stuff will start like the K27, etc.

>5.  What is the CBV?

Compressor Bypass Valve.  A more efficient one gives no additional HP
or TQ, but brings the boost in MUCH faster after letting off.
