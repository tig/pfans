A "long" block has nearly all the ancillary bits and pieces attached to it,
such as the fuel injection system, electrical system, manifolds, etc. A
"short" block is physically the same insofar as the block, heads, crank and
pistons are concerned, but that's all you get; none of the other stuff is
attached. I don't  think you need a whole short block, just a Turbo head.
Make sure you compare before you buy to be sure a turbo head will fit a
non-turbo block. Call Tom Charlesworth in OK City, OK at 918-664-8714 and
discuss teh differences with him. He's PCA's national tech advisor for 944s.
