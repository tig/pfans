Actually, this one is (presumably) based on the 959 -- a proven engine if
nothing else is.  Also, the Boxster seems stable enough that a derivative
would work out OK.  And do you really think that Porsche would sell a car
that couldn't succeed on the racetrack?  I'm not sure, but isn't the '97
LeMans terror water-cooled?

I don't doubt that the new 911 will the piece of quality engineering that
we've come to expect from Porsche.  However, I do think that the experts
in Germany are making this more of an engine upgrade than a whole-car
upgrade; after all, the costs on the water-cooled 911 are supposed to be
only 5% (is that right?) more than the current one.  Considering worldwide
inflation, that's not much of a hike at all.

My complaint is that it's not everything I was promised.  In fact, a lot
of stuff is missing.  I was told that it was going to be a mid-engine
design complete with suspension improvements.  The power was supposed to
be greater, as was the engine size.  The brakes on the NA model were, so I
was told, going to approach twin-turbo size (although they wouldn't make
it).  A lot of features would become standard, an old Porsche ploy for
claiming to keep the costs down.  You know -- an overall upgrade.  The
"new 911" seems, so far, to be the old 911 with a different body and a
bigger Boxster engine.

I'm awaiting the Porsche minivan with a great deal of dread.
