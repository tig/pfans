Marv, I thoroughly enjoyed your "...holy grill" history.  If I may be so
bold to mention it, my "other" cars include three Lotuses.  There, too,
one may find similar controversy.  The legendary Lotus 7 is now made by
another company entirely, the rights to the name "7" and the design
having been bought by Caterham Cars of England.  In car shows we always
have a delimma - do we allow 7's built by Caterham to be shown in the
"Lotus" class?  Even recently, Korean carmaker Kia purchased the rights 
to the name and even the production tooling for the new encarnation of
the Lotus Elan (now called the M100 Elan).  Heck, the entire company has
been sold about a half dozen times since I've been a fan (most recently by
Proton of Malaysia).

Bottom line is, if Porsche or Lotus puts their name on a car, I'd love to
own one.  My hat's off to Mssrs. Porsche and Chapman, the two greatest
automotive engineers of the last 60 years.
