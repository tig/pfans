Glenn, bop on down to the largest baby store you can find.  Here in the
States the big ones are owned by "Toys R Us" and go by names like "Kids R
Us," and "Babys R Us" (really!).  I found that one or two of the tall,
foam core seats (covered by hard plastic and fabric) would fit nicely in
my '88 944.  Interestingly, my 4-year old is normal sized, and now fits
perfectly in the back seat without a booster.  Yes, I know that's not
legal in most places, but when you look where the belts cross their
bodies, it's in a perfect position.  The laws were meant for most cases,
but sometimes you have to make your own choice.  Good luck with your
shopping and new child.  My two love the Porsche, and beg to go in it
every time.
