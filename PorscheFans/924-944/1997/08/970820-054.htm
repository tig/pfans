Everything under the grease cap, including the clamp nut and thrust 
washer, is immersed in whatever grease you use.  I would not use a 
white lithium grease, just to be clear.  Stick to a good moly disulfide 
wheel bearing grease.

While you're in there, clean and inspect the bearings.  Look for 
browning or greying of the taper rollers, look for pitting or galling 
of the outer races.  If your see any signs, replace the bearing.
