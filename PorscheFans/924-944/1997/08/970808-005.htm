>It's time for the infamous rear speaker replacement.  I managed to dredge
>up an old step-by-step list of instructions on how to get the damn facade
>off, but I'm having some problems since some of the directions aren't too
>clear.  My problem is this: I'm prying the gasket out of the way on the
>inside, because that seems the most logical way to do it.  It also seems
>that the best way to get the window out is to push inward.  Is that right?
>Also, how do I pry the gasket out of the way?  I understand that I don't
>want to remove it (the glass should come out first, right?) but I've been
>warned that some 924s have the window glued to the gasket.  Also, I don't
>see how I can pry enough of the gasket out of the way to make any headway.

You don't really *need* to take out the window, I didn't do it when I
replaced my rear speakers. Ofcourse, the speakers need to be a perfect fit,
since you can't see what you do if you don't take out the window to get the
panels off.. :)

To get out the window, push outwards. The gasket will follow! It is all in
one piece, and should come out with the window. When you reinsert the window
with the gasket on, lubricate it with some soap or something. Should make it
easier to put back in.. Better yet - take the car to a glass shop and let
them take the windows out. Replace the speakers while you are there. You may
have to take out the top of the back seats, so do that just in case. If you
don't take out the window, you definitely need to take it out.
