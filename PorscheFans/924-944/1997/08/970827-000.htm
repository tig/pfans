With regard to the message sent by Anders Svensson, I would just like to
make the following comments.

I think the point that the guy with the red porsche was trying to make
was not that speeding was ok, but that the police were unfairly
inforcing the law. It seems there was a bias toward ticketing red
porsches, but the 'locals' were left alone.

People become offended because the apparent intent of the enforcement
seems incorrect.

The government imposes speed limits to protect people because some in
some areas it may be unsafe (for others) if you travel above a certain
speed (in an average car - not something like a porsche).
For this reason, most people welcome things like phantom warning traps
(ones that look like speed traps but are there not to ticket people but
rather to slow the traffic in that area - especially those with radar
detectors etc) because the intent is correct - to protect the general
public.
What we do have a problem with is when the ticketing becomes either a
profit making enterprise, or a method of taking out the frustrations of
the poor officer who is required to sit all day and man one of these
speed traps. 

Also, you(Anders) said: 
"Why on all earth is it OK to speed (a little), but not OK to rob (just
a
little), rape (just a little) or murder (only a little)? "

I can tell you exactly.
The other "crimes" directly involve other people.
Theft would not be such unless someone was being stolen from.
Murder would not be such unless someone was killed.
And likewise for rape.

For this reason, the other crimes are morally and socially unacceptable,
and therefore making them common law and enforcing it is acceptable to
everyone.
Speed limits on the other hand are taken by most to be a limit on the
freedom and rights of the individual.

When I speed I do so only if I consider it safe to do so.
If I wipe myself out because I was going to fast on an empty road and I
couldn't handle my car then it's my fault and I only endangered myself.
