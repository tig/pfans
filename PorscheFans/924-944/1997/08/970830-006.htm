Chris,

There are typically four parts in the seal kit: two o-rings, a plastic 
spacer, and a plastic hat.  Typically you replace only the lower o-ring 
(injector to intake manifold), and the other parts go unused.

Sometimes the plastic hat on the injector will need replacement because 
of damage.  It's a bit of a bear to do.  You need to carefully pry off 
the old one, and equally carefully install the new one.  The hat 
protects the pintel, and if you hurt the pintel, toss the injector. 
 The best technique for installing a new hat is to seat it in the 
best-fitting deep socket you have (maybe 9 mm, I cannot remember), and 
use that socket as an installation mandrel.

Yes, you can get the new o-ring over the hat with a little fiddling. 
 Try using a straight pick to run it over the hat.

Dunno about the instructions.  They are written and translated by the 
same folks who write them for assembling children's bicycles, I am 
told.
