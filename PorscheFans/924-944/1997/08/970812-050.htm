BTDT, one solution is to use some thin cord wrapped around the screws on
the lid on one end and the screws on the inside upper wall of the
glovebox. First I tried sturdy cord from a fabric store (looked nice too)
but was'nt strong enough and broke. I saw some stronger looking cord at
the local outdoor recreation equip. store in the mtn. climbing dept. &
will try that. When I asked about this on the list last year, one person
used fishing line. There is also some guy in FL? that makes metal stop
replacements, I think they were around $120. Is there a search engine for
the archives?
