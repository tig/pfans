I can't claim that I am an aggressive driver or use my car for track 
events, but my 89 Turbo seems to stop fine, hot or cold, with the metal 
masters.

Dust is reasonable and I can not notice any difference with these pads.  
I put a set on the front of my 944S as well.  I used that greasy kid 
stuff on the back of the pads and have no squeal at all.

If your braking needs are stringent, you might consider upgrading your 
front calipers to the Turbo S style (I think they are the 928 S4 
calipers).  The best way to do this is to get a set of Turbo S spindles, 
but I have seen adapter blocks for the incredible price of $240!  I would 
think that you could buy a set of used spindles for not much more than 
that.  Of course, you will need to replace the rotors with Turbo S 
versions as well.  I would hesitate cross drilling at first.  You may 
find that the 33% larger front pad area more than enough stopping power 
for your application.

I have heard that cross drilling is a bit chancy.  Perhaps your contact 
that did yours knows some tricks that these after market versions don't 
(I have heard they will shatter).

If you are really sold on cross drilled rotors, Porsche makes a version 
that are cast, not drilled.  These are said to be much better quality and 
are resistant to shattering.

Just some things to think about.  Good luck with your Turbo!

Marv
