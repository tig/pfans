>The 89 951 that I purchased in June has 17" tires.  An "expert" told me
>that the speedometer did not have to be recalibrated to take the larger
>tires into account.  If this is true, can someone explain to me why?

You have what's known as a "plus one" wheel/tire configuration. This means
that although the diameter of the wheel is increased, the sidewall height
of the tire is decreased by a corresponding amount.

Your stock tire size is 245/45-16 rear and 225/50-16 front. The new tires
are probably 255/40-17 rear and 225/45-17 front (this is the size I have on
my '89 951). When you compute the total diameter of the wheel/tire combo,
it should be within a few millimeters of the stock configuration -- not
exactly the same, but small enough to account for only a few percent
speedometer error.
