Barry Lenoble writes:

>What do you mean 'respond'? A larger intercooler will help the top
>end by resulting in a denser fuel - air charge, or you can think
>of that as more boost pressure. However, the downside is increased
>lag. The larger the intercooler, the longer the time it takes for
>boost pressure to build. So, for autocross, or mostly street driving,
>where throttle response is very important, a larger intercooler might
>result in decreased performance (or at least enjoyment). 

Correct me if im wrong, but what you are saying is that for the best
results a small intercooler, plumbed in as close as possible to the
intake system is better than a larger intercooler, fitted in the nose.

I have to say that looking in the engine compartment there doesn't seem
to be that much room. The only possible place to fit this 951 intercooler
is infront of the rad.

The only other possible method I can see it to mount a flat intercooler
on top of the engine, ducting air from the bonnet vent. I belive the
GT's did it this way. Shame I can't afford a GT intercooler + pipes.
