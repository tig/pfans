Starter:  Pull it, 17mm and 19mm, reconnect the cables and have a friend
give it a crank.  If it has a quiet smooth operation then inspect the
flywheel ring gear and starter teeth.  The starter can be rebuilt.  A new=

one is big $$$.

Clutch:  Not clear on your problem.  However you can bleed the clutch sla=
ve
cylinder while the starter is off.

Rough idle:  Could be a vacuum leak, try spraying the vacuum lines with
carb cleaner to find the leak.
There should be a vacuum line diagram on the right wheel well next to the=

windshield washer container.

Vibration:  Good chance it is the motor mounts, the right one usually goe=
s
first.  Replace with the updated mounts.  Cost approx $85  You will also
need different bolts (38 or 40mm? long).

Oil pressure:  Sending unit is located on the lower right forward side of=

the engine.  I has two wires attached to it.  Try reversing them, this ma=
y
solve your problem.  If not try switching it with a unit that does work. =

Not sure if your 924 sender is the same.

Heater:  Control valve is located under the hood on the rear top of the
engine over the bell housing.  Check your temperature control switch on
your dash and the plastic clips that hold the control flap.

Power steering:  Unit can be resealed or can be purchased rebuilt or used=
 .
