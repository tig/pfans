	I don't think that having two cars that will look very similar, and
from the front the same, is very wise. Porsche has used water cooling in
911s as far back as the '70s to facilitate the use of 4 valve heads in
their race cars. EU noise regulations have already killed a number of
sports cars. If they didn't change to water cooling there would be no
911s. I don't see the point of making the 911 a full 2+2 the sloping
roof dictates that anyone above 4 feet tall cant fit anyway. Hopefully
the water cooled engine will be able to be retrofitted to 'proper' 911s.
I don't think that the new engine can be called unproven, unless class
wins in GT1 at Le Mans aren't good enough for you. If my family owned
company turned over more than US$ 1 billion last year I would think that
the future was very rosey indeed.
