> Hi folks, I'm back,!
> 
> Saw on the list the other day that some individual complained about
> being caught by the police in his red Porsche.
> 
> While I certainly is sympathetic about that individuals plight, I'll
> like to add my 2 c on this issue (wich is very close to my heart, now
> when I also am a Honoray Police Detective in Cambridge, Ohio -  thanks
> to Rick..)
> 
> There are som really really strange things when comparing speeding to
> other crimes.
> 
> Before anybody screams in agony "if speeding is a crime - whats murder,
> then", let us just calmly accept that a crime is a recognized deviation
> from rules of the society as they are described in the lawbook. There
> are minor crimes and more serious crimes, but the principle is clear, if
> it's against the law - it may be a crime. The punishment usually relates
> to the crime.
> 
> Most (if not all) drivers accept that not following the traffic rules is
> dangerous enough to warrant a punishment (a ticket), in some cases loss
> of the right to drive on public roads and in the extreme cases, also
> loss of freedom.
> 
> You probably understand that I am rather sympathetic to traffic rules,
> by now...
> 
> Now to my observation:
> 
> It seems that the police never (well, almost never) make any mistakes
> when catching speeders. Speeders very seldom, if ever, deny the fact
> that the were speeding - at the most they argue the measured speed.
> Nobody is ever ashamed of being speeding either, it is clearly outside
> of most societys informal and formal rules, that governs most of
> ordinary peoples law abidance (sp?).
> 
> So, never we hear "I didn't do it" - instead we hear "Ok, I did it, but
> I think it shouldn't really count" or "OK, but it was only a little" or
> "OK, I did it, but surely there has to be more important things for you
> to do".
> 
> Why on all earth is it OK to speed (a little), but not OK to rob (just a
> little), rape (just a little) or murder (only a little)?
> 
> And just think about the reactions when, accused for some other crime
> than speeding, you defend your self with:
> "Ah, there was nobody around, so I didn't think it would hurt"
> or:
> "I have a much better judgment (read: car) than most people, so I can
> break some laws without doing any harm"
> or, best of all:
> "Why me, when lots of others go free"
> 
> If this is going too esotheric for some of you, don't mind.
> 
> I just want to point out that speeding, and getting caught is not an
> really an error of the police who nails you. He (or she) is just doing
> his/her job, no more (but no less, either).
> 
> Anyone speeding, should really ask himself just the simple question:
> 
> Will I, a grown-up, with full control of my right foot, be able to take
> the responsibility for my actions today, even if that means getting
> caught by some zealous police officer, maybe with an ulcer and a an urge
> to at least get somebody on this lousy shift - and in that case, am I to
> accept the potential, but unlikely outcome of my descision to drive to
> my hearts desire?
> 
> Depending on that answer, drive accordingly!
> 
> When caught, please don't come whining about police harassment and ask
> for sympathy. Be a Man (or Woman), pay up and act happy.
> 
> Face it, Porsche fellows and habitual speeders, the guy that didn't see


> 'them' first may not really done that good of a job looking out,
> anyway...
