I don't know how you can over rev these engines.  Besides, the 16 valve 
engine is "designed" to run at higher RPM.  However, no engine should be 
run at high RPMs when cold.  

First, this is one of those instances where an attorney should have been 
enlisted long ago instead of a mechanic.  However, it may not be too late.

Do you have an entity analogous to PCNA, or do you use PCNA (Porsche Cars 
North America) as a official contact?  There may be some help there.  
They can also give you the "Porsche" stand on cylinder honing, care, and 
feeding of your car.

As far as I understand, no honing of cylinders should be done to the 
block.  I think it can be done, but the procedure is anything but 
standard.  Who authorized this work?  Did you specifically instruct the 
rebuilder to do this?  I am not sure that protects them from liability if 
they did.  This is where the attorney is required.  Do not waste time, do 
it!

Your attorney will know who is responsible for what, and I know in the 
USA, that the dealer can be held responsible for "lemon" cars (not that 
your car is a lemon).  This could still, despite all of your grief, 
thrust the ultimate responsibility back to the original dealer.  You are 
possibly entitled to compensation for loss of time with this car as well. 
 See your attorney!

Please keep us informed with your progress.

Good luck,

Marv
