At 07:36 PM 8/19/97 -0400, you wrote:
>Gary:
>
>I bought my wife a 92 968 cab with 3k miles on it in May.  The car appeared
>perfect throughout, but after I bought it I noticed that the right glove box
>restraint piece (plastic) had broken at its junction with the glovebox door.
> The left piece was intact, but without the right piece the door opens too
>abruptly and it looked to me like the left piece would break soon.  I tried
>to glue the right piece back on with epoxy, but it just broke again right
>away.  As an alternative, I bought a piece of plastic-coated braided steel
>wire and 2 metal crimping fasteners.  I made a loop in the wire at one end
>and crimped it tightly.  I attached the wire under the screw near the opening
>of the glovebox on the right.  I then figured out how long the wire needed to
>be and cut it, then made another loop and crimped it.  I attached the second
>loop under the screw on the right edge of the glovebox door.  I made sure
>that the wire, rather than the left-sided plastic piece, would take the force
>of the glovebox door opening.
>
>This solution is not very pretty, but it is invisible unless the glovebox is
>open.  Even then it doesn't look too bad.  It is very functional.  My dealer
>quoted me $1500 for the parts required to fix it professionally.  My solution
>cost about 50 cents.
>
>Drop me an e-mail if you have any further questions.
>
