Darrin,

Thanks a bunch, I fixed the odometer, your instructions were very good.
The whole job only took an hour because this is the 3rd time I had the
speedometer out.  I have a couple of hints about the job if you want them.

1) The first thing I did in the engine compartment, I unhooked the
speedometer cable wire clamp.  This gave me an extra foot of cable to feed
into the passenger compartment, so the speedometer could be pulled out
further.


2) I have large hands also, so I couldn't put the right bulb into the
slot.  My solution was to mark the hole with a pencil on the side of the
speedometer, then with the speedometer about half way in, I used a
L-shaped piece of wire to push the bulb into the slot using the pencil
mark as a guide for the hole.  The first time without the wire it took
over three hours just to get that one bulb into the slot, this time it
took less than 5 minutes with the wire.


I think this is the second time the speedometer has been fixed, there was
an odometer reading and someones initials written in pencil on the inside
of the speedometer case.  I guess this is a poor design.


Thanks again,
