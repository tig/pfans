VRBA, if what you described about adjusting the throttle cable and putting
a TRC on is the sum total of your DIY experience on the 944, I would say
that you would be rather ill prepared to undertake a clutch replacement
(even if you had the tools available).  I would not be too enthused about
having relatives help that don't like to work on foreign cars either. 
Doing a clutch is a difficult all weekend job for most of us experienced
shade tree mechanics and it is not all that similar to doing one on your
average front engine-transmission/rear drive American car layout .  Also,
if your dealer will put in a new clutch disk/pressure plate/throwout
bearing and pilot bearing for $800 (including labor?), then it is one of
the least expensive clutch jobs you will find.  I would have expected a
price closer to $1,200.  Just my opinion.....
