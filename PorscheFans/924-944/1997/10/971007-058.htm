Dear Todd
	I would take you car back to the repair shop that did the service on the
car. Have the CO% checked AND Adjusted! The CO% should be in the 0.5-1.5% CO.
The higher the % number the richer the fuel mixture. You need to have the
fuel mixture adjusted with and emission machine - you can not adjust it
"blind" ei by your self. If you are disgusted with the shop that did the 60K,
go to a dealer or independent, they should only charge 1hr's of labor aprox
$65. If you realy want to try your hand the mixture set screw is located on
the air flow meter box, 3 mm alen screw. Make the adjustment when the engine
is fully warmed up, idling, turn the screw clock wise aprox 1/2 to1 turn.
Turn the car off and retest the cold start 5 hours latter.
	I doubt the fuel filter is causing your cold start problem. either the fuel
filter is clogged or it flows fuel!
