Jim Pasha wrote an article on the rubber centered 944 clutch about a
year ago in Excellence.  From what I remember, the rubber clutch could
more than handle the amount of power a NA 944 could output.

I think the failure of the rubber clutches has a lot to do with the
deterioration of the rubber over time.  If a 13 year old clutch fails at
50,000 miles, I don't think it is reasonable to blame Porsche.  Rubber
is only going to survive so many hot/cold cycles before it starts
breaking down (especially on a part that get used as much as a clutch).

Just my 2 cents.
