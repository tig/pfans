I just got around to removing the timing belt cover on my new 87 924S. I was
pleased to see that both the updated water pump and belt tensioning device
are present. The timing belt tension feels appropriate, the balance belt
tension feels too loose, but it has not slipped yet. I have some time to
figure out a few things while I replace the transmission damper before firing

receipts). I have heard that one can use the device to set tension, then

with both the expensive tool and the built in device know what tension the
device alone applies? That would help me know how much relative tension to
put on the balance belt, and if the device should be used only for initial
setup of new belt or for regular retensioning. I also have 93k on the
original water pump which appears to be fine (though I will check the
bearings when I get the belt off) Should I replace it as preventive, or not
