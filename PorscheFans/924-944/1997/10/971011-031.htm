You folks must like your instruments to be bathed in daylight!  I have my 
brightness control turned down about half way.

Two things will cause dim output:

1.  A filmy coating on the bulbs (mentioned by another list member earlier
in the week), which is made of the same contaminents that deposit on the
inside of the windscreen and other interior glass..  cigarette smoke and
evaporation from the vinyl being the worst offenders. 

2.  As the bulbs age, they darken and become dimmer, so replacing them 
would be a good start. I replaced a dim interior lamp last week.. the 
bulb was black from age, but still worked.

My folks have a Toyota, and the dash instruments are illuminated from the 
rear (the numbers and pointers glow).  Dad cranks up the brightness all 
the way, and I am blinded every time I drive the car.  Just an anectdote.
