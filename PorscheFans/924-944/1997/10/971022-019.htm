	I'm not a mechanic - just mechanically inclined. I am a service advisor for
a Bosh independent repair shop. But I do most of the work ( including the
last clutch job!) on my 1986 951! I also have a few friends that who work in
a Porsche dealer. I have seen a few failures of the chain tensioner (about
four in the last year!), repairs range from $1000- $4000. Some of the techs
believe that because of infrequent oil changes, the tensioner gets gummed up
and fails. I'm not sure on the why - but If it was my car I know I would
replace the tensioner - esp if it has never been replaced! The tensioner is
located under the valve cover, there is a chain the connects the two
camshafts together. The chain is in the center of the cylinder head, and the
tensioner keeps that chain slacked tight and tensioned!
