1. I don't know that there is any difference in putting the calipers ahead
or behind the axle.

2. You didn't describe the current wheels and tires you have on your car.
Assuming they are the 15" "cookie cutter" alloys with 215/60-15 tires, a
new set of the right wheels may save you some unsprung weight. The
Bridgestone S-02s are among the highest performance street tires around,
but don't expect them to last longer because they go around fewer times.
Tire life depends much more on construction, tread compound and driving
patterns than on diameter. In fact, if the overall diameter of the S-02/16"
combination is the same (and you should select tire sizes so it is) as the
15" configuration, the overall revs per mile will be the same.
