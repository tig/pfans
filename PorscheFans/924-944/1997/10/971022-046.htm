On Wed, 22 Oct 1997, Michael Concordia wrote:

> Gregor, as a fellow 944 enthusiast and as someone who does the belt
> changing (and tensioning) on his own car, I really would discourage you
> from tampering with your timing & balance shaft belts unless you really
> know what you are doing and have the correct tools.  The worst you can do
> is MUCH worse than simply "slipping a cog" (can you say "bent valves?"). 

I would never consider touching the timing belt.  The balance belt is the 
culprit in this case.

The balance shaft has no mechanical interaction with the timing drive
whatsoever, so if it does slip a tooth, the worse damage is a vibrating
engine and a ruined belt.  I do agree that a _broken_ balance shaft belt
could conceivably get caught between the timing belt and a roller/cog and
cause severe damage. 

> If your mechanic has double checked the tension and is sure it's correct
> (you might want to ask him what setting he is using and see if that
> corresponds to list knowledge) , I would suggest running the car for a few
> thousand miles and letting the belt teeth wear in a little bit.  In my
> experience they do quiet down to an acceptable level.

The current factory spec for a used belt is 2.0 +/- .3, so I had him set 
it to the loose side of the spec (1.8).  That change is so slight it made 
no difference in the noise, and after 3000 miles, it is not getting any 
better.

I can't believe these cars rolled out of the factory sounding like this.
