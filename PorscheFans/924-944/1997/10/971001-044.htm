CamberBalls won't change static camber, but they will help you get by with
less negative camber than you'd otherwise need, since they hold the top of
the strut solidly in place under dynamic cornering loads.  A rubber upper
strut bearing/turnstyle will flex under load, allowing the outside wheel to
move toward positive camber.  This is somewhat less of a problem with the
951s,  since they have  stiffer (though not solid) upper strut turnstyles.
By the way, I read in another post the opinion that slotting the strut towers
to achieve more negative camber would be legal in stock PCA classes.  I
believe that would violate the rule Barry cites above.
