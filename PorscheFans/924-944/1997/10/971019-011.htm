Hi Alex, You Wrote,
>1.  Is it possible to make a copy of the wheel-lock keys?  

Yes the dealer has blanks and will cut them for you.  (Porsche keys are
generally made of hard steel and many independant kay places will not cut
them for fear of ruining their cutting wheels) If they don't fit, the
dealer will recut at no cost - at least they did for me. 
>
>2.  I plan on driving the car during the winter.  Any suggestions regarding 
>"Winterization" aside from snow tires and rust treatment?  I was only
going to 
>put snows on the rear wheels.  Is this OK?

Just do the normal stuff - check coolant strength, Battery, oil, plugs etc.
 I run snows on all four corners, it does make a difference on my car and IMO.
>
>3.  With regards to snow tires, I was planning on picking up a spare set of 
>rims and just using those during the winter.  Are the wheels unique for
the 968 
>or can I use any 924/944 rims?

 A spare set is a good idea. You can only use minimum 16" rims in order to
clear the brake calipers.  Your offsets are: Fronts 55mm, Rears 52,3mm.
Any 87 and later 944 with 16" wheels will fit. Also later 911 and 928
should fit but I would call "Wheel Enhancement" and speak to Robert Wood to
get the full story.
