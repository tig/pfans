Kevin B wrote:
>Just a caution on using 265/45R16's on the rear -- this will significantly
>increase the rolling circumference (vs. the stock 245's).  The effect will be
>slower acceleration; probably more than enough to negate any gain in lateral
>grip.

Very good point and one I assumed people would have realized.  On the
track, we've found the taller gearing to make little difference in lap
times, but in auto-x, it can have a significant difference.  We've found
that the taller gearing has actually helped me because my chips have
a relatively low rev limit of 6800rpm compared to the APE units of
7200rpm so it gives me a little bit extra room to move, but it does
slow down the exit of corners.  Trade offs, trade offs, trade offs,
seems racing is all about trade offs!
