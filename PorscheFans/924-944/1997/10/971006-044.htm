Dear Vibration Problems:

Wooaahh!!  First for Ben, remember those pickup trucks and muscle cars have 
very crudely retained solid rear axles and not a multilink, fully independent 
suspension with limited slip differential. Apples and oranges I would suggest. 
 No, I would guess there is a mechanical problem, not a design problem.

As the owner of 3-951's, I also experienced what both of you have described. 
However, that problem disappeared when the CV joints were replaced in my '86. 
Once the CV's were changed, the noise and vibration that showed up under hard 
accelleration, sometimes, went away. The CV joints never felt "loose" when up 
on the lift because of the load the suspension places on them when fully 
extended. But, when taken apart, the bearings were quite worn and replacing 
them totally solved the problem for me. You might take special note of the 
PorscheFan comments on the Vanagon CV joints. I haven't tried it myself but it 
does sound interesting.

I might also suggest changing the rear axle lubricant and using a sythetic 
gear lube. It quieted things down in back for me and I'm sure it provides 
better protection as well.

Keep us posted on your solution. Good luck.
