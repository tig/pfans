>The first thing I would do to your S-2 would be to replace the hydro-chain
>tensioner inside of the cylinder head. The tensioner has a trend to not keep
>tension and then all the valves will be bent! The tensioner cost around $300
>and will save you thousands of dollars in the long run. I have seen the
>tensioner fail as low as 40K miles (but most failures are around 50K
>Mileage). To be safe I would always replace the tensioner every 45K miles
>when replacing the belts (cam & t-belts)!
>        As far as a revy engine - get a chip! A chip will definitly perk up
>performance!

How tough is it to replacement the cam chain and tensioner? I'm hoping
there's a way that will not involve removing the timing belt.  Having at
least looked at the chain, it seems that loosening the cam bearings and
removing the rear bearings might let you slide the chain down the cams.
Another option I've heard is to use a universal chain of the same length,
and lock the links after installation around the new tensioner.

On the latter point, I would like to hear from anyone with an S2 that
bought an aftermarket chip and thought that it perked up performance
sufficiently to justify the cost.
