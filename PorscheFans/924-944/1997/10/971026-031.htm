Todd, I have done this (remove oil pan without removing engine) on an NA
944 so I would think it can be done on a turbo (although a few more parts
would undoubtedly need removing).  The way to do this is to make up a beam
that reaches across the  strut towers and then from this, heavy rope or
cable can be looped around the motor mount bases.  The weight of the engine
is thus supported when you remove the cross member (which is where the
motor mounts are anchored).  When I did this I used 2 2x4's nailed together
as the main beam and then added some shorter pieces under each end where it
sits on top of the strut tower.  I also used 1/2 inch dia nylon rope to
hang the engine from.  If I'm not mistaken, the Haynes manual on the 944
details a set up like this.  For safety, I would position a jack stand or
hydraulic jack under the clutch housing.
