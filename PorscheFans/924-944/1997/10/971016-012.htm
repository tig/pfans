>P'Fans,
>I've seen numerous questions and comments about stock seats vs. aftermarket
>ones slotted for shoulder harnesses.  Unmodified, I believe the stock seats
>are a hazard with shoulder harnesses, for both the driver AND the
>instructor/passenger.  For the track, you can't beat a quality competition
>seat.  However, for many of us who drive our cars daily as well as on the
>track, a competition seat (or seats, if you carry passengers at the track) is
>impractical unless you swap it in and out ( a PITA).  In addition, good
>aftermarket seats are expensive.
>Some may cringe at this suggestion, but on my last two cars (944 and 951S) I
>slotted the stock seats to allow the harnesses to pass through the seatback.
>As bad as it sounds, it actually looks fine, and works very well to locate
>the harnesses properly for all but the very tall. I don't want to gross
>everybody out, so if you're interested in how I did it, let me know and I'll
>send you a description.
