Folks,

The gender thing is very tough with the 944.
If you've ever looked at the thing from a couple
of stories up, it's definitely a "she" - the door
and rear fender lines are definitely waist and hips.  On
the other hand, the car is not petite, and the rather
heavy-handed Germanic interior appointments might
preclude a feminine endearment.

          > Even in my tenderest moments, I've only referred to 944s
          > as "the fun car" or "road buddy".  In less tender moments,
          > I've applied "slow" or "fat understeering cow".  Guess
          > "cow" implies gender.  Not sure where this is leading --
          > does anyone reflexively assign a gender to their 944? Had
          > an Alfa Guilietta Spyder once that was a "she", and a Chevy
          > van that definitely wasn't a "she"....

          > I have owned my 944 for over a year now and just recently
          > started calling it "my baby".  I don't necessarily think
          > of it as she or he, just "my baby".  Of course I'm female so
          > that may have something to do with it. What I'm saying is
          > that it doesn't matter if you refer to your car as a he or a
          > she, your attachment to it still comes out regardless.
          > For instance "road buddy" or "fat understeering cow",
          > depending on how it's running.

My own 944 S2 has been referred to largely in
gender-neutral terms, though a big-haired girl friend
remarked that "she looks like she's in bondage" when
first fitted with a bra (perhaps the use of the bra renders
it female?).

This fall I started calling it "sweetie" --  for
example in tight auto-x turns with the understeer
threatening I found myself coaxing "c'mon sweetie,
stick ..."

I'm sure that this winter, as various upkeep projects begin
stripping the skin off my knuckles, other names will emerge.

my view
