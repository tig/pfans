Yes, it is perfectly normal and is called "break-away torque."  Past a
certain torque value, the two axles attached to the LSD rotate
independently, when not driven by the engine.  I think the spec is 10 - 
35 Nm, but that's from memory.  When I rebuild an LSD, I pin down one
side of it (using a tool I made from a spare stub axle), then turn the
other side with a another made-up tool and a wand-style torque wrench,
doing so to verify break-away torque.

I don't think you will gain any performance from a chip in a 944S2.
 The factory did a good job mapping the DME, and with two knock sensors 
the DME is very good at skirting the knock limit, and by so doing
giving you as much safe HP as practical.
