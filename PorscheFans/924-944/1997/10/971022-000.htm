Hi John
	Have you check for vacum leaks? You can use a can of CRC "brake-kleen"
non-flamable! With the engine running: spary "brake-kleen" - down along the
intake system, ei: fuel injectors, rubber intake boot, throttle body, ect...
If the engine sputters you found the vacum leak! You might even pull off the
rubber intake boot going into the turbo - it can develop cracks or the clamps
can loosen up over time. Check all the vacum lines also. 
	You might also have a bad idle stabalier valve (IAC), it sits bellow the
intake manifold betwwen cylinders #3 & #4. with the idle low or high, smack
the valve with a screw driver or small hammer. If the idle suddenly clears up
- you have a bad defective IAC valve! 	
	Also check you motor mounts. if the right mount collapses, the engine	 will
lean over to the right, causing the engine to tug on the accelerator cable.
