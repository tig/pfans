>So much for the background info. Now, to my question for the digest but 
>particularly to Farzaan:  DO AFTERMARKET CHIPS ACTUALLY SMOTH OUT THE POWER 
>DELIVERY ON 951's?

Yes, yes, yes :)  This is something that is easily seen on the dyno charts.
Forget about the boost control method and look at the two charts, both
are under my name.  One has the APE 2.7 chips with the drilled cycling valve
and the other is the Cup chips with the drilled cycling valve.  Both are at
the same boost level and use the same boost control (drilled valve).  You
will notice that the APE chips actually move the torque curve up and also
smoothen out the power increase (torque) to better manage the power
at the wheels.  This is noticeable when you drive as the Cup chips feel
stronger because it builds the boost in a 930 Turbo fashion, no power,
then too much power.  The APE builds it up gradually making it feel slower,
but it's the same in the end.  Of course, I stuck with the Cup computers :)

>did also pull VERY strongly to 6,000. However, it was my definate impression 
>that the chip change smoothed out the power delivery by providing more power 
>and, most importantly, more torque at lower RPM than the stock chip. You 
>really noticed the difference on the track where smooth power delivery matters 
>most.

Absolutely.  My car is really difficult to drive in an auto-x because of
the boost lag, whereas I find Paul's chips much easier to drive even
though we have the same turbos, just different chips.
