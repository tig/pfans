I don't think replacement would be very cost effective; they're 
both easy to test.  First, ensure they are both receiving power by 
measuring voltage across terminal ends on harness.  It might help 
to warm engine first.  You should get charging voltage (13-14 V).

Check your Haynes for the exact test specs, but I think you should 
measure 20 ohms resistance across terminals on warm-up regulator, and 
30 ohms on the aux air valve.  Your problem sounds like an aux air 
valve stuck closed, especially if your car seems to be running OK at 
300 rpm!  

The aux air valve is on top of your engine; it's not too hard to get 
off, and you can test it by heating hooking it up to a 12V source and 
seeing if it closes.  

Good luck.
