Since this is a NA 944 you will do fine so long as you do not have a
heavy right foot. Snow tires really help, and I would even go for
studded tires if you plan to drive in Aspen. Understand that you should
not use studded tires on your 944 on dry pavement or even light snow,
for the health of your 944 and the road. I have driven two winters in WA
state through the worst storms in a few decades, and my 944 never missed
a beat. In fact other people--blondes incidentally--kept losing control
of their vehicles and slamming into my 944! Look at your factory manual
for recommended snow tire sizes/profiles. Normally the skinnier the
better, and look for the most aggressive tread pattern you can find.
Remember these are not performance speed-rated tires, don't use them on
your next road-race to New Mexico. Rear snows are adequate for traction,
but all four snows make for acceptable handling also.

> 1.      Temperature gauge still shows overheat, it's looks to be about a
> quarter or 1 bar off.  The fans work and come on when the line is at the
> edge of the red zone instead of at 3/4's or 3 bar as it should do under

Any number of things you will need to check come summer. Radiator could
be plugged (don't use ordinary coolant, this is an aluminum engine), the
temp gauge could be off (use a pyrometer to check real temp), the hoses
could be bad, thermostat could be rated high or stuck, washer around
thermostat could be missing. Your car is hot-running by nature, and if
you almost overheat in October in Aspen, you will need to fix this
problem before you have to drive with the A/C on through New Mexico next
summer!

> 3.      Turns over just like that (one turn of the key) when cold but
> two, sometimes three when warm (ambient or engine).  Have used two tank
> loads of techron, use premium fuel and still have the multiple keying

Classic symptoms. You are losing fuel pressure and getting vapour lock.
Bad fuel pump check valve, fuel pressure
regulator, fuel pressure dampener, and/or leaky injector(s). You drive a
NA 944 with lower compression than some Saturns and Fords. No need to
use premium fuel unless you notice a difference in performance.
Recommended fuel for your car is 87 octane.
