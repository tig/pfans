On Wed, 29 Oct 1997, Darrin Smith wrote:

> A couple of wiper replated questions.
> 
> First, my front intermittant wipers are not working.
> The other speeds work fine and the wiper returns to the rest
> position after a sweep. I assume it's the relay, can it be replaced
> with a regular relay or does it need the identical porsche one (ouch!)

Most likely the relay.  Be brave and remove the cover.. clean up the
contacts with a file or fine emery paper if they are not completely burned
away.  Replace with identical part only.  Unlike 924's, fewer 944 relays
interchange with a like VW part.  A clue is to look for the VW logo or a
VW style part number on the relay. 
 
> Also my rear wiper has never returned to the rest position after a sweep.
> ie. if you switch it off in the middle of a sweep it'll just stop dead
> right then. I assume it's supposed to return to rest?? (although you can
> have comps while driving to see who can get it to stop closest to rest!)

You will want to disassemble the rear wiper motor assembly and find out
why the cam-operated park switch is not not contacting properly.  I took
mine apart last year.. when I did so I was greeted with a splash of water
(!) and dry, horrible looking grease.  I cleaned up the whole lot, 
regreased and reassembled..  just like new. 

> Could this be related to the front intermittant wiper problem?
> I only have the haynes manual so the wiring diagram is far from helpful.

No relation whatsoever.. just coincidence.
