On Tue, 28 Oct 1997, Jerry Holtrey wrote:

> 	What steps should I be taking to store my 85.5 944 for the winter?

The best thing you can do for any car during the winter is drive it if 
possible.  Keeping things moving and heated keeps rubber supple, metal 
coated with oil where it needs to be, grease distributed, and moisture away.

Changing all fluids, clean and dry car inside and out, then driving the
car once a week should keep it healthy.  Do it for 15 minutes or at least
5 miles to burn off any accumulated moisture in the crankcase.  Don't
forget to run the A/C once a month for 5 minutes to keep the compressor's
seals from drying out.  Otherwise they will shrink and allow your precious
freon to leak.  If the car is located outside, get a good quality car
cover to keep it clean and dry.  Wash car well before putting the cover
on.
