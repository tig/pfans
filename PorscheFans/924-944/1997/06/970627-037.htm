>Hey Peter,
>
>Hood strut replacement, while difficult, is not impossible. Also, have a
>friend available.  I'll explain later.
>
>Remove the struts by first: propping up the hood; using a big screw
>driver and prying the side attached to the engine compartment off.  It
>is a ball and socket joint and should pop off.  Be careful not to
>scratch the paint.  I use a small piece of wood as a wedge.  Next remove
>the clevis pin on the hood side of the strut.  Repeat for the other
>side.
>
>To install the new one I attach the side to the engine compartment
>first.  (I find this easier than starting with the hood side.) Just pop
>the strut onto the ball connection.  It will lock in place with a loud
>snap.
>
>Now comes the difficult part.  The new strut is not easy to compress but
>you need to compress the strut slightly while guiding it to the mounting
>point on the hood.  This is where it's good to have a friend hold the
>hood up as far as it will go.  Once you have the strut lined up with
>hood mounting point, you can relieve the pressure. You may have to use a
>small phillips screw driver to line up the holes. After the holes are
>lined up, put some grease on the pin, clip on the clevis and repeat for
>the other side.  I have only performed this fix twice.  Maybe someone
>else has an easier way to perform this task.  All I can say is it worked
>for me.  Hope this helps.

DANGER-DANGER-DANGER   WARNING-WARNING-WARNING

Speaking from PERSONAL experience, you have to be VERY careful about
holding the hood up as far as it will go, because it might just go up
farther than you want it to, and could crack the windshield, as mine did
last fall.  My insurance company just paid $900 for a new one for me 6
weeks ago.  I cracked the windshield using the method above on the
passenger side, only I didn't have a helper.  I did the driver side by
attaching the hood portion first and then compressing the strut against the
inside top of the fender - no windshield damage.  I found it much easier to
attach the strut to the ball connection under the pressure of the strut
expanding than trying to line up the pin with the holes in the strut and
hood.  That's my experience with the problem.
Now you have a difficult choice to make.
