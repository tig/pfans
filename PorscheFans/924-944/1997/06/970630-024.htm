There is a 'special tool' for this. It is U shaped, and have small
indentations in the nail-shaped tips. Those indentations 'locks' in the
catch and then becomes handles for pulling out the radio. 

Not having access to those handles, nails will do nicely. If the radio
is stubborn there is also a possibility to push from the inside if the
box under the radio is taken out. Many 944:s have a cassette- or
CD-holder there, but it is easy to take out - no screws.

Nobody really wants to have the original handles in the glove
compartment, for fear of thieves, (they usually carry their own).  
AFAIK, DIN radio handles can be had for next to nothing from a car radio
dealer.
