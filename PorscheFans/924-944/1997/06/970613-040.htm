First, consider general upkeep level, that is the most important
parameter.

944:s are somewhat 'simpler', as they have less components in the
engine. The turbo is a known failure point and may well be described as
a 'subsystem with a finite lifespan'. but OTOH, the turbo have beefier
components on other vulnerable points, and that may well affect the
total reliability value - depending on your (or the previous owners)
driving style.

The difference between a 944S and a 'plain' 944 is not as big as one
would like to think, basicale a more expensive repairbill if the cam
belt breaks... 

It all boils down to the 'fact' that any well cared for car (true for
all makes) will be more reliable, model may have less to do with that.

As for 'bang for the buck' (at least at the US price level), the turbo
seem hard to beat, tho'
