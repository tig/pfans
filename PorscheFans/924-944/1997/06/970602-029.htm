I have an '85 944. There are three things to look at. first, be sure the
water pump isn;t on the fritz. There was a design change made in the '86-'87
period to improve their lifespan. A bad pump will conribute to overheating
at low speed 'cause its not moving the water fast enough.

Second, the aluminum 944 radiators are subject to corrosion if the wrong
coolant is used, and the corrosion acts as an insulator. Try flushing the
system with a good cleaning solution, and refill only with phosphate-free,
VW/Porshe-approved coolant mixed as directed with water.

Third, if all else fails, replace the radiator. I did that on mine after it
sprung a leak at one of the hose fittings. Night and day difference in
temperature.
