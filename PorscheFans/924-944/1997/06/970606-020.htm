At 09:05 PM 6/5/97 -0500, you wrote:
>I am going to attempt to change the brake fluid for the first time (mine
1st time, not the car's) in my 86 951.  I have a Mitivac to use.  Does
anyone have any words of wisdom?  BTW, how much fluid do I need.  I bought
1 liter of ATE Super Blue.  Thanks.
>
1 liter should do it. I'd recommend sucking the fluid out of the reservoir
first, then refilling it with fresh fluid, then start bleeding with the
right rear wheel. With the Mitivac, you will get bubbles constantly. I'd
recommend taking out about 1/2 container full (or until the fluid color is
blue if your current fluid is of a different color) of fluid from each
bleeder screw (2 on each wheel) and then finish off the bleeding with a
person pumping the brakes inside the car. That way you can tell if there
are any bubbles in the line. Make sure you check the level in the master
cylinder frequently.

good luck
