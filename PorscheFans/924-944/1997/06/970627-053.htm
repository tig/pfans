Two answers: If you brake hard and often, like racing it is essential.
If you brake hard only in an emergency, you will not notice it.

The idea (it's more of a a fact, rather) is that the slot (or slots, big
pads may have more than one) help ventilate gas that comes from the
resin in the pad when braking hard. A completely new pad is not only
unseated, it also 'green', and emits gasses that is detrimental to brake
function. Those gasses are vented via that channel. 

It is strange that the Metal Master is sold without that slot machined,
as it is marketed as a performance oriented pad.
