     it lights before key is in start position, but you can see it when you

     alternator is being excited before you start the car (or something 
     like that--it seemed weird to me but I've had this fact confirmed by 
     several individuals).  In any case, I was relieved that I wouldn't 
     have to replace my alternator.
        But after 2 hours of labor to get in and out of my dash, my 
     mechanic has discovered that the bulb is intact (this procedure was 
     still necessary since the bulb would not light when trying to pass 
     juice through it directly from a loop under the hood).  Now I am told 
     that my factory alarm's wiring (which was unplugged a few months ago 
     due to false alarms that I didn't want to diagnose) is probably the 
     cause.
        Has anyone heard of this alarm/charging problem before?  My 
     mechanic says that it's pretty common, but more prevalent among 911's. 
      Any ideas?  All suggestions and donations are welcome.
