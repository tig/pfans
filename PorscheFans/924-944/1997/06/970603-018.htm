I'll jump in on this thread.  I bought one of the Mity-Vac
hand-pump bleeder devices.  The package consists of a hand-operated
vacuum pump, a hose from the pump to a jar, and another hose from
the jar to the bleeder nipple.  In theory, one draws a vacuum on
the jar, loosens the bleeder screw and hence draws old fluid into
the jar.

In practice, air enters through the bleeder screw threads, and
probably around the hose that's pressed over the nipple.  The flow
of fluid is always, in my experience, full of air bubbles.  It's
impossible to tell if these are from the caliper, or from the
above-mentioned contamination.

If anyone uses one of these devices sucessfully, let me know what
I'm doing wrong.  Otherwise I must conclude that the device is
worthless, and everyone selling one (Tweeks, Automotion ...) is
conning us.

There is, I'm told, a device called EZ Bleed, that pressurizes the
master cylinder reservoir; following which one simply opens the
bleeder screws to discharge old fluid.  This sounds like the way
to go -- but none of the local auto supply places have heard of this.
Does anyone have one of these, or know where to get one?
