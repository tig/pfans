Well I can tell you what worked on my Fan speed switch.
I cleaned the overheat/fire-prevent-thermo-switches on th resistorbank.
If these two isolate (dust and dirt) only speed 4 will work, since this
speed does not have a resistor series connection with the Fan.
After switching to speed 4 the other speeds will work most of the time. The
airflow at speed 4 cleans the thermoswitches for some time, or the current
will burn through the dust. This only cures the problem for a short time.
The resistorbank is located at the top of the rightside of the air-intake,
just near to the fusebox, under the plastic cover. Tightened with two
screws.
I cleaned the thermoswitches with Magnetic Tape Head Cleaner and a
contactfile. (Do not use a nailfile or you can throw the device away).
Be sure to clean it correctly, and look to it that the thermoswitches are
fitting oke, otherwise you might end up with burning the resistors, or even
your car.
If your not sure, buy a new resistorbank.
I attached 2 photo's  to explain the location of the resistorbank and the
location of the thermoswitches.

I hope you will find the cause to you problem quicker then I did on mine.
I even disassembled the automatic temp. control device, ending up with
nothing but a lot of wasted time.
