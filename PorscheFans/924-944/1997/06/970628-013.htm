Victoria,

If the whining goes away when you depress the clutch pedal, it is 
normal transmission noise.  It can sound like chains being dragged 
across a grating, esp. when the car and transmission are nice and warm.

If not, it may be one of two other things.  The first is normal tire 
noise.  Performance tires are pretty bad in this respect, while regular 
street tires receive a fair amount of attention to reducing noise in 
their design.  The second possibility is a wheel bearing.  Try to 
determine whether the noise comes from one side or the other, either by 
listening for it coming/going as your turn corners, or by rocking the 
car as your drive at speed.

Two things to get looked at if you purchase a 944S.  The first is the 
cam chain tensioner, which if not ever replaced is long overdue at 
140K.  Not cheap, probably around $300 for the parts and an hour of 
labor.  The second thing to have checked is the cam timing belt and 
possibly its rollers.  Make sure you have the latest design belt in the 
car, that with the .02 version code.

Enjoy your car,

Kevin
