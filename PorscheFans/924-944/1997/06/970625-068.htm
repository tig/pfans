>The leather on my shift knob in the 90 944 S2 is looking pretty bad.  I
>think I want to replace the knob with either a Momo or a Razo.  How easy
>is it to change?  I thought I read that it is a bit involved due to the
>way Porsche attaches the knob.  Also, what did you guys do about the
>shift boot so it still looks good?


Only thing easier than replacing the leather shift boot is changing the
windshield wiper blades. Both Performance Products
(http://www.performanceproducts.com) and AutoAtlanta
(http://www.autoatlanta.com) carry replacements.  Performance Products
is $39.95 (blk or brn other colors $5 extra). AutoAtlanta is $35-39 (not
all colors).  AutoAtlanta also carries Inner Shift Boot Liner and Shift
Boot Clamping Base.

I've replaced 2 of these on my cars and a half dozen on others.  It
takes about 1/2 hour if you go real slow and careful.  Full instructions
come with each kit, but basic steps are:
	1) lift shift boot claming base, lift inner liner, pull wire clamp off,
lift shift lever off
	2) CAREFULLY!!! pry out or push up from bottom the shift pattern
insert.  (slow & easy keeps you from breaking the 3 prongs that clamp
the insert to the shift lever).
	3) remove old leather, slide on (this is the hard part) new leather
	4) install insert, reinsert shift lever, replace wire clamp, install
clamping base and insert into console.

Now that the secrets out, there's no excuse to see a worn through
leather boot cover on a great looking car.
