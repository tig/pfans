I have a Snap-On tool catalog and it has a belt tensioning tool that has
3 fingers and a dial.   It appears to works syringe fashion where you
push on a ball like knob and the middle finger separates from the other
outer 2 fingers enough for you to slide this device onto the belt in
question.  I called the Snap-On people in Carlsbad CA and they said it
cost $113...  this may have to be calibrated to Porsche units.   This is
probably what the Eurospeed has done and they may have replaced the
dial.

(For you picky types note the use of the word "probably"   and    "may"
in the last sentence.)
