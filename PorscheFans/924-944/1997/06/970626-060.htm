> Well, 2000 mostly highway miles later, the hatch is fine, with no

> latch post to get a tight seal and replace one of the lift cylinders. I
> found that the reason my frame was bent was because the lift cylinders
> were of different strengths!
> 
> The straightening process is just a matter of bending the frame in
> whatever way is appropriate for you. If you have any experience at
> bending body sheetmetal it'll help you here. The best way is to support
> the frame on a workbench, preferably in a padded vice, and band away
> with a body hammer, preferably with a soft face (since the frame is
> aluminum).
> 
> Don't try to flatten the metal between the hammer and a flat surface;
> rather, gently "caress" the metal into shape by placing the dolly or
> other flat hard surface _next_ to the high spot on one side, the hammer
> on the high spot to lower it. Then repeat for the other side to obtain
> smoothness. This is the same way you straighten a body panel.
>
