Nope. Flatspotting increases braking distances. When your tires lock you
loose traction. In the same way as when you smoke your rear tires off
the line you are not getting maximum acceleration. The only time ABS
stops you in a longer time is in gravel ( some would also argue the same
goes for snow but due to the possible presence of ice you would really
be taking your chances ). Porsche SuperCup cars have an ABS "kill
switch" but this is for when they run off into the sand traps. I recall
an interesting article  about the supercup cars. The Porsche race
director stated that because racing was so close you would see racers
turning off the ABS to try and gain an advantage. He went on to say you
would see those same racers fall off the road because nothing could pull
up those cars faster than the reprogrammed Bosch 5 system onboard.
