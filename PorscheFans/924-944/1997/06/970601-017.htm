Travis;

You know that black aluminum piece that holds on the clear plastic lens
over the speedo?  You have to remove that alum. piece.  Take a file to
the BACK of the alum. part and file it until you reach the steel of the
housing.  Then take a screwdriver and slowly bend the part that is bent
over the housing all the way around the unit.  You will then be able to
remove the lens and the alum. piece.  With the two screws that you have
already removed, the speedo will then come out.  You will likely find a
small white gear cracked.  I epoxied mine and got abouth 80,000 miles on
it so its worth doing.  At this point, mine broke again and would not
repond to the epoxy (too worn?).  In any case, N. Hollywood speedo fixed
it and converted the 85 MPH speedo to the normal 170 for a cost of $105
or so (1995 price).
