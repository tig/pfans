Ben,
    Changing the motor mounts is not technically very difficult, it takes some 
patience though. You need to place the vehicle on jack stands, remove the 
engine shield, wheels, use an engine hoist, (I have a design that uses the 
strut towers for support, and plastic coated steel cables) to hold the engine 
up. You then remove the crossmember and motor mounts (taking care with the 
steering rack, etc..). The difficult part with the motor mounts is getting the 
bolts off the top of them. On the passenger side, the exhaust manifold is in 
the way, the driver's side is easier, but also less likely to be bad. You will 
need extensions and a U joint for your socket wrench. The Haynes manual has 
some info, and I can send you the plans for the engine hoist.
Good Luck,
 Ezra
