I don't remember the part number of the receiver/dryer from Napa, but it 
shouldn't have to be replaced unless the system has been open (exposed 
hose fitting) to the atmosphere for some time.  The system will also need 
to be evacuated before recharging with refrigerant oil and R12 (or 
whatever is in your system).

A slow leak doesn't qualify the dryer for replacement.  A competant shop
should be able to detect and fix your leak, then refill the system in
short order.  Once recharged, the low pressure cutout switch will close,
allowing the compressor to operate again.  Take a look around the engine
compartment - if you see dirty oily deposits on any AC fittings (or behind
the compressor clutch) you have found a major leak.  Refrigerant oil likes
to follow out with freon and marks the spot.
