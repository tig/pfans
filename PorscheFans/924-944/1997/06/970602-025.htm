I have had these on my car for about 5 years now and no problems.  They are
not any different than regular brake hoses as far as installation goes: 
You must screw the 'down stream' end in first and tighten fully, then
install the hard brake line fitting on the 'up stream' end (this rotates;
the hose should not).  On the front, the 'down stream' end is the brake
caliper assembly and on the rear brakes, it's the opposite end of the hard
line that goes to the caliper.  Under no circumstances should the hoses (or
braided lines) rotate more than a few degrees.  As I recall, I did need to
improvise the grommet for mounting the hose on the strut assy.  I think I
cut the original grommet, installed the braided hose and then used tie
wraps to keep it together.  This is important since you don't want the
braided line to chafe on the hose/strut attachment point (which is metal).
