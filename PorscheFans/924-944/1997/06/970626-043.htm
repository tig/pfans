This reminds me of a problem I had in my old Rabbit (you knew it was
coming ;). I was in a parking lot, started up the car, and my horn
immediately started going!  I literally ripped the horn pad off trying
to stop it (benefits of having a cheap car), when it finally stopped. It
turned out that a key on my keyring had slipped through a slot between
the steering column housing and the wheel, grounding the horn and
completing the circuit!  To make a long story short, the design may be
similar, the entire wheel head may be energized, waiting for a
convenient path to ground and set off the horns, which is happening when
you turn the wheel to the right spot! Maybe a loose wire in there?
