Brake fluids come in different colours, and this can be utilized when
changing fluids. The thing is to choose a different color than the one
you have, and then it is easy to see when all the old fluid is evacuated
from the line. For those using the expensive ATE Blue racing fluid,
consider flushing with a standard brake fluid - then you will see blue,
flush until white/yellow - and then blue again.

Brake fluid is extremely hygroscopical, and the fluid that isn't used
will quickly be 'old'. One liter (2 qt) is sufficient for two cars... so
it may be a good idea to do the family car at the same time.
