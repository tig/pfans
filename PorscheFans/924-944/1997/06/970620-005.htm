Automotion is a good source for parts, as well as Automobile Atlanta.

>The gas gauge jumps.  When it does, it is off about a quarter tank. 
>What is up with this?

This sounds like a common 924 problem - poor electrical grounding.  It
may also affect your temp gauge.  People at Automotion or Automobile
Atlanta should have ground upgrading kits that will improve this situation.

>The filter sucks air through a small hole.  Should I modify the "air
>restrictor" in any way such as cutting it off?

NO!  The air intake was designed to take in cool, outside air from the
fender inlet area.  If you cut it off, you will take in hot, under hood air and
reduce your performance.

>I have a leak in the front passenger side.  I was told that it is probably
>the battery compartment.  The person that told me so said that he could
>fix it with fiberglass for $50.  I have an arc welder.  Should I do it with
>sheat metal, or should I pay the guy $50 to do it?

I would personally stay away from fiberglass, as it is non-original and
that sounds too cheap to really address the rust issue.  A professional
body shop estimate of the extent of the problem is a good start point. 
One other idea to check is if you have a plugged drain tube for the A/C. 
If so, the drain tray will overflow into the passenger footwell.

>My front wheels seem wobly.  It seems to be that the struts woble,
>although they seem to compress correctly.  Will replacing them give me
>much better handling?  Are they hard to replace?

Wobble could be from bad wheel bearings.  Replacing struts with Konis
or equivalent will improve handling.  Remember, suspension components
are linked, so improving one area may lead you to upgrading other areas.

>Are there any ways to really boost the performance inexpensivly?  I
>just passed emmisions, so I can get away with quite a bit for a while.  I
>already have racing headers.

In my experience, without spending dramatic sums of money, it is difficult
to improve the performance of the 924 in a really measurable manner.  It
is a good all around car, but is down on horsepower.

>I have heard something about being able to put disk breaks from a super
>beattle onto the back of the 924.  Is this possible?  Is it easy?

No experience with VW conversions, but Porsche did make versions of
the 924 with 4wheel disc brakes.  This would be the preferred
conversion.

>Do 924s come stock with anti-sway bars?  If not, are they a
>worth-while upgrade?

If memory serves me correctly, they came with a front bar, but not rear. 
As to upgrading, it depends on your objective.  You can sink a lot of
money into the car and make it do almost anything you want, or you
could sell, and invest the proceeds and the projected upgrade cost into a
good used 944 :)  Based on my experience, this would be the preferred
path to improved performance.
