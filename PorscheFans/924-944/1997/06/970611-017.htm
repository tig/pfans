> I recently bought a '92 968 cabriolet for my wife which she has really
> enjoyed driving, often on the highway with the top down.  The owners
> manual says that the boot should be applied before driving on the highway
> to avoid damage to the convertible top from the wind.  She therefore keep
> the top up if she is in a hurry, since putting on the boot takes a few
> minutes, as does removing it. 
> Is this really necessary?  She would be able to use the top more
> frequently if the boot were optional.  Does anyone with a 944 or 968 cab
> have experience with this? 

Good question ... I have a '91 944 cab.

I've observed that the aerodynamics of the car with the top down are 
pretty good.  If I don't have the boot on, I don't notice much movement 
(flapping) of the fabric as I drive.

My big concern really is the headliner.  If I'm just going to drive a
little around town, I don't bother with the boot -- however I don't leave
it parked without the boot for fear that some bird will crap on the
headliner.  I recently noticed on my car that one of the headliner pins
broke free (fabric is OK, plastic pin just won't hold).  I don't know what
might have caused that, but driving without the boot is a possibility. 

For highway speed trips greater than 10 miles or so, I'd definitely use
the boot.
