I have got one of these. It is extremely simple in construction, and I
think one could easily DIY one.

It works by attaching a temporary lid on the brake fluid c=FDlinder. That=

lid has a rubber tube attached, with a nipple that goes to the left
front wheel. Then, the wheel pressurizes the brake system and bleeding
is done, just by opening each bleeding nipple. As long as the fluid is
refilled often, this device works extremely well.

It could be made by a standard rabbit/jetta brake fluid lid, a nipple, a
rubber tube and a tire pump adapte. It is not even critical (for the
brakes, anyway) if it leaks a little, the worst thing a failure will
bring is a flat tyre. =


A ready made one from ATE costed around 15 dollars. (In Sweden, that
is)....
