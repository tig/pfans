I do use one of these and find that they work O.K., although one's hand
will get sore from pumping it.  While it is true that air does leak in
around the bleeder nipple threads, it simply gets turned right around and
goes back out with the fluid.  It is true that one cannot tell if all air
is expelled by visual means because of these bubbles.
I find the device most useful when changing brake fluid (i.e. for driver's
ed) and that bleeding about one vacuum canister's worth of fluid per wheel
is sufficient to ensure a hard pedal.  If you are just doing a quick bleed
to make sure no air is trapped, then it is probably easier to do it the old
fashioned way (as long as you have someone to pump the pedal).  These
devices are also handy for helping to diagnose vacuum operated hardware
(diaphragms, etc.) problems under the hood or dashboard.
