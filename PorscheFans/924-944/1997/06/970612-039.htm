Hello,

I have an annoying transmission noise that I hope is the same as the
one Roger Moore fixed with Swepco.  I have a 1986 951 that is quiet when
cold, but is hellaciously loud after the transmission oil warms up.  At
idle, it's
a rattle coming from the rear of the transmission - can hear it best from
under the rear of the car.  The noise is really evident when
engaging/disengaging
the clutch in neutral at about 3000 rpm.  Can also hear it when letting off the
throttle in first or second gears at speeds of 20-25 mph - transmission sounds
much like a diesel engine under full power a few blocks away.  It hasn't
worsened
in the 12,000 miles since I bought the car (has 84k now), so I'm hoping that
this
is the 5th gear idler rattle mentioned in the 944 FAQ.

Where is a good place to purchase Swepco 201?
Is it OK to use with the limited slip transaxle (opt. code 220)?

Replacing the entire cam drive system is not too difficult - my local dealer
wanted $250 just to change belts. Considering that the belts are the cheapest
part of the whole system (about $40 for both), and the fact that both idlers
had loose, noisy bearings,  I bought the whole deal (cam and balance 
shaft belts and all idlers) for $130 from Vertex.  Before disassembling the old
system, I got a good "feel" for how tight the belts were so that I could get the
tension on the new ones close.  I then drove the car around for about 300 miles
(to get the belts through the initial "stretching" phase) and took it to the
dealer.
With belt cover off, I asked them to tension the belts.  At first they
wanted $125
(slimy) but they settled on $70, still a ripoff for 15 minutes' work.  Even
so, for
a total of $200 I have a properly-tensioned, broken-in new belt/roller system.

Starter was noisy, so I removed it and rebuilt it on the garage floor.  It
has a neat
inline planetary gearset in front of the motor.  Had to drill the screws out
that hold the 
solenoid to the housing (frozen), but that was the hardest part - all
bushings and
brushes were in great shape.  I greased everything, painted the body, and
reassembled - 
total cost $1.00 (for three stainless solenoid screws).  Engine has never
cranked faster
or quieter - starter whistles like a dentist's drill!

(1.6 on gage). Since this is only about 8.7 psi, and the car is supposed (?)
to make
10.9 psi, I have been fiddling with it.  Have tried:
- Autothority stage 2 chips (1990 version) - Boost level went from 1.5 to
1.6 bar.  I did
  not put the restrictor in the wastegate signal line, but wouldn't the
separate KLR box 
  chip be able to increase boost by changing the wastegate solenoid pulsewidth?
- Removed intercooler and pressure checked - OK (cleaned it while it was out)
- Squeezed off hose leading to wastegate w/vise-grips and carefully tried a
full throttle
  run - boost went to 1.8 bar (11.6 psi) with wastegate locked closed!
- Removed wastegate to check for leakage - diaphragm OK, valve opens at
about 6 psi.
  Lapped the valve to assure no leakage.
- No exhaust or intake system leaks.
- No Motronic codes.
- Ignition system OK.
Am I chasing a nonexistent problem, or does 0.6 bar boost sound low?  Seems
like the turbo
should be capable of higher boost levels than this, particularly at high
