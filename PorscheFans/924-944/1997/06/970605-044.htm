> No matter how good you are with the brakes, you can't beat
> the computer. Here's why:
> 
> Motorcycles have two brake levers, one for the front brake, and
> another for the rear brake. Why? Well because different conditions
> require different brake pressure for front vs rear.
> 
> Cars have 4 brakes, but only 1 pedal. You can't control one brake
> indepandantly, but the computer can. Without ABS, when *1* brake
> starts to lock up, you have to release ALL brakes to keep the
> car in control. With ABS, when 1 brake starts to lock, the computer
> releases braking *for that brake only*.
> 
> For the track that means you can trail brake an ABS car much more
> than a non ABS car. You can start braking later, and continue the
> braking deeper into a corner than a non ABS car. On the track,
> especially when racing, that's a big advantage. If you are braking,
> and start to turn on, as the inside rear wheel gets light and
> starts to lock, the computer releases pressure on that wheel,
> but the heavily loaded front wheels can do a lot more braking,
> and ABS allows you to take advantage of it.
> 
> For some empirical evidence, Porsche puts ABS on the real race
> cars, the 911 GT1 has it, so does the GT 2. If ABS wasn't an
> advantage, it wouldn't be on the race car. It's that simple.
