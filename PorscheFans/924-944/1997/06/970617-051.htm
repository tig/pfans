The rear sway bar drop links terminate in a stud coming from the camber 
eccentric.  If you don't already have a rear sway bar, you need to replace the 
camber eccentrics which do not have the stud, with ones that do.  You can try 
marking the orientation of the camber eccentric as you swap the part, and you 
should be pretty close if you're careful.  I've done this, seems to repeat 
pretty well.

The design is, to my befuddled mind, enormously stupid.  I don't know what 
benefit Porsche saw in conflating the rear camber adjustment with the sway bar 
drop link attachment.  Moreover, the drop link attaches in single shear, which 
even the most casual reader of Carroll Smith knows is one of the signs of the 
Apocalypse.

Kevin
