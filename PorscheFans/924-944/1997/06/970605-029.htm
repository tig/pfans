The power difference was the most obvious difference. And yes, it was
much scarier. But that's what learning to drive fast is all about. I had
become used to the speeds of the NA 944 so I could practically go to
sleep and still turn decent lap times. This car still scares the
bejeezus out of me after 2 days of track time. My previous best in the
NA 944 was a 2:03 on very used Yokohama A008RSIIs. I was down to 1:57 by
the end of the third session the first day on new unshaved BFG R1s with
a 'passenger' (Barry Lenoble of this list) in the passenger seat giving
me some expert instruction. I didn't get any more times the rerst of the
weekend, but I probably chopped another 2 or 2 seconds off the 1:57. I
simply couldn't believe how well the car turned in compared to my '84. I
think half of it was due to the R1s and the other half due to a much
better balance. Hopefully, I can get comfortable with the power before
Bridgehampton *really* closes.
