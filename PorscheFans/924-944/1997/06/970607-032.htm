On Sat, 7 Jun 1997, Wes Shew wrote:

> When I desoldered the #9 spade the 2 diodes became unconnected, this is
> normal. With a little patience I resoldered the 2 diodes.

Yep, that happened to me, and I spent about 10 frantic minutes trying to
figure out how the spades went in the bakelite base.  It was like a
dimestore children's puzzle.  I should have inserted a caution.  If you
leave the spades installed in the base while you desolder, it should stay
together, but you will have to fiddle to get the #9 spade out (lift the
others up a little - makes sense if you see it). 

> Actually it's preferable to use a terminal "X" power source, which fuse
> #25 happens to be. The difference with X power is that it is not hot while
> the starter is running. This saves more battery power for starting the car
> in case you left the fog light switch in the on position.
  
Agreed.  That's the best route.

> Be sure you feed the wire to the inside leg of fuse #25. If you tap
> the wire into the outer leg of the fuse, you've actually bypassed the
> fuse. I replaced the fuse with a 10 amp rating instead of the 25 amp
> originally intended for the heated seats. 10 amps should be ample to
> trigger the fog relay. 

Just to verify:  When you say 'inside leg of the fuse' do you mean the 
leg closest to the relays?  That is the leg I used.  BTW, the actual 
relay coil winding draws well under 1 amp, so don't worry about excessive 
draw.  The actual lamps themselves are on a separate circuit.

> I went crosseyed looking at the Pano wiring diagram and this mod seems to
> be A OK. If you happen to have OEM heated seating I'm not sure which fuse
> would be best. Don't use unused #12. But unused #26, 29, or 34 MAY be X
> powered. From the electrical diagram it looks like you could hook into
> empty relay #G15/terminal #87 and have fused X power. Double check
> yourself before trying these.

Even if you have heated seats, it is fine to tap into that circuit, since 
the relay winding draws a tiny amount of current.  But there are plenty 
of alternatives, should you be concerned.

I went crosseyed looking at the factory microfiche wiring diagrams to 
verify I wasn't going to melt down my electrics..  ;-)

I am glad the mod worked for folks.
