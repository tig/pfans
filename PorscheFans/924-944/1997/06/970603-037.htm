FWIW, found this on the Red Line technical info page:

     CORROSION PROTECTION
     
     The extreme pressure chemistry used in many gear oils can be corrosive to
     brass and bronze used in synchronizers and bushings. Most gear oils are
     corrosive at temperatures of 200F. Red Line Gear Oils are non-corrosive
     up to 300F and the MTL in excess of 350F. A corrosive gear oil can
     shorten synchronizer life by half and can also contribute to rust problems.
     Red Line Gear Oils are designed to provide synchro and bushing
     compatibility and to prevent rust even when water is present.
     
These guys have an LSD lubricant and also suggest using their non-LSD lube to
tighten up the LSD for track applications.

Wonder how much of it is true?
