Supporting the notion that lifting one side at a time is safe on the 
suspension, the force vector on the wheels while cornering at 0.8 G's of 
lateral force is actually 1.281 G's at an angle of 51.34 Degrees, which would 
only be obtainable in the garage by increasing the vehicle weight by 28.1% 
while jacking one side of the vehicle to a height of 1.25 times the distance 
between the tires, at which point the car would roll over onto its roof (or 
into the side of your garage), and even then, the suspension would still be 
ok, but you sure would have some body work to do...

Happy Jacking..

Ezra

On Thu, 05 Jun 1997 21:51:39 +0200 Anders Svensson wrote:
>There has been concerns about possible problems with rising a 944 on two
>wheels, and if that may be dangerous and creates to heaavy loads on the
>twoo wheels that are still on the ground,
>
>Think of it:
>
>The cars center of gravity (CG) is not changed in any way. The weight
>supported by the uplifted wheels is now supported of the jack insted. If
>we do not take the (slight) angle into account, there will be no
>additional load on the wheels still remaining on the ground...
>
>And for those still worrying:
>
>The sideways load on the wheels when cornering (real) hard is around
>0.8 G. That is approximately 0.8 of the cars weight and that load is
>mostly taken up by the outer tires. 
>
>So be assured, no harm will be done to your car by letting it rest on
>any two wheels, even if you rise it to a considerable degree.
