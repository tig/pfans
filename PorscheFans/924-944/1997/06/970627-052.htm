> I saw this and thought of the thread the other day.

> > I discovered on my '86 944 that the position of the lock-nuts on the
> > wheel studs can cause a shimmy. I have phone-dial wheels. I found that
> > if I did not put the locking nut on the correct stud, the steering
> > wheel would shake at around 55-60 mph (and smooth out at higher
> > speeds). The lock nut being so light, you wouldn't think it would
> > matter...but apparently, the effect can be significant.
> >

I have locknuts of steel (!). I took them away - and the unbalance
disappearead. The Porsche have a 130 mm bolt circle, and that is a lot
more than most cars. This is probably a contributing reason for a heavy
locknut to have this effect. 

Now, it's just a question of painting one of the studs yellow, put a
yellow dab of paint (incospicously) in one of the nut recesses and
remember to always put the stud in that hole, and the locknut on that
stud, and then find a tire shop that spin the wheel on the car...

Heureka !
