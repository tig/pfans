If you want to do it that way, get a new or old brake reservoir cap and
drill a small hole into it.  If you are working by yourself, make the
hole large enough to insert a rubber hose that you can put around the
end of a air gun blower nozzle.  With low air pressure (either with a
regulator or being very careful), you can then pressurize the system.  

944 turbos have to have the clutch line bleed also where it comes up
from the bell housing and goes back down to the reservoir.

My recommendation would be for you to also replace the clutch slave
cylinder.  We see 5 to 1 slave failures to master cylinders.  They are
inexpensive, so do all this work just once.
