  With new brake fluid coursing through the veins of my '86 944, I attended a 
Corvette sponsored Auto X on Sunday in Edinburgh, Ny. There was an assortment 
of ~35 vehicles: 3 Corvairs, 2 RX-7's, 2 Rabbits, a Jetta, several Cameros, 
many early and late 80's corvettes, 1 open frame buggy, 1 early 911, a 
Corrado, a Z24, some hot rod from the 50's, and me in my guards red '86 944. I 
did feel a bit out of place, and my wife thought we were all nuts. My friend 
was #2 with his street prepared Rabbit with Hoosiers, and I was #19. Not 
having the money to buy a helmet, my friend lent me his. We arrived around 
10:30am, unloaded all the stuff from the car, registered, signed the all 
inclusive waiver (they all laughed at me when I read it), went through tech 
inspection, attended the driver's meeting, and awaited our turns. The course 
started out with a loop around an oval next to the timing shack, with a sharp 
~290 Deg right turn into the course. This led up hill, into a sharp left hand 
turn onto a straight section where my speeds approached ~60MPH. Braking hard 
at the end of this straight, the course leads into a U bringing the car back 
through an S section, and around one final banked uphill turn before entering 
the loop by the timing shack, and then once more around the course. My friend 
ran times ~1:30:xx for the first two runs in his rabbit. My first run was 
1:38:xx, which included a two second penalty for knocking over a cone. My 
second two runs were ~1:34:60 and ~1:34:40. The best time was ~1:27:xx, with 
most around the 1:28:xx - 1:30:xx range. Several people were in the upper 
1:30's and low 1:40's. I had a great time driving the course. The oversteer 
definitely slowed me down, but made driving lots of fun. The event cost $10. 
My only regret is that I didn't wear more sunscreen, ouch!

 I plan to attend an Auto-X at the Fort Devens Air Force base in Ayers Mass on 
June 29th, but need to borrow a helmet. Anyone on this list going? Would you 
be willing to share your helmet? (My hair is clean). Also, I am looking for 
two used 215-50 15 D40M2 or other quality street rubber to place on the front 
rims. I would then move the D40M2 225-50 15's from the front to the rear rims 
replacing the M+S hard rubber that is there now. I am hoping this will make 
the vehicle more balanced, but don't have the money to buy new rubber.
