It's a good thing, too, because when a Tokico dies, it does so in a very
nasty way: the shock assembly becomes a solid component, i.e., it won't
compress. As you can imagine, this isn't a good thing for your other
suspension components and wheels. I've seen this first-hand (an Illumina)
and heard about it from others.

I had Tokico Illuminas on an auto-x VW and liked them quite a bit -- never
failed on my car. Price was comparable to Koni adjustables at the time (3-4
years ago).
