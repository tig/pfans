What is a 3-peice wheel?

Answer:

A three peice wheel is comprised of an inner rim, and outer rim, and a
wheel center, all bolted togeher around the perimeter of the wheel
center, usually with 20, 25, or 30 bolts.

They are nice because bent wheel halves can easily be replaced, or
wheel width/offset can be changed and custom wheels can be built.
Generaly either a large o-ring (BBS) is used between the halves, or
silicone sealant (RTV) is used to prevent air leakage.

Wheels can be built with the centers in, out, or in between the halves,
depending on desired appearance and/or offset.

The downside is that the wheels tend to be more expensive than one-or two-pecie wheels.

Two peice wheels generally have the center and wither the inner or outer 
halve as a single casting or forging.
