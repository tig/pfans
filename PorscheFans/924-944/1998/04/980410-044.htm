The Bosch FI manual states that the system is programmed to deliver a fairly
rich charge upon initial throttle opening due to the fact that the intake
manifold retains some of the fuel on the walls. When the car has been
running with a completely closed throttle the fuel on the walls of the
intake manifold will evaporate and be consumed. When the throttle is opened
the system injects an extra quantity of fuel to wet the walls again
resulting in a rich condition.
Interesting!! The additional fuel would explain the initial puff of black
smoke on hard acceleration.
