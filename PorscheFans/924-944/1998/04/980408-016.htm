>Apparently, my rear rotors are slightly warped as evidenced by brake
>noises coming from the back when lightly applying the brakes.  When I took
>my car in to have its belts retensioned, I asked the shop to check the
>brakes and verify my theory.  They either took the brakes apart, or
>removed the pads or something, checked and said my hypothesis is correct.   
>
>Well, now I can hear my rear brakes rattle every time the rear wheels go
>over a bump and I can also hear them rattle as they follow the rotors on
>the highway.  :-(.  I know it is the brakes because light left foot
>braking while driving gets rid of the noise. My car was not quiet to begin
>w/, but now it is ridiculous.  How can I get rid of the noises?  Is that
>what the strips of aluminum are about, or was that for squeal reduction? 
>I took the car back to the shop, but I want to have a backup plan in case
>they say they cannot restore the brake to their previous state of
>quietness.

Yes, that is what the aluminum strips are about. Cut 2 strips about 2" long
and about 1/2 " wide and place them under the rear pads (one for each
wheel). The easiest way to do this is to remove the pads, put the aluminum
shims in and the put the pads on top of them. 

This problem seems to be at its worst when using MetalMaster pads. If you
try a different brand of street pad you may not have the problem.
