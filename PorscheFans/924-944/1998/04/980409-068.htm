Removing the fuel level sender is very easy. There are contacts that can =
be cleaned. I did this not long ago but it didn't improve the actions of =
the gauge. It is probably a faulty sender. To get the sender out remove =
the carpet and rubber pad under the carpet in the hatch. It might be a =
good idea to depressurize the fuel system. Don't know if its necessary =
but I did it, CYA. Remove the fuel pump fuse and then run the engine til =
it dies (about 2-3 sec). Back in the hatch pull up the fiberglass(?) pad =
and you'll see the wires to the sender. I made a sketch of the connection=
s for reinstallation. Remove the wires (spade connections), remove the =
bolts and pull out the sender. Installation is the reverse of removal...t=
he Haynes motto.
