Been there, flushed that... We replaced the entire oil cooler when it
failed. When you get oil in the water, you also get water in the oil. After
replacing the cooler and flushing the cooling system (do it twice), drain
and change the oil and filter. If you're really thorough-minded, I would
run the car about 500 miles and then drain and change the oil and filter
again.

BTW, after flushing, the car ran very much cooler than it had. Track even
tdriving barely raised the temp needle into the operating zone.
