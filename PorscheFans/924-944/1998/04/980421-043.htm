You should reconsider if you are expecting 110% reliability.  The 951 is
an excellent car, but it is not a Toyota Supra TT in terms of reliability.
But even with the slightly reduced reliability, I'd take the 951 anyday.

What you are reading generally are age and wear related problems.  951's
get driven a lot, especially round and round on race tracks.  I think it
was a Porsche engineer that said 1 mile on the track is equal to 1000 miles
of driving on the road, in terms of wear, etc.  People like Barry Lenoble
drive their cars every weekend on the track without many problems.

It really comes down to these factors:
1. If you have the time and mechanical ability to work on your own car, these
   cars are relatively cheap to run for their excitement value.
2. If you don't have the time or are scared of working on your car (as I am),
   then you'd better have a big bank balance (as I don't) to handle any
   unusual things, like my head stud problem.
