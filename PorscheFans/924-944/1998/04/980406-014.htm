Your rotor is probably glued onto the shaft like mine was. 
The rotor is attached to a connector piece, which is in turn
attached to the camshaft by a small bolt. You can remove this
small bolt and the rotor/connector assembly should come right
off.

Rather than mess with getting the $&^%! rotor off the connector,
I simply bought a new connector piece for around $30.  There is
a tech note in the old Parts and Technical Reference manual which
recommends that you glue the rotor onto the connector, because
otherwise, it can cause damage to the "distributor."  I'm not
sure how this can happen (I guess it comes flying off), but I
re-glued mine.  (It also recommends putting locktite on the
allen screw).
