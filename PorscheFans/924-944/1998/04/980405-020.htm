--Stock sway bars off of a '88 or '89 944 Turbo S (do I need bigger
bushings/mounts, or do I just need the bars?)
>>>>>

If you are only going to use the car on tracks that are smooth, I'd
recommend going for the Weltmeister adjustable front swaybar instead.
The problem with this bar is that you must drill a hole through the
steel control arm to mount it. I have broken 3 control arms in half
right through this hole so I'd recommend looking for stress cracks after
every session and replacing the control arms on a regular basis. But the
adjustability of the Weltmeister bar will probably be too large of a
benefit to lose on a purpose-built race car. I don't know if IT rules
allow it, but you might consider going to aluminum or Fabcar control
arms, or having the steel ones strengthened by welding Rebar or some
other metal rod to the front and rear edges. The control arms are THE
BIG weak points with these cars on the track - beware!

Run - don't walk - away from 60 profile balloon tires. The 50 profile
tires are much better and the smaller diameter really helps the 944's
gearing. I ran 225/50x15s for years. Do it! You could even run
245/50x15s on the rear but I think the car really responds to the
gearing the 225s give it and you'd have even more understeer.
