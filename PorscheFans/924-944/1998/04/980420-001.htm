I live in Poland where Porsche is still something starange. I am about
clutch replacement in my 1976 924. There is a lot of problems getting
original clutch components for my car, but... I discovered studying
SACHS and LUK catalogues, databases (sachs-boge.co.uk - this is cool
!!!) that clutch cover and throw out bearing comes directly (the same
part numbers!) that from AUDI (80/100; 1.9-2.2; 80-85). In case of
SACHS, the cover code is MF 215 K. OK, lower price, no avialbility
problems. But the clutch plate is biger problem. For the same complete
clutch package for Audi there is two types of clutch plate: 215TD and
215TPD. Catalog says about clutch type for Porsche 924 - 215PSD. The
clutch diameter (215) and number of splines in shaft hole are the same
(24) for 215TD, 215TPD, 215PSD. All three disks fits the same cover and
throw out bearing. The only difference I have noticed in catalogs is in
"Fit time" and it goes as follows: 215TD - 3.40, 215 TPD - 4.30, 215PSD
- 5.50. Looking for other cars I have discovered the rule: stronger
engine = higher fit time.
