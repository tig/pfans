Jad, my car reacted exactly like to what you described: it would idle
fine after initial cold startup but then when I wanted to move off the
driveway, if I didn't let it warm up, it would hesitate, cough but not
backfire, and wanted to shut off. What I did during that situation was
while the car was moving, I would let it come back down to just around
1500 RPM or so where the engine was running more smoothly, then slowly
increase the throttle little by little until it would let me get over
3500 RPM. Just around then it would act like it found  "new energy" and
rev across the tach smoothly. Once warmed up, the engine would behave
normally but kinda lagging and not that fully peppy like I know it can
be and was. During the period of troubleshooting for those days of
confusion and frustration, I would allow the engine to warm up for
around five to seven minutes in the morning to temporarily
circumnavigate the problem at hand. So, yeah, it short it did behave
"like a turbo" though we all know it isn't. I hope this is the solution.
Still, I would like to be able to calibrate the position of the wiper
arm to the air flow damper that's inside the box. Like I said, I think
I'm running a tad bit rich. When adjusting while idling, you have to
imagine what position the air flow damper might be and while the setting
screw is slack you move the black plastic wheel that the wiper arm
contacts are connected to to find the right mixture. You'll find that as
you rotate the wiper arm back and forth, you're sending different
signals to the computer of what the air flow damper should have been
reading when in reality that damper is still stationary in it's original
position at idle. On the two extreme ends of the rotation ranges you'll
find that the engine will become unsettled and idle roughly and want to
cut off. I played with mine back and forth to find a moderate setting in
between, tighten the set screw and blip the throttle to test how the
engine would behave. Couple times, I had it on a setting where when I
blip the throttle, it had a mistiming and gave somewhat like a backfire
into the intake manifold. I heard this is supposed to be bad and
damaging to the system. I readjusted to where it revved smoothly
throughout the throttle range. There is a bit of area in between where
it'll run happily but I need to readjust back down a little to get a
more economical fuel consumption but maintain that nice throttle punch.
By the way, my engine is all stock -- I didn't buy that throttle cam
yet. I guess after my wedding on the 25th of July, I'll splurge a little
bit and get some of those nice gadgets for my car. Let me know what
happens.
