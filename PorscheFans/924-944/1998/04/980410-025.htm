This sounds like a failing dual mass flywheel.  I have seen this before. I had
a 968 customer that had the same symptoms on his '94 968 w/26K miles. I
replaced the flywheel and disc, and the problem went away (until the next time
the d/m fails). My parts guy claims they sell lots of 968 flywheels. 

Porsche would not even talk to him about warranty on anything clutch related.
I think they saved their dual mass failure "Goodwill pennies" for the 911
customers.  

BTW, the 968 uses a 993 style clutch.  The disc does not have any springs and
utilizes a solid hub.  The pressure plate is aluminum (993 style) and very
expensive.  Apparently, before the D/M flywheel prematurely fails, it is
supposed to dampen the driveline snap, much like the damper springs on a
standard clutch.  

The entire clutch repair at list which includes a disc, pressure plate,
flywheel, t/o bearing, will cost $1900 for parts alone.  The labor is much
less than the usual 944/951 because the 968 has a two piece 928 style
bellhousing.  Nonetheless, the full bend over on this deal could easily run to
$2400!!

Porsche MotorSport sells a 968 sport flywheel and clutch setup for $2600
WHOLESALE!!!  I intend to disassemble the old D/M flywheel, remove the D/M
mechanism, machine a custom hub adapter, and fix the problem for much less on
the next one.
