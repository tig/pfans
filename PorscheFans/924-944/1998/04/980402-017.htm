Here is the straight poop on the big red caliper/rotor conversion on the 951.

The operative word here is 928 GTS. If you have a 951 S with S-4 calipers, the
big reds, or in the case of the 928 GTS, big black calipers, will bolt right
on. If you have a '87 or later 951 that has the standard 930 calipers, all
that is required is buying the vertical to horizontal mount adapters from
someone like PowerHaus (tell David I sent you) and bolting these big calipers
right on to your existing spindles. Or you can make them out of 1"X1" bar
stock like I do. 

Rotors: the 928 GTS rotors will bolt right onto '87 and later wheel hubs. The
GTS rotors do not have crack inducing, surface area reducing, boy racer cast
holes and are about $100 cheaper EACH! Here are the part numbers: 928 351 045
01 L, 928 351 046 01 R  

The money you save over the 993TT rotors will go towards gas slotting,
cryogenics or if you're like me, your next set of rotors. 

BTW, I run 275/40-17's on the front and 315/35-17's on the rear of my '88 tubo
S with no cutting.
