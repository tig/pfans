My PO put a new clutch in at around 95k miles.  When I recently checked it at
about 145k miles I had about 80% clutch thickness remaining.  I started taking
drivers ed shortly after I bought the car, and I believe that learning to
toe/heel quickly, learning to allow the clutch to engage before getting back
into the throttle (From the time I bought the car to my first DE, I was
begining to develop a habit of power-shifting into second), and not to launch
the car off the line (calm down, part throttle until you're up to about 3k on
the tac, and pull the trigger!) are the reasons I have so much clutch left.
