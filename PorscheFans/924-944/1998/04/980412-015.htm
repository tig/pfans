Daniel: sounds like you've been driving my 944! I don't know the
solution to your problem but I can tell you what I have tried so you can
save some money and time.

Here are the symptoms on my '84 944:
1. Starts up fine after sitting a month, idles smooth.
2. Starts up fine every morning, idles smooth.
3. Starts and idles fine during the day if it has stopped less than an
hour.
4. Anytime it has been driven enough to get warm/hot, and sits for
between an hour and 12 hours, it starts poorly, idles rough for 5
minutes.

I have measured fuel pressure at rail and at pump and they are at spec.
Hardly any fuel leaks down so injector seals etc. are ok. Fuel pressure
at rail is marginally higher than spec at idle. Have changed cap, rotor,
wires, plugs and coil -- no effect.

So the summary is, definitely not an electrical problem. Could be the
fuel pressure regulator (does your fuel pump whine at times?), or a
dirty fuel filter. According to the dealer it could even be the winter
gas here in CO but I know the car enough to recognize this as a
consistent fuel-delivery related problem. Haven't gone all out to solve
it yet because it is just annoying, runs fine after a few minutes.

Let me know if you figure this one out or get any suggestions from
listmembers, TIA.
