Clemens, I can assure you that Dot 3 and Dot 4 are compatible although I
have heard some horror stories concerning old British sports cars with
natural rubber seals that do not take kindly to Dot 3.  One thing to
consider though; the Dot 4 has a higher boiling point so if you do any
Driver's Ed or just spirited driving, you have more 'brake insurance' with
the Dot 4.  Dot 5 is the silicone fluid which has an ever higher boiling
point and prevents corrosion of brake system parts since it does not absorb
moisture (as normal brake fluid will).  I'm not positive, but I don't
believe there are compatibility problems with it and the other two fluids. 
I have heard that it is much more difficult to get the Dot 5 properly bled
since it tends to absorb air and so the pedal feels a bit spongy compared
to conventional fluid.  Perhaps some list members have firsthand experience
with this.
