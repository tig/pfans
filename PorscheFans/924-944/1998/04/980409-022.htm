The Ignition system on these cars has a ballasted coil supply. This means
that when the engine is being cranked the coil is fed from the starter
motor circuit (terminal 16 on the starter according to the Haynes book) to
ensure full voltage at the coil under starting load. When the key is
released back to the normal run position the coil is fed through the
ballast resistor or resistor wire on some models. If the resistor is open
circuit the car will start but wont keep running. I am not sure of the
location of the actual resistor but it should be somewhere near either the
coil or electronic ignition module, If your car has a ballast wire it is
the wire from the ignition switch to the coil. Replacement resistors are
sold at most autoparts stores as these items are common to a lot of cars.
For a quick check place a voltmeter between the positive terminal of the
coil and the cars body.
When cranking the reading will be between 7-12 volts, In the run position
it should be between 6-10 volts.
