> While test driving the car I noticed grinding in the transmission at the
> shifter when changing from 1st to 2nd and 3rd to 4th, especially when it was
> done quickly.  It looks like the synchronizers could not keep up with the
> gears.  Is this a common problem?  Serious, or just an annoyance?

Worn syncros.  Bad news.

> My other question is regarding the driver's door, which closes with a loud
> bang as though there is no rubber bumper or any weather stripping (of course
> it does have weather stripping).  I'm trying to determine if the car has
> been in an accident.  It has been repainted.  So is this also a
> characteristic of the car?

Not normal.  The door should close with a solid, German thunk, unless it 
has been abused or the car has 300,000 miles on it.  IMHO, a repaint 
(especially an obvious one) signifies a questionable past.  If it was not 
wrecked, it probably sat out in the sun a lot, or was otherwise neglected.

I would keep looking, unless this car is cheap, and you are willing to
invest some $$$ and time to get it into the condition you desire.  Its
easier to better to find a good car, so you can spend more time driving
it, and less time fixing what the PO neglected.
