I'm not familiar with the Alpine model you mentioned, but I installed a 
Kenwood deck with the flip around face with no problems.  This type has a 
blank face that rotates to reveal the display and controls, and I have 
not had any interference problems.  As far as the space below your 
stereo, that is where a stock slide in storage compartment goes.  I 
installed a full DIN size Kenwood EQ in that space, and it fits very 
well.  I did have to trim some of the console at the bottom of this space 
(a horizontal bar and 2 screw holes), but it then slid in just fine.  
The console still stays in place even without those 2 screws.  You should 
not have to go to the trouble or expense of having a custom console 
built.
