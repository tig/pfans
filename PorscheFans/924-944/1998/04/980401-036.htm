All the porsches I have seen are very asymmetrical, so his experience
is not unusual.

I know for a fact that with a little "tweeking" of the fenders, 
315/35/17 can fit if the spacing is just right.

What he can do is cut the lip off with a air-cutter wheel or die grinder.

Alternatively he can stop by a bodyshop and have the entire fender
pushed outward with a Porta-power-ram (we did this on my 911 to fit
335/35/17).  THis requires some skill  but can be done without cracking the paint.

A combination of the two should yeild some happy fenders.

I suspect that the offset on the wheels is not optimized.  I generally
try to setup wheels with abit too much negative offset and fine tune
using spacers.


My 944turboS has 235/275 and there is a TON of room inner and outer.
