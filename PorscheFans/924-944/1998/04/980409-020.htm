No noise was introduced when the belts were replaced.  The whine was
introduced when the belts were retensioned at 731 miles after the
replacement.  I did not have the heart to see what 4000rpm sounded like,
it was bad enough at 3000rpm and then I took the car back to the shop.

It REALLY bugs me that the shop I took my car to uses the auto-tensioner
and not the 9201 tool to retension the timing belt.  I do not want to
re-ignite the belt thread, so I will just state I thought most felt the
auto-tensioner puts the tension in the ballpark, the use of the tool is
preferred (for those that do use the tool) and prescribed by the factory.
I will be seriously upset with the shop if any of my new H20 pump and
rollers die(s) in a year (or even worse in two after the warranty runs
out) because they have overtensioned the timing belt.  I am guessing they
overtightened it so the tension will be appropriate at least 30,000 miles. 
Myself, I would rather retension the belts at 15k miles and replace at 30k
and then skip the whine completely.
