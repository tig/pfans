If the escaping refrigerant gets on you, it can cause severe frostbite.  
Let a shop handle it.  You don't want to save a 
few measly bucks only to wind up losing a finger or worse.

> [...]
> I think that if you fill the system with the can upside down,
> it will flow faster, and the pressure in the can will open the
> low pressure switch, so you should not have to jumper the switch.

If you invert the can, you'll be adding liquid refrigerant to the
system instead of vapor.   You don't want to do that at the low
pressure side because the compressor doesn't compress liquids very
well.
