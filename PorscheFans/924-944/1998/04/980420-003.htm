Hi PFans,
	Last week, while on holiday from work, I managed to get to the Design
Museum in London (near Tower Bridge, on the South Bank), and went around
the 'Ferdinand Porsche Design Dinasty' Exhibition that is currently on
there, forming part of the 50th Anniverary celebrations.

Though there are only a small number of exhibits, if you are in , or can
get to, London during the summer, it's well worth a visit.  Included are
wind tunnel models, full size cars including the Beatle cabriolet
prototype, engines, models and cars from the Lohner-Porsche electric
vehicle, to the new 996 with the most significant models, (but no full
size, front engined water pumpers) the 944 2.5l, and 928 32V engines,
the former being sectioned.  There is also a fully sectioned Boxter,
built by the Porsche trainees.

The exhibition runs from early April to August.  Entrance fee is just
over a fiver.
