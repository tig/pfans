in my $0.02 here!

When using a torque wrench on any bolt - DO NOT USE ANY LUBRICANT unless
the factory manual suggest to - and only use what is recommended - i.e.
sometimes a light oil. Cleaning threads is a good idea but when you read a
torque value it depends on the presence of a certain amount of friction to
generate a reasonable fit - when using a lubricant everything changes -
view it as a 'torque multiplier' the ME's out there can add real numbers
here. This is also true for wheel nuts as well. Also remember that when you
torque stuff it should be at 'room' temperature - So don't torque hot wheel
nuts - this will also greatly distort the lugs and render them a bit
difficult to remove when cold!
