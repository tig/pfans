Clark,

Yes, I do know *why* the tap is tapered.  I didn't say it was tapered to hold
the bolt or stud, only that it *will* hold the bolt or stud in an interference
fit.

As for the difference in the coefficient of friction with oil vs. anti seize,
I'm sure anti seize is a little better, but probably not much ( molly
lubricants is like .06-.07).

I never said anybody *should* go use anti seize on their head studs.  What I
said, or meant to say was that *I* feel from *my* experience in similar
applications was that *if* anti seize had been used on the stud during initial
assembly, the threads would have had a *much* better chance of not pulling out
of the block.  I also said the reason *I* felt like Porsche used the locktite
on the studs initially was because they never intended the studs to be
removed.  Again *I* feel like if they had anticipated continuous R&R, they
would have used a compound other than locktite, like they did on lug nuts.
This in was way means "go out and use anti seize on your head studs".  If you
ask me what *I* would do?  Well I wouldn't be afraid at all to use studs
processed with my dry film lubricant (.030 coefficient of friction) on my head
studs.  I would use it in a second, because I have "real world" experience with
it in very similar applications.  Hey, but then again I also use a tool for the
timing belt, and we already know where people on this list stand on that.
