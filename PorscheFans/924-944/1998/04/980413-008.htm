Don't have the Carbon Fiber, but owning a classic Mercedes Benz, I had to 
have some real wood to contrast the stark interior.  Looking at the kits 
out there, ($$$$$) I wanted some new mechanical pieces more (Belts, Rollers 
Chain Tensioner, etc..).

 I found a Walnut Veneer kit at my local Home Builders supply store that 
had a glue backing that was activated with heat. (Iron or heat gun). Took 
my dash strip off, along with the Window switch area and made templates for 
all.   Cut the veneer to match, attached with the heat, finished the edges 
with black marker, and then applied a spray on Deftthane quick dry finish. 
 One weekend and it looks great.   I took photos with my camera of the 
steps and I'm going to write an article for others to try.   Total cost was 
less than $25.00.
