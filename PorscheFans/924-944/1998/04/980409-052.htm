Dave: this is your cruise-control servo. It does this when the control
unit goes bad, fairly common. The clicking is the servo getting a bad
signal from the cruise computer and trying to take over the throttle, so
I would immediately disconnect the cruise computer. In my '84 944 the
clicking progressed to all sorts of weird throttle pulses, sticking
throttle etc. More often and severe in cold weather.

If your car is an early 944, the cruise computer is behind the radio.
You can reach it from the driver footwell after undoing the black trim.
It is a flat silver box, with a thick black harness. It is secured with
two screws and slides out. I bought a used computer from a listmember,
no clicks, cruise works fine. The servo rarely fails, but you could try
cleaning/lubricating it.
