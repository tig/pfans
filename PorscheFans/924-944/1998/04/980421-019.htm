To anyone setting a 924/931 timing belt,

Beware that the square shaped indentation on the back side of the 
cam sprocket is NOT the timing mark to align with the pointer on the
valve cover.  The proper mark on the cam sprocket is the round indentation
on the front side, opposite the valve cover.  The first time I replaced
my 931 timing belt, I used the square mark since it was closer to the
pointer and it costed me 4 intake valves.  I think the 924 uses the same
sprocket.  The square mark has some purpose in another application of
the sprocket, but for 931s it serves only to ruin intake valves.

(924s are not interference engines, so the intake valves are not ruined.
The engine will not start though.)

ALIGN THE ROUND INDENTATION ON THE CAM SPROCKET TO THE POINTER.
