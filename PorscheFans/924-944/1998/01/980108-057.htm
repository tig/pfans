In a message dated 98-01-08 12:37:24 EST, zmf@uwyo.edu writes:

<< One comment I found puzzling in
 the article was to do more coasting.  I assume this means setting up for
 early apexing and early acceleration out of a corner, but it seems to
 contradict all the textbook admonitions to be either hard on the brakes or
 hard on the throttle. >>

Carrol Smith addresses this in one of his "...to Win"  books (either Tune to
Win or Drive to Win -- both are excellent).  Coasting, in this context, means
a steady throttle, rather than no throttle -- just enough to overcome inertia,
friction, aero drag, etc. so as to keep the car at a constant speed.
Typically, you'll do this in the part of a turn where you're generating
maximum cornering force, which requires that you not be generating any
acceleration or braking forces (typically, the segment just before the apex). 
By the way, this doesn't contradict Barry Lenoble's comments in a related post
regarding keeping the throttle down in certain types of turns (this track
driving stuff is complicated -- that's what makes it fun!).  Barry's
addressing the need under certain conditions to settle the rear suspension and
increase the vertical load on the rear tires to increase their grip.
