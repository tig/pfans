> I just got used to it. Set at 60 to go 70, set at 70 to go 80, etc...

> To Gregor Diseth --
> 
> You have a malfunction someplace in your cruise control if the RPMs
> climb when you depress the clutch. First thing I'd check is the clutch
> switch. Both my '84 and 85.5 944's would disengage the cruise control
> the moment I depress the clutch. It would re-engage as soon as I let out
> the clutch. In both cars, the cruise completely shuts off when you tap
> the brake, and must be reset manually after that. This is normal
> operation. It's on page 22 of the '84 owner's manual, and on page 24 of
> the 85.5 owner's manual. The only caution (in both manuals) is not to
> put the car in Neutral with the cruise control on "to prevent excessive
> engine rpm".
