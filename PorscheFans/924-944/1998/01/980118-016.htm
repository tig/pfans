Our shop always has to deal with this particular 944 customer complaint.
It's almost always a two part scenerio as follows.
1. Battery area fills up due to lack of service in the battery drains.  The
early 944's have a round "flapper" hole which lets the water accumulation go
out the front of the battery box area into the firewall.  The later version
drains the water underneath the battery into the R/F wheel arch.  In both
versions it remains important to maintain vigilance in keeping drains clean
of debris(leaves etc)
2. When left unattended for any appreciable amount of time,  the battery
area will develop rust and holes will appear on its floor, necessitating
welding cracks or holes closed and re-touching the undercoating.
    Most important issue is later 944's(85.5 and up) is location of engine
computer right underneath the battery area.  It should not enjoy a bath of
water on a regular basis.  Guess what a new DME box costs?
