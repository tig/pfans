>dsholt@hotmail.com wrote:
>> My '84 944 with 68k miles has an odd warm-running problem that
>> I've partially diagnosed.  After warming up for about 15 minutes,
>> the engine will suddenly start idling at 200rpm, and the power
>> becomes very low at all engine RPMs.  The engine will return to
>> normal once it has cooled down completely.
>> Using a timing light, I found that cylinders #2 and #3 had an
>> intermittent (or perhaps weak?) spark at idle, and no spark at
>> off-idle.   [.. deletions ..]

And then he wrote:
>I think I've fixed my problem.  I found some tiny metal shavings
>clinging to the tip of the reference mark sensor.  (The sensor is
>magnetic).  With the shavings removed, the engine ran perfectly
>when warm for the first time since the problem arose.  Shavings
>were also clinging to the speed sensor.
>With the advice and encouragement from a list member (thanks Barry),
>and with the proper tool (10mm swivel socket), I had the sensors
>out in a few minutes.
>My theory is that the metal shavings lessened the clearance between
>the sensor and the flywheel.  The engine heat caused the flywheel
>to expand, which made a marginal clearance even smaller, thus causing
>faulty sensor operation.
>Doug Holtsinger
