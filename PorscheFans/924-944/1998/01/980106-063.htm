joeybabe writes:

"After driving about an hour, particularly if in traffic it just dies.
Just cranks, have never gotten under the hood to see if it has a spark.
Always alone when it happens.  Then after 15 to 20 minutes starts up no
problem."

My 87 944T did about the same.  I removed the distributor cap and noticed
corrosion and damage at the center electrode.  I replaced the cap and rotor
while I was there, and it cranks, and stays running.  New cap ~ $45, new rotor
~$30.

Terry writes:

"I am currently trying to troubleshoot a noise from the distributor cap
area of my 1987 944s. The noise is only present at startup, and disappears
after only 1-2 minutes of running. "

Did you notice the gasket under the dist. cap?  These things take a set, and
become about 1/8 of their original thickness.  If they aren't thick enough to
resist the cap when the 1/4 turn fasteners are engaged, the cap can actually
rattle.  Look for mild scratches/scarring on the bottom outside edges, or
bottom face.  New gasket ~ $2.
