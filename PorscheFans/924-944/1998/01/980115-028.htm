Actually, the "unsprung weight" theory isn't that far from the truth.  Any
gaseous substance lighter than ordinary air would be used to lighten the
weight of the vehicles strictly for economies of freight.  After all, most
transportation costs are based on the shipment's weight, and if you can use
something lighter than air, the resultant freight charge would be less.
Just imagine how much the "destination charge" would be on our cars if
ordinary air was used.
