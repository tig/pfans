You should also replace the rotor as well as the distributor cap.  If 
you're not getting good spark, I'd check the coil by measuring both 
primary and secondary impedences.  You can also try disconnecting one 
spark plug at a time to see whether that makes a difference.  If any 
one plug makes no difference, there's the guilty one.

I assume you have good system voltage.

When this sort of problem occurs, it can be due to a bad temp sender. 
 If your engine looks to the DME like it's always cold, it will run way 
rich and, as such, poorly.
