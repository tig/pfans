Anyway, you asked for comments, I can't resist:

Hardware:

A 951 is  can be a very neutral car.  However, one pays the price by being
very close to oversteer. Its the nature of the beast. My S2 has the turbo S
suspension and is set up for autocross. You sneeze on the throttle at the
wrong point and this car spins. My 924S is(was) much more forgiving and I
still find the difference quite amazing. OTOH, the turbo suspension is
capable of much more. Farzaan has commented on your tire choice and I
agree. What size tires are you running? 225's and 245's will break away
much more abruptly than, say 205's and 225's or 205's all around. 

Software:

This isn't as cut-and-dry as you may think. IMHO, the only way you would
have been able to avoid what happenned is if you had previously experienced
it and practiced getting out of it. Theory and talk just don't go very far.
You have to drive, take the car to a deserted parking lot in slippery
conditions and do bad things, see what happens, and practice until you
don't have to think about what your reaction should be. Autocross is a fun
way of doing this but often doesn't offer enough seat time to truly "get
the hang of it."
