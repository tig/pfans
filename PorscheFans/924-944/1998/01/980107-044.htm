I was a big fan of self-preservation when I instructed (I don't anymore). I
was somewhat delighted to hear Mr. Cobb's first words to an (experienced)
student were the same as I used to say - "Don't scare me!" Technically (as
opposed to mentally), most beginners turn in way to early if left to their
own devices. This is very scary to the instructor. (The student usually
just tightens up on corner exit and doesn't know any better). Thus, one
teaches a very safe line, late apexes, straight-line braking only, etc.
etc. etc. Once the student can drive reasonably at that level, then the
parameters can change...

Remember, everyone has a different style of driving, and there is no one
correct "method."
