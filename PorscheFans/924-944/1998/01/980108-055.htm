On Thu, 8 Jan 1998, Brian Burch wrote:

>> I have an '81 924 which runs beautifully until it rains.  Then it misfires
terribly and barely runs.  I have replaced the plugs, rotor, cap, ignition
wires and coil but the problem is still there. I've even hosed down the
engine but it only misfires under load, not at idle. <<

Remove the distributor cap to see if there is any moisture condensed on 
the inside surface.  Such moisture will make the car run horribly if at all.

>> Another related problem is that I occasionally pick up water in the
gas. Not alot but enough to cause the car to, once again, misfire until
the fuel additive and/or a new tank of gas runs through it Any Ideas? <<

It is ok to use a fuel system additive that is a fuel dryer.  These 
usually contain methanol or other similar alcohol, which will absorb 
water into the fuel, allowing it to pass through without causing running 
problems.
