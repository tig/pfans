Hello Brian & P-Fans
	Did you check to see if the plug wires are rubbing or arcing on the 
inside of the hood? Typically the hood insulation will fall/decay/rub off 
the inside of the hood area, then the plug wires arc under load w/wet!
	Porsche sell a replacement pad, a small insulator to prevent this on 
NA 944's; part #944-556-772-01. This pad is listed in the 924/944 Parts
Book.
	Did you check the set screw that holds down the ignition rotor?

Hello Manfred & P-Fans
	The problem you drscribe is a bad fuel pressure regulator! 
The regulator is sticking causing the fuel pressure to go sky high 
(the fuel reg is on the return side of the fuel rail, back of the rail 
by the battery), I forget why pulling off one electrical connector 
to a  injector fixes the problem but it does. REPLACE THAT
 FUEL REGULATOR! Before it leaks fuel all over the engine! 
 Don't even mess with those 3 Bar (stock is 2.5 Bar)  or higher 
regulators - you will not get more power just less throttle
 response and fuel mileage! 

	Fuel pressure regulator part #944-110-198-03, $70.
