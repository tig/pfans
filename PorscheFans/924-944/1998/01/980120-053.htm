Tim, my '85 944 has some tappet noise on start up (about 3 - 5 seconds when
left overnight; longer if the car sits several days).  Tappet noise is
harmless.  It just indicates that the lifters are worn a bit and will leak
down over time.  Even running the engine with a bad tappet will not really
harm anything; performance will suffer and emissions will go up (perhaps
this could shorten the life of the cat converter).  

What is important is that you verify that it is tappet noise and not bottom
end noise (which is usually characterized as a more heavy sounding thud
rather than the 'tick-tick' sound of tappets).  Loose bearing clearances in
the connecting rods and/or the main bearings are definitely something to
worry about.  A good mechanic should be able to tell you which noise your
car is making without having to remove anything. I use a stethoscope-like
instrument (a long thin rod attached to a diaphragm and ear tubes) to
listen to and isolate where noises are coming from.  It works surprisingly
well and should be available in auto part stores.  Good luck.
