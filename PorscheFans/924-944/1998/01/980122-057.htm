There's been some discussion on the startig problems with the 924's.
Here's my personal experience with this anoying problem. I have a 77' 924
with alittle over 100K miles. From the first day I bought it, it's had the
hot starting problem. I have gone and replaced the two fuel accumilators,
the pump, injector seals and yet the problem is still there. 
What my mechanic did was to take off the cold shart injector wire and hook
it upto a manual switch. Now when I push this switsh while cranking the
engine fuel goes to the engine. Just enough to catch the engine and after
that the normal fuel presure kicks in. At this pint the only thig I can
think of is the injectors themselves. If they are leaking after I stop the
car then that cauld be the hot start problem. 
I have had this system for the last year or so and never failed. The only
compromise is the cold starts. You need to press the button when you
cranck the engine in the mornning. I can live with that. 
My advise is if you want a quick solution then do what I did. If you
really want to solve the problem then do a fuel presure test in various
places alonf the fuel line. If your loosing fuel presure after you shut
down the engine then this test will point out where the problem is. 
Good luck. Thats all I can say for now.
