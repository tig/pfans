At 09:29 AM 1/26/98 , Don Istook wrote:

>to the locking mechanism, you will find this broken piece.  Usually it
>it a spot weld that has failed.  

>There are different ways to fix this problem.  The easiest, but maybe
>not the longest lasting, is to drill a small hole through the two pieces
>of metal that should be welded together. Screw in a small sheet metal
>screw, shortened for the proper length.  This fix can last quite a long
>time.  Other ways are to re-spotweld, weld, or epoxy the pieces back
>together.  I usually go for the screw method.

Hi Don, have you ever noticed this broken spot weld to contribute to the
power locks cycling up and down when the key is inserted?

I had that very problem plus the central locking light was on all the time.
Once I rewelded the spot, the central locking worked properly and the power
locks quit doing the fandango.

A note to anyone contemplating spot welding is to disconnect your battery
to protect your DME. I even pulled the plug on the DME as I didn't want to
leave anything to chance.

Another thing that contributes to window rattling is the felt strip slides
down in the channel thus the glass is loose.
