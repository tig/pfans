To figure your true speed in mph use this simple formula:

rpm x tire_circumference/(trans_gear_ratio x diff_gear_ratio x 1056.0)

where:

rpm =3D engine speed, revs/min.
tire_circumference =3D rolling circumference of tire, inches.
trans_gear_ratio =3D transmission gear ratio.
diff_gear_ratio =3D differential gear ratio.
1056.0 =3D conversion factor to convert units to mph.
Note: for circumference in cm, use 2682.24 instead of 1056.0


=46or example.  Pick 3000 rpm in 5th gear of my S2 with 225/50-16 rear
tires.  The circumference is approximately (not counting for tire growth
at speed) equal to:

[(225 * 0.5 * 2) / 25.4 + 16 ] * 3.14159 =3D 78.1"

rpm =3D 3000
tire_circumference =3D 78.1 inches
trans_gear_ratio =3D 0.78, 5th gear
diff_gear_ratio =3D 3.889

We get:

3000 x 78.1/(0.78 x 3.889 x 1056.0) =3D  73.1 mph
