To answer to question about what the balance shafts do. Each shaft has a
counter weight on it. The counterweights are aligned 180 degrees out of
sync with each other. Large in-line four cylinder engines inherently
produce large second order harmonic vibrations. The balance shaft turn the
counterweights at twice the rpm of the engine. With one located high on one
side of the engine and the other low on the opposite side of the engine, it
balances the vibrations of the large spinning mass inside the engine.

With the balance shaft belt off you can actually turn the shafts by hand
and feel if there is a problem with the alignment. It's a fairly lengthy
explanation though and I won't go into it unless you really want to hear
it.
