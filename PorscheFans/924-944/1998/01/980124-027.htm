No, I don't think so.  The standard 968 does not have the 951S type
suspension or brakes.  According to the April 1993 Excellence, the M030
has the 11.97" rotors, 32mm thick (ala 928S4), while the standard car has
11.73" x 28mm rotors.  The following changes are highlighted:

=46ront Axle:

Steering knuckles
Wheel hub, to accommodate larger disc
Coil spring, height adjustable and stiffer
Shock - Koni adjustable
Stabilizer - 30mm tubular
Brake disc, perforated
Caliper - 36/44mm pistons vs 36/40 std
Brake pads, hoses and lines are different

Rear Axle:

Vibration damper - Koni with additional spring, torsion bars are normal
Stabilizer - 19mm, manual trans (text says it is 20mm)
Brake disc, perforated
Brake pads

Excellence described the handling as somewhat hair-trigger if you push it
due to the very stiff rear suspension and increased oversteer.  In daily
driving it is very, very neutral however and any tendency to understeer
has been eliminated. "Option M030 makes this 968 the most responsive
Porsche you can buy today.  It reacts quicker to steering input than even
the RS America."
