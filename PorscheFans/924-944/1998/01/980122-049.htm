> The above post concerns metal shavings affecting sensors in the
> bellhousing, but I've also seen these on the wheel speed sensors for ABS on
> my S2.  They stand on end, pointing away from the sensor.  Don't know if
> the ABS sensor is magnetic;

It is magnetic.  The ABS sensor is a magnetic pickup.  It uses the toothed hoop
that is pressed around the hub to determine the speed of a wheel.

> the last year or so, since I've been doing a lot of heavy braking, it's
> seemed like the ABS system is not consistent. Sometimes I can haul down
> smoothly from high speeds, and sometimes the brakes shudder so much it
> feels like the rotors are warped.

I suppose too many shavings could affect the readings of the pickup.  I'd guess
that the ABS light would come on because the pickup would be inconsistent with
the rest of the car.

As for your ABS working.  Seems fine to me.  If your smoothly braking, then
your ABS is not engaged.  If your brakes are grabbing then releasing then it is
working.  The ABS unit gives off a humm when engaged.  You'll here it coming
from under the dash on the passengers side.
