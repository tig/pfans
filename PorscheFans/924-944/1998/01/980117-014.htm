Someone at the dealership swapped wheels. It happened all the time when
911s and 944s came with the wheels that were interchangable. Most
first-time Porsche buyers din't realize the Fuchs wheels were
considerably more expensive than the cast wheels (phone dials or cookie
cutters). So you dicker with the dealer and he decides to switch the
wheels to give you a 'discount'. The other variation is the customer who
came before you and wanted a car with this option that didn't come with
it. The 944s that came with the Fuchs 7s and 8s were particularly
popular with the 911 crowd. I'm sure if you traced it back to the dealer
that he would state this discrepancy was mentioned.

There is a world of difference between forged wheels and cast wheels.
Forged wheels are considerably stronger and lighter. Cast wheels are
considerably cheaper. Also, beware that there are aftermarket cast
wheels that look identical to Fuchs. They are junk. Look in the
Automotion catalog for an example.
