Just remember...don't rely on jacks, even hydaulic ones, while doing
ANY work on/underneath the car. Use jack stands. And then keep the jack
in place with just a little tension on it as a back up. Watch your
fingers in case something slips. Once one of my jackstands slipped a
notch, luckily I was not under it at the time. So I'm still not 100%
comfortable with the above setup especially if I have the jackstand
heigher than base height.

In order to get my hydraulic jack underneath the front frame rail I
back up onto ramps then lift each front side and place a jackstand
under the frame rail. The hydraulic jack fits under the rear frame rail
to lift the rear when I need to remove rear tires.

For the person needing to jack at the track I would lift the rear first
with a hydraulic jack, put a jackstand under, remove jack, lift front,
place a second jackstand under, then remove/change tires. Remove
jackstands and repeat for other side. Takes only a few minutes per
side.
