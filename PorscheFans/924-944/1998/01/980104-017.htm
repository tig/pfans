This is of course a meaningless test since spark plugs don't fire in free
air, but under high pressure in a combustion chamber.  I think you would
get the same demonstration results by using an old, worn out "normal"
carbon wire and a brand new "normal" carbon wire.

>The store said that this special cable system produces extra horsepower,
>increase mileage up to 15%, and increase acceleration.

No spark plug cable will "increase" horsepower.  Let me make that clear.
None, zero, zip, nada.  The best that can be expected is that the cable
will deliver most of the energy that the system puts out.  You can't get
more power, or higher than stock voltage by changing wires.  All you can
do is try to minimize the losses that all wires have.  That's why the
newest systems don't use wires at all.  Each plug has its own remote coil
system that attaches directly to each spark plug.

My advice would be to return these gimmicky wires and get some high
quality normal wires.
