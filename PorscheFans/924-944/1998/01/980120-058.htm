I would knock them out from behind with a long brass drift, or pull 
them out with an oil seal puller.  A screwdriver is not a very 
convenient way to get these seals removed.

A set of brass drifts will come in hand when removing and reinstalling 
the bearing outer races.  A plumber's propane torch is very handy too - 
it lets you heat up the hub enough to shrink the races in and out. 
 (Aluminum has roughly twice the thermal coeff. of expansion of steel.)

If your car is very high mileage (say, 150K miles and up), I recommend 
replacing the spindle clamps and their thrust washers.  The parts are 
not cheap, but they are a good investment in safety.
