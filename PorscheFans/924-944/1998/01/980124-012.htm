> While I currently believe that the 944 block has liners, I'll take some
time
> trying to get information from either Porsche or KS to see if the
> block is made
> completely of the AlSi alloy or not.
>
> My reasons for believing that the block uses liners are:
>
> -The 944 blocks themselves have a slight separation in the cylinder
> tops, as if
> there is a liner installed in the block.
>
> -Liners or inserts are available from KS to "re-new" the block.  These
liners
> are currently used by KS to manufacturer aluminum blocks.  KS casts the
blocks
> around the liners, providing a better bonding and seal to the liners than
> boring and installing.
>
> -AlSi is not needed for the whole block, only for the cylinders.
>
>
> I'll try to keep people updated.  If the 944 block is a complete Alusil
> product, then I'll let the list know (and I'll admit I'm wrong about the
> liners).
>
> Regardless of what the block is, KS does and can provide liners with the
same
> bore dimensions of the 944 block that can be installed into the block.
This
> may provide a better alternative to cast iron sleeves or overboring.
Whether
> or not it is a cost effective method of redoing a whole block is another
> matter.  It may be cheaper to overbore and find oversize pistons.
