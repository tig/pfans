I too have found this to be true. I believe the "round sockets" are used
to attach the car to the assembly line while it is being built. When I
was autocrossing my NA 944 I'd swap wheels as many as 30 times per year
and everything got bent over time. Now I usually jack the car from the
rear suspension frame located just behind the door. It balances the car
as well as the 'diamond spot' so you lift both ends the same amount and
it doesn't deform over time.  Jack stands can then be deployed at the
rear of the front suspension attachment points and at the end of the
torsion tubes in the rear.
