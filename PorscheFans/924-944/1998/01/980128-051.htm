Steve,

Curiousity got the better of me, and I just tried the following
(unscientific) test:

In 8 test tubes containing 150 ml. of Techron each, I placed some aluminium,
brass, painted steel, portion of a fuel line, vacuum line, galvanized steel,
and a rubber band.  Within 45 minutes the rubber band was soft, the vacuum
line and fuel line were disintergrating, the painted steel was not affected.
Much of the oxidation was cleaned off the brass, but the aluminium and
galvanized steel was totally cleaned.

I have no way of knowing for sure without further testing, but under
microscope the dissolved rubber from the fuel line in the test tube appeared
strikingly similar to the black deposits in the gasoline from my fuel filter
last week.

Thanks for the tip.
