>Thought I might give you a heads up on an issue I just became aware of. 
>When you replace the balance shaft belt you want to do two things. The 
>first being upgrade to the 18 mm wide belt and the second being make 
>certain the belt is retensioned approx. 2k miles after it is installed.
>
>This just occurred on my 968 only 5k after the new belt was installed.  My 
>mechanic had written on the repair order I was supposed to come in for the 
>retension after 2k but I didn't read it/ didn't remember/screwed up. This 
>is not something I will do again.  Fortunately, retiming was <$200. and no 
>damage was incurred.

Thanks for the input, Michael. I'll add that I had a new balance shaft belt
slip in just 1100 miles.  And all I did was accept an invitation from a
T-bird to drag to the next stop light.  The vibration set in immediately. 
Depending on how much you trust your mechanic, you might want to have it
retensioned sooner, particularly if you're scheduled for some hard driving.
 During this episode, I had two putatively good mechanics in two states
screw up the timing, one a dealership mechanic who had worked in Germany
(are there no absolutes in life?).  Overall, it took 5 tries to get it
right. Obvious advice, but when you have a balance shaft belt replaced,
make sure the engine feels the same as it used to when you drive away.
