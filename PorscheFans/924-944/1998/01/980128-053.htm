Well, the saga continues. I have determined that my clicking fuel relay is due
to not enough steady trigger voltage at the transistor that controls the
relay. I have determined that if I have only one fuel injector plugged in it
will not click and there is enough voltage to keep the relay closed, but if I
connect any two injectors, does not matter which (even though they fire as
pairs) I get the click. All the injectors indicate the same resistance on an
ohmeter, so either there is not enough current getting to the them, which the
transistor apparently senses, or they are using too much, which seems unlikely
at this point, since I cannot imagine that they have all gone wacko at once.
One member of the group suggested installing a capacitor across the relay coil
to stabilize the juice, which makes me wonder if there is one in the dme which
is shot. There does seem to be a huge demand for the schematics on the dme,
based on my in-mail this morning, so if I find a source I will post it. As
usual, any thoughts on this appreciated. Frenna@aol.com (87924S)
