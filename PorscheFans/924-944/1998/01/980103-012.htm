Steve,

The 944S had a pressure switch in its power steering pump, which when 
the pump was loaded heavily was to have opened the idle valve a little 
extra to help the engine idle smoothly.  The switch took +12 straight 
from the 30 circuit - whichever bozo designed the circuit left a fuse 
out.  The PS pump switch would fail periodically, grounding the +12 and 
burning up the harness.

Porsche finally woke up and issued a recall.  The service involved 
abandoning one of the wires to the PS pump switch and running a new one 
from the CEB via a spare fused circuit. (I got the notice, scheduled a 
service for the following week, and the next day had my harness burn - 
great timing!)

In actuality, the PS pump switch is not very useful or effective.  I 
replaced my leaking pump with one without a switch and my car has run 
fine.
