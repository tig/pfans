>NBC Dateline did a story on Sears batteries a few months ago.  NBC
>bought a Sears battery, discharged it, and then returned it to a Sears
>store. Later on, they went to a different Sears store that was nearby
>and found their used battery back on the shelf for sale as a "new"
>battery. (Before they returned the battery to Sears, NBC had burned a
>"NBC" symbol into the battery case in order to identify it as theirs).
>NBC also sent a "new" Sears battery to this private lab which tore it
>apart for analysis.  The lab concluded that the battery was not a new
>battery, but a used one. (Sears disputed those findings).
>NBC didn't establish whether this was a widespread practice or limited
>to just a few stores.  However, they did find a Sears training manual
>which emphasized that battery returns hurt their business.
>Last year, Florida's Attorney General's office launched an investigation
>into allegations that Sears was selling used batteries as new.
>Personal note: I have had two Diehard batteries go dead on me, both
>times when I let my 944 sit for a few weeks.  The first one was
>installed by the PO, and it died about a year after I got the car.  The
>second one died after two years of use, and a few weeks after I saw the
>NBC story.  Both batteries were purchased in the Central Texas area.
>I replaced the last Diehard with a battery from Walmart (snigger).  I've
>let the car sit for a few weeks since, and the battery is still strong,
>so I don't think there's anything wrong with the car.
