>At the risk of opening a giant can o' worms, what is the opinion on tires?
>This is a "summer only" car.  To help narrow it down, I was originally
>looking at Dunlop D40 M2 versus the Dunlop Sport 4000.  The guys at Tire
>Rack suggested the Bridgestone Potenza RE71 as a significantly better tire.

IMHO:

RE71 : Good, cheap.
AVSs1: Better, not bad ("affordable").
Pilot: Better yet, way too expensive.

Deal: Yoko AVSi's once they start clearing them out.

BTW, the above tires will give a tread life that is disconcertingly short
compared to a Sport 4000. About one-third, I'd guess. 

(FWIW, I have Sport 4000's thanks to a PO that I'll be selling in the spring.)

>Also, I currently have 225/50R16 at all four corners.  What is the
>recommendation on sizes?  Tire Rack said that 245/45R16 would fit for the

 Depends. For pure street use, 205/55's front and 225/50 rear is v.
nice. In more vigorous driving 225/50 and 245/45 is the way to go. My track
tires are 245/45 fr 265/45 rear and these sizes are not very, er,
user-friendly on the street, and I'm not convinced they offer any great
advantage (unless I went to wider rims).

>rears and then I assume that I would replace the fronts with 225/50R16
>again.  Will the extra wide tires fit on the rims?

245's will fit on 8" rims (maybe even 7"), but some 245's are moulded to
really prefer a 9" rim.
