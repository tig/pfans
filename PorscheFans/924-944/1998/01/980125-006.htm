Ditto to what George B said.

I have had two (not 20 or so in my case...) 944 blocks apart, and there
ain't no sleeves as the block arrives from the factory. Plain old
aluminum (as far as I can see) with (I'm sure) whatever other compatible
alloys Porsche threw in the mix for strength, wear resistance, and all
that other mechanical design stuff.

All this talk of hi-tech "siliconizing" and such...well...I just dunno
if it's real, needed, or 'after-marketing' hype. Sure, in boring, you
need to re-sleeve things, because you are enlarging the cylinders from
stock. And steel seems an easy, cheap (I know, that's a relative term
when appled to Porsches) and a reliable way to reinstate wall strength
intregity.
