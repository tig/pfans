I seem to have the typical noise from my new balance belt. (97 924S) Its not
all that bad, and I may well ignore it and see if it dissapears after some
break in time. I did replace the rollers, and the old belt was quite loose,
but seemed to be working fine. According to the factory manual, it seems the
idler roller for this belt is to be set so that there is a half milimeter gap
from roller to belt as it goes around lower balance shaft sprocket, and at the
same time the roller should be deflecting the long span of the belt above half
to one millimeter. This is how I have it set up, but considering that this
requires a belt tooth slapping this roller every millisecond, I have to wonder
if this is really what they mean, or if perhaps they have changed the setting
to zero deflection or even a slight gap. I assume this roller serves to
prevent both tooth jumping on the sprocket below, which does not wrap around
much, and slapping of the belt above the roller, which the old one did a lot
of due to looseness, to the extent of rubbing a line in the water pump pulley
(pump also replaced, by the way). Anyone have any thoughts on this. If its
still making noise after a thousand miles I may just go ahead and reduce the
