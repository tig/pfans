I was just doing some calculations on tires, and decide to ask to the
collective wisdom to know if they are OK. I'm running on 195/65-14 on
my 924. I know (I guess) that the original tires are 185/70-14. So,
I've decided to determine the speedometer error. I think that the
second range of numbers (e.g. 65 and 70) is the percentage of the
width which will give the height of the tire.
So, I must multiply the width of the tire by the percentage, multiply
by 2 (in order to obtain the diameter), add the wheel (14 ' = 35.6 cm),
and determine the perimeter.
So for the 185/70-14, I obtain:
(18.5 x 0.7 x 2 + 35.6) x PI = 193.208 cm
For the 195/65-14:
(19.5 x 0.65 x 2 + 35.6) x PI = 191.480 cm
Subtracting 193.208 - 191.480 = 1.728 cm
With the 195/65-70, for each complete rev of the tire, I will go less
1.728 cm. This is 0.894% of 193.208 cm. Do this mean that I have only a
0.894% error on the speedometer?
It seems to me that is a to little error, but these are my
calculations. Am I running on a error?
