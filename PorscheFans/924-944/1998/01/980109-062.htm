On Sat, 10 Jan 1998, Tom Pultz wrote:

> The fluid is currently draining from the non-LSD transmission in my 1990
> S2.  Tomorrow I'll be replacing the fluid with Red Line synthetic 75W90NS.
> What I need to know is: what is the correct procedure?  Do I just put in
> approximately 2.0 liter (2 quarts plus about 4 oz) and call it OK, or do I
> need to fill it to some prescribed level?  I don't have the manuals for
> the S2 but the Turbo Supplement says it also holds 2.0 liters and that the
> fluid level should be about 6-8mm below the filler neck.
> 
> At this point I plan to just add 2 quarts plus a few oz and call it OK.
> Anyone else do it differently?  Thanks.

Yes!  Fill the transmission until the oil starts to drool out the fill
hole.  The level should be just at the bottom of the fill hole.  Fill
slowly, as oil may come out prematurely, giving the impression the gearbox
is full when in fact it is not.  You can jack up the car on the passenger
side should you want the oil level slightly below the fill hole.. I doubt
it matters, though.  I probably overfilled mine slightly this summer, as
it did take about 2 1/2 quarts (including some spillage).

If you are wondering how to fill.. I just bought some clear hose at the
hardware store and pushed it over the bottle nipple.  Use the largest
diameter that will fit tightly on the nipple, or the oil will be difficult
to squeeze through it.  I then threaded the hose though a top opening in
the rear wheel, over the exhaust pipe, and into the transmission.  I then
stood patiently by the wheel with the bottle held up above transmission
level, and patiently squeezed away.
