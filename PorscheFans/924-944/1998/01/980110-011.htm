Yes, you want to be about 6 - 8 mm below the filler bore.  It will 
probably not be the end of the world to be a little high or low.  There 
is a trade-off made in gearbox design when picking fill level.  It 
needs to be high enough that oil gets picked up by all the gear teeth 
as they ride through it.  But not so high that it churns and foams, 
getting overheated and sapping energy.

Since I cannot really feel transmission oil with my pinky, I made a 
little dipstick for myself some years ago.  Take a little brass shim 
stock, bend a 90 degree angle in it, and scribe lines at 6 and 8 mm 
from the bend.  Just lay it on the base of the bore, et voila.
