Capt. Timothy Radsick, USAF wrote:
> 
> The main question I have is regarding the calibration bar.  I figured 
> out how to pull the pin on the gage, lower the sliding arm, put 
> the bar in the gauge, and slide the arm back. When I do that, the 
> gauge reads about 3.8.  What is it supposed to read? What am I 
> calibrating it to?
> 
Unload the gauge. Zero the gauge using the rotating dial. Load in the
reference shim. Insert the small hex adjusting tool into the little hole
and adjust until the gauge reads 4.0. Remove shim and verify zero. Repeat
until the gauge reads zero unloaded and 4.0 loaded without any adjustment.
>
> The other question I have is about the gauge itself.  When I have the 
> calibration bar inserted it reads about 3.8. If I increase the 
> reading a bit manually (with a gentle push of the finger) the gauge 
> increases, but when I release pressure it returns only to around 4.1 
> or so. If I push back on the bar, it will go back to 3.8.  It would 
> seem to me that hysteresis would not be a good thing in a $400 
> measuring tool.  Is my tool defective and should I send it back to 
> Vertex? Or is it supposed to be that way? If so, on which "side" of 
> the hysteresis loop should I measure?
> 
You are correct, the gauge isn't perfect and probably isn't the tool of
choice for the NASA technicians trying to adjust the tension on the latest
rover treads or whatever. Just always take the measurement by loading the
tool on the belt and take the measurement on the low side of the hysteresis.
