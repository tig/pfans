In the Jan 10 posting I noticed a reference to 'street driven' braided s.s.
brake lines to the effect that it was wrong to put them on a street car. 
While I can understand that some racing parts are not ideal for street
applications (such as the Fabcar arms), I am not sure how the s.s. lines
fall into the same category (apparently I missed the thread on these from
some time ago).  I installed a set of the s.s. lines on my car (in which I
used to do frequent driver's ed) several years ago and have had no trouble
with them whatsoever.  Nor do I ever wash the underside of my car.  How is
it that these type of brake lines are unsuitable for street use?  I suspect
any problems with these stem from incorrect installation.
