Well I have the brochure in front of me.  The 944 GTP (not GTS)
was raced in 1981 and led to the introduction, in 1982, of
the street 944.  There is 900 lbs difference between the two
cars.  Here is some interesting text:

944GTP: 4-cylinder, in-line, two overhead cams, four
valves per cylinder, liquid cooled, front engine with
turbocharger, 2479 cc, 410 hp.  Weight: 2094 lbs.
Top speed: 185+ mph.

944 Turbo: 4-cylinder, in-line, single overhead cam,
liquid-cooled front engine with turbocharger and
intercooler, 2479 cc, 217 hp, transaxle design.
Weight: 2998 lbs.  Top speed: 152 mph.

Some other specs from the brochure (printed in 1987):

962C: 6-cyl,2999cc, 700 hp, 1929 lbs, top speed: 225+
911 Carrera RSR Turbo: 6-cyl, 2142 cc, 500 hp, 1653 lbs, top speed: 185+
911 Carrera: 6-cyl, 3164 cc, 214 hp, 2756 lbs, top speed: 149
928S: 8-cyl, 4957 cc, 316 hp, 3505 lbs, top speed: 165

Then there is the 1988 Indy car:

Indy car: 8-cylinder, 90-degree V, four overhead cam, four valves per
cylinder, liquid-cooled methanol engine with turbocharger,
2649 cc, 750+ hp, monocoque chassis.  Weight: 1553 lbs,
top speed: 215+
