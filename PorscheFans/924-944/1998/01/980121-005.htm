Check your electrical system. I seem to remeber on the 924 there being a =
relay to kick in the fuel pump on start. If the car does not start, the =
pump should continue to run to build fuel system pressure for the next =
attempt. I believe this relay is also tied into the ignition system =
(it's been awhile) to prevent spark until FI pressure has had a chance =
to build. To test, click the starter (without actually starting the car, =
which shouldn't be a problem) and return the ignition to the ON =
position. You should hear the fuel pump run for 2-3 seconds. Another =
potential problem is simply a bad connection on of these control relays =
which are mounted just to the left of the clutch pedal. Unplug and =
examine for corrosion, brush with a small wirebrush, wiggle and waggle, =
etc. Some of them are identical relays and can be swapped for a quick =
test.=20
