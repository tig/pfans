Christopher, you made some good points.  IMHO, good dancing shoes, usually
make good driving shoes especially when it comes to heel and toe. 
Something made of nice soft leather or whatever, something with relatively
thin soles.  Enough traction to keep the shoe on the pedal, not enough to
keep you from sliding the foot when you want to.  And thick enough to be
better than driving barefoot but not too thick to interfere with feeling
the pedals.  Most sneakers can take the pounding of running and jumping
but stink as dancing shoes.

As for use of heel and toe on the street, I use it lots on the street. 
Whenever I want to shift down really smoothly, especially in slippery
conditions, I have found heel and toe invaluable.
