The knocking is pretty unusual.  I would check that the fuel you're 
buying is OK, perhaps try a different service station.  I would also 
check basic mixture, look for large vacuum leaks, check the lambda 
sensor, and check the timing.

The knock sensor really cannot be tested.  It may be that you can scan 
a fault code out of the DME that identifies a failed sensor.  However, 
there is no reliable, simple test, such as checking its impedence. 
 Generally, if the sensor is bad, the DME should go defensive in its 
timing, retard rather than advance.
