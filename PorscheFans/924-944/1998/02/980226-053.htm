Bob,

I've rebuilt them, and it's not terribly difficult.  Folks on the list 
can give you their favorite techniques for removing the pistons (I use 
compressed air and try to keep my fingers out of the way).  Yes, don't 
split the calipers.

Clean the pistons and piston bores free of deposits/crud.  I use a fine 
nylon scouring pad, the kind sold at hardware stores as a synthetic 000 
steel wool substitute, typically for wood refinishing.

Depending on whether the o-rings ride in the piston vs. the caliper 
bore, look for pits in the opposite surface.  For example, if the 
o-rings install into the caliper bore, and there is pitting in the 
piston, replace the piston.  You don't want pits riding over the 
o-rings.

Cleanliness is the goal.  Flush them out with whatever solvent you 
prefer.  Reassemble with a smear coat of Unisilicon paste 
(000.043.117.00) or brake fluid on the o-ring and sliding surfaces. 
 Replace and marginal bleeder valves, dust caps, cross-over pipes, end 
plates.

At the risk of preaching, don't screw up - it could get you or someone 
else killed.  If you run into a problem, take the caliper to a 
professional.
