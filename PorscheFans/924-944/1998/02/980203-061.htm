> 1.  What is the correct procedure for adjusting the position of the "idle"
> pulley for the balance-shaft belt?  I have heard so many opinions on this my
> head is spinning, I'd like to hear what the factory manual has to say on
> this one.
>
There is a tool that Porsche sells that you put between the idler pulley and
the lower balance shaft pulley (with balance shaft belt on).  Your suppose to
turn the idler pulley clockwise until it is touching the tool, then tighten it
down.

The tool is nothing more than a bent sheet of metal.

If I remember correctly the manual states that a .5mm feeler gauge can be
substituted for the tool.  In all, it is a PITA to make sure the pulleys don't
budge while you tighten them.

> 2.  Is the "automatic" spring tensioner on the '87 designed to properly
> tension a new belt or a run-in belt?  I.E. should I allow the automatic
> tensioner re-tension my now run-in belt?  I don't have the $500 tension tool
> ; (

I believe it is suppose to be set to the run in tension.  I don't remember the
manual specifying it though.  I used mine for my new belt and when I
retensioned it.  The newly designed back belt covers don't allow enough room to
put the belt gauge between the timing belt and back cover without applying
tension to the timing belt.

The faq has a procedure for using the auto tensioner.  Last time I looked it
says to back off 1.5 teeth on the timing belt.  Do not back off when using the
auto-tensioner (only back off when using the tensioning tool)

The bad thing is the auto tensioner is only for the timing belt.
