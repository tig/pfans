You are correct, it is not a metal float, but I thought the concept was
better understood that way. Effectively what we are looking at is float with
locking relay, how it was implemented is through a sensor to a lock and hold
circuit ( now in 74 series logic) with a cap and drain resistor to keep the
lock engaged. 

Now if you look at the wiring diagram you will see what looks like an old
float & lock.

Bottom line, motor has gone onto start cycle (only place it gets checked)
and the oil level is more than ½ the way to low range marks. So the circuit
is set high.

So lets say your are on a long trip and don't stop the motor you can run the
oil level to zip, without the light coming on. (but it will be a long trip)
