I've seen other Porsche Turbo drivers (usually 930s) use the HKS EVC
IV (about $500 retail) as a boost control.  It "learns" the optimum
boost curve and allows you to provide a "low" and "high" setting which
you select at the press of a button.  In addition, it will provide
overboost protection by limiting the amount and duration of boost that
can occur (again, at your discretion) before it returns to the normal
boost level.  There is a "universal fit" unit available which my
roommate has used on his 1994 C2 Turbo.  It also doubles as a boost
gauge.  Keep in mind that you may need to update the wastegate to a
"dual port" design (available from HKS as well).

In addiiton, similar boost controllers and wastegates are available
from GReddy and others (including a boost control unit from Mike
Levitas at Turbo Performace Centers which is a more basic unit without
the learning curve parameters).
