Sheridan,

There is a nifty tool made specially for the removal of the hub nut.  I don't
know the exact metric size of the nut, but it is quite large; 27 mm maybe.
This special tool is made of ~1 inch thick black iron, has a hex hole that
fits the nut, another hole to fit a 1/2" socket wrench, and has a striking 
surface to whack with a hammer to loosen the nut.  The nut is torqued to 
around 200 ft-lbs.  Rather than buy a large spanner, I'd look for one of these 
special tools.  I think I paid about $US10 for mine.
