Plugs:   (Pertains to stock or slightly modified.)  After visiting the T/M website, the plugs appear to be similar (at least in concept) to the plugs used in my turbo-rotary engine (a.k.a. MAZDA).  Next time you're at the auto parts store (non-Porsche), ask to look at NGK plugs for an '87 Mazda 1.3L turbo.  Disclaimer-I don't proclaim to be an ignition system engineer!  However, I've fooled with various ignition systems, accessories & applications over the years (Accel, Mallory, MSD, Jacobs, HEI, dual point, indexing, grooved & split electrode designs, more/less gap, more voltage, etc.)  Generalizations:  I concur with comments suggesting that significant improvement may be impossible if the existing setup performs adequately.  If there are ignition +/or maintenance-related problems, correct them first before upgrading.  Also any 'noticeable' or 'seat-of-the-pants improvement' can originate from knowing that you've spent real money & real time to experiment.  For plugs for instance, it may help to have a trustworthy friend, etc. do the "install" for you - without telling you whether it was done or not!  I've seen things like idle quality change minute to minute without doing a single thing.  Be mindful of your testing methods (accurate gauges, analyzers, drag strip, etc.) & conduct more than just a couple of before-and-after tests/trials.  Also don't base mileage tests on just a few tankfuls!  My cars seem to do very well with the stock systems - I'd feel otherwise if I dealt with sky-high compression ratios or increased boost levels.  I'll be trying some of the cheaper plug alternatives soon & hope to post results (meaningful or otherwise) here.  BTW, I've always liked the MSD stuff although I'm not sure I would install on my 944.
