Here on the east coast, we do see some heavy duty machines. At some
Bridgehampton DE's I've seen Art Pilla's IMSA GT 1 911, Michael
Schromm's monster 911 turbo (he hit about 190 at Pocono), Dick Howe's
924 GTR (yes, that LeMans running GTR, complete with 400+ HP 4 cyl
motor), and Nick Ventura's 'batmobile'. The batmobile started as a
914/6. Now it's a complete tube frame open cockpit car with a 962
motor. Now that I think about it, it's pretty similar to an IMSA WSC or
USRRC Can Am car. Nick did laps at Lime Rock down around 55 seconds.
That was quite a bit faster than the GT PSC cars ran during the
Memorial Day races.

One time at Bridgehampton, I was flagging at the Bridge flag station
when Art was driving the 911. He would come down the front straight at
full throttle, and just fly under the bridge before zooming down the
hill. The car was moving so much air that when he went under the
bridge, I would hear a WHUMP, and the bridge would actually move down
a few inches as the air got sucked out from under it. It took a few laps
before I was confident that he wasn't going to suck the bridge right
down the hill with him!
