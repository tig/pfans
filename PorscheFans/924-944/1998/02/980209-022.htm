My "new" 944S goes wonderfully, I'm really delighted. 

Only one point of concern. When warm (not very warm,  BTW) engine idles
very well at some 900 r.p.m. However at idling speed, oil pressure needle
fluctuates quickly (+- 0.5 bars) around the 4 bar mark. Is this acceptable?
Could be a pressure sensor failure or something worse?

On the 944 Haynes manual.

I have the Haynes manual of most of the cars I've had: Ford Escort &
Fiesta; Volkswagen Beetle, Golf GTI Mk I & II, Talbot Samba (a very strange
hybrid), Peugeot 405 (a car qualified as a "dog" in the US), etc... Quality
of manuals used to be rather good. Specifically the Haynes manual for the
Peugeot 405 is a fantastic, useful  book (well explained ,comprehensive,
very clear pictures,...).

When I bought the 944S I did order the Haynes manual and the results have
been quite deceptive. The copy I've got is (I beg you pardon) a piece of
s**t.

I think it is crazy to print such a manual in recycled paper. Pictures are
indistinguishable, content unclear and rather incomplete. A similar  level
I've found at the  Porsche 911(another disaster). Why Haynes  has edited
manuals for the Porsches that bad?. Porsches are not run-from-the-mill
machinery, but I'm sure that a well published Porsche manual will sell much
better than, for instance, the Talbot Samba manual (a very useful one,
BTW).

For purchasing in Europe the Haynes manuals and other motoring books I
strongly recommend : Mill House Books in England.
