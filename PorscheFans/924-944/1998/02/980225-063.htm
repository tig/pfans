> Hey there P-Fans
> 	I just wanted to remind people that the 944 Turbo has a
> tendancy to "walk"
> the stub axle nut right off the splines. Especially cars that have been
> "chiped", the extra toque causes the nut to back off. In part due to the
fact
> that Porsche did not install a cotter pin and drilled hole in the stub
axle!
> Grab a 32mm socket, and torque that stub axle nut to 369 ft/lbs (yes - 500
> Nm!) or more. You guys with NA 944's do not Attempt this high torque, it
will
> cause your two piece "VW beetle-style" wheel bearings to "sieze" under
that
> holding pressure. The 951 uses a beefed up pressed-in one-peice wheel
bearing
> unit.
