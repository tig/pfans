Yes, you can leave the tie rods in place, but remove the four bolts 
holding the rack to the cross member. You probably also want to remove 
the ground wire connecting the steering column to the cross member.  At 
your car's age, I would replace rather than re-use the four bolts.

For coolant, it's got to be phosphate free.  Formerly, this meant going 
for a VW/Audi, Mercedes, or Saab (or perhaps Volvo, I forget) OE 
product.  Now there are phosphate free generic brands.  I always mix 
with distilled water to minimize problems with mineral deposits.  Buy a 
five or ten gallon container at your local boating supply place to 
store your own 50/50 mix, mix it up, and you're all set.

I've used Mobil 1 synth 75W90 for a bunch of years, works great.  The 
Castrol product is fine, but I don't use it much.  Never heard of 
Caltex.
