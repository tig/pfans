>>>>>
>In Reply to: Porsche 911 retrofit posted by Rob Garrett on December 15,
1997
>at 17:27:56:
>I honestly suggest you keep it R-12. Porsches are not known for outstanding
>A/C performance. In any case, you will need a total system flush, a new
>filter, added condensation capacity (a key issue, specailly on a Porsche),
>hose replacement (depending on the car's age), new ports, and tags. Because
>of the hose routing on some Porsches, I suggest you use ester oil if you
>retrofit to R-134a. Ester oil is compatible with both major refrigerants.
>The condenser capacity in your case may be increased by means of a 7-inch
>fan. If you can obtain an R-134a condenser from a 1993 and newer Porsche
>like yours, it would be a big plus.
>>>>>
>Also, consider the following:
>>>>>
>In Reply to: Re: Retrofitting A Fool and His Money posted by John Mitchell
>on August 07, 1997 at 10:20:51:
>Besides that, we're talking refrigeration, not points. Retrofits ARE
>possible, but should be avoided until absolutely essential. When retrofits
>are done, the WHOLE system should be considered- including oil, hoses,
>condenser and evaporator core materials, condensation/boiling point curves,
>pressure/temperature regulation, etc. The "Kits" I see in parts stores for
>converting to R134a are a SCAM and will give all conversions a black eye.
>>>>>
>Certainly, anyone contemplating using a kit should consider the
alternatives
>carefully.  The EPA says to check with your Porsche dealer before
>considering an aftermarket kit for Porsche.
