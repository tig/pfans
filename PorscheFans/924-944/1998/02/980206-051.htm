MT-90 is pretty similar to 75W90NS except it does not contain the
additives for GL-5.  Like the NS, it is "non-slippery" formula which
enhances syncro action.  I run regular MTL in my Honda 5-speed and it
works great, hot or cold.  Honda just uses normal 10W30 or 10W40 motor
oil, which are equivalent viscosity ranges to MTL and MT-90.  For a 944 I
would hesitate to use MTL or MT-90 because they don't contain the
additives to protect hypoid gears as well as the 75W90 gear oil.

>Talked to Redline representative about the lubricates.  He informed me=20
>that for a 944 only using 75W90 might be too slippery. He recommended=20
>mixing the 75W90 and 75W90 NS. He said the mixture ratio is different=20
>for every car (i.e., how hard the car has been driven).=20

What you can do is use all 75W90NS and then add enough Red Line friction
modifier (just a few ounces) until LSD clutch chatter just disappears.  I
use straight NS formula in my S2 non-LSD trans and it shifts very nicely.
