Regarding scanning. My son in Baltimore is an imaging, etc., consultant and
knows many installations where 120 page per minute paper page scanners are
in use in or near DC, some of them do both sides of the page at once (front
and back). At that rate a 240 page paper manual is only 2 minutes to scan at
readable resolution where handwriting must be viewable and readable. I
believe it is not unusual to scan them to a CD for shipment to a remote
location for cheap keying in of data to go with the images. So it must be
that some service bureaus have this equipment, too. For hire. (They
sometimes ship stuff offshore for keying). The copyright is still a problem.
But I don't think the copyright would prevent the owner of the fiche from
printing a manual for his own use, at least not the one he owns, since he
has already paid for the material. I'll ask my son if he knows of any that
can also copy fiche to CD, and give you an update. Maybe the cost would be
lower if many fiche owners all asked for the same CD, since only one scan
would be made and many CD's. If this works, we can seek permission from the
fiche sales outlet or Porsche, and I think we would have to be given it. How
could they say no? We might place a lot of orders for fiche if they say yes,
although the more expensive paper manual sales might decline.
