If you have large feet or long legs try using the left side of your foot
on the brake and the right side on the accelerator.

If you really want to know what heel and toe is all about go to one of
the driving schools that use the FFs that have gears that need to be
double clutched and you must go through each gear. That is heel and toe!
The modern day rev matching is nothing by comparison.

I'll also let you in on a secret: I sometimes rev match and let out the
clutch after braking and before the apex of the turn so I don't have to
'heel and toe'. My problem is that my legs do not have enough clearance
to do much fancy stuff down there such as left foot brake. Short people
have a distinct advantage.
