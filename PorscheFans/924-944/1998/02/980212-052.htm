Alex Chiu wrote:
> 
> I am planning to corner balance my car which is a 93 968 M030.  The
> front perches are currently about 3/4" from the lowest limit and the car
> sits with a nose down attitude.  I think the PO thought it would be cool
> to lower the front but did not lower the rear to match.  From speaking
> to other autocrossers, the consensus is to set the rear of the car about
> level or half an inch lower than the front.  
> 
> How much adjustability is there on the rear without re-indexing the
> torsion bar?
> 
If it hasn't already been lowered, you should be able to get the rear about
3/4" lower using the eccentric adjusters.
>
> If I have to re-index, how much time does that take and does anyone have
> instructions on how to do it?
> 
Working alone without a lift will take you about 8-12 hours. It sounds like
the front may be too low already so you are probably best lowering the rear
as far as the adjusters will allow, then raising the front as required to
reduce the rake to the desire level. I say this because the front arms / ball
joints are not going to be safe with the front dropped really low.
