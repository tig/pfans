Thanks for the interesting description of the "day on the dyno" you
enjoyed.  I enjoyed a similar day in October of last year which
brought some intresting vehicles to my mechanic's (and now roommate!!)
garage.  I don't remember all of the specifics but here goes (all
numbers are rear wheel hp):

1.  1994 911 Turbo:  337hp with a 3.3 motor with .9 bar boost, K-29
turbo and Borla exhaust  The car (my roommate's) now has a 3.6 with
1.2 bar boost, Crankfire ignition, twin-plug heads and a K-36 turbo.

2.  1994 Surpa Twin Turbo:  in the neighborhood of 420 hp with a
variety of HKS products and 20+ pounds of boost.

3.  1988 951 (my car):  240hp with a K-27T6, APE chips, gutted cat and
1.1 bar boost (tired motor - 105,000+ miles).  Car now has a K-26S
turbo, Borla XR-1 muffler and Mass Air setup.  The driveability is
much improved but probably not making a whole lot more hp due to the
smaller turbo.

Looking forward to another dyno session in the spring!!

Quick question:  What conversion factor were you told to use to
calculate flywheel hp and/or torque?  They told me to divide my rear
wheel #s by .8 or multiply by 1.25 (e.g. 20% loss).
