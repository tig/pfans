My engine undercover was discarded by the PO, and I haven't missed it.  
It makes maintenance and oil changes easier and less messy.  I suppose it 
a) might decrease wind resistance under the car, and b) might deflect 
rain away from the belts.  I do get PS belt slippage for a few moments if 
I hit a deep puddle, but thats harmless.  A friend with the under pan 
present gets belt slippage from puddles with his pan installed, so who knows?

> Also, I saw in the Auto Atlanta black thin catalog on pg 100 that the
> alternator carries a cooling duct/tube. Again, my engine does not have
> one attached to the alternator. How detrimental is this bugger to the
> alternator's efficiency and lifespan? So far, I've been lucky not to
> need to replace it......so as long as I don't fool around w/ the
> electrical system and fry the regulator!!! :-)

My 1989 has a cooling duct that runs from the alternator to the inside of 
the bumper, to pick up cool air there.  An alternator will last longer if 
not 'cooled' by the already hot air in the engine compartment.  In the 
summertime, with the AC on and the alternator running a high load, this 
is especially important.  It is a known fact that an alternator's 
amperage production decreases slightly with heat, as well.

If the tin shround behind the alternator is missing, replace that, along 
with the duct.
