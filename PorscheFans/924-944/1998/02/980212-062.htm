No, it's a fairly small circlip under the epoxy, but it has 
reverse-chamfered ends that make removal darned difficult.  It retains 
a plate, and behind the plate there is a shallow spring between it and 
the ball.

More importantly, if whoever you're thinking of having reman your arms 
does not replace the ball-and-stem, think again.  The stem fatigues and 
breaks, normally at the groove for the locking cross bolt.  If all 
they're doing is replacing the nylon cup, they're not doing you any 
favors.

(FWIW, I personally would not put a remanufactured control arm in my 
own car.)
