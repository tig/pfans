I haven't found that you need too many special tools. Just a good selection
of metric sockets, wrenches, and allen tools. Depending on the job, you
might need some more. For example, if you have to replace the clutch,
you'll need flex head sockets, flex head ratchets, and wobble extentions
(and a lot of patience). For working on the front part of the motor
(seals, belts, etc.) you'll need the flywheel lock, and the sprocket
holder (Automotion sells both). The only other special tool I can think
of at the moment are the 'triple square' or 12 point allen tools that
are used to remove the CV joint bolts, flywheel bolts, and the bolt
that holds the cam pullet on. Snap On and Lisle sell those.
