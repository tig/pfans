My procedure is an adaptation of the hole in the filter trick.  It requires
an air compressor or at least a small quantity of compressed air.  After
punching the hole, I press the blow gun nozzle (rubber tip only unless you
want a "crude" shower) into the hole and force out the oil.  Be sure to
turn down the pressure to less than the normal maximum oil pressure.  After
a short wait, usually the anount of time to drain the oil, remove the
filter with minimal mess.  BTW, have the oil cap off or you might launch
the dipstick....
