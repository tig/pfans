Hi All,
   If you really want a tire engineer to tell you useful information, 
start asking what HOT pressures you should be looking for.  If 
you ask for cold starting pressures, they immediately know you 
don't know what you are talking about and will feed you what 
could be called a "defensive line" intended to get you off their 
backs without doing any harm.
   Cold pressures are irrelevant for track cars.  Conditions change 
too fast.  Day to day, driver on or off, sunny to cloudy, 
etc.,etc.,etc.  If you only pay attention to your cold pressures, you 
will never know why you car works one day and is all over the track 
the next.
