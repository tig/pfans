Hi Wes,

It has to do with vectors. Physics 201 don't fail me now... 

If your harness is attaced at exactly the same angle as your shoulders,
the force vector is a straight line going straight forward (all of this
assumes a sudden decelleration). Let's say that the force is 10 Gs as an
example. If the harness loops around a bar and attaches at a lower
point, the magnitude of the force vector is the hypoteneuse of the
triangle which is sqrt(a**2 + b**2). Assuming a 45 degree triangle, then
a and b are each 10 Gs. So in this case the magnitude is sqrt(10**2 +
10**2) = 14 Gs.
