Roger: and I thought I had the lowest mileage '84 944 running on its
original clutch! Here are a couple of additions to your accurate
summary:

> After removing the radio blanking plate , you will see a black plastic wire
> loom with three wires emerging.
>
> Red/Blue-----Switched 12V from the radio relay to power the radio.
> Red/Yellow---From the instrument lights and the dimming potentiometer
> Brown--------Chassis ground
> And for antenna power, a white wire all by itself.

My '84 944 came with a Reno and the Blau. BPA-430 (4x30watts digital
amp) factory installed. Besides the leads you mention above, a pink
unswitched +12v lead comes out of the wiring loom. There is also a
16-pin connector from the ARI adaptor that came standard with the Reno.

> installed an extra rocker switch in the console to interrupt the antenna
power
> lead so that when using the tape player (it would be a CD player now, of
> course) I could keep the antenna retracted.

Both the Reno and the Houston (and the Monterey) have a power antenna
output (switched +12v) that switches off the antenna and retracts it
when the radio is not in use, i.e. in tape mode. If you use this instead
of the car's switched +12v to operate the antenna, you will only use the
power antenna when the radio is working.
