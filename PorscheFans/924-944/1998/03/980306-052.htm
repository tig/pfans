Craig,

In reality, with a water pump on a car, if you had a coolant expansion tank
that was higher that the one that was in the car, it could eliminate the
cavitation. It would at least increase the RPM at which the water pump
cavitated. However, actually placing the expansion tank at a higher point
in the car isn't very practical.

The same would be true with the oil level. I don't think anyone can say
conclusively whether the oil pumps on 944s cavitates or whether the pump
suction is becoming uncovered. It is fact that increasing the oil level
would increase the net positive suction head to the pump. However, the oil
pump on the 944 is a positive displacement gear pump. Porsche actually
calls it a sickle pump. The water pump on your car is a centrifugal pump.
Centrifugal pumps are much more prone to cavitation than positive
displacement pumps. I've worked with positive displacement and centrifugal
pumps for about 18 years and I've never seen a positive displacement pump
cavitate. I must admit that I have never dealt with positive displacement
pumps that rotate at 6000 + RPM. I have on the other had seen many
centrifugal pump impellers completely destroyed by cavitation. Therefore, I
seriously doubt if the oil pumps on the 944s cavitate. I think it much more
likely that the suction is becoming uncovered and air is getting entrained
with the oil. Although I've had plenty of engineering courses, I'm not a
mechanical engineer. So, I'm waiting for our mechanical engineers out there
(Is Dr. Steve in the house?) to blow my theory out of the water.
