Jeff,
     I believe you can remove the oil pump seal with one of those oil seal 
pullers available at your local auto parts store. When I replaced ours, I 
built an an engine hoist out of wood that rested on the strut towers, and used 
turnbuckles and steel cables to lift the engine. This allowed me to remove the 
crossmember and oil pan. However, if your oil pan seal doesn't need 
replacement, don't wast your time doing it this way. If you do want details on 
building the engine hoist (cost is ~$15), let me know. Also, if you do decide 
to remove the oil pump, you will need some of that magic anaerobic loctite 
574. When doing ours, I spent a lot of time cleaning the old loctite 574 off 
the pump and block. Since then I have read that old loctite 574 doesn't need 
to be removed as it is dissolved by application of new loctite 574. Also, all 
those oil pan bolts are a real pain, so if you can avoid removal of the oil 
pump....

Good Luck!
