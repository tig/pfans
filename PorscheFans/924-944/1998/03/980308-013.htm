> This is what my engine builder and the burner builder told me.  The stock
> 944 turbo wastegate is a single chamber.  When the boost pressure gets
> close to blow off or there is a pressure wave spike then the wastegate
> valve lifts off the seat and starts chattering.  You can shim the wastegate
> to make the spring have more tension to stop the chattering.  The
> chattering causes the boost to bled off sooner than you want and also wears
> the valve seat over time.  The valve seat wear causes the the wastegate to
> leak which give you less boost.  If you shim the wastegate too much like we
> did, you can overboost and break a turbo shaft $.  The stock single chamber
> wastegate will not allow the aftermarket controllers to work correctly as
> they are designed for dual chamber units.  The controller does not allow
> the dual chamber units to blow off until the indicated amount of boost is
> reached.  If I remember the dual unit valve is held shut by pressure rather
> than a spring.  The wastegate instant release keeps the turbo from
> overspooling and overboosting the engine.
>
