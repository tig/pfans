>The movement of the sensor plate is the first thing to check.  A
>backfire in the manifold drives the sensor plate against its spring
>support so that the pressure is vented abround the edge of the
plate
>and the sensor plate level can be bent or misaligned.  The sensor
>plate is driven past the zero position.  Check the movement,
>centering, and height.  It sounds as though this mechanism is
>sticking.
>
>OR for some reason the fuel pressure counterforce on the top of the
>plunger could be too great.  Either the damping restrictor or the
>flow restrictor in the fuel distributor is clogged causing too
great
>a control pressure.  More rarely, there could be a restriction in
>the return fuel line.  On these possibilities, you will have to do
>the fuel pressure tests.
