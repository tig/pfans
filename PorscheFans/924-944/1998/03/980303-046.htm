The first thing you need to do is find somebody else to work on your car.
Not changing the brake fluid will cost you more money in the long run than
changing it now and accepting any consequences (though I think they would
be minimal anyway). Brake fluid is hygroscopic which means it readily
absorbs moisture (even out of the air). The moisture it absorbs will cause
your brake system components to rust internally which can cause major
problems. Also, when brake fluid has a high moisture content, the water
will flash to steam when the brakes get extremely hot (i.e. during an
autocross) and you will lose your brakes. Most Driver's Eds and autocrosses
I've been to require proof that your brake fluid has been changed within
the previous 2-3 months. Hope this helps.
