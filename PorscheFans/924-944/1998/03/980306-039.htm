Quoting from the FAQ:

******************************************************************************
9.1 When I release my clutch in neutral, I hear noises, is this bad?

Not usually. The transmission has several shafts that the gears move back and 
forth on. On the end of one of these shafts is the 5th gear idler. This gear 
rattles back and forth
because it has a larger tolerance than the others. Porsche apparently stopped 
all warranty repairs for this noise because there is no real repair - the 
larger tolerance is normal.
The noise is particularly evident because it vibrates the input shaft which is 
connected to the torque tube that runs up the middle of your car. This creates 
a megaphone type
effect that amplifies the noise.

If you're unsure if your particularly "noise" is normal or not, taking it to a 
mechanic who has looked at a few 944's should clear the situation up for you. 
Some owners have
expressed that using Swepco transmission oil quiets down the rattle somewhat.
