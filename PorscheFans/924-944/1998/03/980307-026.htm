Early 944s ('83, '84, '85) came with a 1-wire Oxygen sensor in the US.
The exceptions were CA registered new 944s, which came with the 3-wire
sensor. '85.5 and up all 944s were shipped with 3-wire sensors.

Functionally the only difference between the two sensors is a small
heating element in the 3-wire sensor that brings it up to operating
temps a few minutes faster than the 1-wire sensor. If you buy a 1-wire
sensor for your CA 944, you can simply ignore the connector for the
heating element (male double connector at firewall, in front of
fresh-air fan) and just connect the black sensor wire. When you go in
for your next emissions test remember to warm up to operating temps
first.

Conversely if you want to use a 3-wire sensor in an early non-CA 944,
find an ignition switched 12v source for the two heater element wires,
and connect the black sensor wire to the existing O2 sensor connector
(cylindrical yellow connector at firewall, in front of fresh-air fan).
