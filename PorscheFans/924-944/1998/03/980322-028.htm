They are a bear to remove.  You can buy a special tool that makes the 
job easier, basically a set of plier with a split cylindrical jaw.

Or, you can use a pair of regular pliers, the kind that have a jaw that 
is half flat, half round.  Put a towel over the spark plug connector, 
hook the pliers around the connector end where the jaw changes shape, 
and yank.

Don't pry on the connector leg, say, with a large screwdriver.  You'll 
snap the leg off.
