Clark,

When my rack went bad, it was the seals. If you haven't alread, peel back
the protective boots at both ends and see if fluid leaks out. I'm sure you
can guess the cause.

There has been a number of posts over the past few months on the pros and
cons of rebuilt racks and a few folks who were able to successfully rebuild
their own by just buying seals. If you haven't noticed those, check the
archives and I'm sure you'll find alot of helpful info. I had a used rack
installed from Zims and it has worked fine for over a year now. It has also
been strongly recommended that if the rack is replaced you also
clean/replace the reservoir as it is likely the filter at the bottom of it
is clogged up. I replaced mine when I also replaced those expensive power
steering hoses which started leaking big time about 3 months after replacing
the rack.

It's likely others will have a more technical response, but FWIW...
