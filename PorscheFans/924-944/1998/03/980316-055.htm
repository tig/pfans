     Hi Bob, i did my seal a few months ago and it was not fun!  Primary 
     problem is the fact that it was a messy job and the oil fitting that 
     is attached to the hose that goes to the external oil cooler would not 
     budge!  Even after the change i got a little brown stuff in my coolent 
     tank.  I'm not sure where it is coming from but i have no water in my 
     oil.  Below are some hints:
     
     1.  Removing the heatshield around the exhaust is easy but putting
         back the screw in the middle of the two part heatshield is not.
         I got small hands and i think i'm pretty good at this sort of
         thing but i gave up!  I'll put the one screw back when i mess with
         the exhaust header later.
     
     2.  I was able to do the job without removing the oil fitting.  I
         just moved the power steering pump out of the way and rotated the 
         assembly down.  
     
     3.  Make sure you replace the two o-rings inside the housing cover
         with the updated 'green' o-rings.  The old ones were red.
     
     4.  Make sure you use the factory recommended Loctite for the new
         seal.  I can't remember the number but i can get the number if you
         need it.  I got it from the dealer for a couple of bucks.
     
     5.  When you are ready to remove the bolts around the housing
         cover, put a large drain pan under the whole area and remove 
         the bolts slowly.  It will start dripping oil and water.  Let
         it drain slowly and then remove the bolts.  Good luck!
     
     I did this job because i had the oil leak around the seal.  It fixed 
     the problem.
     
     Ok, now on my continuing saga of the front wheel shimmy.  I replaced 
     the strut bearings with the updated sealed bearing units and the rear 
     A-arm rubber bushings and no change!  The only thing left is the a-arm 
     ball joints/bushings and steering tie rods inner and outer. What do 
     you think?
     
     By the way, the updated strut bearings vastly improved my ride and 
     decreased the steering effort.
