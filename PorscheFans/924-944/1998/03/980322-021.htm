I didn't see the start of this thread.  If the problem is belt noise in 
a 944S2, be aware that Porsche reported a problem with "belt slap" in 
the 944S2 when the factory tensioning tool was not used.  Perhaps this 
is your problem.

You may also, conceivably, have a bad roller or idler.  You can usually 
pinpoint the culprit by holding a long screwdriver blade on the center 
of the roller (i.e., the bolt holding it on) while the engine is 
running.  With your finger on the blade, you will feel a lot more 
vibration from the bad part than from the others.
