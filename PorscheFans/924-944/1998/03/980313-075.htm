Derek et al.,

>Finally, I've read about the usage of the "993 Compressor Bypass
>Valve" for 951s.  What does this do?  Part #?  Price?

When you close the throttle this valve opens up and allows air to bypass
around the compressor side of the turbo.  This helps to keep the turbo =
rpm
up during shifts since the compressor isn't trying to compress air =
against
a closed throttle plate.   Thus turbo lag is reduced once you get back on
the throttle.  The 993 part has a metal reinforced internal diaphragm as
opposed to the 951 plastic one that breaks.

I ordered one of these valves for my son's car, but today I found out =
they
are still on back order.  Sorry, but I have the part number at work.  The
list is about $35-38.
