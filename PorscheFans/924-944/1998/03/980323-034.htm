Kevin B. and others wrote to the effect that:

>--any track driver who hasn't practiced maintaining or regaining control in an
>emergency should leave a safety margin (eg, a few feet of road at the track-
>out point) and seek out opportunities to repetitively practice car control
>techniques.  Warning -- it's not sufficient to understand what you SHOULD do
>in these situations (remember what happens to your conscious or "thinking"
>mind); you need to know for sure how you WILL respond, and that you can only
>know through experience.  Get that experience at slower speeds and in safer
>environs before you end up in a little metal ball against the guard rail. 

One way to develop reflexes for that inevitable emergency situation is to
do some off-road driving, preferably not in your Porsche.  I enjoy driving
my Pathfinder on dirt and/or loose-gravel roads, and sometimes purposely
take curves a little too fast to induce a slide that can be caught with the
right throttle and steering inputs.  This sort of practice can be done at
low speeds in areas with safe runoff.  It gives you a quick education on
what throttle, brake and steering inputs do to a vehicle in a low-traction
situation, which is not too different from coming in too hot on the wrong
line on a smooth track with a well sorted out 944.
