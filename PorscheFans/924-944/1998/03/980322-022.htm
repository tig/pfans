You're right in saying that these Scotchbright pads will remove metal. 
 I use the 000 grade all the time, for all kinds of cleaning 
applications, esp. metal-to-metal mating surfaces that will be sealed, 
for example, with a case sealant.  While it will remove a little metal, 
it won't remove so much that it makes any kind of difference, so long 
as you don't go crazy with it.

Even with the cylinder walls, if you use it lightly, just enough to 
skim off anything that doesn't belong there, you'll be fine, in my 
opinion.  Wash down with carb cleaner, e.g., afterwards to inspect the 
results.

BTW, this is the sort of reason why I was taught to do this type of 
cleaning by hand and not with a power tool.  Even with a very fine pad, 
a power tool can go through a lot of metal too quickly.
