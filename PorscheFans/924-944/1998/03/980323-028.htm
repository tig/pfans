Yes it is.

> Is there a way to check the oil sending unit to see if it is bad before I
> replace it? (I think they are about $55.)  What is the procedure.

Screw off the wires. Then check the gauge and the light. Now connect the
wires to any grounding point (anywhere on a clear metal place at the motor
or chassis wil do). You should see opposite readings. When your gauge does
go to zero (when connecting ground to the wires), the sender is gone. If you
want some more testing, connect a potentiometer (330 Ohms) between the gauge
wire and ground. Turn the pot.meter and your gauge will react.My guess,
without checking, that your sender is gone. Replacing without draining the
oil is possible but also a little messy.

Good luck.
