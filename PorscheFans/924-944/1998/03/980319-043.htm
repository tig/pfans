I concur with Steve as everything I've read in various publications
indicate that 17psi is the max (safely).  I posted a formula for
calculating maximum hp from a given injector size (and vice-versa) a
couple of days ago.  Here is it again with a minor grammatical
correction, sorry:

Formula as taken from pp. 67 and 69 of the April 1998 issue of
European Car.  Thought you might find it of interest...

          Flow Rate = (HP * BSFC) / (#Injectors * Maxcycle)

Flow Rate is in pounds per hour of gasoline
HP is projected hp of the ENGINE
BSFC is Brake Specific Fuel Consumption in pounds per horsepower-hour,
generally .4 to .6 (possible higher) at full throttle.  Start with .45
for NA engines, .55 for 951s.
#Injectors, 'nuff said.
Maxcycle is the maximum duty cycle of the injector, figure .8 (80%)
for a typical OEM E.C.U.  Above this, the injectors might begin to
overheat, which can cause it to lose consistency - or even fail
completely!!

To convert pounds per hour to cc/min:
  1.  Divide by 60, giving pounds per minute.
  2.  Divide this by 6, giving gallons per minute.
  3.  Multiply this by 3.785, giving liters per minute.
  4.  Multiply by 1000, giving cc's per minute.

Now let's say you're looking to modify your car and need to know what
the maximum hp you can generate given the injector size, simply revise
the equation as follows:

  HP = (Flow Rate * #Injectors * Maxcycle) / BSFC

Rob, given that the 370cc injectors are essentially equivalent to 36
pound injectors based on the above equation, the above equation will
give us the following maximum hp potential of a 951 as follows:

80% maxcycle/.55 BSFC = 209hp
90% maxcycle/.55 BSFC = 236hp
100% maxcycle/.55 BSFC = 262hp

Obviously, there are a lot of us that KNOW we have more than 262 hp
and with David Reins pushing 460hp with stock injectors, I admit that
this equation is suspect, or maybe we're using the wrong BSFC factor. 
Again, this is for discussion purposes only.
