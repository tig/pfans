The proper way to do it is to remove the exhaust manifolds, remove
the head (if necessary) and replace the stud.

The drill and tap method is risky, and may end up costing you a head, or
at least a helicoil job for the head (after you remove the manifolds and the
head (necessary at this point)...

Why is it that no one has time to do it right the first time, but always
has time to do it over???
