unsantified hands in and tear out that windshild washer motor and tear it
down and fix it myself. If you are interested, here is what I found. Once
you get the motor from the car you will notice that the leads are marked +
and -. Hook a battary up to these leads, if you can hear (and feel) the
motor turning, you have a chance to fix it. If the motor does not spin, make
the call, it's dead. But if it does spin, unhook it and remove the 4 small
bolts, this will seperate the 3 main components. Look up inside the motor
you should see the U shaped device, this fits around the white wheel you see
on the mating part. Turn the wheel, is it free?? Seperate the white housing
from the metal plate and you will see the two gears. Lightly sand the brass
plate shinny, lightly sand the brass gear, including the cogs. WD-40 them
and put them back togather. I can't believe Porsche would spend so much
money making windshield washers like this. These things are made better than
