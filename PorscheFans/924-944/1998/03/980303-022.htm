European Car Magazine published an article a few years ago which described
the process that Porsche uses to paint it's calipers. Apparently Glasurit
makes a high heat additive for it's automotive paint, so Porsche mixes this
additive with regular epoxy enamel. To be honest, I am not positive that it
was epoxy enamel that they mentioned, but I assume that it must be (or
polyurethane) to withstand brake fluid.
