I would not be so quick to dismiss the idle stabilizer as the source of
the problem - I suspect this problem is not as rare as you suggest.  I
fully agree that Micheal should try to track down and dismiss all
possibilities of a vaccum leak first, but not dismiss the idle
stabilizer, especially if Micheal's 944 has a lot of miles on it.  My
64K mile 944 has had no problem yet.  A Saab I once owned had an idle
stabilizer go at 150K miles or so (BOSCH idle stabilizer is rather
similar in a SAAB as in a Porsche).  I was able to disassemble the idle
stabilizer, clean the electric contacts within and continue on for
another 25K miles before I sold the SAAB.  I expect I may well run into
the same situation with my 944 when I reach this kind of mileage,
although I admit it will be much more of a chore to remove the idle
stablizer from my 944, due to the "convenient" location Porsche chose,
beneath the intake manifold.

I hope my input of is some use.
