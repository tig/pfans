At 07:57 PM 3/30/98 -0500, you wrote:
>I've seen PCA regions implement passing protocol both ways: passed car stays
>on-line or moves off-line.  I have a preference (esp. for beginning DE
>students), and I've done it both ways, but that's not a major issue to me.

My personal preference is to let let the passee decide whether to keep the
line or no, based on track geography. In general I don't like passee
students to have to move off-line because of the possibility of marbles,
possibility of task overload, etc.

>The safety issue with the "Backwards Blinker" usage as I'll call it, is that
>it requires drivers of 10, 20, 30 years - however long - to spontaneously
>learn to use the signal opposite to how they've been using it for their
>driving life.  Introducing such divergence from standard leads to confusion,
>misuse, and potentially dangerous situations where two drivers do not
>communicate quickly and clearly.  This situation does not have to exist and
>can be eliminated with a different approach.

This is true. The way I get around it personally is to visualize myself in
the passing car and signal accordingly. However, I also have no problems
"following" the signal (i.e., doing the "wrong" thing) when I'm the one
doing the passing. My own fave is the hand motions, but I suspect that is
because that's what I learned first .

I don't think we'll ever have consensus on this and a lot of issues related
to driver ed. There certainly seems to be a large element of NIH present.
(It can't be right/good because it was not-invented-here) The Internet has
"made the world smaller" - before we used to discuss differences within
Zone (everyone in Zone1 remember the 944 control arm issue?), now it seems
there are even larger differences coast-to-coast. So be it. Let's hope we
can learn from each other and make things safer for everyone.

Cheers,
Craig.
