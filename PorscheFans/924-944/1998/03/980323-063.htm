Frenna is correct.  Scotchbrite will most certainly remove metal.  I use it
expressly for that purpose.  I would be very careful using it on cylinder
walls since the bore/hone process on the high silicon content aluminum
finishes with an etch which is intended to leave a silicon-rich layer at
the surface of the cylinder wall.  I don't believe Scotchbrite will do the
same.  Also, if you are changing rings with the engine still installed (it
is possible as long as the head is off), I would be very cautious about
having any gritty substance washing down onto the crankshaft bearing
surfaces.
