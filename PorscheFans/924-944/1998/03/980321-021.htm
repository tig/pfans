>>> I bought a 83, 944 that had been stored for 4 years, (owner thought it
>>>would go up in price ha ha) When it was started it ran for only a couple of
>>>seconds and boom the timing belt and the balancing belt both broke. I bought
>>>the car with only 42,000 miles on it with a motor lock up. I had to replace
>>>5 valves ( 2 were laying broken on the piston) and had to have the head
>>>welded up and 2 valve seats replaced. This may sound hard, but it's a piece
>>>of cake for a head shop. The cost of the valves were $36 ea for the exh and
>>>$18 for the int. The machine work was $125.00. The total job (me doing the
>>>work) was $450.00. I replace both belts and tighten them with the shade tree
>>>1/2 turn, works so far. While I had the belts off, I took off each idoler
>>>and the cam gear adjuster, here is what I found. The plastic idoler would
>>>not turn and had not for sometime. I soaked it in stanisal over night and
>>>soaked it in lube for 2 days. I works fine now, no need to throw things away
>>>most can be fixed. The cam adjusted when tighten down hit the plastic behind
>>>the gear. I put a thin washer behind the gear and it works fine. Ran the car
>>>easy for 50 miles and retighened the belts (shade tree way) and I have a
