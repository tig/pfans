> Since the harness is in tension there will be

> order to get your 45 degree angle for the resultant force, your scenario

This is exactly how they are typically used and why they are so unsafe
in 944s. The angle my Weltmeister bar makes with the rear seat belt
mounting points is actually greater than 90 degrees! And this is the
typical mounting scenario for both the Weltmeister and the Brey-Krause
bars. The other alternative is to run the harnesses back into the rear
compartment. Unless you get fancy with metal cables such as Henry Hoeh
has, this will result in the harness being too long and it will stretch
too much to do any good.

> The _key point_ of all this however, is that the load in the harness bar
> is NOT straight forward, like it would be if the harness were attached
> directly to the bar and your shoulders were at bar height.  Harness bars
> are not normally designed to take  a large load in the forward direction,
> so if you attach your harness directly to a bar that is designed to serve
> as a _guide only_, you run the real risk of bar failure in an accident.

This is my real concern. They are simply not safe for anything but
autocrossing. And both the Weltmeister and the Brey-Krause harness bars
are illegal for SCCA Solo II because they do not telescope.
