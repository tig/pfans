Another point of interest is that the later 968's used a Torsen style 
limited slip.  The early ones used a Salisbury style, as used in 915 
and 944 series transmissions.  The Salisbury uses clutch packs and 
ramps to sense torque, the Torsen uses the tendency of worm-style gears 
to want to turn in only one direction to generate lock-up.

I'd be interested in hearing anyone's experiences and impressions of 
the relative performance of the two designs.

(BTW, the Quaiffe isn't a Torsen; it's yet another design...)
