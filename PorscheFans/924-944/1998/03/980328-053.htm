Chris, once you get the CV's out and wipe off as much grease as possible,
you can disassemble them by carefully rotating the inner race and sliding
it out with the ball carrying cage.  I think Haynes 944 book had a good
procedure for this.  I believe it is best to try and keep track of which
ball goes in which groove on the inner and outer races for re-assembly. 
Once you get the inner and outer races clean, look for galled areas (rough,
porous looking patches) in the ball grooves.  It should be pretty obvious
on a bad joint.
