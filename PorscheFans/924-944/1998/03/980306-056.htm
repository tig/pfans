     The fix below may or may not fix your noise problem.  I have done the 
     belt job many times on my 951 and i found that you can properly setup 
     using the gauge (i have one) and the Porsche roller setting tool, and 
     still have the noise.  Some times the belt would be silent and some 
     times it would sound like a jet engine.  
     
     The only sure fire method is to setup the belts and rollers per 
     factory specs, install the intercooler plumbing and start the car 
     without the belt covers.  You can watch the belt as you rev the motor 
     to see if you get any standing waves on the belt.  If you do, readjust 
     the roller and try agian.  There is more than one way to have the 
     correct reading on the gauge.  Make sure you cover the intake of the 
     airflow sensor with a piece of nylon stocking or equivalent to prevent 
     sucking in junk.  BTW this is a great way to observe the action of 
     your airflow sensor plate as you rev the motor.  The S2 may be easier 
     beacuse there is no intercooler plumbing.
     
     Also, if you have used the gauge before, you know that using it takes 
     some skill.  It is not that simple to get a consistent reading.  You 
     must get multiple readings on differing points on the belt to be 
     convinced that your are reading what you are reading.  There is VERY 
     little adjustment on the tensioner to go from 2.7 to 4.0.  Using the 
     guage is not that easy!
