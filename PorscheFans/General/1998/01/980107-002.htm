The subject of the effectiveness of Slick-50 and other teflon oil additives
comes up every once in a while, so I thought some readers might find this
interesting.  The source is The Associated Press (AP).
  The FTC has ruled that "Slick-50's claims that compared to motor oil alone,
it reduces engine wear, lengthens engine life and provides a host of other
benefits were deceptive and unproven."  In a settlement with Quaker State, the
manufacturers have agreed not to make false and unsubstantiated claimes for
the additive.  The agreement bars them from making any claims about
performance and benefits of any Slick-50 product or additive without
sufficient substantiation.  The agreement holds open the option that the FTC
may seek consumer redress if current private class-action suits do not provide
at least $10 million in settlements.  The ruling applies to Quaker State and
its Slick-50 subsidiaries under the name Blue Coral. 

Bottom line: Save your money.
