I read the autobiography of Ferri Porsche about a year ago.  It's a good
Porsche history book, covers Porsche car business in great detail up to about 1970.
The Porsche family's involvement with the Nazi's that I recall from book was
the designing of military equipment such as Schwimmer, Kubelwagen, and
tanks.  The Porsche firm was strictly a design studio until after the war.
Manufacturing of Porsche cars started in Gmund while it was occupied
by US troops. 
Dr. Ferdinand Porsche was asked to come to France after the war 
to assist in designing a vehicle for France's government.
Dr. Porsche and Ferri Porsche were arrested when they arrived in France.
They were accused of something to do with selling parts to the Nazi's.
Neither were ever charged with any specific crimes.  Ferri was detained for
about 6-9 months.  Dr. Porsche was held for about a year and a half total.
Dr. Porsche health deteriorate during his detainment and died within about
two years after his release.
The autobiography of Ferri Porsche, I found it at the local public library.
