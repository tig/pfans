Goon Koch is right.  Any SUV not yet on the market will probably fall
victim to saturation.  According to Road & Track, there's even a
Cadillac version in the works.  Import, domestic, big, small, Spartan,
luxury--the bases are all covered.  Now, if there were an SUV that could
be autocrossed without being laughable, Porsche would make it, but that
would be at cross-principles:  for an SUV you need suppleness and lots
of clearance; for autocross you need the opposite.

Anyone remember the GMC Typhoon?  It was a Jimmy (i.e., GMC Blazer),
with a turbo 3.8, lowered, and speed-rated street tires.  It was sporty
and fast, but of limited utility.
