me off so much...................Ah, I have it.
Purebred sportscars and race cars. That's what I think of when I talk Porsche.
Not Grand Cherokees.  That just rubs me wrong and somehow lessens the whole
mental picture I have of Porsche AG. I would argue that there is a huge
difference between a 2-door and 4-door design. And again, it's image. I don't
think it's healthy for Porsche to convey an image that they are a 4-door
company. And the off-road 4-wheel drive stuff was only to test and refine
their 4wd road cars. Right now, Porsche's image is, ABSOLUTE WINNER.  Building
a 4-door or SUV screams FAMILY.  Das geht nicht. It just doesn't go.  Now
there's nothing wrong with a family, or family sedans or SUV's.  And I'm not
trying to be a snob about Porsches.  It's something like this:  I like
sardines. And I like ice cream. But I never think of putting sardines on my
ice cream. The two don't go together and the thought of them together is
revolting ( there's probably some guy in Winnipeg right now saying, "Hey, I
love sardines on my ice cream!" But hey, there's no accounting for taste.  And
I'm not some rich guy who can afford to burn a $100 bill.  But there are some
