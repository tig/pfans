the road cars. Porsche doesn't rally for fun. If they could beat the hell out
of the tranny in the Paris-Dakar rally and place 1,2 and 6 (?), it was
dependable enough for the road cars, hence the 964 C4 and AWD Turbo.  The
system was also tested on race courses in the 961 prior to going into the road
cars.  And what has Elford in a 66 911 (2 wheel drive) got to do with 4wd
testing and refining? That was just another endurance test to show the world
the dependability of an existing design, not to refine a 4wd system that
didn't exist at the time.
