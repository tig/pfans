Hi Joe,
MIME stands for Multipart Internet Mail Extensions and it is a standard
proposed (and currently in wide use) to allow diverse mail programs to
decode mail from one another.  The multipart reference is the way the
standard speaks of different components of the actual mail data.  For
example, if you attach a Microsoft Word document to an e-mail, MIME will
put the text of the e-mail message in one "part" and the M$ Word document
in another "part".  The receiving mail program can then look at each "part"
and decide what it understands and can deal with.

I hope this doesn't sound too much like jibberish.  If your mail program is
done right you should be blissfully unaware of the MIME machinations going
on under the covers.

I have not looked at the webtv stuff but if you are seeing these things, it
sounds like your mail program is not MIME compliant.  Are there any
configuration options for it?  Maybe you can turn MIME on and off.
