Yet another '911 is gone' story.
Note also the 225Kw 996 versus the 235Kw M3.
Mr Porsche is bringing out his clean new car with table manners, a Boxster
upbringing and less horses than the current M3 -- seems like Porsche is
willing to say that BMW can build 'better' engines.  Good grief, their
marketing people should be taken out and shot.

"Last year's 3.6-litre two-valve boxer developed 213 kW at 6,100 rpm, the
new water-cooled 3.4-litre four-valve engine needs 6,800 rpm to churn out
225 kW."

I believe Porsche quotes 282 hp for a '96 993, 272 hp for a '95 993, 252 hp
for a '94 964 and now 300hp for a '99 996.  It seems like a good growth
curve, but the reality for 4 or 5 valve engines today is a measureably
higher specific output (power for displacement, ie. Kw per litre or
horsepower per cubic inch.)
