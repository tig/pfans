From Panorama, March 1985:

	Porsche traces the history of the 944 Turbo back to 1981 at LeMans
where, disguised as a 924GTP, an experimental racing version of the car
finished a credible seventh overall and won a prize for reliability
based on having spent the least time in the pits.  Last June, a
pre-production version 944 Turbo fitted with a standard catalytic
converter won the 24 Hours of Nelson Ledges with a 40 lap advantage. 
The car was so quiet that the drivers kept themselves alert through the
long hours by listening to the radio.
	The catalytic converter concept was part of Porsche's basic plan from
the beginning. (and the article continues)

This leads me to think that whoever says that you should punch out the
