My solution has been to removed the wiring harness from the stalk, then 
tighten by inserting a long allen key, to memory an M10.  The wiring 
harness is a major pain to remove because it involves unlocking the 
connectors pins from the connector block in the mirror housing.  These 
are among the shabbiest connectors Porsche uses, and you will probably 
have to replace one or more when they break on the way out.  The part 
numbers are N.017.562.4 for the female, N.017.564.1 for the male.

Alternatively, Baum sells a tool which lets you tighten this fastener 
without removing the harness.  It is tool P1001, and I don't have a 
price for it.  BTW, Baum has a huge line of Stahlwille tools, quality 
professional stuff which is competitive with Snap On both in price and 
quality. They also have lines of specialized Porsche and Mercedes 
tools.  Usual disclaimer here about lack of fiduciary conflict.
