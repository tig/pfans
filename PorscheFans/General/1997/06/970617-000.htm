By the way, from model year 1995-on the 911's are also called by their 
factory model number:  993 (which is the "cool" way of referring to them!) 
 You should also consider posting to the 911@porschefans.com group (see my

Now for the help (note I didn't use the word "answer" as these are not the 
definitive last words on 911s, just my own learning from the past few 
months of ownership; other P-fans please join in!)

1.  The engine undertray heat problem came from the previous generation of 
911s (the '89 to '94 models, aka the 964's)  The 993 undertray is 
supposedly better at removing excess heat, but Bruce Anderson (world 
renowned Porsche guru and 911@porschefans subscriber) said in a recent 
issue of Excellence magazine (a great Porsche educational resource) that he 
still recommends removing the undertray on the 993, even though there 
hasn't been enough data collected on the 993 undertray issue as there is 
with the 964.  (I am waiting to hear more evidence before removing mine.)

2.  I *think* the reason for no stationary warm-up is that with the 
air-cooled engine it is better to get moving ASAP to get moving air to the 
rear and help the fan cool the engine.  Anybody with a better theory out 
there?

3.  I asked the Porsche dealer about weight of Mobil 1, and he recommended 
using the "appropriate temperature range" oil, which here in sunny CA is 
the higher weight 15W-50.

4.  The puff of smoke is from oil in the cylinders.  Same dealer said that 
since the engine is a "flat" six that overnight oil seeps into the 
horizontal cylinders, and so you'll get the puff of oil smoke in the 
morning.  This is part of the reason why the owner's manual also said that 
the engine is designed to consume oil, and you have to add oil 
occasionally.  For comparison, my two Japanese sports cars never needed 
extra oil in the eight years I had 'em, yet my 3000-mile 993 needs an extra 
quart every 1000 - 1500 miles, sort of like the '76 Impala I once had with 
200,000 miles on it!

Some of the resources I use to educate myself on the marque include two 
magazines dedicated to Porsches:  Excellence and 911 & Porsche World, the 
occasional European Car magazine, joining the PCA, and of course this email 
group.

Have fun with your new 993, mine has been worth every cent!
