In response to Dana Kincaid's question about whether police have to obey
speed limits, although I'm not a police person myself, I know a CHP
officer who told me that as a matter of professional courtesy, most cops
simply don't write up other cops.  This is not the case with DUIs, where
even judges and former DAs have been apprehended and punished, but
run-of-the-mill speeding violations are routinely overlooked.  Spouses,
especially in the same jurisdiction where everyone knows each other, can
be accorded the same privilege.

It's a perk of the job, like diplomatic immunity.  Of course it's not
fair, but what is?
