Ah but numbers aren't everything.  Numbers belong in the sciences, not in
art.  It depends on the individual's perception of the "Sports Car"
concept - personally I think the design and manufacture of a worthy
product is as much if not more an art form than a science (especially
given the mass nature of modern production).  Some peoples' perceptions
are skewed the other way - and that's fine by me.  My point though is that
anybody could build a frame with a big engine and end up with better
"numbers" than a Corvette.  In fact, the Corvette might as well be
manufactured by Ford or any other company - it has no special value as an
individual work of art to be appreciated.  It's a tool, certainly evokes
no emotion in me.  Perhaps a very effective tool for now, but tool
technologies and fashions come and go.  A Porsche is different.
