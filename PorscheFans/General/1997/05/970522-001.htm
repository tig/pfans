     ______________________________________________________________
     Once again, we disagree.  While R1's are better than slicks in the 
     rain, they aren't great in the rain compared to a full treaded, well 
     designed street tire, for a couple of reasons.  One is that they only 
     have tread pattern on 2/3 of the tire - not great for displacing 
     water.  Second, even full tread R1's have less tread depth than your 
     average full tread street tire.  Further, your comment about the soft 
     compound enabling the R1's to come up to temperature easier in the 
     rain is mystifying.  Yokohama A008 RS's are also a soft compound R 
     rated tire, and they don't come up to temps nearly as quickly as R1's 
     on a dry track.  Street tires, on the other hand, are designed to work 
     at lower temps, which is why they "come up to temp" faster on a dry or 
     wet track.  That's why street tires are "safer" for the novice on the 
     street, because he/she need be less concerned about them sticking 
     through the first couple of turns on the Angeles Crest Highway (or 
     other twisty road). 
     
     
     
When I bought my first set of R1's I went to Lime Rock for a Time Trial. The 
practice sessions were dry, then it poured for the TT. On the way home [I don't 
have a trailer, so I was driving home in the car, with the R1's on] I
was paranoid about driving in the rain on R1's. To my great surprise, I have 
never had a tire that handled as well in the rain. Unless I went through a 
puddle, the tire gripped and gripped and gripped.
