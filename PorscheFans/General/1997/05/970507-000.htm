The codes are placed by the tire manufacturer on Porsche approved specific=
 tire models and sizes. The March 97 issue of Christophorus (#265) has an=
 article on page 36 describing Porsches testing program and listing approved=
 summer tires for 1997 for Boxster and 911 models. I'll quote extensively:

"...] tires are an important design element, particularly for=
 high-performance sports cars where their properties are co-determinant for=
 the character and handling. This demonstrates one basic reason for the=
 extensive tire tests at Porsche. They are the only way to permit approval=
 of products whose properties are suitably vehicle-specific. [...]=20

Only tires identified on their sidewalls as Porsche tires (N0 to N3)=
 absolutely guarantee original equipment quality under aftermarket sales=
 conditions. In addition, there is no legal definition for a high speed=
 tire(apart from measurements with tolerances and speed categories) and=
 certainly not for handling. Marque ties for Porsche deliver other=
 advantages as well. For all wheel drive models, for instance, dimension=
 constancy in tires is exceptionally important so that larger revolution=
 differences cannot appear in the course of time between front and rear=
 axles, since these can overload the drive system."=20
