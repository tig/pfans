This is not the way MY street tires have behaved. Before I moved to
competition tires (BFG R1, Yoko A008RS, Eagle GS CS) I used street
rubber that came on the car (BFG Comp T/A VR, Dunlop D40 M2, Dunlop
SP8000).

I found the street tires were much easier to drive at the limit than
the track tires. The street tires provided much more feedback (via
squealing, rolling onto sidewalls, etc.) that they were about to let
go, and the transition from stick to slide was much more gentle and
easy to control than the competition tires. With R1's, there is not a
lot of difference between stick and slide. IMHO, a novice would have a
very difficult time learning where that line was, and would cross it
too many times. In fact, only a fool could get in trouble with the
D40 M2's. Those things squeal so loud at the limit that you would
probably deafen yourself before you ran out of track.

Me too. Only I think you have posted the confusing information! I completely 
agree with Dodge.

IMHO, full tread depth R1's are GREAT in the rain. It's because the tread
is soft and will come up to temperature even on a wet track. As Dodge stated,
they don't work AT ALL in standing water, so if the track puddles, watch out.
However, if the track is wet, a car on full tread R1's will be faster than
a car on full tread street tires. 

When I bought my first set of R1's I went to Lime Rock for a Time Trial. The
practice sessions were dry, then it poured for the TT. On the way home [I don't
have a trailer, so I was driving home in the car, with the R1's on] I
was paranoid about driving in the rain on R1's. To my great surprise, I have
never had a tire that handled as well in the rain. Unless I went through a 
puddle, the tire gripped and gripped and gripped.
