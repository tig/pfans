Seems to me that the original question had to do with airbags on the 
track.  I'm pretty sure that airbags are beneficial in a head-on 
collision when the driver is wearing a lap/shoulder belt.  But on the 
track, with a set of five-point harnesses?  The original poster is 
probably right--I'd suspect that the bags would be nothing but a hazard.  
You can imagine all sorts of situations where a deployed airbag would 
turn a minor problem into a full blown disaster.

If I had airbags, I'd be hot to disable them when I was on the track 
(heck, even when I was autocrossing)--as long as I had a good set of 
harnesses.
