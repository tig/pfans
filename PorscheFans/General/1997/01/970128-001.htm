I just got off the phone with a Yokohama motorsports rep (I had some 
questions about autocross tires).  While I had him on the fone, I asked 
about the dots and arrows that come on Yokohama tires.

Here's the deal:

The Yellow Dot is placed opposite the heaviest spot on the tire.  The dot 
should be mounted by the valve stem.  Thus the valve and the heavy spot 
partially balance each other, and less weight is needed overall.

The Red Triangle is at the point of greatest radial runout, the point 
where the tire carcass has the most side-to-side variation.  Evidently 
some fancy wheels (BMW, Merc, Porsche?) have similar markings, which 
should be matched up.  This evidently reduces harmonics.

Seems like the Yellow Dot is the more widely applicable.
