I missed the start of this thread so forgive me for jumping in...

There's alot of negativity towards these 'new' Splitfire plugs, platinum
or not.  I say 'new' as the concept of split electrodes has been around
for awhile.  Supposedly, the spark kernel extends out from within the
split to better ignite the air/fuel mixture, not have multiple sparks
from both ends of the electrode (right?).

Real life:
1.  1984 928S - Yes I bit and installed splitfires.  Idle quality kinda
rough, sometimes does a 'half-misfire' or stumble.  On the gas, runs
fine.  Do not perceive any feelable hp increase seat-of-pants-wise.  

2.  1992 Miata - also has the same at-idle stumbles and some
idle-stalling even.  Dozen's of other owners in the Miata club report
same.  Club recommends pulling splitfires out and using standard tip'd
plugs.  Done deal, problems gone.

3.  Since these are 'fun' cars, I don't really watch mpg so no info
there, sorry...
I spent the bucks for these plugs so I'll keep 'em in both cars until
R&R needed.  Then back to Bosche platinums!
