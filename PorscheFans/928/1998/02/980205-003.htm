Hi again Jim,

Generally it doesn't figure in - just bleed the same way.  However,
there is some fluid stored in the ABS so, when you first get an ABS car
with old fluid, as I did, change the fluid immediately and then used the
ABS for a month or so, and then change the fluid again.  That way, you
cylce the old fluid out of the ABS and then out of the system.  Normal
bleeding doesn't go through the ABS pump.  On the newer BMW's (oooo! bad
word), the fancy computer hookup can open a valve in the ABS to allow
fluid to bleed through.  If you run all the fluid out when bleeding in
the usual way, this can be a problem (although I've done it and
recovered w/o a trip to a dealer).
