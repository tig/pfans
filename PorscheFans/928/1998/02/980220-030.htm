	First thing that I would check are fuel pressures.  There are
three types of pressure: system, control and residual pressure.  The
system pressure is the total fuel pump pressure when running constantly.
This is very important in a CIS system.  Control pressure is metered at
the fuel plunger.  The warm-up regulator play a role in the control
pressure as well.  The residual pressure is maintained by the fuel
accumulator after engine shutdown.  A properly working fuel accumulator
allows easy startup when engine is warm.

	Verify these before you do anything else.
