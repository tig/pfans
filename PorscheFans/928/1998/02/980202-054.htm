This is a more interesting question than I first thought. I've always seen
DIN numbers come out just a little higher (about1%) than SAE net. It turns
out that one metric horsepower (called "PS" in German and "ch" in France)
is defined as 735.499 W, while a good old American (and British, going back
to James Watt's definition of 550 ft-lb/sec) horsepower is defined as
745.70 W, or 1.0139 PS. This, rather than simple metric compulsiveness, may
explain the recent trend to measuring engine output in kW.
