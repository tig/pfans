(Alex wrote:)
> Why do we need to flush and change brake fluid to save the calipers?

  Brake fluid is very hydroscopic - i.e. it absorbs water.  Water in the
  braking system leads to corrosion which does bad things to the metal and
  seals of brake components.  Also, water in brake fluid lowers the boiling 
  point of the fluid which can lead to steam and hence air in the system.
  This leads to a mushy pedal and less effective braking since you're now
  compressing air vs. hydraulic fluid.
