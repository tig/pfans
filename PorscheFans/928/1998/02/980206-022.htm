Well, outside the Empire/Commonwealth, people tend to use metric units.
Pounds (abbreviated "lb") and newtons (abbreviated "N") are both units of
force; 1 lb = 4.44822 N. Similarly, feet (abbreviated "ft") and meters
(abbreviated "m" are both units of distance; 1 ft = 0.3048 m.

Since torque is the moment of a force applied at a radius, its units are
force times distance. Using the conversion factors just mentioned, 1 lb-ft
= 1.3558 Nm.
