	Axle tramp should be livable with new shocks, hey this is not a drag 
car.  If you wanted a toy drag car go get a Nova put in a big block and 
set it up to do nothing but accelerate in a straight  line!  There is a 
good book on the subject, How to make your car handle by Fed Phun (I hope 
I got the name right).  You can set your car up to do anything but not 
everything, a good drag car can't corner, a good stock car has a hard 
time going in a straight line.  A 928 does everything better than 
anything else, you can get beat at a drag by a kids Nova, but you will 
pass him in a corner, and he won't beat you by much in that drag!

	No starts, if it runs fine most of the time I would start by taking out 
the alarm.  The factory manual has a procedure, remove alarm and relay 
then add jumper where relay went.  One of the functions of the alarm is a 
no start....   Another possible source is the switch on the automatic 
box, manual box's don't have this feature.  Some other cars have a no 
start switch on the clutch pedal.   I took out my alarm after it went off 
3 nights in a row, middle of the night, HONK HONK!

	If you adjust your spring hieght (ride hieght), you will need a 
alignment. I raised my sagging car with sport srings, those are supposed 
to lower the car, but mine was really sagging, so it went up!  Anyhow all 
4 inside edges were worn badly in a few weeks, didn't matter tires were 
done.  If you have good tires, you might want to let the alignment shop 
adjust the ground clearence.
