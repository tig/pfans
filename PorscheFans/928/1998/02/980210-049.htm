>      Dear Stephane:
> 
>      As I had mentioned at the Frenzy, each full turn changes the height
>      about 2 mm on stock springs. Going counterclockwise will raise that
>      corner, but will cause the diagonally opposed corner to get slightly
>      lower. After making height adjustments it is advisable to have the car
>      corner balanced and aligned.
> 
>      A possible reason that the first change in height seemed to lower the
>      car may be because the car was not completely settled when first
>      measured. Apparently, after several ups and downs, the car is now
>      higher since you say the space between the fender-well and the tire is
>      20 mm. On my 86 that space is about 30 mm and (IMHO) it looks good.
>      Continue turning counter clockwise until the right height is reached.
> 
>      I would also suggest that you inspect the upper control arm support
>      bolts and nuts. Make sure they are good and tight. I once found the
>      right side to be loose causing an excessive amount of negative camber.
> 
>      Good luck and happy motoring. Ed
