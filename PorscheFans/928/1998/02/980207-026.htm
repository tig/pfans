I am going to check into this subject further.  Last time I spoke to an
electrical design engineer and an electrical inspector for a large
Detroit suburb, I was told that there isn't really such a thing as
"conductive" grease.  The grease electricians use on electrical
connections do not conduct electrons.  What it does do is prevent any
corrosion or oxidation from occuring in electrical connections. 
Ox-Gard, Penetrox, and a few others fall into this category.  Some are
stated to be for aluminum joints, because the aluminum begins to oxidize
immediately after being cleaned and exposed to the air.  This grease
also allows the disassembly of aluminum parts and connections after a
period of time in the elements.
