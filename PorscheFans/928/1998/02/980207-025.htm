Speaking of flying cars and aerodynamics, man, I haven't read as much
sniveling and finger pointing since the latest Clinton scandel of the week.

I think you're all in violent agreement with each other. The difference is in
what the person is defining as "aerodynamics". One could argue that the
classical two pairs of opposing forces acting on an aircraft: lift vs.weight
and thrust vs.drag are the encompassing aerodynamic forces. One could also
argue that weight is not a first order contributor to automobile aerodynamics
since it's always reacted by an equal push from the ground. So if one defines
aerodynamics as solely lift (or downforce) vs.drag, than weight won't be a
factor. But a lighter car goes faster and speed certainly is a first order
contributor to aerodynamics. Weight (or mass) also affects how much downforce
is needed to keep the car negotiating a curve and not going straight off the
road.

So, IMHO, both camps are right. It depends on what is being defined as
aerodynamics.
