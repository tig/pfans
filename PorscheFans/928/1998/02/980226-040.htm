	PT said that some O2 sensors have a higher "resolution".  This, I interpret as
just being more sensitive; outputting more voltage variation given a certain amount of
time.  "Cross-checking" is probably a better word.  Depending on the particular EFI system
in use, the brain relies on this frequency to make the necessary adjustment.  It would not
be unreasonable for me to say that that is why some people feel a degradation in
performance and/or driveability problems because they could have a perfectly new O2 sensor
(but not OEM) on their vehicle.  The brain is programmed to behave a certain way given the
lattitude and performance of all emission sensors in the vehicle.  When you introduce a
component that is not within spec, the brain gets thrown off; e.g. CHECK ENGINE light will
lit.  This is much more critical on newer EFI system.
