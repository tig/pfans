(Dave wrote:)
> I'm thinking about my ball joints.  What I call the
> control arms seem to be aluminum (titanium, I wish). 
> How do I tell if the ball joints are aluminum.  Or, are
> the control arms the suspect parts prone to breakage?
> Do I need to replace all of it?

  You probably don't need to replace any of it.  I believe
  (but would like someone to verify this for me) that the
  change to steel ball joints was made at the beginning of
  the MY 84 production.  Your car should be ok.
  
  The aluminum ball joints were prone to break.  The actual
  ball joint and its associated carrier are one unit, so
  during replacement you simply unbolt the carrier and install
  the new one.  If you clean the carrier well, you could use a 
  magnet to verify that you have the steel ones; if the magnet 
  sticks to the carrier they're steel, otherwise they're
  Titanium, I mean Aluminum.
