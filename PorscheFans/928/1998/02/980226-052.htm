Titanium is a metal.  It is not an alloy. To be precise,
it is element 22 of the periodic table and has a density
of 4.5 grams/cubic centimeter.  So if someone want to try,
you could weigh the lug nut then imerse it under water and
measure the volume change wich would be equal to the volume
of the lug nut.  Divide the weight by the volume and you
will get the density.  If its 4.5, you have a good chance
to prove me wrong.

For your info, Aluminum has a density of 2.7, if the lugs
are from aluminum alloy (and the alloy is mostly Al), the lug
density should then be less then 4.5.  this means that Porsche
saved even MORE weight by using the Al alloy then using
titanium!!!
