> We noticed while replacing some intake manifold and cam cover gaskets

> opened and closed the flap a few times and cleaned where the flap seats

> vacuum line to the actuator is accessable or not for testing.  In any
> case, careful observation of the top of that shaft as rpm's increase
> would tell the story.  I wonder how long that flap had been stuck.
> (This applies only to '87 and later I think.)

You are right on the 87 and later.  Just a warning, there are a few
vacuum line that can only be put back after removing the intake
manifold.
This is a pretty involved job.  So just be carfull if you need to "tug"
on those vacuum hose.  you might make your problem much worse.
