Don't forget to make a volume correction for temperature change.

(P1*V1)/T1 = (P2 * V2)/ T2

Where P = pressure
	V = Volume
	T = Temperature (in kelvin)

	The set of value with 1 are the initial condition, the
value with 2 are the final condition.

This might make a big difference in your system.  The other problem
will be to calculate the amount of back pressure you have in your
system with different volume of gas going through it (would assume that
the higher volume would have more backpressure given similar
 restriction).  A good starting point would assume P1 = P2

But it's just my 0.02
