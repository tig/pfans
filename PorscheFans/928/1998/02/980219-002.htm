Ken: The wheel bearing r&r job is not that difficult..just did one. 
The hardest part is removal of the axle nut. Locate a 32mm, 6 point
socket, 3/4 drive and use a 4 -5' pipe over a breaker bar. Loosen the
upper shock mounting nuts, remove the top hub pivot bolt, remove the
brake dust shield, rotor & caliper (tie it out of the way), disconnect
the CV at the transaxle, release the handbrake cable attachment mech. &
abs sensor mounts (3-6mm bolts), and the large front nut/washer on the
pivot shaft (your hub rotates on this along with your shock). Drive this
shaft out only to clear the hub.  The hub/axle comes right out.  The
splined axle shaft slides out of the hub (you may have to tap it with a
plastic hammer). My local machine shop R&R'd the bearings for $25, its
not a big deal. Tools (17mm, 19mm wrench and sockets, 10 mm wrench and
socket, 22mm socket, 32mm socket).  Tricky part is lining up the hub
carrier and carefully driving the pivot shaft back in. A helper makes
this easy to do.
