Further, depending upon your success with either method, there are some
pitfalls to keep in mind:

1.  Getting the assembly back together 
2.  Potential damage to the plastic shifter cup forward of the shifter
(located on top of the torque tube - any extra jiggling of the shifter
could accelerate wear and pop it right off - then you're in trouble!)
3.  Unwanted movement of the shift rod to the transaxle causing other
undue stress or wear
4.  Loss of brownie points at a concours?

This is definately not your father's Oldsmobile - stray from stock and
you could be asking for trouble, YMMV!
