> Looking at the wiring diagrams, I noticed that the heater blower
> resistor pack includes a thermal protective switch, which is set up to
> bypass the resistor pack when the internal temperature of the pack
> exceeds preset limits. This eliminates current flow through the
> resistors, and therefore eliminates the heat. When the thermal switch
> cools down, it drops out of the circuit, forcing the current flow
> through the resistors, slowing the blower.
> 
> As to why some blowers speed up and some don't, my guess is that a) some
> blowers pull more current than others due to tighter bearings, etc.,
> creating more heat; or, b) some thermal switches are more sensitive than
> others.
> 
> Possible repair could thus include freeing up or lubing the bearings, or
> replacing the thermal switch. Has anyone opened the resistor pack?


Yes, that seems to be the way it's supposed to work, BUT - I replaced 
both my resistor pack AND my blower motor with BRAND NEW ones (for other 
reasons - several fan speeds didn't work due to burned out resistors, 
and the blower motor bearings were shot/noisy), and my car still does 
it.  It went away for a short while, but came back.  So, there must be 
more stuff involved in this problem, presumably EVERYTHING in the 
circuit is suspect, speed switch, any relays, grounds, wires 
themselves?, etc., etc., etc....
