turning off the engine, the 944 temperature gauge would read very high, until
I got the engine going at which time the temp would drop down into the normal
range. That I would expect with the circulating coolant. I noticed no such
phenomenia with the 928. A few minutes after shut down, I turn the key
(without starting the motor) to get a temp reading and it's still in the
normal range. I would expect it to get hotter like the 944. I don't know if
that's a function of the engine or the gage.

From the subscribers response I gather their fans rarely if ever come on with
