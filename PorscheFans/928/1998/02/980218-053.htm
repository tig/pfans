Howard, I don't know what kind of magic thermostats they have on 944s, but
changing the 928 T/S to a lower temp unit (e.g., 75C), will not in any way,
shape or form alter the cooling fan trip point.  The engine will run cooler (a
good thing, generally), but the fans will still come on at the same temp as
they do currently.

The only way to alter this is to replace the coolant temp sensor mounted at
the bottom of the radiator for one that has a different resistance vs.
temperature relationship.  Other triggers for the cooling fans include
switches (meaning a single temp value) for auto transmission fluid, A/C system
pressure, and intake manifold air temp (this one triggers the fans only when
the motor is off), and the pushbutton for the A/C system which has a
microswitch which triggers the fans ON.
