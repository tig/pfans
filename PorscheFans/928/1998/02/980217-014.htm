At 02:44 PM 2/12/98 -0600, you wrote:
>Or put a bit of loctite on the threads close to the heads.  This would
>only put 3 or 4 tread with loctite inside the block.

If you're lucky. Because the exact size of the bolt and the tapped hole
vary from example to example, it's difficult to guarantee the size of the
clearance between them. Capillary action will pull Loctite up as far as the
volume of material and the volume offered by this clearance permit, and the
length of bolt which winds up covered will vary accordingly.

>Also, you could use a lesser loctite.  The red stuff is pretty strong,
>you might want to try the blue stuff, maybe even the purple.

Autosport's bad experience was with Loctite Blue. The Loctite rep did not
suggest any less strong alternative.
