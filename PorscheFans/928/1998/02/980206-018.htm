My rack did about the same as yours.  It needed to be replaced.

Stephane, if you want this for the FAQ, it's yours.

Here's the replacement procedure - it'll take you two-three hours the =
first time, =3D
and (god help you) one the next:=3D20
Tips - buy and use latex gloves as this is a potentially messy job. use =
=3D
a big pan, and a tarp or oil mat. Have a few towels and beer handy. =3D
Call your mother often.
1.. Jack the car and USE STANDS AND CHOCKS - your face will be at stake =
=3D
if the car falls on you. I like my face how it is, and you should, too. =
=3D
As with other phallic things, get it up as high as you can.
2.. Put your key in the ignition and unlock the steering wheel. =3D20
3.. Loosen the tie rod lock nuts just enough to unscrew the tie rod =3D
ends. No need to "pull" them off.=3D20
4.. Completely unscrew the tie rods, but don't move your lock bolts =3D
- you will want to reassemble where they were.=3D20
5.. Using a turkey baster (big opportunity for bad humor here, but =3D
trust me on this) empty out the power steering reservoir well.=3D20
6.. Place a drip pan under there, and undo the TWO (just two) banjo =3D
bolts on the rack's fluid power unit. There are four bolts. Only the =3D
two that go UP into the car need to be removed. The other two are =3D
attached to the rack, and will come out with the car. Fish the four =3D
brass washers out of the fluid while saying something mean to them.=3D20
7.. Unbolt the two large bolts for the sway bar on the drivers' =3D
side, and loosen the two on the passenger side. These are the bolts =3D
that bolt the bar to the frame.
8.. Pull down on the bar a bit. If it doesn't give some, you will =3D
have to REMOVE the other bolts. The reason I hesitate on removing them =
=3D
if you don't have to is because of the challenge of re-aligning them to =
=3D
reinstall.
9.. There is a cover plate under the rack - remove all the smaller =3D
nuts.
10.. Get out from under the car and get the oily sand out of your =3D
eyes and mouth, wash hands and face, repeat.
11.. To remove the larger plate-bolts (4), you have your challenge: =3D
How do I hold the tops of the bolts (! ?) You will either need a =3D
special tool of some unknown description (cha-ching), or just use a =3D
small-medium allen wrench kind of wedged in from above to keep the =3D
bolt-head from turning. Sounds harder than it is.
12.. There is one more bolt way up and left. Easy enough to get to.
13.. Drop this cover, repeat step 10 above.
14.. Completely unbolt the "universal Joint" lock bolts. This is a =3D
splined shaft and aluminum joint and may need some coaxing to undo. I =
=3D
just used a fat daddy screwdriver to spread it out a bit, and a 15mm+ =
=3D
wrench pushed into the gap between the joint and housing to pry it off. =
=3D
It would have been easier if I could have loosened the top half of the =
=3D
joint from the shaft, but. . . . . .(Note: it is impossible to =3D
completely remove/install the joint onto the spline with the rack firmly =
=3D
in place. Lower the rack a bit and pry some more) =3D20
15.. Drop the rack with the tie rod ends still attached. You may =3D
have to use some finesse (more foul language) here to coax it out, but =
=3D
it'll come out. =3D20
16.. Clean the garage floor of all the power steering fluid you got =3D
everywhere and apologize to your wife for the mess and your bad =3D
language.
17.. After removal, use one of those battery terminal brushes to =3D
clean out the inside of the joint's (female) splines, and a little jet =
=3D
of ol' WD40. Also, on your new rack, do the same on the splined part.
18.. Taking the little shafts off the rack is the worst part. Take =3D
the rack to the corner service station where they have those HOSS metric =
=3D
open end wrenches, or do this:
19.. Pull back the rubber boots from the rack toward the ends. =3D
There is a set of grooves near the end of the rack that you hold, and =
=3D
UNSCREW the tie rod portion from the rack. Grunt loudly, get ten bucks =
=3D
out of your wallet and go back to step 18. If you injured yourself, get =
=3D
your hernia operation and go back to step 18.
20.. If you bought a used or rebuilt rack, make sure that there is =3D
no more of that wretched goo (old parts cleaner fluid) in the rack's =3D
plumbing. Stand the rack on the ends and press down a few times. =3D
Expect the goo to spew, so be prepared with a shop towel.
21.. Use loctite to reinstall this part.
22.. Note that there is a groove in the splined shaft on the rack. =3D
This is where the joint's lock bolt passed-by. There is margin for =3D
error, but align this as best you can upon assembly.
23.. Undocumented tip for anal-retentive (perfectionist) shark =3D
owners you do not NEED to do this: Your rack has a little plastic thumb =
=3D
screw that comes off with a twist. (that's what thumb screws do, I'm =3D
told) Once you have your rack back in, at least have your universal =3D
joint bolted on, you can perfectly align your steering wheel to the =3D
center of the rack. Have an assistant turn the steering wheel slowly =3D
until the dimple, visible through the thumb-screw hole is perfectly =3D
centered. Pull off the steering wheel, and drop it back on, centered. =
=3D20
24.. The rest is basically "the reverse of removal."
25.. Use ATF, not power steering fluid. Follow your owner's manual =3D
here.
26.. Lower the car.
27.. Prime the pump ! Don't just fire it up ! (I'm serious) Fill =3D
the reservoir and bump the starter three times. refill and run for =3D
three seconds three times. Stop and refill. Start the car and turn lock =
=3D
to lock three times slowly. Refill and let any bubbles settle. One =3D
more lock-to-lock and the air should be gone.
28.. Get an alignment. Remember, not every shop can align your car. =3D
Don't let them lift the car and decompress the struts. . .and don't let =
=3D
them tell you that the caster/camber is not adjustable. Don't skimp =3D
here. Ask another list member for local advice.
29.. Wash your towels and then wipe out the washing machine. The =3D
next time you do laundry, you'll be very sorry if you don't. . =3D
..especially if it one of your wife's new shirts was in the load (take my =
=3D
word for this, too)
30.. Send cash to Steve Peck >WINK< (stevepeck@bigfoot.com)
