Richard B. Hirsch wrote:

> Are the piston heads in our 928s aluminum or steel?  If they are
> aluminum,
> how does the engine block tolerate the different expansion rate of the
>
> aluminum block?  If steel, same question.  Is there a steel lining in
> the
> cylinder wall that prevents the expansion?  Thanks, Richard

Everything, except for fasteners, is aluminum.  The pistons have a
special coating to reduce
friction and the bloc is casted in a special manner to obtain a silica
rich layer on the
cylinder walls.  Everything being aluminum, it all expand at the same
rate.

As for the exhaust manifolds, you notice that they put expansion joints
on them to minimize
stress.
