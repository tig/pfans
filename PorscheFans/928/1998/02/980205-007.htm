I should leave this to be answered by one of our physics professors,
but I won't. Newton-meters (Nm) are just another unit for measuring
torque. They are in the MKS (meter-kilogram-second) system, as opposed
to our supremely logical English furlong-tonne-fortnight system.
Turns out that 1 Nm = 0.737 lb-ft; I won't burden you with why
the physics professors like lb-ft rather than ft-lb, unlike
everybody in the real world of auto mechanics.

Look at the other side of the torque wrench you surely have for 
making sure that your wheel lugs are appropriately tightened,
and you might even see Nm there.
