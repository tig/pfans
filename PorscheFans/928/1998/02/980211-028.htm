I have reviewed your application and have a few suggestions.

As you know, Loctite threadlockers are single compound liquid adhesives
which completely fill the microscopic gaps between interfacing threads.
They cure to a tough solid when they come in contact with metal in the
absence of air (anaerobic).

Loctite 243 achieves significant strength in the presence of light oils
whereas Loctite 242 should be applied to a clean surface.  Loctite 243 has
a breakaway value of 180 in.-lbs. as compared to Loctite 242 which is rated
at 115 in.-lbs.  Both values were on a 3/8" x 16 steel to steel bolt.  I
believe the chemical reaction you are referring to is the curing of the
anaerobic product.  As you can see, product 243 is stronger than 242.

It is suggested that a 8.8 or 10.9 grade steel bolt be used in a Porsche
application.
