After my engine rebuild, as usual, I carefully monitored the overflow
tank in case of head gasket failure or any other coolant 'seepage'.   I
was alarmed cuz after several cycles running to operating temp, the tank
would go down by and inch or two.   Fearing an internal problem (head
gasket allowing coolant into cylinder?), I used my old Stant radiator
tester to check.   System stabilized under static pressure test, much to
my relief, and the tank again lowered an inch.   After refilling to
proper level, it has remained 'normal'.   I think there were several air
pockets in the engine that the tester cleared.   If I crack the coolant
system again, part of my operation will be to apply pressure to the
system to purge any air that might be trapped.
