	In a situation like this, I generally would take a brute force
approach.  With a schematic at hand, I would tap a wire at various break
points along the starting circuit.  Label it appropriately.  Leave the
wires where it would be accessible relatively fast.  Keep a voltmeter
handy in your 928.  When the situation arises again, start taking
voltage measurement at each tapped wire.  Make a note of each reading.
Armed with this information, you should be able to locate the culprit.
You should be able to tap into any circuit at the fuse panel and its
multiple connector plugs.

	I've used this approach successfully in troubleshooting a break
along the charging system's circuit.  The problem only occurs when I
drive the 928S for about 30-45 minutes, and never at idle.  Found that
the D+ wire has a heat and vibration problem within the engine harness.
Takes alot of patience and time, but pays off handsomely.  Good luck.
