I understand the upper and lower assy's are different.

unit (bummer) and the lower assy. is built-up.  The

>(Dave wrote:)

>> My control arms (carriers?) ARE aluminum.  Am I
>> supposed to replace these?
>
>  The upper and lower control arms of all 928s are aluminum and
>  should never need to be replaced unless a) the ball joint in
>  the upper control goes bad (a rare occurence), or b) you get
>  into an accident which damages the control arms.
>  
>> Would someone tell me if what I call "control arm" is
>> the same as what others call "ball joint carrier".
>
>  They are not the same.  These are two distinct parts.  
>  The ball joint carrier is a ~5" long base on which the 
>  lower ball joint is actually mounted.  If you crawl under your car, 
>  you will see two rounded-triangular "keys" mounted to the
>  outer edge of the lower control arm near the spindle; these are
>  used to align the front end.  The keys sit against the lower
>  ball joint carrier, and the bolts that go through the center of 
>  these keys pass through the carrier before they thread into the
>  lower control arm.
>  
>                
>                                            __
> control arm->  -------------------------  /  \ <-ball joint
>                                        | /    \
>                         bolts-> |   |  |--------       
>                -----------------|---|---       |
>                            |    |   |          | <-carrier
>                            -----|---|-----------
>                          key-> --- --- <-key
>                                 
> 
>   NOTE:  the description and picture above only apply through
>          mid-'86.  After that, the S4 front suspension was used
>          and the ball joint and carrier were flipped to sit on
>          TOP of the lower control arm.
