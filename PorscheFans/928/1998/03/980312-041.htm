> There should be no bodywork required for this. When I restored my 78
> Euro, I removed the S model spoiler that the PO had put on the car.
> Actually, he had replaced the whole rear hatch with one from a later car
> - why, I'm not sure. Anyway, The difference is in the exterior window
> trim.
> 
> If your car now has the spoiler, you will have two small L-shaped trim
> pieces that fit across the top of the window, meeting with a joiner in
> the middle, and curve part way down the sides of the glass. Cars without
> the spoiler have three pieces of exterior window trim - two larger
> L-shaped pieces that replace the small ones you have, and a single one
> that covers the lower part of the window and curves up to meet the two
> side/top pieces. This is the piece that has the nice, tasteful (as
> Leonard Laub states, although I haven't confirmed this by licking just
> yet) 928 embossing. There are also three trim joiners required.
> 
> Remove the spoiler (the screws and nuts that hold it in place are
> exposed by removing the interior hatch trim), and remove the upper metal
> trim. Note the the black plastic retainers underneath the trim.  When
> you get the new trim pieces you need, make sure you get enough of these
> trim retainers, and screws, to retain the new trim. The originals also
> had little rubber washers under the screw heads, although I suggest you
> add a little silicone as well. It's all pretty straight-forward.
> 
> The side pieces of the spoiler do attach to the directly to the body
> through holes that are not there in an "unspoiled" car. Fortunately,
> with the exception of one (on each side), they are hidden by the side
> window trim. The two that you can see are on the inner edge of the hatch
> opening. You can't see these when the hatch is closed. I'm sure you
> could get a small, flat plastic decorative plug of some sort to hide the
> hole. Or just put the screw back in after you remove the spoiler ends.
> I'm a little unclear as to how these attach, since my car did not have
> them. I did obtain them, and the two flared trim pieces required to
> mount them, with the intent of completing my spoiler, but decided to put
> the car back to its original look instead.
