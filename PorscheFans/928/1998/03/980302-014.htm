Nope. To quote Porsche's 1987 model year US sales brochure:

	The original 928 swept the European Car of the Year nominations.
	The second, "S" edition of 4 years ago brought more power, even

	cylinder edition of two years brought a dramatic increase in the
	total performance of this automobile. Now to mark the 928's
	Tenth Anniversary, Porsche designers and engineers proudly present
	their fourth edition 928, the "928 S4"...

>Hence no S3.

four-valve cars (US, Japan, Canada, and, for '86, Australia), but I haven't
seen any badges or paperwork with that designation. It's reasonable, it's
descriptive, but it's unofficial.
