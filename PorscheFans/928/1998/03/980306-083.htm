To Mike Somerville....

Absolutely can "they" get you from the rear with radar.  Doppler shift works
no matter what direction the car is moving relative to the radar source (which
can also be moving).  All they do is add the reading from the radar unit to
their calibrated speedo...and you're toast!

However, off-axis readings will be subject to cosine error, as you mentioned.
But there is no cosine error from behind (so the reading is even more accurate
than one taken from a stationary unit on the side of the road, or from an
oncoming police car).
