  I've never heard anyone on the list complain about warped brake
  rotors on the 928, so I guess we can conclude that Porsche did
  a thorough job of R&D on that aspect of our cars.  Anyway, the
  April issue of Road & Track has an excellent article on what causes
  brake rotors to warp.  Surprisingly, it isn't excessive heat that
  causes the warping, but rather the rate at which the rotors cool.
  The author interviewed several brake experts and all reported that
  increasing the mass of a rotor is the best way to prevent warping.
  There's also some discussion on the optimal way to design vanes into
  vented rotors.
  
  I'd recommend this article to anyone interested in the topic.
