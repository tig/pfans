We saw an '87 S4 this week with a stuck resonance butterfly flap (in
intake plenum) - the flap which I suppose distinguishes the S4 32-valve
motor from the earlier non-S4.  So I checked my own '87 S4 by applying
vacuum to the actuator and watching the top of the shaft.  Sure enough
it was stuck too, but lightly tapping it freed it up.  I'm not sure how
common this is or how to effectively prevent it.  But I think there's
about 30 hp and a good bit of torque at stake.  Has anyone been here
before, or is there anything in the workshop manuals about this?
