If you buy something from Porsche you have a much better chance of getting
as good or  better than the original quality, if  you buy from whomever you
are getting their version of quality. This can be great or terrible, that's
the problem, you just don't know.

With Porsche on the other hand you have a darn good chance of the quality
being consistent.

I can give you an example that has nothing to do with Porsche. A few years
ago I bought a "rebuilt with factory parts" waterpump for my Jeep at Napa
(had lifetime warranty etc.).  A month later I start to hear a strange sound
coming from the engine and waterpump area which gets drastically worse over
the next few weeks.
I pull the pump off and see that the impeller was eating its way through the
pump housing, not quite the quality I was assured of.

So I go buy a rebuilt pump from the Jeep dealership ( cost me $12 more than
the other one).
When I pulled it out of the box I was immediately amazed at how heavy it
was, it weight twice the weight compared to what  the "High Quality Rebuilt
with Factory Parts" pump weighed.

This immediately told me that I was dealing with a little denser metal in
the factory pump casing and that the other was a "Cost Effective Asian
Version". made from CRAP!

Water pump is now 3 years old and quiet as a mouse.
