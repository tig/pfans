The HE designation refers to the May cylinder head, May being a Swiss
engineer who created the High Energy combustion chamber configuration
back in the gas-crunch days.  The HE gets "somewhat" better mileage.  In
a 12 cylinder, this is rather moot, but the factory had to do something.
My Euro-spec car cites 299HP (SAE) from its 5.3 liters and gets 15mpg. 
Not sure if a non-HE motor is much different in the HP or mpg
departments.

Note that the tranny is a GM Turbo Hydromatic 3-speed with a truck
heritage.  It's bullet-proof.  Now, as to the electrical system, does
the name Lucas tell you anything?
