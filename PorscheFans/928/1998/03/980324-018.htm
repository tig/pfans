There is, as St=E9phane Chabot pointed out, a nice, established, French word
"voil=E0", which literally means "there is" and practically means "behold" o=
r
"there it is". However, there is a persistent distortion of that word
dating, if I remember correctly, from the "Our Gang" comedies, in which it
was cute for the Little Rascals to come up with their own approaches to
language.

At least one of said Rascals would exclaim, when finding or demonstrating
something of interest, "Viola!" People laughed because they knew the
adorable creature really meant "Voil=E0!" Decades later, the "viola" usage
has become a thing unto itself (sui generis, for those who crave Latin).
