> >(Glenn wrote:)
> >I have an 86 928s with 225 50 16 tires on  the stock rims (I think 7" wide).
> >
> >Can I put 245 tires on these rims.  I know I wont get as much of a contact
> >area on the ground as by putting 245 on an 8" wide rim, but am I better off
> >with the 245 than the 225 on the 7" rim?
>
(Leonard wrote:)
> According to the tire makers, no. The minimum rim width for 245/45x16 tires
> is 8.0 inches. On 7" rims, you may curve the tread or even prevent the bed
> from seating and sealing. Stick with the 225/50x16 size, in which you can
> get extremely good tires, or get S4 or other suitable (correct offsets, no
> interference or rubbing) rear wheels in 8" or greater width.

  Well...  According to the tire makers, yes _and_ no.  Glenn didn't
  specify which profile was to be used with the 245's.  Leonard is 
  correct in that a 245/45-16 won't fit on a 7" rim, but a 245/50-16 
  will.  As to whether you're better off with a 245/50 vs a 225/50, the
  proper answer is probably no. 
  
  BUT!, as an owner of a car originally delivered without rear wheel 
  spacers, I can tell you that 225/50's on the rears look damn puny in 
  those big wheel wells.  After owning the car for a year, I just couldn't 
  stand it anymore and had to break the Porsche credence of function over
  form by installing 245/50s.  They ended up working fine on both road and 
  track so I attest that they are a viable alternative.
  
  As Leonard pointed out, the proper solution is to upgrade to wider rims
  to accomodate the 245/45-16 that the factory has specified.  I knew this
  all along and finally upgraded my rims last year, so make sure those
  flame throwers are turned on low heat before scolding me.
