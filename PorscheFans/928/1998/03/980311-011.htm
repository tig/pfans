Just a thought on proper torque techniques...

Someone (sorry, forgot who) mentioned that the stretch on a bolt changes
substantially for a given torque, depending on whether the threads are dry or
lubricated.

Precisely because of this, it is important that the torque is measured with
the threads prepared as per the manufacturer's requirements.  If you lubricate
threads that are specified to be dry during the torque process, you'll end up
with way too much stress on the bolts/studs.  Don't assume that all threads
should be lubricated.  When assembling engines, most of the torque readings
are with clean, dry threads, with lubricant specifically prohibited.

Any way, just make sure that the threads are dry or lubricated, as required.
