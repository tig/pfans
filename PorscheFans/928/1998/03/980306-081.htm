Actually, Michigan does make a hell of a lot of money with RADAR & LIDAR.
But NOT with the Same Lane units.  To my knowledge they don't use them here
in Michigan.  I've heard of them in use out west but don't know where else.
And yes, you bet your *ss that I would fight one of those tickets received
with S-L RADAR.  That's probably why they're not to popular.  Now
unfortunately for us Michigan residents or Mich travelers, the MSP (Mich
State Police) as well as county & city boys are some of the best trained
RADAR officers in the nation.  In other words the chances of getting out of
a RADAR ticket in Mich is slim.  They usually have all of their ducks in a
row when they go to court.

I can't say I feel "safer" with a detector, but I *can't* count the number
of times I've been saved by mine.  I just make sure I'm not the one out
front, or like you said speed very little at night unless someone ahead of
me is stupid enough to be my "bear bait".  Then I follow about 1/8 of a
mile behind & that has saved me many of times.

The big benefit with a detector (even with instant on) is that you are
hoping for a warning from the officer checking someone else way in front of
you.  That's when you get a chance to slow down.
