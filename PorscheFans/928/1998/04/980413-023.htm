>The S4 rear wheels are offset half an inch less than the S wheels (ET 52.3
>v. 65) but are an inch wider. The net effect is to put the inside edge of
>the S4 wheel at the same plane as that of the S wheel, and the outside edge
>a full inch further out. Unless you have a lot of clearance between the
>outside edge of your current wheel and tire and the inside of the fender,
>remove the spacer (which is probably 17 or 21 mm).
>
>Will you then be going to 245/45ZR16 tires on the rear wheels?
>
>Leonard Laub (leonard@visionthree.com)

Stephane,
Best way to find out is to try it.
I just installed a set of Design 90 wheels from a 91 GT on my 85. I though
for sure I would have to remove the spacers (21mm), but decided to
cautiously try it with them first. The GT wheels are 9" wide with the 55mm
offset and you can barely get a small finger in between the tire and fender
lip, but no contact under hard cornering yet with 245/45-16s, and it looks
Killer.

Give it a try, but use common sense so as not to damage your tire or fender.
