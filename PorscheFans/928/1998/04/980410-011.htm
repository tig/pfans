I've noticed an awful lot of chatter about tracking down stray vacuum leaks,
rough running when hot due to stray air intake etc., and people are putting
in too much effort tracking down these problems when a very simple technique
exists.

With the engine idling use a can of spray carb cleaner and just start hosing
down any suspect area, you will immediately notice a change in the engine if
there is a leak.  You will be surprised to find how frequently these develop
around intake tubes and generally all along the top of the engine; I do this
every month and normally find some clamp a bit too loose.

Use same technique under the car if auto trans, helps find shifting
problems.
