Ken,

WOAH!  No need to fabricate anything!  Those rubber mounts are 
adjustable!  You'll notice that the rubber part is not straight up and 
down, it's angled slightly.  If you loosen the locking nut at the base 
of the support rod, you can rotate the whole thing.  By rotating it, you 
can get the rubber bushings to lean forward toward the front of the car, 
which mounts the airbox closer and pushes the airflow sensor deeper into 
the plenum (for a better seal).  I did this exact thing on my car, as I 
had the same problem you're describing.  Didn't help with my idle 
"hunting" though.  :-(
