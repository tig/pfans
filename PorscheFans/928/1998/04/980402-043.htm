chuck the whole airpump assembly.  I am

disabled the the rotors (this can be done either
with finesse or with violence; the result is the
same,although I want to save the equipment in
case the law ever makes me put it back).  It
retains it's position in the pulley triad, so there

far I can go.  I only am doing it to stay basically
clean and logical, not to gain power, but I
wouldn't doubt that there is a horse or two to be
gained, as well as the common sense of losing 15
pounds of equipment.  The original suggestion
came when a mechanic noticed that the airpump
was grumbling, so why have it working hard at
all.
