The switch is the tensioner itself.  There is a copper "wire" (more like
a small flat plate) that run down the tensioner.  This makes a 
connection to the engine ground.  If the belt is loose, the tensioner
will "float" away and the connection will be broken.  This is when the
light comes on.

Check all the wire connection to the tensioner (there should be a small
plug on your passenger side belt cover, right behind the oil dip-stick)
