I would like to let Nigel know that what he experienced is called TTO or
"Trailing Throttle Oversteer." Sometime it's called lift throttle oversteer.
Basically, it's when you enter a turn (or bend) at or near the tires limits
of adhesion and trail off the gas or (worse) onto the brakes. The weight of
the car is then shifted towards the front which takes traction away from the
rear tires. The result is the desire for the rear of the car to come around
towards the front. TTO is more pronounced in a rear engine rear wheel drive
car (like a 911) than on a 928. However, it is still possible to do in any
Porsche, and in some cases it is desired, but in street driving it is
usually not something one wants to happen. To achieve TTO is very easy. To
control TTO takes practice, practice, and more practice. I suggest that you
should try to experience it at least once, but do it in a controled
situation, like an empty parking lot or at an autocross.

Happy motoring. Ed
