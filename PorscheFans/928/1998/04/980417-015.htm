The temperature switch at the front base of my radiator is making an
intermittent connection.  I'd like to replace it to prevent any potential
overheating (and the part is only $40.00).  Has anyone replaced their
switch, and is it necessary to remove the radiator?  It is in a very
inaccessible place.>>

You don't really need to remove the radiator! Just drain teh cooolant (one
plug on the bottom of the radiator , opposite to the offending switch, and one
on each side of the engine block)
After you remove the splashguard underneath (9 fasteners + 2 Allen screws),
it is pretty easy (but you need to crawl under the car!!)

Use a deep socket (forgot the size...sorry) to remove it: it has a tendency to
"rust in" the radiator. The socket gives you better contact on all 6 faces
(don't use a 12 sides socket!!!) ..a flat wrench may slip....

And don't forget a fresh gasket...

Looks like a good opportunity to replace that old coolant by some fresh
extended life stuff. (Nothing's too good for our baby!)

Hope this helps!
