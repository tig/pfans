  I think you can go either way.   I installed mine before putting the
engine back in the car, but didn't get the throwout arm positioned over the
'ball' on the bell housing - so it necessitated pulling the clutch assembly
out to get that arm into position.   It turned out to be easy enough, and in
my mind decided I should have simply mated engine to housing, bolted
together, then install clutch setup through the access plate on the bottom.
As long as the exhaust is already removed, I think you buy some wiggle room
for the engine by installing clutch setup after mating the engine to
bellhousing.
