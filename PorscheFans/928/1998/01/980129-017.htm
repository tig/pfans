It's really not the weight, it's the aerodynamics.  Indy and other
racing cars are much lighter and faster than a 928 and manager to stay
on the road.  If Porsche wanted weight, there would be allot less
aluminum used in the car.

I have a brochure from the late 70's or early 80's from Porsche on the
design and development of the 928.  One of the items mentioned in the
brochure is the body shape/aerodynamics used to resist crosswinds and
keep the 928 where the driver expects it to be.  I drove my 928 in a
wind storm over a bridge 100+ feet in the air with not so much as a
budge as the other car's and SUV's were being pushed halfway into my
lane.  A near miss as I seemed to be the only one on the road not being
blown to the left.
