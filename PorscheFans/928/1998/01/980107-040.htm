As cheap as CDROMs are to make (blank CD-R's are approaching $1.00 in
100's) that AOL, Compuserve, and others mail millions unsolicited
attempting to generate business, Porsche should do the same with their
service manuals. The more widely available the service manuals are, the
more likely they are to sell parts. And the better kept older Porsches
are the better their resale value. The better the resale value of used
Porsches the higher they can keep new Porsche prices.

Once they've bothered to write the manual it costs almost nothing to
produce the 2nd copy.

and/or html. Make mine Unix and Macintosh.
