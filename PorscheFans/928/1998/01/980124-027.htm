Dear "Land Shark" tamers:

I forgot to mention that the definition of "severe" when applied to the
drain interval for oil includes "Stop and Go driving". Think about it. Which
is harder on the engine: a) steady state cruising at any given speed, or 2)
having the engine start when cold, driving a bit but not enough for the oil
to boil off any moisture. A drive across the Gobi Desert (steady state) may
actually be less harmful to the engine than a drive in city traffic.

If you drive under mostly "stop and go" conditions, you are in the "severe"
use category.
