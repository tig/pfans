	My ideal setup for brakes on an S4 are going to be as such:

GTS brakes on front, stock rears, cross drilled rotors on all four corners.
The GTS fronts have subtsantially more gripping power and pad area than the
S4 brakes, and cross drilled rotors allow better heat dissipation. If you
wanted to go nuts, you could upgrade your lines with aeroquip braided
stainless lines, which improve responsiveness and do not bulge under heavy
loads like the stock rubber ones. 
	The GTS brakes won't fit with stock rims, and I would choose to use a
regular type pad on them, not a race pad. Racing pads take a while to heat
up, and could actually be dangerous when cold. They are not intended for
street usage, although some people use them.
	I personally have not had my brake pad rupture, but I do find the 928's
brakes a bit lacking compared to a 911.
