	The Porsche dealer probably misunderstood what you were telling
him/her.  There is an electrical switch assembly behind the tumbler.
What you've described sounds like the tumbler, key, or both is worn.
There's probably nothing wrong with the electrical switch assembly but
worth inspecting since the charging system relies on a particular
contact within the assembly to operate optimally.  If I remember
correctly, you can purchase a new key and tumbler from Automotion for
about $120.  I have some reservation about the locksmith fixing the
tumbler.  Your 928's lock is not like your typical Ford.  The keys are
"laser cut"; with all sort of funny angles and I'm sure the matching
tumbler is just as special.  Porsche want to make it difficult for the
criminal element.  Hope I've been of help.
