Ken,

  According to Devek's page, the clutch master cylinder is easy to get to
  once the brake vacuum booster has been removed.  I recently replaced my
  vacuum booster and brake master cylinder and had started writing up the
  procedure to put in the FAQ (before getting sidetracked).  My instructions 
  won't help with the actual clutch mc, but at least it will get you most 
  of the way there.  I'll try to finish them up within the next week.
  
  The slave is pretty cheap and replacing it will require bleeding the system,
  so I would be tempted to do both the master and slave at once just to save
  some effort down the road.  You may also want to replace the rubber line
  between the resevoir and the clutch mc.
