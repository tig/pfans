In fact the field coils are connected to the battery through the
"Generator" warning light on the dash. Normally, the voltage
produced in the alternator to feed the field coils is about
the same as the battery voltage, so the warning lamp stays
dark. If the voltage produced gets very different, current
will be drawn through the lamp sufficient to make it light.
And yes, there is a resistor in parallel with the lamp, so
that the electrical system won't fail if the warning bulb
burns out.
