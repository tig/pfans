Dave, I think you've started something...

My best Porsche story has to do with meeting Shaquille O'Neal.  I was in
Baton Rouge, Louisiana working on a project in 93 or 94.  Shaq was taking
some classes at LSU during the off season to finish his degree.  One
morning, a friend of mine asked for a ride to campus.  After dropping him
off, I noticed two guys walking across the parking lot in front of me.  One
was pretty tall, and the other was pointing at my car, a dark metallic grey
86 928S 32V, with an S4 wing, chrome-plated AZEV 17-inch wheels, tinted
windows -- VERY sharp.  As I got closer, I recognized the tall man as
Shaquille O'Neal.  I stopped the car and got out, approached Mr. O'Neal, and
said, "Hi, I know you're on your way to class.  I just wanted to meet you.
My name is Tony Golden."  He shook my hand and said, "Nice to meet you.  I'm
Shaquille O'Neal."  I said, "Yeah, I know. Nice to meet you."  Then I got
back in my car and left.  Later that evening, I was telling my friend about
the incident and he said sometimes Shaq comes to the gym in the evenings
when they're playing basketball.  A few days later, my friend was at the gym
and Shaq came in.  After the initial crowd dissipated, my friend walked up
to Shaq and introduced himself.  He then said, "Hey, a buddy of mine said he
met you the other day."  Shaq said, "Oh yeah?  What's his name?"  My friend
said, "Tony Golden"  Shaq pondered a moment, then said, "Hmmm... Tony
Golden....  gray Porsche -- right?"  "That's him!" my friend said.

The "moral" to this story is that if I had driven a Chevy (or a Ford) Shaq
probably wouldn't have remembered me :)

I have never felt that my 81 S ran quite the way it should...yesterday I threw
it up on the rack and was amazed at the way the shop that did the EPA
conversion used this mickey mouse tiny converter that was only 1 7/8 on one
side, and 2" on the other.  They had both pipes from the engine Y'd into this,

well as it did.  I have temporarily re-run twin pipes to the original exhaust,
until I can find a suitable cat , which I am looking for at the moment.  Wow.
Is this a happy car.  I had always been disappointed that it didnt feel
anywhere as strong as the 85 I once owned.  That's no longer the case.  This
puppy flat screams.  Biggest problem now is driving without wanting to plant
my foot in it everytime I leave the light.  I can see new tires in the near
future......
