There is a shunting block directly behind the voltmeter and
instrumentation cluster housing that is probably loose.  It is held on
by a single nut.  The electrical edge connectors are also notorious for
making intermittent connection.  I have yet to see an actually DEAD
voltmeter.  VDO gauges, in my opinion, can take alot of abuse and is
quite reliable.  You need to remove the pod in order to get into the
instrumentation cluster.  R/R of the individual gauges are relatively
easy, just takes alot of patience.  I've posted a brief procedure on the
cluster removal last week and you probably haven't caught up with the
thread.  About your regulator... you did not mention which year is your
928, but probably a Paris-Rhone or a Bosch.  Both of these uses an
internal electronic regulator.  A typical regulator is quite robust.
Generally a bad rectifier diode or a diode trio would cause the
destruction of the regulator, which in turn, for the worst, overcharge
the battery, boil the electrolyte, and/or damage the plates.  As far as
testing of the alternator, I've posted a procedure for that last week as
well.
