Wow!

"Major pain" is a relative term which I believe is completely wrong for
the effort needed to replace the front _STRUTS_. No disrespect intended.

I did it in 45 minutes. Mind you, I didn't replace just the shocks...I
replaced the struts (new springs already mounted on new shocks). 

Pulling out the old struts and putting in the new struts was easy (for me).

Jack the car up.
Take the wheel off.
Remove both upper a-arm nuts (engine side of wheel housing)
remove lower strut bolt.
remove upper strut nuts.
drop strut downward into wheel house. Lower a-arm will need to go down.
pull upper a-arm outward and out of inner fender (ball joint still attached)
swivel upper a-arm and strut 90 degrees rearward within wheel house.
pull strut out of the upper a-arm.

reverse for re-assy. (New lock nuts on a-arm recommended)
