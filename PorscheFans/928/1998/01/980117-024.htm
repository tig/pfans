Porsche spelled this out in their publication WKD 495 921, "928 GT '89
Information Technik" (thanks again, David), which lists the following
differences between the S4 and GT:

- higher output engine (326 v. 316 SAE bhp via tweaked pistons, cams,
computer programs)
- faster idle (775 v. 675 rpm)
- higher red line (6800 v. 6600 rpm)
- shorter gearing
- lighter (and noisier) exhaust system with two small tailpipes
- 40% LSD standard
- 20 mm shorter shift lever
- 8" f and 9" r forged Club Sport wheels
- wider front and rear track (17 mm spacers at rear)
- spacers to increase rear track

The suspension, however, is straight S4: GT cars are supplied with standard
running gear, including shock absorbers. Boge gas pressure shock absorbers
can be ordered as optional extra equipment (M 474) for entry of the car in
sport racing events."
