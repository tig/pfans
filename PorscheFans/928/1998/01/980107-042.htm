Del Lisk wrote....

Fans will turn on with ignition OFF if

>1. temp exceeding 87.5 degrees C registered at the temp. sensor mounted on
top >of the intake manifold plenum.
>2. coolant temp exceeding 85 degrees C registered at the radiator-mounted
temp >sensor. 

>I was told the ign.-on fan activating temps are higher than the temps above
which >explains why the fans stop when the ign is turned off.  Anyone is
welcome to have >at me on this, but I buy it.

Del, I presume you meant to say that the ignition ON temps are LOWER than the
ignition OFF temps, which would indeed explain why the fans normally turn off
after the ignition is tuned off.  In any case, I don't have the manuals in
front of me, but if memory serves me right, the intake plenum temp for fans ON
is around 82C, and the coolant temp for fans ON is around 78C (more or less).
So, if the ignition OFF temp info you were given by PCNA is correct, then it
makes the observed operation of the fans and the described operation in the
owner's manual jive....that's good.

However, doesn't it make you a little P.O.'d that the ignition OFF temp values
are not provided in the service manuals?  How are you ever supposed to figure
out what's going on if this kind of key information is secreted away by the
factory.  This is something that should definitely be added to the FAQ list to
clear up fan operation once and for all.

....but we better be sure about this info...maybe someone can try activating
the fans by heating up the plenum sensor with a heat source (light bulb?) with
the ignition OFF to see if it does indeed activate the fans.
