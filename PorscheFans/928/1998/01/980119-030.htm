All relays have numbers by their pins.

31 - ground
15 - switched positive
30 - battery positive direct

The wiring diagrams have "current tracks" that match the above. They also
provide this information for all the colored wires in all of those multi-
plugs that grace the bottom of the fuse panel. With the wiring diagrams,
it's easy to find a wire of the desired need and tap into it. Of course,
if you do that then you're going to be adding the load of your attachment
to the load of the relay and the fuse in line with that wire.
