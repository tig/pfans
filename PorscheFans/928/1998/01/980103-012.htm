Whoa, hoss. You're right about the pulley being held on by a single bolt
and about the procedure for retensioning the drive belt, but I lost you
here. The shop manual is loaded with torque specs (as are the cute little
spec books), which tend to be listed by the items they describe; e.g., at
the beginning of Section 48, "Steering", where the bolt holding on the
pulley to the power steering pump is described as a self locking nut M 14 x
1.5, material 8, to be torqued to 50 Nm (36 lb-ft).

Generally, you'll find specs for all specialized or unusual fasteners. The
list for the engine also gives specs for "all remaining bolts and nuts" as
follows: M6 to 8 Nm =B1 2, M8 to 20 Nm =B1 2, and M10 to 40 Nm =B1 5.
