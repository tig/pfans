Do a simple test with WHATEVER oil you're running:

 - dip a metal part, like a bolt or something, in HOT oil (heat it in
your microwave or whatever)
 - hang it from a string in your garage someplace
 - let it sit there for a week or two

See if all the oil runs off or not.  Petroleums generally will, and
pretty
quickly.  A good synthetic will keep a fairly thick film of oil for a
LONG
time (weeks or months!).

Why does this matter?  If you don't drive your car for a few days, and
you're
running a petroleum oil, then YES, you'll have metal-to-metal contact
for
a while.  If you're running a good synthetic, then you should NOT have 
metal-to-metal contact on startup, as there will still be a decent film
of 
oil all over the various engine parts.
