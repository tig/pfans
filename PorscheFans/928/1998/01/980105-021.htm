Mike Holman told Jim Helman that... >My car, and
others I've spoken to, have the same dilemma.  When I check the
alternator output with a voltmeter- all is fine, even when driving under
heavy load.  It seems there are some connectors in the instrument cluster that
get
grungy and allow for false readings...  On your car, the alternator may be
just fine and the battery is on its way to the grave.<

I certainly agree with this possibility.  My voltmeter would typically read
around 12V after driving around for 20-30 minutes or so (would start out at
14V after starting).  Dealer checked it out and said all charging parameters
were nominal.  Just to assure myself that dealer was right, I hooked up my
voltmeter to the battery while driving (one good thing about the battery
location) to see what was what.

Lo and behold, my voltmeter read 13.5 to 14.0V, regardless of what the in-car
unit read.  Obviously, both the alternator and battery were both good.  Now I
don't worry when I see 12V on the voltmeter anymore, especially at night with
the lights on.  You may want to check your in-car voltmeter the same way.
