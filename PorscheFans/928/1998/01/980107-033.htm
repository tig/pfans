> Okay, this is interesting.  I've definitely had cars run without a
> battery when, in the case of a dead battery and no jumper leads I
> have put the battery out of another car in, started the car, and
> while it's running swapped the dead battery back in so that it will
> get charged.

> The point is, both times I've done this the car didn't die.  I'd
> have to check whether the Lotus Seven I did it on had a generator or
> an alternator, but the Esprit and the Triumph were both definitely
> alternators.

> So... why?  (I agree with Vince - it shouldn't work in theory.)

Once the alternator is running on the good battery I believe it can
feed its own field coils even without any battery.  While connected
to a running alternator (or generator), the battery is acting mainly
as a load and voltage stabilizer.  Now if the voltage regulator
disconnects the alternator's output from the battery line for too
long or if you hook up a really dead battery, the current supplied
to the field coils could drop low enough that the alternator will be
unable to maintain a field.  But other than that, I think it should
run.  However without a battery or a really, really big capacitor to
stabilize the voltage, I'd be worried about frying the car's
electronics.
