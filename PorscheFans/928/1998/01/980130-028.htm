	A while ago sombody gave some info about how to fix the voltmeter, and 
then recently somebody said they never fail.  Well mine didn't work very 
well, so I went to radio shack to look for the recomended 100 ohm 
potentiometer.  Well they didn't have that but they did have a bag of 
assorted ones for about $3.00, how could I lose?  Well some wern't marked 
but using a meter I found a 200 ohm petentiometer, that worked.  It was a 
little tricky to mount/solder the new part on, but I figured nothing to 
lose, it don't work now!  I just love projects that you can't make 
worse...  Anyhow you don't have to be real picky about finding a 100 ohm 
potentimeter, a larger one will work but will make the adjustment more 
sesitive.  I think you could go as high as 500, so long as it will fit, 
thats part of the trick.  I happend to have 2 six volt batteries and some 
D cells in the den (airplane shop), so I was able to adjust it before 
going back into the unheated garage.  12 volts battery, 12 plus D cell 
charging.....
