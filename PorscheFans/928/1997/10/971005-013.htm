ABS Fault: dealer or specialist has plug-in diagnostics module to read error
codes.  Unless an obvious broken wheel sensor or fuse problem isn't found,
the diagnostic check is the way to go, can save money due to "cherry
picking" guesses.

AT Performance: As you can see, the factory manuals are the way to go.  Here
is some data from the North America factory manuals to provide answers
regarding the 928 AT.   There are two AT "gearing schemes" used, the earlier
'83-88 has a numerically smaller (higher ratio) 1st gear and final drive,
and the '89s and beyond have the numerically higher (lower ratio) 1st gear
and final drive.  Combining normal 2nd gear stars, the higher ratios with
the lower HP and higher torque converter stall speeds of the earlier models
yields the slower feeling "off the line" acceleration followed by a burst of
power as the engine gets into it's higher HP/Torque bands.  (Some manual
ratios provided for comparison)

a. Model Types

928 S USA/Japan model '83-84 transmission type: AT = A28.01

928 S USA/Japan model '85: AT = A28.04, manual = G 28/11

928 S USA/Japan model '86: AT = A28.07, manual = G 28/11

928 S4 USA/Japan model '87-88: AT = A28.12, manual = G 28/13

928 S4 worldwide '89-91: AT = A28.16, manual = G28/55

928 GTS worldwide 92-93: AT = A28.18, manual = G28/75 (couldn't find ratios)
