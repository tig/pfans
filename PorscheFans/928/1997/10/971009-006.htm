It probably doesn't matter, but Chevron has built a reputation for

>The BMW group suggest using regular instead of premium in moderate
>climates because it burns better in the 12-cylinder engine.

Let them eat low-octane cake. I doubt that they're right even for their own
cars, but they're definitely wrong for yours and mine.

>My 928 owners manual says only use premium.

It speaks the truth. Your car's engine control computers will adapt to
whatever fuel you put in by retarding the spark to avoid knock. However,
though they can preserve smooth running, they cannot preserve power under
these conditions. Once again: use the highest octane gas you can get.

>Are there any major engine precautions I should be aware of?

Just one; make sure the main timing belt is retensioned every 15,000 miles
or so and replaced every 45,000 to 60,000 miles. If the BELT TEN warning
light comes one, stop the engine NOW. If it does not come on when you
restart, just keep an eye out for it. Otherwise, flatbed the car to the
shop. A broken or significantly slipped belt can result in valves hitting
pistons. When you replace the timing belt, you may want to replace the
water pump as a proactive measure.
