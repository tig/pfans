I have a 1987 928 S4 auto that has done 67,000 miles.  At 49,000 miles I
installed new Boge shocks front & rear.  However, the front springs have
sagged enough to cause the front wheels to toe out, wearing the insides 
of the front tires.  My car does not have the adjustable spring platforms.
I can toe in the front wheels, but this does not address the sag. It's been
suggested that I install Automotion Weltmeister 928 Sport Springs front &
rear, with the height adjustment kit, and Koni red adjustable shocks.  This
should sharpen the handling, enable the height to be restored, and remove 
the 'float' from the ride.  I live in New Zealand, a mountainous country 
with narrow, and poorly surfaced roads.

Questions:
 1) Will this make the car too stiff for daily use?
 2) How will the car corner on uneven surfaces - will it jump around?
 3) On long journeys, will I (and the car) be shaken to bits?
 4) Where is a good (cheap) place to source these parts?
 5) Are the Koni's OK, or should I look at Bilstein's?
 6) Are there any other parts I should consider?  Devec coil overs may be 
    too stiff, what about Eibach springs?

Any advice greatefully received.
