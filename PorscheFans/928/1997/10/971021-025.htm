>Eng/Trans number:	M2821-82G      G2810-11G

This is Euro stuff right down the line. M 28.21 is the '85-86 Euro engine
(4.7 liters, 16 valves, 310 DIN bhp) for five-speed cars; 82G is the
beginning of a serial number for that engine ("8" = 8 cylinders, "2" means
non-US, "G" means '86). G 28.10 is the matching transmission and 11G is
similarly the beginning of its serial number ("1" means for an 8-cylinder
engine, "1" means 5-speed, and "G" means '86).

>The only option code listed looks like C00 (Eurospec Emissions).

It's C00 and that's what it means.

Now check the engine (and/or transmission) for stamped codes and see how
they compare to these items from the paper sticker.
