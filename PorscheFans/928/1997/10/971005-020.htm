You may be disappointed with the 928 if you're looking for Japanese-type
reliability.  These are not cars that can be just driven day in and day out
with little upkeep like you can do with most Japanese cars.  These cars
require lots of little (and some big) things to be checked periodically and
you need to stay on top of the maintenance routine.

On the other hand, with a little extra care, this car will tirelessly give
you more pleasure per mile than any other car on the road and will still be
rock solid long after the 240SX's of the world have been recycled.

If little things like switches and electrical connections that occasionally
go bad really bug you, you'd probably be happier with a Japanese car.
However, if you can tolerate the occasional failures that occur on a car of
this complexity, I say go for it.
