Check the build sticker on your nice new car (instructions on Phil Tong's
Web site at http://www.928s4vr.com/; go to the "Option Codes" page. If
you find 474 among the codes, you have the optional sport shocks, which
are quite a bit stiffer than the standard units. If you have these and
don't like them, they can be swapped.


MonacoMy second question
relates to Leonard's car. I remember that several months ago ne mentioned
getting a custom programmed chip for his 928 somewhere on Long Island. As
I hope to "chip" my 928 as well, could I ask where that was and what they
did?



Autosport Designs (http://www.smarttrac.com/autosport/index.htm) put my
car on their chassis dyno, hooked up their computer to the car's fuel and
ignition computers, and proceeded interactively to reprogram both
computers from the ground up to optimize their operation to my taste
(keep the broad torque curve but raise it; sharpen throttle response) for
my specific engine (there are always unit-to-unit variations).


They found me about 50 bhp more without any loss of smoothness, fuel
economy, or any other desirable characteristic. This was done well over a
year ago and I remain most pleased.
