second year of track driving (last year was only 3 days and it rained

I hope the bungee cording was BIG.  I'm not sure our region's tech
inspectors would buy it - please be sure that thing won't come loose in
an 80 mph head-on impact.  If you stop from 80 mph in 4 ft, the
*average* acceleration is over 50 g's meaning that a 5 lb setup exerts
an average force of more than 270 pounds on those bungee cords and the
peak force is no doubt considerably higher.

I really like Phil's setup of a seperate camera - that way the recording
device can be safely bolted somewhere else and the high mounted thing is
really light.

The footage is wonderful - it's really useful to review how the day
went, what you did wrong, and where you might improve and no doubt a
great thing to watch BEFORE showing up at the track the next time.  I
think this is especially important when you don't have an instructor
along for the ride and also for autoX, where you don't get to go through
the turn 500 times before adding serious speed.
