To clean carpets and auto upholstery I use a Bissell Little Green machine. 
 The machine is not so powerful as to shoot solution deep into the seat 
foam or carpet jute, so it dries in a few hours.  It sucks the dirt out 
rather than bury it or mask it with "brightners".  It's small and easy to 
use, setup and clean when done.  Works great on house furniture too.

TIP: Use ScotchGuard as soon as your done and it's still wet.  The 
Scotchguard will bond better and you don't need to use as much of it.  It's 
several $ a can at K-Mart, but one can will do an interior and then some. 

Someone told me of a product called "Harleys" that restored leather.  I 
have no personal experience with it though.
