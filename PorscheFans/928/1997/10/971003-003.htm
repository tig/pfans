Pat:

First thing that snaps to mind is the need to keep the input shaft turning
whenever the car is moving. The oil pump is driven by the input shaft, so
all transmission lubrication ceases when the pump shaft stops turning.
While having a clutch at the front end of the shaft would be interesting, it
would also need to be disabled so that it could not be disengaged.

Other issues like having all that extra rotating mass could also affect the
driveability of the car.

All this is in advance of whether the mechanical bits would even fit.
