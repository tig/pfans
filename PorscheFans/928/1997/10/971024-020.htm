Subject:928's vs. C5 Vette -- Something I wrote
Date:Fri, 24 Oct 1997 16:48:23 -0400
Both nice cars.  I think I paid about $100 for a rebuilt 928 water pump and 
the new one was $320 or so.  To replace it, the shop charged me 13 hours to 
do that and the timing belt.  I did it myself next time around in 9 hours 
and that was being real careful and slow so as not to break any of those 
aluminum parts.  I didn't need another pump 45k later when I did it, the 
belt tensioned gave out and I needed a new belt.  But while I was there it 
seemed like a good idea as it's a big job.

The timing belt does not add much extra time to the job, perhaps 1 hour.  I 
don't know that one should replace the pump without the belt as coolant is 
likely to get on the belt and make a mess when you do the job.

As for reliability, a friend has an '83 US spec 928 with 110k on the 
original water pump and ALL belts/hoses, against my warnings.  Worse case 
he figures is he will need a tow when the belt breaks.  He has done 
virtually nothing other than the usual oil changes, brakes, tires.  He has 
the original clutch to boot.  Very reliable.  My '84 928S has 96K on the 
original clutch and it's going strong too.


Bill Smyth
