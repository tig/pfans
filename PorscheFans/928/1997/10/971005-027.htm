>If there is a AT guru that could comment on the effect of AT stall speed and
>gearing, that would be an interesting discussion topic.

Stall speed is the engine rpm at which the torque converter is doing the
most torque multiplication (typically around 2:1). This should be matched
to the engine's torque characteristics; higher stall speeds usually go with
more "tuned" engines which need to wind up higher before supplying lots of
torque (check the drag racers' choices, e.g.,
http://www.bmracing.com/catalog/fullline/pages/racesel.html).

However, as many on the list have noted, this combination does not give
great results off the line unless you wind up the engine to around this
speed with the brakes applied and then let go, a technique known for
accelerating the aging of torque converters.

Later cars produce more torque at low speeds and are happier with lower
stall speeds.
