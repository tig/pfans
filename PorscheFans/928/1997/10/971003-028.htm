I use 4 jack stands, with a 6" long 2x4 between the jack stand and the
lift point.  (longer or wider block will chow on the shark's soft belly
material!)

Jack up the rear of the car first, one side at a time, jack under the
rear lift point.  When it's high enough to slip the FRONT stand + wood
block under the front lift point, do it....lower it down, do the other
side.

Then, put the jack + 1/2" thick wood block under that honkin' great rear
suspension crossover, and jack up the rear until you can get jack stands
+ wood blocks under both rear lift points.

This works great, although you get mighty tired of dragging the damn
floor jack.  It's always really stable for me, no wobbles....but I have
a garage floor smooth & level as a pool table (well, some pool tables).

Alternatively, you could stack the shop manual binders all the same way,
and make about a 3 foot high ramp.....nah....
