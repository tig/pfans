There are two sensors that control the downshift pattern of the AT.  You will
have a downshift occur based on throttle position and kickdown switch.  The
throttle position is measured, and the corresponding gear is chosen.  Nail
the throttle to the floor and you will also hit the kickdown switch and force
another gear change.  

Ever notice when you start from a standing stop that if you just roll out
gently you will be in 2nd gear, and if you give it a lot of throttle (without
bottoming out the pedal into the switch) you will hit first gear instead?

Read the archives for information on adding your very own "on command"
kickdown switch.  Or maybe it is in the faq by now.
