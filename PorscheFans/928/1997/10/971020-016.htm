What do you mean by handles better?  I prefer the balance the 928 achieved 
between handling and comfort.  For raw speed on a twisty track, I think the 
944 has the edge.  There are also allot more "tuning" options for the 944 
such as shock, spring, swaybar, wheel choices.  So getting exactly what you 
want may be easier.

$4-6k won't buy much in the way of a 928.  Perhaps if you find a senile old 
geezer you may luck out, but otherwise not likely.  I'm not sure what a 
decent 944 goes for these days.  Bear in mind that maintenance is really 
expensive, more so on the 928.  One broken timing belt on a 944 can cost 
you a few grand to fix.

You may want to check into a 924 also, I know they are very cheap.  Their 
age and rust problems may be of concern though depending on what part of 
the country your in.  They can be just as much fun, perhaps more so since 
your initial investment is less you are more likely to do different things 
without the guilt.

Get some catalogs and check part prices.  Ask yourself if you could handle 
a new radiator, exhaust system, computer brain, etc.  Ask on the 944 list 
what common things go wrong and their cost.

I don't want you to have a big letdown, they are great cars, but only if 
you get the right one and can really afford it.  They make lousy 
fixer-uppers.  You may find an old RX7, 280/300Z or Camaro a reasonable 
alternative till you get the cash for a nice clean Porsche.

BTW, be sure to check out insurance costs.  Porsche is on the upper end of 
the scale.  Your age + Porsche = big $.

Bill Smyth
'84 928S
