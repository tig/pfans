Now - for an interesting repair.   Upon handling the back glass after
removal from the hatch, I inadvertantly broke off one of the connectors
soldered onto the glass for the rear window defrost.  I've had similar
troubles in the past and never found a good fix - until now.   While on
a parts run to Radio Shack, I discovered an item they call 'Solder
Tape.'   It is very thin strips of solder about 1/4" by 1/2"........with
a melting point low enough that matches will melt it.   I took two
pieces and placed on the pads of the connector, positioned the connector
on the glass and held it with a flat blade screwdriver, and heated the
tape with a blower.   It is not a hair blower, but one used for paint
removal and heavy-duty hot air applications.   Anyhow, the solder melted
and voila!   It checked out with a volt meter for electrical integrity
and holds mechanically as orginally designed.

Maybe this is old news to most of you, but I've never seen anything
about repair of bonded solder joints on glass.   This technique will
work for a broken connector pin on the back of the side mirror (for
heated mirrors).

Sorry for the long post, but wanted to share this info.
