Jim Stadter wrote:

>   Just go out there and do it!  You'll have a blast.  Get there early enough
>   to walk the course a few times and pay attention to turn in points, apexes,
>   and road surface (watch out for areas with pebbles).  Croutch down at various
>   points while you're walking so that you can view it from the same height that
>   you'll be at when sitting in your car.  Basically, you want to get rid of
>   that "sea of cones" effect that you'll have if you try to drive the course
>   cold.
>
>   If you don't have a harness in your car, take one of those metal clips
>   that are used to lock the seatbelts in place when using a child's car
>   seat.  Cinch your belt tight and put the clip in place.  In AutoX, you
>   tend to take strong G-loads more frequently than a road course, so you
>   don't want to be thrown around the car.  (Not that you would on a road
>   course either...)
>
>   Write off your first run as a fast lap and use it to drive smoothly through
>   the course so that you can get a feel for the layout.  On the next runs
>   (usually there are three for each driver) pick up the speed, BUT BE SMOOTH!
>
>   Once you try AutoXing once, you'll probably be hooked.  Hope you have
>   a good time.
>
> Jim Stadter
