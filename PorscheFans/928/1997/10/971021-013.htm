Oh, by the way (superficial cover for glaring oversight in my previous post):

- the M 28.21 engine in the Euro '86 928S has two valves per cylinder (one
intake and one exhaust; total of 16 valves in the engine) and one cam
(operating both intake and exhaust valves) per bank of cylinders (total of
two in the engine).

- the M 28.43 engine in the US 928S has two valves per cylinder (two intake
and two exhaust; total of 32 in the engine) and two cams per bank of
cylinders (one for the intake valves, one for the exhaust; total of four in
the engine).

The 16V, two cam engines drive the cams with the famous timing belt. The
32V, four-cam engines also have this timing belt, but then also have a
short chain drive on each bank of the V to drive the two cams for that
bank. This chain has its own tensioner, which is the subject of some recent
posts containing its reliability.
