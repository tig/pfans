>I believe the problem is worn guides at the top of the door. Going by =

>at work right now), there are two guides on each window - one near the =
front and
>one near the rear. These are bolted on the inside of the exterior part =
of the door
>shell. They are rubber with a felt-like covering. The outside of the =
window slides
>on these guides and they hold the window from rattling back and forth. =
I've seen
>replacement guides in both the Automotion and 928 International =
catalogs. I don't
>remember how much they are, but do remember thinking that the price =
seemed
>excessive. They shouldn't be too difficult to replace. I think there =

>on each one, and they are accessible with the window down, inner door =
panel off.

Some more thoughts on this:
Roll the window down....look inside and you will see the two Allen bolts =
that hold the=20
window guides (under rubber trim...on top...you really need to remove =
the door panel
to get to it).    It is possible that one of the guides came loose and =
needs to be re-
adjusted and tightened (they slide back and forth to put different =
degrees of pressure on the window).=20

On mine...the felt/rubber cover had broke loose off the window guide =
bracket...so it
needed replaced.  I also recall also that the part was expensive for =
such a simple
thing,  my cheap/quick fix:

1.  Cut piece of rubber hose the width of window guide bracket (around =
3/4 inch).=20
Cut that piece to fit the window guide bracket (around 45 degrees of =
circle...small
piece...but the hose has the curvature to match the original guide =
"bumper").

2.  Tape piece of hose to window guide bracket with electrical tape.

3.  Stick a piece of Velcro(soft side) on outside of electrical tape =
where bumper=20
contacts window.

When you re-install the guide make sure that it pushes against the =
window enough to
keep it from wobbling on the way up....and so that it seals properly =
when shut.
