The short answer is that the real Club Sport (not the cosmetics-only
version), which was sold only in Europe and only in the '88 model year, was
essentially a practice run for the 928 GT;  the same cams, wheels,
suspension parts (M474 shocks), and so forth. The '85-'89 spec book and the
shop manual go into detail about engine numbers, cam profiles, etc.

If the Club Sport is interesting to you, you may be best off buying a GT,
which gets you at least as hot a package in a US-spec car. Putting a Club
Sport or GT together from parts installed in an S4 will cost more than
finding a clean GT.
