As you no doubt know, API and other impartial entities issue specifications
and the fat of a given oil's meeting a given spec is printed right there on
the (I guess you can't call that plastic jug a) can. The problem is similar
that with audio equipment, in that the basic specs don't tell the whole
story, leaving many discernible differences unmeasured.

I suspect that car makers, having compelled oil makers to show compliance
with the basic API specs, could do the same for more advanced test,
including the ASTM four-ball wear test. If the car makers are too timid to
do this, then the tremendous moral authority of the Porsche Fans 928 list
should certainly suffice.
