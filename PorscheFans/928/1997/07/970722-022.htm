     Holley:
     
     I had the same problem with my '82.  Your mechanic is correct, the 
     hose is permanently attached to the injector.  It would be a real pain 
     in the butt to separate it and replace it only to have it start 
     leaking again.  The labor (your's or your mechanic's) would far 
     outweigh the savings.  Also, once the injector has been bathed in fuel 
     for a while, the 2 gaskets ($2) that hold it in place deteriorate 
     quickly and need to be should be replaced at the dame time.  I bought 
     new injectors from Vertex for about $65 each (I think) and was able to 
     install them in about a half hour each.  I had two that started 
     leaking and although I am sure a solid case could be made for 
     replacing all 8 at the same time, my car has been running perfectly 
     for thousands of miles with 2 new ones and 6 originals.
