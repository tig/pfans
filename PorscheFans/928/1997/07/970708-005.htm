Vince Yu wrote:

>>I plan to paint the car myself.  I wonder if there are some solvent that I can use to dissolve the adhesive backing... <<

3M makes Trim Adhesive in both regular and high-temp versions.  The
regular is great for every type of interior trim.  I've used it to
reattach the carpeting at the bottom of the door panels, vinyl and
leather covering on interior panels, etc.  I've never used the High-Temp
Adhesive (the only use I could come up with would be for the
sound-deadening pad on the inside of the hood).  

3M also makes Trim Adhesive Remover.  This is what you need.  I paid
under $8 for a 16oz can.  This solvent is great.  It will dissolve any
adhesive without damaging the paint.  It WILL however, disolve plastic. 
If these "decals" are plastic, you are S-O-L.  But, if they are foil
(like on 911s) or metallic paper, this stuff might just do the trick.

Later, you can use the Trim Adhesive to reattach your door jamb labels.
