So-called short-shifters are common-place amongst Japanese cars.  Most
just move the pivot point of the shift lever up a little which often
doesn't really shorten the throw all that much.  I didn't feel the need
to "dimension the part" or "create any geometrical models" just to saw
off 2" of mild steel shifter lever.  Seems a little extreme for an
experiment that can be cheaply and easily reversed.  My error was in
removing the lever.  It was a real bear for me to get those circlips
back on the lever pins while keeping the springs compressed.
