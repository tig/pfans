Just to add a bit to what's below:  AFN is a large Porsche dealership in
Chiswick, England.  Their phone number (from the US) is
011-44-1-81-742-7700.  In the spring of 1995 they wanted 175 British
pounds, exclusive of VAT and shipping, for the car seat I describe
below.
-----------------------------------------------------------
To sum up, as far as I know no one has come up with a US-made car seat
to fit the rear of a 911.  Some folks have bought them from AFN in the
UK, but AFN now says that the seats available now require 3-point belts.
When we were in Germany October 1995, we bought a car seat from the
factory which fits perfectly in our 1979 911SC, and as a bonus can be
used as a booster seat in any vehicle. This seems to be what AFN is
selling now.  It consists of four pieces, each sold separately:  a seat
bottom, a backrest (that straps to the seat bottom), a shield or table
that the seatbelt passes through and secures the child across the thighs
and midsection, and an insert that fits under the seat bottom
(928/944/968 only).
The parts you need depend on weight ranges:  for 9 kg to 18 kg you need
all the pieces, for 15 kg to 25 kg you don't need the backrest, and for
15 kg to 36 kg you only need the bottom.  The part numbers are: 000
802021002CZ for the seat bottom and 000 802041002CZ for the table.  We
didn't need the backrest, so I don't know that part number or the cost.
The instructions say that you need a 3-point belt, but the salesperson
told us we didn't, so I didn't realize it until I got home.  In use, I
don't see that the 3-point belt is really needed when used with the
table and the seat bottom.  You are supposed to thread the lap belt
through a couple of hooks on the seat bottom, then the table, and then
fasten it to the seat belt buckle.  This secures both pieces of the seat
and fastens the table snuggly against the child's body to keep him/her
from flying forward.  In this respect, it looks much like a typical US
booster seat.  The shoulder portion of the belt comes up through the
center of the table, but that puts it several inches away from the child
depending on his/her size.  I don't see that it does much good there.  A
lap belt only seems to work fine to fasten the table and hold the child
snuggly in the seat.  To me, it looks safer than many US booster seats.
However, I don't think I would want to use this seat with the
inertia-type two-point lap belts that I think were put in the late-model
911s.  They may not allow you to cinch things tightly enough.
For the higher weight range, the seat bottom serves to boost the child
up so the conventional seatbelt is more effective.  You definitely need
a three point belt for this.
The factory told me they would not ship to the US, but maybe things have
changed.  You could try calling the parts department at (0711) 827-0.=20
The table and seat bottom cost us about 85DM each.  If the factory won't
ship, maybe a German dealership will.=20
Sorry for this disclaimer, but:  I doubt the European seats are approved
by DOT, NHTSA, etc.  Also, I am not advocating using the above child
seat against the instructions with only a lap belt.  Do so at your own
risk.
Hope this helps.
