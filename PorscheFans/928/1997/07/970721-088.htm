You wrote:
>How many AAA tow drivers would know that if he jump starts a 928 with a >totally dead battery, then disconnects the booster, he has blown the >alternator?

In my local AAA magazine's Q&A section, an AAA service man responded to
the question: why do you advice to put the rear window heater and the
blower on, just before removing the jump start cables? He answered: (I
quote) "modern cars are loaded with sensitive electronics like the
injection computer and ABS computer. They often can't handle high
voltage peaks. When jump starting the empty battery draws current and
the alternator works at full load. But at first the empty battery does
not (here I think he should have deleted the word not, Math) take the
current very well, the current remains high. When taking off the jumper
cables, the current interruption causes a voltage spike, possibly
harming electronics. The blower and rear window heater are large current
consumers and thereby dim the voltage spike."
