Re JUMPING:

How to safely jump start with a dead battery without having the same effe=
ct as disconnecting the battery while
the engine is running?

Do you all agree that either of the two following procedures would not =
damage the
alternator provided the 928's own battery is chargeable? With both proced=
ures the idea is to have sufficent
charge in the 928's battery when the booster is disconnected.

1.  Leave the booster connected to the 928 battery for a while before try=
ing to start.

After the booster has been connected a while, disconnect it, try the head=
lights. If they are bright, the 928's battery must have accepted at least=
 some charge. Now you hope you know that when you disconnect the booster,=
 the 928's
battery should have enough charge to prevent alternator damage. Then reco=
nnect the booster, wait a while
again (to replace the charge just drained by the headlights (and to ponde=
r the cost of an alternator?)), and
start. Then disconnect the booster.

If  the headlights are not bright, try some more charging from the booste=
r. If you can't eventually get the
headlights bright when only on the 928's battery, better not start. Get =
a new battery or find out why else it
isn't charging, like maybe a short.

2.  Start as soon as the booster is connected.

Leave the booster connected a while after the engine is started, to, hope=
fully, put some charge into the 928's battery before disconnecting the =
booster.

Personally, I think #1, above, would be safer.

As for whether it is a good idea to turn on something for current drain =
before starting, I don't know. There is nothing in the laws of electricit=
y to prevent designing a circuit so that that would be a good idea. Howev=
er, the circuit could also be designed the other way: so that more curren=
t drain caused more damage. The latter seems more intuitive, but I don't =
have enough confidence in that intuition to bet an alternator on it. To =
be sure, I suppose one would have to study, and perhaps experiment with, =
the diagram and the specs of all the components, at least I would.

However, for what its worth, if I ever have to jump start my 928, thus =
I have to bet one way or the other, I will not turn on anything, except =
as described in procedure #1, above.
