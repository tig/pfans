It really depends on the type and brand of the paint; urethane, laquer,
enamel etc. Some paint uses a catalyst to harden it and some don't.
Usually if paint is exposed to the air and heat, it will dry up.  I take
a piece of cardboard and cut it the same size diameter of the paint can.
Then I shove the cardboard into the paint can so that it will expell out
the air and slightly touches the surface of the paint.  I then place a
sheet of plastic (saran wrap) over the can before I pound the lid on.
If you store it this way, it will last a long time.

My first experience with painting cars date back in the hot roddin' days
and working part-time in a body shop.  No longer am a body man.  You can
rent a paint booth for about $50/hr. or call your local community
college's auto shop.
