(the ability of a liquid to hold gas in solution drops with rising

A nice example of this is a glass of cold water left on a table for a
while. When it warms up to room temperature, you see little bubbles on the
inside of the glass. This is air which had been dissolved in the water; its
absence changes the taste of the water (which is "flat" with no dissolved air).
