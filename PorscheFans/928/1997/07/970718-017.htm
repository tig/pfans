Now to decipher your timeslip.  The only things that affect MPH are shift
points and HP.  The driver has almost nothing to do with MPH.  I took
a street start launch on my second run and ran 15.377 @ 102.6 so you can
see the MPH will be +-2mph.  For each 1mph you want to go up, you generally
need 10hp in this weight range of cars.

The first 60' is everything in drag racing.  Being that we are turbo cars,
it's hard for us to leave the line well.  The best 60' time you can
realistically get in a RWD street tire equipped car is 1.950.  For every
1/10th you drop off 60', you multiply by 2X at the end for RWD cars and
3X for AWD cars.

So, theortically, your car is capable of: 2.191 - 1.950 = 0.241 X 2 = 0.482
Take your ET of 14.245 - 0.482 = 13.763 @ 99mph.  That would be the best
run possible in your car if the MPH does not change.

For the 944T guys, assuming a 103.3mph, the best ET we can get is:
2.264 - 1.950 = 0.314 X 2 = 0.628.  Take the ET 13.960 - 0.628 = 13.332.

For my car, it would be: 2.379 - 1.950 = 0.429 X 2 = 0.858.  Take the ET
and you get 14.086 - 0.858 = 13.228.  Notice how close the two 944T's
come even though they have quite different runs.
