If you plan to perform ANY maintenence yourself (other than changing oil
and filter) buy the real thing.  You will be glad you spent the $275 for
the real 9 vol manual set the first time you have to do a non-trivial
repair.  Actually even the trivial can get VERY expensive if you try to
remove a part incorrectly and break it.

BTW, I bought the fiche set for around $20 - not $85, but later
exchanged it for the real manual set.

If you have an early car <1980 and don't care about updates, a used 928
Manual Set would be a good way to go.  They have only 6 vols, and don't
cover the 32v engine.
