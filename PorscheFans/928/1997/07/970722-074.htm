I've probably jump-started my 928 over a dozen times (about 3 weeks of
non-use is all it takes to discharge).  I just use my truck with the engine
running and some jumper cables to charge the battery for about 10 minutes,
then hop in the 928 and fire it up (with the truck still connected and
running). It's worked flawlessly every time.  If anyone can convince me why
I'm on borrowed time with this method, I'm listening.  I suppose there
could be some interaction between the two vehicles voltage regulators but
the connection is not strong enough to generate damaging currents in the
short time they're connected.  By the time I disconnect the cables from the
928 right after I start it, the battery has a good charge so spikes are
minimal.  Flame away.
