> 2) After driving for about 30 minutes the red "!" light came on.  I wondered
> if this was the low fuel indicator as I was almost empty, but when I filled
> it up the light stayed on.  The next time that I drove it it stayed off...
> I noticed this lights up when the hand brake is engaged, so it must be a
> multi-purpose warning light, either that, or it is malfunctioning...  Is it
> an overheating light...

This is a multi-purpose light.  If it lights up for a "minor" problem,
you
can cancel the alarm by pressing the indicator on the center console. 
If
it is a major alarm (ie timing belt tension), pressing the senter
console
light will not cancel the alarm.  There should be another light going on
indicating what is the problem with the car.  If it is the belt tension,
get it fix ASAP.
 
> 3)  My rear hatch won't stay up - and it's bloody heavy to hold up with one
> hand when trying to load/unload stuff from the back.  Are the two shocks on
> the sides what lifts and holds up the rear hatch or is there also a spring
> mechanism somewhere.

No other mechanism than the 2 rear shock.  They are easily replaced
after
you remove the little clip that holds the ends in place.  The original
shock have these little wire retaining clips that almost look like they
are spring loaded and would give way if you pulled on the shock.  DON"T
do this!!  Use a pair of pliers and remove the clip THEN take the shock
out.
