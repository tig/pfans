If you ever install an amp in the hump of a 928 that did not come with the 
optional factory booster, make sure you cut out the vent holes in the 
carpet that is glued to the hump. If you look inside the hump you will see 
the pre-punch holes in the ends of the hump, this will make cutting the 
carpet to factory specs a "snap". Be conservative with the hole size in the 
carpet, it doesn't have to be quite as large as the pre-punched holes.

P.S. I do have a Late Model 928 factory 6-channel amp (4ch plus 2ch Sub) 
that is used with the Factory 10 Speaker system in case you are interested 
in Factory Original "stuff".
