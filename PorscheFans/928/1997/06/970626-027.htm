The way the list is sent out seems a bit inefficient to me.  My suggestion:
 rather than having the whole list blasted out to all the subscribers, why
not use an email to html converter that takes an "inbox", creates links to
all the mail messages and allows you to view messages sorted by subject,
author or date.  These converters also allow "threading" of messages, which
groups "replies" to messages together with the original message.  The
addition of search capabilities would be trivial (e.g. use a perl script
that searches through html files on the server for a match -- several
exist).  Users could then browse for info they wanted.

The one converter I know of is called "hypermail" and its free.  We used
this internally at my previous job with great sucess.  Find it at:
http://www.eit.com/software/hypermail/hypermail.html

I'm sure other packages exist, as well.
