Belly pan

Is there any possibility of damaging the car by performing a compression

I spent about two hours with it today and uncovered only four problems:

*Sunroof not working.  (It worked two weeks ago.)

*Hairline cracks in windshield near lower left and lower right corners

*"Brake Pad" light on  (This was preceded by the ! light.  When I 
pressed it, the "Brake Pad" light remained on.  The brakes are not 
squealing, and when I mashed them at 40 MPH, they almost threw me out.  
The car has ABS.)

*Moisture around the fog light

*Cracked tailight lens

The roaring noise from the rear of the car I heard last time I drove it 
was gone.  (Road surface?)  The transmission shifts wonderfully, perhaps 
the best of any car I've ever driven.  There are no dents except a minor 
door ding.  The chin spoiler is not cracked or scratched.  There are 
very small cracks in the paint on the car's nose and it's a bit faded 
there.  The interior is perfect.  All the serial numbers on the car's 
parts match.  None of the wiring under the hood or dash appears to have 
been monkeyed with.  The owner's manual, spare tire and all the tools in 
the toolkit are present.  The owner has every record since 3/27/87, and 
the cambelts have been changed.  It was serviced regularly by German 
Auto Imports in Atlanta and they stamped the owner's manual each time.  
There's very little grime on top of the engine and no oil.  However, 
some of the paint is worn from the bar between the strutts and the cam 
covers.  One thing that worries me is that the owner only changed oil 
every 15000 miles from the initial 700 mile oil change to about 45000 
miles.  After that it was every 4000 or so.  If this had produced 
damage, do you think it would have shown up by now?

The original owner was an Alabama MD, and the second owner lived in
