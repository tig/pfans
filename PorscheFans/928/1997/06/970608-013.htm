 I have a couple of 928s; a red one fully prepared for
autocross/time-trials,
and one highly modified black street machine (plus tons of spare
parts, engines, etc). =20
 I'll skip my autocrosser (this one is fully sorted), and talk about the=20
black one ("Porscheneggar"). Based on a 1986 US model, it has the full=20
DP wide-body conversion, and a 32 valve, supercharged engine.  Again, I
won't=20
waist time (bandwidth) talking about body/suspension stuff--so here's
the=20
engine/drive train:
 1) Camden (Roots-type) supercharger.
    a) 7.5lbs (measured) boost.
    b) 15 lb pop-off valve (safety from intake backfires).
    c) Four 90lb injectors, running on top of SC (wet).
 2) Pistons are milled, for a static CR of 7.5:1.
 3) Custom aluminum manifold.
 4) Massively ported heads.
    a) Larger valves.
 5) 100mm throttle-body, breathing through 12" x 6" K&N.
 6) Electromotive TEC II, running SuperBlend=AE software.
    a) Crank trigger.
    b) TPS sensor.
    b) MAP sensor.
    c) MAT sensor.
    d) Coolant sensor.
    e) Coil per cylinder (no machanical distributor).
 7) MSDS headers, jet-hot coated.
    a) Dual 2.5" exhausts.
    b) Dual 2.5"/2.5" Edelbrock.
    c) Balance tube.
    d) No Cats. (Yes, it's pretty ferocious sounding)
 8) External oil-cooler mounted in front of the A/C condenser.
 9) Custom double-disk, clutch.
 10) Limited-slip.
 11) 345's on rear.
 12) High-capacity electric radiator fan, with custom shroud.
 13) Large, functional, custom hood scoop, directed towards windshield.

 Although I have not dyno'd it yet, it has an immense amount of
torque--I estimate 450-500+ ft-lbs, although the HP does seem a bit down
(?)...

  I am now in the final planning stages of a TT conversion--and since
there
are damn few people in this "area," it would have been nice to exchange=20
some data--maybe even consult with some others.
 Although I have "completed" the overall design, at any point, if I find
a better way from someone else to do some part of it, I'll modify as I
go.=20
 "Stage I":
 1) Keep what I have right now, as far as the engine. (It's been pretty
reliable at 7.5 lbs boost for a year.)
 2) Relocate alternator above crank, just on top of water neck--also
convert to smaller/lighter racing type.
 3) Custom-make a "log-type" header for left bank feeding turbo where
alternator used to be.
 4) Custom-make a "log-type" header for right bank feeding turbo located
located in region in front of passenger floorboard (lots of room).
 5) Use region where air-pump used to be for ducting.
 6) Put K&N air filters behind/under each headlight. Left one ducted to
left turbo, etc.
 7) Move radiator back about 3" (I will obviously be removing the custom
drive pulley.)
 8) Move A/C condensor forward a little, about 1/2 inch.
 8) Custom 3" thick intercooler made by my friend (Todd Knighton) at
Protomotive Engineering--based on same one they use on their 700hp 911
upgrades.  Mount between A/C condensor and radiator.
 9) Duct each of the turbos output underneath radiator to common plenum.
Intercooler output ducted up, over radiator through the two factory
fresh-air ducts back to air-flow meter.
10) Replace custom blower intake manifold with original factory intake
runners/manifolding/plenums/injectors (injectors upgraded, though).
11) Utilize two K26 turbos (already have), run this at 10-12lbs.
12) Utilize the Electromotive in it already.

"Stage II":
 1) Replace milled pistons with forged-aluminum J&E's, maybe go up to 8
to 8 1/2:1.
 2) Carillo Rods, or equivalent.
 3) Redrill crank for >6500 RPM oil problem.
 4) O-ring heads.
 5) Possibly brace tops of sleeves.
 6) Semi-custom copper head gasket from PowerHaus (modification of 944
version).
 7) 15+ lbs boost.

 I have spent many, many hours thinking, measuring, re-thinking,
re-measuring,=20
and this seems to be my current, best solution(s). Again, I (and
Porscheneggar)
would appreciate any ideas/comments anyone might have, as I am ready to
begin=20
the real assembly/fabrication now.
