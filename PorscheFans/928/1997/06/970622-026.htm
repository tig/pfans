        When I read your post it seems to me that you have a classic case of
semi TOAST batter syndrome.  Usual symtoms are.. car starts slugishly,
voltage is low at idle and shoots up when reved above idle.  The fault is
that the battery isn't taking a charge correcly, due to either low
electrolite levels (the battery acid), the electrolite level became low and
has permadently damaged the battery or that the metal plates inside the
batter have become saturated with "debris".  To test my hypothesis just
remove your old battery and just set in "temperaroly" a "good" battery and
start the car.  Check the voltage readings again to see if they appear
"normal".  Measuring with a proper volt meter to the batter when the car is
running at idle you should find a reading of 12.7-13.8 volts.  

BTW- for all you other 928'ers I'd recomend NOT idleing too long with a lot
of equipment running, ie on a hot raining night running headlights, AC,
wipers, and sitting in the drive way listing to the soothing Purr of your
928.  Because it is idleing the votage regulator can not compensate for the
electrical demand of the vehicle for extended periods.
