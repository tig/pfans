Yup, I changed coolant before these events and probably ended up near
60% water and 40% antifreeze.  Griff Miller responded that the
antifreeze lubes the waterpump seal - it also raises the boiling
temperature.  The potential for boiling fluid is really bad because
local hot spots form a vapor which reduces the cooling at the hot spot
so that it heats more and ....  So I suppose if you can be sure that
pure water will conduct enough, you'd win but it's probably a risky
game.
