I love that one. It's the law in Germany anyway, but there are numerous
"environmentalists" and other "teachers" there, who see faster drivers as
"energy wasting bastards". They almost pretend to "own" the left lane and
want to teach us a lessen about what they consider "sensible driving". While
these drivers could be ticketed, those who tailgate or pass on the right can
get into even more trouble (suspension of license, etc.), if caught. 

It's nasty game, and today German police moitors traffic with unmarked police
cars. These can be anything fast, including powerful VW Golf GTIs, Audis,
BMWs, Mercedes and Porsches. I have personally seen a grey-metallic Audi 100
with a flip-up camera between the seats and a photo camera on the dash
(followed and photographed me a couple of years back), and a beige 944, but I
know they have several 911s now! They don't go after minor violations. So if
they stop you, they have proof of your violation on video, and might take
your license on the spot!

Unfortunately the Autobahn is no longer paradise - it's only some stretches,
mainly in states with a conservative government, and mainly on Sunday
mornings. The rest is either speed limited (96% of German roads are limited)
or too busy. An interesting innovation: Around Stuttgart they invented the
"intelligent road", a system that monitors traffic and weather conditions,
and sets the speed limit for each individual lane to maximize traffic
capacity and safety.

Sorry to hit you with this kind of reality. If it makes you feel better,
think about the Merceded Benz test drivers, who believed there was no speed
limit in Montana anymore, and got ticketed for going at "Autobahn speeds"...
