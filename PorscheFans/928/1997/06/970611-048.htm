>Hilsch vortex tubes are typically run at pressures up to 10
>atmospheres. They are supposed to be noisy as all hell.

Yes, noisy for it's size (8" long), about like 4 air nozzles running at once.

The trick to their function seems to be letting the right amount of air out
of the hot side, my tube has a threaded end that is essentially a flow
valve.  Lowest flow = Hottest air.  Some of the air spinning around the
inside walls of the tube is released out one end, but there is ANOTHER
outlet.. The remainder of the air reverses direction
(ithinkbutdontquoteme)and cruises to the other end of the tube at a lower
pressure and temp..
