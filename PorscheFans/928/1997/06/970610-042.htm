Hi All!

Another fault down. I fixed my driver's side electrically adjustable mirror
today. The fault was a connector terminal that had come out of its socket.
Whilst I had the mirror apart, I examined the workings and was amazed to see
an electric motor, a gearbox, two cable drums and a solenoid operated
selector to arrange that the motor drives one drum or the other. These drums
ran cord through pulleys to move the flat plate that the mirror is attached
to. In addition to this, the mirror itself has a four and a half watt
heating element in it (31 ohms resistance). Very neat!
