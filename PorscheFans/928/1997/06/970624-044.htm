> Just thought that I would throw in my 2c worth on this topic.
> I have had Alfa Romeos for many years and they have a timing chain to run
> around their twin overhead cam sprockets. They are very reliable and have a
> tensioner to take up any stretch. As one other person on this list
> commented, the timing chains are usually replaced at an engine rebuild.
> My Suzuki Vitara 4WD has a 2 litre quad cam V6. It has three! timing chains.
> There is a little one between each pair of camshafts and a main one doing
> the round from crankshaft to camshafts. I have a warm and fuzzy feeling that
> these little buggers are going to go round and round for the life of the engine.
> My 928 has a cheaper and quieter rubber belt but the consequences of it
> breaking are hardly worth thinking about. For a few extra dollars on a very
> expensive car, coupled with a bit more engine noise that I wouldn't hear
> anyway due to the voluminous sound deadening in the 928, give me internal
> timing chains any day! Peace of mind.
> Now, if you want timing gear noise, go for gears! I was near a Datsun
> factory rally car years ago and it sounded like the motor was shot!
