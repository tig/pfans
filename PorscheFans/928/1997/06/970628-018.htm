A quick note about the O2 sensor...it almost caused a fire in the fuse
panel of my car when the +12v wire leading to the sensor chafed against the
exhaust heat sheild and shorted.  I had to roll down the windows and pull
off the road immediately and shut everything down as smoke was pouring out
from the passenger footwell.  According to the schematic in the shop
manual, that circuit is not fused.  Some of the wires burned almost
completely in two in less than a minute before I was able to stop the car.
Needless to say, when I replaced the wiring, I put a fuse in series with
it.  If the protective tubing leading from the sensor is intact (mine
wasn't), you shouldn't have this problem, but it wouldn't hurt to look
under there and make sure everything looks ok.

Seems like they could have fused the O2 sensor ckt and put a warning lamp
on the dash to indicate a failure.
