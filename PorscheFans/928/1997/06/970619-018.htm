the "Belt Tension" warning from time to time.  After having the tension
checked a number of times on a few cars I finally just ignored it.  I have

You would have to be nuts to ignore the timing belt tension light, that is
unless you
wish to completely rebuild of your engine (hehehe). If it is an 1986 go
right ahead, ignore it,
it will just make my 1986 928s more endangered and rare (hehehe) The cost
of having
it checked out and adjusted is very little compared to the cost of
replacing your engine.
I bought a 1986 928s two years ago. The lot that I purchased it from told
me that the light was on because
the fan belt needed replacing ( I have a thing about red lights, Thank
Porsche).
I took it to my mechanic "Manfred", and he replaced the belt, about 1
months later
I was racing some teenager in a Mustang on the Hollywood freeway and I
punched it and the light came on
again, I panicked and drove immediately to the shop (the owners manual
recommends smooth acceleration).
I took it to Manfred for an adjustment and viola!,hasn't came on since.
He told me that the belt needs to be broken-in and readjusted after so many
 mile, more so when you
replace it. A brake warning light is very different from a timing light.
Have it checked out.
Its kind of scarey to know that a rubber belt can be the difference between
 joy and castastrophe.
