I used a 5 amp fuse.  The DC impedance of the O2 sensor ckt (circuit)
measured about 2.5 ohms cold.  I figured the impedance probably would
increase significantly as the sensor heated, decreasing current draw.
Sensors generally are not big users of power.  So far, after 2 years, the 5
amp fuse is still intact.  I'm sure the short-ckt current was far greater
than 5 amps considering how quickly it melted the wires.  One interesting
thing...I drove the car for weeks w/o the O2 sensor and did not notice any
change in engine behavior or fuel mileage.

I replaced the idle valve because I was having trouble with the engine
stalling whenever I took my foot off the gas and my old mechanic said the
valve was probably at fault.  The engine would drop below the normal idle
point and sometimes catch itself, most times not.  I've seen some others on
this list with a similar problem.  It turned out that after going through
the contortions necessary to change that valve, it wasn't the problem.  My
new mechanic fixed the problem in about 2 minutes by tweaking up the idle
speed.  The engine now runs smooth as butter.
