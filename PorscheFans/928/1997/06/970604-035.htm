I'll put my diver hat on and if I remember rightly 1 bar = 1 atmosphere
pressure at sea level. For a diver @ 33 feet the pressure is 2 bar. 1 for
the atmosphere and 1 for the 33 feet of water.

 5 bar pressure is equivalent to the weight of water @ 165 feet ( 5 * 33 )
which in PSI is around 72. 

The bar to psi is approximately 14.5 

I believe its a metric (barometric??) measure of pressure which has a lot of
favour in Europe.

I think I've got it right but will happily stand corrected if I've misled
the 'philes.
