Jim,
First the color of the shocks will be very apparent once the dust is 
removed, the color is from head to toe of the shock "body".

The adjustment collars slide over your shock and rest on the same "raised 
stops" that your bottom spring plate normally rests on.
They consist of 3 parts, the inner threaded "ring" that slides down to the 
"stops" on the shock. This piece makes your shock tube like a "bolt" thread 
(about 2" of thread). The outer collar then simply threads up and down on 
this "bolt" thread like a nut. This same outer collar supports the bottom 
spring plate so as you thread the outer collar up and down the "bolt 
thread" it moves the bottom spring plate (and the spring) up and down , 
thus changing height.  I hope this makes sense!!
