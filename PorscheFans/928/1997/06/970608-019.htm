Bob,
 In Vol. "VII" of "UP-FIXIN der PORSCHE"  from PCA they discuss warm start 
problem with early "CIS" fuel injection systems. The culprit seems to be a 
tiny Check Valve in your Fuel Pump, it sticks and causes your problem, it 
is very common. There are 2 solutions: #1 replace fuel pump with new pump 
that was updated in 1981, all new pumps that you get for your car will be 
of this version(this updated  pump has a external screw in check valve, 
instead of a built in check valve). #2 You can buy the external check valve 
assembly and screw it into your existing pump(I think because of your car's 
age I would get the new pump). the check valve part # is 810.906.093 plus 
cap nut part # N011 0691. There is a possibility someone could have already 
installed a later version of the pump. Take a look at it, if it has just a 
long smooth nipple with just a hose clamped on it (between the electrical 
connectors)  that is the old style, if it has a threaded nipple with a 
compression nut,  that is the new style (the threaded nipple contains the 
check valve, above part #) and you could just replace the nipple/check 
valve. If you get the new pump make sure you get a new check valve, they 
are separate parts. If you need help locating the pump or others questions 
let me know .
