> 
> The far better way to adjust ignition timing, along with all other
> operating parameters of the engine, is to reprogram the ignition and fuel
> computers. This is even less for the faint of heart, in that it requires an
> intimate knowledge of the multi-dimensional control "map" used by these
> computers to set parameters based on operating conditions and control
> positions, and is best done interactively with the car on a chassis dyno.
> 
> There are a few shops actually capable of doing this on a custom basis
> (Autosport Designs, http://www.smarttrac.com/autosport/index.htm, did it
> very successfully for my car). I recommend that you do whatever you're
> going to do of a mechanical nature (e.g., air filter, exhaust, headers,
> cams, stroker cranks) first and then take the car to an expert for computer
> reprogramming. Not only will this take all the mechanical mods into
> account, it will also permit optimization to your taste (e.g., broad torque
> curve v. high rpm peak). Generic chips do neither of these.
>
