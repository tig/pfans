WRONG! Get your car in NOW for retensioning!

A new belt will stretch in early use and the array of authorities I respect
recommend retensioning between 1000 and 3000 miles after installation and
initial tensioning. The dealer may be bound by a P&L warranty on the belt
replacement, but they will not smilingly rebuild your engine on their
nickel when the belt suddenly slips enough to jump a tooth or several and
valves meet pistons. Yes, the warning light will come on then, but that
won't help.

The 15,000 mile interval may be a good one for retensioning thereafter, but
NOT for the initial retensioning.
