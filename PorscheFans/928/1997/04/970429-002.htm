I think some questions about the relay in the headlight circuit have come up.  
In any circuit, sizing wiring, switches, and relays will be based on what is 
consuming the electricity.  In this case, it's the headlights.   A wire with 
sufficient size to carry the necessary amperage will then be specified.  A 
fuse will be put into the circuit to protect the circuit against faults.  An 
over current situation will "blow" the fuse ending the flow of current.  If 
anything in the circuit fails, it had better be the fuse -- else, carry a good 
fire extinguisher.
