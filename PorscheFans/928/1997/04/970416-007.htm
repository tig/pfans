My 83 had the same problem, what i found was that the water valve =
located right underneath the air cleaner assembly(you have to take the =
assembly off to see it) had a pinhole leak in the vacuum diaphragm. =
Unfortunately, the default position for it is open(heating). Allyou will =
need is a good vacuum tester, b/c you can see the little plastic arm =
move without removing the valve. If your summer is anything like =
mine(Florida) then you may just opt to unsnap the vacuum diaphragm and =
move the valve shut w/ your finger, otherwise, check for vacuum when it =
is supposed to be cool.
