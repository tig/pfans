Check the two edge connectors on the bottom of the instrumentation 
cluster, one on each side.  Clean the contacts if necessary.  If that 
doesn't solve the problem, then you need to remove the cluster and the 
flexible printed circuit board.  You'll see a few rivets close to the 
center of the board.  You need to make sure that these rivets are 
tightly clamped against the copper traces, making a good connection.  
This is a known problem with early 928s.  The plastic printed circuit 
board shrinks given time and eventually will loose the electrical 
connection from the rivets.  Good luck.
