 Actually there is a relay, and then a fuse for each of the four
individual filaments. In truth the "relay" is a complicated bugger,
with three separate relays in one can. One concern would be the
rating on the relay contacts. At least if you fry it, it's easy
(if expensive) to replace; it's down there under the passenger's
feet, just like all the rest of the usual suspects. You don't
have to worry about damaging the headlight switch or the dimmer
switch, they just control the relays.
