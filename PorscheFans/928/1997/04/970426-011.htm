> I am also interested in hearing from anyone that has tried the low
> restriction K&N air filter (45% cfm increase). Does this come at the
> expense of sucking sand into my expensive engine?
> 

The K&N touts superior filtration vs. standard paper elements - and it gets
better comparatively the dirtier it gets.  I have mixed feelings regarding
the performance aspect.  The dyno test didn't show much improvement (with
other mod's) but then the car was static and not getting any
ram-air/cold-air benefit from speed.  Where I did see real improvement was
at the track - my 928 S ran 15.0's all day which is 0.50 sec faster than


manufacturer data in the manual!

In any event, the less foreign matter our engines breathe the better, so
the K&N is a definate must have for any enthusiast for sure...  Cool
sticker for the airbox too, I bet we all have 'em in the same spot!

> Has anyone tried the splitfire plugs (Performance products page 6) or
> sparkplug indexing on a 32 valve 928?  Any advantages to either of these.
> 

Don't do it (Splitfires).  Those are the plugs I ran for the dyno test. 
Everyone there warned me about them possibly self-destructing in the
engine, especially in boosted engines.  I switched to Bosch platinums
immediately thereafter and felt a change in throttle crispness and went on
to do great 1/4 mile runs for a car with 145k miles!   

The price differential alone would let you have fresh plugs in your car
always.

Another 928'r claims the only plug worth investing in is those
mileage-masters with the full ring electrode not just a L-shape like usual
but I have no personal experience there.  Yet.  :)
