I don't own a chipped 928 so I'm really saying this just for what it's 
worth.  EPROMs do have a data retention life; somewhere in the 50 years 
range.  This is assuming that the chip is not exposed to strong UV 
light thru the window.  I routinely erases EPROMs just by putting them 
on my car's dash to let it expose to sunlight while driving around.  It 
would be erased in about 45 minutes.  The time would be shorter if the 
EPROMs have been reused a few dozen times as well as the ability to 
retain data.  So I trash them after 40 reprogramming.

Now, if Authority uses surplus EPROMs for re-sale, (you can find them 
surplus for about 50 cents a piece...) then I would question the 
reliability of its data retention capability.  How do you know if the 
EPROMs are new?  Well, you really don't know unless a manufacturing 
date is stamped on it.  Something to think about...
