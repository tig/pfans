I had the same problem and finally found that it was the rear wiper
mechanism that was making the noise.

When you open the hatch, just left of center on the body, there's a little
metal "finger" that fits into a slot on the bottom of the wiper assembly
located on the hatch itself when the hatch is closed.  The finger and the
slot it goes into are both metal, so when you rub the two together (if your
hatch moves even the smallest bit during your drive) you'll get noise.

I took a very small amount of grease and lightly coated the finger and
cleaned off the excess.

No more squeaks!
