Let me explain.  On my car, there is a wiring harness that connect to
the
positive terminal on the passenger fender.  The wires goes in front of
the
engine and down to the oil pressure gauge, alternator and other misc.
component
(something like 10 or 12 wires).  In my car, this harness is in the way
of my right
side snorkel and it prevent connecting the snorkel to the timing belt
cover.

So, this is where I went wrong.  I thought that the harness should be
located behind
the timing belt housing in a similar fashion as the wires going to the
alternator.
The only way to place the wires in that position is to remove the cam
gear and rotate
the rear T-Belt housing cover.  Well, the cam gear does not have a
"woodruf"(?) key
and is only held in position with 3 screws that let you adjust the gear
position with
+- 5 degree or so.  My problem is that I did not mark the position of
the gear before I
removed it.  When I put it back on, it was kind of a guess to put it
exactly where it
was before.  My mechanics tells me that I was close but I am a little
off.

The only way of adjusting the timming is to take the t-belt off and the
valve
cover off, put the car a #6 TDC and measure the cam position.  You can
then set
the cam gear.

Well, I am not going to track the car this year and I will keep it this
way until
I need to get the belt retension.  At this point, I will get my mechanic
to take the 
belt off and fix the timing.
