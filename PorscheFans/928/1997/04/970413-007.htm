Found some reccommended reading:

"Project 928: The Making of an Autocross Car", by Tom Green GGR, 5/86 in
volume VII of Up-Fixin der Porsche (384 pages!)   These are complete
technical reprints of the 1984-1986 issues by the PCA.  

This article details in depth the trials and tribulation of setting up a
'79 928 for track wars - suspension settings, shocks, springs, swaybars,
tires, you name it.  Of course YMMV on your own particular car / wants /
tires!  but there are some good general guidelines to follow.  

These books are great reference and there's practically gold inside with
all the tips by Kim Crumb, Bruce Anderson, et al.

Phil-bob sez check 'em out!
