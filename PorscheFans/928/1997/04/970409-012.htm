>      Responding to you inquiry on bolt torque on the many 6mm bolt on the
>      water pump.  My manuals show 8 Newton meters +2 Nm.  I would follow
>      that specification because the Silica enriched aluminum block casting
>      is VERY brittle and it is possible to pull the threads right out of
>      the bore..  Also BE SURE TO "CHASE" (CLEAN WITH A TAP) the threads in
>      the block.  BE SURE TO USE A LIGHT OIL AS YOU RE-TAP THE THREADS.  BE
>      REAL CAREFUL ON THIS STEP.  USE A LIGHT HAND AND FEEL YOUR WAY ALONG.
>      IF POSSIBLE PURCHASE NEW BOLTS IF YOU OBSERVE ANY RUST OR CORROSION ON
>      THE BOLT.  WHEN YOU ARE READY TO RE ASSEMBLE THE PUMP, USE A ANTI-GALL
>      COMPOUND ON THE BOLT THREADS.  THIS IS MESSY BUT WORTH ALL THE MESS.
>      ALSO IT IS NOT ADVISED TO USE A SILICON (RTV) SEALANT ON THE GASKET.
>      I used a very light coat of non hardening aircraft sealant.
