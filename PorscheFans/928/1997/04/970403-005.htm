I would think that the Fuel injection system would be affected (L-LH
Jetronic).

The air-meter applies electrical resistance to the brain depending on
the volume of air flowing into the motor. This in conjuction with the
throttle body position and engine speed, provide the main parameters for
the brain to determine the correct lenght of time for the fuel injectors
to stay open. The meter flap is spring loaded. 

I feel that cramming a lot of air into the flap would cause it to be
pegged back depending on the forward speed of the car.

This would give the brain bad numbers, preventing it from doing its job
of precise fuel delivery.

The addition of a high flow air filter is OK, for the FI  system
measures the air being SUCKED into the motor. It will suck more air and
the meter will read it, and the brain will adjust to deliver the fuel.

The ram air induction would be better off in a car with CIS Injection.
