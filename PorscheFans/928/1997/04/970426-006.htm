>I am interested in hearing from anyone that has insight into the
>alternative computer chips available for a 928...  more specifically the 5
>L 32v engine.  As far as i can tell there are two alternatives:
>AutoThority and SuperChips.  I am concerned about adverse effects, tuning,
>fuel consumption and obviously,the performance increase.

After shopping the generic chips, I decided to spend about $400 more than
Autothority's price to get a full custom reprogramming of both computers
(fuel and ignition) with the car on a chassis dyno. This produced:

  - a solid (ca. 50 bhp) power boost from my engine in its exact condition
and state of tune (no two engines are identical, which is why people report
widely varying results from generic chips)

  - much crisper throttle response

  - a torque curve with the shape I wanted (basically the stock curve, just
higher at all rpm > 3000); this would be difficult to get from generic chips

>I am also interested in hearing from anyone that has tried the low
>restriction K&N air filter (45% cfm increase). Does this come at the
>expense of sucking sand into my expensive engine?

This was the first change I made to my car, and I like it. It produced a
noticeable improvement in "snap", and has not caused any evident problems
in several years of operation. Others on this list have had similar results.
