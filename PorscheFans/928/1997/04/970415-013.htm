The physical connection to the 928's computer is your last worry.  
Chances are that the 928 computer is a serial interface; which mean 
that you can talk to it using a UART chip (e.g. 16450).  You need to 
find out what IEEE interfacing standard it is using.  Then based on 
that standard, determine the software protocol in order to "talk" to 
the 928 brain.  (Hope it's not Burrough bi-sync)  On the laptop side, 
you need to write a low-level driver to poll the serial port; 
preferably by hardware interrupt.  The polling would intercept the 
commands sent via the 928's brain and parse it out, process it and 
display accordingly.  All this is done within your custom-written 
application.

Roughly speaking, and assuming that you have all the technical 
specifications available to do the job, you're dealing with 
approximately 6-12 months of development.
