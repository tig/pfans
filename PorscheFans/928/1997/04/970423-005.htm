The cheapest way to find out is to buy the little spec book for 
less than $10.  HOWEVER, you want to make sure that your mechanic
uses the $400-$550 dollar tool required to get the proper reading
on the belt.  As it is indicated in the factory book, the only
way to measure the tension on your belt is to use the Porsche
P9201 belt tension tool.  Any other method is a recipe for disaster.

As an incentive for doing the job right, remember that you have
32 valves and they are worth ~$30 to $45 each...
