It sounds like someone before you decided to remove some the the cars default
understeer. If you set the cars geometry (alignment) to the factory settings
you should not get significant wear on the insides. Have someone checkout the
complete alignment (including ride height!). This is something everyone ought
to do fairly frequently. All the cars geometry is adustable and as such has a
tendenecy to "change" over time.

BTW my fronts have *really* bad wear on the inside of my street tyres, but
then I have 1.8Deg. neg camber on the front ;-)

I can atest to the AUTOMOTION (aka Kims setup) springs. Although I lost nearly
an inch in ride height, the car grounds on less roads than it used to, which
is still a lot :-( . I have had about 6months experience with them and can
state that in autocrossing the springs reduce my times by 3-4 secs per minute!
This is *THE* best performance upgrade that is possible on the car.
