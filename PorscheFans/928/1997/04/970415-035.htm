Hi Stephane,

The factory manual covers the cam timing pretty well.  If you have the
cams in time with the crank, the rotors that screw to the belt pulleys
will only go on ONE way.  If your motor is like my 88, the screws on the
rotor are not symetrical and will only go on in one position.  With the
cams and crank in time, the rotors point toward the drivers side of the
engine.  The rotor on the left side (as you are looking at the front of
the motor) will point directly to the right (3 o'clock) and the rotor on
the right will point mostly to the right (2 o'clock). HEED THE WARNING
OF A COUPLE OF THE RESPONSES, DO NOT ATTEMPT TO START THE MOTOR WITHOUT
RESOLVING THE TIMING ISSUES.  I will be glad to discuss this with you by
land line.  I left you my number on a previous e mail.
