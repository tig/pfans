Hi John's, it's funny how the 928's all have the same problem.  My
87'Stick had the same problems with the Automatic Climate Control and
the Cruise Control.  The local Porsche garage found out that the sliding
contacts in the Climate Control unit (you 'll see a lot of copper
contacts via the sliding slots) were not making good contacts, thus
causing the hot air.  I don't get any hot air (especially with summer
coming), but I'm still having problems with my A/C.  I only wish there
was a manual override for all the fancy stuff.  The Automatic Climate
Control unit is pretty expensive.  Something like $700 (what my mechanic
quoted me).

As for the Cruise Control.  After looking around the engine bay trying
to figure out what's what.  I found a thin (3/8 inch) plastic hose on
the drive side toward the fireall (vacume hose I assume) that was
loose.  After connecting it, my Cruise Control worked fine.  But, it
kept on coming loose after driving it until I rerouted the hose.  It's
fine now.

> I've had just about every problem you could have with the a/c, so here's
> what I can tell you.
> 
> First, the air mixing flap is controlled by a little black box that you've
> found which is, in turn, reacting to the inputs it receives from the slider
> on the climate control unit and the temperature values from the inside and
> the outside temperature sensors.  Porsche has published a trouble shooting
> manual as there are apparently a LOT of problems in this area.  Somebody I
> know at the local Porsche dealer felt sorry for me and xeroxed a copy, but
> without the cover page so I can't give you the part number.  However, the
> introductory sentence calls it the "test plan for the automatic heating and
> air conditioning system in Type 928 cars."  This leads you through a series
> of tests to isolate your problem, giving resistance and voltage measurements
> at all test points and describing how the air flap and other things should
> be behaving.  I belive your temperature problems can be solved by going
> through these tests (I'd type them in here but it'd take about 15 or 20 pages!).
> 
> The fan speed (at least on my '84 928S) is independent of the temperature
> setting.  However, if you put the slider into the defog position it will
> turn on the a/c, route the air to the windshield, and put the fan on high.
> 
> I don't think there is anything behind the fuse panel that affect the a/c,
> but I could be wrong.  I am under the impression that the temperature is
> controlled by the climate control unit, the temperature sensors, and the air
> flap doodad.  I don't think the car's computer gets into the act.
> 
> By the way, the outside temp sensor is in the left front wheel well and sits
> in an air tube that brings fresh air to the alternator.
