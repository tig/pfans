>cleaning you are supposed to re-oil the filter, but maybe it's just an

NO! It's the oil that traps the dust and grit. You remove the old oil when
you clean the filter, and you MUST replace it to get filter action for
things smaller than marbles. As mentioned by others on this list, it's a
good idea to clean and re-oil the filter more frequently than recommended
by K&N (e.g., at every engine oil change).

With regard to effectiveness of filtering, a lot of serious desert racers
find the K&N to be more effective at stopping dust than a standard paper
filter, as well as being harder to clog.
