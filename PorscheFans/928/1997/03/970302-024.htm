I think I've found a hidden side to this car's performance heritage -
bracket racing!

Saturday was the first NASA drag session at Sear's Point and I ran three,
very consistent runs:

Time trial ET:	15.081 sec / 92.270 mph (dial in guess @ 15.00)
Win #1:		15.122 sec / 92.053 mph (best reaction time .782 sec)
Lost:		15.070 sec / 92.392 mph (reaction time:  .868 sec)

The experience was very satisfying, especially when I told the sub-12
second ZR1 owners (whose Vettes were trailered) that my 928 had 144k miles!
 The car performed flawlessy and I drove it home proudly with the numbering
still on the windshield and side window.

Made some new friends and almost made it into the money rounds!  All in all
the car made an excellent showing, exceeding the specs in the owners manual
(time for 1/4 mile, automatic, 15,5 seconds).  

14.9's or faster would've been a piece of cake with race gas and timing
changes.   Maybe I'll go again if the car hasn't been sold by then!!!  It
sure looked good on a racetrack...
