Well, the spec book says that an '83 uses 19.7 l/100km on the EC
driving cycle, which I guess is something like suburban usage;
that's about 12 mpg US. So you seem to be way ahead of the game.
OTOH, at a constant 90 kph, it gets 24 mpg, which is not so
different from your results. (90 kph = 56 mph). The '84 is
supposed to do a little better at 90 kph (27 mpg), probably
because the aerodynamics is (are) a little better with the
spoilers. My car barely gets warmed up on my normal commute
run, especially in the winter, so it probably spends a lot of time 
running pretty rich. 

As the saying goes, your mileage may vary. Incidentally, I get
about 6 mpg on the track, including warmup and cooldown laps.
