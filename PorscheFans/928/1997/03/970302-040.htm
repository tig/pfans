At 03:18 PM 3/2/97 -0800, you wrote:
>Its an '89 928 S4, with ~37,000 miles. Was running great, until 
>recently. All of a sudden the temperature is running just below the
>2/3 mark on the coolant gauge. Once it get to that temperature it starts 
>pinging above 3,000 RPM. It used to run happily at 1/2 in all weather
>conditions, and I can never remember hearing a single ping.
>
>Not much has changed to my knowledge. I've got an Autothority chip,
>which has been in for about 2 years without a problem.

Apart from an actual failure of the cooling system (e.g., water pump
failure, blockage in a major passageway), I'm put in mind of a radical
change in ignition timing. Advance increases octane requirement, which can
lead to ping. Retard can cause the engine to run hot.

Your car has two chips, one for fuel and the other for ignition. The latter
should control knock avoidance (see your ping above), which it will do by
retarding the spark (see your high temp above). If you have the original
chips, you might want to replace them and see if this changes anything.

Meanwhile, check the belt which drives the water pump and squeeze your hoses.
