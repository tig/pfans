> I have the same; 84 928S AT, 111K. The only mod I know of is a K&N. 
> 
> My 1st shifts at 5700 and when 2nd hits, the rear tires break loose for
about
> 200 millisec and the ass end kicks sideways about 3 or 4 inches! Feels
awesome!
> 
> My AT leaks a couple of drops per day. 4 months, and it's half a quart
low.
> This isn't bad enough to warrant my "going in" there yet. But I have
learned
> some interesting things about the AT fluid level. 
> 
> 1) Too low:
>    After about a quart down, the car redlines too long because the
>    tranny doesn't want to shift. (Not enough pressure?) Eventually, it
does
>    shift, but it's a soft shift. This scenerio seems to rob about 30% of
the
>    torque. After more fluid leaks, the car no longer shifts! That's
right...
>    you're stuck in 1st. At this time, I had no idea of what the problem
with my
>    baby was and, in a state of panic, I limped/rushed right over to my
mechanic.
>    It turned out that the car was about a quart low on AT fluid. Wow!
That
>    was simple and inexpensive and the car feels better that EVER!
>    
> 2) Too full:
>    Oops. Never overfill a cold tranny. I thought I was starting to feel
the
>    torque robbing symptom of low fluid. It was low. The filler tube for
this
>    car is located in a very easy to reach area. (Ha!) Now, after you get
under
>    the car, you realize that you can't pour the fluid into the fill hole
>    because the floorpan is 2 inches above that. Hmmm... Well, I came up
with
>    a clever, simple solution. I had a trigger activate oil can with a
long,
>    thin spout on it. I had a clear piece of tubing similar to aquarium
air
>    filter plumbing, only larger, which fit nice and tightly over the oil
can
>    spout. So, I filled the oil can w/ AT fluid and stuck the hose into
the 
>    filler hole and began pumping. The "zone" markers are on the side of a
>    translucent plastic cylinder (complete with the typical Porsche filter
>    basket at the top). Unfortunately, I just couldn't see the fluid as it
>    rose to, and on through the "zone" lines and I ended up overfilling.
Well,
>    like the ignoramus that I am, I thought that maybe this would all get
>    sucked into the ferrules of the tranny once the engine was fired up.
>    
>    The next day, I started driving the car and noticed that it
consistently
>    shifted WAY too early...like at about 4 or 5K for *every* shift when
floored.
>    Hmmm... When I got home from work, I went back under the car to take a

>    look at the fluid level. (The car is warm now). I took off the cap and
>    AT fluid starts gushing out! Ouch! That stuff is hot! OK, ummm...this
has
>    been overfilled. So I used a clean sponge to dip down into the plastic
>    cylinder and extract excess fluid. Then I capped it off and took it
for a
>    spin. 
>    
>    YES! Back to the crisp, perfect shifting.
>
