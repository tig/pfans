Yo Debbi! (Your 1st post here?  Welcome!):

European regulations -- at least for the period your car was built --
required that every car be able to adjust its headlights according to load.
This was an important consideration, since the Euro's enlightened lighting
laws allowed them much more powerful lights than our pitiful sealed-beams of
the time, and a few cases of wine in the trunk would turn a car into a
retina-burning demon.

The knob next (knext?) to your driver's seat adjusts the vertical angle of
your 928's headlights.  These were removed from US versions (I'm not sure if
this feature was prohibited by law or if some liability lawyer was just
trying to justify his pitiful, parasitic living)*, most of which have a
little hole in that position.  By-the-bye, many earlier 928's also
positioned their stereo's front-to-rear balance/fader control on that same
box next to the driver's seat.

European regulations about driving at night using only the driving lights
seem to vary considerably.  Sorry, I can't help you on fixing the power
up-down function on your headlights, but I'm sure someone on the PorscheFans
list will be able to shed light on the subject (oops... sorry... where DID I
put my medication...?).

Don'tcha just love how wierd these things look with their lights up?  I
wonder what the German word is for "Bugeye" or, as the English call their
early Sprites, "Frogeyes."  Once we settle that, we can dress it up
Nietzsche-style...   "uber-whatever."

Oh, by the way, your Euro-79 should be a 4.5 liter car with the Bosh CIS
mechanical fuel injection (which the folks at Devek Performance like) and
about 280-ish horsepower, a nice increase from the US model's 219.  You'll
want to check on how the US smog conversion was done BEFORE you take it in
for licensing &/or emissions testing.  The quality of these conversions, not
to mention the completeness of the documentation, seems to have varied
enormously, which could screw up your ability to register your car or even
sell it in another state.
