Any decent paint supply should have an optical scanner that can read the
color information directly from the car.  This system calibrates the mixture
to take into account oxidation and other variables that will affect the
color of the paint over the life of the car.  

I had a custom painted 535i trashed by a transport company and thought I
would never get the paint to match again.  (extremely metallic, lots of
solids in the paint)  Obviously it takes a good painter to blend and cut in
new paint next to old paint, but you shouldn't have any trouble getting the
proper color/mixture.
