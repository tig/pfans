There's a headlight relay, on the board under the passenger's feet, that
could cause that. It's actually three separate relays - on/off,
high/low beam, and motor - in a single can. It's the relay furthest
to the right in the lower row. Probably expensive, but easy to
replace. You could try just unplugging it a couple of times, which
might temporarily clean a bad contact, or just tap it with the headlights
turned on.

Second guess is a poor connection somewhere; third is the switch itself,
though I'm not sure it could cause the lights to fail while the motor
still works.
