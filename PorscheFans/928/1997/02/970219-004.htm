The earlier engines are not interference and also do not require the
expensive tool to adjust the belt tension.  There is another cheaper
tensioning tool you can use (I don't have that part number - it's
kind of a simple mechanical deflection like a dial torque wrench).

Also, the later tensioners are oil filled vs a more primitive mechanical
bolt used on 928 and Ss.  The reason I think is that tensioning is 
not so critical as the valves will not crash if maladjusted.
