>When replacing the timing belt should I also replace the tensioner?

YES!

The tensioner gets tired after a while and either can no longer be adjusted
or, more treacherously, will no longer reliably maintain belt tension. Once
you're in there, replace it.

Also, any new timing belt you buy will have a new profile to the teeth,
which will cause it to slip off the existing rollers. Replace those, too!

The additional parts cost for the tensioner and rollers is extremely
inexpensive insurance for your nice engine.

Remember to have your new belt checked in a few thousand miles and
retensioned at least as often as the factory recommends thereafter.
