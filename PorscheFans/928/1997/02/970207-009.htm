Re: your maintenance list
These can be hard to work on cars due to the cramped packaging, requiring
lots of time and special tools.  For example, the timing belt change
requires a special belt tensioner tool that may be more money than you'd
want to spend.  I congratulate you on any maintenance you've done on the
Fiero but you are graduating to a whole new ballgame and price range with
the 928.  You should be able to do most of the basic stuff no problem.

That's kind of a lead in to putting a fair valuation on that car, as any
possible future repairs could wipe out it's value completely and you'd be
better off leaving it alone.  No kidding.  In any event I've seen '84 928S'
between $3,500 (really poor condition too) to $14,500 (sometimes poor
condition here too!).  The good news is that your friend has had the car
for awhile and you should know it's history better than a total strangers
car.  But that is no replacement for a thorough diagnostic check, OK?

I don't know how much he is asking for his car but take your time to check
around.  And know that a properly maintained 928 can be a powerhouse car
for many years!  My '84 928S has 144k miles, recently dyno'd at just under
200 rear wheel horsepower (I'm sure more now after mod's) and has gone 140
mph (4,000 rpm's in top gear with lots more to go).  For comfort and
low-end grunt, the 928 has it all over the 911 but they are designed
differently.  

Hope to meet you someday - keep watching the list for the next microbrew
get together!
