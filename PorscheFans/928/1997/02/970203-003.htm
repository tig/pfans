I'd have to agree that the principle is a sound one but I don't know
if it's "worth it".

Alot of plug makers employ a split electrode or even have a groove in the
tip.  NGKs for instance have a V in the tip presumably for increased 
surface area and more robust spark.

I tried the Bosch platinums though and didn't like them - they tended
to foul more.  They did appear to be better over a wider temp range though
- cold running was a little smoother.

Stock Bosch (copper) plugs work best for me and are relatively inexpensive.  
Wasn't it Bosch who invented the spark plug in the first place?
