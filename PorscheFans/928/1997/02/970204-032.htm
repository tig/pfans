Brian Robinson wrote:
>I have been following the thread regarding troubleshooting the rear wipers
>and have reached an impass.  

>The power to the rear motor is fine, the switch is fine, and the metal jig
>that runs the wiper arm is fine and making good contact with the arm.

>The arm however does not move when the jig turns, or when I turn it with my
>own hand.  Anybody got any info?

	You will have to take it apart further to unvestigate, but first make sure 
the jig is making positive contact with the driving portion.

>Also the remote trunk release operates intermittently.  It works from time
>to time, but no pattern has emerged yet.  I did notice that when I was
>checking all of the fuses before a long road trip that once they were all
>reinstalled everything worked back there for the longest period of time yet.
>I don't have a fuse list as of yet, so I haven't taken the trouble to pull
>all of them one by one again.  If you are having the same problem or know
>what fuse and relay runs the release I would appreciate it.  

	This should only work with the doors open, and the wires come off the switch 
preety easily, and are close enough together to arc if not set properly. Just 
remove the covers on both sides and check your connections. Then check the 
switch, then check the wiring.
	
>Still looking for info on chemically removing any coating on factory flat
>face rims before polishing them.

	If it is a clear-coat, then you can use paint stripper, if it is anodized or 
alodined, then you can try East-Off oven cleaner spray. I used it on my 
aluminum VFR frame and it took the anodizing right off, however it did turn 
the metal blackish, but that may have been the coating being chemically 
burned. Then I wet-sanded it with 400/600/800/1000/1500/2000 grit sandpaper, 
then used a buffing wheel on a drill to do the polishing with the white rouge. 
Looks killer!
