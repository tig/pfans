> The problem is the E in the VIN. Yours is probably a D, which means
> it's an '83; the E means model year '84. The change was made in
> September '83, according to the tech manual. If you had steel,
> the 6 inch long piece itself would be steel. So it looks like you have
> the earlier joints.
>

John is right... the letter in front of the "s" is critical for Porsche
VIN #'s    e=1984 f=1985 g=1986  etc.

The Way to tell the difference by looking at the ball joint support
is....the new version takes on a more hour glass shape.  The countour of
the edge of the support tapers in between the mounting holes.  Also and
the bigger hole is further from the ball joint.
