On Fri, 31 Jan 1997, Mathew Stewart wrote:

> I would be inerested to know what the opinions are regarding the use of a
> couple different things on my 928.  What should I use to keep the rubber
> rear wing and interior components in good condition.  The PO said that when
> he bought the car and stopped putting Armoral on the dash as the previous PO
> had done religiously the dash crack in a couple of months.  I can't remember
> exactly how he explained it, but basically what he told me was that if you
> use these kind of products you have to keep using them, and if you ever stop
> the car will be worse off than if you had done nothing.  What I want to know
> is what can I do to get the car looking a little better without any long
> term snake oil side effects.


	There is a nifty product called Black Again! that works perfect
	for this. It can also be used on any side moldings, vynyl, etc.
	parts you may have. It removes dirt, old wax residue, road grime,
	etc. It's really good stuff. It's kinda hard to find, though.
	Larry Reynolds at Care Care Specialties carries it and that's
	where I get mine now.



> Another thing that I have heard different opinions on is the use of  SS
> brake lines.  I would like to replace the current ones and had been planing
> on going with the SS lines, until someone on the list said that they should
> only be use for racing.  I had though that they were more reliable than the
> rubber lines, but his/her opinion was that they were prone to complete
> failure and not good for a periodically driven car.  


	They are better than the OEM lines BUT...they require very
	special attention. You will need to check them constantly
	(I mean, like, 1 a day, 2 days at the outside) to make sure
	the fittings are tight and correct. Many people put them on
	and then forget about them. That's a no-no because when they
	fail, they often fail catastrophically and BOOM! No brakes.
	Bad thing to have happen at 70 on the freeway...
