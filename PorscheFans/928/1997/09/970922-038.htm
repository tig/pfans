However,  I feel that you, Brian,  may well have come upon a major 
performance improvement.  I am quite sure that everyone on the 928 list has 
read and therein hold close to heart "Theory of Wing Sections" by Abbott 
and Doenhoff.  In this tome, there is a section which deals with surface 
configuration and coefficients of friction of the materials of 
construction.  As we all know, the rear wing (S4 and later) is a typical 
inverted airfoil.  This airfoil produces lift (downforce in our case) by 
the simple method of speed of the airflow over the respective surfaces. 
(read: Bernoulli)  The top or "flat portion" has little effect of the 
airflow and pressure.  However, the lower curved section has a pronounced 
effect in that it accelerates the air flow, therein reducing the pressure 
on this side of the airfoil.  I.E. DOWNFORCE.

The point of this is quite simple:  Peanut butter has a very high 
coefficient of friction. (At least it does in my mouth).  Whereas, your 
normal jelly has a very low coefficient of friction.(in as new condition) 
 Therefore, if one were to coat the top (or Flat side of the wing) with 
Peanut butter and the lower curved part with Jelly, the differentials in 
pressure would be enhanced. Ergo, more downforce.

It would seem an interesting discussion for all of us as to the variations 
inherent in the different brands of both Peanut butter and Jelly and the 
actual effects these may have upon lap times at your favorite circuit. 
 There are many technical problems that will have to be dealt with however. 
 Solubility is tantamount.  Just as you must choose a good rain tire, would 
you than have to choose a different brand of Peanut butter and jelly for 
the track conditions.  I can see the engineering difficulties and therein
