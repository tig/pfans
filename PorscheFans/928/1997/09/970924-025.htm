Shane,

Yup, BTDT on my S4.  Not a bad job - you do have to wrestle the rack a
little when putting it up and starting the nuts with the other hand.  I
used Griffiths Tech in NJ (Pano advertisement or www page) or see your
local P-dealer (if they give you a 20% discount).  On the S4's, the 2
large nuts on the left side are difficult to hold.  I wedged a piece of
..080" thick copper in there to hold the nut against the frame recess.
If you're not the original owner, flush 2-3 quarts of fluid through the
pump with the rack off.  Make a funnel out of Aluminum foil and put it
over the lines and into an topless old plastic milk jug, then have your
SO start the car briefly while you pour fluid into the reservoir.  (You
can never be sure what a PO put in to make it stop leaking - if it
"started" leaking while you owned it, well, maybe the stuff he put in
"worked.")
