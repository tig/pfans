> - the gauges in the left half of the pod are dead.  Only the TAIL LAMP and
>         STOP LAMP lights light (of those on the left side) when you turn on
>         the ignition.  No speedo or odo, no temp gauge, no fuel gauge and no
>         other warning lights.  The Central Warning Light, and everything to
>         its right works, except the oil pressure gauge, which is stuck off
>         the scale past "5".  I'm hoping this is a well known failure mode
>         with a simple solution.  OK, so I'm slightly optimistic. :-)  If it's
>         not easy to fix, what are my chances of finding a recycled pod
>         that'll work?

A lot of people have had this problem.  This comes from corroding
connector between 
the pod and the main harness.  Look for the connector, take the
connection apart,
clean and try again.

> - the power steering boost is inconsistent.  As you turn the steering wheel,
>         it feels like the boost is coming and going suddenly (kind of like
>         you're turning it through big lumps).  The PO said he replaced the
>         rack, and the mechanic told him the steering column had to be
>         replaced.  On the road, this isn't too bothersome until the boost
>         suddenly reappears as I'm making a slow turn and I suddenly find I've
>         over-shot my intended steering movement and turned the wheel too far.

Just remember that the powersteering fluid to use in a 928 is AUTOMATIC
TRANSMISSION
FLUID!!! (At least in my 85).  Putting the regular steering fluid will
lead to a leaky
rack...

> 
> - when started cold, the engine acts as though the EFI doesn't know the
>         engine is cold.  It tends to stall at first, before finally settling
>         into a (seemingly correct) slightly high cold idle.  Using the loud
>         pedal is pointless, however, until it's warmed up as it stumbles,
>         coughs and basically acts like the EFI doesn't know it's cold - like
>         a carb with a choke that isn't working.

This is just to put you on the track as I am not familiar with your
injection system.  There is a cold start injector that some people
had some problem with.  But I am just guessing ;-)


> Is it worth my while to get the L-Jetronic manual?  Is the Chilton's book
> any good?  I can't believe Chilton's lumped the 928 and 924 into the same
> book.

The chilton book should be use as a last ditch reference.  Some of the
information is inaccurate.  There are a lot of book on the Bosch
injection
system out there that could help you out.  For mechanical repair, the 
factory manual is the only way to go but is ~$280.  A cheaper
alternative is
the microfiche version for ~$40 but the picture quality is poor.  A good
third party reference would be the Mitchell Book.  Consult your local
library for availablity.
