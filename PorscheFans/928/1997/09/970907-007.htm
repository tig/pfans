Interesting...

I had the opposite experience with my '85.  I had little knowledge of the
critical nature of the timing belt on these cars when I bought mine 4 yrs
ago so I kept resetting the (!) warning light whenever the "Belt Tension"
light came on.  I figured I'd get it fixed soon which became several
months.  It got to the point where almost every time I accelerated hard,
the warning would come on.  When I finally did get it fixed, my mechanic
said the belt was very loose.  Fortunately, on my car there was more than
ample warning of impending trouble.  Seems like these things behave
differently from car to car.  Also strange that you didn't get any internal
engine damage.  It was a 32 valve car, right?

I thought I'd pass along an actual road experience with a cam belt which
jumped some teeth at highway speed.  The short story is there was no
valve/piston contact and no serious engine damage.  The repair was about
$750 so not a fun experience - but not so severe as described elsewhere.
The only parts replaced were two tension rollers for $100 - the rest was
12 hours labor!
This was with an 85 928S US model - no mods - mileage was 24,064.
....the big red light came on (didn't notice the belt tension light
until later) and I pulled over immediately (the light wouldn't reset).
As I slowed to around 10 or 20 mph the engine ran VERY rough - spooky.
