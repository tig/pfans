Dear fellow Land Shark tamers.
Here are a few personal experiences that may help answer questions posed
today. James Morrison is absolutely correct about the 928 alignment.
Actually, to be done properly, it takes three adjustments of each adjustable
setting. In addition, even the best qualified technician can not align the
car within factory specs if it is TOO LOW. Even if you have not lowered your
car, over time ALL 928 original springs tend to sag and allow the car to get
too low. Thus, the car must first be brought back within the acceptable
height range, and then it can be aligned. If you like the LOW look on your
928, just be ready to fork out the bucks for tires every 10K miles or less.

Recently my car began to run a bit hot (at or slightly above the top white
line). The coolant  had been replaced (with 100% phosphate free) as well as
the thermostat and water pump. The problem was a weak fan clutch. Since the
repair it rarely approaches the upper white line even on the 105 degree day
we had over a month ago. Once I'm moving it barely makes it above the lower
white line. Check out the fan clutch. They do get weak over time. My car is
an 86 with over 121K miles. Twelve years on the original fan clutch is
probably about what you should expect.
