>>If the synchros on 1st, 2nd and 3rd gears are shot am I better off
getting
> it repaired/rebuilt or just dropping in a rebuilt transaxle (928 Int'l
> has
> them for $1800)?  Is there anything that can be done to increase the
> longevity of the synchros?  I'm not an abusive shifter, but they're
> apparently a weak point.
> 
Trans lube choice is very important.  You want a GL-5 (not a GL-4 like
most transmissions), higher extreme pressure protection I believe.  Next
trick is getting something that's not TOO slippery (that slays
synchros!).

Red Line makes a 75W90NS (the NS is very important: = "not slippery")
that's ideal.  Doesn't run off the surfaces, relatively unaffected by
cold and
heat, and tremendous film strength (avoid those metal-to-metal contact
horrors).
