Surface area,  is after all,  surface area from a thermodynamic standpoint. 
  However, the insides of the holes do contribute equally with the surface 
of the rotor due to the pumping action of the interior vanes as well the 
passing of the pad over them.  These two items allow for airflow over these 
surfaces, therein the exchange of heat.  If you look at interior of Rotors 
as manufactured by AP for Large sports cars which cannot use Carbon rotors 
(due to rules) you will see that in addition to having holes and vanes on 
the interior, they also have "stalactites"  intruding into the airspace 
between the vanes from each interior rotor half.  This is done to increase 
surface area for heat exchange.  The actual increase in surface area with 
this design is approximately 45%.  This rotors have shown a significant 
decrease in cooling cycle times and much improved pad wear in long distance 
events.
