> I have mixed feelings about this.  The larger the surface, the more heat it
> can dissipate, right?  So if you have a drilled rotor, you effectively
> reduce the surface.  Having said that, I see those rotors used in some
> race applications, I wonder if they are larger?  The look incredibly cool

Cooling in the crossdrilled increase brake because of the increase
airflow.  The higher
mass enables you to get more heat in without raising the temperature so,
1. the larger disk will not heat up as quick because they have a higher
mass
2. The crossdrilled disk will cool faster because of
	- lesser mass (does not retain heat as much)
	- increase airflow
But the cross drilled disk will wear faster but they reduce the unsprung
weight
and inertia of the wheel...

As you can see, you could argue for either type but the cross drill do 
look cool...
