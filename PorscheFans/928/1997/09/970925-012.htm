Whoa, Steve. I respect your tolerance of poor cosmetics and desire to save
money, but autocross and time trials put a far higher load on your wheels
than normal street driving, and so your wheels really must be straight and
strong (no cracks). Real (not replica) Porsche wheels are considerably
stronger than required by the already tough T=DCV specs to which most
aftermarket wheels are built, so it's a good idea to stick with Porsche
wheels, but scratches lead to cracks, and are often due to curb surfing,
which engenders bends and cracks.
