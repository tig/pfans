All this camshaft turning on static engines notwithstanding, if the timing
belt slips or breaks on any 928 engine other than '78-79 units with 8.2:1
CR while it's operating, valves WILL meet pistons and you'll be unhappy.

By the way, even though the water pump is driven by the timing belt, it's
driven by the back (non-toothy) side, so that pulley need not be changed at
such time as you adopt the new HTD belt. You will need to change the
sprockets on the camshafts, oil pump, and crankshaft sprockets.
