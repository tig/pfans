If your mechanic says there are more hydrocarbons after the converter than 
before it -- go to another mechanic.  Catalytic converters rarely cause 
problems.  Most often if the converter is bad, it's because something upstream 
of it has gone awry and in turn damages the converter.  If you put on a new 
converter without fixing the problem, it's only a matter of time before the 
new converter will be damaged.

For reference purposes, my '83 has 13ppm hydrocarbon (the limit for 1983 model 
cars is 220ppm) and 0% CO.
