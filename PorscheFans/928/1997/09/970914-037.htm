The theory is that when the hot areas in the engine are close to the boiling point the area that is insulated from coolant can't conduct heat away due
to the steam that's gathering to produce a bubble. This cuts down on transfer ararea to coolant and places more area in steam. Look at the bottom of a pot of 
water that's about to boil. You'll find that nearly the whole surface
at the heat source is occupied by vapor, not water.
