I've heard of a couple done, and I've seen one personally.
The guy that did it is Bill Driskell, and he lives in Floyd,
VA.  He did a really good job, but it took a lot of work.
He had to cut away seven inches of the trunk, put big flares
on, and use a 915 tranny with a custom shift linkage.  This 


doesn't include putting a big radiator in the front trunk and
cutting away the wheel wells for air to exit.  Like I said, 
it's a beautiful car, but from what I've heard you're better
off putting a 911 motor in the car.  If you're willing to
spend some money, you can put a 300hp 911 motor in that weighs
a lot less and is a lot more reliable.  If you love V-8's,
you could always drop a small block in and go the Pors-chev 
route for a lot less than a 928 motor.  Also, I think there
are a lot more people that have built really high hp small 
blocks than 928 motors.  If you're really stuck on a 928 
motor, contact Devek Performance.  They have ads in Panorama,
and deal exclusively in 928's, so they could give you some
price quotes on a used motor and hop ups, etc.  Personally,
I'd put a 3.2 liter 911 motor in my 14 if I had the money, but
I'm in college, so that's going to wait a while.  I've head
of people making 200+ hp out of a 2.4 liter, so God knows how 
much power you could make if you were willing to put a 911 
turbo motor in a 914.  Another redeeming factor is that you
keep the cool exhaust note that a flat motor produces.  That's
my two cents.
