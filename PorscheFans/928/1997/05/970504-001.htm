Others have suggested syncros could be the problem. That wasn't the only 
problem in my '83 928S's 5-speed on the 1 to 2 shift, a $3 (Porsche Full 
Suggested Retail Price) spring plate broke, preventing the reverse gear 
lockout from working. My 1 to 2 grind was reverse.

How to test the function of your reverse gear lockout mechanism: on a 1 to 
2 shift hold the shift lever against the left gates when pushing forward. 
Before getting into reverse you should feel a "curve" forcing you to the 
right into 2nd. If moving, shifting this way will not grind. If you don't 
hold pressure to the left when shifting and you get a grind then its likely 
you are grinding reverse.

It appears the '85 transmission got a redesign on reverse. The new reverse 
gears have a syncro. I accidently purchased the wrong replacement reverse 
gears for my '83 and waited too long to return them. I've got a set for 
sale. Maybe I ought to buy an '85 and rebuild its transmission... :-)
