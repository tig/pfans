At 02:23 PM 5/28/97 -0400, you wrote:
>Take those nice wheels off and look at the shocks.  The bottom spring
>perches have adjustable collars on them.  Turn the nut and that corner goes
>up or down - do both sides the same and it's nice to corner balance when
>you're done.

More than nice, it's really necessary, and requires a scale at each wheel
so you can get each wheel to bear down with the same force. This a project
that's easy to start, but difficult to finish correctly.

>Shouln't affect ride quality (bumps) at all unless you scrape.

Not so. You're running the wheels up into the wheel wells. Either the bump
stops or the inside of the fender well will limit upward travel, so a hard
"jounce" will become abruptly much harder. Serious lowering should probably
be done with shorter, higher rate springs, so that the wheel will travel
upwards less for the same applied force.

>(Remember you'll have to adjust front toe (more toe in) and you'll have
>more negative camber.)

and everything else. Don't casually screw around with your suspension geometry!
