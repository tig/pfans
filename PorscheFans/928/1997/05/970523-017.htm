>The main reason that I wanted cross-drilled rotors is b/c my car is an
>automatic and the rotors tend to get glazed after hard use

Have you actually observed this, or is it just that the brakes are fading
after sustained hard use? If something is "glazing", it's much more likely
to be the pads (for which there are all sorts of racy replacements) than
the rotors. Cross-drilling the rotors is supposed to vent the gases coming
off the pads during hard braking (the friction material vaporizes) to keep
the pads from lifting off the rotors.

>How difficult is it to reproportion the front/rear brake bias?

One straightforward way is to replace the stock proportioning valve with an
available Porsche part (as St=E9phane pointed out; John White is also
familiar with this trick, which originates with Kim Crumb of the PCA). I
can't find the number right now, but I'll dig it up.

A more gung-ho approach is to buy an adjustable proportioning valve from a
racing supply house (check ads in Panorama). You're on your own with
respect to installation, and some setting will produce highly dangerous
results, but you'll have control (or at least input)!
