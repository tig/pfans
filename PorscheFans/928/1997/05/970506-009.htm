A big project for sure.  Have you considered:
* EQ & tape in glovebox, amp on passenger tray (driver tray too shallow for
your equipment?)
* or get rid of clock & ashtray and mount eq or tape there
*  custom double-DIN mount (at proper angle too to eliminate glare in the
928 console)

* close proximity of LF spkr and straight shot of RR speaker will give you
imaging fits
*  close proximity of RF spkr and straight shot of LR speaker will give
passenger imaging fits

*  rears should be low-freq rear fill only,if that much, leaving most of
the mid/hi-freqs to the front channels
* no x-overs on coaxials (network mounted on speaker usually) but not as
good sounding or power handling as separate components
* Focals, Quarts are excellent choices just remember the best speakers in a
poor location will sound worse than poor speakers in the best location
*  Sub sounds cool!  Be careful of sparks in the spare tire well - even if
you have a 'sealed' battery.  There was a report of at least one battery
explosion on this list.
* Use only properly rated components/interconnects.  The one lousy fuse
housing I scrimped on melted after 45 seconds of full-blast.  The good news
is that the lead (+ve) didn't ground out and set the car on fire - the bad
news is it was in the spare tire well too.
