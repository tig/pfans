Steve, you are correct.  The extra fuel in the combustion chamber will cool 
the surfaces of the combustion chamber.  Then, as the unburnt fuel leave the 
combustion chamber, additional air is pumped into the exhaust system by the -- 
what's commonly called -- smog pump.  Now the unburnt fuel has addtional 
oxygen plus the high heat of the cat. converter, and bingo, meltdown of the 
converter's substratum.  That's why if you replace a melted converter without 
fixing the engine problem, the new converter will be damaged also.
