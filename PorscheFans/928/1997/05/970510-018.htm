Leonard,


I decided to post this in case someone has some insight into what I am
trying to do. I can do 10 updates per second running 38.4k baud on the
rover to the laptop for logging.  The Differential station updates at 1
Hz over a 4800bps wireless link and is interpolated (forward predicted by
the rover. This latency only means 5 to 10cm of accuracy loss.


Position Accuracy is 40cm for the short baselines that i have in mind. 
Velocity accuracy is 0.05 mph. I log to a laptop and process in Excell or
Matlab.  I will have to come up with a test run protocol that is similar
to the inertia wheel dyno tests.  I figure multiple passes (in opposite
directions) for each scenario (chip) to compensate for wind, slope.


I presume they usually use 4th or 5th gear and start at 1000 rpm or so
and go full throttle until redline.  4th makes sense for a road test so
that I don't have to drive at 160mph and it will also make the wind
resistance a lot less of a factor.


The most challenging part will be to find a suitable place to do the runs
in the Phoenix Area.  I will also weigh the car before the test.


I will HAPPILY publish the results
since no chip manufacturer would give me dyno results for a GT.  I
presume that this is because the increase in performance on a GT is
negligible for any chip upgrade.
