No, they aren't hydraulic jacks at all. They are a ratchet type jack. (like a
bumper jack) I place them on the jack points and use jack stands beside them
(not on the jack point locations) I place the front jack stands on the
underbody right by the jacks and the rear on the aluminum suspension mount
point right by the lift point. I know that if the car fell off the jacks,
that the jackstands would catch it. However, I realize there could
potentially be some damage to the underbody (at least in front) if it ever
did fall with the jackstands not being on the actual lift pads. Believe me, I
don't want to die. (although I'd almost wish I was dead if I hurt my baby!!!)
