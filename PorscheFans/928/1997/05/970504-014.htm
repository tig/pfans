>Vince:

>The lever does not "load" to neutral but simply reduces the throw of the
shift. 
>
>It is as easy to install as your factory lever as it is desinged as a direct
>replacment.
>
>The top guide rod is simply used as a pivot point and is held in its
>vertical position by a guide just forward of the shifter opening. The rod
itself should really only rotate. The lower rod does actually move down
>yet it should not bind anywhere.
>
>I have the shifter installed on an '80 Euro model with both the factory
>leather and the factory rubber boots and it works quite well.
>
>The only real pain is trying to use the old nylon bearings. I replaced mine
>and there is little or no play left in the mount.
>
>By the way Vince, your mod (and your freind's) should work to effectively
>reduce throw...but once you 'hack up' your factory peice, there is no going
>back!
>
>FYI, my piece is by no means a "hacked-up" piece. Both the factory and the
>'final' lever have been dimensioned and blue printed and their shift
>geometries modeled. The lever is fabricated from 304 stainless and is welded
>on a jig to ensure parallel placement of the bushings. The cylidrical
>extension for the aftermarket knob is notched for added stregth. It is then
>cleaned and lghtly polished...its very nice.
>
>As needed, I will have more fabricated and will sell them at cost. Since
>there are no economies of scale here, I would expect them to be around $200
>+ shipping but I'm not certain at this point.

>>You would do the usual; remove shift boot, base plate, etc...  Then I 
>>removed the lever from the shift assembly and put it on a vise.  Took a 
>>hacksaw and just went for it. You'll be wasting your time if your 
>>hacksaw blade is dull.  Spend $1.59 and get a new one.  Use a rat tail 
>>file to notch the top just like the stock and you're in business.
>>
>>Now, the length that you want to hack off is really personal 
>>preference.  Just keep in mind that the shorter the lever, the more 
>>effort the throw.  (duhh... its the principle of leverage, ya know!)  I 
>>found 2.5 inches off is comfortable for me.  Like Steve said, after the 
>>mod, it'll take more effort to shift, but you'll get used to it in a 
>>few miles.  In a few months, you'll find that your right biceps are 
>>more symetrical to your left one for a change!  
>>
>>This is also a good opportunity to do some greasing during re-assembly. 
>>"While you're there"... check for excess play on the linkage coupler.  
>>If your 928 have some serious mileage on it like mine does, you just 
>>might be surprised! (..now, that's another thread.. but don't say I 
>>didn't warn you!)  Took me about an hour.  The only thing that still 
>>bugs me about the whole shifting geometry is that the shifter is NOT 
>>spring loaded in neutral; because I use second gear from a standstill.  
>>I've heard that Porsche changed it to load in neutral in later models.  
>>I would be interested in hearing somebody out there that MIGHT have a 
>>mod for this.
