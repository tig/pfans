At 09:44 PM 5/15/97 -0400, you wrote:
>Should my timing belt light ever come on?

Not without your stopping your engine and car (preferably in that order )
ASAP and, in all likelihood, having the car flatbedded to your preferred
shop. The valves in the 32V engines can and will hit the pistons if the
belt slips or breaks, even at cranking rpms.

>Does the belt require re-tensioning after installation?

Yes; give it about 3000 miles to do its infant stretching.
