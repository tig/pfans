>    hi-  I was wondering how nitrous oxide would be in an '89s4.  

When the NOS hooks up it would be AWESOME - the problem is getting all that
power to the ground.  The 928 is undertired to begin with and I think the
widest rims made to fit are still only 10"s.  You'd spin at full-throttle
in any gear probably up to 100+mph (how many of us remember catching rubber
at highway speeds!)

The plumbing is what would really set your S4 apart from any other.  Go
first-class on fittings and design or leave it alone.

How much
> power to add?  

Good question - I don't know what the HP design limits are for the stock
engine.  

What's being done on stock and drag prep'd engines:
50 - 80HP 	4 bangers
75 - 100 	Rotary's & 6's (except Buick turbo V6 - use V8 jets below)
150 - 250 - ?+ 	V8's 

>Will it destroy the engine?  

Executed improperly - yes.  Immediately.  

However, if you jet conservatively, avoid detonation like the plaque and
remember not to blow it all in one mega-hi-speed-run-till-the-bottle's-dry
moment of insanity the Porsche V8 should take at least a 100HP boost quite
nicely on the stock pistons and rods for short blasts (quite enough to
'kill' your opponent unless it's a ringer also).  Not that I've been there
so YMMV!!!   

Again, the key is going to be overall system design.  

Stock FI system up to snuff currently?  Compression/leak-down tight and to
spec?  Current oil consumption? (as a condition indicator). Ignition system
ok?  Cooling system up to snuff?  No driveline problems?  Brakes good?

I would say at the minimum you'd need a dual injector "wet-manifold"
direct-port, multi-port fogger setup (one for each bank of cylinders if not
eight individuals which is the ideal) ,  auxilliary fuel pump(s) & pressure
reg, single 10lb. NOS bottle (double that later) , and free-flow exhaust. 
Nice to haves would be bigger stock injectors & press. reg, nitrous bottle
warmer & pressure gauge(s).  Don't forget to retard your timing or you will
be sorry.  I don't know if any of the current retard devices can be
backfitted to the dual distributor / knock sensor setup but ifso then add
to the minimum requirements list.

NOS is called 'throttle-in-a-bottle" but just remember that's when most
engines blow - on-throttle. Now add additional NOS & fuel mixture to the
picture and you get the picture.  But I repeat, used judiciously NOS is
safe.  Even one of the big auto mfr.s is planning on making it an option on
new cars - was it Jaguar or Infinity or something.

If I've whetted your appetite and you're still up to facing all the

heard of a NOS-boosted S4 yet.  The crowd will go wild though and people
will be falling all over themselves at first to help you do it, then to
check it out!
