>> '83 928S  (ok, I give up... why do some of you folks that own 'S's

>Oh Leonard.... 

All right, here's what I've got:

1. Every document I have from Porsche AG or PCNA refers to the model in
question as "928 S", and the later models as "928 S4", "928 GT", and "928
GTS". This is what I had in mind when I issued my bulletin/apparent threat
(Hello, Stephane and John!) on the subject.

2. Since the question arose, I checked the model badges (actually stickers)
on the rears of these models, and these read as follows:

	928S

	928 S
                    4

	928
	       GT

	928 GTS

The first three of these are more accurately depicted in the attached
image, cribbed (credit hereby) from the current Automotion catalog. All but
the first put a space after the "928".

3. I guess I wind up sticking with the space when writing the name, on the
theory that this is what the maker (no capital initial) does, and that the
model badge is a design element.

4. Do whatever you want, but don't expect me to like it..
