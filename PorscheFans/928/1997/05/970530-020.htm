Vince,

We're dealing with a lever here. The position of the pivot point in any lever
determines the multiplication factor applied when measuring the effects of
moving one side or the other of a lever.

Imagine a see-saw with the pivot point located ridiculously close to one or
the other seat/end. Moving the short end a small distance will cause the long
end to move a much greater distance.
