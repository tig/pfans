>4. Try to find subwoffers that would fit in the rear position (behind

Do you mean in the wheel well speaker locations?  I think the speaker
locations are best left for mid bass, and the rear cargo area is best suited
to an add-on subwoofer box.  

>#4 is the biggest problem in my quest.  There is not many decent sub
>that are only 6".  

There are not any subwoofers that are 6 inch.  They get close to the
frequency response of a true subwoofer, but not quite.  They are simply too
small in diameter to reproduce the lowest bass notes.  By coupling them
together in various congfigurations, some companies have been able to get
them pretty low.  By putting them in the wheel well, you wont be able to tie
them together in the same space, or with the same wiring, to get increased
response.  

>If I could find boxes to put in that place that would hold 8", I would be
>all set.

Do you mean the rear cargo area?  I have a 10" sub, in a sealed box, that
takes up about 12" of depth, and 6" of height,  in the cargo area.  I have
enough room next to it for the CD changer.  I also have plent of room at the
back of the trunk for suitcases, or gorceries.  It works well, and fits
better.  The company is SpeakerWorks, and they are now out of business.  Too
bad, but I am sure someone else makes a similar setup.
