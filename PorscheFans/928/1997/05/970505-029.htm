Not at all.  Your problem might be that you are missing the
aluminum O0ring between the unit and the block.

To remove the sender, you will need to remove the oil filter so
you can grab the "hex" shape portion of the sender.  It requires
a 20 mm wrench or something in that range.

Just unscrew the old one and put the new one one (or in your case, 
try a new O-ring??).  Just be careful because there is ~ 1 cup of oil
standing over the sender.  If you know about it, you can prepare, in
my case, well lets just say that it is one more lesson that will be
"ingraved" on my garage floor...

Also, when you restart the engine, you might ear some funny noise for
the first 10 second.  I assume it is the time it takes for that oil
galery to fill back up.
