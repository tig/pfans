The tensioner is self-adjusting only to some extent.  The adjustment is
limited to the variation between a cold engine and a warm engine.  If
your belt has any loose in it, or is not tensioned properly when
installed,
the tensioner will not help you.

> 
> Q1:  Should my timing belt light ever come on?  Does the belt require
> re-tensioning after installation?

Belt does need to be retension after 1,000 miles.  This is very
important as
a tensionner that fails means an engine that fails in our 32 valves
models 
(you will experience valve-piston crash).

Did your shop use the special Porsche belt tension indicator?  Porsche
(indicator), there is NO substitute...
