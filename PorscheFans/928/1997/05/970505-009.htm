> While there is no question that a K&N filter has less resistance, there is 
> a question about the size of particle it filters. Maybe another case of 
> Urban Folklore, but supposedly a large construction company, mining 
> company, or somebody who ran a large fleet tried the K&N filters. This 
> company also routinely did oil testing when the oil was changed. And all 
> vehicles with K&N filters had more fine dirt in the oil than the disposable 
> paper filters.
> 
> Others claim K&N must be good because racers use it, and so did the Army in 
> Desert Storm. But in both cases their priorities are different than mine. 
> In general a racer is happy if his engine lasts the race. The Army is going 
> to be more concerned about being able to wash and reuse a filter in the 
> field that is full of sand, and will be happy not to have to carry spares.

  I've read that same case study by the person who worked for the mining company.
  You can't argue with the amount of effort that went into the study or the
  objectivity of the author.  FWIW, the author found that Amsoil oiled foam filters 
  outperformed both paper and K&N in both filtering capability AND airflow.  
  Supposedly Amsoil only offers their filters for a very limited range of 928 
  applications (approx '88 - '90), but I haven't verified that with their tech 
  department yet.
  
  I'll surf a little to see if I can find the study on-line.  I think it was 
  originally posted on a BMW motorcycle newsgroup.
