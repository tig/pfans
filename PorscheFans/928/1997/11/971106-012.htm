Speaking of your hybrid...no, I have not done a time trial up the
Catalina Hwy.  Perhaps Rob has.  For those readers not in the know, the
Catalina Hwy runs 20-plus miles from Tucson at 2500 feet elevation to
the top of Mt Lemmon, at 10,000-plus feet.  On weekends the road, a
fair-to-good blacktop 2-lane, is choked with cafe racers, bicycles,
turbo 911s, and assorted tourists. Tuesday mid-morning, however...

The road up Kitt Peak is another great run.  It goes from about 2000
feet to over 7000 in about half the distance as the Catalina Hwy, sees a
fraction of the traffic, and is in truly awesome condition (wide,
smooth, sweepers, run-offs, etc).  It also comes with the one of the
world's best collections of telescopes at the top.  And they're open to
the public every day of the year, to boot.
