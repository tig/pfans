I guess I'm the one that got this thread about the brake proportioning valves
started. I apologize that I haven't had the chance to respond or reply about
the subject more. Perhaps they can't reduce nose dive, but from seat of the
pants experience before and after, they sure seem like they do. 
That being said, the brake force regulators may work a little differently
than a straight-up proportioning valve, because, until pressure rises to the
switchover point, the same pressure is applied to the front and rear brakes,
and when the pressure rises further on the input side, the pressure to the
rear brakes is reduced by a set factor. 
A straight-up proportioning valve may, in fact, be applying pressures that
are divided from the get-go, with no switchover point per-say. 
For example: say with my current 55 bar brake force regulator, I drive with a
club foot, and I immediately go beyond the switchover point, then it is
acting more like a pure proportioning valve.  Does this seem logical? 
Oh well, as far as I can figure, especially in a car with ABS, the move from
an 18 bar to a 33 or 55 bar unit, has many advantages with no real drawbacks.
Incidentally, Kim Crumb did say a 33 bar might be better suited for a person
who tends to slam on the brakes instead of easing into them.
