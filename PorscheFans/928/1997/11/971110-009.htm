Below please find the standard equations required to calculate the force 
developed and therein the horsepower required to achieve given speeds.

Force = (.0025) (Cd) (A) (V)2

Frontal area = A
Non dimensional air drag coefficient = Cd
Air velocity of the body in mph = V

Note: the above is for standard air temperature and pressure.

Horsepower = (.0027) (F) (V)

Force = F  (from above calculation)
Air velocity of the body in mph = V

As can be seen from the above, forces increase as the square of velocity, 
and horsepower required increases as the cube of velocity.
