Does your Euro have two distributors like the 84 16-valve engine?  I have heard
of them getting out of synch due to a stretched chain (yes, they're linked
together by a chain).

> Now the car will start okay but dies after a couple of seconds,
> occasionally I can get it to run to high rpm by pumping the accelerator, it

This is a fairly classic control pressure symptom. But there can be other,
maybe multiple causes.

> I've checked; fuel pump(fuel flow OK), throttle potentiometer, air mass
> sensor as best I can (don't have oscilloscope), engine temp. sensor II
> (changed for new), injectors (cleaned), and there are no: leaks in the

> stripped and checked).
> I am in the process of checking the LH control unit (Bosch are sending me a
> wiring diagram)then will start back at the beginning and check everything

I really doubt that the problem will prove to be electrical. Most FI problems
are related to control pressure, or some similar type of fuel restriction.

> Only then will I take it to the boys with the diagnostic machines, which I
> don't have. I was wondering if you might have any suggestions.

Actually, most FI problems require about $150 worth ogf equipment to diagnose.
I just bought today on sale at Automotion (20% off every item in their catalog
11/11-14/97) the fuel pressure test settup ($108 after discount) the Bosch FI
manual is $17(Automotion has it) and then you might need a medium priced
digital multimeter (around $30-40 from Radio Shack or similar).  Hook up the
fuel pressure test instrument as described in the Bosch manual, take readings
and start eliminating your suspects.  Or, a really good 928 mechanic should be
able to diagnose any fuel problem for about the same cost - mine did.

Or,....

The REALLY cheap approach -- get a copy of Performance Products' catalog and
work through the FI troubleshooting guide (in my catalog #42A, it's on page
95).
