> Carlos Amezcua wrote:

> Carlos,
>
> Congrats on the purchase.  My S4 5sp is also a family/track car.  Your
>
> S4 has rear seat shoulder belt pickup points in the C pillars (back by
>
> the hatch).  See one of the used parts guys (David Roberts for example
>
> had a set for $100) - of course some of the S4's came with them as an
> option but I don't think it was very many.  If you get the belts from
> a
> later car, you need the receivers that go in the center console as
> well
> as they changed styles between '87-88 and '89 on.  (When you install
> them, you don't have to remove the interior panels - there's already a
>
> hole in yours so you just cut the hole in the leather and bolt it in.
> Look at an '89 up to get an idea of the location.  The rear seat backs
>
> must be removed for this though.)
>
> Now, about car seats:  I modified one of the (few years old)
> Fischer-Price seats so it would fit in the back for my 17 month old
> son
> (my 4.5 yr. daughter wears the shoulder belt).  I cut some of the
> plastic off the side and bent the rear of the steel rails inward.  F-P
>
> no longer sells these but you can get them at lots of garage sales -
> they were very popular (with good reason - they're sensibly designed
> unlike many others).  If you do this, make sure you're absolutely
> confident in what you're doing.
>
> Porsche now sells a car seat in the US - it was made for the new 911
> but
> it should fit the 928's and 944's.  It's very cute my parts guy has
> said
> - teddy bears and cars with "Porsche" written everywhere or something.
>
> List is for $150-$200 (??) and with 20% off for PCA, it's pretty
> affordable.  This is what I would've done if it was available at the
> time I needed it (I tried to purchase the previous Porsche car seat
> from
> Europe but timing/$ motivated me to try the above).
>
> However, if I were you (which you're not), unless your kid is a
> smallish
> pre-4 year old, I would skip the car seat and belt them in direclty
> with
> the shoulder belt - lap belts are pure terror, if you go here at all,
> make sure the passenger seat is backed up almost against the kid (or
> put
> the kid in the front seat if it's only 2 of you).  Additionally,
> you've
> probably already discovered that the height of the receiver in the
> rear
> center console is too high for a small child - appropriate for an
> adult.  I sacrificed my center console cover (on one side) by cutting
> a
> slot in it (and then sewing on some binding) so that the receiver
> comes
> out of the console just above where it is bolted in.  This give a nice
>
> and much much better fit to a 4 year old.  Again, if you're worried
> about mucking up the car, buy a replacement console, cut up the worst
> one, and save the better one.  Safety (for all occupants) is too
> important.
>
> Best,
> Kurt
> '87 S4 5sp (the list goes on Weltmeister Springs, Koni Reds, and some
> pretty sticky tires)
