Gentlemen, please!  It seems that we are all ignoring several key
calculational variables here.  First, has the cow been "chipped"?  Second,
how much does the cow's rolling radius change at speed?  Third, how does one
calculate the venturi effects of the infinitely variable (rpm dependent, I
think) bovine sphincter on afterburner?  Fourth, does ethanol change the
fuel-air ratio?  Fifth, will a sufficiently powerful radar detector emit
enough microwave energy to affect the pre-cooked weight?   Sixth, if, when
travelling over the car's roof, a cow spins clockwise north of the equator,
does it spin counterclockwise in the southern hemisphere?  Finally, seventh,
if a cow has four stomachs, shouldn't this discussion be on the 356, 924/944
or 914 lists?

Application of Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principal would lead us to question
direct audio observation of the cow's trajectory, as Doppler effects would
change the expected "Moooo?" to "Moo-waah-Moo-ooo-wah..."  (Best observed
with sunroof open.)
