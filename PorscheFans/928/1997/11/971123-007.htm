Andy,

I didn't want to own a Porsche.  I've never wanted one at all.  I have had Lotus' and wanted
Ferraris for years.  Then I got BMWs kinda by mistake - I fell into a very cheap 323i when
I needed another car.  And that taught me two things... it showed me German build quality
and engineering.  And it showed me I didn't mind people thinking I was a wanker for having
a BMW.

I worked my way up the range to an immaculate 535i with all the M-Tech bits.  Driving it
home from the Bay of Islands one afternoon I took some back roads and simply reinforced
the fact that it was a lounge suite.  A couch and two armchairs capable of 0-60 in 7.5
and 220 km/h.  And that was a good thing.  Because I needed a lie down after the
Kaukapakapa - Wellsford road at speed.

My neighbour (a Ferrari owner) was over for a beer the following night and I was speaking
at length about the shortfalls of the 535i and how I should have an M5 instead.  He
handed me an AutoTrader with a 1988 928 5 spd for $70,000 ono and said "Same money,
heaps more car."  I protested loudly that I would never own a Porsche.

He smiled.  He dropped me in a coupe of books with 928 road tests the next day.  I
phoned about the black '88 car and it turned out to be something special.  The rest
is history.  (The story of buying it makes good telling - sometime...)

I simply bought it because it's the fastest, best handling, most practical car that my
budget could buy.  The looks grew on me once I bought it.  Now... I'd probably 
still sell it for a Countach.  Heh heh.  Yeah, like who's kidding who.

Regards,
