There was apparently an option available for our cars of
21mm wheel spacers on the rear. The effect of the spacers
is to make the front and rear track width about the same.
Of course you need longer wheel bolts (studs) to use
the spacers; they are standard Porsche parts.

The reason I say "apparently an option" is that it may
have become standard at some point, but I have no idea
when (if) that happened. Anyway, Porsche must have
thought it was OK for handling, so why should we
doubt them?
