Now on the camera side of the issue, if you get a small camera head that
you can mount "just about anywhere" you'll need some kind of separate
recording device.  Some camcorders are A/V input capable but a lot are
not.  If you have one that is then you can get a camera head with just
two wires that run to the power supply and one to the camcorder.  The
camcorder is now just a slave unit or just a recording device, (ie. you
are not using the lens on the camcorder.)  This (camcorder/recording
device) can then be seat belted or mounded in where ever it's safe and
virtually vibration free.  The camera head power supply on my setup is a
stand alone video battery for Matsushita made camcorders.  They are
commonly used for all Matsushita (Panasonic, Magnavox, Quasar, GE, Curtis
Mathis, Sylvania) full size VHS camcorders and a lot of cellular bag
phones.  They can be had for cheap and supply 12v and are rated at
2000-2300mAh.  This will run the camera head easily for a full day.  The
recording device should have it's own battery supply or a 12v adaptor
made from the manufacturer.


If your camcorder doesn't have A/V input capabilities then you have to
buy a small VCR like a Sony Video Watchman that has it.  Or replace the
camcorder you have with one that has that option.  The Watchman has an
advantage of having a screen to monitor your status when you're setting
the camera up or when playing back you tapes.  Also confirmation that
everything is running when you think it is.

Next issue is the audio.  I use a Radio Shack Stereo Amplified Listener
for $22.00.  CAT# 33-1093A  It's a stereo mic that raises it's mic level
to line level to go into a line input of a camcorder or other recording
device.  Do not attempt to plug a mic into the "mic input" jack when
using an external camera head.  It won't work.

For you guys that are mounting a camcorder itself as the entire package,
I would recommend either 8mm, Hi Band 8mm, or digital video.  They're the
lightest and highest quality.  The use of a full size camcorder is
extremely dangerous due to inertia as well as a huge blind spot for the
driver.  VHS-C sucks due to the lack of recording time and lower quality.
 In a previous post by Kurt Gibble, I agree 100% as to using a car
adapter instead of a camera mounted battery.  You want to make the
camcorder as light as possible.

Also, I personally would NEVER use a plastic tripod head to mount a
camera in a moving vehicle.  Additional tie downs or not, it's just not
worth the risk.  Get a Bogen Head made of metal.  You have a safer and a
more stable mount for your camera.  Then use the additional safety straps
or tie downs.  It's a few bucks more but just like our 928's & Valentine
1's,  "There is no Substitute" for the right stuff.
