At 03:00 AM 11/21/97 +0000, you wrote:
>At a guess I'd say that Porsche went for a seemingly low pressure
>on the rear brakes to aid braking in corners.

Hmm; asking more braking from the fronts in a corner reduces the traction
available to those tires for cornering, leading to (increased) understeer,
the extreme case of which is plowing straight off the road nose first.
Asking too much braking of the rears in a corner can lead to a spin due to
the rear end going straight off the road. Neither of these is pretty,
either to the driver or to an onlooker, so I'd still prefer to see the
fronts and rears working about equally hard on braking.
