I've felt far too many mushy pedals after bleeding to not always be suspicious!
Now you have to wonder whether you still got all the air out!  (I go on muttering
something about the demise of the British car industry to myself.)  Seeing or not
seeing bubbles is rarely a good test of success - the pads move so little and the
pressures are so high (>50 bar as you know), that all it takes is a small bubble
to soften the pedal or change the bias.  I'm guessing that such a small bubble
could easily "cling" to the inside of a bleeder nipple on its way out of the
system.
