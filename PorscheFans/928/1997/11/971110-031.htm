I know how you feel, Paul!  When I was moving from India-no-place to Tucson
-- a clear, dry  Sunday morning (10am) in January 1995, westbound on I-70 in
Kansas, driving alone in a plain Mercury Tracer with Indiana plates, I was
stopped using instant-on radar (clocked at 73 in a 65 zone).  OK, so I get a
ticket after six years with clean record, no big deal.  The cop then
announced that "Sir, this is a major drug trafficing corridor, so I would
like to search your car."  I politely responded "Officer, this is the only
interstate across Kansas, so it must be a major corridor for everything.
 Thank you for asking, but I prefer you did not search my car."  He said "OK,
be that way.  I'm going to keep you here while we get a drug sniffing dog."
 It's his right to do this, so I wait... 40 minutes in the freezing cold
before fido shows up and finds nothing (even the socks were clean!).  I take
my ticket and go.  Six miles down the road, same county, cruise control
locked on 64 mph, I am stopped by one of the first officer's cohorts and
promptly written up for 87 mph!  The two tickets, which I did not contest,
cost $125, but the net cumulative three-year effect on my insurance was
(please sit down) $3,000!
