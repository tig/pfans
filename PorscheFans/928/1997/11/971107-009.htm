I think that all of you are forgetting one minor thing in this top speed
calculations war - Mother Nature.

With every mph increase the wind resistance on the front of your car
also increases (more like doubles I think).  I remember reading a road
test in Road & Track (?) a few years ago that took 6 highly modified
Porsches (ranging from RUF 911's to stock 944 Turbos) and tried to break
200 mph with each car.  I remember it readingthat once you get to 185 or
190 to increase even 1 (ONE) mph takes something like 40 hp.  All the
math was there too.  So, if your car will do 180 stock it will take
approx. 800 extra hp to reach 200 mph.  The same type of numbers apply
to boats to 100 mph as well.
Crazy isn't it?
