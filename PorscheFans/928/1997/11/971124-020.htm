I used an industrial strength carpet adhesive on mine when it started
coming up. 

1. Get a tiny attachment on your vacuum and suck out all the little powdery
old dried up glue. 
2. Apply the adhesive all around the edge of the cardboard (underneath the
leather/vinyl upholstery)
3. Take a ruler or some strips of balsa wood, and make a 'splint' around
the edges of the whole piece with ordinary clothespins or some clamps. You
want the pressure applied evenly so use common sense here, even using balsa
wood to protect the leather/vinyl you can still get uneven attachment if
you dont use enough clothespins. (I had to do mine twice before it looked
right)


If I ever have to do it again I will probably do it a different way. The
way I described is what i used to repair the pieces of 'headliner' or
whatever you want to call it that surround the top of the quarter glass
windows and run down the length of the hatch. If I had it to do over again
i think I would take the whole assembly down to an upholstery shop and have
them make a new one... Then coat the sucker with sealant so i never had to
worry about it.
