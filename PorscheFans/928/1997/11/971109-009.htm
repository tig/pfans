I'm sorry to both of you, but rules of thumb like this are less accurate
that others which are available (e.g., a good calculator for this problem
can be found at http://devserve.cebaf.gov/~bowling/aero.html and is one of
a nice collection of such calculators found at
http://devserve.cebaf.gov/~bowling/auto.html).

Two general issues: first, aerodynamic drag goes up as at least the square
of speed for automotive speeds. It is proportional to the drag coefficient
(Cd) and to the frontal area of the car (which includes the width of the
tires). It is unrelated to the weight of the car.

However (second), rolling resistance of tires also goes up sharply with
speed, and is related to the weight of the car (more properly the weight
minus aerodynamic lift).
