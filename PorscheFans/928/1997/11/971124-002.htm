Each engine has a specific code stamped on it.  The engine code should
be ________ (in the US it would be M28/43 for manual & M28/44 for
automatic).  I do not have the engine serial numbers for euro cars. The
1985 engine for a non-US is a 16-valve car, easily identifiable by the
'spider' air tubes vs. the US has the 32-valve engine with big square
airbox.

> 
> I got this number of the body under the bonnet...WPOZZZ92ZDS840108 is it
> the chassis no?

Yes - Vehicle Id Number as well.  The 'DS' portion indicates a 1983
model not an '85.  The ZZZ portion indicates non-US car although note
all fenders are stamped ZZZ, even in the US.  Have you looked for
stickers at other locations as outlined on my webpage?

> 
> Lastly the gearbox is a bit sticky (the clutch was replaced less than
> 10k ago) can I use (add) some of these magic oil formulas that are
> advertised which give a coating or assist in helping to do all sorts of
> clever things, or do I just stick to the Porsche manual (1983) which
> tells me not to?

Correct, do not add foreign fluids.  Have the level checked it is
probably low.  The 928 transaxle saw improved Borg-Warner synchros from
model year 1985, although all 928 gearboxes are in general, balky when
cold.  Another tip is when first starting out, pause the shifter in
neutral for a count or so between shifts to aide in rpm matching so the
lever slides to the next gear without force.  Once the 'box is warmed it
should shift quite nicely, unless the synchros are worn or someother
maladies affect it...
