Jerry asked: 

>When refilling the tranny the oil goes into the hole right above the
drain hole?

Jerry, the drain hole you're referring to is for the differential only.
 There is a separate drain plug at the other end of the transaxle (below the
torque tube) to allow the transmission part of the transaxle to be drained.

There is only one filler hole; the one you planned on using.  When refilling,
you know you're done when oil comes out of the filler hole (it must come out
to know that you've achieved the correct level).  Your transaxle should take
about 4 quarts of SAE 75W90/GL5 gear oil, if it doesn't, you forgot to drain
the transmission.

Torque all 3 plugs to 15 ft-lbs (very easy to overtighten with such large
allen plugs).
