At 06:02 AM 11/19/97 -0500, you wrote:
>At 60 mph, my tach reads 1725 rpm. (reading between the 200 rpm
>hash marks). At 80 mph, my tach reads 2300 rpm.

Holy cow, Batman! Since the 60 and 80 mph tach readings are in the same
proportion as the road speeds, it's not as though your tach is conking out
at higher engine speeds (not that 2300 rpm is high). If these readings are
correct, you either have a mysterious 2.04:1 final drive (2.20:1 stock) or
an equally mysterious 0.928:1 fifth gear (1.00:1 stock)

>FWIW, One published spec I found in Performance Portfolio gives a speed of
>32.5 mph per 1000 rpm, and another gives 33 mph per 1000 rpm. (in 5th gear)
>Which would figure to be about: 
>60 mph at 1850 rpm, 80 mph at 2450 rpm, and 160 mph at 4900 rpm.   
>While my car figures to give a speed of very near 35 mph per 1000 rpm.....?

Stock gears and tires lead to a figure of 32.3 mph per 1000 rpm.

>My car idles between 650 rpm and 700 rpm. Seems about right to me.....
>(now checking spec)
>The spec says idle is supposed to be 675 rpm, + or - 25 rpm. (not adjustable)
>I would think from my current speed verses rpm observations, that my tach
>must be way out of wack, but the idle speed doesn't seem to bear this out.
>My rev limiter does work, (scares the bejesus out of me when it kicks in) and
>from the few times it's kicked in, (not intentional, mind you) I believe it
>cuts fuel at around 6600 rpm. (the spec says 6600 rpm)

The idle and rev limiter speeds are right, and so constitute a reasonable
test of the tach at the low and high ends of its range. Since it's also
indicating proportional to road speed, it's really not showing any bad
symptoms. If you can arrange it, I'd be interested in seeing what a clip-on
external tach shows when your built-in tach indicates 2300.

>As you can probably guess, I'm still confused. Perhaps I have some kind
>of super-duper gear in my car!   (top speed = warp 9)  :)

If your tach checks out, you do (although it would be too tall a gear to
permit the engine to power the car to a higher-than-stock top speed). This
confuses me, since Porsche doesn't admit to having made more than one
gearing configuration for '87-88 US-spec S4s (no apostrophe!). Maybe others
on the list with manual-shift cars of this vintage can take a moment to
check their tach readings at 80 mph in 5th (I get 2550, Greg gets 2300) and
report back.
