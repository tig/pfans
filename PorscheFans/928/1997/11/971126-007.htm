John,

"Tuck-In" is the equivalent term for oversteer.  That is, if your rear end
oversteers (i.e., goes out), then conversely you front end tucks in.  No more
complicated than that.  Some people like to refer to the front end when
discussing cornering effect; understeer refers to front end behavior and so
does tuck in, while oversteer really refers to what's going on at the back.
They're both "effect", not "cause".

I've seen this term used all over the place to describe cornering behavior.
