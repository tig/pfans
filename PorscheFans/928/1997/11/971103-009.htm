At 08:13 PM 10/31/97 -0600, you wrote:
>I have an 87 S4.  When I turn the headlights on they do a lot of 
>flickering.  If I step on the brake pedal it seems to stop for several 
>seconds  then the flickering continues.  Could the problem be a failing 
>fuze, circut breaker or selenoid or what ever the large device is that 
>is located in the fuze panel.

Maybe, but it could also be one or both of the push-on connectors behind
the bulbs coming loose. These have a tricky latch which sometimes comes
undone (or is left loose by those of little patience). Remove the plastic
rear cover from each headlight assembly (turn head lights on and then
switch off ignition to leave the frog eyes up but not lit; remove three
flush screws, NOT the ones recessed through holes; pull gently back) and
check to see if the connectors are fully on and latched.
