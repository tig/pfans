In a message dated 11-21, Andy McIntyre writes:
The Steel panels are Hot-dip galvanized.  This deposites a layer of Zinc over
the top of the steel.  Zinc, like aluminium, doesn't rust.  The coating can
be worn away by scratching, sanding, etc.  Thus we may also be concerned over
the reactions between zinc and Al.  The term Galvanizing comes from L.
Galvani, an Italian Physiologist who died in 1798.  He used DC electricity
for medical purposes.
