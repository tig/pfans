If one has an adjustable brake bias control you can tune front-rear
brake bias.  Take an old set of tires (all the same) to a big parking lot
and do a hard stop from 40mph or so locking up all 4 wheels.  Will not work
with abs on.  The objective is to get all 4 wheels to lock at the same time.
Measure the distance between the start of the front tire skids to the start
of the rear wheel skids.  If wheels lock at the same time this distance will
be the wheel base of the car.  If your front brakes are locking before your
rear brakes more presure to the rears will reduce nose dive somewhat by
reducing weight transfer to the front wheels.
