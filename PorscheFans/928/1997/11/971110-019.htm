J P Rodkey wrote:

> Some of you know I'm chasing an engine/idle problem (starts okay, as
> warms up, worse until it won't run.   Sooty plugs, very rich smell).

Too rich could be a leaking cold start injector or one that fails to
shut off when the engine becomes warm.

> Car can/will start just fine and can freely rev, but as engine warms
> (e.g. fuel flow over time), loses power, runs rough, then stalls.   It

> can usually be fired right back up w/o trouble, but quickly revs drop
> back down, tries to run for 15 seconds or so, then stalls.

>  I no longer think problem is heat related - I think it's fuel
> volume/flow related.

I agree.  I vote for fuel system pressure regulator, control pressure
regulator, leaking cold start injector or clogged fuel filter. You have
to run the complete fuel pressure test using a fuel pressure gauge with
hoses and valve similar to that sold by Performance Products and
others.  See Motorbooks "How to Tune & Modify Bosch Fuel Injection" ISBN
0-87938-570-7 about $20.  Essential reading for any Bosch injected car.
Covers Motronic & Jetronic D,L,LH, K, KE systems. Has extensive "how to"
guides for testing the system.

>My question(s):
>        1.    Anyone experience a fuel pump (I have the single pump
>directly under the fuel tank system) to initially pass pressure/volume
>delivery test but degrade rapidly as the engine warms up?

I don't believe so.  When I thought I had a failing fuel pump, I was
told that the failure mode is either reducing output volume over a
period of time (as pump gets weaker) followed by sudden total failure or
just sudden failure. My fuel pump was fine.

>        2.    Can faulty fuel distributor restrict bypass of fuel (re
> above test for fuel bypass/return).

This is the last place to look.  The distributor is VERY expensive and
allegedly not rebuildable.  But the fuel LINES might be a good place to
look, check for kinks, tight bends etc.

>        3.    Any suggestions to help diagnose and isolate problem?

None other than doing or having done a pressure test.  Should take about
an hour of labor to perform - or buy the gauge (around $130) and book
($20) and do it yourself.
