(David wrote:)
> as I removed the battery I noticed the tell-tail yellow/white
> powder-like spoor at the bottom of the battery box.  I coated the
> bottom of the box in a bit of baking soda and sprayed some water over
> it and carefully rinsed with clean water.  Most of the water and
> salt-goo drained onto the ground through the box's weep holes.  Then I
> thoroughly dried the box.  When I was done it was as good as new - no
> rust, no acid salts.
> 
> Total cost - about $0.50. Did I do a bad thing?  I think I'd rather do
> this once or twice a year rather then leave a mat in the box.

  I don't think that using baking soda and water (and a thorough drying)
  was a bad thing; at least you neutralized all of the acid.  The advantage
  of using a battery mat is that it continuously neutralizes the acid and
  therefore prevents corrosion from occuring.  Your method will neutralize
  acid once or twice a year, but won't prevent corrosion from occuring during
  the time between cleanings.
  
  BTW, I understand that the Battery Mat was originally developed for mil-spec
  applications, so it inevitably went through some (rigorous) testing.
