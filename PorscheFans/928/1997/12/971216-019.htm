Several posts have mentioned different chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) and what 
the numbering means. I will try to clear up the subject. The nomenclature 
for CFCs is a code based on the "rule of 90". To derive the chemical formula 
for cfc-22, e.g., add "90" to 22, the resulting number is 112 indicates 
1-carbon 1-hydrogen & 2 fluorine yielding ClCHF2 (chlorodifluromethane). For 
cfc-12, 90+12=102, 1-carbon, 0-hydrogen, 2-fluorine yielding CCl2F2 ( 
dichlorodifluoromethane). cfc-114, 90+114=204, 2-carbon 0-hydrogen & 
4-fluorine yielding C2Cl2F4 (1,2-dichloro-1,1,2,2,-tetrafluoroethane ).
