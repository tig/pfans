I know I posted earlier this year, and there was a two-week thread about
fuel additives.   But, just for argument sake, here's my .02 worth:

Having lived six years in Wisconsin and during that time dealing with
storage of cars, boats, chainsaws, lawnmowers, motorcycles, etc., I've
come to the conclusion that Sta-Bil is best used when the stored item is
in a heated area.   If stored in a pole barn, for example, Sta-Bil has
no appreciative effect compared with 'unprotected' engines.   No science
here, just personal observation.

I often had trouble getting a 'Sta-Bil'ed engine running and tuned after
treatment, but really had no trouble at all with engines left alone
(other than fogging) at storage time.   If these items were going to be
stored longer than one year, it may be a different story.   My
experience convinced me to quit using the stuff and concentrate on other
aspects of storage.

Good luck to all putting their sharks to temporary sleep!
