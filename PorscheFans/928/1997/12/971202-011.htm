Hi Pat,
Couple of things to check.  Did you get brake fluid on the rotors or pads 
while bleeding and refilling?  Did you resurface the rotors?  If you didn't, 
do the rotors have a lot of wear?  It could be taking a while for the new pads 
to bed in to the old rotors.  Did you install the new pads and go racing?  
Without proper bedding in, and high heat loads, the pads can crack.  A bad 
master cylinder will usually send the pedal to the floor.  Installing new pads 
means pushing the caliper piston back into the caliper.  If the brake fluid 
has not been changed regularly, there will be some corrosion in the caliper.  
When the pistons are pushed back into this corrosion, they will sometimes 
stick.  You can't push the pistons too far back into the caliper.  

My Nastassja is going to get new pads, rotors, and bearings this winter.  I 
have 123k on the original rotors.
