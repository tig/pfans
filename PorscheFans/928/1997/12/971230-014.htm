Thought I'd pass along something I stumbled across which may already be public
knowledge. Its a quote of Tony Lapine, from the book "Legendary Porsche Inside
History of the Epic Cars" by Randy Leffingwell. Talking about styling the new
car the quote is as follows:
	"I admired the lads who came up with the 'folded paper' styling first. It was
innovative, exciting. But our tradition was not folds. A shark does not have
creases. A dolphin does not have creases. When 'folded paper' was en vogue, we
had but one crease on the 928. At the Geneva introduction, we were
surrounded."
	Could this be were the 'shark' name come from. I wonder how much the 924/944
clan would take to being called 'dolphin's'.
