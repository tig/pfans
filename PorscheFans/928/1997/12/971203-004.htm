	Well here is my 2 cents worth on insurance:  Never take the first offer, 
tell them you are going to cunsult with a lawer (but don't bother), they 
will come back with a better offer.  Know what you will get will make 
nobody happy, more than what the ins wanted to give but less then you 
want!  If you can prove the car was valued at more than book value give 
it a try.  GOOD LUCK!  Insurance is not there to make you happy, it's 
there to make a really bad situation less bad....   One option total the 
car and buy the scrap car back from the ins company (yes they own it when 
totaled) take the car and the cash to a body shop, tell them you will 
find the parts or give them info needed to get used parts and the best 
prices on new parts, treat yourself to a complete paint job and enjoy 
your fixed car, sell it and loss a ton of money!   See the body shop 
quote included new retail parts,  and you can alwasy get lucky.   There 
were many delays getting my 914 painted, I needed a new windshield for 
the 928, they felt bad about the delay on the 914.  So he called his 
glass supplier, told them he broke the glass, "help",  they had some 
glass that had been sitting on the shelf since these cars were sold new,  
installed $360!   Find a reasonable body shop and be NICE, tell them you 
are not in a hurry, help them find the parts, don't get upset about 
delays.  Look in the adds in the back of the Porsche club to find parts, 
parting out cars.
