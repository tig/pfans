Catherine-Platt.Irwin-1@usa.dupont.com said:
> Below is the answer to your questions: 
>
> When were "Freon" 12 and "Freon" 22 invented?
>
> "Freon" 12 (CFC-12, or dichlorodifluoromethane, CCl2F2) was invented
> in  1928, by Thomas Midgley, who worked for the Frigidare Division of
> General  Motors.  Midgley was trying to find a replacement for toxic
> refrigerants  that were in use at the time:  sulfur dioxide, methyl
> chloride, and  ammonia.  CFC-12 was commercially produced in 1931 by a
> new company,  Kinetic Chemicals, set up by DuPont and General Motors.
>
> "Freon" 22 (HCFC-22 or chlorodifluoromethane, CHClF2) was discovered
> (along  with several other chlorofluorocarbons) over the next 2 - 3
> years.  HCFC-22  became commercially available in 1936, and became
> widely used as a  refrigerant after World War II.
