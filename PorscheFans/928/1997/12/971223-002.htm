Sorry Ed.  I don't have chrome wheels, but I am argumentative AND curious.  So

this is purely and academic excercise for me.  If anyone can explain why or

why not wheels should not be chromed, please do.  I am interested in knowing

if the heat range of the chroming process can actually weaken the temper of

the wheel.  

	In my experience the surface of the object that had to be chromed would first
be submerged in a caustic cleaning solution. Followed by a nice bath in an
acid solution( I believe the local's use sulfuric acid), then the process of
adding a nickel base and polishing to a shine, followed by copper, then nickel
and finally chrome, to achieve a layered effect.  This is the process I am
familiar with.  If this were the same used for alloy wheels.  The wheel
designated for chroming would almost certainly need to be made of a denser or
somewhat different mix of alloy to withstand the chroming process.  Of course,
being that I am a new member to this list, I have missed the first post on
this subject.  So It could be quite possible that there is a different method
to chrome alloy wheels.  But, in the way I described, It would be the acid
weakening the alloy to cause the cracking of the wheel under high load stress.
I guess this does not answer your question about heat affecting temper.  But
it is my understanding that depending on the heat intensity and duration of
heating does affect the temper of metals., We regularly sent finished products
off to be "heat treated" after we plated them,  i.e, our process affected the
temper of grade eight fasteners, and they had to be heat treated after our
process.
	Now of course I could be comparing apples to oranges, so I am prepared for
the inevitable flame.  But, as they say, that's my 928 cents worth.
