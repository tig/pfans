>>I'm sitting here going over the MBS thing and I wonder, could this be a type=
>> of feedback loop?  1) the heat going through the ducts heats up the=
>> resistor pack 2) the car checks the resistor temp 3) the cars tries to cool=
>> them off by turning the fan up for the preset amount of time 4) the preset=
>> sample/wait time goes by...     and back to the top.

You've got the right basic idea. First, the resistor pack has a
thermostatic protective device, that shorts it out when it gets
too hot. That causes the fan speed to go to high. The increased
airflow then cools the resistor pack, so the protective device
opens up again, and the fan speed returns to normal (for a while).

Chances seem good that it's a symptom of the blower getting tired,
and drawing a bit more current than it should. The only fly in
the ointment is that people on this list have replaced both
the blower and the resistor pack (separately) without curing
the MBS. Nobody has taken the current readings to verify all
this, as far as I've heard.
