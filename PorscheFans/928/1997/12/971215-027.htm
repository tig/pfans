>>I have at technical question:  Wy do we need both a fuel regulator
>>and a fuel damper?
>>
>>I understand the need for having a constant pressure on the injector
>>to control fuel volume delivery to each cylinder.  However, I think
>>having just a fuel damper should be up to the job of keeping the
>>right pressure in the fuel rail.
>>
Interesting question. Notice that earlier L-Jet cars have one damper
and two regulators, later LH-Jet cars have two dampers and one
regulator. My guess is just that it's two stages of regulation, to
get a steadier pressure. One thing to be aware of is that the fuel
pressure is constant relative to the manifold pressure (vacuum,
really) so that there's a constant pressure drop across each
injector.
