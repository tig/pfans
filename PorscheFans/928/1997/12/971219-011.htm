I also had the hose, just under the air box fail. This is a very short,   
bell shaped hose. I almost took the car to the shop when it started   
spitting and steaming in traffic. Fortunately, the temp never climbed   
very high, so I decided to drive the car home and look for myself first.   
Good thing. I replaced it in just a few minutes (after a quick trip to   
the parts store) for about $2. As much as I like my mechanic, this would   
have been at least $35 - $50 w/ labor.

Moral of the story - Look before you leap.
