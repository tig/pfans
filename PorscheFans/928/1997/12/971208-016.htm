intake manifold (both sides).  The 16V engine has the plenum chamber in the
middle, with a Porsche crest.  (32V has dual plenums, one on either side).

Just a note on the "specs" for a 928 vundercar...

I don't believe a ring/pinion from the automatic transaxle will fit a manual,
and vice-versa, since the pinion design is different (I believe the ring gear
is the same; auto or manual).  The auto pinion has a through-hole which allows
it to be placed on the output shaft of the transmission which protrudes into
the separate differential case where the pinion is located.

In the manual, the transmission and diff cases are one piece, and the pinion
has a long shaft which actually fits into the transmission part of the
combined case.  So unless someone knows otherwise, the auto 2.20 ring/pinion
won't fit into the 5-speed.  So you'll have to use one of the available manual
ring/pinion combinations for the vundercar.
