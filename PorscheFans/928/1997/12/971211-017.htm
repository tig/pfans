My 85 had a similar problem.  The thing appear to have flodded for no
good reason.

Same type of repairs that I tried was to change the fuel pump,
check the relay, then, after recharging the battery, I decided to 
let the engine crank a bit more and the car almost started.

Let the battery recharge and crank some more and the thing finally
started.  A lot of smoke came out for at least 5 minutes, and I think
now that my O2 sensor might be bad.  The fuel pressure is OK and
the car did not have any other problem since.

If you think the car is flodded, take the spark plugs out
for the night to let the gas evaporate.  Then try starting the car.
I would find it curious that the computer died for no apparent
reasons (except if your battery charger has a problem?)
