Mark,
 I had a receiver hitch welded to the rear bumper with two triangulated 
supports.  Behind the license plate, a square hole was cut into the rear 
bumper cover where the receiver was lined up and welded flush with the 
bumper cover.  Now, I just tilt my license plate up on a spring hinge, 
insert the hitch, and voila, instant 928 tow-vehicle.  (I used it mostly 
for towing my 4 x 8 race trailer and jet-ski.)
 One time, I put a bigger ball on it, rented a car-dolly from U-Haul*, 
and towed my DP 928 from Dallas to Austin (210 miles) with no problems.  
I got quite a few strange looks, seeing a "regular" looking 928 towing a 
wide-body 928 with a blower sticking out of the engine bay.
 The towing vehicle weighed 3130 lbs, DP weighed about 3500lbs + misc 
luggage/tools.  Brakes worked pretty well, considering over 100% increase 
in mass (I did have Porterfield racing pads on there).  The only thing I 
felt took a little strain was the double-disk clutch (more slipping, 
naturally, to get things going). But I saw no ill-effects in the long 
term.


 * U-Haul laughed at me when I told them what the tow vehicle was, and 
declined to rent it to me.  I came back later with a borrowed truck. ;)
