> Grand Auto has a 12 quart catch pan that will slide under the 928. 
> Automotion sells those drain plug spigot adaptors.  The big problem is
> you gotta lift the car to get at the oil filter, then lower it to drain
> as much old oil out of the pan.

Why lower it before draining? I have (4) "ramps" made of two 2"x8"
boards, stacked. My ramps are short enough to go under the car yet tall
enough to change the oil filter once the car is up.

Place one before each wheel. Back up a bit. Charge forward. And brake
after the 2nd bump. Its a bit of a challenge as all 4 wheels have to 
bump up the 1-1/2" board thickness at the same time.

When all 4 corners are up, the car is properly level for draining and 
filling oil.
