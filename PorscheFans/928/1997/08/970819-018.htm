Steve Peck asks:
>
> Can anyone send me the procedures for rear wheel bearing replacement?  

0) Buy new bearings. Also suggest new C-clips for retaining bearings.
   And a jar of anti-seize compond.
1) Jack the car up. Support with jack stands.
2) Take rear wheels off.
3) Figure out how to get that blasted nut off the halfshaft.
   Its tigtening torque is 300 ft-lbs.
4) Take the rear brakes off.
5) Disconnect everything from the big casting housing the bearing.
6) Remove this casting.
7) Do same for other side.
8) Drive other car to machine shop. Pay $15 each to have assembly
   heated, old bearings removed, new bearings installed.
9) Assembly is reverse of disassembly. :-)
