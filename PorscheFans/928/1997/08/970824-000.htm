Hi Madison:

This is a good question for Susan or Marc, since Devek pioneered the
swaps I think and I do hope someone here can lend their experience
also.  FWIW, alot of race cars employ a double-disk clutch because it's
supposed to be smaller and lighter with less rotational mass but the
same or higher gripping surface area vs. a single disk.  Note that
Porsche did dispense with the double disk themselves though, returning
to a single disk.  Evidently even the single disk variety differ from
each other ie. Lucky installed a GTS clutch in my '90.

Another factor to consider is the cost of parts...  time to start
calling around and perusing ads in excellence!  And of course you are
always welcome to check out my shop manuals, I'll take a look also.
