> My first thought at this point was that perhaps the signal which

> below). Please note that you have an intermittent problem. The fact

> the pump and associated wiring. 
> 
> >   As far as the "tricks", here's what I've tried with varying luck at 
> >   different times.  None of these tricks have made the car start each
> >   time I try them, so I haven't been able to isolate any particular bad

> >     1) jumper around the fuel pump relay
> >     2) remove the air filter and slightly depress the flap on the air

> #1 seems to argue against my idea of the relay signal wiring being at

> This sounds like another trick--one which shouldn't have anything to
> do with the problem apparently fixing the problem, but actually

> >   Can anyone tell from this account what might be wrong?  Could the symptoms

> A tough problem, Jim. Check your wiring diagram--could the problem be
> an intermittent contact (one of many) in the ignition switch? The
> ignition switch seems like one area where side effects (trying it
> again) would be going on when you tried one of the tricks.
> 
> The one solid piece of data you list is that when the car won't start,
> the fuel pump isn't running. This to some extent isolates the
> problem. Add to this that simply jumpering the fuel pump relay does
> not always make it start, and you have a larger problem--something
> else besides the fuel pump must also not be working. Check back
> through the wiring diagram to see what else is controlled by the fuel
> pump relay signal, and see what switches that. This train of logic is
> how I got to the ignition switch. 
> 
> You might also try to reset your ideas about the problem. Maybe you
> are overlooking something, or maybe you don't quite have the
> description of what works and what doesn't right.
