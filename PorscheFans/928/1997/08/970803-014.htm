>Even the final 928's had awful Cd's.

Gosh, David, how awful is a Cd of 0.34, which is characteristic of every
'87+ 928?

>important for a car such as the 928 to be stable at high speeds

You bet, which is why the '87+ cars have the rear wing (upside-down airfoil
to produce downforce increasing with speed; does so more effectively and
with better downforce-to-drag than the lip spoiler it replaced) as well as
the air dam up front (reduces airflow under the car, which otherwise acts
as a big rightside-up airfoil). The front belly pan smooths out under-car
airflow, improving the effectiveness of the dam and reducing drag.
