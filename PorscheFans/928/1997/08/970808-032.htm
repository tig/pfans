The cooling system design is such that, when the thermostat is
fully closed, all the water leaving the engine goes through the 
"bypass" to the waterpump; the exit to the radiator is blocked
off. When the thermostat is fully open, the bypass is blocked
off and the exit to the radiator is open. Partially open
thermostat, some water goes each way. Ditto for no thermostat,
so I agree that the cooling in that case is probably less than
with a fully open thermostat (don't worry about Kurt, he's 
driving his BMW anyway, I bet).
