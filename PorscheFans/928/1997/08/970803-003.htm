I have done the spray can dyes as well as the 'proper' matched-dye
sprayed via a detail spray gun.  Here are my three cents worth:
    1.    The spray can color dye is the least desirable.   You will get
a decent result, but the stuff can be scratched off and rubbed off over
time.   Also, matching colors is sheer luck.
    2.    The professional fabric/trim dye, purchased from auto paint
supply stores is the way to go (like PPG, etal).  It will give a much
more durable finish, and you can get an exact match - in various
glosses, etc.   I have painted black door panels to a light blue with
very satisfactory results (one coat!).   Therefore, you absolutely CAN
paint light colors over dark (including black).
    3.    The best fix, of course, is to replace the panels if at all
practical and skip the whole mess/bother/risk of trying to match and
duplicate.

Hope this helps!
