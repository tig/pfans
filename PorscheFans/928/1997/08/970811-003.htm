This saturday, while talking to my mechanic (t-belt is now retensioned),
I asked how he sets a 928 on jack stand.  His recommendation:

Rear end:
	Place jackstand (with a rubber piece on it) in the middle
	of the big aluminum crossbar under the transmission.
	This would raise the whole rear end.

Front end:
	Place a 2x4 under the steel cross-member behind the
	steering rack.  Put jack in the middle and lift.

Is anyone using these lifting point on their car?  I know they are not
the recommended lifting spot but they do apear to make some sense.  The
rear crossmember has to support the whole rear end, it should be
strong enough to lift the car.  The front end, should have a crossection
strong enough but I have not tried it yet.

Comments are welcomed.
