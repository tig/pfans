>Someone (sorry, I forget who) pointed out that the resistor pack
>which controls the fan speed has thermostatic protection; when
>the pack gets hot, the thermostat shorts across all the resistors,
>which causes the fan to run at full speed, producing airflow across
>the resistor pack, which then cools down so that the thermostat
>opens up and the fan returns to normal speed. The whole process
>takes five to ten seconds. Also, the most power is dissipated in
>the resistors when the fan is running at its lowest speed; that's
>the only time it ever happens to my car, I finally realized.
>
>So far, so good. However, that doesn't explain how the fan can run
