Something's wrong. It should be very rare for the gauge to indicate
anywhere above the second white line, even in the recent heat with full
a/c. The electric cooling fans shouldn't be needed except in stop-and-go
city traffic.

You may have some sort of clog in your cooling system, or your water pump
may be getting weak. A competent shop can check all of this quickly and
unambiguously.

If you're on the highway and keep the car in a lower gear without doing
hard acceleration (e.g., in third at 60-70, waiting/hoping for traffic to
clear on a crowded interstate), coolant temperature can climb impressively,
but this goes away in tens of seconds as soon as you shift up and get the
revs down.
