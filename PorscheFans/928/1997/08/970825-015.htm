>I really want a GT and it has to be a '89 or newer. Am I dreaming???

No, there are good cars available.  But, you must be patient. An
inexpensive start would be to purchase Issue #66, December 1996 of
Excellence magazine.  Back issues are available for $5.00 each (includes
P+H) from Excellence, P.O. Box 1529, Ross, CA 94957.  Bruce Anderson
does his usual fine job of describing the various models, years,
features, pitfalls, and market values of the 928s. 

Also, a web search on 928 will turn up a lot of interesting info. Be
sure to visit Phil Tong's web site, call or e-mail Marc & Susan at Devek
(they may have a customer who has a car for sale) and follow the basic
rule about searching for anything - tell everyone you know! You probably
already know the First Rule about buying a Porsche - buy the newest and
best example you can afford (easier said than done).

Since S4s and GTs were both sold in the US from 1989-91 the surefire way
to tell a real GT is that the engine is type M28.47 whereas the S4 is
type M28.42. Also, the GT has trans type G28.55 for the manual w/ltd
slip standard.

Porsche Market Letter, Classifieds2000 or a broker such as Brian could
also be helpful in locating your car.
