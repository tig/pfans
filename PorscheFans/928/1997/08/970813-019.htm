This is something you can only observe under full-tilt racing conditions,
and can be fixed by baffles in the pan (sold, strangely enough, by Devek).
No street situation or likely PCA driver ed situation will ever expose you
to risk here, or with the other pre-GTS oiling problem, a starvation at
certain main bearings at sustained (10+ seconds) red-line (6500+ rpm)
engine speed. For those who are going racing, your crank can be drilled to
fix this problem.

>Can you retrofit a double-disc unit to a later car?

Lucky Ekman says he can. By the way, I thought you wanted an automatic!

>Any (major) body changes after '87?  (If not, how can you tell S4/GT/GTS
>apart, without seeing a rear model designation?)

A real, original GT has wider wheels than an S4, with scallops rather than
slots around the rim (S4s got slightly different scalloped wheels in '90)
and a dual-outlet exhaust system (though variations abound).

GTS cars have mild barrel-shaped flares at the rear, covering five-spoke
"Cup" design 17" wheels (widely retrofit to earlier cars). A GTS also has a
red reflector strip between the rear lights and a body-color (rather than
matte black, as in S4s and GTs) rear wing.

By the way, most GTs do not have a model designation on the rear.

>I've been working on the assumption that I want an '87+ for the improved
>aerodynamics; I definitely want a 32v engine and ABS. ABS became standard
>in '86, but was it available in '85 as an option?

Not in the US, so far as I know. The improved aerodynamics of the '87+ cars
are part of a (big) package of changes which distinguish the S4 from the S,
but the late '86 cars have most of the engine power and all of the brakes
of the S4. They lack the wider rear wheels and tires, the snazzy-looking
wing, and some of the current price, so may be worth your considering (the
bank you rob can be smaller).
