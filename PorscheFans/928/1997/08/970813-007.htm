Let me add something to all this vacumn, cruise control, temperature
problems.  I have my dash and console all taken apart as part of the
restoration project.  I can tell you all right now that it is worth the
effort to verify ALL vacumn-related functions.  After spending 30+ hours
checking things, I found that I have two bad vacumn actuators, a leaky
rubber coupler and a leaky check valve.  The vacumn relays all checked
out ok.  Since replacing all defective components, I am confident that
my vacumn circuit is tight.  If your 928 have at least 70K on the
speedo,  there is a good chance that you will have at least one bad
vacumn actuator.  These things don't last very long.  The diaphragm
inside get perforated and you get a air leak.  In general, when there is
a vacumn leak the engine will get false air and that will throw off the
brain.  Vacumn problem suck!  :-)
