Your oil pressure gauge SHOULD be pegged pretty much any time the engine is
warm and running at any speed significantly above idle. The spec book and
shop manual state that oil pressure should be at least 5 bar (the top of
the gauge's scale) when the engine is warm and running at 4000 rpm.

Practically, this means that you'll only see the gauge come off the top peg
when you let the engine come down to idle. The gauge is thus a microscope
into slow-speed fluctuations. If you find this strange, welcome to the
club. However, there seems to be some agreement in the Fatherland that this
is the right way to run an oil pressure gauge; my 20 year old Mercedes V8
sedan's oil pressure gauge works the same way.
