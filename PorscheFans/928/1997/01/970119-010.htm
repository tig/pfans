> Dennis,
> 
>     It took me a bit of looking to locate it, even after people telling me
> where it was. If you lay down under the back of your car, on the passenger
> side,  look up between the rear bumper cover and the back of the fuel tank.
> You will see two straps that appear to hold the fuel tank up-they do! If you
> follow the straps about half way down and each one should have a 10mm nut
> holding a cover on. Once you remove the two nuts, the cover can slide toward
> the rear of the car and then down. You will then see, hidden in a recess in
> the tank, the fuel pump and filter. You will need a standard screw driver, a
> 17mm open or flarenut wrench, and two 19mm open or flarenut wrenches. You will
> also need a container for the fuel that will spill out of the old filter(about
> 16 oz.). Make sure your garage door is open, I got a little dizzy when I did
> mine last night. I also recommend the use of latex gloves whenever you work on
> your car, for cleanliness and health reasons(safe wrenching). I removed my
> fuel pump, cleaned it up, and put the rubber boots on correctly, they were
> just hanging there when I removed the cover, the last person must have been
> too lazy to reinstall them correctly. They were a bitch to get on. I used my
> Craftsman cotter key puller, it looks like an icepick with a curve bent on it.
> What a useful tool! It's great for stuck on hoses too. I think its only $3.95
> and worth every penny! Hope this helps you out, I will be looking for a
> posting of your success.
> 
>         Jim Murphy  88 S4 5spd
>       jrmillus@msn.com
