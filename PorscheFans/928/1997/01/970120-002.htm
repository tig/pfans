RE: spark plugs tools and tricks 

I found I need two sockets.  One for installing without the rubber
insert plug gripper and one for removal with the plug gripper.  Or
just use the same one but take out the gripper on installation.  If
I don't take out the gripper, the socket ends up inside with the plug
and I have to remove/reinstall.

Some extension bars and a flex head ratchet work pretty well.   Finger 
tighten the plugs by dropping the plug in the hole and then lower the
socket over the plug, and using the extension bar turn it gently 
before torquing it down.

I tend to use the copper paste anti-seize although I've heard some 
people don't.  Don't over torque it - the block is aluminum - alot
softer than cast iron.
