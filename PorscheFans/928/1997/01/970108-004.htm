     >On a related note, what was the first year for the GTS and what 
     >were its performance specs vs. the S4? Any cosmetic changes?
     
     The GTS first came out in the 1993 model year.  It's a *lot* 
     faster than the S4, at least on paper (I've never driven a GTS).  
     It would also be quite a bit more expensive on the used-car 
     market.  From what I've read here on the list, you could just 
     retrofit a GTS crankshaft in an S4 and wind up with similar 
     power.  Does anybody know how much that would cost, parts and 
     labor?  Car & Driver had the following numbers for the 
     automatic-transmission 1983 S, 1985 S (first 32-valve model), 
     1987 S4, and 1993 GTS (I'm working from memory here,so cut me 
     some slack!):
     
                power   torque  0-60    1/4 mi  handling
     ----------------------------------------------------------------
     1983 S     234     ~270    7.0     ~15.3   .76 g (lame tires)
     1985 S     288     302     6.2     14.7    .83
     1987 S4    316     317     6.6     14.7    .85
     1993 GTS   345     369     5.3     13.8    .87
     
     That's not a typo that the '87 was slightly slower in lower-speed 
     acceleration that the '85 model.  It had a higher top speed, 
     though, and higher top-end acceleration.  The only cosmetic 
     change that I'm aware of for the GTS is wider fender flares in 
     back to accomodate the slightly larger wheels.  FWIW, I believe 
     that the '90 S4 had a few changes that sound good to me: 
     electronic limited-slip differential, tire-pressure warning 
     lights, and sophisticated knock sensor. Comments, anyone?
