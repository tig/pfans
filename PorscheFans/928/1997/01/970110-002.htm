>>The 928 rear ratio isn't really all that high (numerically low).  5th in the

>>5spd's.  What you want to look at is the final drive ratio which is takes into

OK, I'll bite - maybe the real measure is "feet traveled per cylinder
firing". Back of the envelope: my 928 goes about 70 mph at 2000 rpm;
that's 6160 feet per minute at 8000 firings per minute = 0.77. My
4-cylinder Toyota goes maybe 60 at 2500 rpm, for an FPF (feet per
firing) of 1.05. So the 928 rear axle ratio is actually rather
high, numerically. 

I still would prefer the 2.7, personally.
