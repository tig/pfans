Just for fun, I went to Bowling's Automotive Programs:
http://devserve.cebaf.gov/~bowling/auto.html
which has a whole set of programs to calculate auto-related
quantities. Plugging in some reasonable numbers, it says my
car would take 224 hp to go 146 mph, its official factory top
speed. Fairly close, actual driveline losses probably a little
higher though. Using the same numbers, the program calculates
that it would take 348 hp to go 169 mph (= 272 kph), and 580 hp
to go an even 200 mph. Bring on the superchargers!

Incidentally the same site has a "Highway dynamometer" program
that lets you get a reasonable estimate of your engine's power
curve. Pretty cute.
