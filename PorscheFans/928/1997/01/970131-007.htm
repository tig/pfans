On the contrary, Split Fires' electrode arrangement is based on a sound 
combustion principle.  The split ground electrode allows the air fuel mixture 
to reach the spark.  Plus the split electrode allows the resulting flame 
kernel to ignite the rest of the air fuel mixture in the combustion chamber 
more easily.  It's the same principle that racers use when they index the 
spark plugs using shims so the open side of the plug faces the intake port.  
Obviously, Split Fires do not create two sparks.  Their commercial is dumb.  
Whether the Split Fires are worth six bucks a piece, however, is up to the 
individual.
