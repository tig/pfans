I was removing it anyway when I found this hose was in bad shape.  You 
MAY be able to do it depending on your dexterity from the front of the
engine behind the LHS of the thermo housing if you remove the upper
radiator hose and fuel line.  Tight spot though.

The way I installed it was to hook it up and clamp the end INSIDE the
"V" first, then stick the end of the hose out through the intake
and upon installing the manifold/dome, attach/clamp it to the input
in the valve cover.

The 87 intake design is much different in this regard though from the 86.  
There is a solid dome intake vs rubber pipes so the procedure and space
in there could be different.

It's a fun little procedure to mess with the intake even on the solid
dome of the 87.  Getting 8 intake pipes all lined up like on the 85
and 86 would probably be even more fun!  ;)
