Fellow 928 owners:

I'm trying to troubleshoot a problem with the rear window wiper on my 
'88 S4.  The symptom is no action, motor or relay sound, or any kind of 
response when activating the switch on the console.

So far I've checked for proper circuit closure at the switch (learned 
how by recently restoring the adjacent power window switch) -- the 
switch checked out OK.

I've also probed the harness plug back at the wiper motor... and here's 
where I became puzzled:  the wiper motor appears to to only have 3 
controlling wires (red and green arriving via connector plug, and a 
separate brown one which seems to be a ground attached to motor body).

I'm puzzled because the 2 wires leading to the wiper motor appear to be 
"hot" (12 volts potential) across them at all times.  Maybe this is my 
problem (a short?), but I don't understand what's going on with this 
part of the 928 wiring.

Anyone worked on this area of the electrical system -- and can explain 
what behavior I should be seeing?  Unfortunately I don't (yet) have 
a set of shop manuals.
