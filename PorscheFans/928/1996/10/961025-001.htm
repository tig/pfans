>My dash, which is not covered in leather, was cracked.  No doubt sitting
>outside for the past 13 years didn't do it any good.  Anyway, I installed a
>Coverlay plastic dash cover.  To install it, I had to take off the sun visors
>so I could pull the head liner down around the top of the windshield.  With
>the headliner down, I then took off the trim covering the "A" pillars.  I was
>then able to install the dash cover.  Some trimming of the cover here and
>there was needed.  The cover is siliconed in place.  I then put the trim
>pieces back on and the headliner back up with the sun visors.  All in all, the
>cover looks better than the cracked dash.  The more effort that is put into
>trimming it perfectly and then holding down the cover as it is siliconed, the
>better it looks.
