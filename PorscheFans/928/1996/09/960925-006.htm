When weird things happen, check the ground.  (of the alt.,
the bat, and the engine)

What kind of mods?  Do you have an electronic regulator,
or a mechanical (relay)?  Where is the alternator sense lead?
If it is on the battery, and not a mid-point of your
electrical system, the alt may not be seeing the
voltage you want.

When the battery is low, there is a continuous load on
the system, and the alternator must stay ON, at low RPMs.
Once the battery is charged, there is less load, so
the alternator may drop back production.  This is common
on many types of cars.
