At 11:25 AM 9/25/96 -0700, you wrote:
>According to 928 International, the 928 engine has a design flaw in
>lubrication, so I'd be reeeeel cautious about adding horses... or
>sustaining high RPMs.

Only the latter matters. The passageways through the crankshaft don't go
everywhere they should, with the result that SUSTAINED (not transient, as
in fast acceleration) engine speeds above 6500 rpm will lead to loss of
lubrication of some of the main bearings.

The 928 International and Devek people deserve credit for discovering this
(the hard way) and working out a solution (appropriate additional drilling
of the crankshaft; don't try this at home).

As long as you don't sustain more than 6500 rpm, you can make lots of power
with 928 engines. Richard Miller lives with a roughly 600 bhp example,
which has not done anything rude to him.
