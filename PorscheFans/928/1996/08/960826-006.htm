RE: removing the injector connectors

Once you remove the air intake tubes and crossmember more stuff becomes 
accessible.  You'll have to remove the fuel rail if you need to take out 
the injectors from the intake, which is a little more involved.

If you have the rubber intake tubes I've heard these are more prone to 
leaks than the solid "dome" type of the S4.  The whole system appears to 
be somewhat sensitive to these leaks.

Once you start working on the car, you'll get more familiar with how things
go together.  It's a beast of a car to drive everyday though (I mean that in 
a positive way) - great feeling and I look forward to it every drive home!
