Leonard Laub  Wrote:
| It is generally regarded as a not-good idea to run lower rear pressures
| than front. If the rear of the car loses traction under braking, it will
| come around (because it's sliding while the front's being retarded) and you
| will spin. There may be more important concerns than even tire wear,
| particularly in a car with same size tires all around.

Thanks Leonard for the warning.  It's nice to know somebody cares.  I guess 
your assumption is that the lower pressure in the rear results in less 
traction than the front.  On the contrary, with 32 in the front, I have found 
them to be quite well balanced with 28 in the rear.  I also believe that the 
even wear at 28 is an indication that more of the tire is in contact with the 
pavement than at higher pressures where uneven wear occurs.
