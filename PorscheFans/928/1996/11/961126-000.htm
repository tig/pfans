At 02:32 pm 11/23/96 -0500, you wrote:
>The manual recommends 35 psi front and 44 psi rear for my 88 S4.  This seems
>like a lot of pressure.

The manual wants 36 in the front and 44 in the rear, and those are good
pressures for your wheel and tire sizes. Less will produce soggier handling
and, eventually, bad behavior (e.g., sudden breakaway) under hard cornering.

>My tires are Goodrich Comp T/A Z rated and are the factory sizes.  Wheels
>are BBS 3 piece.  
>
>I get alot of road noise and tire rumble when the
>pressures are set to the above settings.

The effects you describe come from the Comp T/A design, not from the tire
pressures. The Michelin Pilot SX MXX3, which delivers at least all the
performance of the BFG product, is surprisingly quiet and smooth. Other
super tires, like the Bridgestone S-02 and Yokohama AVS Intermediate, are
somewhere between.

>What tire pressure do you use?

When I ran stock tires, I ran stock pressures. Experimentation revealed the
problems discussed above.
