At 09:08 am 11/25/96 EST, you wrote:
>For everyday driving, I run 32 in the fronts and 28 in the rears.

It is generally regarded as a not-good idea to run lower rear pressures
than front. If the rear of the car loses traction under braking, it will
come around (because it's sliding while the front's being retarded) and you
will spin. There may be more important concerns than even tire wear,
particularly in a car with same size tires all around.
